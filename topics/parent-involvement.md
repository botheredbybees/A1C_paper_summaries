## Parent Involvement in Autism Treatment

The sources highlight the critical role of parent involvement in autism treatment interventions. Specifically, Pivotal Response Treatment (PRT) emphasizes parental engagement as a cornerstone for its efficacy. Parents are actively trained to implement strategies learned during therapy sessions at home, thereby extending therapeutic benefits beyond clinical settings and enhancing children's developmental progress. However, there is an implicit acknowledgment that such intensive involvement requires substantial training and support for parents to effectively apply these techniques.

## Key Findings

- Parental engagement in behavioral therapies like PRT enhances treatment outcomes.
- Training parents to implement therapy strategies outside of clinical settings increases overall efficacy.
- Effective parent involvement necessitates comprehensive training and support.

## Open Questions

- How can the feasibility and sustainability of intensive parental involvement be balanced with practical constraints?
