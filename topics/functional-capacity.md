## Functional Capacity Improvement in Older Adults

The sources collectively explore dance as a therapeutic intervention to enhance physical capabilities and reduce fall risks among older adults. These studies indicate that regular participation in dance programs can significantly improve balance, flexibility, coordination, and strength. Such improvements contribute positively to overall functional capacity by reducing the likelihood of falls and promoting better quality of life.

## Key Findings

- Dance interventions are effective for improving physical capabilities such as balance and flexibility in older adults.
- Regular engagement in dance activities can reduce fall risks among elderly populations.
- Functional capacity enhancement through dance contributes to maintaining independence and mobility.

## Open Questions

- What long-term effects do sustained dance interventions have on the functional capacity of aging individuals?
