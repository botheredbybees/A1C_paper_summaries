## Ethical Considerations in Data Dissemination

The provided sources emphasize the importance of ethical considerations when disseminating research findings. Both sources highlight the necessity to preserve participant anonymity and adhere to the Vancouver Protocol for authorship and data reporting, ensuring transparency and accountability. The repeated mention of these principles underscores their significance in maintaining integrity within academic communication practices.

## Key Findings

- The preservation of participant anonymity is crucial during data dissemination
- Adherence to the Vancouver Protocol ensures ethical standards are met regarding authorship and data reporting

## Open Questions

- How can researchers ensure full transparency while simultaneously protecting participants' privacy?
