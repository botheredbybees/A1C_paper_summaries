## Underrepresentation of Artists with Disabilities

Research on arts and disability in Australia highlights significant challenges faced by artists with disabilities, including underrepresentation, barriers to participation, and negative attitudes from the broader community. The underrepresentation of artists with disabilities is a persistent issue across various artistic disciplines, suggesting systemic constraints rather than individual limitations. Barriers to entry and engagement are multifaceted, encompassing access issues such as physical inaccessibility, financial barriers, and lack of inclusive opportunities tailored to diverse needs. Additionally, negative attitudes and stigma surrounding disability further marginalize this population, limiting their visibility within the arts community. These challenges underscore a broader societal issue where artists with disabilities face systemic discrimination and exclusion.

## Key Findings

- Artists with disabilities are significantly underrepresented across various artistic disciplines in Australia.
- Barriers to participation for artists with disabilities include physical inaccessibility, financial constraints, and lack of inclusive opportunities.
- Negative attitudes and stigma surrounding disability contribute to the marginalization of artists with disabilities.
