## Missed Early Intervention Opportunities for Children With Autism Spectrum Disorder

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act Part C (IDEA Part C) focuses on providing early intervention services to children from birth to age three who have disabilities or are at risk of developmental delays. However, many sources highlight the critical need for timely diagnosis and treatment, suggesting that opportunities for early intervention may be missed due to delayed diagnoses. This delay in access to appropriate interventions can significantly affect a child's development and future educational outcomes. The challenge lies not only in improving diagnostic procedures but also in ensuring families are aware of and have access to IDEA Part C services.

## Key Findings

- Timely diagnosis and intervention are crucial for positive developmental outcomes in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD).
- The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act Part C provides early intervention services for young children with disabilities or at risk of developmental delays.
- Many families face barriers to accessing these critical early intervention services due to delayed diagnoses.

## Open Questions
