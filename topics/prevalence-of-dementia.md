## Prevalence Of Dementia

The provided sources explore the diverse impacts and variations in dementia prevalence across different populations, focusing particularly on Aboriginal Australians. The research highlights significant disparities between urban and regional Aboriginal communities regarding Alzheimer's disease and mixed dementia rates compared to non-Aboriginal groups. While studies confirm a higher incidence of neurocognitive disorders among older Indigenous people due to socio-economic factors and health inequalities, there is a lack of comprehensive data specific to different geographical areas within these populations, leading to challenges in tailoring effective interventions or public health strategies.

## Key Findings

- Higher prevalence rates of dementia are observed among Aboriginal Australians compared to non-Aboriginal groups.
- Urban vs. regional disparities exist within the Aboriginal population regarding dementia incidence due to varying socio-economic conditions.
- Health inequalities contribute significantly to increased neurocognitive disorder risks for older Indigenous individuals.
