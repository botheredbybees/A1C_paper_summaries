## Salivary Cortisol Measurement

The use of salivary cortisol and alpha-amylase as biomarkers to assess the impact of interventions on stress is highlighted across several studies. Specifically, in a multinational cluster-randomized trial examining music interventions for care home residents with dementia, salivary cortisol levels were measured alongside other indicators like alpha-amylase activity. The research indicates that group music therapy and recreational choir singing can have short-term positive effects on reducing stress biomarkers such as salivary cortisol, suggesting these methods are effective in managing stress among this population. However, the application of similar methodologies to broader populations and different contexts remains an open question.

## Key Findings

- Salivary cortisol is a reliable marker for assessing short-term impacts of interventions on stress levels.
- Group music therapy and recreational choir singing have positive effects on reducing salivary cortisol in care home residents with dementia.
- Alpha-amylase, alongside salivary cortisol, provides additional insights into physiological responses to stress.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of group music interventions on salivary cortisol levels and overall well-being?
