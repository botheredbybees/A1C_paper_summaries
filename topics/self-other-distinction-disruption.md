## Self-Other Distinction Disruption

The concept of self-other distinction disruption is prominently featured in studies exploring the effects of trance dance on individuals. San trance dance is one such activity that engages participants through intense, ritualistic movements and can lead to altered states of consciousness. This state involves a disruption of the usual cognitive process where an individual differentiates between their own sense of self and others. The literature highlights the activation of the sympathetic nervous system during such dances, contributing to heightened arousal and emotional engagement which may facilitate this disruption. Key findings across sources indicate that trance dance can induce significant neurological changes, including altered states of consciousness and a reduction in self-awareness, promoting a more fluid boundary between the self and others.

## Key Findings

- Trance dance induces alterations in cognitive processes related to self-awareness.
- Activation of the sympathetic nervous system contributes to heightened emotional engagement during trance dance.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects on individuals who frequently experience disruptions in self-other distinction through trance dances?
