## Interactive Learning in Educational Settings

The synthesis of research on school-based drama interventions and broader approaches to interactive learning reveals a consistent emphasis on the role of experiential engagement in fostering cognitive and social development among young learners. Sources highlight how educational drama, as an intervention strategy, leverages principles of self-reflection theory to enhance students' ability to process information critically and creatively. This approach not only supports traditional academic objectives but also promotes emotional well-being through its facilitation of self-awareness and empathy. Despite the documented benefits of interactive learning methods, there are concerns about their scalability within large educational systems where standardized testing pressures may prioritize more didactic forms of instruction.

## Key Findings

- School-based drama interventions enhance cognitive processing through experiential engagement
- Educational drama supports emotional well-being and self-awareness among students
- Interactive learning methods can be challenging to implement in large educational systems

## Open Questions

- How can interactive learning strategies like school-based drama be effectively scaled up within broader education frameworks?
