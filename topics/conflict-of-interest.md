## Conflict of Interest

The concept of conflict of interest is crucial in maintaining the integrity and ethics of research and professional practices. Across sources, it is recognized as a significant barrier to unbiased research outcomes and effective interventions. Sources highlight the need for clear guidelines and responsibilities regarding conflicts of interest to ensure that participant benefits are prioritized over personal or institutional gains. This includes disclosing any potential conflicts early on in research endeavors to maintain transparency and trust within the scientific community.

## Key Findings

- Conflict of interest poses a significant ethical challenge in both research and practice.
- Clear guidelines for managing conflict of interest enhance professional responsibilities.
- Disclosure of conflicts ensures participant benefits are prioritized over personal gains.

## Open Questions

- How can disclosure policies be universally applied to ensure equity across different institutional contexts?
