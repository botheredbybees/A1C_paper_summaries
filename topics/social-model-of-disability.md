## Social Model Of Disability

The Social Model of Disability is a pivotal theoretical framework that challenges traditional understandings by shifting the focus from individual impairments to societal barriers. It asserts that disability is not an attribute of individuals but rather the result of social and environmental obstacles that hinder full participation in society (Key Concept: Social Model of Disability). Sources emphasize the importance of language in perpetuating or dismantling these barriers; while some advocate for person-first language as a means of promoting dignity, others argue that identity-first language is more empowering by directly addressing disability identity (Key Concept: person-first-language, identity-first-language). Additionally, sources like the PWDA Language Guide highlight how even well-intentioned expressions can be exclusionary or stigmatizing, reinforcing the need for inclusive language and attitudes. In practical applications, this model informs initiatives such as building dementia-friendly communities which aim to enhance accessibility and support for individuals with disabilities (Key Concept: Healthy Cities, Compassionate Cities). Another application is through inclusive arts programs that utilize the Social Model's principles to foster a more therapeutic environment that embraces diversity and creativity (Key Concept: inclusive arts, social model of disability, therapeutic focus). However, there are tensions between different approaches to language usage and varying interpretations of what constitutes an inclusive society.

## Key Findings

- The Social Model of Disability reframes disability as primarily a societal issue rather than an individual condition.
- Language plays a critical role in shaping perceptions and attitudes towards disability; both person-first and identity-first languages are considered but with different implications for empowerment.
- Inclusive practices like dementia-friendly communities and inclusive arts initiatives embody the principles of the Social Model.

## Open Questions

- What is the most effective approach to language usage when considering both individual preferences and societal impact?
