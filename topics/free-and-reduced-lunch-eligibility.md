## Free And Reduced Lunch Eligibility

The analysis of the impact of extracurricular participation on academic outcomes and attendance highlights significant variations based on free-and-reduced-lunch (FRL) eligibility. The research underscores demographic disparities, particularly focusing on how FRL-eligible students often face challenges that non-FRL-eligible peers do not. Key concepts such as PARCC test scores reveal differential performance between these groups, suggesting that FRL status is a critical indicator of academic success and engagement in extracurricular activities. However, the extent to which participation in extracurriculars mitigates this gap remains unclear.

## Key Findings

- PARCC test scores indicate disparities among students based on free-and-reduced-lunch eligibility.
- Demographic disparities are exacerbated by access to or lack of engagement in extracurricular activities.
- Extracurricular participation may have a positive impact on attendance and academic outcomes for FRL-eligible students.

## Open Questions

- What is the extent to which participation in extracurriculars can mitigate the performance gap between free-and-reduced-lunch eligible and non-eligible students?
