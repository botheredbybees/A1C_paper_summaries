## Art Interventions in Occupational Therapy

The integration of visual art into occupational therapy (OT) is explored across two sources, highlighting its multifaceted benefits for diverse populations. Source 1 focuses on the rehabilitation goals and self-esteem enhancement among individuals with neurological conditions through visual art practices. The narrative underscores the clients' perspectives, indicating that engaging in such activities not only aids physical recovery but also boosts psychological well-being by fostering a sense of accomplishment and control over their lives. In contrast, Source 2 presents a scoping review on creative arts interventions for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), emphasizing its role in improving performance skills and social interactions. The study aligns with the Occupational Therapy Practice Framework, suggesting that these interventions can facilitate sensory integration, enhance fine motor skills, and provide opportunities for emotional expression and communication. Both sources highlight the therapeutic potential of art-based activities within OT practices, though they differ in their targeted populations and specific outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Art interventions are effective in enhancing self-esteem among individuals recovering from neurological conditions.
- Creative arts can improve social interaction skills and performance abilities for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).
- Occupational therapy practices incorporating art-based activities align with the Occupational Therapy Practice Framework.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which art interventions contribute to both physical rehabilitation goals and psychological well-being in neurological patients?
