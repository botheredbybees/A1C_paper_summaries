## Therapeutic Functions Of Art

The therapeutic functions of art are highlighted across sources as a means to promote emotional and psychological healing through creative expression. One key theme is the use of art in therapy to facilitate self-expression, which can be particularly beneficial for individuals who struggle with verbal communication or have experienced trauma. Sources also emphasize collaborative teaching approaches where artists and therapists work together to develop therapeutic interventions that leverage artistic processes to address mental health issues. However, there are some tensions regarding how strictly the boundaries between art making and therapeutic practice should be defined, with some advocating for a more integrated approach while others suggest maintaining clearer distinctions.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy promotes emotional healing through creative expression
- Collaborative teaching approaches between artists and therapists enhance therapeutic interventions
- Creative processes in art therapy can benefit those who struggle with verbal communication

## Open Questions

- How should the boundaries be defined between artistic practice and therapeutic intervention?
