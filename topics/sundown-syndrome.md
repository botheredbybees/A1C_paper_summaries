## Sundown Syndrome

Sundown syndrome, a common phenomenon experienced by individuals with dementia, manifests as increased agitation and confusion during the late afternoon or evening. Across multiple sources, it is clear that sundowner symptoms can significantly impact both patients' quality of life and caregivers’ well-being. Support systems play a pivotal role in managing this condition; however, inconsistencies arise regarding the most effective interventions. While some emphasize environmental modifications and consistent daily routines, others highlight the importance of pharmacological treatments or psychological support for caregivers.

## Key Findings

- Sundown syndrome is characterized by increased agitation and confusion in individuals with dementia during late afternoon and evening hours.
- Support systems are crucial in managing sundowner symptoms through various interventions.
- There is a need for tailored approaches to address sundown syndrome, considering both environmental modifications and psychological support.
