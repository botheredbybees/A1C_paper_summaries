## Educational Performance

The synthesis of studies focusing on the 'Big Noise' program in Scotland reveals a consistent theme regarding its impact on educational performance. The Big Noise program, modelled after Venezuela's El Sistema, aims to improve not just musical abilities but also academic outcomes and overall health and wellbeing among participants. Across multiple case studies, it is observed that students involved in such music-based interventions show significant improvements in their educational performance metrics compared to non-participants. However, there are tensions regarding the extent to which these programs can be scaled up while maintaining their effectiveness and impact on a broader population. Additionally, some sources highlight variability in outcomes across different communities or demographics, suggesting a need for more nuanced understanding of program adaptation.

## Key Findings

- Music-based interventions like Big Noise positively influence educational performance metrics among participants.
- There is evidence that such programs also contribute to health and wellbeing improvements alongside academic gains.
- Implementation of music intervention programs requires careful consideration regarding scalability and sustainability.

## Open Questions

- What are the mechanisms through which music education specifically impacts educational performance?
