## Behavioral Neuroscience

Behavioral neuroscience synthesizes biological and psychological perspectives to understand human behavior. Sources discuss the application of the scientific method in studying brain-behavior relationships (Source 1) and delve into specific developmental stages, such as adolescent neurodevelopment (Source 2). Source 2 highlights how synaptic pruning, myelination, and changes in functional connectivity shape cognitive and emotional development during adolescence. Additionally, hormonal and socioeconomic factors influence neural maturation processes, particularly affecting the prefrontal cortex's growth and function.

## Key Findings

- Behavioral neuroscience integrates biological and psychological theories to explore brain-behavior relationships.
- Adolescent neurodevelopment involves significant changes in synaptic pruning, myelination, and functional connectivity.
- Hormonal changes and socioeconomic factors play crucial roles in shaping adolescent neural development.
