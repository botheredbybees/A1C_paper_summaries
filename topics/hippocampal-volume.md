## Hippocampal Volume Changes

The hippocampus, a brain region critical for memory and learning, exhibits changes in volume over time that are influenced by factors such as cortisol levels (glucocorticoids) and physical activity. Source 1 indicates that elevated glucocorticoid exposure during aging is predictive of reduced hippocampal volume and impaired delayed recall memory. In contrast, Source 2 highlights the beneficial effects of exercise on Alzheimer's disease, a condition often characterized by diminished hippocampal volume. Exercise promotes brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) production which supports neuronal health and potentially mitigates age-related cognitive decline. The interplay between stress hormones like cortisol and physical activity underscores the importance of lifestyle factors in maintaining hippocampal integrity throughout life.

## Key Findings

- Hippocampal volume decreases with aging, correlating with higher levels of glucocorticoids such as cortisol.
- Physical exercise increases BDNF production which can mitigate age-related cognitive decline and preserve hippocampal volume.
- Exercise may be a protective factor against Alzheimer's disease by supporting neuronal health.
