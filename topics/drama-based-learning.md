## Drama-Based Learning

Drama-based learning serves as a versatile educational strategy that enhances engagement and comprehension among diverse populations. Sources highlight its effectiveness in sexual health promotion for multicultural youth, emphasizing the interactive theatre approach of Sharing Stories to enhance knowledge retention and comfort with sensitive topics (Source 1). Another application is seen through Sydney Theatre Company's initiative aimed at integrating refugees into Australian society via creative activities (Source 2), underscoring drama-based learning's adaptability across various educational needs. Both sources indicate that this method not only enriches cultural understanding but also aids in language acquisition for non-native speakers, suggesting a broader impact beyond initial health or social integration goals.

## Key Findings

- Drama-based learning enhances sexual health knowledge and awareness among multicultural youth populations through interactive theatre approaches.
- The method is effective in supporting English as an Additional Language (EAL) learners by facilitating cultural engagement and language acquisition in a creative context.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term educational and social benefits of drama-based learning for refugee and multicultural student populations?
