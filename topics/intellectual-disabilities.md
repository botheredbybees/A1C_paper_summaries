## Intellectual Disabilities

The study of individuals with intellectual disabilities encompasses various aspects such as self-determination and social inclusion through community art interventions (Source 1) and the use of therapeutic clowns to enhance playfulness (Source 2). These approaches highlight diverse methodologies aimed at improving quality of life. Community art programs are designed to foster autonomy and social engagement, whereas clown therapy focuses on playful interactions that may facilitate emotional well-being and cognitive development. Both sources underscore the importance of tailored interventions that address specific needs and preferences of individuals with intellectual disabilities.

## Key Findings

- Community art interventions support self-determination and social inclusion for people with intellectual disabilities
- Therapeutic clown interventions can enhance playfulness in children with intellectual disabilities

## Open Questions

- How do different intervention types compare in their effectiveness for improving specific developmental milestones in individuals with intellectual disabilities?
