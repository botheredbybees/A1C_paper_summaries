## Historical Trauma

The concept of historical trauma is explored across various disciplines as a foundational theory to understand the intergenerational transmission of psychological distress and mental health issues within specific populations. In the context provided, historical trauma specifically impacts Indigenous communities by exacerbating mental health challenges through layers of collective trauma inherited over generations due to systemic oppression and cultural disruption. The role of art in healing historical trauma emerges as a significant theme, highlighting its potential for therapeutic intervention and community resilience. However, effective responses to historical trauma must consider the nuances of co-design processes that respect Indigenous knowledge systems and traditions, such as the concept of 'The Dreaming'. This highlights both the therapeutic benefits of engaging with cultural practices and the critical importance of culturally appropriate methodologies when addressing complex social issues related to historical trauma.

## Key Findings

- Historical trauma has a profound impact on mental health within Indigenous populations, manifesting through intergenerational transmission.
- Art serves as a powerful medium for healing and therapeutic intervention in communities grappling with historical trauma.
- Co-design processes are essential when developing interventions to address historical trauma, ensuring they respect local cultural contexts.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of sustained engagement with art-based therapies on intergenerational transmission of historical trauma?
