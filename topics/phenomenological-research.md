## Phenomenological Research Methods

Phenomenological research methods, as discussed across the sources provided, focus on understanding experiences through an individual's subjective perspective. In Source 1, social dancing and walks for dementia patients are explored using Husserl’s philosophical framework to highlight positive emotional responses influenced by environmental factors. This approach emphasizes the importance of context in shaping personal experience and emotion. Meanwhile, Source 2 introduces a specific application of phenomenological-hermeneutic methods within Talking Pictures Therapy, which utilizes photographs as therapeutic tools to facilitate deeper understanding and interpretation of life experiences. Both sources underscore the value of detailed qualitative analysis for uncovering nuanced insights into human behavior and emotional well-being.

## Key Findings

- Phenomenology provides a rich framework for exploring subjective experiences in therapeutic contexts.
- Environmental factors significantly influence emotional responses, especially among individuals with dementia.
- Photographs serve as powerful tools for fostering personal reflection and interpretation within therapy.

## Open Questions

- How can phenomenological methods be further adapted to address the unique challenges faced by diverse populations, such as those with varying cognitive abilities or cultural backgrounds?
