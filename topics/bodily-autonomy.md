## Bodily Autonomy

The concept of bodily autonomy is central to understanding how individuals with dementia experience their sense of personhood and rights. The notion of embodied selfhood emphasizes that the physical body is integral to one's identity, suggesting that respecting bodily autonomy is crucial in maintaining a patient’s dignity and sense of self. However, this framework also highlights the complexity of tacit knowledge and the sedimentation of habits within individuals with dementia, indicating challenges when caregivers try to preserve bodily autonomy while addressing safety concerns or care needs. These sources underscore the need for an ethical approach that respects the embodied selfhood of patients but must navigate the intricate balance between preserving autonomy and ensuring care.

## Key Findings

- Bodily autonomy is a fundamental aspect of maintaining personhood in individuals with dementia.
- The integration of tacit knowledge and sedimentation of habits complicates efforts to preserve bodily autonomy.
- There is an ethical imperative to respect the embodied selfhood while addressing care needs.

## Open Questions

- How can caregivers effectively balance respecting bodily autonomy with ensuring patient safety and well-being?
