## Music Therapy for Depression

The efficacy of music therapy in treating depression is increasingly recognized, with a notable focus on understanding the mechanisms through which it operates. Interactive music therapy (IMT), characterized by active engagement between therapist and patient, stands out due to its potential for deep relational engagement that fosters therapeutic alliances. IMT encourages patients to express themselves through music, facilitating emotional release and fostering a sense of self-efficacy. Physical engagement in music therapy is another significant component; it includes bodily movements such as playing instruments or singing, which can enhance mood by promoting endorphin release. However, despite empirical evidence supporting its benefits, the precise pathways through which IMT exerts its therapeutic influence remain underexplored, leaving questions about how physical and relational engagements specifically contribute to improved mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Interactive music therapy (IMT) is effective in treating depression due to its potential for fostering strong therapeutic alliances.
- Physical engagement through IMT can enhance mood by promoting the release of endorphins, contributing positively to mental health.
- The integration of aesthetic and relational engagements within IMT creates a holistic approach that addresses multiple aspects of depressive symptoms.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms link physical engagement in music therapy with improvements in depression?
