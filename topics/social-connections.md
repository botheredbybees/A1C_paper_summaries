## Social Connections Through Arts and Therapy

The integration of arts within healthcare settings serves as a conduit for enhancing social connections among diverse populations, including those on the autism spectrum. The National Arts and Health Framework emphasizes health-environment-integration to foster well-being through art-based interventions that go beyond therapy-specific practices towards general engagement. Meanwhile, studies focusing on expressive arts therapy highlight its effectiveness in building empathy and promoting therapeutic relationships in individuals with autism. These frameworks underscore the potential of arts in creating inclusive environments where social bonds are strengthened regardless of individual diagnoses. However, while there is agreement on the benefits of engaging in artistic activities for improving social skills, specific methodologies vary widely between general engagement strategies and specialized practices like tele-dance movement psychotherapy.

## Key Findings

- The integration of arts into healthcare settings improves social connections among various populations.
- Expressive arts therapy enhances empathy mirroring in individuals with autism spectrum disorders.
- Health-environment-integration through the National Arts and Health Framework supports general engagement rather than solely therapeutic interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal methodologies for integrating arts into healthcare to maximize social connections for different patient groups?
