## Model Of Place In Dementia Care

The concept of the 'model of place' in dementia care is integral to understanding how physical environments interact with individuals suffering from dementia. Both sources emphasize the interplay between environmental factors and individual capabilities as described by the Individual Competence and Environmental Press (ICEP) model. The ICEP framework suggests that a supportive environment can mitigate the challenges posed by dementia, enhancing residents' quality of life and daily functioning. This concept is further elaborated through the 'progressively lowered threshold' model, which highlights how environmental adjustments are necessary as cognitive abilities decline over time. Both chapters assert the critical role of thoughtful design in creating safe, engaging spaces that cater to the specific needs of people with dementia.

## Key Findings

- The physical environment plays a crucial role in supporting individuals with dementia through enhancing their daily functioning and quality of life.
- The Individual Competence and Environmental Press (ICEP) model underscores the importance of balancing individual capabilities with environmental demands.
- Design adjustments are necessary to accommodate cognitive decline as suggested by the 'progressively lowered threshold' model.
