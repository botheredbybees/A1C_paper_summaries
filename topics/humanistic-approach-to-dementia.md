## Humanistic Approach To Dementia

The humanistic approach to dementia emphasizes the importance of understanding individuals with dementia within their unique contexts and as part of a dynamic social framework. Source 1 highlights that this approach moves away from viewing dementia purely through medical or biological lenses, instead focusing on the person's subjective experience and societal role. Social constructivism plays a crucial role in this theory, suggesting that the way society perceives and interacts with individuals diagnosed with dementia significantly shapes their lived reality. Contextual interpretation is another key aspect of this approach, which requires practitioners to continually reassess the individual’s needs within evolving personal and social contexts.

## Key Findings

- The humanistic approach emphasizes understanding individuals with dementia in a broader social context rather than solely through clinical perspectives.
- Social constructivism influences how society perceives and interacts with people who have dementia, impacting their quality of life.
- Contextual interpretation is essential for continually adapting care strategies to fit the evolving needs and circumstances of individuals living with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare professionals be better trained in applying a humanistic approach that incorporates social constructivism and contextual interpretation?
