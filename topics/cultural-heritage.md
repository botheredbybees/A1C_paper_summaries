## Cultural Heritage and Community Well-Being

The exploration of cultural heritage through art and community engagement highlights its potential for fostering collective memory and enhancing individual well-being. In Cork's Shandon Big Wash Up murals, the focus is on using public art to re-enchant urban spaces and promote a sense of citizenship among residents. Conversely, museums and galleries are examined for their capacity to mitigate social isolation and support individuals with mental health conditions or dementia through cultural engagement. Both sources emphasize the restorative power of cultural heritage by fostering community connections and providing therapeutic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Public art initiatives can enhance collective memory and civic engagement.
- Museums and galleries offer unique opportunities for reducing social isolation and supporting mental health.
- Cultural heritage sites play a crucial role in re-enchanting urban environments, contributing to the overall well-being of communities.

## Open Questions

- How do varying cultural contexts affect the efficacy of public art interventions on collective memory and community engagement?
