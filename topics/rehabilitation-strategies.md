## Rehabilitation Strategies in Mental Health and Dementia

The exploration of rehabilitation strategies in both dementia care and mental health support highlights a common theme of using creative and therapeutic interventions to improve cognitive function and overall well-being. Source 1 focuses on cognitive training techniques as part of rehabilitation for individuals with dementia, emphasizing the dual approach of enhancing internal memory strategies alongside utilizing external aids such as calendars or reminder cards. In contrast, Source 2 shifts towards creative pathways in juvenile justice rehabilitation, particularly advocating for music-based interventions that foster a holistic and person-centered treatment plan. Similarly, Source 3 examines the role of art therapy not only as a therapeutic tool but also as an integral component in promoting mental health through facilitated creative expression. Despite differences in patient populations (dementia vs. youth involved in juvenile justice), these sources share an understanding that rehabilitation strategies should be adaptable and tailored to individual needs, leveraging creativity to address both cognitive challenges and emotional well-being.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive training and memory aids are crucial components of dementia care.
- Music-based interventions offer a holistic approach for youth in juvenile justice systems.
- Art therapy serves as an effective mental health rehabilitation tool.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of music-based and art therapies be integrated into cognitive rehabilitation programs for individuals with dementia to enhance their efficacy?
