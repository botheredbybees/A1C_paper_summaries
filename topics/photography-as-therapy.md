## Photography as Therapy

The integration of photographic practices into therapeutic settings highlights the unique capacity of this medium to facilitate profound personal reflection and emotional expression. Source 1 examines '28 Solomon Street', a photo essay that delves deeply into the therapeutic process through Roland Barthes' Camera Lucida, which underscores photography's ability to capture moments with profound meaning and emotional resonance. This aligns with Source 2’s exploration of photography as an intervention for depression, emphasizing its capacity to aid in trauma processing and self-reflection. Both sources underscore how engaging with photography can serve as a powerful means for individuals to articulate their internal experiences, thereby facilitating healing through creative expression.

## Key Findings

- Photography allows for deep personal reflection and emotional articulation.
- Engaging in photographic practices can be beneficial in the context of treating depression.
- Trauma processing and self-reflection are significant therapeutic outcomes facilitated by photography.
