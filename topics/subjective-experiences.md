## The Experience Of Dementia

The experience of dementia is a multifaceted and deeply personal phenomenon that significantly impacts an individual's sense of self and their relationships with others. Sources emphasize the importance of personhood theory in understanding how individuals with dementia maintain a sense of identity amidst cognitive decline. Intersubjectivity, or the shared understanding between people, plays a crucial role as caregivers and patients co-create meaning in interactions despite communication challenges.

## Key Findings

- The experience of dementia involves significant changes to an individual's sense of self.
- Personhood theory is essential for comprehending how individuals maintain identity amidst cognitive decline.
- Intersubjectivity in caregiver-patient relationships helps navigate communication difficulties.

## Open Questions

- How can intersubjective understanding be effectively integrated into clinical practice and care policies?
