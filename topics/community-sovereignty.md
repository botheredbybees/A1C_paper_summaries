## Community Sovereignty in Health

The sources collectively emphasize the importance of integrating indigenous epistemologies and community sovereignty into public health initiatives aimed at improving the well-being of marginalized communities. While acknowledging the significance of social determinants of health (SDH), there is a call for moving beyond these to incorporate culturally specific frameworks that reflect the unique needs, values, and healing practices of indigenous populations. This shift towards more holistic approaches underscores the need for interventions that not only address structural issues but also honor the cultural and spiritual dimensions of wellness.

## Key Findings

- The social determinants of health (SDH) framework is insufficient on its own to improve health outcomes for marginalized communities, especially indigenous populations.
- Community sovereignty represents a critical component in reclaiming health practices that are culturally relevant and empowering for indigenous communities.
- Interventions need to integrate indigenous epistemologies to address the unique challenges faced by these groups.

## Open Questions

- How can public health policies effectively incorporate community sovereignty without perpetuating paternalistic or neo-colonial approaches?
