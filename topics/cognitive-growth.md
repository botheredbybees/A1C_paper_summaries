## Cognitive Growth Across Developmental Stages

The concept of cognitive growth is explored across different developmental stages in the provided sources. Source 1 highlights infancy and the early years as critical periods for developing foundational learning abilities, emphasizing the importance of safe and stimulating environments such as tummy-time activities to foster these skills. In contrast, Source 2 shifts focus to adolescence, a period characterized by significant cognitive development and the need for comprehensive sexuality education alongside life skills development. Both sources underscore the necessity of tailored approaches that support cognitive growth at different developmental stages, but diverge in their specific recommendations and contexts (infancy versus adolescence). There is an underlying agreement on the importance of age-appropriate interventions to facilitate optimal cognitive development.

## Key Findings

- Safe and stimulating environments are crucial for early cognitive development.
- Tailored educational programs such as tummy-time activities support foundational learning abilities in infancy.
- Comprehensive sexuality education and life skills development play pivotal roles during adolescence.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal strategies to bridge the gap between infancy and adolescence in terms of continuous cognitive growth?
