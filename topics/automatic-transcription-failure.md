## Automatic Transcription Failure

The examination of methods used in transcription technologies reveals significant issues related to the failure of automatic transcription and subsequent unavailability of captions. Across various studies focusing on these technological failures, there is a common concern that automatic transcription tools often fail to accurately transcribe audio or video content, leading to caption inaccuracies or complete lack thereof. This problem affects not only the accessibility but also the reliability of digital media resources. Despite advancements in machine learning and natural language processing, challenges persist due to variations in speech patterns, accents, and background noise. The synthesis of these findings highlights the need for further research into improving transcription algorithms while ensuring greater accuracy and reliability.

## Key Findings

- Automatic transcription tools often fail to accurately transcribe audio or video content.
- Caption unavailability is a significant issue arising from automatic transcription failures.
- Variations in speech patterns, accents, and background noise contribute to the challenges faced by transcription technologies.
