## Activities of Daily Living

The concept of Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) emerges as a critical element in the management and intervention for individuals with Alzheimer's disease. Sources highlight how engagement in structured activities, particularly those involving music, can enhance quality of life by improving functional abilities related to ADLs such as bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, transferring, and continence. However, there is a gap in understanding the full economic impact of these interventions on long-term care costs reduction. While preliminary studies indicate potential monetization pathways for enhancing patient independence through music-based activities, further research is needed to quantify the broader economic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Activities of Daily Living are crucial measures for assessing functional ability and quality of life in individuals with Alzheimer's disease.
- Music engagement can positively influence ADL capabilities among people suffering from dementia by enhancing cognitive functions and emotional well-being.
- Potential exists to monetize improvements in ADL through cost savings on long-term care expenses due to increased patient independence.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific economic benefits of music-based interventions for enhancing activities of daily living, and how can these be quantified more precisely?
