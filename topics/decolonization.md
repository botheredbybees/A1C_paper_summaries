## Decolonization

The concept of decolonization in the context of arts-based work with Native communities emphasizes an ethical systems approach that integrates cultural resilience and traditional knowledge. This theoretical framework aims to dismantle colonial structures and revive indigenous practices, ensuring the preservation and enhancement of native cultures. The sources highlight the critical role of traditional knowledge in fostering cultural resilience and promoting a sustainable path for decolonization efforts.

## Key Findings

- Decolonization is inherently linked with ethical systems that promote cultural revitalization among indigenous communities.
- Traditional knowledge plays a pivotal role in building cultural resilience and enabling meaningful change within Native societies.

## Open Questions

- How can traditional knowledge be effectively integrated into contemporary decolonization efforts without risking its integrity or authenticity?
