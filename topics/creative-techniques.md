## Creative Techniques in Photography and Therapy

The sources presented explore the application of creative techniques across two distinct fields: photography and therapeutic practice with children. In photography, the use of rules like the rule of thirds (Source 1) enhances composition, while a three-picture technique can construct a cohesive narrative to engage viewers emotionally (Source 2). Similarly, in therapeutic settings, employing abstract thinking skills through expressive arts such as photography can aid trauma-exposed children's emotional healing (Source 3). Both contexts highlight how creative techniques facilitate engagement and expression, whether for artistic storytelling or psychological recovery. However, while the photographic methods are geared towards enhancing visual communication and viewer interaction, the therapeutic application focuses on aiding personal development and coping mechanisms in a clinical setting.

## Key Findings

- Creative techniques enhance both aesthetic composition in photography and emotional healing in trauma-exposed children
- The use of rules such as the rule of thirds aids in effective image composition
- A three-picture narrative technique can engage viewers emotionally and provide structure to photo stories

## Open Questions

- How do specific photographic techniques impact therapeutic outcomes when used by traumatized children?
