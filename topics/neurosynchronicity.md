## Neurosynchronicity

The concept of neurosynchronicity represents a theoretical framework that explores the coordinated neural activities across individuals, particularly among musicians. This theory highlights communal brain functions and their therapeutic implications for cognitive disorders. The research underscores how synchronized neural patterns can lead to enhanced cognitive processes and social integration within groups. Sources discuss these phenomena in detail, emphasizing the potential benefits of neurosynchronicity for understanding and treating conditions such as autism spectrum disorder and schizophrenia. However, while there is agreement on the importance of communal brain functions, the exact mechanisms by which these networks form and operate remain largely speculative.

## Key Findings

- Neurosynchronicity involves coordinated neural activities across individuals.
- Communal brain functions can have therapeutic implications for cognitive disorders.
- Enhanced social integration through synchronized neural patterns

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms behind the formation of communal neural networks?
