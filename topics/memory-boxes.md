## Memory Boxes

The concept of memory boxes as an intervention in supporting cognitive and emotional well-being is explored through various studies. Memory boxes are physical collections of items that hold sentimental or nostalgic value, often used to stimulate sensory engagement and evoke personal memories. The use of these boxes helps individuals connect with their past experiences, fostering a sense of identity and continuity. While memory trees represent another method for visually mapping out an individual's life events, the tactile nature of memory boxes adds a unique layer of interaction that can be particularly beneficial for those who find it challenging to articulate their memories through speech or writing alone. The sensory stimulation provided by physical objects in memory boxes is seen as crucial for engaging individuals with cognitive impairments, such as dementia patients, enhancing recall and emotional connections.

## Key Findings

- Memory boxes serve as effective tools for stimulating sensory engagement and evoking personal memories.
- The tactile interaction offered by memory boxes complements verbal or written methods like memory trees in fostering a sense of identity and continuity.
- Sensory stimulation through physical objects is beneficial for individuals with cognitive impairments.
