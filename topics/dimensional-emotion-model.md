## Dimensional Emotion Model

The sources discuss the application of different models to understand music-induced emotions. The Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS) uses a dimensional approach to describe emotional responses, focusing on two main dimensions: valence and arousal. This model contrasts with discrete emotion theories that identify specific types of emotions like happiness or sadness. While GEMS provides a nuanced understanding by capturing the complexity of emotional experiences in response to music, it also highlights personality biases influencing how individuals perceive and rate their emotional states. A key challenge identified is the need for further research on refining measurement tools such as GEMS to better capture individual differences in emotional responses.

## Key Findings

- The Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS) employs a dimensional approach to assess emotions induced by music.
- Discrete emotion models identify specific types of emotional experiences but may miss the nuances captured through dimensional approaches.
- Personality biases affect how individuals perceive and rate their emotional responses to music.

## Open Questions

- How can measurement tools like GEMS be improved to more accurately capture individual differences in emotional response?
