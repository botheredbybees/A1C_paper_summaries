## Expressions Of Resistance

The examination of caregivers' singing as a form of Music Therapeutic Caregiving (MTC) reveals its potential to reduce expressions of resistance in persons with dementia during morning care situations. This intervention is shown to foster positive emotions, indicating that music can be an effective tool for emotional regulation and easing stress-related behaviors. However, the research also highlights the complexity involved in managing expressions of resistance through such interventions, suggesting a need for further exploration into how MTC can better accommodate the diverse needs and responses of individuals with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Music Therapeutic Caregiving (MTC) reduces expressions of resistance.
- Caregivers' singing fosters positive emotions during morning care routines.
- The impact of MTC varies, suggesting a need for personalized approaches

## Open Questions

- How can the variability in responses to MTC be better understood and addressed?
