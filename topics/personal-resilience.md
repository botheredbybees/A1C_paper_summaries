## Personal Resilience

The concept of personal resilience emerges from two distinct yet complementary perspectives. The first source emphasizes the therapeutic benefits of nature and engaging in creative hobbies as means to foster psychological resilience during challenging times such as lockdowns. This approach highlights how reconnecting with one's environment and nurturing one’s creativity can serve as protective factors against mental health decline, illustrating a proactive stance towards maintaining well-being. In contrast, the second source delves into memory loss and its impact on personal identity, suggesting that resilience also involves processes of remembrance and reconstruction, especially in the face of debilitating conditions like dementia. This perspective underscores the importance of preserving and reclaiming fragmented memories to maintain a sense of self continuity and emotional stability despite cognitive impairments.

## Key Findings

- Nature and creative activities contribute significantly to psychological resilience during stressful periods.
- Reclaiming lost or fragmented personal memories can serve as a form of therapeutic intervention in conditions that affect memory, such as dementia.
- Personal resilience involves both proactive engagement with one's environment (like nature) and reactive measures like remembering lost experiences.

## Open Questions

- How do the protective factors identified for psychological resilience during lockdowns translate to individuals facing chronic cognitive decline due to long-term illnesses?
