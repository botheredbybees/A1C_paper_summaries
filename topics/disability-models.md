## Disability Models

The examination of community perspectives on support goals for young autistic children highlights the complexity and variability in understanding disability models. While there is a consensus on the importance of these goals, especially regarding quality-of-life improvements, different communities may emphasize distinct aspects based on their philosophical and practical approaches to disability. The document underscores the need for inclusive dialogue that incorporates various stakeholders' views to develop effective support systems.

## Key Findings

- Support goals are universally recognized as crucial in enhancing the quality of life for young autistic children.
- Community perspectives vary significantly, reflecting different interpretations of disability models and their implications.
- Inclusive participation from diverse community members is essential for crafting holistic and responsive support strategies.

## Open Questions

- How can research better incorporate the varied perspectives on disability models to ensure equitable support systems?
