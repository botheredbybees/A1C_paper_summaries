## Tactile Exhibits in Museums

The synthesis of practices aimed at enhancing museum accessibility for visually impaired visitors highlights the importance of tactile exhibits and multisensory experiences. Both sources emphasize the role of technology, such as audio-guides and 3D printing, to provide alternative ways for visitors to engage with the exhibits. Source one discusses best practices including the creation of multilingual audio guides that go beyond textual descriptions to include soundscapes and environmental sounds. Similarly, source two highlights Louvre Abu Dhabi's Tactile Studio, which uses an inclusive design approach by integrating 3D-printing and VR technology to create tactile models of artworks for better sensory exploration. Despite these advancements, both sources acknowledge the challenge in fully realizing inclusivity due to limitations such as cost, technology access, and varying visitor needs.

## Key Findings

- Multisensory experiences are crucial for enhancing museum accessibility for visually impaired visitors.
- Technological tools like audio-guides and 3D printing play a significant role in making exhibits more accessible.
- Inclusive design approaches are essential to consider diverse user requirements.

## Open Questions

- How can museums balance the cost of implementing advanced technologies with the need for inclusivity?
