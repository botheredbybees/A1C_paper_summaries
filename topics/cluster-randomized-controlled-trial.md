## Cluster-Randomized Controlled Trials

The cluster-randomized controlled trial (CRCT) is a methodological approach in health research where clusters of individuals rather than individuals are randomly assigned to intervention or control groups. This study on 'Social Dancing to Reduce Falls in Older Adults' illustrates the application of CRCT for evaluating fall prevention strategies like balance improvement exercises, suggesting that CRCTs can effectively assess interventions within communities. The use of this method allows for a more naturalistic and pragmatic approach to research by considering the social context and interdependencies among individuals within clusters.

## Key Findings

- Cluster-randomized controlled trials (CRCTs) are effective in evaluating community-based interventions like fall prevention strategies in older adults.
- Social dancing as an intervention can improve balance in older adults, reducing their risk of falls.
- The CRCT method accommodates the evaluation of complex interventions within real-world settings.

## Open Questions
