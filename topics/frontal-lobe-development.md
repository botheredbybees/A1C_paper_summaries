## Frontal Lobe Development

The topic of frontal lobe development in adolescents highlights significant changes such as synaptic pruning and neuroplasticity. Synaptic pruning is the process by which unused neural connections are eliminated, allowing for more efficient brain function. Neuroplasticity involves the brain's ability to adapt and change throughout life. These processes significantly impact cognitive functions like decision-making, problem-solving, and impulse control. Sources concur on these developmental changes but differ in their focus on how these changes manifest individually versus collectively.

## Key Findings

- Synaptic pruning and neuroplasticity are crucial for frontal lobe development during adolescence
- Frontal lobe development influences higher-order cognitive functions such as decision-making, problem-solving, and impulse control

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in brain plasticity impact the trajectory of frontal lobe development?
