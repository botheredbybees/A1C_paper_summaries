## Stress Management Interventions

The exploration of stress management interventions across multiple sources highlights diverse approaches that include music therapy, journaling, humor and resiliency, and creative arts therapies. Music-based interventions such as group music therapy and choir singing are shown to reduce physiological markers of stress like salivary cortisol and alpha-amylase in care home residents with dementia. Journaling offers benefits through emotional processing, mindfulness, and expressive writing. The influence of optimism and music training on music use indicates the importance of personal preference and cognitive stimulation for effective stress management. Resiliency, humor, and positive psychology are also proposed as critical components that enhance emotion regulation and coping strategies. Creative arts therapies encompass a broad spectrum of interventions under the PICOS principle, providing a systematic approach to stress prevention and management.

## Key Findings

- Music-based interventions reduce physiological markers of stress in care home residents with dementia
- Journaling benefits mental health through emotional processing and mindfulness practices
- Creative arts therapies offer systematic approaches for stress prevention and management

## Open Questions

- How do different types of creative arts therapies compare in effectiveness across various populations?
