## Pedagogies Of Visual Literacy

The synthesis integrates the concept of 'pedagogies of visual literacy' as a method to enhance creative recovery and improve community well-being. The Afloat Project exemplifies an intervention that uses visual literacies to foster resilience, creativity, and social cohesion among communities facing various challenges. Across different contexts, these pedagogical approaches encourage learners to engage with the world through images, fostering critical thinking about visual media in everyday life. However, there is a tension between the potential for broad application versus specific cultural relevance, suggesting that while such interventions can be transformative, their efficacy may vary based on local conditions and community dynamics.

## Key Findings

- Pedagogies of visual literacy are effective tools for fostering creative recovery within communities
- Visual literacies can enhance social cohesion and individual well-being by engaging learners critically with the visual world
- Community-based interventions that incorporate visual literacies often report positive impacts on resilience

## Open Questions

- How do pedagogies of visual literacy need to be adapted for different cultural contexts to ensure they are both relevant and effective?
