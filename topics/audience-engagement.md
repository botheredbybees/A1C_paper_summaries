## Audience Engagement

The topic of Audience Engagement integrates the concept across two distinct fields—Applied Theatre and Digital Storytelling. In Applied Theatre, as described in 'Transcript: Applied Theatre -- TDF's Theatre Dictionary -- Defining Theatre Terms,' audience engagement can take various forms, including interactive and participatory practices that extend traditional performance boundaries into community settings to address social issues such as cyberbullying. Meanwhile, the article '6 Questions Journalists Should Be Able to Answer Before Pitching a Story' underscores the importance of understanding one's target audience deeply in digital storytelling contexts, emphasizing thorough research and consideration for how content can resonate with specific demographics or communities. Both sources highlight the critical role of engagement strategies that involve direct interaction and relevance to the audience, suggesting a broader application of these principles beyond traditional media to include interactive theatre performances and digital narratives.

## Key Findings

- Audience engagement in both Applied Theatre and Digital Storytelling requires an understanding of community needs and issues.
- Interactive practices are central to enhancing audience involvement and can extend the reach of content beyond passive consumption.
- Effective engagement strategies necessitate deep research into the target demographic's interests, challenges, and digital behaviors.

## Open Questions
