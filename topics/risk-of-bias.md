## Risk Of Bias Assessment

The evaluation of the risk of bias is a critical component in systematic reviews and meta-analyses to ensure that study findings are reliable. The ROB-ME tool, designed specifically for assessing risks associated with missing evidence, complements other existing tools like ROBINS-I, which focuses on non-randomized studies. Both these tools emphasize the importance of methodological rigor and transparency in reporting risk assessments. Source 3 highlights that the rationale behind rating the risk of bias should be transparently documented to enhance the credibility and reproducibility of systematic reviews.

## Key Findings

- ROB-ME provides a specialized tool for assessing missing evidence in systematic reviews
- ROBINS-I evaluates risks specific to non-randomized studies, underlining methodological rigor
- Transparency in reporting risk-of-bias ratings is essential for credibility

## Open Questions
