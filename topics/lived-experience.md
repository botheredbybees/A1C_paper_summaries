## Lived Experience in Service Design

The concept of lived experience is explored through two perspectives that highlight its importance within the context of health and social services. Source 1 emphasizes resilience as a key element in narratives that emerge from personal challenges, focusing on visual storytelling as a means to convey these stories effectively. This source underscores how capturing one's journey can provide insight into the impact of interventions like social prescribing, thereby enhancing understanding among practitioners and the public. In contrast, Source 2 shifts focus towards the operationalization of lived experience in service design and partnerships between service providers and individuals with personal experiences of the services being designed or delivered. It stresses the value of an 'authentic voice'—a genuine and direct expression of one's experience—which is essential for creating more responsive and effective services.

## Key Findings

- Visual storytelling can effectively capture resilience in narratives of lived experience, aiding in public understanding and advocacy efforts
- The inclusion of authentic voices from individuals with lived experience enhances the design and delivery of health and social services
- Resilience is a core theme within narratives that emphasize personal journeys through challenges

## Open Questions

- How can visual storytelling be integrated more broadly into service design to reflect diverse experiences?
