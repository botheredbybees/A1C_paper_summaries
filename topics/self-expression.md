## Self-expression in Dementia and Autism

The sources collectively emphasize the therapeutic potential of self-expression through various art forms for individuals with dementia and autism. They highlight how engaging in creative activities, such as making music or creating visual arts, can foster neural plasticity, enhance emotional well-being, improve non-verbal communication skills, and promote cognitive empathy. These interventions provide a platform for individuals to express themselves beyond verbal limitations, thereby enhancing their quality of life and self-esteem. Each source underscores the importance of meaningful engagement in creative practices as an essential aspect of holistic care.

## Key Findings

- Self-expression through art forms such as music and visual arts can enhance emotional well-being and neural plasticity for individuals with dementia.
- Creative activities offer a non-verbal communication platform, enabling expression beyond language limitations, particularly beneficial in dementia and autism contexts.
- Engaging in expressive therapies can improve cognitive empathy and self-esteem in adolescents with autism.

## Open Questions

- How do the effects of creative interventions vary between different neurodegenerative conditions (e.g., Alzheimer's disease versus Parkinson’s disease) or developmental disorders?
