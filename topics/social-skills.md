## Social Skills Development in Therapeutic Settings

This synthesis examines the role of expressive therapies in enhancing social skills among children and adolescents with developmental challenges. Source 1 discusses an art therapy intervention using puppet making to promote emotional empathy, a critical component of social interaction, for an adolescent with autism. This approach leverages the Expressive Therapies Continuum (ETC) to foster both cognitive and affective empathy, which are essential skills for understanding others' emotions. Conversely, Source 2 explores the impact of therapeutic clowns on the playfulness of children with intellectual disabilities, highlighting how clown therapy can enhance social engagement through fun and interactive activities. Both studies underscore the efficacy of expressive therapies in promoting various aspects of social skills, including empathy and playful interaction.

## Key Findings

- Expressive therapies such as art therapy and clown therapy are effective interventions for enhancing social skills among children and adolescents with developmental challenges.
- The Expressive Therapies Continuum (ETC) provides a framework to support the development of emotional empathy, which is crucial for social interaction.
- Therapeutic clowns can significantly improve playfulness in children with intellectual disabilities, contributing positively to their social experiences.

## Open Questions

- How do different forms of expressive therapies compare in terms of their effectiveness and applicability across diverse populations?
