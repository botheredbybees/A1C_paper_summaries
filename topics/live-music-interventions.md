## Live Music Interventions

The integration of live music into therapeutic and wellbeing programs demonstrates significant potential for enhancing physical and mental health. Sources highlight the role of live music in promoting neuroplasticity, indicating that dynamic musical experiences can support brain function adaptability. Art-based interventions such as dance and music offer versatile platforms for personal expression and social interaction, crucial elements in rehabilitation and stress reduction. The presence of live music appears to elevate these benefits beyond what is achieved with recorded audio alone, due perhaps to the unique interactive nature and emotional engagement inherent in live performances.

## Key Findings

- Live music interventions are associated with enhanced neuroplasticity.
- Art-based therapeutic programs that include dance and music offer significant health benefits.
- Live musical experiences tend to provide greater psychological and physiological impact than recorded music.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms underlie the superior efficacy of live music compared to recorded music in therapeutic settings?
