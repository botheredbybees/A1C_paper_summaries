## Social Interaction in Aged Care

The sources collectively highlight the significant role of social interactions and creative activities in improving quality of life for older adults, particularly those with dementia. Improvisation, humor therapy, singing, reminiscence therapy, theater-making, and museum visits are presented as valuable interventions that enhance cognitive stimulation, emotional well-being, and social engagement. These activities not only offer health benefits but also promote cultural value, authenticity realization, and meaningful conversations. Despite the diverse array of approaches, a common thread is the importance of spontaneity, intrinsic value in cultural experiences, and tailored interaction strategies to address individual needs.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions such as improvisation, humor therapy, singing, and theater-making improve cognitive stimulation and quality of life for older adults.
- Recreational activities like reminiscence therapy and museum visits enhance emotional well-being by promoting meaningful conversations and cultural engagement.
- Tailored interaction strategies that incorporate sensory triggers and spontaneous activities are beneficial in dementia care.

## Open Questions

- How can the intrinsic value of different cultural experiences be measured and incorporated into standard healthcare practices for older adults?
