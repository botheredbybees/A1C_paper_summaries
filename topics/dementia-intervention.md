## Dance Movement Therapy for Dementia

Dance movement therapy has been explored as an intervention for individuals with dementia, aiming to provide both psychological and physiological benefits. Source 1 highlights the use of dance movement therapy alongside standard care in a randomized controlled trial setting, emphasizing its potential therapeutic effects on people suffering from dementia. Similarly, Source 2 presents another randomized controlled trial focusing on the psychophysiological impacts of dance movement therapy compared to physical exercise for older adults with mild dementia. Both sources emphasize nonpharmacological interventions as viable options for managing symptoms associated with dementia. However, while Source 1 does not specify the severity level of participants' dementia, Source 2 specifically targets individuals with mild dementia and measures outcomes such as diurnal cortisol slope, indicating varying degrees of therapeutic application.

## Key Findings

- Dance movement therapy can be a valuable nonpharmacological intervention for people with dementia.
- Randomized controlled trials demonstrate the efficacy of dance movement therapy in improving both psychological and physiological health markers among individuals with dementia.
- Diurnal cortisol slope is a useful indicator to measure the impact of psychophysiological interventions on older adults with mild dementia.
