## Photographic Composition Techniques

The sources explore various aspects of photographic composition techniques that enhance image quality and visual impact. Source 1 focuses on foundational concepts such as depth-of-field, rule-of-thirds, color-theory, and manual-exposure-modes, providing photographers with essential tools to control light, focus, framing, and aesthetics in their images. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the nuances of point of view composition, highlighting dynamic perspectives and unique vantage points that can dramatically alter an image's narrative and emotional impact. Both sources underscore the importance of understanding one's camera thoroughly to effectively employ these techniques.

## Key Findings

- Photographic compositions are significantly influenced by depth-of-field, rule-of-thirds, color-theory, and manual exposure modes.
- Effective point of view composition can alter an image's narrative through dynamic perspectives and unique vantage points.

## Open Questions

- How do photographers balance the application of traditional composition rules with innovative techniques for point of view?
