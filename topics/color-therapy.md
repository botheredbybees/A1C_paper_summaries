## Color Therapy

Color therapy, also known as chromotherapy, is an intervention aimed at improving various aspects of a person's health and well-being through the use of colors. Sources discuss its application in both individual patient recovery contexts and community-based projects. In these settings, color therapy is used to enhance emotional balance and physical healing. The integration of color into visual arts provides a medium for expressing feelings and promoting mental wellness. While sources emphasize the positive impact of color therapy on personal health outcomes and communal engagement, there are no specific comparative studies mentioned that quantify its effectiveness against other therapeutic approaches.

## Key Findings

- Color therapy is utilized in both individual recovery and community settings.
- Visual arts serve as a medium to enhance emotional well-being through color application.
- There is an emphasis on the positive health outcomes of engaging with colors in therapy.
