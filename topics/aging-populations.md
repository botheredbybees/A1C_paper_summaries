## Creative Arts Engagement for Aging Populations

The integration of creative arts into community settings emerges as a potent intervention strategy to address the healthcare needs and social dynamics of aging populations. Source 1 highlights the role of creative therapies within health policy frameworks, underscoring their importance in enhancing wellbeing and quality of life for older adults. Meanwhile, Source 2 elaborates on specific interventions like 'Arts on Prescription', which align with demographic shifts and aim to improve health outcomes through structured arts engagement programs. Both sources converge in emphasizing the therapeutic value of creative arts but diverge slightly in their focus—Source 1 provides a broader policy context while Source 2 zeroes in on practical implementations such as Arts on Prescription.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions, such as Arts on Prescription, are increasingly recognized for their contributions to health and wellbeing among aging populations.
- There is growing evidence supporting the integration of creative therapies into public health policy frameworks.
