## Wellbeing Through Creative Arts

Creative arts interventions have been studied for their impact on various aspects of wellbeing. Music interventions specifically are associated with improvements in health-related quality of life, as measured by mental and physical component scores from the SF-36 and SF-12 surveys. This aligns with broader creative arts initiatives such as 'arts-on-prescription' programs aimed at enhancing social cohesion and health outcomes within communities. However, while there is evidence supporting music's role in improving quality of life, the integration of these interventions into community-based practices has not been thoroughly explored.

## Key Findings

- Music interventions are associated with enhanced mental and physical components of health-related quality of life
- Creative arts initiatives like 'arts-on-prescription' aim to improve social cohesion and health outcomes in communities

## Open Questions

- How can music interventions be effectively integrated into community-based creative arts programs?
