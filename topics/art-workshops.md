## Art Workshops

The integration of themes across the provided sources highlights the multifaceted nature of art workshops as a therapeutic intervention. Sources underscore that art workshops can serve both as part of creative arts therapy and also function in non-therapeutic settings, focusing on the concept of 'flow' to enhance participant engagement and creativity. A central theme is the ability of these workshops to provide an immersive experience where participants are fully engaged with the task at hand, experiencing enjoyment and fulfillment. However, there is a noted tension between therapeutic applications that aim for specific psychological outcomes versus the more general benefits of creative expression in non-clinical settings.

## Key Findings

- Art workshops can facilitate the concept of 'flow', enhancing participant engagement and creativity.
- There are both therapeutic and non-therapeutic approaches to art workshops, each with distinct goals.
- The immersive nature of art workshops contributes significantly to their effectiveness across various applications.

## Open Questions

- How do different contexts (therapeutic vs. non-therapeutic) influence the outcomes and impact of art workshops?
