## Neurorehabilitation Interventions for Traumatic Brain Injury

The integration of various creative therapies in the rehabilitation process for individuals with traumatic brain injury (TBI) highlights a shift towards more holistic and personalized treatment approaches. Source 1 examines music-supported therapy, emphasizing its capacity to induce neuroplastic changes in the orbitofrontal cortex and promote cognitive rehabilitation and behavioral adjustments. Similarly, Source 2 underscores the role of art therapy as an integral component of comprehensive neurorehabilitation plans, focusing on enhancing neuroplasticity through self-exploration and holistic treatment strategies. Both sources advocate for interventions that go beyond traditional medical treatments to incorporate patient-centered approaches that address psychological and social aspects alongside physiological recovery.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapies such as music-supported therapy and art therapy can induce significant neuroplastic changes in TBI patients.
- Holistic treatment plans are essential in the rehabilitation of individuals with traumatic brain injury, addressing both cognitive and behavioral needs.
- Self-exploration through creative activities supports comprehensive neurorehabilitation by promoting psychological well-being.

## Open Questions

- How can different forms of creative therapy be integrated effectively into standard medical treatment protocols for TBI patients?
