## Recovery-Oriented Music Therapy

Music therapy is highlighted as a beneficial mental health intervention in schools with a strong emphasis on recovery orientation. It aims to promote mental health and support the therapeutic process by fostering resilience, hope, and empowerment among students. Across studies, music therapy is seen not only as an adjunctive treatment but also as a primary means of supporting individuals in their journey towards mental well-being. However, there remains a need for more research on how different methodologies within music therapy align with recovery principles and achieve long-term positive outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances mental health by fostering resilience, hope, and empowerment.
- It is recognized as an intervention that supports the therapeutic process in educational settings.
- Further study is required to understand its role in achieving sustained recovery-oriented goals.

## Open Questions

- How do different methodologies within music therapy align with recovery principles and contribute to long-term positive outcomes?
