## Disaster Recovery and Resilience

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights the role of various interventions in disaster recovery, focusing on resilience through art therapy (Source 1), ethical considerations in international practice (Source 2), children's outdoor play environments (Sources 3 and 4). The PR-6 model of resilience emphasizes arts-based healing approaches to promote psychological well-being. Ethical guidelines for art therapists are crucial when engaging with diverse populations post-disaster, underlining the importance of trauma theory awareness and cross-cultural communication. Children's outdoor spaces serve as therapeutic playgrounds where play can foster recovery, whether through constructed or found spaces that encourage physical activity and mental health benefits.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based healing approaches are effective in promoting resilience among disaster-affected communities.
- Ethical considerations are critical when implementing international art therapy practices post-disaster.
- Children's outdoor play environments, both constructed and natural, support mental and physical well-being after disasters.
