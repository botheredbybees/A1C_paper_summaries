## Thematic Analysis in Arts and Health Research

Thematic analysis is a crucial methodological approach in arts and health research, enabling the identification of patterns within qualitative data. Source 1 highlights how thematic analysis can be used to invite open responses from participants, focusing on descriptive statistics as part of its analytical framework. This aligns with Source 3's application of grounded theory and thematic analysis to understand the quality-of-life experiences in music therapy for palliative care patients. Both sources illustrate thematic analysis’s flexibility and depth when exploring subjective experiences within different arts interventions. Meanwhile, Source 2 introduces a broader cultural context, emphasizing the value of arts from indigenous perspectives and incorporating thematic analysis to explore these culturally rich datasets. This source underscores how thematic analysis can transcend healthcare settings to assess public and cultural values related to the arts. Together, these sources paint a picture of thematic analysis as a versatile tool for uncovering nuanced insights across various dimensions of arts and health research.

## Key Findings

- Thematic analysis is adaptable in both clinical and cultural contexts within arts and health research.
- It serves as an effective method to explore qualitative data from participant perspectives, enhancing understanding of quality-of-life issues.
- The integration of thematic analysis with other methodologies like grounded theory enriches the analytical depth of studies.
