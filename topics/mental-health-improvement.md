## Mental Health Improvement Through Creative Arts

The sources collectively explore various creative arts interventions aimed at enhancing mental health across different demographics. Source 1 examines the feasibility of online mindfulness-based dance/movement therapy for older adults experiencing cognitive decline, highlighting its potential in reducing loneliness and promoting physical activity as means to improve mental health. This aligns with the broader trend of leveraging artistic activities to foster well-being seen in sources 2, 3, and 4 which investigate photography/videography's role in personal growth (flow theory, self-determination theory), choir singing's positive effects on mental health despite potential vocal pathology issues, and creative arts projects tailored for gender diverse youth focusing on building supportive communities and enhancing self-esteem. Across these interventions, a consistent theme is the therapeutic value of engaging with art forms that encourage emotional expression and connection, even when barriers such as aging or specific developmental needs exist.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies can significantly improve mental health across diverse populations.
- Online delivery models for creative arts therapies are feasible and beneficial during public health crises like the pandemic.
- Interventions that address unique challenges (e.g., vocal strain from singing) while promoting mental health show promising results.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts interventions be tailored to maximize their benefits for individuals with varying levels of physical ability and accessibility needs?
