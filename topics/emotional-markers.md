## Emotional Markers in Neuroimaging

The exploration of emotional markers through neuroimaging techniques, such as the use of Guided Imagery and Music (GIM) with dual-EEG technology, provides insight into how emotions are processed and shared between individuals. The study highlights the potential for GIM to reveal neural dynamics associated with emotion processing during guided imagery sessions. However, the integration of multiple participants' brain activities using hyperscanning raises questions about the reliability and consistency of emotional markers across different individuals.

## Key Findings

- Guided Imagery and Music (GIM) can be used as a method to explore neural dynamics in emotion processing
- Dual-EEG technology allows for the identification of emotional markers during GIM sessions
- Hyperscanning techniques reveal shared neural processes between individuals engaged in GIM

## Open Questions

- What are the limitations and challenges associated with using hyperscanning to identify consistent emotional markers across different participants?
