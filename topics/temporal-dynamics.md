## Temporal Dynamics in Emotional Responses and Brain Function

This topic explores the methodological approaches to understanding temporal dynamics within both emotional responses to music and brain function. Source 1 examines the multidimensional scaling of emotional responses over time when exposed to music, indicating that there are distinct phases in how emotions evolve in response to auditory stimuli. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the evaluation of temporal synchrony between different brain regions in individuals with autism compared to neurotypical individuals. This study highlights variations in functional connectivity and cognitive processing speed among these groups, emphasizing differences in neural network dynamics that occur over time. While both sources focus on temporal dimensions, they approach the subject from different angles: emotional response trajectories versus neurological connectivity changes.

## Key Findings

- Temporal synchrony between brain regions varies significantly between individuals with autism and neurotypical controls.
- Multidimensional scaling can be used to analyze how emotions change in response to music over time.

## Open Questions

- How do the temporal patterns of emotional responses to music relate to underlying neurological processes, particularly in the context of cognitive processing speed?
