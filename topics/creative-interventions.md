## Creative Interventions in Dementia Care

The integration of creative interventions in dementia care is highlighted across multiple sources. These interventions, such as arts-based activities and music therapy, aim to enhance person-centered care by focusing on the emotional and mental well-being of individuals with dementia. Sources indicate that artistic expression and therapeutic engagement through mediums like music or puppet-assisted play can significantly contribute to re-engaging patients emotionally and improving their quality of life. Additionally, there is a growing recognition for incorporating non-medical approaches into holistic healthcare models, emphasizing creative health as an essential component in mental well-being across the lifespan. However, while the financial benefits of such interventions are acknowledged, questions remain regarding how these practices can be more effectively integrated within broader healthcare sectors.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions improve emotional engagement and mental well-being among dementia patients.
- Arts-based activities support person-centered care in dementia settings.
- Non-medical approaches contribute to holistic healthcare models.

## Open Questions

- How can creative health practices be better integrated into mainstream healthcare systems?
