## Homo Ludens in Dementia Care

The concept of 'homo ludens'—the notion that humans are fundamentally playful beings—is explored within the context of dementia care, focusing on interventions such as the TimeSlips program and the Alzheimer's Poetry Project. These initiatives aim to engage individuals with dementia in creative activities, suggesting a shift from traditional approaches emphasizing deficits towards ones celebrating humanity through playfulness. The humanities-based inquiry reveals that both programs foster social interaction, emotional expression, and cognitive engagement among participants, contributing positively to their overall well-being. However, the effectiveness of these playful interventions can vary based on individual preferences and the specific cultural context in which they are implemented.

## Key Findings

- Playful activities like TimeSlips and the Alzheimer's Poetry Project enhance social interaction and emotional expression for individuals with dementia.
- The concept of 'homo ludens' supports a paradigm shift from focusing on deficits to celebrating human potential through playfulness in dementia care.
- Creative interventions tailored to individual preferences can improve quality of life among people living with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and personal backgrounds influence the effectiveness and acceptance of playful interventions such as TimeSlips or the Alzheimer's Poetry Project?
