## Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy

Intensive child-centered play therapy (ICPT) emerges as a promising intervention for children on the autism spectrum. This approach emphasizes tailored therapeutic interactions focused on the unique needs and strengths of each child, aiming to improve social interaction skills, emotional regulation, and overall quality of life. The pilot study highlights ICPT's potential in addressing behavioral challenges common among autistic children through play-based activities. However, while initial findings suggest positive outcomes, there is a need for larger-scale studies to confirm efficacy across diverse populations and settings.

## Key Findings

- ICPT focuses on individualized treatment plans tailored to each child.
- Pilot study results indicate improved social interaction skills in children with autism.
- Behavioral interventions using play therapy are seen as beneficial in addressing common challenges faced by autistic children.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of ICPT on children's developmental trajectories?
