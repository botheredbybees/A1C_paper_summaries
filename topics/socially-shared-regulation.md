## Socially-Shared Regulation

The concept of socially-shared regulation involves the collective management and adjustment of individual behaviors in a social context. This theme is prominently featured in 'A New Context Affording for Regulation: The Case of Musical Play,' which highlights how regulatory behaviors, cognitive monitoring, and emotionally-motivational monitoring operate within a group setting to achieve shared goals. Sources converge on the notion that socially-shared regulation facilitates cooperative learning and development by enabling individuals to monitor each other's actions and adjust their own accordingly.

## Key Findings

- Regulatory behaviors are enhanced in social contexts.
- Cognitive monitoring plays a critical role in socially-shared regulation.
- Emotionally-motivational monitoring is integral for effective group functioning.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences impact the effectiveness of socially-shared regulation?
