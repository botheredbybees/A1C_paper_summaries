## Articulatory Tempo in Singing and Aphasia

Research on singing and aphasia highlights the importance of articulatory tempo as a critical component in speech rehabilitation. Sources underscore how varying tempos can engage different neural networks, influencing the efficacy of interventions like Melodic Intonation Therapy (MIT). Studies reveal that slower tempos may enhance motor planning and sequencing abilities crucial for patients with aphasia, but there is also evidence suggesting that optimal therapeutic outcomes might be achieved through a balance in tempo modulation. The interplay between articulatory tempo and neural network engagement remains an area of active investigation, with implications for tailoring interventions to individual patient needs.

## Key Findings

- Articulatory tempo significantly influences the effectiveness of singing-based therapies for aphasia.
- Melodic Intonation Therapy benefits from adjustments in tempo to optimize neural network activation.
- Slower tempos may improve motor planning and sequencing abilities, crucial elements for speech recovery.

## Open Questions

- What is the optimal range of articulatory tempo that maximizes therapeutic outcomes for aphasia patients?
