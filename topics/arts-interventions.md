## Arts Interventions in Healthcare

The exploration of arts interventions as a means to improve health and wellbeing is multifaceted. Sources highlight the use of creative therapies such as visual art activities (Source 3) and drama (Source 5) as effective methods for enhancing mental health, particularly in elderly populations with dementia or younger individuals dealing with mental health challenges. These interventions not only focus on individual improvement but also foster social connections and community engagement through critical awareness-building exercises that promote trust among peers (Source 5). Furthermore, systematic reviews of economic evaluations suggest cost-effectiveness as a compelling rationale for the integration of arts therapies in healthcare settings (Source 2), while tools like THRivE provide essential methods to assess mental health outcomes rigorously (Source 4). However, despite these promising findings, challenges remain in standardizing core outcome measures across different cultural contexts and populations, underscoring the need for nuanced approaches that accommodate local practices.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapies improve quality of life for individuals with dementia and mental health issues.
- Arts interventions can foster social connections and community engagement among participants.
- Systematic reviews indicate cost-effectiveness as a strong argument for integrating arts-based strategies into healthcare.

## Open Questions

- How to standardize core outcome measures across diverse cultural contexts in arts-based interventions?
