## Process Evaluations

The provided sources emphasize the importance of process evaluations in understanding the implementation and effectiveness of interventions. Process evaluations focus on examining how a program is delivered and its mechanisms of impact. They are essential for providing detailed insights into the dynamics between intervention design, delivery, and outcomes. The chapter from 'Arts in Health' highlights that while outcome research focuses on what happens as a result of an intervention, process evaluation delves deeper into how the intervention unfolds and the underlying factors influencing its success or failure. Ethnographic studies are mentioned as valuable tools for capturing nuanced insights into the implementation process, which can be challenging to obtain through quantitative methods alone.

## Key Findings

- Process evaluations complement outcome research by focusing on the 'how' rather than just the 'what'.
- Ethnographic methods provide rich qualitative data necessary for understanding the complexities of intervention delivery and context.
