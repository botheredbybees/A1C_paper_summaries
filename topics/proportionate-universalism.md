## Proportionate Universalism

The concept of proportionate universalism emerges as a pivotal theory in addressing health inequalities through targeted yet universally accessible interventions. Originating from the social determinants of health framework, it emphasizes that while certain populations may require more focused attention due to disproportionate burdens of ill health, all individuals should benefit from initiatives aimed at improving daily living conditions. This nuanced approach seeks to balance equity and universality, ensuring that resources are distributed according to need without excluding anyone from potential benefits. The life course perspective complements this theory by highlighting the importance of addressing inequalities throughout an individual's lifetime, reinforcing the necessity for comprehensive, age-appropriate interventions.

## Key Findings

- Proportionate universalism aims to address health disparities through targeted yet universally accessible strategies.
- The approach seeks balance between equity and universality in resource distribution.
- A life course perspective underscores the importance of addressing inequalities at various stages of an individual's life.
