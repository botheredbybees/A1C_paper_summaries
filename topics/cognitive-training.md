## Cognitive Training

The concept of cognitive training is a key intervention category within the broader context of person-centered dementia care. Across multiple studies reviewed in this systematic review, cognitive training interventions are highlighted as beneficial for enhancing cognitive function and mental health among individuals with dementia. These interventions often focus on maintaining or improving cognitive abilities through structured exercises that target specific cognitive domains such as memory, attention, and problem-solving skills. The effectiveness of these interventions is bolstered by their person-centered approach, emphasizing individual needs and preferences to tailor training sessions appropriately. However, the review also underscores challenges in standardizing intervention protocols due to variability in patient conditions and the dynamic nature of dementia progression.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive training improves cognitive functions in individuals with dementia when tailored to individual needs.
- Person-centered approaches enhance the effectiveness of cognitive training interventions.
- Standardization of cognitive training protocols is challenging but necessary for consistent outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can cognitive training be effectively personalized to address varying degrees and stages of dementia?
