## Stakeholder Engagement

Both sources emphasize the importance of stakeholder engagement in enhancing research and practice methodologies. Source 1 outlines a step-by-step approach to the research process that values scientific rigor within creativity, advocating for robust inclusion of stakeholders from the inception of projects to ensure relevance and applicability. Similarly, Source 2 delves into co-production as an integral part of evaluation practices in arts, health, and well-being contexts, highlighting the challenges and opportunities inherent in involving multiple stakeholders throughout processes aimed at social impact and innovation. Both sources underscore the necessity of engaging diverse stakeholder groups for more credible and creative outcomes in research and practice.

## Key Findings

- Stakeholder engagement is crucial for enhancing scientific rigor and creativity in research methodology.
- Co-production methods offer a robust framework for evaluating arts, health, and well-being initiatives by involving multiple stakeholders throughout the process.
- Engaging diverse stakeholders from early stages of project development can lead to more relevant and impactful results.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers effectively manage the challenges associated with co-production in stakeholder engagement?
