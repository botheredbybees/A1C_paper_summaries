## Arts in Dementia Care

The integration of arts-based therapies in dementia care has been advocated as a beneficial intervention that can enhance the quality of life for individuals living with dementia. Lecture notes from FXA100 emphasize the importance of evidence-based practice when implementing arts-based interventions, underscoring the need for rigorous evaluation and research to substantiate their efficacy and appropriateness. Similarly, week 4 activities in the same course highlight specific applications such as music therapy and singing interventions within the context of dementia discourse. These activities suggest that engaging with music and vocal expression can be particularly impactful, offering a non-pharmacological approach to managing symptoms while fostering social interaction and emotional well-being among patients.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based therapies are seen as beneficial interventions for individuals living with dementia.
- Music therapy and singing interventions are specifically highlighted as effective within the broader context of arts in dementia care.
- There is an emphasis on evidence-based practice when implementing such interventions to ensure their efficacy and appropriateness.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of arts-based therapies be optimized for diverse populations within dementia care, considering individual preferences and cultural backgrounds?
