## Randomized Controlled Trials

This synthesis integrates insights from a randomized controlled trial examining the impact of visual art training on dementia patients and a methodological guide to assessing risk of bias in randomized trials. The art intervention study highlights improvements in delayed recall and working memory, demonstrating the potential for such interventions within clinical settings. Meanwhile, the Cochrane Collaboration's tool underscores the importance of rigorous criteria for evaluating the reliability and validity of randomized controlled trial outcomes. Together, these sources emphasize both the therapeutic efficacy of specific interventions and the methodological rigor required to establish their effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Visual art training can improve cognitive functions such as delayed recall and working memory in dementia patients.
- The Cochrane Collaboration's risk-of-bias tool is crucial for assessing the reliability of randomized controlled trials.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers ensure that interventions like visual art training are effectively evaluated in terms of their long-term cognitive benefits?
