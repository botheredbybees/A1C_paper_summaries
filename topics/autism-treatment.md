## Play Therapy in Autism Treatment

The application of play therapy in treating autism spectrum disorder (ASD) encompasses a variety of approaches aimed at enhancing emotional regulation and adaptive behavior. Sources highlight the efficacy of Child-Centred Play Therapy (CCPT) and Rhythmic Relating in improving emotion regulation, alongside the AutPlay® framework which emphasizes empathetic engagement and reflective practices. These interventions not only foster communication skills but also address sensory integration needs. However, while CCPT and rhythmic relating focus on emotional aspects through play, the AutPlay® method integrates broader therapeutic frameworks including cognitive-behavioral techniques to empower neurodivergent children. Challenges include understanding how these diverse strategies intersect within a unified treatment plan for ASD.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centred Play Therapy (CCPT) and rhythmic relating are effective in enhancing emotion regulation among autistic children.
- The AutPlay® framework, emphasizing empathy-in-therapy, is crucial for neurodivergent children's growth and empowerment.
- Play therapy methods address multiple facets of autism treatment, including sensory integration needs.

## Open Questions

- How can the various play therapy approaches be effectively integrated into a comprehensive treatment plan for autism?
