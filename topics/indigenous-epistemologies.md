## Indigenous Epistemologies in Health

The concept of Indigenous epistemologies is central to understanding and reclaiming health within Native American communities. Sources emphasize the importance of moving beyond conventional social determinants of health (SDH) frameworks, which often fail to incorporate the unique cultural and historical contexts faced by indigenous populations. These contexts include systemic inequalities rooted in colonialism and a loss of traditional lands and practices that have profoundly affected health outcomes. Central to these discussions is the idea of community sovereignty, suggesting that genuine progress requires empowering communities with control over their own health initiatives, which can better reflect Indigenous epistemologies than top-down approaches from non-indigenous systems.

## Key Findings

- Indigenous epistemologies are crucial for understanding and improving health outcomes in Native American communities.
- Traditional social determinants of health frameworks inadequately address the unique challenges faced by indigenous populations due to historical and cultural factors.
- Community sovereignty is essential for effective health initiatives within indigenous communities.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare systems integrate Indigenous epistemologies into practice while respecting and empowering community sovereignty?
