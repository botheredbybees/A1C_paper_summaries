## Social Personae in Dementia Care

The concept of 'social personae' in dementia care highlights the importance of understanding and addressing how individuals with dementia are perceived and treated by others, including healthcare providers. The notion of social personae emphasizes that people maintain a sense of identity through their interactions with society. Diagnostic overshadowing, where symptoms due to conditions other than dementia are overlooked because they coexist with it, can undermine this social persona. Sources discuss the impact of stereotypic views on individuals and emphasize the need for personalized care that acknowledges and respects each person's unique identity beyond their diagnosis. However, there is a tension between maintaining an individual’s dignity and addressing the challenges posed by dementia.

## Key Findings

- Social personae are crucial in preserving an individual's sense of self in dementia care.
- Diagnostic overshadowing can negatively impact patient care and personal identity.
- Stereotypic views hinder effective personalized care for individuals with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare providers balance addressing medical symptoms without undermining a patient’s social persona?
