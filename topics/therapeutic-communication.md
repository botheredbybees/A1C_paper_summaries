## Therapeutic Communication

The utilization of non-traditional methods for therapeutic communication highlights innovative approaches to engage and support patients. In pediatric settings, nurses utilize puppets within the Pup-Ed framework, emphasizing the reduction of anxiety in children through playful interaction (Source 1). This approach reflects a broader trend towards incorporating creative elements into patient care to enhance emotional well-being. Meanwhile, medical clowns focus on leveraging their unique communication skills and predefined therapeutic goals to create positive emotional experiences for patients (Source 2). Both interventions underscore the importance of adaptability in communication strategies to cater to diverse needs within healthcare environments.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions like puppets and clowning can significantly reduce patient anxiety, particularly in pediatric settings.
- Therapeutic goals are effectively achieved through tailored communication skills that go beyond traditional medical interactions.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultural contexts influence the acceptance and effectiveness of creative therapeutic communication strategies?
