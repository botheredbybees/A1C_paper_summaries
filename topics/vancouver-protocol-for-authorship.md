## Vancouver Protocol For Authorship

The Vancouver Protocol for authorship provides a framework for the contribution and recognition of contributors in academic research. It emphasizes the importance of transparency and accountability regarding contributions to conception, design, analysis, interpretation, and writing within scientific literature. Sources highlight the ethical considerations inherent in this protocol, such as ensuring anonymity of participants and accurately reporting data. However, sources may vary on specific implementation details, leading to potential discrepancies in how authorship roles are understood and applied across different research contexts.

## Key Findings

- The Vancouver Protocol for authorship is a widely recognized framework for attributing contributions in academic papers.
- Ethical considerations such as participant anonymity and accurate data reporting are integral components of the protocol.
- Discrepancies in implementation may exist, leading to potential gaps in consistency across different research fields.

## Open Questions

- How can discrepancies in interpretation and application of the Vancouver Protocol be minimized to ensure uniform ethical standards?
