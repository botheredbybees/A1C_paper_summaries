## Patient-Centered Care

The concept of Patient-Centered Care (PCC) emphasizes the importance of considering individual patient needs and preferences in healthcare delivery. Across sources, PCC is recognized as a critical approach to enhancing quality improvement measures by aligning with patients' values and health goals. The integration of tools like PESTLE analysis aids in systematically evaluating external factors that influence care, thereby supporting the development of interventions that are tailored to meet patient needs effectively. However, while there is agreement on the benefits of PCC, tensions exist regarding its implementation challenges and varying interpretations of what constitutes truly 'patient-centered' care.

## Key Findings

- Patient-Centered Care emphasizes individual needs and preferences in healthcare delivery.
- Quality improvement measures are enhanced through alignment with patient values and health goals.
- Tools such as PESTLE analysis support the development of tailored interventions.
