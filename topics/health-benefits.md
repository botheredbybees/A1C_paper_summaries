## Health Benefits of Arts Participation

This synthesis explores the health benefits derived from arts participation, specifically through singing and theater-making for older adults. Source 1 presents a qualitative study that examines the health and well-being impacts of group singing on individuals with dementia and their caregivers. The findings suggest that regular singing sessions contribute significantly to improved quality of life, emotional support, social interaction, and cognitive stimulation. Meanwhile, Source 2 offers a review highlighting the intrinsic cultural value of theater-making for older adults, emphasizing its role in enhancing mental health, reducing isolation, and fostering community engagement. Both sources underscore the positive impacts of arts-based interventions on participants' physical and psychological well-being, suggesting that these activities play an essential role in supporting aging populations.

## Key Findings

- Group singing significantly enhances quality of life for individuals with dementia and their caregivers through emotional support and social interaction.
- Theater-making experiences are culturally valuable and contribute to mental health improvement and reduced isolation among older adults.
- Arts-based interventions like group singing and theater-making offer substantial benefits to the physical and psychological well-being of aging populations.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of sustained participation in arts activities, such as singing or theater, on the health outcomes for individuals with dementia?
