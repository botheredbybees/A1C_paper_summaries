## Therapeutic Photography

Therapeutic photography emerges as an intervention aimed at enhancing individuals' mental health through the act of taking and reflecting upon photographs. This practice is rooted in self-esteem enhancement, resilience building, and providing a therapeutic framework for personal expression and reflection. Sources concur on its potential to improve psychological well-being by encouraging participants to engage with their own narratives visually, fostering deeper understanding and acceptance of oneself. However, there is variability in how therapeutic photography is implemented across different populations, leading to some tension regarding the extent of its effectiveness and applicability.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography enhances self-esteem.
- It aids in building resilience among participants.
- Facilitates personal expression and reflection.

## Open Questions

- How does variability in implementation impact therapeutic outcomes across different populations?
