## Photography as a Tool for Mental Wellbeing

Both sources explore photography's role in enhancing mental health through mindfulness and neural plasticity. Source 1 introduces 'Mindful Photography' emphasizing the concept of 'Attentional Aperture', suggesting that mindful engagement with photography can improve one's ability to focus on the present moment and enhance appreciation of daily life. This ties into therapeutic photography, a method highlighted by Source 2 which uses photography as an expressive tool for healing. Both sources acknowledge that regular practice can lead to neural plastic changes, indicating potential long-term benefits in mental wellbeing. However, they also touch upon negative aspects like nomophobia (fear or worry about being without one's mobile phone), suggesting the need for balanced use of photographic activities.

## Key Findings

- Photography can serve as a therapeutic tool to enhance mental health through mindfulness.
- Regular practice of photography can lead to changes in neural plasticity, indicating potential long-term benefits.
- Therapeutic photography allows individuals to express themselves and process emotions visually.

## Open Questions

- How does the balanced use of photographic activities mitigate negative impacts like nomophobia?
