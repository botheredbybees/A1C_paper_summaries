## Guided Imagery and Music (GIM)

The intervention known as Guided Imagery and Music (GIM) utilizes music to guide an individual's mental imagery for therapeutic purposes. Studies using dual-EEG technology have provided insights into the neural dynamics of emotion processing during GIM sessions, identifying specific emotional markers that correlate with different phases of the therapy. These findings suggest that GIM can effectively modulate brain activity associated with emotional regulation and provide a unique perspective on how music influences psychological processes.

## Key Findings

- Guided Imagery and Music (GIM) serves as an effective therapeutic intervention for emotion processing.
- Dual-EEG technology has revealed specific neural markers of emotion during GIM sessions, offering insights into the neurological basis of emotional regulation.
- Music plays a critical role in guiding mental imagery and affecting brain activity associated with emotional states.
