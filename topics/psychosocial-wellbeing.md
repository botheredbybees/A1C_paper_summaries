## Arts on Prescription for Psychosocial Wellbeing

The systematic review and meta-analysis examining the impact of Arts on Prescription (AoP) on individual health and well-being highlights a positive association between AoP interventions and improved psychosocial outcomes. The literature converges on the therapeutic potential of arts-based activities in enhancing mental health, reducing feelings of isolation, and fostering community engagement. However, variations in study design, intervention protocols, and participant characteristics introduce complexities in interpreting these results uniformly. While there is consensus on the benefits of AoP for psychological resilience and social connectedness, nuances arise regarding its effectiveness across diverse demographics and specific psychosocial conditions.

## Key Findings

- Arts on Prescription (AoP) interventions contribute positively to individuals' mental health and well-being.
- AoP has demonstrated efficacy in reducing isolation and enhancing community engagement among participants.
- The therapeutic benefits of AoP vary based on the specific nature of the arts activity and participant demographics.

## Open Questions

- How do variations in intervention design, such as different types of artistic activities or duration of programs, impact the effectiveness of AoP for psychosocial wellbeing?
