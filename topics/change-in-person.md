## Change in Person with Dementia

The analysis of Helen Morse's perspective on dementia through drama highlights the concept of 'change in person' as a multifaceted experience, influenced by resilience and humor. Through multidisciplinary collaboration, particularly with dramatists, new insights into how individuals cope with changes due to dementia are revealed. This approach not only sheds light on the emotional and psychological transformation undergone but also emphasizes the importance of creative methods for understanding and addressing such changes.

## Key Findings

- The experience of 'change in person' due to dementia is profoundly influenced by personal resilience and humor.
- Multidisciplinary collaboration, including drama, offers unique perspectives on how individuals cope with dementia-related changes.

## Open Questions

- How can creative methodologies like drama be more widely integrated into therapeutic practices for those experiencing 'change in person' due to dementia?
