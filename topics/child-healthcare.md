## Interactive Puppet Simulation in Child Healthcare

The literature review and empirical study explore the utility of interactive puppet simulation as an intervention for improving communication and reducing anxiety in pediatric healthcare settings. Both sources highlight the role of puppets as therapeutic tools that serve to facilitate three-way communication between children, parents, and healthcare professionals. The Pup-Ed framework is introduced as a structured approach to incorporate expressive therapeutics through puppet use, aiming to enhance patient engagement and education. However, while the review underscores the broad applicability and benefits of interactive puppet simulation in nursing education and child healthcare contexts, the empirical study focuses on specific nurse experiences within an acute pediatric unit, emphasizing practical challenges such as logistical constraints and varying levels of acceptance among staff.

## Key Findings

- Interactive puppets are effective tools for enhancing communication between children and healthcare providers
- The Pup-Ed framework supports structured use of puppet therapy in healthcare settings
- Puppet-based interventions can reduce child anxiety during medical procedures

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term outcomes and benefits of incorporating interactive puppet simulation in routine pediatric care?
