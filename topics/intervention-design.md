## Intervention Design in Health Care

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights a comprehensive approach to intervention design in healthcare. Source 1 emphasizes planning interventions through PESTLE analysis and quality improvement measures, with an explicit focus on patient-centered care. This foundational stage sets the context for understanding external factors (PESTLE) impacting intervention success and ensures that any new initiative is embedded within the existing quality framework of healthcare systems. Source 2 shifts focus to implementation and evaluation phases, introducing methodologies such as pilot projects and iterative refinement. These processes allow for testing interventions on a small scale before full-scale deployment, providing opportunities for continuous improvement based on formative evaluations. Meanwhile, Source 3 incorporates design thinking and problem tree analysis, offering systematic tools for developing research-related projects and understanding complex healthcare issues from multiple angles. Systems thinking is highlighted as essential to address the interconnected nature of health problems.

## Key Findings

- Intervention design requires thorough planning that considers external factors (PESTLE) and internal quality measures.
- Implementation of interventions should begin with pilot projects followed by iterative refinement based on formative evaluations.
- Design thinking, problem tree analysis, and systems thinking are critical for comprehensive intervention development.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of PESTLE analysis in planning be optimized to better align with design thinking and systems approaches?
