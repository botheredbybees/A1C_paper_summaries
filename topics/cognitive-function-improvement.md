## Cognitive Function Improvement

The integration of various arts-based interventions for enhancing cognitive function in elderly populations is gaining recognition. Source 1 highlights the efficacy of dramatherapy and dance therapy in dementia care, emphasizing emotional expression as a crucial element alongside person-centered care strategies. These therapeutic methods support not only emotional health but also contribute to cognitive function improvement through structured yet flexible activities that engage participants on multiple levels. In contrast, Source 2 introduces 'El Sistema' as an approach rooted in music education and its potential for building resilience in individuals. This program indirectly supports cognitive function enhancement by fostering engagement with creative arts, much like the methodologies discussed in Source 1 but focusing more explicitly on musical experiences. Both sources agree that engaging with art forms can offer substantial benefits to cognitive health and overall well-being of elderly people or those suffering from dementia.

## Key Findings

- Dramatherapy and dance therapy enhance emotional expression, contributing to cognitive function improvement in individuals with dementia.
- Music-based interventions like 'El Sistema' promote resilience and indirectly support cognitive enhancement through creative engagement.
- Person-centered care is essential for tailoring therapeutic approaches to individual needs, thereby optimizing the effectiveness of arts-based interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can the efficacy of dramatherapy and dance therapy be measured in comparison with music-based programs like 'El Sistema'?
