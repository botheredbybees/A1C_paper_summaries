## Maintaining Selfhood in Dementia Care

The sources emphasize the importance of person-centered care as an intervention to maintain the selfhood of individuals with dementia. Person-centered care focuses on understanding and respecting each individual's unique history, preferences, and needs, even in the face of neurological impairments. This approach seeks to foster high-quality interpersonal relationships that support the dignity and identity of people living with dementia. While all sources highlight the critical role of person-centered care, they also underscore the challenge of sustaining this type of care due to systemic barriers such as lack of training for caregivers and limited resources.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered care is essential in maintaining the selfhood of individuals with dementia.
- High-quality interpersonal relationships are a cornerstone of effective person-centered care.
- Neurological impairment and psychosocial factors complicate but do not negate the possibility of providing person-centered care.

## Open Questions
