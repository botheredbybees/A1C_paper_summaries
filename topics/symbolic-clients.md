## Symbolic Clients in Therapy

The concept of 'symbolic clients' emerges as a therapeutic tool aimed at externalizing internal struggles and emotions, thereby facilitating discussion and problem-solving in a less personal context. Sources indicate that symbolic clients can provide individuals with the psychological distance needed to address mental health issues more objectively. This method is particularly relevant within creative arts therapies and play therapy contexts where externalization of emotional problems aids in enhancing mental wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Symbolic clients offer a non-personal medium for discussing internal emotions.
- The use of symbolic clients is an effective intervention in creative arts and play therapies.
- Externalizing emotions through symbolic representation helps individuals tackle personal issues more objectively.

## Open Questions

- How does the efficacy of using symbolic clients vary across different cultural contexts?
