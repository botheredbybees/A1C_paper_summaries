## Patient Wellbeing Interventions

The provided sources focus on two distinct yet complementary interventions designed to improve patient wellbeing. Heritage-based health and well-being interventions are highlighted in Source 1 as a means of promoting mental health benefits, fostering social interaction, and motivating individual experiences. These interventions draw upon the cultural heritage of participants to enhance their psychological and social wellness. In contrast, Source 2 explores medical clowning as an intervention that utilizes communication skills to achieve therapeutic goals. Medical clowns use humor and playfulness to address patients' emotional needs and facilitate better doctor-patient relationships. While both interventions aim to improve patient wellbeing through different approaches—one culturally enriching and the other entertainment-driven—the synthesis reveals a common theme of using external stimuli (heritage, clowns) to positively influence mental health and social engagement.

## Key Findings

- Heritage-based activities can provide significant mental health benefits by promoting social interaction and personal motivation among participants.
- Medical clowning employs communication skills to achieve therapeutic goals, enhancing patient care through humor and playfulness.
- Both heritage-focused interventions and medical clowning aim to improve patients' psychological and social wellbeing.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of using external stimuli such as heritage or medical clowns in healthcare settings on patient outcomes?
