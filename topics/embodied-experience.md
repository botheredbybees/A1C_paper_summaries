## Embodied Experience In Dementia

This synthesis explores the integration of embodied music-making experiences for individuals with dementia. Source 1 examines the sensory and emotional engagement during improvised musical interactions, highlighting how such activities enhance personal identity through an 'in-the-moment' experience that supports a sense of agency. In contrast, Source 2 broadens this perspective by proposing a relational citizenship model to understand musicality in dementia as both individual and interdependent. It underscores the social dimensions of music-making, emphasizing its role beyond personal enhancement towards fostering relationships and community involvement. Both sources converge on the importance of embodiment but diverge slightly in their focus: one on immediate psychological benefits, the other on broader socio-relational impacts.

## Key Findings

- Embodied musical experiences can enhance personal identity for individuals with dementia by providing a sense of agency and momentary self-continuity through sensory engagement.
- Music-making interventions should be designed to promote relational citizenship, recognizing the interdependence between individuals with dementia and their social environment.

## Open Questions

- How do we balance individual psychological benefits (e.g., personal identity enhancement) with socio-relational objectives in music therapy for people with dementia?
