## Self-Rated Health Among Male International Migrants

The study on the health and well-being of male international migrants in Bangladesh underscores several key issues affecting self-rated health. The research highlights significant differences between migrant populations and non-migrant groups, particularly concerning conditions related to migration such as stress, poor living conditions, and economic instability. These factors are closely linked with higher rates of depressive symptoms among migrants compared to their non-migrating counterparts. Additionally, the study identifies a notable prevalence of overweight-obesity risk and hypertension in the migrant population, which could be exacerbated by lifestyle changes associated with migration. Overall, the findings suggest that self-rated health is not only influenced by physical health conditions but also by psychosocial factors directly linked to the experience of migration.

## Key Findings

- Migrant populations experience higher rates of depressive symptoms compared to non-migrants due to stress and poor living conditions related to migration.
- Male international migrants have a greater risk of overweight-obesity and hypertension than non-migrants.
- Self-rated health is influenced by both physical health status and psychosocial factors associated with migration.
