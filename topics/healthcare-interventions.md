## Arts-Based Healthcare Interventions

The evaluation and research of arts-based healthcare interventions highlight the importance of methodological rigor in assessing complex interventions. Source 1 emphasizes randomized controlled trials as a standard for establishing evidence-based claims, while also recognizing the challenges posed by evaluating multifaceted therapies such as music therapy or dance programs. These interventions often aim to improve mental health through social interaction and individual motivational engagement (Source 2). Source 3 provides case studies that illustrate specific therapeutic benefits of rhythmic auditory stimulation in enhancing motor skills and overall wellbeing among participants. Despite these advances, there remains a need for nuanced approaches beyond RCTs, capable of capturing the diverse impacts arts-based therapies have on health and well-being.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions such as music therapy show potential for improving mental health through social interaction and motivation.
- Rhythmic auditory stimulation is a specific therapeutic application within music therapy that enhances motor skills in participants.
- Methodological challenges exist when evaluating complex interventions due to their multifaceted nature.

## Open Questions

- How can research methodologies be adapted to better capture the nuanced impacts of arts-based healthcare interventions beyond traditional RCT standards?
