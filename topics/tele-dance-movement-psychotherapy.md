## Tele-Dance Movement Psychotherapy

The integration of telecommunication technology with dance movement psychotherapy (DMT) is explored in the context of providing therapeutic support to individuals on the autism spectrum. This approach, termed tele-dance movement psychotherapy, aims to leverage the benefits of DMT while overcoming geographical and accessibility barriers. Expressive arts therapy, which includes various forms of artistic expression like painting or music alongside dance movements, provides a rich framework for understanding how individuals communicate through non-verbal means. The concept of empathy mirroring suggests that therapists can enhance their intuitive understanding and rapport with clients by resonating with their body language and movements during sessions. However, the shift to telecommunication platforms introduces challenges such as reduced sensory feedback and technological limitations that may affect the therapeutic alliance and effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Tele-dance movement psychotherapy combines dance therapy techniques with telecommunication technology
- Expressive arts therapies offer a multifaceted approach to non-verbal communication in autism spectrum support
- Empathy mirroring is crucial for building rapport between therapist and client

## Open Questions

- How do technological limitations affect the therapeutic alliance and effectiveness of tele-dance movement psychotherapy?
