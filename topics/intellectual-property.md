## Intellectual Property in Arts and Health Collaboration

The integration of arts into healthcare projects necessitates a careful consideration of intellectual property rights. Sources highlight the importance of drawing up clear contracts to protect artists' work, which often includes detailed ethical guidelines to ensure fair treatment and respect for creative contributions. However, there is a tension between ensuring that artists are properly compensated and protected versus maintaining an environment where creativity can flourish freely without overly restrictive legal constraints.

## Key Findings

- Drawing up clear contracts is essential in arts and health collaborations.
- Ethical guidelines complement contractual agreements to support artists' rights.
- There needs to be a balance between protecting intellectual property and fostering creative freedom.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare organizations ensure fair treatment of artists while avoiding overly restrictive legal environments?
