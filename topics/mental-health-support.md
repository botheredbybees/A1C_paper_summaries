## Mental Health Support Through Creative Interventions

The integration of various forms of artistic and creative practices as interventions for mental health support demonstrates a consistent trend across several sources. These interventions include mindful photography, literature through shared reading groups, social prescribing programs that integrate socio-economic factors with healthcare needs, play therapy designed to foster growth in neurodivergent children, use of hand puppets to facilitate emotional safety and open dialogue among young victims of domestic violence, and arts-based psychosocial training post-disaster. Each intervention aims at fostering psychological resilience, encouraging reflective practice, and promoting social connectivity through creative expression and therapeutic processes. However, there is variability in how these practices are integrated into broader healthcare sectors, indicating a gap between innovative mental health interventions and their systemic implementation.

## Key Findings

- Creative expressions such as photography, literature, and visual arts serve as effective therapeutic tools for managing psychological distress and enhancing emotional well-being.
- Reflective practice and creative expression promote social connectivity and shared experiences in recovery contexts, indicating the role of community support in mental health interventions.
- Integrating socio-economic factors into healthcare services through programs like social prescribing enhances accessibility to mental health resources.

## Open Questions

- How can innovative mental health practices such as arts-based training and creative therapies be effectively integrated into mainstream healthcare systems to reach a wider population?
