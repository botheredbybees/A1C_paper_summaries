## Broadened Cultural Definitions

The concept of 'broadened cultural definitions' emerges from a need to challenge traditional disciplinary boundaries and foster interdisciplinary cooperation in the study of arts, culture, migration, and societal change. This approach emphasizes the rehumanization of migrants by shifting focus away from static categorizations towards dynamic, nuanced understandings that embrace diverse lived experiences. By adopting broadened cultural definitions, researchers can better capture the complexity of migrant identities and contributions to host societies. However, this paradigm shift also poses significant challenges in terms of methodological alignment across different academic disciplines, necessitating innovative approaches to collaboration.

## Key Findings

- Multidisciplinary collaboration is essential for advancing research on migration and cultural change.
- Broadened cultural definitions facilitate a rehumanization perspective that acknowledges the dynamic nature of migrant identities.
- Challenges in integrating diverse disciplinary perspectives highlight the need for methodological innovation.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective strategies for facilitating interdisciplinary collaboration in studies involving migration and cultural change?
