## Arts-Based Community Development

The sources collectively highlight the role of arts and performance as pivotal elements in fostering sustainable community development. Performance studies and ethnographic research are highlighted for their ability to provide deep insights into cultural practices, which can inform intervention strategies that are culturally relevant and effective. However, there is a tension between academic study and practical application, with some sources emphasizing the importance of theoretical frameworks while others underscore the necessity of actionable interventions within communities. The integration of arts-based approaches with community needs presents opportunities for enhancing social cohesion and economic resilience but also poses challenges in terms of scaling such initiatives effectively.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based methods are crucial for understanding local cultural practices, enabling more effective community development.
- Theoretical frameworks from performance studies contribute significantly to the practice of arts-based interventions.
- There is a need for practical strategies that bridge academic insights and on-the-ground community needs.

## Open Questions

- How can theoretical models in performance studies be effectively translated into actionable policies and practices for community development?
