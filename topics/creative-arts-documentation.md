## Creative Arts Documentation

Creative arts documentation involves the recording and reflection of experiences through artistic means. This method is used to capture sensory experiences such as those encountered in Japanese gardens, where visual, auditory, olfactory, and tactile elements are documented through various art forms. The sources highlight the use of creative documentation to enhance understanding and appreciation of cultural spaces like the Royal Tasmania Botanical Gardens. However, there is a tension between the subjective nature of artistic representation and its objective value as a research tool.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts documentation captures sensory experiences in cultural settings.
- Artistic methods can enhance visitors' engagement with environments such as Japanese gardens.
- Documentation through creative means reflects personal and emotional responses to spaces.

## Open Questions

- How does the subjective nature of artistic representation affect its utility in research contexts?
