## Vagal System in Trauma Therapy

The vagal system plays a critical role in trauma therapy by influencing the balance of the autonomic nervous system, particularly through breathing techniques and body mapping. These interventions help clients regulate their physiological responses to stress and trauma, thereby promoting healing. Sources highlight the importance of engaging the parasympathetic branch via slow exhalation exercises and creating visual representations of bodily sensations (body-mapping) to enhance self-awareness and emotional regulation.

## Key Findings

- The vagal system is pivotal in modulating physiological responses during trauma therapy.
- Breathing techniques are effective for promoting relaxation by engaging the parasympathetic nervous system.
- Body mapping enhances clients' awareness of bodily sensations, facilitating a deeper understanding of trauma impacts.
