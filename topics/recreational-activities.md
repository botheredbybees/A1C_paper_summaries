## Recreational Activities

The integration of recreational activities into the recovery process following a disaster is highlighted as crucial for both mental and physical health in children. Across studies, there's a consensus on the importance of providing safe spaces and structured activities that foster social interaction and emotional resilience among affected youth. However, some sources suggest that despite recognizing the benefits, there are challenges in implementation due to resource constraints and prioritization of immediate survival needs over long-term mental health initiatives. This synthesis reveals both the recognized value of recreational activities post-disaster and the practical barriers encountered during their integration.

## Key Findings

- Recreational activities play a crucial role in aiding children's recovery from disasters.
- Structured social interactions through recreational activities can enhance emotional resilience among affected youth.
- Implementing recreational programs faces challenges such as resource limitations.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective ways to integrate long-term mental health initiatives, including recreational activities, into immediate post-disaster response efforts?
