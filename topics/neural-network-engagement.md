## Neural Network Engagement

The concept of neural network engagement is central to understanding interventions such as singing therapy for individuals with aphasia. The summary highlights the significance of articulatory tempo and melodic intonation therapy in engaging specific neural networks, crucial for speech recovery among those suffering from aphasia after brain injury. Key themes include the importance of rhythm and melody in facilitating access to alternative pathways within the brain's communication network. However, there is a noted tension in the research regarding how best to measure engagement across different populations, indicating a need for standardized methodologies.

## Key Findings

- Articulatory tempo plays a critical role in neural network engagement.
- Melodic intonation therapy facilitates the use of alternative neural pathways for speech recovery.
- Engagement with specific neural networks is enhanced through rhythm and melody.

## Open Questions

- How can engagement be consistently measured across diverse populations?
