## Nonpharmacological Interventions for Dementia

Research into nonpharmacological interventions for individuals with dementia highlights the multifaceted benefits of such approaches in enhancing quality of life, reducing symptoms, and promoting engagement. Creative arts therapy has been systematically reviewed as an effective means to improve the quality of life for those suffering from Alzheimer's disease and other dementias. Dance movement therapy combined with physical exercise demonstrates psychophysiological effects on older adults with mild dementia, notably influencing diurnal cortisol slopes which are critical indicators of stress regulation. Additionally, engagement is a core concept in measuring the efficacy of these nonpharmacological interventions, indicating that such therapies not only address physiological markers but also enhance cognitive states and overall wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapy improves quality of life for individuals with dementia.
- Combining dance movement therapy and physical exercise positively influences stress regulation in older adults with mild dementia.
- Engagement is a critical measure in assessing the efficacy of nonpharmacological interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can engagement metrics be standardized across different nonpharmacological intervention studies to facilitate comparison?
