## Empirical Aesthetics

The field of empirical aesthetics explores the psychological processes behind aesthetic judgments and experiences. Source 1 discusses individual subjective evaluations concerning symmetry and complexity in graphic patterns, emphasizing personal definitions of beauty as varying across individuals. In contrast, Source 2 offers a broader theoretical framework over ten years, evolving Berlyne's psycho-biological model to include the 'aesthetic episode'—an experience involving aesthetic appreciation leading to judgments. This model incorporates both cognitive and emotional processes, highlighting the role of aesthetic emotions in shaping perceptions. While both sources address individual differences in aesthetic preferences, there is a notable gap between empirical analyses focusing on specific stimuli (like graphic patterns) versus comprehensive models aiming to explain a wide range of aesthetic experiences.

## Key Findings

- Symmetry and complexity are significant factors in determining the aesthetic value of visual patterns according to both sources.
- Personal definitions of beauty vary widely among individuals, impacting subjective judgments.
- Aesthetic emotions play a crucial role in shaping perceptions and appreciations.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in personal definitions of beauty align with or deviate from broader theoretical models of aesthetic appreciation?
