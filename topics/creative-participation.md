## Creative Participation in Older Populations and Trauma Recovery

The synthesis integrates insights from two distinct yet interconnected areas of creative participation: the role of theatre in enriching the lives of older adults and the therapeutic value of art therapy for trauma recovery. Bernard (2015) highlights how engaging in theatre challenges age-related stereotypes, promoting social engagement and personal growth among seniors. Similarly, studies on art therapy underscore its capacity to address diverse traumatic experiences through structured creative interventions. Both sources emphasize the transformative potential of creative arts in enhancing quality of life and fostering resilience. Despite these shared goals, there is a notable difference in the specific methodologies employed: theatre emphasizes communal storytelling and role-playing, whereas art therapy focuses more on individualized artistic expression within therapeutic settings.

## Key Findings

- Creative participation through theatre can challenge negative stereotypes about aging and promote social engagement among older adults.
- Art therapy offers structured interventions that are effective in addressing a range of traumatic experiences.
- Both theatre involvement for seniors and art therapy for trauma recovery highlight the importance of creative expression in mental health support.

## Open Questions

- How can the methodologies from theatre-based creative participation be adapted to enhance or complement existing art therapy practices?
