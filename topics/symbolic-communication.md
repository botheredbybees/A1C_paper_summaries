## Symbolic Communication in Play Therapy

Play therapy emerges as a potent therapeutic intervention for children, capitalizing on their innate ability to communicate through symbolic means. This approach leverages Mildred Parten's developmental stages of play to facilitate emotional intelligence and address psychological issues through play. Sources underscore the significance of symbolic communication in facilitating self-expression and problem-solving among young patients. However, there is a tension between the structured theoretical framework provided by play therapy and the spontaneous nature of children’s imaginative play.

## Key Findings

- Play therapy utilizes symbolic communication as a core mechanism for therapeutic intervention.
- Mildred Parten's stages of play development provide a foundational structure for understanding the progression of symbolic play in children.
- Emotional intelligence is fostered through play therapy, allowing children to better express and process their emotions.

## Open Questions

- How does the structured approach of play therapy accommodate the spontaneous nature of children's symbolic communication during sessions?
