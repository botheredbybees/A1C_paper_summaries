## Health Disparities in End-of-Life Care and Migration

The provided sources highlight significant health disparities affecting both end-of-life care recipients and international migrants. Source 1 discusses the importance of personalized care and creative arts therapy in enhancing end-of-life experiences, emphasizing that cultural and individual needs are critical components of effective palliative care strategies. Meanwhile, Source 2 focuses on male international migrants' health status in Bangladesh, indicating higher risks for depressive symptoms and chronic conditions like hypertension compared to non-migrants. These findings underscore the complexity of healthcare provision across diverse populations, highlighting a need for culturally sensitive practices that address unique challenges faced by marginalized groups. Both sources advocate for integrated care models as an approach to improve service delivery and patient outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Personalized care approaches are essential in end-of-life scenarios to meet individual needs effectively.
- Male international migrants face higher health risks compared to non-migrants, including depressive symptoms and chronic diseases.
- Integrated care models are recommended across both studies as a means of addressing the complex healthcare needs of diverse populations.
