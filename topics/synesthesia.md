## Synesthesia

The study of synesthesia integrates insights from neurobiological and cognitive perspectives. Source 2 explores the hierarchical-cortical-sensory-pathways involved in synesthesia, proposing a neurobiological theory that explains how sensory pathways may cross and connect differently in individuals with synesthesia compared to those without it. This mechanism suggests that synaptic connections are not entirely fixed but can vary between individuals due to unique developmental trajectories and experiences, which ties into the findings from Source 3 about consistent grapheme-to-color associations among adults experiencing synesthesia. Furthermore, divergent thinking, as mentioned in Source 1, may play a role in enhancing creativity by allowing for more flexible and varied connections within neural pathways. This theoretical framework highlights how individual differences and developmental stages influence the manifestation of synesthesia, offering a rich basis for further empirical research.

## Key Findings

- Hierarchical-cortical-sensory-pathways provide a neurobiological basis for understanding synesthetic experiences.
- Consistent grapheme-to-color associations indicate stability in synesthesia over time.
- Individual differences and developmental stages influence the manifestation of synesthesia.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences and environmental factors interact to shape the development and expression of synesthesia?
