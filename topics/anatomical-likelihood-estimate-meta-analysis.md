## Anatomical Likelihood Estimate Meta-Analysis

The sources highlight the application of Anatomical Likelihood Estimate (ALE) meta-analysis in conjunction with voxel-based morphometry (VBM) to identify brain regions affected by various developmental disorders. The primary focus is on uncovering gray matter alterations within distinct cerebellar areas associated with autism spectrum disorder, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), and developmental dyslexia. ALE methodology allows for a meta-analytical approach that integrates results across multiple studies, providing robust evidence of regional brain differences in these conditions.

## Key Findings

- Anatomical Likelihood Estimate (ALE) meta-analysis is used to synthesize data from numerous VBM studies.
- Distinct cerebellar regions show gray matter reductions in autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, and developmental dyslexia.
- Meta-analytical approaches provide a comprehensive overview of brain alterations associated with developmental disorders.

## Open Questions

- What are the potential neurodevelopmental mechanisms underlying gray matter changes in specific cerebellar areas among diverse populations affected by autism, ADHD, or dyslexia?
