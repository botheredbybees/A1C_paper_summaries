## Emotional Resilience

Emotional resilience is a key intervention for managing stress and improving mental health across various contexts. Sources highlight its importance in the context of chronic illnesses like type 1 diabetes, as well as in adolescent development and healthcare settings. The Type 1 Diabetes Story illustrates how families can build emotional resilience through spiritual support to cope with ongoing medical challenges. In contrast, '5 Ways to #StandByTeens' emphasizes the role of balanced parenting and stress management techniques for enhancing emotional resilience among teenagers. Additionally, sources on Arts in Adolescence and Applied Theatre demonstrate that arts-based interventions, such as puppetry and active listening, can foster cognitive benefits, socio-emotional skills, and identity formation—critical components of emotional resilience. These cross-disciplinary frameworks underscore the versatility and importance of tailored approaches to nurturing emotional resilience across diverse populations and settings.

## Key Findings

- Spiritual support is a vital component for building emotional resilience in families dealing with chronic illnesses like type 1 diabetes.
- Balanced parenting and stress management techniques are effective strategies for promoting emotional resilience among teenagers.
- Arts-based interventions, such as puppetry and active listening, contribute to the development of cognitive skills and socio-emotional capabilities that underpin emotional resilience.

## Open Questions

- What specific components of arts-based interventions (e.g., puppetry, music therapy) are most effective in fostering emotional resilience among adolescents?
