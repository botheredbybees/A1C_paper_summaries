## Wellbeing Improvement Through Arts and Music

The integration of arts and music interventions for improving individual wellbeing is highlighted across two distinct but related fields: visual arts community projects and group singing initiatives. Both sources emphasize the therapeutic benefits derived from creative engagement, each presenting a unique approach to fostering mental health and overall wellness. The first source delves into the therapeutic effects associated with engaging in visual art activities within a community setting, suggesting that such engagements can enhance personal wellbeing through various mechanisms including color therapy and artistic expression. Conversely, the second source examines group singing as an intervention for reducing mental distress and promoting psychological well-being among participants. Both studies underscore the communal aspect of these interventions, indicating that social interaction coupled with creative or musical activity is beneficial for mental health. Despite their different methodologies and focuses, both sources converge on the critical role of participatory activities in enhancing personal wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Community-based arts projects can significantly improve individual well-being through mechanisms such as color therapy and artistic expression.
- Group singing initiatives are effective in reducing mental distress and fostering psychological health among participants.
- Both visual art engagement and group singing interventions highlight the importance of social interaction for enhancing personal well-being.

## Open Questions

- How do the specific therapeutic elements (e.g., color therapy, vocal expression) differ in their impact on individual versus communal wellbeing?
