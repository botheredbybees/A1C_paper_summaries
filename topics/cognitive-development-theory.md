## Cognitive Development Theory

Chapter 11 of the human development textbook examines how and why individuals change over time, emphasizing cognitive developmental theories. It explores how environmental factors interact with genetic predispositions to shape cognitive growth. The chapter delves into Jean Piaget's stages of cognitive development, illustrating how children construct an understanding of the world through a series of logical operations that evolve with age. Epigenetic influences and social-moral development are also highlighted as critical components in this process, underscoring the interplay between biological and environmental factors.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive development theory posits stages through which individuals progress as they mature.
- Epigenetics plays a significant role alongside cognitive development by influencing gene expression based on environmental stimuli.
- Social interactions and moral reasoning significantly impact cognitive growth and adaptability.

## Open Questions
