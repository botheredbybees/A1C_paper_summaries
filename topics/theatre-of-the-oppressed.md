## Theatre of the Oppressed

Augusto Boal's Theatre of the Oppressed is a transformative intervention that integrates political activism and theatrical techniques to empower marginalized communities. The core concept revolves around 'Forum Theatre,' where participants transform from spectators into 'spect-actors' who engage actively in problem-solving through role-play scenarios. This approach aims to foster social change by addressing and challenging oppressive societal structures. Boal's work has been adapted across various contexts, including dementia care, under the Veder method, which aligns with person-centred care guidelines. The Theatre of the Oppressed serves as a powerful tool for giving voice to underrepresented groups and enabling them to enact personal and communal transformation.

## Key Findings

- Theatre of the Oppressed empowers individuals by shifting their role from passive spectators to active participants ('spect-actors') in social change.
- Techniques such as Forum Theatre are adaptable across diverse fields, including dementia care, where they support person-centred approaches.
- Boal's Theatre of the Oppressed is fundamentally a political tool aimed at amplifying the voices of marginalized groups and challenging oppressive societal norms.
