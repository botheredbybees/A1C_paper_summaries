## Hannah Arendt's Notion Of Power

Exploring the critiques of the social model of disability through the lens of Hannah Arendt’s notion of power highlights a transformative possibility. This theory suggests that power lies in collective action and engagement rather than individual struggle, offering an alternative framework to understand societal roles and structures beyond traditional models of disability advocacy. Integrating Arendt's political philosophy with contemporary discussions on social justice provides a nuanced perspective on how marginalized groups can actively participate in shaping their own liberation. However, the synthesis also underscores challenges in translating theoretical power into tangible, systemic change.

## Key Findings

- Hannah Arendt’s notion of power offers a unique critique to the social model of disability by emphasizing collective action and engagement over individual rights or accommodations.
- Arendt's theory suggests that marginalized groups can transform societal structures through political participation rather than merely seeking integration into existing norms.
- There is transformative potential in leveraging Arendtian concepts to redefine how disabled individuals engage with societal power dynamics.

## Open Questions

- How can the abstract notion of power as articulated by Hannah Arendt be operationalized within practical disability rights movements?
