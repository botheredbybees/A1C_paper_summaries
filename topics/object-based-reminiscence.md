## Object-Based Reminiscence

Object-based reminiscence is an intervention that leverages personal items or objects to stimulate memories and facilitate communication among individuals with dementia. This approach is often integrated into life-story work, which aims to create a comprehensive narrative of one’s life through various forms such as sensory-based writing. The use of tangible objects in object-based reminiscence not only aids in recalling specific events but also enhances the emotional connection between patients and their past experiences, thereby improving their quality of life.

## Key Findings

- Object-based reminiscence helps individuals with dementia recall personal memories.
- It is often used alongside life-story work to enrich the narrative of a person’s life.
- The method improves communication and emotional well-being in those suffering from dementia.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of incorporating object-based reminiscence into routine care for individuals with dementia?
