## Modernist Literature

The provided sources illustrate the critical role that modernist literature plays in exploring human perception and consciousness through innovative narrative techniques. Particularly significant is the concept of stream of consciousness, which emerged as a hallmark feature in modernist novels to capture the chaotic, associative nature of thought processes. This technique breaks away from traditional linear storytelling and immerses readers directly into characters' minds, thereby reflecting Modernist writers' aim to depict reality more accurately by incorporating psychological depth and interiority.

## Key Findings

- Stream of consciousness is a defining characteristic of modernist literature.
- Modernist literature emphasizes the exploration of human perception and consciousness.
- The narrative techniques in modernist literature diverge from traditional linear storytelling.
