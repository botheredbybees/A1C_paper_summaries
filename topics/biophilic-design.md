## Biophilic Design in Aged Care

The concept of biophilic design in aged care environments is explored through the integration of natural elements and spaces that encourage a connection to nature. Source 1, 'Thinking differently about aged care,' emphasizes the use of photovoice techniques and documentary photography as means to capture the perspectives of older adults on their living environments. This approach highlights the importance of community engagement in the design process, ensuring that interventions like biophilic design are tailored to meet the specific needs and preferences of residents. The inclusion of natural light, green spaces, and views of nature within care facilities is shown to enhance physical and mental health outcomes for older adults, fostering a sense of well-being and connection with their surroundings.

## Key Findings

- Biophilic design enhances quality of life in aged care by incorporating natural elements into living spaces.
- Photovoice and documentary photography are effective tools for capturing resident perspectives on environment design.
- Community engagement is crucial for the successful implementation of biophilic design principles.

## Open Questions

- How can biophilic design be sustainably integrated into existing aged care facilities without significant renovation costs?
