## Creative Health

The concept of creative health is emerging as a transformative approach to healthcare that integrates creativity and arts into preventive measures and treatment paradigms. Across the reviewed sources, there is consensus on the importance of viewing health in a holistic manner, encompassing not just physical but also mental and social well-being. Several key themes arise: first, the potential for creative interventions to address health inequalities by engaging marginalized communities through accessible art-based initiatives. Second, policy recommendations call for integrated care systems that value creativity as an essential determinant of health alongside traditional medical practices. Additionally, cost-effectiveness analyses suggest the economic viability of arts-based social prescriptions in reducing healthcare costs and improving population well-being over time. Despite these positive outcomes, there is a noted tension between the theoretical benefits of creative health interventions and their practical implementation within existing healthcare frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Creative health initiatives have potential to address health inequalities by reaching marginalized communities through accessible art-based programs
- Policymakers recommend integrating creativity as a determinant of health in holistic care systems alongside traditional medical approaches
- Arts-based social prescriptions demonstrate cost-effectiveness and positive impact on population well-being

## Open Questions

- How can existing healthcare frameworks be adapted to effectively incorporate creative health interventions without undermining current practices?
