## Neural Predictive Functions

The neural predictive functions play a crucial role in cognitive processes related to music perception and memory. Studies suggest that the brain uses predictive coding mechanisms to anticipate upcoming sounds, which enhances auditory memory encoding and supports efficient processing of musical information. This theory is particularly relevant during periods of heightened personal significance, such as adolescence (reminiscence bump), where musical experiences are more strongly encoded due to their emotional salience and repeated exposure.

## Key Findings

- Neural predictive functions support the brain's ability to anticipate upcoming auditory events, enhancing memory encoding.
- Music perception involves complex neural networks that utilize predictive coding for efficient processing of sound patterns.
- The reminiscence bump highlights a period where musical experiences are more deeply encoded due to emotional significance.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in cognitive and perceptual abilities influence the effectiveness of neural predictive functions in music perception?
