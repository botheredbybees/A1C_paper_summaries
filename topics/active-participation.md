## Active Participation

The concept of active participation emphasizes the engagement and involvement of individuals in their own health and wellbeing through various interventions. Sources highlight that active participation is crucial for equitable research, ensuring that participants are not merely passive subjects but co-creators of knowledge. This approach fosters a sense of ownership among participants, enhancing both the relevance and effectiveness of interventions. However, there are challenges in implementing active participation effectively across diverse populations, particularly those with limited access to resources or lacking cultural support within traditional research settings.

## Key Findings

- Active participation enhances engagement and involvement of individuals in their health and wellbeing.
- Equitable research practices benefit from active participant co-creation of knowledge.
- Challenges exist in implementing active participation across diverse populations.

## Open Questions

- How can barriers to active participation be effectively addressed in underrepresented or marginalized communities?
