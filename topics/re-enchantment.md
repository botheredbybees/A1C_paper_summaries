## Re-Enchantment

The concept of 're-enchantment' emerges across sources as a theoretical framework that explores the revitalization and rediscovery of cultural significance in contemporary contexts. In Cork, Ireland, the Shandon Big Wash Up murals serve as an emblematic case study for re-enchantment through community engagement and collective memory. This initiative reinvigorates public spaces with historical narratives and community identity, fostering a sense of belonging and active citizenship among residents. The process involves local communities in mural creation, which not only preserves cultural heritage but also engages younger generations to reconnect with their roots. Overall, the notion of re-enchantment encapsulates a broader shift towards communal revitalization and the rediscovery of meaning in modern society.

## Key Findings

- Re-enchantment is characterized by community engagement and collective memory as key mechanisms for revitalizing cultural significance.
- Public art initiatives like murals can serve as catalysts for re-enchantment by involving local communities in preserving and celebrating their heritage.
- The concept of re-enchantment promotes a sense of belonging and active citizenship among younger generations.

## Open Questions

- How scalable are public art initiatives such as murals in fostering re-enchantment across diverse urban environments?
