## Social Return On Investment (Sroi)

The concept of Social Return on Investment (SROI) emerges as a critical approach to evaluating the broader societal impacts of interventions, beyond their financial metrics. This method is highlighted in the context of Clown Doctors Australia's impact summary where SROI is used alongside social impact measurement frameworks to assess quality of care and other non-financial outcomes. The adoption of SROI underscores its utility in quantifying and qualifying the benefits that extend to community well-being and societal health, indicating a robust shift towards holistic evaluation methodologies.

## Key Findings

- SROI methodology is employed to measure social impact beyond financial returns
- Quality of care improvement can be evaluated using SROI principles
- Integration of SIM frameworks with SROI enhances comprehensive assessment
