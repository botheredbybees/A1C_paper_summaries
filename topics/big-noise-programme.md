## Big Noise Programme

The Big Noise Programme, initiated by Sistema Scotland and inspired by El Sistema in Venezuela, aims to engage children from deprived backgrounds through intensive orchestral music education. Two studies highlight its impact: one focusing on educational performance and health wellbeing in a general context (Source 1), another evaluating emotional wellbeing and social skills development specifically in the Douglas community (Source 2). Both sources agree that the programme positively influences participants' personal and academic trajectories, with observed improvements in mental health indicators and school attendance. However, discrepancies arise regarding the extent to which these benefits can be attributed solely to music education versus broader socio-economic supports within the community.

## Key Findings

- The Big Noise Programme improves educational performance among participating children.
- Enhanced emotional wellbeing is a consistent outcome across different communities engaging in the programme.
- Social skills development, including better interaction and communication abilities, is observed through participation.

## Open Questions

- To what extent does music education alone contribute to positive outcomes compared to other socio-economic supports provided by the programme?
