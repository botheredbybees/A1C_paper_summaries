## Caption Editing in Photographic Journalism

The practice of caption editing is central to the creation and interpretation of photo essays. Caption editing enhances the narrative aspect of photographs by providing contextual information that can alter audience perception. This process often involves journaling, where photographers keep detailed notes about their photoshoots which later guide the crafting of captions. Peer feedback plays a crucial role in refining these captions, offering alternative perspectives that may enrich or challenge the original interpretation. The integration of well-crafted captions not only aids in storytelling but also deepens viewer engagement with visual content.

## Key Findings

- Caption editing is essential for enhancing the narrative and informational aspects of photo essays.
- Journaling supports effective caption creation by providing photographers with detailed context about their images.
- Peer feedback during the caption-editing process can lead to more impactful and nuanced interpretations.

## Open Questions

- How does the level of detail in journaling correlate with the effectiveness of subsequent caption editing?
