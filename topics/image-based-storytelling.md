## Image-Based Storytelling

The concept of image-based storytelling is introduced as a creative and engaging narrative technique that leverages visual elements to convey stories. Sources emphasize the importance of imagery in complementing or driving narratives, often suggesting it can be more powerful than text alone in evoking emotions and conveying complex ideas. However, there are varying approaches on how this storytelling method should be implemented; some sources highlight free-writing exercises as a foundational step before integrating visual elements, while others focus solely on the visual aspect itself. This diversity reflects both the versatility of image-based storytelling and the need for practitioners to experiment with different methods to find what best suits their audience and medium.

## Key Findings

- Image-based storytelling is a versatile narrative technique that can be more emotionally resonant than text alone.
- Free-writing exercises are recommended as a preparatory step before engaging in visual storytelling.
- Different approaches exist on how to effectively integrate imagery into stories, reflecting the method's flexibility.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods for integrating free-writing exercises with image-based storytelling to enhance narrative richness and emotional impact?
