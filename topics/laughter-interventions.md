## Laughter Interventions

The efficacy of laughter and humor interventions for improving mental well-being is a subject of significant interest. A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials highlights the positive effects on depression, anxiety, and sleep quality in adults. However, the systematic review focusing on older adults identifies substantial methodological limitations across studies, suggesting inconsistency and variability in intervention designs and outcomes. While both sources agree that laughter interventions can have beneficial impacts, there is a clear need for more rigorously designed and executed research to establish their efficacy definitively.

## Key Findings

- Laughter interventions show promise for improving mental health indicators such as depression, anxiety, and sleep quality in adults.
- There is evidence of methodological limitations in studies focusing on older adults, indicating a need for more robust intervention designs.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal design features of laughter interventions to ensure consistent positive outcomes across diverse populations?
