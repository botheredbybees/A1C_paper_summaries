## Mandatory Psychiatric Emergency Hospitalizations

The retrospective observational study examining the impact of the Great East Japan earthquake on mandatory psychiatric emergency hospitalizations in Tokyo reveals significant increases in hospitalization rates among individuals indirectly exposed to the disaster. This underscores the heightened psychological distress and need for mental health intervention following a major catastrophe, even among those not directly experiencing its immediate effects. The findings suggest that indirect exposure can lead to substantial mental health burdens, necessitating proactive psychiatric emergency measures.

## Key Findings

- Mandatory psychiatric emergency hospitalizations increased in Tokyo post-Great East Japan earthquake
- Individuals indirectly exposed to the disaster experienced significant psychological distress
- Proactive measures for psychiatric emergencies are crucial following major disasters

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and societal factors influence mandatory psychiatric emergency responses during indirect disaster exposure?
