## Dementia Care Experiences Through Theatre

The integration of theatre and dementia care presents a unique intervention aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals living with dementia. Both sources highlight the use of theatre as an aesthetic experience that can evoke emotional responses and foster identification with characters, contributing to dignity enhancement (Source 1: Brightness in the Theatre). The theatre also serves as a platform for raising awareness about dementia through lived-experience narratives, creating a space where people can connect with others who are experiencing similar challenges (Source 2: KAGE Physical Theatre's Sundowner). However, these initiatives require careful consideration of how they represent and respect the dignity of those living with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Theatre interventions enhance emotional connections for individuals living with dementia through identification with characters
- Lived-experience narratives in theatre raise awareness about dementia within communities
- Dignity enhancement is a core benefit derived from participation in and creation of theatrical experiences

## Open Questions

- How can theatre-based interventions be tailored to effectively cater to the diverse needs and preferences of people living with dementia?
