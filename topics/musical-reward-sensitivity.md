## Musical Reward Sensitivity

The engagement of the orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex in early adolescents during music exposure highlights a direct link between musical stimuli and reward processing. This neural activity suggests that individuals may experience pleasure, motivation, and emotional responses through music due to activation of these brain regions associated with reward anticipation and valuation. The findings underscore the complexity of human interaction with music on neurological levels.

## Key Findings

- Music activates brain areas involved in reward processing such as the orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex.
- Early adolescents exhibit specific patterns of neural engagement when exposed to musical stimuli, indicating a critical period for understanding the interplay between music and neurodevelopment.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term implications of early adolescent brain responses to music on later cognitive and emotional development?
