## Child-Centered Play Therapy for Children with Autism

The integration of findings from the reviewed sources highlights Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) as a promising intervention strategy for children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Source 2 presents a literature review underscoring CCPT's efficacy in addressing various developmental and behavioral challenges faced by children with ASD. This is complemented by Source 1, which introduces the combination of CCPT with Rhythmic Relating to enhance emotion regulation skills specifically for autistic children, noting initial positive results from a single-case pilot study. Additionally, Source 3 contributes empirical evidence through a randomized controlled trial (RCT) demonstrating significant improvements in brain EEG activity among autistic children following CCPT intervention, suggesting physiological changes associated with enhanced cognitive and emotional processing. However, the synthesis also identifies potential gender-based considerations not fully addressed within these studies.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy is a beneficial intervention for children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- Combining CCPT with Rhythmic Relating can enhance emotion regulation in autistic children
- CCPT has been shown to influence brain EEG activity, indicating physiological improvements
