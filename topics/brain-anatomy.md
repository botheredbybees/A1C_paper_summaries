## Brain Anatomy in Neurodegenerative Diseases

The analysis of brain anatomy in neurodegenerative diseases reveals two prominent conditions: Lewy Body Dementia and Alzheimer's Disease. Both involve significant changes at the cellular and molecular levels within the brain that lead to cognitive impairment. In Lewy Body Dementia, the accumulation of abnormal alpha-synuclein aggregates form Lewy bodies, disrupting neuronal function and leading to cognitive decline. Conversely, Alzheimer’s Disease is characterized by beta-amyloid plaques and tau protein tangles, which are associated with synaptic loss and neuronal death. Both conditions have distinct pathologies but share the common symptomatology of progressive cognitive impairment. Treatments for these diseases focus on managing symptoms rather than halting progression; for example, acetylcholine inhibitors aim to maintain cognitive function in Alzheimer’s Disease patients, while immune response training vaccines are being explored as a potential therapeutic strategy.

## Key Findings

- Lewy Body Dementia and Alzheimer's Disease exhibit distinct molecular pathologies involving different proteins but both lead to cognitive decline through synaptic disruption and neuronal death.
- Both diseases share common symptoms of progressive cognitive impairment, despite having different underlying cellular mechanisms.
- Treatment strategies for neurodegenerative diseases focus primarily on symptom management rather than disease modification.
