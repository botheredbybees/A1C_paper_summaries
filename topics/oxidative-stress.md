## Oxidative Stress

The provided source highlights the role of oxidative stress in the progression of Alzheimer's disease (AD), suggesting that antidiabetic drugs may serve as therapeutic agents due to their potential influence on neuronal health, tau pathology, and insulin signaling disruption. The key concept here revolves around oxidative stress being a critical factor contributing to neurodegeneration. This review underscores the need for further investigation into how interventions targeting oxidative stress could mitigate AD progression.

## Key Findings

- Antidiabetic drugs may have therapeutic potential in Alzheimer's disease by influencing neuronal health.
- Oxidative stress plays a significant role in tau pathology and neuronal damage related to Alzheimer's disease.
- Insulin signaling disruption is linked with increased oxidative stress and is implicated in the pathogenesis of Alzheimer's disease.
