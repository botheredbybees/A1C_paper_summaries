## Neuroplasticity in Brain Rehabilitation

The sources collectively highlight the potential of music therapy as a therapeutic intervention to enhance neuroplasticity and support brain rehabilitation. Source 2 establishes historical and contemporary evidence for music's role in mitigating psychological distress and enhancing cognitive functions post-brain injury, while Source 3 provides empirical evidence that targeted music therapy can induce specific neural changes, particularly within the orbitofrontal cortex of TBI patients, leading to observable improvements in cognitive performance and social interaction. Meanwhile, Source 1 indirectly supports these findings by emphasizing the importance of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) regulation for maintaining optimal brain health, suggesting that interventions such as music therapy may influence BDNF levels positively. The synthesis reveals a consistent theme across sources: musical activities can stimulate neuroplasticity and promote cognitive rehabilitation in individuals affected by brain damage.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances neuroplastic changes and improves cognitive functions post-TBI.
- Historical and contemporary evidence supports music's therapeutic benefits for psychological distress and cognitive decline.
- Interventions like music therapy positively influence brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) regulation, essential for optimal brain health.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which music therapy influences BDNF levels and promotes neuroplasticity?
