## Tell-Show-Do Technique in Therapy

The 'tell-show-do' technique is a widely recognized method within pediatric therapy to facilitate cooperation and reduce anxiety. This approach, which involves explaining procedures (telling), demonstrating them with a model or prop (showing), and then proceeding with the actual procedure (doing), has been successfully applied in various therapeutic contexts, including dental care as seen in emergency pediatric settings. Source 1 focuses on puppet play therapy within an emergency pediatric dental clinic context where 'tell-show-do' is employed alongside other techniques to manage anxiety using the Venham Anxiety Rating Scale for evaluation. The integration of puppetry enhances this approach by making it more engaging and less threatening for children.

## Key Findings

- 'Tell-Show-Do' technique significantly reduces anxiety in pediatric dental procedures when used effectively with appropriate props or aids like puppets.
- Puppet play therapy is an innovative application of the 'tell-show-do' method, providing a non-threatening way to communicate about medical procedures.
