## Visual Hierarchy Through Negative and Positive Space

The concept of visual hierarchy is intricately linked with the principles of negative and positive space in both photography and graphic design. Negative space refers to the area around a subject, while positive space encompasses the objects of interest within an image or composition. Both concepts are crucial for directing viewer attention through effective use of minimalism and contrast. Across sources, there is agreement on how these spaces contribute to creating balance and emphasis in visual communication. However, tensions arise when considering how different cultural contexts might interpret negative versus positive space differently, affecting the perceived hierarchy.

## Key Findings

- Negative space serves a critical role in establishing focus by providing context for the main subject (positive space).
- Minimalist approaches to photography and design rely heavily on the strategic use of negative space to convey simplicity and clarity.
- Understanding visual hierarchy is essential for creating impactful compositions that guide viewer attention effectively.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences influence perceptions of balance and emphasis when using negative versus positive space?
