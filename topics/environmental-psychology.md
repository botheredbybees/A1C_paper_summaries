## Environmental Psychology in Museum Design

The integration of environmental psychology into museum architecture aims to enhance visitor wellbeing through thoughtful design that prioritizes inclusion and accessibility. Sources emphasize the importance of health-promoting design elements such as natural light, green spaces, and adaptive facilities to support all visitors' physical and mental health. Despite this focus on beneficial environmental factors, there remains a need for more comprehensive research into how specific architectural features directly impact visitor wellbeing across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Health-promoting museum design includes elements like natural light and green spaces.
- Inclusion and accessibility are key considerations in modern museum architecture.
- Environmental psychology informs the creation of well-being enhancing environments.

## Open Questions

- How do specific architectural features impact visitor mental health across diverse populations?
