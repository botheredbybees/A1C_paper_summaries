## Arts Participation and Social Wellbeing

The integration of arts-based interventions such as singing, dancing, and ballet is highlighted across multiple sources to enhance social wellbeing among diverse populations. Source 1 emphasizes how music-based interventions like singing can reduce stress hormones and increase melatonin levels, contributing significantly to mental health improvements through community support networks (Key Concepts: stress-hormone-reduction, melatonin-levels-increase, community-support-networks). Similarly, Source 2 discusses the resilience-building capabilities of engaging with music and dance programs, which are further supported by cognitive function improvement observed in participants (El Sistema, resilience-building, cognitive-function-improvement). A distinctive focus on ballet for seniors in Source 3 showcases how lifelong learning initiatives can foster community engagement, highlighting the importance of age-appropriate arts activities that promote social connection and physical activity (community-engagement, lifelong-learning). Furthermore, Source 4 provides a broad overview of various modes of engagement with healing practices through arts, reinforcing the interconnectedness between artistic expression, neural development, and overall wellbeing (engagement modes, healing practices, neural interactions). However, there is a noticeable gap in detailing specific mechanisms through which these interventions produce their benefits.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions such as singing and dancing can significantly improve mental health by reducing stress hormones and increasing melatonin levels
- Community support networks are crucial for the success of arts-based intervention programs
- Cognitive function improvement is a common benefit observed across various forms of arts engagement

## Open Questions

- What specific neural mechanisms underlie the positive effects of music and dance on cognitive functions and mental health?
