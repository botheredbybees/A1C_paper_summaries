## Empathetic Care

The concept of empathetic care is deeply rooted in the practice of narrative medicine and storytelling techniques (Source 1), which are crucial for understanding patients' experiences through their personal narratives. This approach emphasizes the importance of human connection, ethical reflection, and cultural sensitivity (Source 2). Both sources highlight how engaging with the arts and humanities can enhance medical education by fostering a more empathetic and patient-centered practice. However, integrating these methodologies into rigorous clinical training remains challenging due to traditional curricular constraints.

## Key Findings

- Narrative medicine enhances understanding of patients' experiences through storytelling techniques
- Empathetic care is fostered by incorporating arts and humanities in medical education
- Cultural sensitivity is a key component of empathetic-care

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of narrative medicine be effectively incorporated into standard clinical training?
