## Communication in Dementia Through Art and Dance

The provided sources investigate non-verbal forms of communication among individuals with dementia through the lenses of art and dance therapy. Source 1 highlights the use of painting as a means to tap into transmodal creativity and activate the reward system, thereby bypassing the limitations imposed by distributed neural networks affected in dementia patients. This approach underscores the therapeutic potential of visual arts for engaging parts of the brain that remain relatively intact despite cognitive decline. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on dance therapy, emphasizing its role in facilitating multi-modal expression and enhancing non-verbal communication among demented persons. The study suggests that by integrating bodily movements with music and social interactions, patients can express emotions and communicate in ways that words alone cannot achieve. Both sources indicate the effectiveness of alternative forms of communication for those experiencing verbal communication difficulties due to dementia.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy activates transmodal creativity and reward systems in individuals with dementia.
- Dance therapy facilitates non-verbal expression and enhances multi-modal communication.
- Both art and dance therapies offer effective alternatives to traditional verbal communication strategies for people with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of these interventions compare, particularly in terms of quality of life and symptom management?
