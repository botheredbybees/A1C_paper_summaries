## Therapeutic Interventions for Children's Mental Health

The provided sources collectively explore various therapeutic interventions aimed at enhancing the mental health of children through diverse approaches. Source 1 underscores the significance of free play in fostering secure attachment and managing positive stress, advocating against overly structured regimens that may hinder development. Conversely, Source 2 introduces expressive arts therapy as a potent method to build resilience in young people facing mental health challenges, leveraging creative expression for therapeutic applications. Meanwhile, Source 3 provides insight into practical interventions within domestic violence shelters, highlighting the use of hand puppets like Tony the Turtle to facilitate open dialogue and promote emotional safety and self-regulation skills among children. While these sources emphasize different modalities—free play, expressive arts therapy, and puppet-based dialogue—they share a foundational belief in the efficacy of non-invasive, child-centered approaches that prioritize comfort and engagement over rigid therapeutic frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Free play fosters secure attachment and positive stress regulation among children.
- Expressive arts therapies are effective for building resilience in young people facing mental health challenges.
- Creative tools like hand puppets can promote open dialogue, emotional safety, and self-regulation skills.

## Open Questions

- How do different therapeutic interventions integrate to provide a holistic approach to children's mental health?
