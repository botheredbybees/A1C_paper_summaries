## Creative Arts Interventions for Stress Management

The synthesis of the sources reveals a focus on creative arts therapies (CATs) as an effective intervention for stress management and prevention. The application of the PICOS principle in research design highlights the importance of patient or participant inclusion criteria, interventions tested, comparators used, outcomes measured, and study designs employed to ensure rigorous evaluation of CATs' efficacy. Studies emphasize the broad applicability of creative arts techniques across various populations and suggest positive impacts on mental health. However, there is a noted gap in understanding long-term effects and the need for more diverse population inclusion.

## Key Findings

- Creative Arts Therapies are effective interventions for stress management and prevention.
- The PICOS principle enhances the rigor of research designs evaluating CATs' efficacy.
- There is evidence supporting the positive impacts of creative arts techniques on mental health.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of Creative Arts Therapies on stress and mental health?
