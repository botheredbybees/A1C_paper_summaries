## Behavioral Changes

The integration of sources reveals a focus on the impact of therapeutic interventions, particularly music-supported therapy, on patients with traumatic brain injury. Source 1 emphasizes neuroplastic effects in this patient group following such therapy, indicating potential for behavioral changes. However, there is limited detail provided about specific mechanisms or generalizability across other populations or contexts.

## Key Findings

- Music-supported therapy can induce neuroplastic changes in the orbitofrontal cortex of patients with traumatic brain injury.
- Cognitive rehabilitation strategies are instrumental in facilitating behavioral changes following traumatic brain injuries.

## Open Questions

- What mechanisms underlie the observed behavioral changes, and how do these translate to other patient populations or therapeutic contexts?
