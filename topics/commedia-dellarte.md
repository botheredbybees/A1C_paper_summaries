## Commedia Dell'Arte

Commedia dell'arte is an ancient form of Italian theatre characterized by its use of stock characters and masks. Its origins are traced back to the character Pulcinella, who later evolved into Punch in the English puppet show known as 'Punch & Judy.' This art form emphasizes improvisation and relies on a set of scenarios and rhetorical techniques that provide a framework for performers while allowing flexibility for spontaneous interaction with audiences and fellow actors. The theatrical style is deeply rooted in historical and literary contexts, offering both an insight into past societal norms and a platform for commentary through performance.

## Key Findings

- Commedia dell'arte features stock characters and masks that have evolved over time.
- Improvisation plays a crucial role within the framework of predefined scenarios and rhetorical techniques.
- The theatrical form has historical roots in Pulcinella, who influenced the development of Punch & Judy.
