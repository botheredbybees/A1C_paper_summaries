## Electroencephalography (EEG)

The use of electroencephalography (EEG) spans various domains including neuroscience and therapy evaluation. In a neuroanthropological study, EEG is employed to capture the brain activity patterns during shamanic trance states in ritual specialists from Mexico, reflecting cultural-neuroscience integration and spiritual experiences' neural correlates. Conversely, in a randomized controlled trial evaluating child-centered play therapy (CCPT) for children with autism, EEG serves as a biomarker to assess therapeutic efficacy by monitoring changes in brain activity post-intervention. Both sources highlight the versatility of EEG as a methodological tool but diverge significantly in their applications—one focusing on cultural and spiritual phenomena and the other on clinical outcomes in pediatric populations.

## Key Findings

- EEG is utilized across diverse fields including neuroanthropology and clinical therapy evaluation.
- Electroencephalography can serve both to explore the neural basis of altered states of consciousness like shamanic trance and to monitor changes in brain activity due to therapeutic interventions.
- EEG as a methodological tool offers insights into cultural neuroscience and mental health outcomes through different applications.

## Open Questions
