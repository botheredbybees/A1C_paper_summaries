## Embodied Selfhood and Social Conventions

The concept of 'embodied selfhood' in the context of dementia care is explored as a theoretical framework that emphasizes understanding an individual's identity through their physical presence and interactions. This approach goes beyond cognitive or psychological interpretations by focusing on how social conventions influence and are influenced by embodied experiences. Key tensions arise around balancing traditional care models that may adhere to rigid social norms with person-centered approaches that prioritize the unique needs and expressions of selfhood in individuals living with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Social conventions impact perceptions of selfhood, especially in older adults and those with cognitive impairments.
- Person-centered dementia care integrates understanding of embodied selfhood to enhance quality of life.
- There is a need for balancing traditional social norms with personalized care approaches based on individual needs.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare practices effectively adapt social conventions to better support the concept of embodied selfhood in dementia care?
