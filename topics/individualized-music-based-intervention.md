## Individualized Music-Based Intervention

The study 'An Individualized Music-Based Intervention for Acute Neuropsychiatric Symptoms in Hospitalized Older Adults With Cognitive Impairment' explores the use of personalized music therapy as a nonpharmacological intervention. It highlights how tailored musical experiences can mitigate acute neuropsychiatric symptoms without relying on medication, thereby addressing an unmet need in patient care. The research underscores the potential for individualized approaches to be effective and adaptable solutions for managing distressing symptoms in cognitively impaired older adults.

## Key Findings

- Individualized music-based interventions are a nonpharmacological alternative for managing acute neuropsychiatric symptoms in hospitalized older adults with cognitive impairment.
- Music therapy can provide tailored experiences that address unique patient needs and preferences, enhancing its effectiveness as an intervention.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of individualized music-based interventions beyond the acute phase?
