## Arts-Informed Research

Arts-informed research emerges as a valuable method for studying the experiences of marginalized communities, particularly refugee youth in the context of migration. This approach leverages symbolic expression through various arts-based techniques to capture tacit knowledge and understand shifts in roles and responsibilities among participants. Both sources highlight the utility of this method in uncovering nuanced insights into how youth navigate complex social landscapes post-migration, offering a more holistic perspective than traditional research methods alone. However, there is an underlying tension regarding the extent to which arts-informed research can fully represent the lived experiences of refugee youth, especially when dealing with sensitive topics and vulnerable populations.

## Key Findings

- Arts-informed research effectively captures tacit knowledge and shifts in roles and responsibilities among refugee youth
- This method provides a holistic perspective on understanding changes faced by marginalized communities post-migration

## Open Questions

- How can arts-informed research adequately address the sensitive and often traumatic experiences of refugee youth without causing further harm?
