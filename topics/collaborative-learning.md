## Collaborative Learning Through Creative Arts

The integration of collaborative learning through creative arts highlights a transformative approach to engaging individuals with dementia (Source 1) and educating health professionals (Source 2). The concept of 'spect-actors' within musical theatre groups underscores the empowerment and autonomy granted to dementia patients, facilitating their active participation and creativity despite cognitive challenges. This model contrasts sharply with traditional caregiving approaches that may focus more on passive care rather than participatory engagement. Meanwhile, in educational settings, creative arts are employed not just as a means of skill acquisition but also as a way to cultivate compassion and instill non-normative ethical values among students. Both sources emphasize the importance of fostering an environment where mutual learning and respect for individual capabilities transcend traditional hierarchies between educators/trainers and participants.

## Key Findings

- Empowerment through creative participation for individuals with dementia
- Use of creative arts to foster compassion and non-normative ethics in healthcare professionals
- Shift from passive care models to active, collaborative engagement

## Open Questions

- How can the spect-actor model be adapted across different forms of creative arts beyond musical theatre?
