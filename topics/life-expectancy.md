## Life Expectancy

The Happy Planet Index (HPI) of 2024 emphasizes the interplay between life expectancy and other quality-of-life indicators like self-reported wellbeing and environmental sustainability. It reveals that while some countries achieve high life expectancy, this is often coupled with a significant carbon footprint, indicating a trade-off between longevity and environmental health. The HPI suggests that for sustainable global well-being, it's crucial to consider not just the length of life but also its quality and environmental impact.

## Key Findings

- High life expectancy does not necessarily correlate with high self-reported wellbeing or low carbon footprint
- Sustainable living practices can contribute positively to both longevity and overall happiness
- There is a complex relationship between economic development, environmental sustainability, and human health metrics

## Open Questions

- How can societies achieve high life expectancy while minimizing their ecological impact?
