## Societal Attitudes Towards Care

The provided sources collectively explore the theoretical underpinnings of how society perceives and interacts with care practices. Source 1, which focuses on 'Theatre of Wandering: Rehearsal and devising notes [Part 2]', delves into community involvement and co-production as key elements in understanding societal attitudes towards care. The concept of care is examined through the lens of collaborative art practices where communities are actively involved in creating narratives that reflect their experiences with care, thereby challenging traditional views on who provides care and how it should be perceived.

## Key Findings

- Community involvement enhances understanding and acceptance of care services.
- Co-production models can shift societal attitudes towards more inclusive perspectives of care.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultural contexts influence the effectiveness of community-involvement in shaping positive societal attitudes towards care?
