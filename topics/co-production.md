## Co-production in Arts and Health

Co-production is highlighted across both sources as a collaborative approach that integrates stakeholders at all levels of an initiative. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of co-production for creating effective evaluation practices in arts-based health programs, stressing its role not just in the execution but also in assessing these initiatives. It underscores how involving beneficiaries and community members enhances credibility and creativity in evaluation processes. In contrast, Source 2 focuses more on the practice itself within a theatre context, showing how co-production fosters greater societal engagement with care issues through direct involvement of community members in artistic production. Both sources concur that co-production is beneficial for enhancing social inclusion and improving outcomes relevant to both arts and health domains.

## Key Findings

- Co-production enhances credibility and creativity in evaluation processes within arts-based health initiatives.
- Direct community involvement through co-production fosters greater societal engagement with care issues.
- Co-production can improve social inclusion and outcome relevance in both arts and health contexts.

## Open Questions

- How does the extent of stakeholder engagement vary across different models of co-production, and what impact does this variation have on evaluation outcomes?
