## Problem Externalization in Narrative Therapy

Problem externalization is a core technique within narrative therapy, aiming to help clients view their problems as separate entities from themselves. This process can be facilitated through various methods including the use of puppets and other objectification techniques. Source 1 highlights how using puppets with children in narrative therapy effectively externalizes problems, allowing them to take on an identity distinct from the child's own self-concept. While this method has been found beneficial for distancing individuals from their issues, it also emphasizes the importance of choosing appropriate metaphors or objects that resonate with the client and aid in constructing a new story about themselves outside the context of the problem.

## Key Findings

- Problem externalization helps clients view problems as separate entities rather than inherent personal traits.
- Using puppets can be an effective tool for children to externalize their problems in narrative therapy sessions.
- Choosing appropriate metaphors and objects is crucial for successful externalization in narrative therapy.
