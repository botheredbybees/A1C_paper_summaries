## Cognitive Impairments and Music Therapy

The role of music in addressing cognitive impairments is a prominent theme across sources. In 'Introduction to Week 2: Music and the Brain', the focus lies on how music can enhance brain function and serve as an effective tool for managing conditions that affect cognitive abilities, such as dementia. Conversely, 'Playfully engaging people living with dementia' delves into the use of playful engagement methods, like relational clowning, to improve quality of life for individuals experiencing cognitive impairments. Both sources highlight the significance of music's role in memory recall and emotional connection, underscoring its therapeutic value beyond purely cognitive functions.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can significantly enhance brain function and support individuals with cognitive impairments.
- Playful engagement techniques, such as relational clowning, improve quality of life for those living with dementia.
- The use of music in cognitive therapy aids in the retrieval of musical memories, which can positively influence overall well-being.

## Open Questions

- How do playful engagement methods like relational clowning complement or enhance traditional music-based interventions?
