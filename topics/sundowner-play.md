## Sundown Syndrome in Theatre

The Sundowner play by KAGE Physical Theatre focuses on portraying the experiences of people with dementia during sundown syndrome, a phenomenon characterized by increased confusion and agitation often seen at the end of the day. This theatrical intervention aims to raise awareness about this condition among the general public through lived-experience narratives. The play uses storytelling to bridge emotional gaps between those unfamiliar with dementia and individuals living with it or their caregivers, offering an empathetic view that challenges stereotypes associated with cognitive decline.

## Key Findings

- Sundowner syndrome is a significant issue for people living with dementia and their caretakers.
- Theatre can serve as an effective platform to educate the public about sundown syndrome through lived-experience narratives.
- Portraying real-life challenges faced by individuals with dementia helps in dispelling common misconceptions.
