## Creative Arts in Community Health

The integration of creative arts into community health interventions offers a multifaceted approach to enhancing both social inclusion and emotional well-being. Sources highlight the application of various art forms such as drama, theatre, literature, and visual arts (Source 1) to foster an environment where individuals can flourish through unconditional positive regard and reflective practice (Sources 1 & 4). Additionally, these interventions aim at promoting health outcomes beyond mental and social benefits by focusing on physical wellness too (Source 2). Specifically, creative writing for well-being, shared reading groups, and drama workshops are utilized to build self-esteem, peer trust, and critical awareness among young black men (Sources 3 & 4), thereby addressing specific needs of marginalized populations. However, while the arts-on-prescription model demonstrates positive health and social cohesion benefits, there is a need for more tailored interventions that address diverse community contexts and individual differences.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts contribute to emotional well-being through reflective practice and unconditional positive regard
- Interventions like shared reading groups and drama workshops improve self-esteem and critical awareness among marginalized populations
- Arts-on-prescription models enhance health outcomes, including social cohesion

## Open Questions

- How can community-based creative arts interventions be tailored to better serve diverse cultural contexts?
