## Protein Aggregation in Neurodegenerative Diseases

The sources explore the critical role of protein aggregation in neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD). Source 1 discusses how autophagy-mediated pathways, regulated by factors like diet and physical activity, can influence protein aggregation in Alzheimer's disease. It highlights that these non-pharmacological strategies could serve as potential interventions to mitigate the accumulation of toxic proteins. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on FTD, which is also characterized by abnormal protein aggregation but places emphasis on specific cognitive impairments such as inhibitory control and language function deficits. Both sources underscore the importance of understanding the mechanisms behind protein aggregation in developing effective therapeutic strategies.

## Key Findings

- Protein aggregation plays a central role in both Alzheimer's disease and Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD).
- Autophagy-mediated pathways are key to managing protein aggregation in neurodegenerative conditions.
- Non-pharmacological interventions such as diet and physical activity can regulate autophagy.

## Open Questions
