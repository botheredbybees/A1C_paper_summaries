## Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia

The management and understanding of behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) is a critical area in geriatric care, with diverse approaches focusing on both pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of person-centered care in addressing BPSD, noting that while medications may be necessary for certain cases, non-pharmacological methods are often preferred due to their holistic approach and lower risk profile. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the epidemiology and factors influencing BPSD within a population setting, highlighting patterns and co-occurrences of symptoms using a Four-Factor Solution framework. This model aids in categorizing symptom clusters, which is crucial for tailored treatment approaches but requires further research to fully understand its clinical implications.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered care emphasizes the importance of individualized non-pharmacological interventions over medication.
- Non-pharmacological methods are preferred due to lower risks and holistic benefits in managing BPSD.
- A Four-Factor Solution framework aids in categorizing symptom clusters for better treatment personalization.
