## Solution-Focused Brief Therapy

Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT) is a form of psychotherapy that concentrates on the present and future rather than delving into past events. Its application extends to various therapeutic contexts, including child-centered therapy where tools like puppet play are used to enhance communication and engagement. The Berkeley Puppet Interview technique exemplifies how SFBT can be adapted creatively for children, facilitating discussions about solutions in a playful environment. Despite its practical applications, the integration of SFBT with other modalities such as puppet play therapy reveals both potential benefits and areas where further research is needed.

## Key Findings

- Solution-Focused Brief Therapy emphasizes current strengths and future possibilities over past issues.
- Puppet play can be an effective tool in child-centered therapy to engage children in SFBT practices.
- The Berkeley Puppet Interview technique illustrates creative adaptation of SFBT for pediatric settings.
