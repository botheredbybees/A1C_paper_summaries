## Neurodevelopmental Differences in Autism and Trauma

The neurodevelopmental differences discussed in both sources underscore the complexity of interventions tailored for individuals with autism spectrum condition (ASC) or trauma-related symptoms. Source one explores age-related changes in white matter diffusion measures, such as fractional anisotropy and radial diffusivity, which are associated with brain maturation processes that may differ in individuals with ASC. Conversely, source two focuses on the impact of Gestalt play therapy on self-regulation abilities among children experiencing trauma during middle childhood. While these sources emphasize distinct neurodevelopmental aspects and therapeutic interventions, they collectively highlight the importance of understanding specific developmental trajectories to inform effective treatment strategies.

## Key Findings

- Age-related differences in white matter diffusion measures can distinguish individuals with ASC from typically developing peers.
- Gestalt play therapy is beneficial for enhancing self-regulation skills in children affected by trauma.

## Open Questions
