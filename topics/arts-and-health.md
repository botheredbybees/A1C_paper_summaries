## Arts and Health

The integration of arts into health practices is a multifaceted intervention that encompasses various methodologies for evaluating its impact on individuals' wellbeing. Sources highlight the importance of both quantitative and qualitative research methods, including randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and creative methodologies, to assess the efficacy of arts-based interventions in healthcare settings. A critical reflection approach through photo essays provides insights into themes such as patient experiences and health outcomes. Evaluating community projects using validated surveys underscores the need for rigorous frameworks that can capture the nuanced benefits of art as therapy or social intervention.

## Key Findings

- Arts and health interventions benefit from a mixed-methods research design
- Critical reflection through photo essays is valuable in understanding arts-integrated health themes
- Validation of survey tools enhances the credibility of evaluating creative arts projects

## Open Questions

- How can critical reflection methodologies be standardized across different types of arts-based healthcare interventions?
