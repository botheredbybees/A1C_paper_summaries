## Power Dynamics in Interviews

The concept of power dynamics in interviews is a critical aspect of understanding the interaction between interviewer and interviewee. Sources emphasize that these dynamics are not static but evolve throughout the interaction, influenced by factors such as authority, subjectivity, and visual mediums like photography. Therapeutic photography is highlighted for its role in altering traditional power balances through reflexive practices, where both parties engage in a more equitable exchange of ideas and experiences. Visual pedagogy highlights that images can serve as tools to democratize the interview process and foster an environment of mutual respect and learning. Emancipatory research underpins these discussions by advocating for methods that empower participants, challenging hierarchical relationships often inherent in traditional interviewing.

## Key Findings

- Power dynamics in interviews are dynamic and influence the direction and outcomes of conversations.
- Visual pedagogy through therapeutic photography can alter power imbalances in favor of a more equitable exchange between interviewer and interviewee.
- Emancipatory research methods aim to empower participants, promoting mutual respect and learning.

## Open Questions

- How can visual methodologies be effectively integrated into interviewing practices to enhance equality and reciprocity?
