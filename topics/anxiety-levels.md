## Anxiety Levels in Traumatized Youth

The reviewed studies on creative arts-based interventions for children and adolescents exposed to traumatic events highlight the significant role these therapies play in mitigating symptoms such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), negative mood, externalizing problems, and anxiety levels. While there is substantial evidence supporting the efficacy of creative arts therapy in reducing overall distress, inconsistencies exist regarding the long-term effectiveness of these interventions. Some studies suggest that while immediate symptom reduction is evident, sustaining benefits over time remains a challenge. The integration of various art forms (e.g., visual arts, drama, and music) appears to offer diverse pathways for emotional expression and processing, which can contribute to improved mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts-based interventions effectively reduce anxiety levels in children and adolescents exposed to trauma.
- Inconsistencies exist regarding the sustained benefits of these therapies over longer periods.
- Various forms of creative arts therapy offer unique pathways for emotional expression, contributing to overall mental health improvement.

## Open Questions

- What are the mechanisms that determine long-term efficacy in reducing anxiety levels among traumatized youth through creative arts interventions?
