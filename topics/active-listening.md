## Active Listening

The concept of active listening is a pivotal aspect in various therapeutic and educational interventions, notably highlighted in the context of applied theatre and puppetry within healthcare settings. This framework aims to enhance emotional resilience by fostering empathetic communication skills among practitioners through interactive methodologies like active listening. Sources converge on the significance of this technique for building strong client-practitioner relationships, improving patient outcomes, and training health professionals effectively. However, while there is agreement on its efficacy in enhancing emotional intelligence, sources differ slightly in their approaches to measuring long-term impact.

## Key Findings

- Active listening significantly contributes to the enhancement of emotional resilience among participants.
- It plays a crucial role in improving communication skills and fostering empathetic relationships within healthcare settings.
- The application of active listening techniques can be effectively integrated into cross-disciplinary training frameworks.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of incorporating active listening practices on professional development and patient care outcomes?
