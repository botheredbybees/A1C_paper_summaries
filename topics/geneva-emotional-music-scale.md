## Geneva Emotional Music Scale

The Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS) is a methodological framework designed to characterize and measure the emotions evoked by music. This scale provides a structured approach to understanding how various types of emotional responses are elicited in listeners, thereby enhancing our ability to classify these reactions systematically. While GEMS offers a standardized tool for researchers and practitioners to study musical-emotional interactions, its application reveals both strengths and limitations across different cultural contexts and listener demographics. There is significant agreement among studies on the utility of GEMS in identifying primary emotions (such as happiness or sadness) evoked by music but less clarity regarding more nuanced emotional states. Additionally, there are ongoing discussions about the extent to which GEMS can effectively capture cross-cultural similarities and differences in musical-emotional experiences.

## Key Findings

- The Geneva Emotional Music Scale provides a standardized method for characterizing and measuring emotions evoked by music.
- There is agreement on GEMS's effectiveness in identifying primary emotional responses such as happiness or sadness.
- Ongoing discussions highlight the need to adapt GEMS for diverse cultural contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can the Geneva Emotional Music Scale be effectively adapted to capture cross-cultural similarities and differences in musical-emotional experiences?
