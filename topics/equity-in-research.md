## Equity in Research

The concept of equity in research is explored across various sources with a focus on ensuring that all community members have equal opportunities to participate and benefit from the research process. The Week 6 Activities in Arts in the Community emphasize active participation as a key component of equitable research practices, highlighting the importance of engaging marginalized or underrepresented groups directly in the creation and implementation of research initiatives. This source underscores the necessity for health and wellbeing outcomes to reflect community needs accurately when research is conducted with genuine equity in mind.

## Key Findings

- Active participation by all members of a community enhances the validity and relevance of research findings.
- Equitable research practices are essential for ensuring that marginalized groups can benefit from research outcomes.
- Health and wellbeing initiatives should be informed by community-driven insights to address local needs effectively.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers ensure sustained active participation from all community members throughout the entire research process?
