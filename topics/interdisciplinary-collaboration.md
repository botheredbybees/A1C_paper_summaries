## Interdisciplinary Collaboration in Neuroaesthetic and Educational Approaches

The synthesis explores the intersection of interdisciplinary collaboration within neuroaesthetic principles and educational approaches for individuals with autism spectrum disorders (ASD). Source 1 emphasizes the importance of sensory experiences and neuroaesthetic principles to enhance health and well-being through creative arts therapies. Meanwhile, Source 2 focuses on developing research-driven interventions and educational methods tailored specifically to students with ASD. Both sources highlight the necessity of interdisciplinary collaboration between fields such as psychology, neuroscience, education, and arts therapy to effectively address individual needs and improve outcomes for people with ASD. There is a noted agreement in both sources regarding the integration of sensory experiences into therapeutic and educational frameworks, yet there remains an underexplored tension around how these principles are practically applied across diverse settings.

## Key Findings

- Neuroaesthetic principles play a crucial role in enhancing well-being through creative arts therapies.
- Educational approaches for ASD should be research-driven to ensure effectiveness and inclusivity.
- Interdisciplinary collaboration is essential for the successful application of neuroaesthetic and educational interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can sensory experiences and neuroaesthetic principles be effectively integrated into everyday educational settings for students with ASD?
