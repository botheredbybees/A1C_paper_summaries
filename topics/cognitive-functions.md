## Cognitive Functions

The cognitive functions of the human brain are intricately linked to various biological and anatomical components. Source 1 highlights the critical role of visceral adipose tissue in regulating key neurotrophic factors such as Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) and chemokine ligand CX3CL1, which play a vital part in maintaining cognitive functions. Conversely, Source 2 delves into the structural aspects of the brain, focusing on regions like the limbic system, hippocampus, and amygdala that are pivotal for emotional regulation, memory formation, and processing fear responses. While both sources focus on different dimensions of the brain's functionality, they collectively underscore the complexity of cognitive processes which are influenced by biological substrates such as visceral adipose tissue, along with anatomical structures like the limbic system.

## Key Findings

- Visceral adipose tissue regulates key neurotrophic factors essential for maintaining cognitive functions.
- Brain regions including the hippocampus and amygdala play critical roles in emotional regulation and memory formation.
- Cognitive processes are influenced by both biological substrates like visceral fat and anatomical brain structures.

## Open Questions
