## Creative Expansion With Age

The concept of 'creative expansion with age' emphasizes the positive relationship between aging and artistic engagement. The continuity-and-adaptation theory posits that older adults maintain their core identity while adapting to new circumstances, leading to a potential increase in creative activities as they seek fulfillment and meaning. Gerotranscendence further supports this idea by suggesting that aging individuals move beyond everyday concerns towards deeper existential experiences often expressed through art. However, the literature highlights variability in how different individuals engage with creativity based on their personal histories and current life circumstances.

## Key Findings

- Creative engagement among older adults can serve as a means to maintain identity and find meaning during later stages of life
- Gerotranscendence may encourage a deeper existential exploration that is often expressed through artistic endeavors in the elderly population
- There is variability in how individuals engage with creative activities based on personal histories and current contexts

## Open Questions
