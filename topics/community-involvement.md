## Community Involvement in Dementia Care

Both sources highlight the importance of community involvement in enhancing dementia care. Source 1 emphasizes building inclusive environments and understanding legal obligations that encourage businesses to become dementia-friendly, promoting a supportive society for individuals with dementia. Meanwhile, Source 2 explores the Theatre of Wandering project, which uses co-production methods involving people with dementia and their caregivers to devise theatrical performances that raise societal awareness about caregiving attitudes. Both sources underscore how community engagement can transform public perception and facilitate more effective care environments by integrating legal requirements with social initiatives.

## Key Findings

- Community involvement is crucial for fostering inclusive environments in businesses and society at large.
- Legal obligations play a significant role in encouraging businesses to contribute positively towards dementia-friendly communities.
- Co-production methods involving people with dementia can effectively challenge societal attitudes towards caregiving.

## Open Questions

- How can co-production models be scaled up to incorporate broader community participation in enhancing dementia care?
