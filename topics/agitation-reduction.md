## Music Therapy and Agitation Reduction in Dementia Care

Music therapy emerges as a promising intervention for reducing agitation in dementia care, integrating both therapeutic practice and caregiver-assisted strategies. Both sources highlight its efficacy in enhancing patient relationships and improving brain plasticity among individuals with dementia. The Homeside trial mentioned in the first source suggests that embedding music into care pathways can lead to significant reductions in agitated behaviors. However, there is a noted gap regarding standardized methods for integrating music therapy within routine clinical settings. While both sources underscore the potential of music therapy for agitation reduction and improving brain plasticity, they also suggest challenges in operationalizing these practices consistently across different caregiving environments.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy shows promise in reducing agitation among dementia patients.
- Integration of music into care pathways can improve patient relationships and reduce agitated behaviors.
- Brain plasticity may be enhanced through the application of music-assisted therapeutic interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the best methods for integrating music therapy consistently across diverse caregiving settings?
