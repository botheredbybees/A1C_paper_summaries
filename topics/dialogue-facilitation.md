## Dialogue Facilitation Through Puppet Therapy

The use of puppet therapy as a form of dialogue facilitation reveals its unique ability to engage adults through the medium of childlike wonder and culturally sensitive puppetry. Across sources, there is an agreement on how puppets can act as a safe space for individuals to express themselves without feeling directly exposed or vulnerable. This method fosters engagement by allowing participants to access emotions and thoughts that might otherwise remain unarticulated due to social barriers. The role of the facilitator in maintaining a culturally sensitive approach to puppetry ensures that all participants feel respected and understood, enhancing the effectiveness of the dialogue. However, while there is recognition of the potential benefits of this method, there remains an open question regarding its broader applicability across different cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Puppet therapy serves as a tool for engaging adults in emotional and psychological expression.
- Culturally sensitive puppetry is crucial to ensure all participants feel respected and understood.
- The facilitator's role in maintaining the therapeutic environment is pivotal.

## Open Questions

- How can puppet therapy be adapted or implemented effectively across diverse cultural contexts?
