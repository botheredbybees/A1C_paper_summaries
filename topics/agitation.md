## Music Therapy for Agitation in Dementia

This synthesis focuses on the efficacy of music therapy as an intervention to reduce agitation among individuals with dementia. Source 1 discusses a randomized controlled trial comparing music therapy with general recreational activities, highlighting that while both interventions are beneficial, music therapy appears more effective in reducing agitation levels. The study underscores the unique benefits of structured musical engagement compared to non-specific leisure pursuits.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy is more effective than general recreational activities in alleviating agitation in dementia patients.
- Structured musical engagement provides unique therapeutic advantages over less directed forms of activity for individuals with dementia.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and sustainability of music therapy's benefits on reducing agitation?
