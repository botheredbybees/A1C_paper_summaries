## Health Humanities

The concept of Health Humanities encompasses interdisciplinary approaches that integrate humanities disciplines such as literature, philosophy, and visual arts into the study and practice of healthcare. Anna Mackintosh's critical reflection on her photographic essay highlights the use of natural light photography to evoke a sense of wabi-sabi, emphasizing imperfection and transience in human experiences. This approach underscores how health humanities can facilitate emotional healing through creative expression, aligning with concepts from phototherapy which uses visual arts as therapeutic tools.

## Key Findings

- Health humanities integrate various forms of artistic expression into healthcare practices.
- Photography, particularly natural light photography, can serve as a medium for expressing personal and cultural narratives related to health experiences.
- The philosophy of wabi-sabi in photography reflects the imperfections inherent in human life, which can be therapeutic.

## Open Questions

- How do different artistic modalities within health humanities contribute uniquely to patient care and wellbeing?
