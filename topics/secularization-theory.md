## Secularization Theory

Secularization theory discusses the process by which societies move from religious dominance to a situation where religion has less influence. In Alain de Botton's perspective, this secular trend affects how art is perceived and utilized in society. De Botton posits that art can serve a therapeutic role in modern life by addressing emotional needs traditionally met through religious practices, thereby filling the gap left by declining religiosity. This didactic purpose of art suggests an adaptive function in a more secular world where traditional forms of spiritual support are less prevalent or effective.

## Key Findings

- Secularization theory explains societal shifts away from religious dominance towards non-religious frameworks.
- Art can take on therapeutic roles as society becomes more secular, addressing emotional needs formerly fulfilled by religion.
- The didactic purpose of art increases as traditional spiritual support diminishes.

## Open Questions

- How does the increasing reliance on art for therapy and education affect other non-art forms of secular support?
