## Interventions for Children with Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition that requires tailored intervention strategies. Across the sources, multiple forms of therapeutic approaches are highlighted as beneficial in addressing various challenges faced by children diagnosed with ASD. Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT), a non-directive approach that encourages self-expression and emotional exploration through play, has been identified as an effective intervention for facilitating social skills improvement and enhancing communication abilities among these children. Similarly, dramatherapy emerges as another creative method to support the socio-emotional development of individuals with ASD, emphasizing the therapeutic use of drama in educational settings. Both CCPT and dramatherapy underscore the importance of individualized treatment approaches that cater specifically to the unique needs of each child. The literature suggests that while these interventions offer promising avenues for supporting children with ASD, there is a need for further research to establish standardized protocols and measure long-term efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) enhances social skills and communication in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).
- Dramatherapy offers therapeutic benefits for socio-emotional development of children and adolescents with ASD.
- Individualized education plans tailored to the specific needs of each child are essential for effective interventions.

## Open Questions

- What standardized protocols can be developed to ensure consistent application and efficacy measurement of CCPT and dramatherapy?
