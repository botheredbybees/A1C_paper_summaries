## Emotional Reactions in Aesthetic Appreciation

The concept of emotional reactions within the framework of aesthetic appreciation is explored through two distinct but interrelated perspectives. Source one discusses Berlyne's psycho-biological aesthetics and a model of aesthetic appreciation, focusing on how aesthetic emotions are triggered during the 'aesthetic episode'. This model suggests that these episodes involve cognitive processing alongside emotional responses to artistic stimuli. Meanwhile, source two delves into the neural underpinnings of emotional reactions to poetry specifically, identifying the nucleus accumbens as a key brain region involved in peak emotion experiences and aesthetic pleasure. Together, these sources illuminate both the theoretical framework surrounding aesthetic emotions and their physiological correlates, emphasizing the complexity of emotional responses that extend beyond simple cognitive evaluation.

## Key Findings

- Aesthetic appreciation involves complex interactions between cognitive and affective processes during an 'aesthetic episode'.
- The nucleus accumbens plays a crucial role in processing peak emotion experiences and aesthetic pleasure, particularly in response to poetry.
- Berlyne's psycho-biological aesthetics model provides a theoretical framework for understanding the emotional responses elicited by art.

## Open Questions
