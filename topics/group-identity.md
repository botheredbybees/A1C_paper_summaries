## Group Identity in Choir Singing

Choir singing emerges as a multifaceted intervention offering social and mental health benefits for disadvantaged adults. This practice fosters emotional regulation by providing a supportive environment where individuals can express their feelings safely, enhancing both personal well-being and communal cohesion. The experience is also spiritual, transcending the mundane to offer a sense of transcendence that deepens individual and collective identity. Choirs act as platforms for self-reflection, allowing participants to enhance their self-perception through group activities. These benefits highlight choir singing's potential in addressing social isolation and mental health issues within specific communities.

## Key Findings

- Choir singing offers significant emotional regulation support for individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds
- Participating in choirs provides a spiritual experience that enriches individual identity and sense of belonging
- Self-perception is positively influenced through collective group activities

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of choir participation compare to other forms of social interventions?
