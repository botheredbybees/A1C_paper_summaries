## Art as Self-Expression

The role of art as a medium for self-expression is explored in the context of Josie Webster's work conquering the art world. Art serves not only as an aesthetic pursuit but also as a powerful means for individuals, particularly those with disabilities such as Down syndrome, to challenge societal norms and assert their identity. The integration of inclusive practices that involve artists with diverse abilities highlights the transformative potential of self-expression in broadening public understanding and appreciation of unique perspectives. However, this narrative raises questions about the extent to which traditional art world standards accommodate non-traditional forms of expression.

## Key Findings

- Art can be a tool for challenging societal norms and promoting inclusion.
- Self-expression through art is beneficial for individuals with disabilities.
- Inclusive practices in the arts are necessary for broader cultural acceptance.

## Open Questions

- How do traditional art institutions adapt to incorporate diverse forms of self-expression?
