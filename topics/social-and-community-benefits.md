## Social and Community Benefits

The synthesis examines the social and community benefits derived from various interventions such as arts-based activities for individuals with dementia. Across different studies, visual art activities are shown to enhance quality of life and foster a sense of community among participants. However, there is a tension between the observed positive outcomes in smaller scale studies and the need for broader implementation and validation through larger-scale research. Each source highlights unique aspects but collectively underscores the importance of social connectedness and communal engagement for therapeutic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Visual art activities improve quality of life for individuals with dementia.
- Participation in arts-based interventions fosters a sense of community and social connection.
- Social and community benefits are integral to holistic care and support strategies.

## Open Questions

- How can the positive impacts observed in smaller, localized studies be scaled up to wider communities effectively?
