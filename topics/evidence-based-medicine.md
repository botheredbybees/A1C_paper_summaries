## Evidence-Based Medicine

The concept of Evidence-Based Medicine (EBM) is explored through two lenses in the provided sources. The first source delves into the GRADE system, which provides a structured approach to assess and rate the quality of evidence and strength of clinical recommendations, aiming for consistency and transparency in medical decision-making. Conversely, the second source emphasizes the importance of integrating humanistic elements within EBM, suggesting that while scientific rigor is essential, it should be complemented by a holistic understanding that includes personal narratives and patient values to offer more complete care. Both sources highlight the dual nature of contemporary healthcare: the need for robust empirical evidence coupled with a humane approach that recognizes the individuality of each patient.

## Key Findings

- The GRADE system offers a standardized framework for evaluating the quality of medical evidence and formulating recommendations.
- There is an emerging consensus on improving the methods used to rate the quality of evidence in clinical practice.
- Integrating humanistic elements within EBM can lead to more personalized and patient-centered care.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of medical humanism enhance or be integrated with the rigorous standards set by systems like GRADE?
