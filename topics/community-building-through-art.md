## Community Building Through Art

The use of art as an intervention for fostering community building is evident in the case of intergenerational choirs. These choirs serve multiple therapeutic purposes, including managing memory loss and enhancing mental health through the therapeutic benefits of music. The interplay between older adults experiencing memory loss and younger participants creates a unique environment where both groups can benefit from social interaction and shared experiences. However, while the choir setting clearly promotes community building, there are gaps in understanding how these effects generalize to other forms of art-based interventions or communities with different demographic compositions.

## Key Findings

- Intergenerational choirs provide a therapeutic environment for managing memory loss through music therapy
- Music serves as a powerful medium for social cohesion and community building across generational divides
- Artistic interventions like intergenerational choirs offer mental health benefits to participants

## Open Questions

- How do the therapeutic benefits of music translate into other forms of artistic community-building efforts?
