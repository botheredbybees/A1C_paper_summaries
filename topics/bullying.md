## Bullying and Its Impact on Adolescent Mental Health

The Mission Australia Youth Survey highlights the pervasive issue of bullying among adolescents and its significant impacts on their mental health. The survey underscores the detrimental effects of both traditional and cyberbullying, often exacerbated by excessive use of social media. Findings indicate that victims of bullying frequently experience heightened anxiety, depression, and a decline in overall wellbeing. While the study identifies the broad scope of the problem, it also suggests potential areas for intervention through increased support services and education programs aimed at addressing bullying behaviors before they escalate. However, there is an ongoing debate regarding the most effective methods to mitigate the adverse effects of social media on youth mental health.

## Key Findings

- Bullying negatively impacts adolescent mental health leading to higher levels of anxiety and depression.
- Social media amplifies the reach and severity of bullying incidents among young people.
- There is a need for comprehensive intervention strategies targeting both bullies and victims.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective approaches to reduce the negative mental health impacts associated with social media use in adolescents?
