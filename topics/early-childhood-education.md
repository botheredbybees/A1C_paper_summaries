## Early Childhood Education Pedagogies

The provided sources highlight different pedagogic interventions in early childhood education, emphasizing the role of playful and interactive learning. Source 1 emphasizes David Whitebread's contributions to understanding self-regulation and play-based learning as foundational elements for developing executive function skills in young children. Play-based approaches are seen as a means to foster both academic achievement and social-emotional development through hands-on experiences that encourage exploration, problem-solving, and creativity. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the use of puppets as pedagogical tools within the classroom setting, underscoring their potential to create a positive learning environment where children feel more comfortable expressing themselves and engaging in cooperative activities. The review suggests that puppets can be instrumental in facilitating communication generation, promoting a cooperative learning atmosphere, and changing attitudes towards education positively.

## Key Findings

- Play-based learning enhances self-regulation skills in young children.
- Puppets serve as effective pedagogical tools to foster positive classroom climates and cooperative learning among early childhood learners.
- Both play-based approaches and the use of puppets contribute to a supportive educational environment that promotes social-emotional development alongside cognitive growth.

## Open Questions

- How can educators effectively integrate self-regulation practices with the use of pedagogical tools like puppets to enhance learning outcomes in early childhood education?
