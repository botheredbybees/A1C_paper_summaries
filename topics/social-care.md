## Creative Health in Social Care

The concept of creative health emerges as a significant intervention within social care, focusing on integrating arts-based approaches to enhance overall well-being. Both sources highlight the importance of holistic and person-centered care models that utilize creative activities such as arts-based social prescribing to address health inequalities. These interventions aim not only to improve individual outcomes but also to align with broader sustainable development goals by providing cost-effective solutions. However, there is a tension between the theoretical benefits and practical implementation challenges in policy adaptation.

## Key Findings

- Creative health initiatives can help reduce health inequalities through arts-based social prescribing.
- Holistic and person-centered care approaches are central to creative health interventions.
- Integrating creative health into social care policies faces practical implementation hurdles.
