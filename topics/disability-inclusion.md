## Disability Inclusion

The discourse on disability inclusion spans from legal rights and accommodations to practical engagements that promote social participation. Source 1 highlights the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), emphasizing reasonable accommodation as a fundamental right, while also introducing human-rights scrutiny to ensure policies do not inadvertently discriminate against individuals with disabilities. In contrast, Source 2 shifts focus to the operational level through the lens of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) and explores how music engagement can facilitate social inclusion for people with disabilities. This source underscores the use of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) as a tool in understanding neural responses during musical activities, providing insights into therapeutic applications that support disability-inclusion initiatives.

## Key Findings

- Reasonable accommodation is recognized globally through international conventions like CRPD to ensure legal protection and rights for people with disabilities.
- Music engagement offers an accessible intervention method that can enhance social inclusion among individuals with various types of disabilities.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles outlined in the CRPD be more effectively integrated into practical disability-inclusion programs such as those supported by NDIS?
