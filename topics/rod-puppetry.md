## Rod Puppetry

Rod puppetry is an ancient form of theatre that employs rods or sticks to manipulate puppets and create narratives. The practice involves intricate craftsmanship in creating the puppet characters and designing mechanisms for their movement, often with a focus on enhancing expressiveness through subtle manipulations by skilled puppeteers. Across sources, rod puppetry is highlighted as both an art form and a method of storytelling that engages audiences through dynamic performances. However, while there are detailed discussions about the creation and manipulation techniques in one source, another emphasizes its role in cultural expression and education without delving into technical specifics.

## Key Findings

- Rod puppetry involves intricate manipulations to create expressive characters.
- It serves as a method of storytelling with significant cultural and educational value.
