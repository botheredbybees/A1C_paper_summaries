## Medial Orbitofrontal Cortex in Adolescent Music Processing

Research focusing on the medial orbitofrontal cortex (mOFC) during adolescent music processing highlights its role as a crucial reward system. Studies reveal that engagement of mOFC correlates with musical reward sensitivity, indicating it plays a significant role in emotional and pleasure-related responses to music. The ventromedial prefrontal cortex, closely related to the mOFC, also contributes by mediating social and affective processes. While these findings offer insights into the neural mechanisms involved in music appreciation during adolescence, there are tensions regarding how specific neurodevelopmental stages might influence variability in reward sensitivity.

## Key Findings

- The medial orbitofrontal cortex is activated during musical experiences suggesting its role in rewarding aspects of music.
- Engagement of mOFC correlates with individual differences in musical reward sensitivity
- Ventromedial prefrontal cortex works in conjunction with the mOFC, contributing to social and emotional processing

## Open Questions

- How do developmental stages during adolescence affect the engagement and maturation of mOFC?
