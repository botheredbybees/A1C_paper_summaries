## Hermeneutic Phenomenology

The concept of hermeneutic phenomenology is pivotal in understanding how individuals interpret and experience the world around them. This approach, as seen through arts-based research methods such as photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry in aged care settings, allows for a deep exploration of the lived experiences of aging. It emphasizes the importance of personal interpretation and contextual understanding within the broader social framework, providing unique insights into how elderly individuals perceive their reality. By integrating creative arts interventions, hermeneutic phenomenology enables researchers to capture nuanced and multi-layered narratives that traditional research methods might miss.

## Key Findings

- Hermeneutic phenomenology facilitates a deeper understanding of individual lived experiences by focusing on personal interpretation within social contexts.
- Arts-based research techniques such as photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry enhance the richness of data collected in hermeneutic-phenomenological studies.
- Creative arts interventions are effective in uncovering nuanced aspects of aging that traditional research methods may overlook.

## Open Questions

- How can hermeneutic phenomenology be further integrated with other qualitative research methodologies to broaden its applicability and depth?
