## Functional Connectivity In Neurodevelopment

The investigation of functional connectivity in neurodevelopment spans from infancy through adolescence. Two studies offer insights into this process: one examines EEG data to explore how neural integration occurs during the first year of life, specifically for infants at risk for autism spectrum disorder (ASD). This study reveals patterns of functional connectivity that may indicate early developmental differences. Another research focuses on teenagers, highlighting activation in specific brain regions like the medial orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex when exposed to music, suggesting a critical period where neural systems related to reward processing mature and integrate with auditory perception during adolescence. Both studies underscore the importance of functional connectivity assessments for understanding developmental trajectories and neurological conditions.

## Key Findings

- Functional connectivity measures in infants at risk for ASD reveal distinct patterns compared to typically developing peers.
- Adolescent brain responses to music indicate engagement of reward circuitry involving orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex.
- Developmental stages, from early infancy to adolescence, show varying levels and types of neural integration.

## Open Questions

- How do functional connectivity patterns in infants at risk for ASD evolve over time compared to their typically developing peers?
