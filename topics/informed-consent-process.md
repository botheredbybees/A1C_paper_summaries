## Informed Consent Process

The informed consent process in arts-based health interventions is a critical component of research ethics. Sources emphasize the importance of obtaining informed consent from participants to ensure they understand and agree to their involvement, acknowledging potential risks and benefits. This involves more than just legal compliance; it requires ethical considerations that go beyond the traditional understanding of medical procedures or social science studies. The process must be tailored to suit the unique nature of arts-based interventions, which may involve emotional and psychological elements not typically addressed in standard informed consent practices. Professional conduct principles also guide researchers in maintaining transparency, respect for autonomy, and ongoing communication with participants throughout the intervention.

## Key Findings

- The informed consent process is crucial for ethical research involving arts-based health interventions.
- Informed consent should be tailored to account for unique aspects of arts-based activities beyond traditional medical or social science considerations.
- Professional conduct principles support the need for ongoing communication and transparency in obtaining informed consent.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers effectively communicate complex risks and benefits associated with arts-based interventions during the informed consent process?
