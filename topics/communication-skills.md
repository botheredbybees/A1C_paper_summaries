## Communication Skills in Dementia Care

The provided sources discuss interventions aimed at enhancing communication skills for individuals with dementia through visual arts. Two studies highlight the use of a visual arts program to improve engagement, quality of life, and well-being among dementia patients (Source 1 & Source 2). These programs focus not only on tangible outcomes such as self-reported quality of life but also on fostering an environment that encourages communication and cognitive activities. However, while both sources emphasize the positive impact of these visual arts interventions, they do not delve deeply into how specific communication skills are developed or refined through participation in these programs. Source 3 introduces therapeutic photography as another method for improving mental health outcomes such as self-esteem and resilience. Although this approach does address communication indirectly by promoting emotional expression and interpersonal connections, it lacks a direct examination of the mechanisms by which communication skills specifically improve within dementia care.

## Key Findings

- Visual arts programs can enhance quality of life and engagement among individuals with dementia
- Improvement in well-being is associated with participation in visual arts activities for people living with dementia
- Therapeutic photography may contribute to mental health resilience, although direct communication skill development is less emphasized

## Open Questions

- How do specific communication skills evolve through engagement with visual arts and therapeutic photography interventions?
