## Facilitated Creativity

The concept of facilitated creativity in the context of persons with dementia highlights a therapeutic intervention aimed at improving communication and fostering positive participation through non-verbal activities. The sources emphasize how creative interventions can bridge cultural perceptions that often stigmatize or devalue individuals with dementia, instead positioning them as active participants in meaningful social engagement. Facilitated creativity encompasses various methods including visual arts, music, and other sensory-rich experiences that cater to the unique needs of people with dementia, enhancing their quality of life through enhanced communication and emotional expression.

## Key Findings

- Facilitated creativity improves non-verbal communication for individuals with dementia
- Creative interventions can challenge cultural stigmas associated with dementia
- Positive participation in creative activities enhances the quality of life for those with dementia
