## Cognitive Training and Rehabilitation in Dementia Care

The chapter emphasizes the role of both internal and external strategies for managing dementia symptoms. Internal strategies involve enhancing an individual's ability to compensate for cognitive deficits through methods like cognitive training, whereas external memory aids are used as a supportive tool. The integration of these approaches highlights their complementary nature in addressing different aspects of dementia care: internal strategies focus on improving mental functions directly, while external aids assist with practical challenges.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive rehabilitation and internal strategy use can enhance cognitive functioning in individuals with dementia.
- External memory aids complement internal strategies by providing practical support for daily living activities.
- The combination of both internal and external methods offers a comprehensive approach to managing the symptoms of dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do the effects of cognitive training differ between early-stage and advanced stages of dementia?
