## Synaptic Pruning in Adolescent Development

Synaptic pruning is a critical process during adolescence that significantly impacts brain development and functionality. Across the provided sources, synaptic pruning is consistently identified as a key mechanism contributing to both structural changes and functional improvements within the adolescent brain. It involves the selective elimination of unused or inefficient synapses, thereby enhancing neural efficiency through processes such as myelination and reorganization of neural circuits. This refinement not only supports the maturation of higher cognitive functions but also plays a role in shaping behavioral patterns associated with decision-making and identity formation. However, while synaptic pruning is widely acknowledged for its positive effects on brain development, there are tensions regarding how socioeconomic factors might influence this process, potentially creating disparities in developmental outcomes across different populations.

## Key Findings

- Synaptic pruning enhances neural efficiency through selective elimination of synapses.
- Myelination and reorganization of neural circuits support maturation of higher cognitive functions.
- Identity formation and decision-making are influenced by synaptic pruning during adolescence.

## Open Questions
