## Randomized Controlled Trials

The provided source highlights the utilization of Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) to evaluate the efficacy of Child-Centered Play Therapy Intervention on children diagnosed with Autism. This study emphasizes the importance of empirical evidence in assessing therapeutic interventions, particularly through measuring changes in brain EEG activity. The findings suggest that CCPT can influence neurobiological markers associated with autism, thereby enhancing clinical understanding and treatment approaches for this population.

## Key Findings

- RCTs provide robust evidence for evaluating the efficacy of therapeutic interventions like Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) on neurological outcomes in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).
- Neurobiological changes reflected through brain EEG activity indicate potential mechanisms by which CCPT impacts symptomatology and adaptive functioning in ASD.
- Integration of neuroscientific methods into RCTs enhances the validity and generalizability of clinical intervention research.

## Open Questions

- Further investigation is required to elucidate the specific neurological pathways influenced by CCPT, as well as how these changes correlate with long-term behavioral outcomes in children with ASD.
