## Resilience to Stress Through Art

The synthesis explores the role of art, specifically visual art production and cognitive evaluation, in enhancing resilience to stress. Studies indicate that engaging with artistic activities can modify functional brain connectivity within the default-mode network (DMN), a neural system involved in introspective thought and self-referential processing. The alteration of DMN connectivity through visual art creation is linked to improved mental health outcomes by fostering adaptive coping strategies against psychological stressors. However, there remains debate on whether cognitive evaluation of art alone can yield similar physiological benefits as the act of creating it. This discrepancy suggests potential variability in neural responses and resilience-building mechanisms between active participation versus passive appreciation of artworks.

## Key Findings

- Engaging with visual art production influences default-mode network connectivity.
- Cognitive evaluation of visual art may have differential effects compared to producing artwork.
- Both creating and evaluating art can contribute to building resilience against stress.

## Open Questions

- To what extent does passive engagement in the appreciation of art impact neural networks involved in resilience?
