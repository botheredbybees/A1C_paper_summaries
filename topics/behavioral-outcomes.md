## Behavioral Outcomes in Therapeutic Interventions

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights diverse behavioral outcomes from different therapeutic interventions. Source 1 explores the impact of Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) on children with autism, demonstrating how this therapy influences brain EEG activity in a randomized controlled trial. This study provides empirical evidence for the neurological benefits associated with CCPT, suggesting improvements that extend beyond observable behaviors to changes at a neural level. On the other hand, Source 2 focuses on the therapeutic utility of medical clowns in hospital settings, aiming to elevate patient satisfaction and reduce aggressive tendencies among both pediatric and adult patients. While this intervention does not target specific neurological outcomes like CCPT, it underscores the broader psychological and social impacts of engaging therapies, emphasizing their role in enhancing emotional well-being and reducing negative behavioral expressions.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy positively influences brain EEG activity in children with autism.
- Medical clowns contribute to increased patient satisfaction in hospital settings.
- Both interventions reduce aggressive tendencies in patients.
