## Locus Of Control

The concept of locus of control is explored across two sources which focus on the relationship between fine arts participation and personal empowerment among individuals with developmental disabilities. Both studies emphasize the connection between internal locus of control, self-determination, and active involvement in the fine arts as a means to enhance autonomy and independence. They highlight that engaging in creative activities can foster a sense of agency and responsibility, thereby encouraging an internal rather than external orientation towards life events and challenges. This perspective underscores the role of intrinsic motivation in shaping one's locus of control, suggesting that individuals who perceive themselves as having more influence over their lives are likely to participate actively in fine arts programs and benefit from enhanced self-determination.

## Key Findings

- Fine arts participation is linked to an internal locus of control.
- Self-determination theory underpins the relationship between personal agency and creative engagement.
- Individuals with developmental disabilities who have a greater sense of self-determination are more likely to engage in fine arts activities.

## Open Questions

- What role does external support play in enabling individuals with developmental disabilities to achieve an internal locus of control through fine arts participation?
