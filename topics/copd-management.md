## Singing as a COPD Management Tool

Both sources highlight singing as an intervention for individuals with Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD). The first source, 'Singing for Better Breathing - Transcript', emphasizes the role of singing in improving quality of life and fostering social engagement among COPD patients. In contrast, the second source, 'Singing for Better Breathing Project', focuses on respiratory function assessment as a critical aspect of managing COPD through singing. Despite the differing emphases, both sources acknowledge singing's potential to enhance physical and psychological well-being in COPD management.

## Key Findings

- Singing is beneficial for individuals with COPD due to its impact on quality of life.
- Respiratory function assessment plays a significant role in evaluating the effectiveness of singing as an intervention.
- Social engagement through group singing activities can improve mental health and well-being among COPD patients.
