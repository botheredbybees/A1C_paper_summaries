## Disability-Led Theatre

The importance of disability-led theatre is highlighted across sources as a significant method for promoting societal understanding and inclusivity. This form of theatre not only fosters an environment where disabled individuals can express themselves freely but also challenges the traditional narratives that often marginalize this population. Sources underscore the critical role such performances play in building confidence among performers, thereby contributing to a broader shift towards more inclusive representations within society. However, there is a tension noted regarding how disability-led theatre can effectively reach wider audiences and influence societal attitudes on a larger scale.

## Key Findings

- Disability-led theatre plays a crucial role in promoting inclusivity by allowing disabled individuals to express themselves authentically.
- Confidence-building among participants through involvement in disability-led theatre is noted as one of its core benefits.
- Societal understanding of disabilities is enhanced through exposure to authentic narratives from within the community.

## Open Questions

- How can disability-led theatre be effectively scaled and promoted to reach wider, non-disabled audiences?
