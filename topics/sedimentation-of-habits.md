## Sedimentation Of Habits

The concept of the 'sedimentation of habits' emerges as a critical lens for understanding personhood and ethical care in dementia contexts. Sources emphasize how personal identity is deeply embedded within habitual practices that have accumulated over time, shaping an individual's sense of self through tacit knowledge (understood implicitly rather than explicitly). This process underscores the importance of respecting bodily autonomy and recognizing the continuity of self despite cognitive decline. The sedimentation of habits thus provides a framework for approaching dementia care with sensitivity to each person’s unique history and routines, ensuring that interventions support rather than disrupt established patterns of behavior.

## Key Findings

- The sedimentation of habits is fundamental in shaping an individual's sense of self over time
- Respecting bodily autonomy in the context of dementia care involves acknowledging the importance of tacit knowledge and existing habitual behaviors
- Understanding personhood through the lens of habit sedimentation can guide ethical approaches to care

## Open Questions
