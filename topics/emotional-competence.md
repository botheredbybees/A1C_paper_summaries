## Emotional Competence

The concept of emotional competence is a central theme in the discourse on psychological well-being and public health. Sources discuss its multifaceted nature, emphasizing both the cognitive and physiological dimensions involved in managing emotions effectively. Emotional competence includes skills such as recognizing and labeling one's own emotions, understanding the impact of these emotions on thinking and behavior, and regulating emotional responses to promote mental health. Physiological mechanisms, like amygdala function and mirror neurons, play crucial roles in how individuals perceive and respond to emotional stimuli. These physiological processes influence social interactions and contribute to broader public health outcomes by facilitating empathy and positive relationships within communities.

## Key Findings

- Emotional competence is a multifaceted construct that integrates cognitive, behavioral, and physiological aspects of emotion regulation.
- Amygdala function and mirror neurons are critical for understanding the physiological underpinnings of emotional competence.
- Cultural activities and creative arts can enhance public health by fostering emotional competence among individuals.
