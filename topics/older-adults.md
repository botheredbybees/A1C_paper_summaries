## Falls Prevention in Older Adults

The topic of falls prevention among older adults is addressed through two distinct but complementary lenses. Source 1 examines the role of participatory arts and creative ageing initiatives, focusing on men's engagement in these activities. While it does not directly address fall prevention measures, it highlights the importance of community-based interventions that enhance social inclusion and mental health—factors indirectly beneficial to overall well-being and potentially to physical balance and coordination. In contrast, Source 2 provides a systematic review and meta-analysis of dance interventions specifically aimed at reducing falls risk in older adults. This source underscores the efficacy of structured dance programs as an effective intervention method, demonstrating improvements in functional outcomes such as balance and gait, which are crucial for preventing falls. The synthesis reveals a convergence on community-based activities promoting health and well-being among older adults; however, while Source 1 lacks specific focus on fall prevention strategies, it indirectly supports the broader goal of enhancing quality of life and possibly physical functioning through social engagement and creative expression.

## Key Findings

- Participatory arts programs can improve mental health and social inclusion in older adult men.
- Structured dance interventions are effective for falls prevention by improving balance and gait.
- Both community-based activities contribute to the overall well-being of older adults, albeit with different focuses and mechanisms of action.

## Open Questions

- How do participatory arts programs, which may improve mental health and social connectedness, indirectly impact physical fall risk factors in older adults?
