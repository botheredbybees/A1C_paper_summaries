## Arts in Health Model

The synthesis integrates findings from sources that discuss the historical development and current practices of incorporating arts into healthcare environments. While there is a consensus on the importance of tailored therapeutic spaces to enhance patient experiences, diverging views emerge about whether a singular 'arts in health' model exists or if interventions should be contextually specific. Sources highlight improvements in clinical outcomes, such as reduced anxiety and pain levels, attributed to artistic activities within healthcare settings. However, there is an acknowledgment that the effectiveness of arts-based practices may vary depending on cultural contexts and patient needs.

## Key Findings

- Arts integration into healthcare environments can lead to improved patient well-being by reducing symptoms like anxiety and pain.
- There is debate over whether a universal 'arts in health' model exists or if interventions should be tailored to specific contexts.
- The historical development of arts-based practices in healthcare shows an evolution from decorative art to active engagement with artistic activities.

## Open Questions

- Is there sufficient evidence for the efficacy of arts-based therapies across different cultural and clinical settings?
