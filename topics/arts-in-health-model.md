## Arts In Health Model

The concept of an 'arts in health' model has been explored through various lenses, including historical development and the creation of tailored therapeutic environments within healthcare settings. Sources highlight the importance of integrating arts activities into medical facilities to enhance patient well-being and improve clinical outcomes. However, there is a notable absence of a universally accepted framework or definition for what constitutes an 'arts in health' model, which has led to varying interpretations and implementations across different contexts. Tailoring interventions to specific needs highlights the complexity of applying such models effectively.

## Key Findings

- The integration of arts activities into healthcare settings enhances patient well-being.
- There is no universally accepted definition or framework for an 'arts in health' model.
- Tailored therapeutic environments are crucial for effective implementation of arts-based interventions.

## Open Questions

- What specific criteria define the efficacy and adaptability of an 'arts in health' intervention?
