## Gene Expression Dysregulation in Autism Spectrum Disorder

The study of gene expression dysregulation across the cerebral cortex highlights significant disruptions in genetic activity linked to autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The research indicates that broad transcriptomic alterations are present not only in specific brain regions but also widely across the cerebral cortex, suggesting a pervasive and complex pattern of gene regulation disturbances. These findings contribute to understanding ASD's neurobiological basis, emphasizing that dysregulation impacts multiple genes involved in synaptic function, neural development, and neuronal connectivity.

## Key Findings

- Broad transcriptomic dysregulation is observed across the cerebral cortex in individuals with autism spectrum disorder.
- Genetic alterations in ASD are not limited to specific brain regions but show a widespread pattern of gene expression changes.
- Dysregulated genes often involve pathways critical for neural development and synaptic function.

## Open Questions

- What mechanisms underlie the pervasive dysregulation of gene expression observed across cerebral cortex regions in autism spectrum disorder?
