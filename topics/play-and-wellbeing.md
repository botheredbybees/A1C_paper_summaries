## Play and Wellbeing

The concept of play as a therapeutic intervention for enhancing wellbeing is explored across various life stages and conditions. Source 1 discusses the importance of play in promoting health throughout the lifespan, emphasizing its role particularly in mitigating stress and fostering work-life balance during adult years. It also touches on the idea that older adults (super-aging individuals) can experience significant benefits from playful activities in terms of mental health and quality of life. Meanwhile, Source 2 focuses more narrowly on play's impact within a neurodiversity framework, suggesting that engaging in creative arts and play activities can support mental wellbeing for those with autism or other developmental conditions. Both sources highlight the transformative power of play as an intervention but differ slightly in their target populations: one broadens the scope to include all ages while the other is more specific about integrating play into disability support.

## Key Findings

- Play activities can enhance mental health and work-life balance for adults across various life stages.
- Creative arts and playful engagement contribute significantly to the wellbeing of individuals with neurodiverse conditions such as autism.

## Open Questions

- How do the therapeutic benefits of play vary between typical aging populations and those who experience super-aging, particularly in terms of mental health?
