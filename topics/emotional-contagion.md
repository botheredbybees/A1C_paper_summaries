## Emotional Contagion in Music

The concept of emotional contagion in music is explored across multiple sources, highlighting its significance in the transmission and experience of emotions through auditory means. Source 1 establishes a correlation between empathy and being moved by sad music, indicating that individuals with high trait empathy are more susceptible to experiencing emotional contagion from musical stimuli. This supports the idea that emotional contagion can be both a cause and consequence of empathy. Sources 2 and 4 delve into the mechanisms underlying emotional responses to music, such as episodic memory retrieval (Source 2) and rhythmic entrainment (Source 4). These processes are integral to how listeners internalize and share emotions through musical engagement. Furthermore, Source 3 introduces the concept of earworms, suggesting that repetitive musical patterns can induce prolonged states of emotional contagion beyond immediate exposure to music. Collectively, these sources underscore the multifaceted nature of emotional contagion in music, encompassing psychological traits like empathy, cognitive processes related to memory and expectation, as well as physical phenomena such as rhythm.

## Key Findings

- High trait empathy is associated with increased susceptibility to experiencing emotional contagion from sad music.
- Musical expectancy and episodic memory play crucial roles in the mechanism of emotional responses to music.
- Rhythmic entrainment facilitates the synchronization of body movements with musical rhythms, enhancing the experience of shared emotions.

## Open Questions
