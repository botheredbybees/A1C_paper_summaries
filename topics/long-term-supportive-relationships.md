## Long-Term Supportive Relationships

The topic of long-term supportive relationships is explored through the lens of Sistema Scotland's Big Noise program, which has been operating in several locations across Scotland for five years. The evaluation of this initiative highlights its transformative effects on young people, particularly those from disadvantaged backgrounds. The program aims to address health inequalities by fostering sustained and nurturing connections between participants and their communities. Over time, these relationships have shown potential to positively impact youth development, resilience building, and overall well-being. However, there are tensions regarding the scalability of such personalized support structures within broader educational systems that typically prioritize standardized outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Long-term supportive relationships foster transformative effects on youth.
- Such relationships can help address health inequalities in disadvantaged communities.
- The Big Noise program demonstrates positive developmental impacts through sustained community engagement.

## Open Questions

- How scalable are long-term supportive relationship programs within broader educational systems?
