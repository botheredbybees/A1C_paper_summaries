## Youth Mental Health and Climate Change

The Mission Australia Youth Survey Information and the Youth Survey Report 2025 both highlight significant concerns among young people regarding their mental health. These surveys underscore that factors such as social media impact, bullying, cost of living, and school-related stress contribute to heightened anxiety and depression rates. While not directly addressing climate change, these findings are pertinent given that environmental issues can also significantly affect youth mental health, thereby connecting the broader context of societal concerns including ecological distress to young people's wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Social media impact and bullying exacerbate mental health challenges among young Australians.
- Cost of living and school-related stress are primary contributors to mental health issues in youth surveys.
- Youth mental health is a multifaceted issue influenced by various societal factors.

## Open Questions

- How do environmental concerns, such as climate change, intersect with the identified social and economic pressures affecting youth mental health?
