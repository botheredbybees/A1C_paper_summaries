## Ethnographic Process Evaluations

The provided sources emphasize the importance of ethnographic process evaluations in understanding and assessing interventions such as arts in health. These evaluations provide a detailed account of how an intervention unfolds over time, capturing qualitative insights that are essential for comprehensive outcome assessment. However, there is variability in how these evaluations are conducted across different contexts, suggesting a need for standardization to ensure consistency in reporting results. Despite this tension, the sources agree on the value of ethnographic methods for gaining deeper understanding into the implementation and impact of arts-based interventions.

## Key Findings

- Ethnographic process evaluations provide rich qualitative data about how an intervention unfolds.
- Outcome assessment benefits from the detailed insights gained through ethnography.
- There is a need to standardize reporting in ethnographic studies to ensure consistency across contexts.
