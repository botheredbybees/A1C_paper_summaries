## Bio-Psycho-Social Model Of Dementia Care

The Bio-Psycho-Social Model of Dementia Care emphasizes a holistic approach to treating individuals with dementia by considering biological, psychological, and social factors. This model is distinct in its multi-faceted treatment strategy compared to traditional medical models that primarily focus on the physiological aspects of dementia. Sources highlight the importance of understanding both explicit and implicit memory functions as they pertain to patient care under this model. Additionally, there's a significant emphasis placed on recognizing environmental influences and social interactions as crucial components affecting the quality of life for patients with dementia. This integration of various disciplines aims at providing comprehensive support systems that cater not just to the physical but also to the emotional needs of individuals suffering from dementia.

## Key Findings

- The Bio-Psycho-Social Model of Dementia Care addresses biological, psychological, and social dimensions of patient care.
- Understanding explicit and implicit memory functions is crucial for effective management under this model.
- Malignant social psychology highlights the negative impact of certain social dynamics on dementia patients.
