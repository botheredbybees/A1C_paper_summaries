## Language Use in Dementia Care

The use of appropriate and respectful language when discussing or caring for individuals with dementia is a critical aspect of their care. Both sources emphasize the importance of person-first language, which prioritizes identifying an individual as a person before any condition they may have, thereby emphasizing their inherent value beyond the disease state. This approach aims to reduce stigma associated with dementia by promoting non-stigmatizing communication that considers cultural sensitivity and nuances in different communities. The impact of language on public perception is also highlighted; using specific terminology can influence how society views people living with dementia, potentially affecting areas such as policy making, research funding, and individual attitudes toward care provision.

## Key Findings

- Person-first language promotes dignity and reduces stigma for individuals with dementia.
- Non-stigmatizing communication in dementia care should consider cultural context and sensitivity.
