## Dance as a Fall Prevention Strategy

Both sources explore dance as an intervention to reduce falls in older adults. Source 1 conducts a systematic review and meta-analysis, concluding that various forms of dance can effectively improve balance and functional capacity, thus reducing fall risk. It integrates findings from multiple studies to provide quantitative evidence supporting the efficacy of dance interventions. In contrast, Source 2 presents a cluster-randomized controlled trial specifically evaluating social dancing as a fall prevention strategy. While it aligns with the broader conclusion that dance improves balance and reduces falls, this study offers more detailed insights into the implementation and impact of social dancing programs in community settings. However, both sources highlight methodological differences—such as varying types of dances used and assessment metrics for measuring balance improvement—which suggest a need for further investigation to pinpoint optimal intervention designs.

## Key Findings

- Dance interventions effectively improve balance and reduce fall risk in older adults
- Both systematic review/meta-analysis and randomized controlled trial support the efficacy of dance as an intervention
- Social dancing offers unique benefits, such as enhanced social engagement, that may contribute to its effectiveness

## Open Questions

- What specific types of dances are most effective for fall prevention in older adults?
