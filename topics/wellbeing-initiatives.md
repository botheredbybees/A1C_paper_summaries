## Museums and Wellbeing Initiatives

The integration of visual arts and cultural interventions within museum settings to promote community wellbeing has emerged as a significant approach in recent years. Sources highlight the Happy Museum Project, which emphasizes well-being promotion through environmental sustainability (Source 2). This initiative is complemented by other projects that foster social connections and utilize sensory-friendly spaces (Sources 1 & 4), addressing diverse needs within communities. Museums are increasingly recognized for their therapeutic value, with activities designed to impact health and wellbeing in holistic manners, considering the social determinants of health (Source 3). The Beaney Health and Wellbeing Toolkit exemplifies how museums can serve as facilitators of such interventions through structured programs aimed at improving societal health (Source 6). A shared theme is the interdisciplinary approach required for effective museum-based wellness initiatives, incorporating elements like inclusive arts and a social model of disability to cater broadly to all community members' needs (Sources 5 & 7).

## Key Findings

- Museums serve as valuable platforms for promoting community wellbeing through cultural interventions.
- Environmental sustainability is an integral aspect of successful museum-based wellbeing initiatives.
- Inclusive and sensory-friendly spaces enhance the accessibility and effectiveness of health-related activities in museums.

## Open Questions

- How can museums effectively balance their role in promoting individual wellness with broader societal health impacts?
