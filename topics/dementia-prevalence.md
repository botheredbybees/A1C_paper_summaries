## Prevalence and Risk Factors for Dementia

The sources collectively underscore the significant global challenge posed by dementia, with a focus on understanding its prevalence and associated risk factors. Sources highlight that Alzheimer's disease is one of the most common forms of dementia, alongside vascular dementia. There are notable disparities in the prevalence of dementia among different populations, particularly between Indigenous Australians and other demographic groups. These differences may be influenced by various socio-economic and cultural factors. Modifiable risk factors for dementia have been identified as a key area for intervention to potentially reduce incidence rates; however, effective strategies require tailored approaches that account for cultural nuances and address health disparities. The studies indicate the need for national action plans to combat dementia, with special attention given to Indigenous communities.

## Key Findings

- Alzheimer's disease is one of the leading causes of dementia globally.
- Dementia prevalence varies significantly between populations, especially in Indigenous Australian groups.
- Modifiable risk factors are crucial for potential prevention and reduction strategies.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective culturally appropriate interventions to reduce dementia prevalence among disadvantaged communities?
