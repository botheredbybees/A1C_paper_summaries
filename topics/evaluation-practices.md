## Evaluation Practices In Arts And Health

The integration of arts and health initiatives necessitates a methodological approach that balances the needs and perspectives of various stakeholders. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of co-production in evaluations, suggesting that it not only enhances credibility but also facilitates a deeper understanding among participants. This collaborative evaluation practice involves active stakeholder engagement which enriches the evaluation process by incorporating diverse viewpoints and insights. However, such an approach presents challenges in ensuring that all voices are equally heard and valued within the evaluation framework.

## Key Findings

- Co-production in evaluations of arts and health initiatives enhances credibility and inclusivity.
- Active stakeholder engagement is crucial for effective co-production in evaluative practices.
- Incorporating diverse perspectives through collaboration can enrich evaluation processes.

## Open Questions

- How to ensure equitable representation and active participation from all stakeholders during the evaluation process?
