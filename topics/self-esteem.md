## Self-Esteem Enhancement Programs

The sources highlight the role of various creative interventions in enhancing self-esteem among different populations. Dance and drama activities are effective for adolescents and young Black men, respectively, by building critical awareness, safe spaces, and peer trust while improving physical health metrics like aerobic capacity and upper body strength. In contrast, a pilot study on individuals with multiple sclerosis underscores the positive impact of creative art programs on self-esteem, alongside hope, perceived social support, and self-efficacy. These interventions offer diverse pathways to boosting self-esteem but cater to specific demographic needs and health conditions.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions enhance self-esteem among various populations
- Physical activities like dance improve physical fitness metrics in adolescents
- Drama-based programs foster critical awareness and peer trust in young Black men
- Art therapy positively impacts multiple aspects of well-being for individuals with MS

## Open Questions

- How do different creative interventions cater to specific demographic needs, and what are their limitations?
