## Play Therapy for Trauma in Children

The integration of child-centered play therapy and gestalt play therapy addresses the treatment needs of children suffering from trauma. Both therapies emphasize the importance of non-directive approaches that foster a safe environment for self-expression and exploration, which is crucial for healing from traumatic experiences. Research indicates these methods are effective in reducing trauma-related symptoms by enhancing children's self-regulation abilities. However, there remains a need to explore how different play therapy techniques can be adapted or combined to better address specific aspects of childhood trauma such as adverse childhood experiences (ACEs).

## Key Findings

- Both child-centered and gestalt play therapies are effective interventions for treating trauma in children.
- Non-directive therapeutic approaches provide a safe space that encourages the expression and processing of traumatic experiences.
- Improvement in self-regulation is a key outcome observed across various forms of play therapy.

## Open Questions

- How can different types of play therapies be integrated to address specific aspects of childhood trauma, such as adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)?
