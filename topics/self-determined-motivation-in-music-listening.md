## Self-Determined Motivation in Music Listening

The concept of self-determined motivation in music listening is explored through its connection to global happiness and subjective well-being. Source 1 emphasizes the association between intrinsic motivation for music listening and positive outcomes such as increased life satisfaction among late adolescents, suggesting that engaging with music autonomously contributes significantly to a sense of fulfillment and contentment. In contrast, Source 2 delves into broader theories concerning subjective well-being (SWB) and mental health, introducing concepts like hedonia and the broaden-and-build theory which describe how positive emotions can counteract negative ones through mechanisms such as the undo hypothesis. Despite differences in focus, both sources align on the importance of intrinsic motivation for enhancing emotional resilience and SWB, indicating that music listening motivated by personal interest rather than external pressures plays a crucial role in psychological health.

## Key Findings

- Intrinsic motivation in music listening is linked to higher subjective well-being and happiness among young adults.
- Theories like broaden-and-build provide context for understanding how positive emotional experiences from self-determined activities can mitigate stress and enhance mental resilience.

## Open Questions

- How does the quality of intrinsic motivation impact the efficacy of music listening as a tool for promoting subjective well-being?
