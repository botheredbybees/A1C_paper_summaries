## Scoping Reviews of Arts Therapies

This cross-source synthesis focuses on the application of arts therapies as an intervention for various populations including those with eating disorders and children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The first two sources, which are identical in content but distinct in reference, emphasize the use of art therapy to improve outcomes among individuals suffering from eating disorders. They explore the effectiveness of different forms of arts therapies such as music, dance/movement, drama, and visual arts therapy in the context of eating disorder treatment. In contrast, Source 3 looks at a specific subset of patients—children with autism spectrum disorder—and explores how creative art interventions might support their development in areas like social interaction and performance skills. Both sets of sources highlight the potential benefits of these therapies but also underscore the need for more research to fully understand their efficacy and optimal implementation.

## Key Findings

- Arts therapies can improve therapeutic outcomes for individuals with eating disorders.
- Creative arts interventions may support social interaction and performance skills in children with autism spectrum disorder.
- There is a need for further research into the effectiveness of arts therapies across various populations.

## Open Questions

- How do different forms of arts therapies vary in their efficacy for treating specific conditions, such as eating disorders versus ASD?
