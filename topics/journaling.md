## Journaling as a Creative Practice

The practice of journaling is highlighted in both sources as an integral part of creative and critical thinking processes. Source 1 outlines the guidelines for journaling and visual research, emphasizing its role in fostering divergent and critical thinking among participants. Journal entries serve not only as a personal documentation tool but also as a medium to explore ideas freely, critique thoughts, and develop insights through reflection. Meanwhile, Source 2 discusses how journals are used during the editing phase of photo essays. Here, journaling is shown to facilitate peer feedback and caption refinement by providing context for images that may have been recorded in initial journal entries, enhancing collaborative understanding and project coherence.

## Key Findings

- Journaling promotes divergent thinking through reflective practice
- Journals are integral to the editing process of creative projects like photo essays
- Peer feedback is enhanced by the contextual information provided in journals

## Open Questions

- How can journaling practices be further integrated into other disciplines for broader applications?
