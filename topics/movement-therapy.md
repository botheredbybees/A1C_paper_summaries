## Dance and Movement Therapy

The integration of dance and movement into therapeutic practices has been shown to positively influence the management of dementia. Source one highlights a randomized controlled study focusing on visuospatial abilities, planning skills, and self-care ability through dance/movement therapy. This intervention demonstrated significant improvements in patients undergoing such treatments compared to control groups, as measured by the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE). Simultaneously, source two discusses projects aimed at enhancing social engagement and cognitive stimulation among dementia patients using movement-based activities, indicating a broader scope of benefits beyond just individual skill enhancement. Lastly, source three delves into the theoretical underpinnings of dance/movement therapy as an interdisciplinary field that combines artistic expression with scientific understanding to offer psychological interventions. This synthesis reveals a consensus across sources on the efficacy and multiple dimensions of benefits derived from incorporating movement therapies in dementia care.

## Key Findings

- Dance and movement therapy can improve visuospatial abilities, planning skills, and self-care ability in individuals with dementia.
- Social engagement and cognitive stimulation are key components of dance/movement therapy for dementia patients.
- The interdisciplinary nature of dance/movement therapy encompasses both artistic expression and scientific understanding.

## Open Questions
