## Arts-Based Intervention

The integration of arts-based interventions as a therapeutic tool highlights their multifaceted benefits across diverse populations. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of person-centered care and creative expression, advocating for flexible evaluation criteria that go beyond traditional metrics to capture holistic well-being improvements. Conversely, Source 2 delves into a specific case study where an Arts-Based Psychosocial Training program was implemented post-Yolanda typhoon in the Philippines, employing Consensual Qualitative Research methodologies and the Training of Trainers model to facilitate recovery through creative processes. Both sources underscore the necessity for adaptable evaluation frameworks that can effectively measure qualitative improvements such as enhanced emotional resilience and social cohesion, reinforcing the role of arts-based practices in fostering comprehensive healing.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions offer a flexible framework for promoting person-centered care and enhancing well-being through creative expression.
- Qualitative research methodologies are essential for capturing the nuanced benefits of arts-based therapeutic approaches.
- Training initiatives that empower local communities to lead recovery efforts can significantly amplify the impact of arts-based psychosocial support.

## Open Questions

- How can evaluation criteria be further refined to accurately reflect the multifaceted outcomes of arts-based interventions in diverse cultural contexts?
