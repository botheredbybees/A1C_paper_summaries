## Child-Centered Play Therapy and Puppet Use in Autism Interventions

This literature review synthesizes findings from interventions aimed at supporting children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) through play therapy and puppet use. Source 1 highlights the effectiveness of child-centered play therapy (CCPT) as a therapeutic approach, emphasizing its ability to enhance social communication skills in ASD children without verbalizing their needs directly. Conversely, Source 2 explores how puppets can serve as educational tools, fostering positive classroom climates and cooperative learning environments. Both sources agree on the importance of non-verbal communication techniques in engaging children with ASD but differ in specific methodologies used—CCPT versus puppetry interventions. While both approaches aim to improve social interaction skills among these children, there is a gap in understanding how these methods integrate or complement each other within a comprehensive intervention strategy.

## Key Findings

- Both child-centered play therapy and the use of puppets are effective non-verbal communication tools for engaging children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD)
- Each method aims to enhance social interaction skills among ASD children, though through different approaches
- There is a need for more research on how these methods can be integrated or used in combination

## Open Questions

- How might child-centered play therapy and the use of puppets as pedagogical tools complement each other in intervention strategies?
