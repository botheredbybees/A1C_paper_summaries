## Music Therapy in Palliative Care

The integration of music therapy into palliative care settings aims to enhance the quality of life for patients and their families during end-of-life stages. Source One outlines a qualitative study conducted at a cancer hospital that utilized grounded theory and thematic analysis to understand how music therapy experiences impact patient wellbeing. It highlights the emotional, spiritual, and psychological benefits accruing from these interventions. In contrast, Source Two presents a randomized controlled trial protocol called 'Song of Life' (SOL), which aims to scientifically assess the efficacy of music therapy in improving multiple facets of quality of life among palliative care recipients. Both studies indicate that music therapy offers a holistic approach to managing symptoms and fostering emotional resilience. However, there is less emphasis on physical aspects or non-musical art forms such as dance/movement therapy discussed in Source Three, despite their potential complementary benefits.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances quality of life for patients undergoing palliative care by addressing emotional, spiritual, and psychological needs.
- Randomized controlled trials are necessary to establish the efficacy and scope of music therapy interventions in palliative settings.
- Qualitative approaches like grounded theory provide nuanced understanding of patient experiences with therapeutic music.

## Open Questions

- How can dance/movement therapies be integrated alongside music therapy to further enhance holistic care in palliative contexts?
