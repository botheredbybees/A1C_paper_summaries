## Community Choir Singing

The synthesis integrates findings from a systematic review of the mental health and wellbeing outcomes associated with group singing for adults with mental health conditions. This intervention highlights how community choir singing can significantly influence mental health, providing psychological benefits such as reduced anxiety, improved mood, and enhanced self-esteem among participants. While the reviewed studies predominantly focus on adult populations with pre-existing mental health conditions, they collectively underscore the therapeutic potential of communal music activities like choirs in fostering social support networks and reducing symptoms associated with various mental illnesses. However, there is a noted gap in research concerning the long-term efficacy of such interventions and their applicability across diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Community choir singing can enhance mental health outcomes by reducing anxiety and improving mood.
- Participation in choirs fosters social support networks that are beneficial for individuals with mental health conditions.
- The therapeutic benefits of group singing extend to improved self-esteem and overall wellbeing.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term efficacy of community choir singing on mental health outcomes?
