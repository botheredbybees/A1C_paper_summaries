## Depression in Adolescents

The sources provided address the intervention of depression among adolescents. Source 1 focuses on the mental health concerns prevalent among young people, including depression and anxiety, while highlighting the importance of identity exploration as a protective environment against these conditions. This study emphasizes various intervention strategies that aim to support adolescent mental health through fostering resilience and coping mechanisms.

In contrast, Source 2 delves into art therapy as an effective treatment for psychological trauma in adults, but also includes findings relevant to depression management. Although this source focuses primarily on adult populations, the principles of art therapy could be extrapolated to adolescents suffering from depression or trauma-related conditions. The integration of creative interventions like art therapy can potentially offer a novel approach to adolescent mental health care.

While both sources discuss intervention strategies for managing depression in different age groups, there is an implied potential for cross-application between the adolescent and adult contexts.

## Key Findings

- Depression and anxiety are significant mental health issues among adolescents.
- Intervention strategies focusing on identity exploration can serve as protective environments against depression in adolescence.
- Creative interventions such as art therapy may be effective in managing depression across different age groups.

## Open Questions

- How effectively can intervention strategies designed for adult populations, like art therapy, be adapted and applied to adolescent mental health care?
