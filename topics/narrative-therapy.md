## Narrative Therapy

Narrative therapy is a therapeutic approach aimed at helping individuals address and overcome personal issues by reconceptualizing them within the framework of their life stories. The sources indicate that this intervention utilizes diverse methods to externalize problems from an individual's identity, including memory trees and boxes for sensory engagement (Source 1), photo elicitation through therapeutic photography (Source 2), and puppetry with children (Source 3). Each method serves to facilitate a dialogic process where clients can see their issues as separate entities rather than intrinsic parts of themselves. This externalization is pivotal in developing resilience and enhancing self-esteem, as it allows individuals to reclaim their personal narratives from the constraints imposed by problematic aspects.

## Key Findings

- Narrative therapy involves techniques such as memory trees, boxes, photo elicitation, and puppetry to help clients externalize problems.
- These methods enable individuals to engage with their issues in a dialogic manner, facilitating healing and self-understanding.
- Externalization of problems is crucial for enhancing resilience and self-esteem in narrative therapy.

## Open Questions
