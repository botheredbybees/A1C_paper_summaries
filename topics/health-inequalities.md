## Health Inequalities and Creative Health Interventions

The concept of health inequalities encompasses disparities in health outcomes and access to resources due to social determinants. Across the provided sources, creative health interventions emerge as a promising approach to mitigate these inequalities through holistic methods that often include arts-based practices such as social prescribing and long-term supportive relationships with youth. Creative Health initiatives aim at equitable distribution of resources and are supported by frameworks like Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). However, effective implementation requires considerations around cost-effectiveness and the sustainability of interventions over time. Evaluations from real-world applications, such as Sistema Scotland’s Big Noise centers in various Scottish cities, highlight transformative effects on youth but also emphasize the need for sustained support to achieve long-term impacts.

## Key Findings

- Creative health initiatives can address social determinants of health contributing to inequalities
- Cost-effectiveness analysis is crucial for the sustainable implementation of creative health interventions
- Long-term supportive relationships are pivotal in achieving transformative effects on youth

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective mechanisms to ensure equitable access to creative health resources across diverse populations?
