## Color Psychology

The concept of color psychology is explored through the integration of various therapeutic and conceptual frameworks. Source 1 highlights the use of therapeutic self-portraiture in trauma recovery, incorporating Jungian archetypes and color symbolism to facilitate emotional healing. This approach leverages color psychology by using hues and tones that resonate with the individual's subjective experience to evoke psychological responses and promote a narrative of recovery.

## Key Findings

- Color psychology is utilized across therapeutic practices to influence mood and emotion.
- The integration of Jungian archetypes enhances the symbolic significance of colors in self-portraiture therapy.
- Therapeutic photography, including self-portraiture, serves as a medium for expressing complex emotions related to trauma.
