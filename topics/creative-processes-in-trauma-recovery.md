## Creative Processes In Trauma Recovery

The integration of creative arts-based methodologies in post-disaster psychosocial support highlights a dynamic and effective approach to aiding individuals affected by traumatic events. Utilizing Consensual Qualitative Research, studies have demonstrated the efficacy of such interventions in promoting resilience and healing among survivors. The Training of Trainers model further enhances this framework by empowering local communities to lead recovery efforts through creative processes. Despite promising outcomes, there remains a need for standardized training and broader empirical validation to fully understand the long-term impact of these creative methodologies.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts-based interventions are effective in promoting psychological resilience among trauma survivors.
- The Training of Trainers model facilitates community-driven recovery efforts by empowering local leaders with specialized skills.
- Consensual Qualitative Research provides a robust framework for evaluating the efficacy and impact of creative processes in trauma recovery.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of arts-based interventions on mental health outcomes?
