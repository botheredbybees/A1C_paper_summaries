## Nursing Students' Art and Drama Integration

The integration of art and drama into nursing education is explored through two sources, each highlighting unique benefits for nursing students. Source 1 emphasizes the use of drama as a pedagogical tool to enhance communication skills and empathy among students by providing a safe learning environment conducive to role-playing and self-reflection. This approach not only helps in developing interpersonal skills but also in fostering an understanding of diverse patient perspectives, which is crucial in healthcare settings. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on the visual arts within health education, specifically referencing historical medical illustrations as a means to understand the evolution of medical practices and patient care through visual storytelling. Both sources underscore the importance of experiential learning methods that go beyond traditional didactic teaching approaches, offering nursing students alternative ways to engage with complex healthcare issues.

## Key Findings

- Drama enhances communication skills and empathy in nursing students
- Visual arts provide historical context and understanding in medical education
- Experiential learning through drama and visual arts offers unique benefits for nursing education

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of using art and drama be measured against traditional teaching methods?
