## Photo-Elicitation

Photo-elicitation emerges as a powerful method in qualitative research and therapeutic contexts, enabling subjects to externalize their experiences through photography. This approach facilitates the recording of personal narratives and inventories such as objects that are significant to the participant's life or challenges they face. Across studies, photo elicitation is used not only for data collection but also as an intervention technique that aids in enhancing self-esteem and resilience by allowing individuals to explore their lives from a new perspective. However, there exists variability in how this method is implemented across different populations and research aims, leading to both cohesive findings on its utility and specific nuances based on context.

## Key Findings

- Photo-elicitation helps externalize personal issues.
- It serves as both a data collection tool and therapeutic intervention.
- Use of photo elicitation can enhance self-esteem and resilience.

## Open Questions

- How does the cultural background of participants affect the outcomes and implementation of photo elicitation?
