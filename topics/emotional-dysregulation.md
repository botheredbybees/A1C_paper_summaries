## Emotional Dysregulation

The sources collectively highlight emotional dysregulation as a significant therapeutic challenge in mental health treatment. Projection-in-therapy techniques and the use of cultural sensitivity are emphasized as crucial methods for addressing this issue effectively. The 'hidden puppet technique' is noted as an innovative approach that may help clients externalize their internal emotions, facilitating a better understanding and regulation of these feelings. However, there appears to be variability in how different therapeutic contexts apply these concepts, suggesting potential gaps in standardization or adaptation practices.

## Key Findings

- Projection-in-therapy aids in addressing emotional dysregulation
- Cultural sensitivity is essential when treating clients with emotional dysregulation
- The 'hidden puppet technique' can assist clients in externalizing and understanding their emotions

## Open Questions

- How can the 'hidden puppet technique' be adapted for different cultural contexts to effectively address emotional dysregulation?
