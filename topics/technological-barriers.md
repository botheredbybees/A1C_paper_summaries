## Technological Barriers in Occupational Therapy

The integration of digital technologies into occupational therapy practices has presented significant challenges. Source 1 highlights the difficulties encountered when transitioning traditional arts-based therapeutic activities to a digital format, emphasizing how these technological barriers can impede effective intervention. The adaptation process requires substantial reconfiguration of established practices and introduces new obstacles related to access, usability, and engagement with technology among both therapists and participants.

## Key Findings

- The transition from analog to digital in occupational therapy faces considerable technological hurdles.
- Technological barriers include issues such as accessibility, user-friendliness, and participant engagement with digital tools.

## Open Questions

- How can the field of occupational therapy better address and mitigate technological barriers when implementing digital interventions?
