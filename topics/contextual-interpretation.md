## Contextual Interpretation in Dementia Care

The concept of contextual interpretation within dementia care emphasizes the importance of understanding each individual's experiences and interactions within their unique social and environmental contexts. This approach contrasts with more traditional medical models that focus primarily on symptomology and pathology. Social constructivism plays a central role, suggesting that memories and identity are not fixed but are continuously constructed through ongoing interaction and communication in society. Humanistic approaches further support this by promoting the dignity and personhood of individuals living with dementia, acknowledging their subjective experiences and interpretations over objective medical diagnoses. Across these sources, there is agreement on the need to shift from a disease-focused perspective towards one that respects individual narratives and acknowledges the fluid nature of memory and identity in dementia care.

## Key Findings

- Contextual interpretation places emphasis on understanding individuals' unique social and environmental contexts in dementia care
- Social constructivism supports the idea that memories and identity are socially constructed and continuously evolving
- Humanistic approaches affirm the dignity and personhood of people with dementia, focusing on subjective experiences

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare practices better integrate contextual interpretation without losing sight of medical treatment needs?
