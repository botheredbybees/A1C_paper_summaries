## End-of-Life Care

The synthesis of end-of-life care interventions reveals a multifaceted approach that includes both traditional and innovative methods. Source 1 highlights the use of photovoice and documentary photography as tools to engage communities in discussions about aged care, fostering understanding and empathy among residents, family members, and healthcare providers. This approach emphasizes the importance of personalized care and community engagement, which align with Source 2's focus on an integrated care model that incorporates advance care planning and creative arts therapy. While both sources underscore the need for individualized care plans and effective communication strategies, they differ in their emphasis: Source 1 focuses more on the communal aspect of caring, whereas Source 2 places a greater importance on personalized medical and psychological support.

## Key Findings

- Photovoice and documentary photography can enhance community engagement and understanding in aged care.
- Integrated models of end-of-life care that include advance care planning are essential for meeting individual needs.
- Creative arts therapy plays a crucial role in providing holistic care at the end of life.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and socio-economic factors influence the effectiveness of community-engagement methods versus personalized medical approaches?
