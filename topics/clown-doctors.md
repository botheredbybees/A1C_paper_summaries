## Clown Doctors

The use of clown doctors as an intervention in healthcare settings demonstrates a multifaceted impact on patients, families, and staff. According to the Social Impact Measurement (SIM) Framework, Clown Doctors Australia highlights that this intervention yields significant social returns such as enhancing quality of care, reducing patient anxiety, and improving communication between patients and medical personnel. A study investigating the impacts on an adolescent psychiatric unit reveals that clown doctors can positively influence ward atmosphere and increase patient engagement through humor-based interventions, contributing to better overall mental health outcomes for young patients. Across these studies, there is a consistent finding that clown doctor programs contribute to higher levels of fun and positive mood among participants. Additionally, they provide a sense of control and familiarity in unfamiliar medical environments, which can be particularly beneficial for pediatric and adolescent populations.

## Key Findings

- Clown doctors significantly reduce patient anxiety and enhance communication within healthcare settings.
- The intervention improves the quality of care through enhanced ward atmosphere and increased patient engagement.
- Humor-based interventions contribute to positive mood and sense of control in pediatric and adolescent patients.

## Open Questions

- How can clown doctor programs be effectively integrated into diverse medical contexts beyond hospitals, such as psychiatric units?
