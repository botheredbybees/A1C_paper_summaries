## Research Methods in Healthcare Evaluation

The sources collectively emphasize the importance of various research methodologies in evaluating healthcare interventions. Source 1 introduces evaluation and research as distinct but complementary processes, with complex interventions often requiring randomized controlled trials (RCTs) for effective assessment. Source 2 further elaborates on process evaluations and their necessity in understanding how interventions are implemented, particularly through ethnographic studies which provide deep qualitative insights into the dynamics of healthcare settings. Source 3 builds upon these concepts by discussing the importance of outcome research to measure the effectiveness of interventions alongside detailed process evaluation findings.

## Key Findings

- Evaluation and research serve distinct but complementary roles in assessing healthcare interventions.
- Ethnographic studies are crucial for understanding implementation processes of complex interventions.
- Outcome assessments provide necessary quantitative data on intervention efficacy.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of ethnographic process evaluations with outcome assessments enhance comprehensive evaluation strategies?
