## Biological Processes Associated with Chronic Stress

Chronic stress is widely recognized for its detrimental impacts on the human body. Sources indicate that prolonged exposure to stress activates a cascade of physiological responses that can lead to inflammation, oxidative stress, and immunosuppression (Source 1). These biological processes are crucial in understanding how chronic stress affects physical health over time. Additionally, Source 2 elaborates on various biological processes triggered by chronic stress, emphasizing the importance of recognizing these mechanisms through biomarkers for clinical diagnosis and management. Despite both sources focusing on similar themes, there is a notable gap in discussing the precise pathways that link psychological stress to these physiological outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Chronic stress induces inflammation
- Oxidative stress can result from prolonged exposure to stress
- Immune function can be compromised by chronic stress

## Open Questions

- What are the specific biochemical pathways linking chronic stress with biological processes such as inflammation and oxidative stress?
