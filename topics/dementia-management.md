## Dementia Management Through Music Therapy

Music therapy emerges as a promising intervention in managing symptoms of dementia, particularly Alzheimer's disease. Functional MRI studies indicate that music activates the salience network, regions often spared by neurodegenerative processes. This activation can enhance emotional and cognitive functions which are crucial for individuals with dementia. Personalized music interventions tailored to individual preferences further elevate their effectiveness, providing a highly personalized care approach. Person-centered care emphasizes maintaining high-quality interpersonal relationships and preserving selfhood despite neurological impairments and psychosocial factors associated with dementia. Integrating music therapy within this framework can support emotional connections and cognitive engagement, thereby enhancing the overall quality of life for patients. However, the integration between neuroscientific insights from music interventions and person-centered holistic approaches requires further exploration to fully understand their synergistic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Music activates regions of the brain spared by Alzheimer's disease, potentially improving emotional and cognitive functions in dementia patients.
- Personalized music therapy tailored to individual preferences enhances therapeutic outcomes.
- Person-centered care emphasizes high-quality interpersonal relationships and selfhood maintenance for individuals with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How can person-centered care principles be effectively integrated with neuroscientific insights from music interventions to enhance holistic care approaches?
