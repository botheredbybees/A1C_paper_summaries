## Therapeutic Dance

The integration of therapeutic dance as an intervention showcases its versatility across different settings and populations. In US hospitals, therapeutic dance is employed to foster holistic healing and community engagement, addressing the physical and emotional needs of patients beyond traditional medical treatments (Source 1). This approach underscores the importance of creative arts therapies in enhancing overall patient well-being. Similarly, a systematic review of literature highlights the use of dancing as a psychosocial intervention within care homes, focusing on its efficacy for mood enhancement and social interaction among residents (Source 2). Both studies indicate that therapeutic dance not only improves physical health but also addresses mental health issues such as depression and anxiety, thereby providing comprehensive support to individuals in need. However, there is a noted gap regarding the long-term benefits of therapeutic dance interventions outside of clinical settings.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic dance significantly contributes to holistic healing by addressing both physical and emotional needs.
- Dancing as an intervention can enhance mood and promote social interaction among care home residents.
- Creative arts therapies like dancing are gaining recognition for their role in comprehensive patient support.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of therapeutic dance interventions outside clinical settings?
