## Gender Diversity in Creative Arts Programs

The provided sources highlight the critical role of creative arts programs in supporting gender-diverse individuals, particularly youth. These programs aim to foster mental well-being and suicide prevention among participants by embracing inclusive practices that challenge societal norms. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of these programs for promoting mental health within a gender-diverse context. Meanwhile, Source 2 delves into specific methodologies such as co-tinkering and anti-oppressive practices used in creative arts interventions for trans, non-binary, and gender-creative youth, underscoring the necessity of intersectional approaches to address multifaceted needs of this population. Lastly, Source 3 provides guidance on celebrating queer and gender-diverse stories through inclusive art that challenges existing societal norms, reinforcing the value of such programs in promoting visibility and acceptance.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions are crucial for supporting mental health among gender-diverse youth.
- Co-tinkering and anti-oppressive practices effectively address specific needs within creative arts programs aimed at queer and trans individuals.
- Inclusive art that challenges societal norms plays a significant role in promoting visibility and acceptance of queer and gender-diverse identities.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts programs be further adapted to ensure they are fully accessible and supportive for all aspects of gender diversity?
