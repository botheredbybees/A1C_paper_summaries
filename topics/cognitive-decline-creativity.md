## Cognitive Decline and Creativity

The studies explore the impact of cognitive decline on an artist's creativity. Source 1 focuses on frontotemporal dementia (FTD), revealing that while FTD can impose significant cognitive constraints, it may also liberate certain aspects of visual creativity. Conversely, Source 2 examines Alzheimer’s disease and highlights that visuoconstructural deficits tend to impair an artist's ability to create coherent compositions, suggesting a more detrimental effect on artistic skills compared to the potential for enhanced creativity noted in FTD cases.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive constraints associated with frontotemporal dementia can paradoxically enhance specific aspects of visual creativity.
- Visuoconstructural deficits linked to Alzheimer's disease tend to diminish an artist’s capacity to produce coherent and structured artworks.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of cognitive decline uniquely influence artistic expression, and what underlying mechanisms account for divergent outcomes in terms of creativity versus impairment?
