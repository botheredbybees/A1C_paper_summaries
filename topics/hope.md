## Hope in Vulnerable Populations

The concept of 'hope' emerges as a critical variable influencing mental health and wellbeing across diverse populations. In the context of individuals with multiple sclerosis (MS), self-esteem, hope, perceived social support, and self-efficacy are interconnected, with creative art programs showing potential to enhance these constructs positively. Resilience is another key component in understanding how young immigrants and refugees navigate their unique challenges. While both sources highlight the importance of hope, they differ in methodological approaches: one employs a pilot study focusing on therapeutic interventions for MS patients, while the other investigates community-based arts practices aimed at fostering resilience among youth from immigrant and refugee backgrounds.

## Key Findings

- Hope is linked to positive mental health outcomes across diverse populations.
- Creative art programs can enhance self-esteem and perceived social support in individuals with multiple sclerosis.
- Community-based arts practices are effective in nurturing resilience and hope among young immigrants and refugees.

## Open Questions

- How do the impacts of creative art programs on hope differ between individuals with specific health conditions (e.g., MS) versus those facing broader socio-cultural challenges?
