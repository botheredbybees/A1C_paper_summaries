## Frontotemporal Dementia

Frontotemporal Dementia (FTD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disease primarily affecting the frontal and temporal lobes of the brain. Characterized by protein aggregation, particularly tau and TDP-43 proteins, FTD leads to a range of neuropsychiatric symptoms including changes in behavior and personality, language dysfunction, and executive function deficits such as impaired inhibitory control. Notably, while many cognitive functions are compromised, some individuals with FTD maintain certain aspects of creativity and artistic expression, suggesting that visual creativity can be preserved even when other cognitive abilities decline. This preservation highlights the complex nature of how different brain regions contribute to various cognitive domains, indicating potential avenues for further research into therapeutic interventions and quality of life improvements.

## Key Findings

- Frontotemporal dementia is characterized by protein aggregation in frontal and temporal lobes leading to neuropsychiatric symptoms.
- Despite widespread cognitive decline, some individuals with FTD retain aspects of visual creativity and artistic expression.
- The preservation of creative abilities suggests selective vulnerability or resilience in specific neural networks.

## Open Questions

- What are the underlying mechanisms that allow for preserved creativity in certain domains while other cognitive functions deteriorate?
