## Laughter Therapy

The synthesis of the two sources highlights laughter therapy as an intervention that focuses on improving patient outcomes through humor and laughter. Clown Doctors Australia uses a structured Social Impact Measurement (SIM) Framework to evaluate their program, which includes metrics such as positive-emotions, intergenerational-interaction, and Quality of Care. The Humour Foundation's approach emphasizes the role of laughter in fostering positive emotions among participants. Both sources converge on the importance of quality care and social interaction but differ slightly in their specific methods of measurement and evaluation.

## Key Findings

- Laughter therapy positively impacts patient well-being through enhanced emotional states.
- Quality of care is a critical metric for evaluating the effectiveness of laughter-based interventions.
