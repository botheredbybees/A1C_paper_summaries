## Creative Practice in Healthcare

The integration of creative practices within the realm of healthcare emerges as a significant interdisciplinary approach, blending traditional medical models with innovative artistic interventions. Source 1 emphasizes the concept of self-therapy through image editing, suggesting that such activities can serve as therapeutic tools for personal growth and emotional healing. This ties into the broader notion of biophilia, underscoring the human inclination towards nature and its beneficial impacts on mental health. Meanwhile, Source 2 introduces the field of health humanities, which seeks to enrich medical practice by incorporating literary, visual, and performing arts. It highlights narrative medicine as a vital component of this approach, where storytelling aids in patient care by allowing for a deeper understanding of individual experiences and challenges. Both sources converge on the idea that creative practices can offer profound benefits within healthcare contexts, though they differ somewhat in their emphasis—Source 1 focuses more narrowly on therapeutic use of specific art forms (image editing), while Source 2 provides a broader overview including literary arts. This synthesis underscores the potential for creative interventions to complement traditional medical treatments by addressing emotional and psychological needs.

## Key Findings

- Creative practices such as image editing can serve as self-therapeutic tools, enhancing mental health through engagement with nature (biophilia).
- Health humanities integrate literature and arts into healthcare, promoting a holistic approach that includes narrative medicine for better patient care.

## Open Questions

- How do specific creative interventions compare in efficacy to traditional medical treatments when addressing emotional and psychological health needs?
