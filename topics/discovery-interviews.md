## Discovery Interviews

The sources highlight the role of discovery interviews in understanding contexts and facilitating reflective practice. The integration of frameworks like PESTLE analysis and Kolb's Reflective Cycle enhances the depth and breadth of insights gained from such interviews, particularly within arts-in-health interventions. These methods enable practitioners to systematically examine external factors (PESTLE) while also reflecting on their own experiences and learning processes (Kolb). However, there is a need for more explicit guidelines on how these frameworks can be effectively combined in practical settings.

## Key Findings

- Discovery interviews serve as a foundational method for gathering contextual data necessary for arts-in-health interventions.
- Frameworks like PESTLE analysis and Kolb’s Reflective Cycle complement discovery interview outcomes by providing structured approaches to external context examination and personal reflection, respectively.
- The combination of these methods facilitates comprehensive understanding and continuous improvement in practice.

## Open Questions

- How can practitioners effectively integrate multiple frameworks such as PESTLE analysis and Kolb's Reflective Cycle into the process of conducting and analyzing discovery interviews?
