## Diagnostic Criteria for Neurocognitive Disorders

The DSM-5-TR Neurocognitive Disorders Supplement outlines updated diagnostic criteria that emphasize the importance of clinical judgment in determining the nature and extent of neurocognitive disorders. The supplement underscores the necessity to rule out medical etiologies before considering a diagnosis under these categories, reflecting an integrative approach to differential diagnosis. Despite the detailed guidelines provided by DSM-5-TR, there is ongoing debate about the sufficiency of current diagnostic criteria in fully capturing the complexity and variability of neurocognitive disorders across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Diagnostic criteria for neurocognitive disorders require careful consideration of medical etiologies to ensure accurate differentiation from other conditions.
- Clinical judgment plays a crucial role alongside standardized diagnostic criteria, highlighting the need for practitioner discretion in diagnosis.

## Open Questions

- How can diagnostic criteria be refined to better address the heterogeneity and diversity within neurocognitive disorders?
