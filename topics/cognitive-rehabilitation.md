## Cognitive Rehabilitation

Cognitive rehabilitation is a multifaceted intervention aimed at improving or maintaining cognitive function in individuals with neurological impairments. Sources highlight the use of various techniques including music-supported therapy, external memory aids, and internal strategies to enhance brain plasticity and improve behavioral changes (Source 2). Cognitive training can address specific deficits such as those seen in dementia, where music has been shown to open doors for memory access and cognitive engagement (Source 3). Additionally, the creative arts are employed to facilitate expression and rehabilitation, providing alternative methods to support cognition beyond traditional approaches. These interventions have common goals of enhancing neuroplasticity and improving daily function, yet each method may offer unique benefits depending on individual patient needs.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive rehabilitation utilizes diverse techniques including music therapy, memory aids, and internal strategies.
- Music-supported therapy can induce behavioral changes and enhance cognitive functions in individuals with traumatic brain injury or dementia.
- The creative arts are valuable tools for cognitive rehabilitation, offering pathways to expression and engagement.
