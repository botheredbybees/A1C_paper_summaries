## Employability Skills

The exploration of employability skills reveals a multifaceted approach to enhancing individuals' career readiness through various interventions. Sources highlight the importance of soft skills such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving in addition to technical competencies for securing employment. The role of arts-based methods like art therapy is noted in fostering these skills and improving health and wellbeing, which are critical aspects that recruiters value when selecting candidates. However, there's a tension between the tangible benefits of arts interventions in employability training and the need for quantifiable evidence of their impact on job placement success.

## Key Findings

- Employability skills encompass both hard and soft skills necessary for career readiness.
- Art therapy can enhance employability by improving health and wellbeing, indirectly contributing to skill development.
- Recruiters prioritize candidates with strong interpersonal and communication abilities alongside technical proficiencies.

## Open Questions

- What is the most effective method of measuring the impact of arts interventions on employment outcomes?
