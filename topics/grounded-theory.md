## Grounded Theory in Arts and Health Research

Grounded theory is a qualitative research methodology that emerged from the need to develop theories directly from data collected through systematic fieldwork. In the context of arts and health, grounded theory provides a robust framework for understanding complex phenomena such as music therapy experiences in palliative care settings. Source 1 discusses the role of qualitative research within creative arts and health disciplines, highlighting how methodologies like grounded theory help researchers to construct theories that are grounded in empirical data rather than starting with preconceived notions or hypotheses. In contrast, Source 2 illustrates a specific application of grounded theory in a study focusing on music therapy practices in palliative care settings, where it was used alongside thematic analysis to explore the quality-of-life experiences of patients and their families. Both sources emphasize the iterative nature of grounded theory, which involves collecting data, analyzing it, and refining research questions until saturation is reached.

## Key Findings

- Grounded theory is a qualitative methodology that builds theories directly from empirical data.
- In arts and health studies, grounded theory provides insights into complex phenomena like music therapy practices in palliative care settings.
- Combining grounded theory with thematic analysis enhances the depth of understanding regarding quality-of-life experiences in healthcare contexts.
