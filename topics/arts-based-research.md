## Arts-Based Research

The integration of arts-based research methodologies is explored in the provided sources with a focus on both theoretical underpinnings and practical applications. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of establishing a clear rationale and assessing feasibility when designing a research protocol, highlighting the necessity for thorough planning to ensure that the proposed project is grounded in solid academic and ethical considerations. In contrast, Source 2 delves into specific methodologies within arts-based research such as photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry, applied particularly within aged care settings to capture and interpret the lived experiences of aging individuals. This source demonstrates how creative arts interventions can serve not only as a means of data collection but also as therapeutic tools that empower participants by allowing them to articulate their narratives in meaningful ways.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based research methodologies require clear rationale and feasibility assessments to ensure ethical and effective implementation
- Photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry are creative methods used in aged care research to capture lived experiences of aging
- Creative arts interventions serve dual purposes: data collection and therapeutic engagement for participants

## Open Questions

- How can the feasibility assessment process be adapted to effectively accommodate diverse arts-based methodologies?
