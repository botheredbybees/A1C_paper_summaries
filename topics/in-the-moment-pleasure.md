## In-The-Moment Pleasure

The concept of 'in-the-moment' pleasure is explored through the lens of arts engagement programs designed to foster a sense of community and connection among participants. These programs aim to provide immediate, tangible benefits that enhance personal well-being by encouraging active participation in artistic activities. The emphasis on 'in-the-moment' pleasure underscores the importance of real-time enjoyment and fulfillment derived from engaging with art, rather than focusing solely on long-term outcomes or external validations. This approach highlights the potential of arts engagement as a means to promote immediate emotional and psychological benefits, contributing positively to individuals' quality of life and community integration.

## Key Findings

- Arts engagement programs enhance participants' sense of connection within their communities.
- In-the-moment pleasure derived from engaging with art can have significant positive impacts on individual well-being.
- Real-time enjoyment is a critical aspect of arts engagement interventions.

## Open Questions

- How do long-term outcomes compare to immediate benefits in the context of arts engagement programs?
