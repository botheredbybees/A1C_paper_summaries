## Community Arts Engagement for Mental Health and Dementia Care in Remote Communities

Both sources highlight the critical role of community arts engagement in addressing mental health issues and caring for individuals with dementia in remote communities. Source 1 focuses on reducing stigma associated with mental health through the FIVE project, which involves local artists collaborating to create awareness and acceptance within their community. Similarly, Source 2 emphasizes the importance of art centers as hubs for culturally relevant care and social participation, particularly beneficial for Aboriginal Australians living with dementia. Both projects underscore the necessity of tailored interventions that are deeply rooted in local culture and community needs, enhancing mental health support and fostering a sense of belonging among participants.

## Key Findings

- Community arts engagement is an effective strategy to reduce stigma associated with mental health issues
- Culturally relevant care provided through art centers can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals with dementia in remote communities
- Tailored interventions rooted in local culture are essential for fostering social participation and improving mental health support
