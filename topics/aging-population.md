## Arts and Creativity Interventions for Older Adults

This synthesis explores the efficacy and economic impact of arts and creativity interventions aimed at enhancing health and wellbeing in older adults. Source 1 provides a systematic review focusing on cost-effectiveness analysis, suggesting that arts-based programs can be economically beneficial while improving physical and mental health outcomes. In contrast, Source 2 delves into drama therapy specifically for individuals with mild to moderate dementia, highlighting its potential to enhance quality of life beyond just cognitive functions. Both sources agree on the positive impact of creative interventions in older adults but differ in scope, with one emphasizing economic benefits over therapeutic efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Arts and creativity interventions can improve physical and mental health outcomes in older adults.
- Creative activities are cost-effective solutions for enhancing wellbeing in aging populations.
- Drama therapy specifically shows potential to enhance quality of life for individuals with dementia.

## Open Questions

- What specific types of arts-based programs yield the most significant economic benefits versus therapeutic improvements?
