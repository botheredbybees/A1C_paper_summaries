## Transformative Potential in Disability Studies

The provided sources converge on the theme of transformative potential within disability studies. The social model of disability (SMD) is critiqued for its limitations, such as neglecting individual experiences and agency. However, Hannah Arendt's concept of power offers a new lens through which to view these critiques, suggesting that true transformation requires collective action and recognition of each person's unique capacity to act in the world. This perspective highlights the importance of understanding disability beyond mere social barriers and includes an acknowledgment of personal empowerment as a key component for societal change.

## Key Findings

- The social model of disability is critiqued for its lack of emphasis on individual agency and experience.
- Hannah Arendt's notion of power provides a framework to understand collective action in the context of disability rights.
- Transformation towards inclusivity involves recognizing each person's capacity to act.

## Open Questions

- How can Arendt's concept of power be applied practically within communities to promote transformative change?
