## Medical Etiologies in Neurocognitive Disorders

The DSM-5-TR Neurocognitive Disorders Supplement underscores the importance of identifying medical etiologies as a foundational step in diagnosing neurocognitive disorders. The supplement emphasizes the role of clinical judgment in making differential diagnoses by considering various medical conditions that could cause cognitive decline. However, the integration of medical etiologies into diagnostic criteria highlights ongoing debates about specificity and inclusivity within classification systems.

## Key Findings

- Clinical judgment is crucial for identifying medical etiologies.
- Diagnoses are contingent upon ruling out potential medical causes of neurocognitive deficits.
- Diagnostic criteria must balance comprehensiveness with the need to specify unique conditions.

## Open Questions

- How can diagnostic accuracy be improved while maintaining sensitivity and specificity in detecting neurocognitive disorders?
