## Alzheimer's Poetry Project

The Alzheimer's Poetry Project (APP) is an intervention aimed at improving the quality of life for individuals living with dementia through creative engagement. Both sources highlight APP as a humanities-based approach that leverages poetry creation to foster social interaction, cognitive stimulation, and emotional expression among participants. The projects share similarities in their focus on non-traditional therapeutic methods designed to engage people with dementia in meaningful activities. Source 1 emphasizes the concept of 'homo ludens'—the human being as a player—to underscore the value of playfulness and creativity in addressing the needs of individuals living with dementia. In contrast, Source 2 places APP within a broader context of similar initiatives like TimeSlips and Sundowner Theatre Productions, suggesting that such programs are part of a growing trend towards incorporating theatre and playful engagement into dementia care strategies. While both sources underscore the benefits of these interventions in enhancing social connections and cognitive well-being among participants, they also highlight the importance of adapting traditional artistic practices to suit the unique challenges posed by dementia.

## Key Findings

- Alzheimer's Poetry Project and similar initiatives like TimeSlips emphasize non-traditional therapeutic methods for dementia care.
- These programs foster social interaction and cognitive engagement through creative activities such as poetry writing and theatrical performances.
- Humanities-based interventions, including APP, are part of a broader trend in dementia care that prioritizes playfulness and creativity.
