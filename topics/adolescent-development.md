## Adolescent Development and Arts Integration

The integration of arts in adolescent development showcases a multifaceted approach to enhancing mental health and cognitive growth. Sources such as the intergenerational choir transcript highlight therapeutic benefits through community-building activities, while music listening studies emphasize its role in supporting adolescents' sense of agency and emotional states. School-based drama interventions further suggest that interactive learning can promote self-reflection and identity formation among youth. Neuroscience literature underscores the critical period of synaptic pruning and myelination during adolescence, indicating a window for targeted interventions to optimize brain development. Hormonal changes influence neural circuitry maturation, suggesting that these biological processes impact cognitive functions and decision-making abilities. Arts-based approaches like educational drama and music listening not only foster emotional resilience but also align with the ongoing brain development of adolescents by enhancing socio-emotional skills and subjective well-being. These findings collectively indicate a positive correlation between creative arts engagement and mental health outcomes in youth, despite some gaps regarding long-term effects and diverse socioeconomic contexts.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions can enhance adolescent mental health and cognitive growth.
- Music listening supports adolescents' sense of agency and emotional well-being.
- School-based drama interventions promote self-reflection and identity formation.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of creative arts on adolescent development in diverse socioeconomic contexts?
