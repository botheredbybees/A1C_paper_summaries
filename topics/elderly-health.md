## Health Interventions for Elderly Adults

The integration of health interventions aimed at elderly adults highlights both diverse approaches and shared concerns. Sources emphasize the multifaceted nature of falls risk in elderly populations, particularly those with dementia. Polypharmacy and nervous-system drugs are identified as key contributors to balance issues that can lead to hospitalised falls among individuals living with dementia (Source 1). In contrast, creative arts interventions such as dance emerge as promising non-pharmacological strategies for reducing fall risks by improving balance and functional capacity in older adults (Source 3). Music and cultural activities also show potential benefits for health and wellbeing across different age groups, impacting salivary cortisol levels positively (Source 2). However, while each intervention shows promise individually, there is a need to explore how these interventions can be integrated effectively into comprehensive care plans that address the specific needs of elderly populations.

## Key Findings

- Polypharmacy and nervous-system drugs increase fall risks among older adults with dementia.
- Creative arts interventions such as dance and music positively impact physical balance and mental health in elderly individuals.
- Integration of non-pharmacological approaches like dance into care plans for managing falls risk is increasingly recognized.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts interventions be effectively integrated into comprehensive care plans that address the specific needs of older adults, particularly those with dementia?
