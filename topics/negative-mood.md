## Creative Arts-Based Interventions for Negative Mood

The synthesis of research on creative arts-based interventions highlights their efficacy in mitigating symptoms associated with negative mood and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) among children and adolescents exposed to traumatic events. These interventions, which include music therapy, drama therapy, and visual art projects, have shown significant reductions in anxiety levels and externalizing problems. However, variability in intervention design and application limits the generalizability of findings across different populations and settings.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts-based interventions are effective in reducing symptoms of PTSD and negative mood among youth exposed to traumatic events.
- These interventions contribute to lowering anxiety levels and decreasing externalizing behavioral problems.
- Intervention outcomes vary with the type, duration, and context-specific application of creative arts therapies.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal conditions for applying creative arts-based interventions to maximize their efficacy in reducing negative mood among youth?
