## Arts On Prescription

Arts On Prescription (AoP) is an innovative intervention aimed at enhancing mental and psychosocial well-being through the prescription of creative activities. The approach has gained traction in response to increased mental health challenges, particularly during times of social isolation such as lockdowns. Source 1 highlights how AoP can adapt creatively within community settings like hospitals or libraries by engaging with artists such as Sir Quentin Blake to offer tailored artistic experiences that foster engagement and support. Source 2 delves into the quantitative outcomes of AoP on mental health metrics, noting significant reductions in anxiety and depression over time, suggesting a positive impact on individual well-being through social prescribing mechanisms. This aligns with Source 3's systematic review findings which also indicate improvements in various aspects of psychosocial wellbeing when participating in AoP programs.

## Key Findings

- Arts On Prescription significantly reduces symptoms of anxiety and depression.
- AoP enhances overall mental health by providing structured social engagement opportunities.
- Participation in creative activities through AoP contributes positively to individual's sense of psychosocial well-being.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of Arts On Prescription on participants' sustained mental and psychosocial health?
