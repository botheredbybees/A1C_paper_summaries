## Resilience-building Through Arts Activities

The provided sources collectively explore the role of arts activities and creative expression in enhancing resilience across various populations. The studies emphasize the value of structured group arts activities, ecopsychosocial theory, and trauma-processing approaches for developing mental well-being and emotional resilience. Creative expressions like visual arts, music, and art therapy are highlighted as effective interventions for diverse groups including children, teens, people living with dementia, and those recovering from natural disasters. The sources suggest that these creative endeavors not only foster personal growth but also promote social skills, stress management techniques, and a positive mindset towards life challenges.

## Key Findings

- Arts activities contribute significantly to the mental well-being and resilience of individuals across various age groups and health conditions.
- Creative expression through visual arts, music, and art therapy serves as an effective intervention for promoting emotional resilience in different populations.
- Structured group activities within creative arts programs are instrumental in developing social skills and stress management techniques.

## Open Questions

- How can the efficacy of arts-based interventions be further evaluated across diverse cultural contexts?
