## Organizational Change in Trauma-Informed Care

The provided sources focus on the implementation of trauma-informed care within organizational settings through structured interventions. Both texts emphasize the importance of adopting a systemic approach to change, recognizing that trauma-informed practices necessitate more than individual staff training; they require an organization-wide commitment and culture shift. The 'Trauma-Informed Care and Practice Organisational Toolkit' (TICPOT) is highlighted as a critical resource for guiding organizations through this complex process. It offers detailed strategies such as conducting organizational audits to assess current practices, developing quality improvement plans based on identified gaps, and fostering a sustainable trauma-informed culture that integrates principles of safety, trustworthiness, choice, collaboration, and empowerment (trauma-informed-principles). The synthesis reveals a consensus among sources about the necessity for comprehensive planning and assessment before embarking on transformation efforts. However, there is an acknowledged gap regarding the sustainability of such changes over time.

## Key Findings

- Organizational change towards trauma-informed care requires a systemic approach involving all levels of the organization.
- The Trauma-Informed Care and Practice Organisational Toolkit (TICPOT) provides essential steps for planning, auditing, and implementing sustainable organizational practices.
- Quality improvement plans are crucial for identifying gaps in current practices and guiding necessary changes.

## Open Questions

- How can organizations ensure the long-term sustainability of trauma-informed practices after initial implementation?
