## Singing Therapy

The efficacy of singing therapy is highlighted across various studies focusing on different aspects such as social bonding and language rehabilitation. Source 1 investigates the impact of singing on social cohesion, demonstrating a rapid enhancement in positive affect and endorphin release among participants. This underscores the potential of singing to facilitate quick interpersonal connections within groups. Conversely, Source 2 delves into the nuances of singing therapy for individuals with aphasia, emphasizing the importance of melodic intonation therapy (MIT) in stimulating neural network engagement, particularly through altered articulatory tempo. While these sources address different applications of singing therapy, they both underscore its broader therapeutic benefits, ranging from enhancing social bonds to aiding neurological recovery.

## Key Findings

- Singing therapy enhances positive affect and endorphin release among participants, fostering rapid social cohesion.
- Melodic intonation therapy (MIT) leverages altered articulatory tempo in singing to engage neural networks effectively for individuals with aphasia.

## Open Questions

- How can the benefits of singing therapy observed in healthy populations be adapted and integrated into clinical settings for neurological disorders such as aphasia?
