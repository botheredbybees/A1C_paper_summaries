## Arts-Based Therapies

Arts-based therapies are recognized as a therapeutic intervention for individuals suffering from eating disorders. This approach encompasses various forms of creative expression such as visual arts, music, and dance to help patients explore their emotions and experiences in non-verbal ways. The empirical evidence supporting the efficacy of these interventions is growing, highlighting improvements not only in symptom management but also in enhancing emotional regulation and self-esteem among participants. However, while there is an increasing body of research indicating the benefits of arts-based therapies, some studies note variability in outcomes depending on the specific type of eating disorder and individual patient characteristics.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based therapies offer a non-verbal approach to treating eating disorders that can be particularly beneficial for patients who struggle with traditional talk therapy.
- Empirical evidence suggests improvements in symptom management, emotional regulation, and self-esteem through arts-based interventions.
- Variability exists in the effectiveness of arts-based therapies across different types of eating disorders.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which arts-based therapies contribute to the improvement of symptoms and overall well-being among patients with eating disorders?
