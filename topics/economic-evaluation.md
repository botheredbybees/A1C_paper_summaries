## Economic Evaluation of Arts and Creativity Interventions

The economic evaluation of arts and creativity interventions highlights their potential to improve health and wellbeing, particularly in older adults. Source one emphasizes the importance of cost-effectiveness analysis in demonstrating the value of these interventions for improving health outcomes. In contrast, source two focuses more on mixed-methods research to explore how visual art activities contribute to quality-of-life improvements and social benefits among individuals with dementia. Both sources suggest that arts-based programs can yield substantial non-monetary benefits, such as enhanced social interaction and personal fulfillment, which are critical components in evaluating the overall impact of these interventions beyond mere cost-benefit ratios.

## Key Findings

- Cost-effectiveness analysis is crucial for demonstrating the value of arts and creativity interventions in healthcare contexts.
- Visual art activities contribute to improved quality of life among individuals with dementia.
- Social interaction and personal fulfillment are significant benefits derived from arts-based programs.

## Open Questions

- How can economic evaluations better account for non-monetary outcomes, such as social and emotional well-being?
