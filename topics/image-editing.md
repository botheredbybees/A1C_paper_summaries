## Image Editing Techniques

The provided sources collectively address various aspects of image editing techniques and software methodologies. Source 1 highlights the use of layer-masking and selection tools in Photoshop to merge images seamlessly, a practice foundational to advanced photo editing. Sources 2 and 3 further expand on different image editing techniques using multiple software tools while introducing concepts like therapeutic photography and the biophilia hypothesis, which suggest that viewing natural scenes through edited imagery can be beneficial for mental health. Source 4 provides basic instructions for fundamental adjustments such as cropping, resizing, rotating, and maintaining aspect ratio, emphasizing the importance of these operations in achieving a professional-looking result. Lastly, Source 5 elaborates on adjusting brightness and contrast to enhance images captured under low-light conditions. These sources collectively illustrate that while image editing is often associated with enhancing visual aesthetics, it can also serve therapeutic purposes.

## Key Findings

- Layer-masking and selection tools are essential for merging and combining images in Photoshop
- Image editing techniques include basic adjustments such as cropping and resizing
- Enhancing brightness and contrast improves the quality of low-light photography
