## Confidentiality In Arts-Based Health Research

The concept of confidentiality is central to the ethical framework governing arts-based health research. This encompasses maintaining participant anonymity and privacy throughout data collection and publication processes, ensuring that sensitive information remains protected. Ethical challenges arise when balancing confidentiality with transparency and accountability in academic publishing and community engagement practices. The meaningful involvement of participants in decision-making about their confidential information adds another layer of complexity to the ethical considerations surrounding confidentiality.

## Key Findings

- Maintaining participant anonymity is critical
- Ethical challenges related to balancing confidentiality and transparency
- Meaningful participation in decisions regarding confidential data

## Open Questions

- How can meaningful involvement be balanced with stringent confidentiality requirements?
