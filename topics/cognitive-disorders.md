## Cognitive Disorders

The exploration of cognitive disorders involves understanding various forms such as Alzheimer's disease, vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, and frontotemporal dementia. Each disorder presents with distinct symptoms impacting memory, behavior, and cognitive functions differently. The study of brain networking among musicians highlights the potential for neurosynchronicity and communal-brain-function in therapeutic implications. This research suggests that understanding the neural pathways used by musicians could offer insights into developing treatments or interventions for individuals suffering from cognitive disorders. However, while these studies provide a foundation for future investigations, there remains a gap in directly linking specific musical practices to measurable improvements in cognitive disorder symptoms.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive disorders such as Alzheimer's disease and vascular dementia present distinct neurological challenges.
- The concept of neurosynchronicity among musicians could offer novel therapeutic approaches for cognitive disorders.
- Understanding communal-brain-function can enhance the development of targeted interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can specific musical practices be directly applied to improve symptoms in individuals with different forms of dementia?
