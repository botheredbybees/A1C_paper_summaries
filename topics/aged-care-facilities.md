## Person-Centered Care in Aged Care Facilities

The integration of creative activities and person-centered care approaches within residential aged care facilities for individuals with dementia has been shown to enhance quality of life and manage the behavioural and psychological symptoms associated with the disease. Source 1 highlights how creative activities such as art, music, and other forms of self-expression can provide cognitive engagement that mitigates BPSD, thereby improving the mental health and overall well-being of residents. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on person-centered care, which emphasizes individualized attention to the needs, preferences, and history of each resident to deliver quality-of-life enhancement services tailored to their unique circumstances. While both sources underscore the importance of individualized approaches in managing dementia-related challenges, there is a need for further research into how these two methodologies can be optimally integrated to maximize patient outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities and person-centered care approaches are beneficial in residential aged care settings for individuals with dementia.
- Both methods aim to improve quality of life and manage symptoms such as BPSD, but they target different aspects of care (cognitive engagement vs. personalized attention).

## Open Questions

- How can creative activities and person-centered care be optimally integrated to enhance the overall effectiveness in managing dementia-related challenges?
