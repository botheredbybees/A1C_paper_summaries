## Taste Perception and Sound

The concept of taste perception being linked to sound is explored through the lens of implicit associations between taste and pitch. Source 1 employs methods such as the Implicit Association Test (IAT) and Go/No-Go Association Task (GNAT) to uncover hidden connections people make between tastes, like sweetness or sourness, and auditory characteristics, specifically pitch. These tests reveal that there is a consistent tendency for certain tastes to be implicitly linked with specific pitches in participants' minds, suggesting an inherent crossmodal perception where taste influences how sound is perceived and vice versa.

Source 2 delves into this notion further by creating a database of subjective rating norms for musical stimuli related to taste. This work identifies the parameters within music that correlate with tastes, providing a rich set of data points that can be used in future research to explore the relationship between auditory experience and gustatory perception. Together, these studies highlight an intricate web of sensory interplay where sound provides cues about taste, and vice versa, shaping how individuals perceive their food and drink experiences.

## Key Findings

- There is a consistent implicit association between certain tastes (sweetness, sourness) and specific pitches in auditory perception.
- Crossmodal perception exists between taste and sound, influencing how people experience both senses.
- Subjective rating norms for musical stimuli have been established to correlate with various tastes, providing a framework for future research.

## Open Questions

- What is the underlying neurological basis for these cross-modal associations between taste and sound?
