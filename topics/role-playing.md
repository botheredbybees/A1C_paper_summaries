## Role-Playing in Therapeutic Settings

The integration of role-playing into therapeutic practices has been explored extensively through the concept of drama therapy. This approach emphasizes the use of role-playing as an embodied practice, allowing individuals to explore and express emotions and experiences through storytelling (North, 2021). Role-playing serves not only as a tool for self-expression but also aids in developing empathy and understanding between participants. However, there is a need to further investigate the efficacy of such methods across diverse populations and contexts, highlighting tensions regarding cultural sensitivity and application.

## Key Findings

- Role-playing within therapeutic settings, particularly through drama therapy, serves multiple purposes including emotional expression and empathy building.
- Storytelling in role-playing activities enhances self-awareness and interpersonal understanding.
- Embodied practice as a component of role-playing allows for a deeper connection between the body and the narrative being explored.

## Open Questions

- How can role-playing be effectively adapted to respect cultural differences and diverse backgrounds?
