## Dementia-Friendly Communities

The concept of dementia-friendly communities emphasizes the creation of environments that support individuals with dementia by fostering inclusivity and social participation. Both sources highlight essential elements such as community involvement, inclusive environments, legal obligations, healthy cities initiatives, compassionate cities, and adherence to a social model of disability. They underscore the importance of businesses, public services, and residents collaborating to remove barriers faced by people living with dementia, thereby enhancing their quality of life through supportive measures.

## Key Findings

- Community involvement is crucial for developing dementia-friendly communities
- Inclusive environments that respect legal obligations are necessary
- Healthy cities and compassionate cities initiatives support the social model of disability

## Open Questions

- How can the balance between community involvement and individual responsibility be optimized in dementia-friendly initiatives?
