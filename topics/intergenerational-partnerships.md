## Intergenerational Partnerships

The case study of the Active Energy project highlights the effectiveness of intergenerational partnerships in fostering creativity among older adults. This initiative leverages democratization of technology to empower elderly participants and stimulate social innovation, underscoring the potential for such collaborations to enhance community engagement and promote cross-generational understanding. The integration of younger individuals with technological expertise supports a more inclusive approach to utilizing emerging technologies, thereby democratizing access and enabling broader participation in creative activities among older people.

## Key Findings

- Intergenerational partnerships can facilitate the adoption of new technologies by older adults through mentorship from younger participants.
- Creative projects that involve intergenerational collaboration tend to generate social innovations that enhance community engagement.
- Democratization of technology is a key driver in enabling older people's participation in creative activities.

## Open Questions

- How can barriers such as ageist attitudes or digital literacy gaps be effectively addressed to ensure equitable participation?
