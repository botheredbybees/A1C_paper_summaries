## Environmental Connectivity in Therapy

The concept of environmental connectivity in therapy is explored through various lenses including biophilia, therapeutic photography, and resilience building. Sources indicate the use of natural environments and imagery to improve mental health (Source 1) alongside projects that aim at enhancing a sense of place and encouraging creative expression as means to foster well-being (Source 2). Both sources highlight the importance of connecting individuals with their environment through visual mediums, suggesting a therapeutic value derived from such connections. However, there is a notable gap in understanding how these interventions interact with diverse cultural backgrounds and individual preferences for natural settings.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography and photo therapy leverage images to enhance mental health.
- Projects focusing on resilience-building contribute significantly to personal well-being by encouraging creative expression.
- A sense of place is crucial in therapeutic projects aimed at environmental connectivity.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences affect the efficacy of environmental connectivity interventions?
