## Linear Narrative in Visual Storytelling

The sources discuss the concept of narrative within visual storytelling, emphasizing the distinction between linear and non-linear narratives. In legal contexts as outlined in Source 1, a linear narrative involves a clear sequence of events that follow a cause-and-effect pattern, enhancing character development and plot clarity through detailed descriptions and structured storylines. Source 2 expands this understanding to broader artistic realms, suggesting that while linear narratives provide a straightforward journey with a beginning, middle, and end, they may limit creative exploration compared to non-linear narratives which offer multiple perspectives and complex interconnections among events.

## Key Findings

- Linear narrative provides clarity in cause-and-effect sequences
- Linear narrative enhances character development through detailed storytelling
- Non-linear narratives offer more flexible storytelling structures

## Open Questions

- How does the effectiveness of linear versus non-linear narratives vary across different cultural contexts?
