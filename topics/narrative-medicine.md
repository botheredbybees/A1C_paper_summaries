## Narrative Medicine

The concept of Narrative Medicine emerges consistently across sources as an interdisciplinary approach that integrates literature and storytelling into clinical practice to enhance empathy and understanding in healthcare settings. This intervention involves teaching clinicians and patients the importance of reflective practice, creative expression, and the use of narrative techniques such as creative writing and storytelling to better understand and communicate patient experiences. Sources highlight its potential for fostering a more empathetic, patient-centered care environment while also emphasizing the critical need for empirical research to validate its efficacy and explore its socio-political dimensions within healthcare practices.

## Key Findings

- Narrative Medicine enhances empathy and understanding between clinicians and patients through storytelling techniques.
- Reflective practice and creative expression are integral components of Narrative Medicine interventions.
- Empirical research is necessary to fully understand the impact of Narrative Medicine on patient outcomes.

## Open Questions

- What is the empirical evidence supporting the effectiveness of Narrative Medicine in improving clinical care?
