## Aggressive Tendencies

The exploration of medical clowns as an intervention in hospital settings reveals their potential to elevate patient satisfaction and reduce aggressive tendencies among both pediatric and adult populations. The use of humor and play by medical clowns provides a unique therapeutic benefit, suggesting that interventions tailored towards fostering positive emotions can mitigate negative behaviors such as aggression. However, there is variability across studies regarding the extent and duration of these benefits, indicating the need for further investigation into the mechanisms behind their effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Medical clowns contribute to elevating patient satisfaction.
- Reduction in aggressive tendencies among patients following clown interventions.
- Therapeutic benefits associated with humor and play

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of clown therapy on aggressive behaviors?
