## Carbon-Dating in Archaeology

The sources collectively highlight the pivotal role of carbon-dating as a methodological cornerstone in archaeology and anthropology. Carbon-dating has enabled scientists to accurately determine the age of organic materials, thereby providing crucial timelines for human migration theories. However, while the precision of radiocarbon dating is widely acknowledged, it also faces challenges such as calibration issues due to variations in atmospheric carbon ratios over time. The integration of multiple methodologies, including genetic and linguistic analyses alongside carbon-dating, has refined our understanding of ancient human migrations and cultural developments.

## Key Findings

- Carbon-dating provides a reliable method for determining the age of organic materials.
- The technique supports migration theories by dating artifacts and remains accurately.
- Combining carbon-dating with other methodologies enhances the accuracy of historical reconstructions.
