## Therapeutic Clowning

The intervention of therapeutic clowning is explored across sources, with a consistent finding that it significantly reduces fear and anxiety in various settings. This method often involves the presence of professional clowns who interact with patients to provide emotional support, alleviate stress, and improve communication between healthcare providers and recipients. Communication improvement is another key benefit noted within these studies, suggesting that clown-doctor sessions not only ease psychological burdens but also enhance verbal interactions among participants.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic clowning effectively reduces fear and anxiety in patients.
- Improved communication is a notable outcome of therapeutic clowning interventions.
- Clown-doctors play a crucial role in the facilitation of emotional support within healthcare environments.

## Open Questions
