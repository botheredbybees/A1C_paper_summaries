## Neuroprotective Effects

The exploration of natural products as therapeutic agents in Alzheimer's disease reveals a multi-faceted approach to mitigating oxidative stress and suppressing amyloid-beta aggregation, both key aspects of neurodegeneration. These studies highlight the potential for these compounds to offer neuroprotective benefits by addressing underlying mechanisms involved in cognitive decline. While promising, further research is needed to fully understand their efficacy and applicability.

## Key Findings

- Natural products can mitigate oxidative stress
- Suppression of amyloid-beta aggregation is a key therapeutic strategy
- Potential exists for natural compounds as neuroprotective agents

## Open Questions

- Further clinical trials are necessary to confirm efficacy
