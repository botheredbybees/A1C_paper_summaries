## Reciprocal Interactions

The use of puppets as a medium to facilitate reciprocal interactions in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) highlights the potential for simplified social stimuli to enhance attention to social cues. This intervention is particularly effective because it provides an environment where social norms are easier to understand and imitate, thereby fostering more natural communication patterns. The key finding from this study suggests that such interventions can significantly improve a child's ability to engage in reciprocal interactions, which are critical for their overall development. However, there is a gap in understanding how long these positive effects last beyond the intervention period.

## Key Findings

- Puppets serve as effective tools for facilitating attention to social cues.
- Simplified social stimuli can enhance engagement and imitation of social norms.
- Reciprocal interactions are critical for developmental progress in children with ASD.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term efficacy of using simplified social stimuli like puppets for promoting reciprocal interactions?
