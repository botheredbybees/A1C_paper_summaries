## Body-Mind Connection in Health and Therapy

The concept of the body-mind connection is explored across two distinct therapeutic realms: dementia care and trauma-informed expressive arts therapy. In dementia, the focus is on understanding the individual as a 'body-subject', emphasizing their lived experience (phenomenal-experience) beyond cognitive decline. This perspective underscores the importance of holistic care that respects the personhood of individuals with dementia through physical engagement and sensory stimuli. Meanwhile, in trauma-informed expressive arts therapy, practitioners emphasize resilience-building and the body-mind connection as a pathway to healing from trauma. Both approaches highlight the significance of addressing patients' embodied experiences for comprehensive therapeutic benefit.

## Key Findings

- The body-mind connection is crucial for effective holistic health interventions.
- Understanding individuals with dementia as 'body-subjects' enriches their care.
- Trauma-informed expressive arts therapy leverages the body-mind connection to foster resilience.

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic approaches effectively integrate physical and mental aspects of healing?
