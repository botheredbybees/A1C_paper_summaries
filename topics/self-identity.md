## Self-Identity Through Creative Expression

The concept of self-identity through creative expression is explored in two distinct contexts: individuals dealing with illness and refugees facing protracted displacement. Both sources highlight the importance of emotional expression and community-building as key components that reinforce a sense of self and social relations. Art-making, whether through visual art or music, offers individuals an avenue to process their experiences and articulate aspects of their identity that might be difficult to express verbally. These creative activities not only provide a platform for personal reflection but also facilitate the formation of supportive networks and a sense of agency, crucial elements in coping with challenging life circumstances.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression through art-making or music aids individuals in processing emotional experiences tied to illness or displacement
- Community-building activities that involve creative work foster stronger social relations and support systems
- Engagement in such interventions enhances a sense of self-identity and personal agency among participants

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences influence the effectiveness of creative expression interventions for identity formation?
