## Addressing Health Inequalities Through Long-Term Support

The sources collectively highlight the role of long-term supportive relationships and interventions in mitigating health inequalities among youth. Sistema Scotland's Big Noise program exemplifies a community-based intervention that fosters transformative effects on participants, including educational and social improvements. While evaluations show promising outcomes, there are gaps in understanding how such programs can be sustained over time and scaled up to reach wider populations.

## Key Findings

- Long-term supportive relationships have significant positive impacts on youth health and well-being
- Community-based interventions like Big Noise demonstrate transformative effects on participants
- Addressing health inequalities requires sustained, tailored support for at-risk youth

## Open Questions

- How can successful community-based programs be scaled up to reach more populations in need?
