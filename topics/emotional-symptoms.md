## Emotional Symptoms in Adolescent Refugees

The evaluation of a school-based creative arts therapy program for adolescent refugees highlights the significant impact of emotional symptoms on their overall development and behavior. Behavioral difficulties, often stemming from unresolved emotional issues, are prevalent among refugee adolescents, affecting not only their social interactions but also cognitive growth. Sources concur that addressing these emotional needs through targeted interventions can lead to substantial improvements in mental health outcomes. However, there is a tension between the efficacy of such programs in reducing emotional symptoms and ensuring long-term support for refugees' psychological well-being.

## Key Findings

- Emotional symptoms are common among adolescent refugees, impacting their cognitive development and behavior.
- Creative arts therapy can be an effective intervention to address emotional symptoms in this population.
- Behavioral difficulties often serve as a symptom of underlying unresolved emotional issues.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term effectiveness of creative arts therapy programs for refugee adolescents' mental health?
