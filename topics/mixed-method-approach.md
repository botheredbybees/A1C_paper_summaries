## Mixed-Methods Approach in Drama Therapy

The integration of qualitative and quantitative methodologies within the context of drama therapy for older people with dementia presents a robust framework to evaluate treatment efficacy. Source one underscores the importance of a mixed-method approach, emphasizing its role in enhancing self-awareness and improving quality of life (QoL) among participants. Qualitative data can provide deep insights into subjective experiences and personal meanings, while quantitative data offers objective measures for assessing therapeutic outcomes such as changes in QoL scores. This dual perspective not only enriches the understanding of individual experiences but also provides a comprehensive assessment that aids in refining therapeutic interventions.

## Key Findings

- Mixed-method approaches provide a more holistic view of therapy effectiveness than either qualitative or quantitative methods alone.
- Self-awareness and quality of life are key indicators for assessing the efficacy of drama therapy among older adults with dementia.
- Combining qualitative insights with quantitative measures offers a nuanced understanding of treatment impact.

## Open Questions

- How can mixed-method approaches be optimized to better understand long-term effects of drama therapy in aging populations?
