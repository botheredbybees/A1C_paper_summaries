## Elder-Clowning in Dementia Care

Elder-clowning is an emerging intervention aimed at mitigating the Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD) among residents with dementia. Both sources emphasize the role of elder-clowns in enhancing engagement, social interaction, and reducing distress in long-term care settings for individuals with dementia. The pilot studies reported improvements measured by the Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Nursing Home version (NPI-NH), a tool commonly used to assess BPSD. However, there is a noted gap in how elder-clowning aligns with established person-centered care principles and dementia-care mapping strategies, which focus on individualized care planning and social environment design. The studies also highlight the potential for elder-clowns to bridge these existing frameworks by providing unique, personalized interaction that can complement traditional therapeutic approaches.

## Key Findings

- Elder-clowning shows promise in reducing BPSD among dementia patients through enhanced engagement and positive social interactions.
- The Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Nursing Home version (NPI-NH) is a valuable tool for assessing the impact of elder-clowning interventions.
- There are potential synergies between elder-clowning and person-centered care principles, although further research is needed to fully understand these relationships.

## Open Questions

- How can elder-clowning be integrated with existing dementia-care mapping strategies without compromising individualized care approaches?
