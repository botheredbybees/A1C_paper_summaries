## Body Mapping in Trauma Therapy

Body mapping is presented as a therapeutic intervention within trauma therapy that focuses on enhancing clients' awareness of their physical sensations, particularly through breathing techniques. This practice aims to engage the vagal system and regulate the autonomic nervous system (ANS), thereby providing a physiological foundation for emotional regulation. The technique involves visualizing or drawing bodily responses to stress or trauma, helping individuals to understand how their body reacts in different situations. Across studies, it becomes evident that integrating somatic awareness through body mapping can provide a non-verbal means of processing traumatic experiences and fostering resilience.

## Key Findings

- Body mapping is an effective intervention for trauma therapy by enhancing awareness of the autonomic nervous system (ANS) responses
- Visualization or drawing of bodily sensations aids in understanding physical reactions to stress or trauma, promoting emotional regulation
- Engaging the vagal system through body-mapping techniques can improve clients' physiological response to traumatic stimuli
