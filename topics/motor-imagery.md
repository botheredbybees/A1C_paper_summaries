## Motor Imagery and Rhythmic Auditory Cueing

The study of motor imagery and its related intervention, rhythmic auditory cueing (RAC), highlights both promising benefits for Parkinson's disease patients and areas needing further exploration. Sources indicate that RAC can positively impact gait and stride in individuals with Parkinson's by entraining neural oscillations, specifically β oscillations, which are crucial for motor control. However, the exact mechanisms through which rhythmic auditory cues facilitate improved motor function remain unclear. Additionally, while motor imagery is suggested to enhance movement planning and execution, its direct relationship with RAC and its efficacy in clinical settings requires more detailed investigation.

## Key Findings

- Rhythmic auditory cueing (RAC) has a positive effect on gait and stride for Parkinson's disease patients
- Motor imagery may contribute to improved motor function planning and execution, but further research is needed
- β oscillations are important in the mechanism through which RAC improves motor control

## Open Questions

- What is the direct relationship between rhythmic auditory cueing (RAC) and motor imagery in enhancing motor function?
