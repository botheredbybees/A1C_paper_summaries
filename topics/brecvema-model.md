## Brecvema Model

The BRECVEMA model emerges as a pivotal theoretical framework within music psychology and neuroscience, integrating cognitive processes that underlie musical experiences such as earworms (involuntary catchy melodies) and the emotional impact of music on mood. This model is rooted in the Doctrine of Affections, an earlier theory suggesting specific musical modes evoke particular emotions. While both models recognize music's profound influence on human emotion and cognition, the BRECVEMA framework offers a more contemporary, detailed understanding by breaking down the complex processes involved in experiencing music emotionally and cognitively.

## Key Findings

- The BRECVEMA model explains intricate cognitive and emotional responses to music.
- Music impacts mood through both involuntary (e.g., earworms) and deliberate experiences.
- Historical theories like the Doctrine of Affections lay foundational principles for modern frameworks.

## Open Questions

- How can contemporary models like BRECVEMA be extended to account for cross-cultural differences in musical emotion?
