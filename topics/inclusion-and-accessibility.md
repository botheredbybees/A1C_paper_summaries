## Inclusion and Accessibility in Museum Design

The concept of museums as sites for promoting wellbeing is explored through the lens of architectural and museographic trends. Central to this discussion are the principles of health-promoting design, which aim to create environments that enhance physical and mental health outcomes for visitors. A key focus is on inclusion and accessibility, ensuring that museum spaces cater to diverse populations, including those with disabilities or chronic conditions. This approach draws heavily from environmental psychology, underscoring the importance of understanding how the built environment influences human behavior and emotional states. Despite these promising trends, there remains a need to balance aesthetic considerations with functional requirements for inclusivity.

## Key Findings

- Health-promoting design is pivotal in museum architecture to enhance visitor wellbeing
- Inclusion and accessibility are core aspects of modern museographic practices
- Environmental psychology informs the creation of inclusive spaces

## Open Questions

- How can museums balance aesthetic design with practical needs for inclusion?
