## Power Dynamics In Therapy

The integration of the concept of power dynamics within therapy reveals a nuanced perspective on how therapeutic practices can either empower or disempower clients, particularly in neurodiverse contexts. The source emphasizes the importance of viewing autism through the lens of 'neurodiversity theory,' which suggests that neurological differences such as those associated with autism should be recognized and respected as a form of human diversity rather than an impairment to be treated. This perspective challenges traditional medical models where therapists often hold a position of power, dictating treatments and norms for clients. By adopting a social justice framework, music therapy can become more inclusive and respectful towards the unique needs and cultural practices (like stimming) of neurodiverse individuals, reducing therapeutic imbalances.

## Key Findings

- Therapy must consider the concept of power dynamics to ensure equitable treatment
- Neurodiversity theory offers a critical perspective on autism that is respectful and empowering
- Inclusive practices in therapy can enhance client-centered care

## Open Questions

- How can music therapists effectively balance professional expertise with respect for neurodiverse cultural practices?
