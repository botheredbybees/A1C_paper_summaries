## Evaluation Versus Research

The distinction between research and evaluation is a critical consideration in the social sciences. While both aim to understand phenomena, they serve different purposes and employ distinct methodologies. 'An Introduction to Research' highlights that research involves rigorous scientific investigation aimed at understanding mechanisms of impact and broader theoretical constructs. In contrast, evaluation typically focuses on assessing the effectiveness or outcomes of specific interventions or programs, often with a direct application in policy or practice settings. This source underscores that while both methods are essential for advancing knowledge and improving practice, they require different approaches to data collection and analysis.

## Key Findings

- Research is characterized by its focus on understanding mechanisms of impact.
- Evaluation aims to assess the effectiveness of interventions or programs.
- Both research and evaluation serve distinct but complementary roles in evidence-based practice.

## Open Questions
