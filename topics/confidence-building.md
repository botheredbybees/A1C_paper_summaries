## Confidence Building

The sources highlight the role of arts-based interventions in fostering confidence among individuals. 'Disability Led Theatre' underscores how theatre can serve as a platform for disabled performers to challenge societal perceptions and build their self-confidence through inclusion and active participation. Similarly, 'Miss Lily's Fabulous Feather Boa' demonstrates that fashion and personal expression can enhance an individual's sense of self-worth by promoting non-judgmental attitudes towards appearance. Both sources converge on the idea that arts-based initiatives can be potent tools for empowering marginalized groups to challenge stereotypes and develop a stronger sense of identity.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions such as theatre and fashion can effectively enhance confidence in individuals from marginalized backgrounds.
- Inclusion and societal understanding are crucial components of effective confidence-building practices.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of arts-based confidence building initiatives compare to traditional forms of therapy or support?
