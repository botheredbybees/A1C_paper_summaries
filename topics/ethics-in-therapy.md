## Ethics In Therapy

The provided sources discuss the ethical implications of different therapeutic approaches. The Handbook of Phototherapy and Therapeutic Photography emphasizes the importance of considering ethical principles in phototherapy and therapeutic photography, such as consent, confidentiality, and visual representation. These concepts highlight the need for therapists to consider the unique challenges posed by these modalities when it comes to patient privacy and data security.

## Key Findings

- Ethics in therapy is crucial for maintaining trust between therapist and client
- Phototherapy and therapeutic photography raise specific ethical concerns such as visual representation and confidentiality
- Research methodologies need to be adapted to address the unique challenges of visual therapies

## Open Questions

- How can therapists effectively navigate the ethical complexities associated with using visual media in therapy?
