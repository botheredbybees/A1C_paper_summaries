## Situated Embodied Agent in Dementia

The concept of the Situated Embodied Agent (SEA) provides a framework for understanding dementia as an ongoing process rather than a decline. It emphasizes the individual's continuous engagement with their environment, suggesting that even as cognitive functions deteriorate, individuals maintain aspects of their identity and personhood through emotional connections and interactions. This perspective challenges traditional views by highlighting the importance of preserving the patient's sense of self and social relationships despite cognitive changes.

## Key Findings

- The SEA concept underlines dementia as an ongoing process that preserves elements of individual identity.
- Emotional connections and environmental engagement are critical in maintaining personhood for those with dementia.
- SEA challenges traditional medical models focusing solely on the loss of cognitive abilities.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare practices better integrate SEA principles to enhance quality of life for individuals living with dementia?
