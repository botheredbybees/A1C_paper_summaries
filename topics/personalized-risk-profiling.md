## Personalized Risk Profiling

The topic of personalized risk profiling highlights the use of individual data to predict health risks like Alzheimer's disease. Source 1 focuses on a program at Cedars-Sinai that uses personalized risk profiling to identify modifiable factors for Alzheimer’s disease in healthy middle-aged individuals. The Montreal Cognitive Assessment is used as part of this profiling process, emphasizing early detection and potential interventions before the onset of full-blown symptoms.

## Key Findings

- Personalized risk profiling can help identify individual-specific risks associated with diseases such as Alzheimer's at an earlier stage.
- The identification of modifiable risk factors through personalized profiling may lead to targeted preventive measures for individuals.

## Open Questions

- How effective are the interventions based on personalized risk profiles in delaying or preventing disease onset?
