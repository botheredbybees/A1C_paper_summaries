## Ethical Data Analysis and Reporting

Sources emphasize the importance of ethical considerations in data analysis and dissemination within arts-in-health research. Both sources highlight the preservation of participant anonymity as a critical aspect, ensuring that all identifiers are removed to protect individual privacy. The Vancouver Protocol is mentioned across both documents as an essential guideline for authorship and reporting standards, underscoring the need for transparency and credibility in academic publications. However, there is a slight variation in terminology; while one source uses 'Vancouver Protocol' broadly, the other specifies it in terms of authorship guidelines, indicating potential nuances in application that require further clarification.

## Key Findings

- Anonymity preservation is crucial to protect participant privacy
- The Vancouver Protocol guides ethical reporting and authorship standards

## Open Questions

- How do the nuances in applying the Vancouver Protocol for authorship differ from its broader implications in data analysis?
