## Global Perspectives On Arts And Health

The integration of creative arts in healthcare frameworks across various cultural contexts has shown significant achievements but also faces substantial challenges. A notable achievement is the broadening understanding of what constitutes health, as defined by the World Health Organization (WHO), beyond mere absence of disease to include mental and social well-being. Community arts practices have been pivotal in addressing these holistic aspects of health, fostering community engagement and resilience globally. However, there are disparities in how creative arts interventions are implemented across different regions due to varying socio-economic conditions, cultural norms, and healthcare infrastructures. These differences highlight the necessity for culturally sensitive approaches and local adaptations while maintaining a global perspective.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts practices contribute significantly to holistic health definitions that extend beyond physical well-being.
- Community-based arts interventions are essential in addressing mental and social aspects of health.
- Cultural context plays a critical role in shaping the effectiveness of creative arts interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can global standards be adapted to local cultural contexts without losing their efficacy?
