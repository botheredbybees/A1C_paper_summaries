## Colour Treatment

The concept of the Colour Treatment emerges as an intersection between art and medicine in addressing various forms of 'nervous disorder.' The intervention leverages abstract theories of colour to provide therapeutic benefits. Sources highlight a convergence where visual aesthetics, particularly through the application of specific colours, are employed in medical contexts for healing purposes. While the treatment's mechanism is grounded in abstract principles about the influence of colors on human emotions and psyche, its practical implementation varies. There is an emphasis on using color therapy as part of a broader regimen to treat patients with nervous disorders within medical institutions.

## Key Findings

- Colour Treatment converges art and medicine for therapeutic purposes.
- Abstract colour theories are applied in treating nervous disorders.
- Colour therapy is implemented in specific settings like the Red Cross Russell Lea Nerve Home.
