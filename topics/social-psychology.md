## Empathy and Prosocial Behavior

The integrative model of empathy posits that empathetic responses are regulated by a control system which mediates between emotional arousal and prosocial behavior. This model highlights the importance of understanding how empathy can be harnessed to promote altruistic acts, as discussed in 'Happy to help?'. That work, focusing on performing acts of kindness, underscores the beneficial effects of such behaviors on subjective well-being through mechanisms of kin and reciprocal altruism, aligning with mutualistic relationships. Both sources converge on the role of empathy in facilitating prosocial behavior, though they differ slightly in their emphasis: one focuses on the control system underlying empathetic responses, while the other centers on the outcomes and benefits of engaging in acts of kindness.

## Key Findings

- Empathy plays a crucial role in eliciting prosocial behaviors through both emotional and cognitive processes.
- Acts of kindness positively influence subjective well-being via mechanisms rooted in evolutionary theory such as kin altruism and reciprocal altruism.

## Open Questions

- How does the integrative model of empathy, which emphasizes the control system's role, interact with findings on acts of kindness to explain variability in prosocial behavior across different contexts?
