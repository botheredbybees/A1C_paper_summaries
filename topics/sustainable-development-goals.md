## Creative Health and Sustainable Development Goals

The concept of 'Creative Health' emerges as a key intervention in addressing health inequalities while aligning with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Sources highlight the integration of arts-based activities within healthcare systems, such as social prescribing programs, which aim to promote individual well-being and community resilience. These initiatives are noted for their potential cost-effectiveness compared to traditional medical interventions. However, there is a tension between the scalability of these creative health practices and the need for robust evaluation frameworks to ensure they meet both public health needs and SDG objectives.

## Key Findings

- Creative Health initiatives can significantly contribute to reducing health inequalities within communities.
- The integration of arts-based social prescribing programs into healthcare systems is gaining recognition as a cost-effective approach to promoting mental and physical well-being.
- There is a growing emphasis on evaluating the long-term impact and scalability of creative health interventions in line with SDGs.

## Open Questions

- How can creative health practices be effectively scaled up while maintaining their alignment with sustainable development goals and cultural sensitivity?
