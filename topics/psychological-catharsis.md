## Psychological Catharsis

The concept of psychological catharsis is explored across various therapeutic contexts, including the use of puppetry in therapy sessions as a diagnostic tool and method for non-verbal expression. Sources highlight how puppets can serve as intermediaries that help clients articulate emotions they might find difficult to express directly due to social inhibitions or trauma-related barriers. This approach facilitates psychological catharsis by providing an avenue for emotional release and processing, which is crucial in therapeutic settings where direct verbal communication may be insufficient or overly challenging.

## Key Findings

- Puppets can act as diagnostic tools that reveal underlying emotional states through non-verbal expression.
- Therapeutic puppetry serves as a method to facilitate psychological catharsis by allowing clients to indirectly express repressed emotions and experiences.
- The use of puppets in therapy can help bridge the gap between cognitive understanding and emotional processing.
