## Social Isolation Among Marginalized Youth

The sources explore how arts engagement and cultural practices can mitigate social isolation among marginalized groups. Source 1 discusses arts programs that enhance a sense of connection and provide in-the-moment pleasure, which are crucial for combating feelings of loneliness and disconnection within communities. Conversely, Source 2 examines the role of hip hop culture in providing migrant youth with a platform to express their cultural identity and negotiate social challenges in regional towns. Both studies highlight arts and culturally significant activities as effective interventions against social isolation by fostering community engagement and self-expression.

## Key Findings

- Arts engagement programs can significantly enhance a sense of connection among individuals.
- Cultural practices, such as hip hop, serve as vital outlets for marginalized youth to express identity and navigate societal challenges.

## Open Questions

- How do varying cultural contexts influence the effectiveness of arts-based interventions in combating social isolation?
