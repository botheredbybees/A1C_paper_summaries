## Continuum Of Applied Theatre

The continuum of applied theatre represents a spectrum of practices that emphasize the transformational power of theatrical activities within various social contexts. The concept underscores the idea that theatre can serve multiple purposes, from direct interventions in community settings to more indirect forms of cultural critique and personal development. Across different sources, there is agreement on the transformative potential inherent in applying theatrical techniques outside traditional performance venues. However, tensions arise regarding how far one can stretch the term 'theatre' without diluting its artistic integrity or efficacy as a social tool. Additionally, while all sources acknowledge the diverse applications of applied theatre, they highlight the need for clear definitions and boundaries to guide practitioners and maintain public understanding.

## Key Findings

- The transformative power of theatre is widely recognized in various social contexts.
- There is agreement on the broad spectrum of practices encompassed by applied theatre.
- Clear definitions are essential for guiding practice and maintaining integrity.

## Open Questions

- How far can the term 'theatre' be stretched while retaining its transformative power?
