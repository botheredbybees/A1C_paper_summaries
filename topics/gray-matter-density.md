## Gray Matter Density Changes Across Lifespan

Research on gray matter density focuses on developmental changes from childhood to young adulthood. Source 1 highlights age-related effects and sex differences in gray matter, indicating variability in volume, mass, and cortical thickness across these stages. In contrast, Source 2 emphasizes brain development during adolescence specifically, noting improvements in executive function and reward processing alongside cognitive-emotional maturation. While both sources underscore the dynamic nature of neural structures over time, they differ slightly in scope, with one detailing sex differences and age-related changes broadly while the other centers on adolescent-specific neurodevelopment.

## Key Findings

- Age is a significant factor influencing gray matter density across different life stages.
- Sex differences play a role in how gray matter density varies over time.
