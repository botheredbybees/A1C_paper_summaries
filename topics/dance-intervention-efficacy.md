## Effectiveness of Dance Interventions

A systematic review and meta-analysis examining the effectiveness of dance interventions for preventing falls in older adults reveals that these programs have a significant positive impact on reducing fall risk. The studies included highlight improvements in balance, gait, and physical function as key outcomes associated with dance intervention participation. However, there is variability in the design and reporting of functional outcome measures across different dance programs, which complicates comparisons between interventions. Despite this variation, the overall consensus is that dance can be a beneficial component of falls prevention strategies for older adults.

## Key Findings

- Dance interventions effectively reduce fall risk in older adults.
- Improvements in balance, gait, and physical function are key outcomes of dance programs.
- Variability exists in reporting functional outcome measures across studies.

## Open Questions

- What standardized functional outcome measures should be used to assess the efficacy of dance interventions?
