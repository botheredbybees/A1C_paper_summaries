## Mental Well-being Through Creative Practices

The integration of creative practices such as art therapy and journaling into mental health interventions demonstrates a positive impact on various aspects of mental well-being. Source 1 highlights the nurturing benefits of creativity for individuals suffering from dementia, illustrating how engaging in artistic activities can improve quality of life beyond purely therapeutic means. This aligns with the broader concept discussed in Sources 3 and 4, which advocate for the National Arts and Health Framework's integration into public health initiatives. These frameworks emphasize not only specialized art therapy but also general engagement with recreational arts as a method to enhance mental well-being in diverse populations. Source 2 introduces reflective journaling as another tool for emotional introspection, complementing the visual creative practices by providing a textual avenue for expression and self-discovery. However, there is an underlying tension between therapeutic specificity (as seen in structured art therapy) and broader general engagement with arts activities which may vary widely in nature and effect.

## Key Findings

- Creative practices such as art therapy and journaling contribute positively to mental well-being across various populations.
- The integration of the National Arts and Health Framework into public health initiatives can enhance accessibility and variety of creative interventions for better mental health outcomes.
- Recreational arts engagement shows a dose-response relationship with improvements in mental well-being, indicating that more frequent participation correlates with greater benefits.

## Open Questions

- How does the efficacy of structured art therapy compare to general recreational arts engagement when considering long-term mental health outcomes?
