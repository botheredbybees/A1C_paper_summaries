## Social-Environmental Factors in Dementia Care

The integration of person-centred dementia care highlights the critical role of social and environmental factors in enhancing patient outcomes. Values-based practice is emphasized, advocating for individualized care that respects each patient's unique needs and preferences. This approach underscores the necessity of adapting environments to support cognitive and emotional well-being, emphasizing the importance of creating supportive spaces that foster dignity and independence. Despite the consensus on the value of tailored interventions, challenges remain in scaling these practices across diverse settings due to resource constraints.

## Key Findings

- values-based practice enhances patient-centered care
- individualization is crucial for effective dementia management
- environmental adaptations improve cognitive and emotional well-being

## Open Questions

- How can person-centred care be effectively implemented in resource-limited settings?
