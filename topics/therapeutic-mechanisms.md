## Mechanisms of Therapeutic Engagement in Arts-Based Interventions

The integration of therapeutic arts-based interventions, such as music therapy and dance/movement therapy, reveals a complex interplay between aesthetic, physical, and relational engagements. Interactive music therapy emphasizes the psychological and emotional impact through music-induced experiences, while dance/movement therapy focuses on somatic modalities and their influence on neurobiological processes. Both therapies suggest that engagement with the arts can lead to beneficial changes in mental health by modulating stress responses, enhancing mood regulation, and fostering social connections. However, there is a need for further research to delineate specific mechanisms of change and their neurological underpinnings.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions like music therapy and dance/movement therapy engage patients through multiple channels including aesthetic, physical, and relational dimensions.
- These therapies potentially influence mental health outcomes by altering neurobiological processes related to stress response and mood regulation.
- The effectiveness of arts-based interventions appears to be tied to active patient participation and the creation of meaningful therapeutic relationships.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neural mechanisms that underpin changes in patients undergoing arts-based therapies, and how do these differ across various types of engagement?
