## Lgbtq Youth

The synthesis of research on LGBTQ youth reveals a consistent focus on the social determinants of their mental health. The concept of minority stress theory is prevalent across studies, highlighting the impact of stigma and discrimination on this population's well-being. Additionally, sources underscore the importance of family rejection as a significant factor contributing to adverse outcomes such as mental health issues. Creative arts therapy emerges as a promising approach for youth empowerment and identity exploration within this community. A life-course perspective is advocated for understanding the long-term impacts of social isolation and connectedness on LGBTQ youth's overall well-being.

## Key Findings

- Stigma and discrimination significantly impact the mental health of LGBTQ youth through minority stress theory.
- Family rejection exacerbates risks to the psychological well-being of LGBTQ adolescents.
- Creative arts therapy offers avenues for empowerment and identity exploration among LGBTQ youth.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts therapies be effectively integrated into comprehensive care models addressing social isolation and family rejection?
