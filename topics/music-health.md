## Music And Health

The exploration of music's role in health and wellbeing reveals two distinct yet interrelated dimensions. The first dimension, as discussed in the source 'A Music and Health Perspective on Music's Perceived Goodness', delves into how intrinsic values attributed to music contribute to its perceived benefits for individuals' psychological and physical well-being. This perspective raises questions about the commodification of music, suggesting that commercial aspects may overshadow or influence the genuine therapeutic potential inherent in musical experiences. The second dimension, outlined in 'Week 6 Activities - FXA301 Arts in the Community', focuses on active-participation activities aimed at promoting health and wellbeing through community engagement with arts and music. This source highlights the importance of equitable access to such resources for all communities to ensure that everyone can benefit from the positive impacts of music. Together, these sources indicate a complex relationship between music's inherent qualities and its practical application in enhancing public health.

## Key Findings

- Music is perceived as having intrinsic value beneficial to health and well-being
- Equitable access to arts and music participation activities can promote community health
- Commercial influences may affect the true therapeutic benefits of music

## Open Questions

- How do commercial aspects influence public perception of music's therapeutic potential?
