## Creative Engagement

The sources collectively highlight the significance of creative engagement as a versatile and effective intervention for enhancing personal well-being across diverse populations. Source 1 underscores the multifaceted nature of creativity, emphasizing personalized care approaches that can be tailored to individual non-verbal communication needs. This foundational understanding is expanded by Source 2, which introduces the measurement of arts participation specific to First Nations communities, indicating a direct correlation between cultural relevance and improved mental health and social connectivity through creative activities. Finally, Source 3 offers a poignant example of how therapeutic clowns can provide emotional support in nursing homes, illustrating another dimension of creative engagement that meets both psychological and physical care needs.

## Key Findings

- Creative engagement is adaptable to various cultural contexts, enhancing mental health outcomes.
- Non-verbal communication plays a crucial role in effective personalized creative interventions.
- Therapeutic clowns serve as an innovative method for providing emotional support to older adults.

## Open Questions
