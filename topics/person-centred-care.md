## Person-Centred Dementia Care

Person-centred dementia care emphasizes the importance of recognizing and respecting each person's unique identity and needs. Sources highlight two key elements in this approach: 'embodied selfhood' (the idea that personal identity is tied to one's physical body and experiences) and values-based practice, which involves individualization based on social and environmental factors. These sources collectively stress the necessity of understanding dementia patients within their broader context, rather than solely focusing on the symptoms or impairments associated with the disease. They also suggest a shift from traditional care approaches that may ignore personal preferences to more holistic methods aimed at preserving dignity and autonomy.

## Key Findings

- Person-centred dementia care emphasizes individualized treatment based on unique needs.
- The concept of 'embodied selfhood' is crucial in understanding the person beyond their illness.
- Values-based practice is integral to effective person-centred care.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare providers effectively implement values-based practices while addressing diverse social and environmental factors?
