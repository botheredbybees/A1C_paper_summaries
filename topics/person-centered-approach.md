## Person-Centered Approach

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights the foundational role of 'personhood' within a person-centered approach. Both sources emphasize that recognizing and affirming an individual's personhood is central to this intervention, aiming to reduce stigma and promote inclusive language (Source 1). This recognition also extends into therapeutic interventions where biographical knowledge about the patient plays a crucial role in maintaining their sense of self and dignity (Source 2). However, while Source 1 focuses on language guidelines to support person-centered practices, Source 2 delves deeper into how these principles are operationalized through specific therapeutic actions. The integration of both perspectives underscores the multidimensional nature of the person-centered approach, which includes not only the way practitioners communicate with patients but also the practical steps they take in treatment settings.

## Key Findings

- Personhood is a core component of person-centered approaches.
- Inclusive language and stigma reduction are important aspects of these interventions.
- Biographical knowledge contributes significantly to maintaining an individual's sense of self in therapeutic contexts.
