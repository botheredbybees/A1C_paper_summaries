## Supporting Youth Mental Health Through Arts-Based Strategies

Arts-based strategies have emerged as a promising approach to supporting youth mental health globally. Both sources emphasize the importance of integrating local cultural practices with arts-and-culture-based interventions to effectively address mental health disorders in youth. Elevating youth voice is seen as critical, ensuring that young people are not only recipients but also active participants and contributors in their own care. However, there is a tension between the need for localized approaches sensitive to specific cultural contexts and the desire for core outcome measures that can be used universally across diverse populations. This highlights the challenge of balancing specificity with generalizability.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based strategies are globally recognized as beneficial for youth mental health.
- Local cultural practices should inform arts-and-culture-based interventions to enhance their relevance and effectiveness.
- Elevating youth voice is crucial in mental health support, encouraging active participation.

## Open Questions

- How can core outcome measures be developed that are both culturally sensitive and universally applicable?
