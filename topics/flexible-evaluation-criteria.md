## Flexible Evaluation Criteria

The concept of flexible evaluation criteria is explored in the context of measuring success and providing person-centered care. It emphasizes the importance of assessing outcomes through methods that are tailored to individual needs rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach. This intervention promotes creativity and flexibility in how individuals' progress and success are evaluated, ensuring that the assessment processes align with personal values and circumstances. Across sources, there is an agreement on the necessity of person-centered care for effective evaluation but tensions arise regarding how to balance individualized criteria with standardized measures.

## Key Findings

- Flexible evaluation criteria support personalized approaches in measuring outcomes.
- Person-centered care enhances the relevance and effectiveness of assessment processes.
- Creative expression plays a crucial role in developing tailored evaluation methods.

## Open Questions

- How can flexible evaluation criteria be effectively integrated into existing healthcare systems while maintaining quality standards?
