## Brain Structure and Function

The exploration of brain structure reveals a complex network where various regions play specific roles in cognitive, emotional, and memory functions. Sources highlight the limbic system's crucial role in both emotion and memory but challenge the notion of it as a single unitary system due to its structural complexity (Source 2). The hippocampus stands out for its pivotal role in memory formation, supported by findings from multiple sources (Sources 2 and 3). Additionally, sensory processing integrates with executive functions, underlining the brain's comprehensive approach to information perception (Source 1). Meanwhile, specific structures like the amygdala are tied closely to emotional responses, while the orbitofrontal cortex modulates decision-making processes in light of emotional contexts. These findings underscore the intricate interplay between different neural regions that support higher cognitive functions.

## Key Findings

- The hippocampus is critical for memory formation and retrieval
- Emotional processing relies heavily on the amygdala, but decision-making involves additional input from the orbitofrontal cortex
- Sensory information processing interacts with executive function to shape perception

## Open Questions

- How do individual neural structures work in concert to achieve complex cognitive tasks such as emotional regulation and memory consolidation?
