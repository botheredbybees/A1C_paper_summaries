## Healing Practices

The concept of healing practices involves the use of various interventions aimed at improving mental and physical well-being through arts-based activities. Across sources, there is a common theme of utilizing engagement modes such as visual arts, music therapy, and other creative expression methods to foster neural interactions that contribute to therapeutic outcomes. Each source highlights specific aspects of these practices, like how different forms of art can activate neural pathways linked with stress reduction and positive emotional regulation, thereby serving as effective healing mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Healing practices encompass a range of arts-based interventions designed to improve mental health and well-being.
- Engagement modes such as visual arts and music therapy are central to these practices, engaging neural pathways involved in stress reduction and positive emotional regulation.
- Neural interactions stimulated through healing practices contribute significantly to the therapeutic benefits experienced by participants.
