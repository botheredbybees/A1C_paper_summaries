## Distraction Therapy

The concept of Distraction Therapy emerges as a valuable intervention in pediatric practice and beyond. It is particularly effective during medical procedures that may cause pain or anxiety, such as anesthesia induction. Therapeutic clowning is one innovative method under this umbrella, designed to divert the patient's attention away from discomfort by engaging them in playful and interactive activities. This form of therapy not only assists children but also helps reduce parental anxiety, creating a more supportive environment for medical procedures. While it shows promise, its broader application across different populations and settings remains an area ripe for exploration.

## Key Findings

- Distraction Therapy is effective during painful or stressful medical procedures.
- Therapeutic clowning is a method of Distraction Therapy that engages children through playful interaction.
- Parental anxiety can be reduced alongside the child's comfort with therapeutic clowning.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and broader applications of Distraction Therapy beyond pediatric anesthesia induction?
