## Systems Thinking

The concept of systems thinking emerges prominently in the sources provided, particularly through its role as a methodology for analyzing complex issues and identifying interconnected relationships. In one source, systems thinking is discussed alongside design-thinking and problem-tree-analysis, indicating its application in developing comprehensive research projects that consider multiple perspectives and factors. These methodologies aim to address problems holistically by examining not just isolated elements but also the interconnections between them, which can lead to more effective solutions and interventions.

## Key Findings

- Systems thinking is employed as a method for addressing complex issues.
- Integration of systems thinking with problem-tree-analysis offers a comprehensive approach to research project development.
- Design-thinking complements systems thinking by focusing on innovative design solutions within the context of systemic analysis.
