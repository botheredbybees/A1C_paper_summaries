## Creative Movement Programs

Creative movement programs, such as dance therapy and intergenerational engagement activities, have emerged as effective interventions to enhance the well-being of various populations. Both sources highlight the importance of these programs in reducing stigma associated with conditions like dementia (Source 1) and fostering generativity through meaningful intergenerational connections (Source 2). The integration of artistic expression and physical movement not only provides therapeutic benefits but also encourages social interaction, thereby enriching the quality of life for participants. However, there is a need to further explore the specific mechanisms by which these programs achieve their positive outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Creative movement programs effectively reduce stigma in populations affected by conditions like dementia.
- Intergenerational engagement through creative movement enhances generativity and social interaction.
- Artistic expression and physical activity are integral to improving quality of life for participants.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms contribute to the therapeutic benefits observed in creative movement programs?
