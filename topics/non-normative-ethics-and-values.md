## Non-Normative Ethics and Values

The concept of non-normative ethics and values within professional education and practice emerges as a significant framework that challenges traditional ethical paradigms. The exploration of compassion in practice, alongside creative curriculums, highlights the importance of fostering unique value systems tailored to individual contexts rather than adhering strictly to universal norms. This approach encourages professionals to develop ethical competencies through experiential learning and reflective practice, thus enabling a more flexible and responsive application of ethics.

## Key Findings

- Non-normative ethics emphasizes values that are context-specific over universally accepted moral standards.
- Creative curriculums enhance the development of non-normative ethics among health professionals through experiential learning.
- Compassion in practice is integral to the application of non-normative ethical frameworks.
