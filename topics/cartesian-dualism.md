## Cartesian Dualism

The concept of Cartesian dualism, introduced by René Descartes, posits a clear distinction between the mind (res cogitans) and body (res extensa). This theory has been influential in shaping various fields including psychology and healthcare. Sigmund Freud's psychoanalytic model builds upon some aspects of this dualistic view, particularly concerning the unconscious mind’s influence on behavior. The biopsychosocial model, however, critiques Cartesian dualism by integrating biological, psychological, and social factors to provide a more holistic understanding of health and illness.

## Key Findings

- Cartesian dualism proposes a clear separation between mind and body.
- Sigmund Freud’s psychoanalytic theory incorporates elements of dualistic thought, especially regarding the unconscious mind.
- The biopsychosocial model challenges Cartesian dualism by emphasizing an integrated approach to understanding health.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare practices effectively integrate a holistic view while acknowledging historical contributions from Cartesian dualism?
