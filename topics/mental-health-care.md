## Arts and Humor Interventions in Mental Health Care

The integration of arts and humor into mental health care has shown promising outcomes across various contexts. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of collaboration between artists and mental health professionals to foster social inclusion and community engagement, highlighting the therapeutic potential of creative activities. This aligns with the findings from Source 2, which demonstrates that a clown doctor program positively impacts mood, patient engagement, and overall ward atmosphere in adolescent psychiatric units through humor-based interventions. Both sources underscore the role of non-traditional therapies in enhancing quality of life and social integration for patients dealing with mental health issues.

## Key Findings

- Collaboration between artists and healthcare professionals can enhance social inclusion in mental health settings.
- Humor-based interventions, such as clown doctor programs, improve mood and patient engagement in psychiatric units.
- Creative arts therapies contribute to a more positive ward atmosphere.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of humor-based interventions on mental health outcomes?
