## Staff Attitudes Towards Creative Expression Interventions

The integration of creative expression interventions like TimeSlips in dementia care settings highlights a shift towards more person-centered and humane approaches. These programs not only benefit nursing home residents with dementia by increasing their engagement but also influence staff attitudes positively. Sources converge on the transformative impact of such interventions, noting that they reduce stress and burnout among caregivers due to enhanced resident interactions. However, there is a tension between recognizing the value of creative interventions and ensuring their consistent implementation across different care settings. The challenge remains in aligning institutional policies with these innovative practices.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression programs like TimeSlips improve resident engagement
- Staff attitudes towards dementia care are positively influenced by such interventions
- Reduced stress and burnout among caregivers correlate with increased use of creative therapies

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to implementing creative intervention programs consistently across various nursing home settings?
