## Therapeutic Photography as Visual Pedagogy

Therapeutic photography is highlighted as an intervention that leverages the power of visual images to enhance self-esteem, self-efficacy, and resilience in individuals. This approach emphasizes visual pedagogy, a method where visual materials are used not just for observation but also for critical reflection and learning. The source underscores the importance of power dynamics between therapists and participants during interviews, indicating that an understanding of these dynamics is crucial for effective therapeutic outcomes. Furthermore, therapeutic photography operates within the framework of emancipatory research, which aims to empower individuals through a participatory process where they take control over their own narratives.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography enhances self-esteem and resilience in participants by providing a platform for visual expression and reflection
- Understanding power dynamics is crucial for effective therapeutic interactions
- Emancipatory research principles underpin the use of therapeutic photography

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic photography be adapted to cater to diverse populations with varying cultural backgrounds?
