## Arts Funding

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights key aspects of arts funding initiatives and processes. Source one ('How to apply for money for your arts project') emphasizes the procedural steps required by funders, including eligibility criteria and evaluation methods for arts projects. Source two ('Peer Handbook for Assessment Panels') focuses on the assessment side, detailing the importance of conflict-of-interest management and adherence to a code of conduct to uphold artistic integrity. Lastly, source three ('Creative Australia: Advancing Arts through Research') introduces specific funding initiatives such as the Creative Futures Fund and mentions research like the Street Dance Survey that contributes to understanding the arts sector. These sources collectively illustrate how arts funding involves not only applications and assessments but also considerations for ethical governance and research.

## Key Findings

- Eligibility criteria are a critical aspect of applying for arts funding.
- Conflict-of-interest management is essential in arts assessment panels.
- Research initiatives like the Creative Futures Fund contribute to understanding arts needs.
