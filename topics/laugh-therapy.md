## Laughter Therapy

Laughter therapy emerges as a unique intervention with potential benefits across diverse patient populations. In chronic kidney disease patients undergoing dialysis, laughter yoga and humor therapy can enhance mood and potentially improve physiological measures (Source 1). Similarly, in long-term care settings for dementia patients, Laughter Care Specialists use novel communication methods to engage residents and provide meaningful interactions at the end of life (Source 2). Despite differing patient populations and therapeutic contexts, both studies highlight laughter's role in fostering social connections and emotional well-being. However, while Source 1 emphasizes physiological benefits, Source 2 focuses more on qualitative aspects such as engagement and communication, suggesting a need for further research to understand broader applications and mechanisms of laughter therapy.

## Key Findings

- Laughter therapy can enhance mood and potentially improve physiological measures in chronic kidney disease patients.
- Novel communication methods facilitated by Laughter Care Specialists can engage dementia patients and provide meaningful interactions.
- Both contexts highlight the social benefits of laughter in fostering connections and enhancing emotional well-being.

## Open Questions

- How do the mechanisms underlying laughter's physiological and psychological benefits differ between acute and chronic health conditions?
