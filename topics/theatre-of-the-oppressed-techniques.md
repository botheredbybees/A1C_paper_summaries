## Theatre Of The Oppressed Techniques

The Theatre of the Oppressed (TO) techniques, originally developed by Augusto Boal as a means for social change and political awareness through theatre, have been adapted into therapeutic interventions in various contexts. One such adaptation is its application in dementia care, where TO techniques are employed to empower individuals with dementia, providing them with opportunities to express their thoughts and feelings through dramatic enactment. This method aligns well with the principles of person-centred care guidelines that emphasize individual choice and autonomy. The integration of these theatrical methods into healthcare settings highlights a shift towards more holistic approaches in therapeutic interventions, aiming to enhance not just physical or mental health but also social engagement and self-expression.

## Key Findings

- Theatre of the Oppressed techniques can be adapted for use in dementia care.
- TO techniques align with person-centred care principles by promoting autonomy and expression.
- These methods are part of a broader trend towards more holistic therapeutic approaches.
