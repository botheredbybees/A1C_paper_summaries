## Reflective Writing

Reflective writing is a method used in various contexts to promote self-reflection and learning through the articulation of personal experiences. Both sources emphasize the importance of journaling as a foundational tool for reflective practice, highlighting its role in capturing raw thoughts and emotions without initial judgment (uncensored-writing). The process involves revisiting these entries later to edit them critically, selecting elements that provide deeper insights or new understandings (caption-editing, peer-feedback). Additionally, visual complements are suggested as an enriching aspect of reflection, where images or other media can enhance the written narrative and create a more holistic expression of experiences (creative-expression, visual-complementarity).

## Key Findings

- Journaling is foundational to reflective writing practice.
- Editing plays a crucial role in developing deeper insights through reflective writing.
- Visual complements can enrich the reflection process.

## Open Questions

- How does the integration of visual elements impact the depth and breadth of reflection?
