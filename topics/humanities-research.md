## Play and People Living With Dementia

The concept of 'homo ludens', or the human as a playing being, is central to understanding interventions such as TimeSlips and the Alzheimer's Poetry Project for individuals with dementia. Both sources emphasize the transformative power of these programs in fostering creativity and social interaction among participants. Despite similarities in objectives, there are nuances in how each program approaches play-based therapy, highlighting both common ground and methodological differences.

## Key Findings

- Play-centric interventions like TimeSlips and Alzheimer's Poetry Project offer unique benefits for individuals with dementia by promoting cognitive engagement and emotional well-being.
- The humanities perspective on therapeutic activities underscores the importance of creativity in addressing the needs of those living with dementia.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of play-based interventions such as TimeSlips and Alzheimer's Poetry Project on individuals living with dementia?
