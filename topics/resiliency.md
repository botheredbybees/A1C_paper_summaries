## Resiliency

The concept of resiliency encompasses various theoretical perspectives, including the role of humor and positive psychology in facilitating coping mechanisms. Resiliency is seen as a dynamic process that enables individuals to adapt effectively to adverse conditions or stressors. Positive psychological approaches emphasize character strengths, such as optimism, hope, and humor, which contribute significantly to resilience. Humor acts as a buffer against negative emotions and facilitates emotion regulation, thereby enhancing adaptive responses in challenging situations. This theoretical framework integrates the idea of resiliency as an active process rather than a static trait.

## Key Findings

- Humor is integral to fostering emotional resilience.
- Character strengths play a crucial role in building psychological resilience.
- Resilience is viewed through a positive psychology lens, focusing on growth and adaptation.

## Open Questions

- How does the integration of humor into traditional coping mechanisms enhance overall resiliency?
