## Normalization Prediction in Autism Research

The 'normalization' prediction within the context of the early brain overgrowth hypothesis of autism suggests a return to typical brain growth patterns as children with autism age. However, recent evidence challenges this assumption by highlighting inconsistencies and interactions with IQ levels that complicate straightforward predictions about brain development trajectories in autistic individuals. Across studies, there is agreement on the need for longitudinal data to better understand developmental pathways, but disagreements arise over how normalization should be defined and measured, particularly considering varying cognitive profiles among those diagnosed with autism.

## Key Findings

- The 'normalization' prediction within the early brain overgrowth hypothesis of autism is under scrutiny.
- IQ levels significantly influence the interpretation of brain development patterns in individuals with autism.
- Longitudinal studies are crucial for understanding developmental trajectories in autism, but there remains a lack of consensus on defining and measuring normalization.

## Open Questions

- How should 'normalization' be operationally defined to account for variability in cognitive abilities among autistic populations?
