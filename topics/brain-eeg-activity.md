## Brain Eeg Activity in Response to Play Therapy

The provided sources indicate the impact of Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) on children with autism as measured by brain EEG activity. Source 1 presents findings from a randomized controlled trial demonstrating how CCPT can alter neural processes in children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), suggesting that play therapy is not only effective at a behavioral and social level but also induces observable changes in the neurological functioning of these individuals. The study underlines the potential of non-pharmacological interventions like CCPT to address both the outward symptoms of ASD as well as underlying neurobiological mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) can influence brain EEG activity in children with autism, indicating changes at a neural level beyond observable behavioral changes.
- Non-pharmacological interventions such as CCPT show promise in addressing both symptomatic and neurological aspects of Autism Spectrum Disorder.
