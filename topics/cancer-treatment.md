## Creative Therapies in Cancer Treatment

The provided sources collectively explore the role of creative therapies like art and music therapy in cancer treatment. Art therapy is highlighted for its capacity to facilitate self-expression, emotional release, and community-building among patients (Sources 1 & 3). Confessional writing through photography emerges as another therapeutic modality, serving as a medium for emotional expression (Source 2). Additionally, the integration of art into healthcare settings is shown to have significant therapeutic benefits, such as fostering hope and strengthening patient-community bonds (Source 4). Music therapy complements these creative interventions by enhancing quality of life and providing palliative care through a grounded theory approach (Source 5). While each source underscores the importance of creativity in healing processes, they also reveal a need for more standardized methodologies to quantify their impact on patient outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapies like art and music provide pathways for emotional expression and community building among cancer patients.
- Photography as a form of confessional writing enables deep emotional processing through visual storytelling.
- Integrating arts into medical facilities can significantly enhance the therapeutic environment, fostering hope and community.

## Open Questions

- How can creative therapies be standardized to measure their effectiveness across diverse patient populations?
