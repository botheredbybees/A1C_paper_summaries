## Puppetry In Creative Arts Therapy

The application of puppetry in creative arts therapy emerges as a versatile tool that supports emotional development and mental well-being. Sources indicate the therapeutic use of puppets, emphasizing indirect expression and communication through objects, which can be less threatening for individuals to articulate their feelings. Puppetry is also positioned within a cross-disciplinary framework that integrates various forms of expressive arts. The integration of puppetry with other creative methods enhances its applicability in addressing mental health issues by providing an alternative medium for emotional exploration and skill development.

## Key Findings

- Puppetry serves as an indirect mode of expression which can aid individuals in conveying complex emotions more comfortably.
- The use of puppets within therapeutic contexts offers a supportive framework for enhancing emotional skills through creative play.
- Integrating puppetry into broader cross-disciplinary creative arts approaches amplifies its effectiveness in treating mental health conditions.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which puppetry enhances emotional expression and mental well-being, beyond indirect communication?
