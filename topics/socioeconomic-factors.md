## Socioeconomic Factors in Health and Development

The role of socioeconomic factors in shaping health outcomes and neurodevelopment is a significant theme across both adolescent neurodevelopment and the social prescribing roundtable report. Both sources highlight that economic and social conditions significantly influence brain development during adolescence, with specific emphasis on processes like synaptic pruning and myelination affecting prefrontal cortex functions. The integration of care through social prescriptions offers practical strategies to mitigate these influences by addressing environmental factors such as poverty and access to resources. However, while the neurodevelopment summary discusses internal biological changes in response to socioeconomic status, the roundtable report focuses more on external interventions aimed at improving socio-economic conditions.

## Key Findings

- Socioeconomic factors impact adolescent brain development.
- Neurodevelopment processes like myelination are influenced by socioeconomic context.
- Integrated care through social prescriptions can address environmental challenges affecting health and neurodevelopment.

## Open Questions

- How do biological changes in the prefrontal cortex interact with socio-environmental interventions to improve developmental outcomes?
