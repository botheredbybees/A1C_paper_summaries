## Frontotemporal Degeneration

The DSM-5-TR Neurocognitive Disorders Supplement and studies on MRI atrophy patterns in Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal lobar degeneration highlight the importance of accurate clinical judgment and diagnostic criteria for distinguishing between different forms of neurodegenerative diseases. The DSM-5-TR focuses on refining diagnostic criteria to capture diverse medical etiologies, emphasizing the need for a thorough understanding of various syndromes within the broader category of frontotemporal lobar degeneration (FTLD). Studies utilizing MRI and voxel-based morphometry further elucidate specific atrophy patterns associated with FTLD, distinct from those in Alzheimer's disease. However, while diagnostic tools and imaging studies aid in differentiation, clinical judgment remains paramount for accurate diagnosis, given the overlapping symptoms and varied presentations of these conditions.

## Key Findings

- Diagnostic criteria are crucial for distinguishing between neurodegenerative diseases such as FTLD and Alzheimer’s
- Clinical judgment plays a pivotal role alongside diagnostic tools like MRI and voxel-based morphometry in identifying specific patterns of atrophy characteristic of FTLD

## Open Questions

- How can clinical judgment be further enhanced or standardized to ensure accurate diagnosis across diverse patient populations?
