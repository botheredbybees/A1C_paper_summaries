## Self-Efficacy Through Creative Arts

The integration of creative arts interventions such as music listening and art programs reveals a consistent impact on enhancing self-efficacy among diverse populations including adolescents, individuals with Multiple Sclerosis (MS), and children exposed to trauma. Source 1 highlights how music listening can support adolescents in cultivating a sense of agency, which is foundational to their self-efficacy. Similarly, Sources 2 and 3 emphasize the role of creative art programs and therapeutic arts use in improving self-efficacy among individuals with MS, indicating that these activities not only bolster emotional well-being but also facilitate motor control, underscoring physical health benefits alongside mental gains. Source 4 synthesizes evidence from a rapid review to indicate that arts activities contribute significantly to developing resilience and overall mental well-being, further reinforcing the link between creative practices and enhanced self-efficacy in youth populations. Finally, Source 5 introduces trauma-informed care as an approach where cultural connections and artistic engagement play pivotal roles in promoting self-efficacy among children who have experienced trauma. Despite these positive outcomes, there remains a need for more detailed exploration of how different types of creative arts interventions uniquely contribute to the various dimensions of self-efficacy (e.g., emotional stability versus physical capability).

## Key Findings

- Music listening and creative art programs positively influence self-efficacy across diverse populations.
- Creative arts activities enhance both mental well-being and physical health, supporting overall self-efficacy development.
- Trauma-informed care integrating cultural connections through artistic engagement can bolster self-efficacy in trauma-exposed youth.

## Open Questions

- What are the distinct mechanisms by which different creative arts interventions uniquely contribute to various dimensions of self-efficacy?
