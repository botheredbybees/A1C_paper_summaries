## Disability Awareness and Etiquette

The concept of disability awareness is explored through the lens of transforming societal perceptions and addressing stereotypes. Sources emphasize the importance of experiential learning to understand disabilities better and promote respectful interactions, such as those designed in Week 9's exercises for understanding various impairments. These activities are grounded in the social model of disability, which focuses on environmental and societal barriers rather than individual medical conditions. Additionally, sources highlight how creative arts can be beneficial for individuals with disabilities by fostering inclusion and enhancing quality of life. However, while these practices offer promising frameworks and methods to improve awareness, there is a need for more robust data evaluating the long-term impacts of such interventions.

## Key Findings

- Disability awareness programs use experiential learning to combat stereotypes and enhance understanding.
- The social model of disability emphasizes removing barriers created by society rather than focusing on individual medical conditions.
- Creative arts activities provide significant benefits for individuals with disabilities, enhancing their quality of life.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of experiential learning and creative arts interventions in fostering lasting changes in attitudes towards disability?
