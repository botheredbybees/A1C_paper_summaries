## Non-pharmacological Interventions

The sources highlight the importance of non-pharmacological interventions in dementia care, emphasizing their potential to enhance quality of life and cognitive function. Cognitive stimulation therapy is noted as a key example within this framework. These interventions are seen as complementary or alternative approaches compared to pharmacological treatments. The emphasis on creativity and personalized activities underscores the need for tailored strategies that engage individuals with dementia beyond traditional medical approaches.

## Key Findings

- Non-pharmacological interventions enhance quality of life in dementia care.
- Cognitive stimulation therapy is a recognized non-pharmacological intervention approach.
- Personalized, creative activities are integral to effective non-pharmacological interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and comparative efficacy of non-pharmacological interventions versus pharmacological ones in managing dementia symptoms?
