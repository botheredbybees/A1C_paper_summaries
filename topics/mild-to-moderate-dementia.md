## Drama Therapy for Mild-to-Moderate Dementia

The integration of drama therapy as an intervention for individuals with mild-to-moderate dementia highlights its potential impact on enhancing quality of life. Across studies, there is a consensus that engaging in creative arts therapies can offer not only therapeutic benefits but also opportunities for social interaction and emotional expression. However, the specific mechanisms through which drama therapy positively influences cognitive function or behavioral symptoms remain underexplored, suggesting a need for further research into its efficacy compared to other established interventions like music therapy.

## Key Findings

- Drama therapy can improve quality of life in individuals with mild-to-moderate dementia.
- Engagement in creative arts therapies offers social and emotional benefits.
- Comparative studies examining the effectiveness of drama therapy against other therapeutic modalities are lacking.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which drama therapy improves quality of life for individuals with mild-to-moderate dementia?
