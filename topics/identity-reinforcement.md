## Identity Reinforcement in Dementia Care

The concept of identity reinforcement in dementia care emphasizes the importance of preserving and enhancing an individual's sense of self despite cognitive decline. Reminiscence therapy, a technique where individuals are encouraged to recall past experiences through dialogue or objects, is highlighted as a method for reinforcing identity. This intervention helps in creating personalized care plans that integrate life histories, thereby addressing the unique needs and identities of patients with dementia. While reminiscence therapy can significantly improve quality of life by strengthening connections to personal narratives, challenges remain regarding its widespread adoption due to resource constraints.

## Key Findings

- Reminiscence therapy is effective in reinforcing identity for individuals with dementia.
- Integration of life histories into care plans enhances individualized and person-centered approaches.
- Challenges exist in the implementation and scaling of reminiscence therapy.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to widespread adoption of identity reinforcement interventions like reminiscence therapy?
