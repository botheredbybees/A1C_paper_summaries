## Intrinsic Value Of Music

The intrinsic value of music is a theoretical construct that explores the inherent benefits and positive attributes attributed to music beyond its commercial or utilitarian applications. Across various sources, there are commonalities in identifying music as having significant intrinsic worth independent of commodification and market-driven perceptions. However, tensions arise regarding how this intrinsic goodness can be maintained while facing increasing pressures from the commercialization of music industries.

## Key Findings

- Music possesses inherent qualities that contribute positively to human health and well-being irrespective of its commercial value or use in therapy.
- The commodification of music challenges its perceived intrinsic value by shifting focus towards profit-driven rather than artistic or cultural aspects.

## Open Questions

- How can the intrinsic value of music be preserved and promoted in a market-driven culture where commercial interests often overshadow creative and communal values?
