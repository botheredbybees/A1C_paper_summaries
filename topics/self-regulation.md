## Self-Regulation in Creative Arts and Play

The concept of self-regulation is central to understanding how individuals manage their behavior, emotions, and cognition in various contexts. Two sources highlight different aspects of self-regulation: one focusing on musical play (Source 1) and the other on sensory experiences and brain development within creative arts (Source 2). Source 1 emphasizes that regulatory behaviors are facilitated through cognitive and emotionally-motivational monitoring during musical activities, suggesting a context-specific approach to regulation. In contrast, Source 2 broadens this perspective by considering self-regulation in broader sensory contexts of creative play, underscoring the role of brain development and diverse sensory inputs in fostering self-regulatory skills. Both sources suggest that creative arts and play can serve as therapeutic interventions enhancing children's ability to regulate themselves.

## Key Findings

- Regulatory behaviors are enhanced through cognitive and emotionally-motivational monitoring during musical activities.
- Brain development is positively influenced by sensory experiences in the context of creative arts and play, contributing to improved self-regulation skills.

## Open Questions

- How do different forms of sensory experience uniquely contribute to the development of self-regulatory behaviors?
