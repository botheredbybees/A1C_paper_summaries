## Positive Self-Identity

The concept of positive self-identity as an intervention is explored across various sources. Source 1 emphasizes the benefits of regular choral activities among working adults, highlighting how these activities contribute to a sense of community and personal healing and happiness, thereby fostering a positive self-identity. While the source underscores the role of group participation in enhancing individual well-being, there are no direct comparisons or contrasts with other interventions in building positive self-identity.

## Key Findings

- Regular choral activities can enhance a sense of community among participants.
- Choral singing is associated with personal healing and happiness, contributing to positive self-identity.
