## Wellbeing And Creativity

The integration of arts participation as a method to assess creativity and wellbeing reveals consistent findings across the reviewed literature. Sources indicate that creative engagement through First Nations Arts, particularly within community settings, significantly contributes to individual and collective wellbeing. However, there are tensions in defining and measuring creativity, with disparities noted in how communities engage with and benefit from these initiatives. A common theme is the importance of arts participation as a tool for enhancing mental health, fostering social cohesion, and providing therapeutic benefits. Despite this consensus, gaps remain in understanding the long-term impacts and scalability of such programs.

## Key Findings

- Creative engagement through First Nations Arts has significant positive effects on individual wellbeing.
- Arts participation fosters community connections and collective wellbeing.
- There is a recognized role for arts-based activities in mental health support.

## Open Questions

- How can the long-term benefits of arts participation programs be effectively measured?
