## Person-Centered Practice

The concept of person-centered care emerges as a pivotal intervention in the management and support for individuals with dementia. Sources highlight the importance of reminiscence therapy, which is grounded in the use of life histories to reinforce individual identity and provide personalized care tailored to each patient's unique background and experiences. This approach not only aids in enhancing communication but also fosters an environment where patients feel valued and understood. Both sources emphasize that a person-centered practice should be creative and communicative, ensuring it adapts to the evolving needs of individuals with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Reminiscence therapy is integral to reinforcing individual identity within person-centered care for those with dementia.
- Creative communication-based approaches enhance person-centered practice by facilitating better understanding and engagement.
- Person-centered care requires an individualized approach that considers the unique life histories of patients.
