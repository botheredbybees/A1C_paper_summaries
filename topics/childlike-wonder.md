## Puppet Therapy and Childlike Wonder

The concept of puppet therapy as an intervention to engage adults through the lens of childlike wonder highlights a unique approach in therapeutic settings. This method leverages culturally sensitive puppetry to facilitate dialogue, providing a safe space for participants to express themselves and explore complex emotions. While the technique is well-regarded for its ability to captivate and engage individuals by tapping into childhood innocence and creativity, there are considerations around cultural appropriateness and the specific contexts in which such interventions can be effectively applied. Sources concur on the value of puppet therapy as a means of expression and emotional exploration but also underscore the need for careful adaptation based on diverse cultural backgrounds.

## Key Findings

- Puppet therapy is effective at engaging adults by harnessing childlike wonder, fostering dialogue, and addressing complex emotions in a therapeutic setting.
- Culturally sensitive puppetry plays a critical role in ensuring that interventions resonate with participants from various backgrounds.

## Open Questions

- How can puppet therapy be adapted to ensure its effectiveness across different cultural contexts?
