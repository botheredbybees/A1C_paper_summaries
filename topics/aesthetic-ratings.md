## Aesthetic Ratings in Art Appreciation

The study of aesthetic ratings in art appreciation reveals that intense aesthetic experiences activate the default mode network (DMN) in the brain. This activation suggests a link between personal engagement with art and neural activity related to self-referential thought, memory recall, and emotional processing. However, individual differences significantly influence how people perceive and rate artistic stimuli, indicating variability in cognitive processes involved in aesthetic judgments.

## Key Findings

- Aesthetic experiences involve the activation of the default mode network (DMN) in the brain.
- Art appreciation is associated with personal engagement that varies among individuals.
- Individual differences play a critical role in how people perceive and rate art.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and societal factors influence individual aesthetic ratings and DMN activation?
