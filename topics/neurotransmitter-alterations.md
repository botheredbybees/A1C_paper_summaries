## Neurotransmitter Alterations in Autism

The study of the neurochemistry underlying Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) reveals a complex interplay between altered neurotransmitters and neural function. A key concept is the disruption in excitatory-inhibitory balance, where an imbalance between excitatory neurotransmitters like glutamate and inhibitory ones such as GABA has been implicated. This imbalance suggests that alterations in these critical neurotransmitter systems may play a role in the neuropathology of ASD. Various studies converge on this idea, indicating that changes in neurochemical transmission patterns contribute significantly to the diverse symptoms observed in individuals with autism.

## Key Findings

- Alterations in excitatory-inhibitory balance are prevalent in Autism Spectrum Disorder.
- Glutamate and GABA neurotransmitters show significant alterations in ASD.
- Neurotransmitter imbalances likely underpin multiple behavioral and cognitive features of autism.

## Open Questions

- What specific neurochemical pathways are most disrupted in different subtypes of ASD?
- How do these neurochemical changes influence the diverse symptomatology observed across individuals with autism?
