## Therapeutic Puppetry

Therapeutic puppetry emerges as a versatile and engaging intervention for enhancing psychological well-being among children. This method leverages the inherent appeal of puppets to create safe, supportive environments that encourage non-verbal expression and facilitate emotional exploration. Source one highlights how therapeutic puppetry can serve as an effective tool for helping children cope with their emotions through object relations theory, allowing them to project feelings onto characters rather than themselves or others directly. In contrast, source two emphasizes the use of puppets in diagnosing and addressing underlying psychological issues by providing a medium for cathartic expression that transcends verbal limitations. Both sources underscore the importance of a therapeutic setting where children feel secure enough to explore their emotions through play.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic puppetry facilitates emotional exploration via non-verbal means in a safe environment.
- Puppets serve as valuable tools for both diagnosis and intervention by allowing projection and expression.
- Children benefit from the psychological catharsis afforded by engaging with puppets.
