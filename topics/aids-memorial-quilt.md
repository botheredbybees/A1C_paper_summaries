## Aids Memorial Quilt

The AIDS Memorial Quilt serves as a powerful intervention that raises public awareness about the HIV/AIDS epidemic and honors those who have died from it. Originating in 1987, this initiative grew into an international project with thousands of participants creating individual panels to commemorate lost loved ones or friends. The quilt has been used as a tool for advocacy and education, moving beyond its original memorial purpose to become a symbol of the ongoing fight against HIV/AIDS. It highlights the impact of AIDS on communities across the globe and continues to evolve in its scope and reach.

## Key Findings

- The AIDS Memorial Quilt was created to honor individuals who died from HIV/AIDS and raise awareness about the epidemic.
- It evolved into a tool for advocacy and education, expanding its initial purpose as a memorial.
- The quilt's impact extends beyond direct participants, reaching global audiences and serving as a symbol of ongoing activism.
