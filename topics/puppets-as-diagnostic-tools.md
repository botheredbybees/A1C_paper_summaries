## Puppets As Diagnostic Tools

The use of puppets in therapeutic settings is highlighted for its unique ability to serve as a diagnostic tool and facilitate non-verbal expression. Across various studies, the puppet intervention has been noted for its potential to provide psychological catharsis, allowing individuals to express emotions that might be difficult or impossible to articulate through conventional verbal means. The key advantage of this approach lies in its capacity to create a safe distance between the individual and their emotional experiences, which can reduce anxiety and encourage deeper self-reflection and healing. However, while there is broad agreement on the potential benefits of puppets as diagnostic tools, questions remain regarding their efficacy across different populations and cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Puppets serve as effective non-verbal expression aids in therapeutic settings
- Psychological catharsis can be achieved through puppetry interventions
- Puppets create emotional distance, enabling individuals to explore difficult emotions

## Open Questions

- What are the cultural implications of using puppets in different diagnostic contexts?
