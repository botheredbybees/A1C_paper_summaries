## Freezing Of Gait

The concept of Freezing of Gait (FOG) has been extensively studied in the context of Parkinson's Disease. Therapeutic interventions such as dancing aim to improve mobility and address FOG through the use of external cues, which are environmental stimuli designed to interrupt episodes of freezing. The studies highlight that while therapeutic dancing shows promise in enhancing motor functions and reducing FOG frequency, there is a need for further research into long-term efficacy and broader application across different patient demographics.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic dancing can improve mobility and reduce the occurrence of freezing-of-gait episodes in Parkinson's disease patients.
- External cues are effective in mitigating freezing of gait during therapeutic interventions like dancing.
- The use of external rhythmic auditory stimulation is a promising technique for managing FOG.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and sustainability of therapeutic dancing as an intervention for reducing freezing-of-gait episodes?
