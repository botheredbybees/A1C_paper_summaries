## Cultural Sensitivity in Therapeutic Practices

The sources highlight the importance of cultural sensitivity across various therapeutic and communal art practices. Source 1 emphasizes the necessity for dementia care professionals to respect cultural norms and address socioeconomic barriers, ensuring confidentiality while providing culturally appropriate care. Sources 2 and 3 discuss collaborative practices in creating community-based art projects and ethical considerations in cross-cultural settings during disaster response, respectively, underscoring the need for therapists and artists to navigate ethical guidelines and trauma-informed approaches effectively. Lastly, Source 4 introduces puppet therapy as a tool that can engage adults with culturally sensitive content while facilitating dialogue and understanding within diverse communities.

## Key Findings

- Cultural sensitivity is essential in therapeutic practices across different populations and settings.
- Collaborative art projects are effective for engaging communities and addressing cultural norms and ethical considerations.
- Puppet therapy offers a unique method to facilitate culturally sensitive dialogues and can be adapted for various contexts.

## Open Questions

- What specific strategies can therapeutic practitioners use to enhance cultural sensitivity in their work, especially when dealing with socioeconomic barriers?
