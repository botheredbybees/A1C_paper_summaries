## Health and Wellbeing of Aboriginal People Through Cultural Engagement

The integration of traditional Aboriginal culture into healthcare strategies shows promise in enhancing the well-being of indigenous communities. Source 1 highlights issues such as underrepresentation of disabled artists within the arts community, suggesting that barriers to participation and negative attitudes may hinder access to culturally relevant therapeutic resources for those with disabilities. In contrast, Source 2 emphasizes the importance of cultural engagement in health outcomes through self-determination, indicating a positive correlation between indigenous culture-based interventions and improved mental and physical health metrics. Both sources underscore the need for inclusive policies that recognize the unique needs and contributions of Aboriginal people, particularly concerning disabilities and cultural practices.

## Key Findings

- Cultural engagement significantly contributes to the health outcomes of Aboriginal communities.
- Negative attitudes towards disability can act as barriers to arts participation among disabled individuals within Aboriginal populations.
- Inclusive policies that recognize indigenous culture are essential for effective healthcare interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can negative attitudes and structural barriers be addressed to ensure equal access to cultural practices and therapeutic resources for disabled Aboriginal people?
