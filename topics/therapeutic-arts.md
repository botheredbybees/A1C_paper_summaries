## Therapeutic Arts

The therapeutic arts encompass a range of creative practices that promote well-being and address various health issues. Sources emphasize the benefits of non-verbal communication through art forms such as puppetry, writing workshops, and drama therapy. Art interventions are particularly beneficial for dementia care patients, enhancing self-expression and emotional communication beyond verbal limits. Creative activities like painting, theater, and dance can improve cognitive function and emotional expression in individuals with chronic conditions such as multiple sclerosis or mental health issues. Studies highlight the importance of person-centered care, active participation, and equity in research to ensure these interventions are effective and accessible to all populations. The application of arts therapy is expanding beyond individual treatment into broader public health policies, suggesting a growing recognition of its benefits across diverse settings.

## Key Findings

- The therapeutic arts enhance non-verbal communication and self-expression for individuals with cognitive or speech limitations.
- Creative activities such as drama, dance, and writing improve emotional expression and cognitive function in patients with chronic conditions.
- Arts interventions promote mental health by enhancing emotional regulation, anxiety management, and social interaction.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of sustained engagement in therapeutic arts on cognitive and emotional well-being?
