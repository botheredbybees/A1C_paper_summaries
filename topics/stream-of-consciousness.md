## Stream Of Consciousness

The concept of stream-of-consciousness is primarily explored in the context of Modernist literature and its application to narrative technique. It involves presenting a character's thoughts in quick succession, reflecting their innermost thoughts without traditional narrative structure or punctuation. This approach contrasts with visual storytelling methods that rely on implied narratives and visual metaphors to convey similar introspective themes through images rather than text.

## Key Findings

- Stream-of-consciousness is predominantly associated with Modernist literature as a literary technique for revealing the internal thought processes of characters
- Visual storytelling can employ visual metaphors and implied narrative techniques to capture psychological depth without direct textual narration
- Both stream-of-consciousness in literature and visual metaphor in image-based narratives aim to portray complex mental states
