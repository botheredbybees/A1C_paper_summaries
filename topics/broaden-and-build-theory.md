## Broaden-and-Build Theory

The Broaden-and-Build theory posits that positive emotions contribute to individuals' psychological and social resources, thereby fostering long-term well-being. Across the sources provided, this theory is consistently linked with two key hypotheses: the 'undo hypothesis,' which explores how positive emotions can counteract the negative effects of stress, and the concept of stress buffering, highlighting positive emotions’ role in mitigating adverse life circumstances' impact on health. Additionally, the notion of hedonia is integrated into discussions about subjective well-being, illustrating how a focus on pleasure and satisfaction aligns with broader goals set by broaden-and-build theory for enhancing quality of life. However, while these sources outline the theoretical framework and its implications on stress management and self-care, they do not extensively explore potential variations or applications of the theory in diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- The Broaden-and-Build theory emphasizes positive emotions as a mechanism to build psychological resources over time.
- Positive emotions can mitigate the negative impacts of stress through the undo hypothesis and stress buffering mechanisms.
- Subjective well-being, including hedonia, is integral to the theoretical framework's goals.

## Open Questions

- How does the Broaden-and-Build theory apply across different cultural contexts where definitions of positive emotions and well-being may vary?
