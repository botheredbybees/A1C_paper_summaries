## Theoretical Underpinnings of Play Therapy

Play therapy is a therapeutic approach that utilizes play to help children express their thoughts and feelings. The use of puppets in this context, as explored by the Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy: Theory, Research, and Practice source, adds an additional layer of engagement and expression for the child client. This method enhances the effectiveness of traditional play therapy techniques through the facilitation of puppet-mediated dialogues that can reach deeper emotional layers of a child's psyche without direct confrontation or verbalization.

## Key Findings

- Puppet-assisted play therapy is an effective extension of traditional play therapy approaches, allowing for greater therapeutic engagement and expression by clients.
- The theoretical framework supporting the use of puppets in therapy emphasizes their ability to mediate dialogues and facilitate emotional exploration.
- Research supports the efficacy of puppet-mediated interventions in enhancing therapeutic outcomes.
