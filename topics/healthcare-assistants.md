## Healthcare Assistants in Dementia Care

The role of healthcare assistants (HCAs) in dementia care is highlighted through the utilization of theatre and educational tools to improve understanding and practice. Theatre-based interventions, as discussed in 'Transforming Dementia Care Through Theatre', serve to convey tacit knowledge and promote person-centered care among HCAs. This approach aims at fostering a deeper connection with individuals suffering from dementia, emphasizing their personal narrative rather than treating them merely as patients. Meanwhile, the transcript of a play trailer underscores the importance of raising awareness through field research and educational tools that help HCAs understand the challenges faced by those with dementia more profoundly.

## Key Findings

- Theatre-based interventions can effectively communicate tacit knowledge to healthcare assistants in dementia care.
- Person-centered care is emphasized as a critical approach for healthcare assistants working with individuals suffering from dementia.
- Field research and educational tools are essential components in training HCAs and enhancing their understanding of the lived experience of dementia patients.

## Open Questions

- How can theatre-based interventions be integrated into formal education programs for healthcare assistants to ensure long-term impact?
