## Early Perceptual Processes

The sources highlight the immediate affective consequences of early perceptual processes and their significance in understanding perception. Necker's smile exemplifies how initial visual perceptions can trigger immediate emotional responses, integrating affect as a fundamental component of perception rather than an aftereffect. This aligns with Gestalt theory's principles emphasizing holistic perception and the interconnectedness between sensory input and emotional response. The studies collectively underscore that early perceptual processes are not just about identifying stimuli but also involve instantaneous emotional engagement, which is crucial for further cognitive processing.

## Key Findings

- Early perceptual processes immediately engage affective responses, contrary to theories suggesting emotion follows perception.
- Affect as a component of perception aligns with Gestalt principles highlighting holistic and interconnected sensory-emotional processing.

## Open Questions

- How does the immediate integration of affect in early perceptual processes influence subsequent cognitive and behavioral responses?
