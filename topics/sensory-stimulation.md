## Sensory Stimulation

The integration of sensory stimulation in interventions for individuals with dementia is a consistent theme across the provided sources. Art engagement as described by the Art Gallery of New South Wales supports self-expression and arts-based therapy, contributing to the overall well-being of people living with dementia. Similarly, 'Creating Resources - Memory Trees and Boxes' highlights sensory stimulation through memory aids like trees and boxes which enhance personal identity and facilitate dialogic engagement. Another approach outlined in 'Unlocking the door to being there', emphasizes the role of creative facilitators who use sensory stimulation within heritage settings to improve well-being. Despite these varied applications, all sources align on the therapeutic benefits of engaging multiple senses for people with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Sensory stimulation is a core component in interventions aimed at improving quality of life for individuals with dementia.
- Memory aids and creative engagement activities enhance personal identity and self-expression among those living with dementia.
- Creative facilitation in heritage settings can significantly contribute to the well-being of people with dementia through sensory-based interactions.

## Open Questions

- How do different forms of sensory stimulation, such as visual versus auditory stimuli, impact the effectiveness of interventions for individuals with varying stages of dementia?
