## Creative Therapies for Mental Health

The sources illustrate a variety of creative therapies that have been developed to support mental health across different life stages and contexts. Music therapy is highlighted as an intervention with specific therapeutic benefits (Source 1). Dance movement therapy alongside physical exercise shows positive psychophysiological effects for older adults with mild dementia, demonstrating the effectiveness of active engagement in combating cognitive decline (Source 2). Photography has emerged as a versatile medium to enhance mental health through daily digital practices and mindfulness techniques (Sources 3-7), while journaling offers emotional processing and gratitude practices that significantly contribute to mental well-being (Sources 9, 18). Art therapy is widely acknowledged for its therapeutic value in trauma recovery, providing an avenue for emotional healing and cognitive processing (Sources 13, 24). Additionally, singing in groups has been shown to foster mental health through community support and stress reduction mechanisms (Sources 14-17). Sources also emphasize the role of creative play and natural spaces for children's mental health development (Sources 19, 22), highlighting the importance of holistic approaches that integrate non-medical solutions. Despite these promising interventions, sources reveal gaps in understanding the long-term effectiveness of these therapies and their applicability across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy offers specific therapeutic benefits.
- Dance movement therapy combined with physical exercise improves psychophysiological health for older adults.
- Photography practices like Therapeutic Photography enhance mental well-being through mindfulness techniques.
- Journaling provides emotional processing support, reducing psychological distress.
- Art and singing therapies are effective in treating trauma and fostering mental well-being among various populations.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term efficacy of creative therapies such as art therapy and music therapy across diverse demographics?
