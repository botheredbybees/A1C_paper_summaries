## Cognitive Monitoring

The concept of cognitive monitoring is explored through the lens of musical play as a form of regulatory behavior. This approach emphasizes how individuals can self-regulate their emotions and motivations in social settings, termed emotionally-motivational-monitoring. Key to this intervention is the idea that shared regulation occurs within groups where members collectively monitor and adjust behaviors. Studies highlight the importance of providing contexts that naturally afford opportunities for such cognitive monitoring, suggesting music as an effective facilitator due to its capacity to engage both emotional and social aspects simultaneously.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive monitoring enhances regulatory behaviors in social settings.
- Musical play provides a context-rich environment for emotionally-motivational-monitoring.
- Socially-shared-regulation is more effective when supported by collective cognitive monitoring practices.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of integrating cognitive monitoring through musical activities in educational and therapeutic settings?
