## Autonomy For Dementia Patients

The concept of enhancing autonomy in dementia patients is explored through the use of a unique intervention involving musical theatre groups. The 'spect-actors' framework suggests an innovative way to engage individuals with dementia, empowering them as active participants rather than passive observers or recipients of care. This approach challenges traditional views by positioning people with dementia at the center of creative activities, thereby promoting their sense of self-worth and independence. While the use of musical theatre for enhancing autonomy is novel, it aligns with broader theoretical frameworks that advocate for person-centered approaches in dementia care.

## Key Findings

- The 'spect-actors' framework can be utilized to enhance the autonomy of dementia patients by engaging them as active participants rather than passive recipients.
- Musical theatre groups provide a platform for individuals with dementia to express themselves creatively, promoting their sense of self-worth and independence.
- Person-centered approaches are increasingly recognized in enhancing quality of life and autonomy among dementia patients.

## Open Questions

- What are the broader implications of using creative arts interventions like musical theatre for long-term care settings involving dementia patients?
