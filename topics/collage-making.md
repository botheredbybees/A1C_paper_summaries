## Collage-Making in Mental Health

The practice of collage-making is emerging as a potent therapeutic intervention within mental health care, particularly when employed in participatory art-based research methods. As described in the source material, this approach allows individuals to express their subjective realities through visual means. By using materials and techniques like photovoice and collage-making, participants are enabled to articulate complex emotions and experiences that may be challenging to convey verbally. These interventions contribute to a qualitative understanding of mental health issues, facilitating deeper insights into the lived experiences of those affected by these conditions.

## Key Findings

- Collage-making facilitates the expression of subjective realities in individuals with mental health challenges.
- Photovoice and collage-making techniques can provide profound qualitative insights into mental health experiences.
- Visual art-based research methods are effective tools for participatory engagement in mental health care.
