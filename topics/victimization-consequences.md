## Consequences of Victimization

The sources collectively explore the aftermath and implications of victimization in different contexts. Social cohesion emerges as a critical factor in mitigating the psychological effects of perceived crime risk, reflecting broader societal structures' ability to offer support and security. Yet, inconsistencies arise in how community resilience is operationalized across distinct social settings. Despite differences in approaches and findings, there's consensus on victimization leading to significant personal and communal consequences, underscoring a need for targeted interventions.

## Key Findings

- Victimization has profound psychological impacts.
- Social cohesion can buffer against the negative effects of crime perception.
- Community resilience is essential but varies across different contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can social cohesion be effectively harnessed to address victimization consequences?
