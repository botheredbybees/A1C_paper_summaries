## Photovoice Methods in Aged Care

Photovoice is an innovative method for collecting qualitative data through the use of photography. Both sources emphasize its application within aged care settings to explore and document lived experiences of aging. While one source delves into the integration of photovoice with documentary photography and poetry, another focuses specifically on creative arts-based research including photovoice. These studies highlight the value of using visual and poetic expressions to capture emotive aspects of aging. The method is often situated within a hermeneutic-phenomenological approach, aiming to understand complex human experiences through interpretative means.

## Key Findings

- Photovoice facilitates self-expression among elderly participants by leveraging their photography skills
- Combining photovoice with other creative arts such as poetry and documentary enhances the depth of understanding aging experiences
- A hermeneutic-phenomenological approach is often adopted to interpret the rich, qualitative data produced

## Open Questions

- How can photovoice methods be further adapted for different cultural contexts in aged care research?
