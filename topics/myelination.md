## Myelination in Adolescence

The study of adolescent neurodevelopment highlights critical processes such as myelination and synaptic pruning that are crucial for brain maturation. Myelination is the process by which nerve cells become coated with a fatty substance, increasing neural conductivity and efficiency. Synaptic pruning involves the removal of unused synapses to enhance neural circuitry specificity and efficiency. Hormonal changes play significant roles in these processes along with socioeconomic factors influencing developmental outcomes. Research emphasizes how myelination impacts cognitive development, particularly affecting functions associated with the prefrontal cortex such as decision-making and impulse control.

## Key Findings

- Myelination enhances neural connectivity and efficiency during adolescence, contributing to the maturation of cognitive abilities.
- Hormonal changes significantly influence neurodevelopment processes including myelination and synaptic pruning.
- Socioeconomic status can impact the trajectory of adolescent brain development through its effects on environmental factors.
