## Hippocratic Medicine

Hippocratic medicine represents a significant paradigm shift in ancient Greek medical thought. Characterized by its emphasis on observation and experience over mythological explanations, it introduced the humoral theory which posits that health is maintained through a balance of four bodily fluids (humors). This approach was significantly expanded upon by Galen, whose commentaries further refined Hippocratic teachings, integrating them with his own anatomical observations. Additionally, Hippocratic medicine provides a rational framework for understanding epidemic diseases, such as loimos, challenging the prevailing views and laying the groundwork for systematic approaches to public health crises.

## Key Findings

- Hippocratic medicine emphasizes empirical observation over mythological explanations in medical practice.
- Humoral theory is central to Hippocratic medicine and posits that balance among four fluids (humors) maintains health.
- Galen's commentaries contributed significantly to the development and expansion of Hippocratic medical principles.
