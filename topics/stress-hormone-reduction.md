## Stress Hormone Reduction

The sources collectively highlight singing and participation in musical activities as an effective intervention for reducing stress hormones. Music-based interventions are noted to increase melatonin levels, a hormone linked with reduced stress and improved sleep quality. Community support networks through such activities provide additional benefits by fostering social connections which further aid in hormonal regulation and psychological well-being.

## Key Findings

- Music-based interventions like singing can reduce stress hormones.
- Increased melatonin levels are associated with these interventions, contributing to better sleep patterns and reduced anxiety.
- Community support networks formed through musical activities enhance the effectiveness of stress-reducing strategies.
