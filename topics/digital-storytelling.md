## Digital Storytelling

The sources explore digital storytelling as an intervention with a focus on self-representation and ethics. Source 1 delves into the remediation of personal photographs, highlighting how digitization transforms traditional forms of personal expression into new narratives with implications for identity politics. This source emphasizes that digital platforms provide unique opportunities for individuals to reclaim control over their representations through editing and sharing capabilities. In contrast, Source 2 introduces a participatory action research approach where individuals engage in the re•vision process facilitated by the Center for Art and Social Justice, indicating how structured frameworks can guide ethical storytelling practices. Both sources highlight the importance of understanding the cultural and political contexts within which digital stories are created.

## Key Findings

- Digital storytelling offers new avenues for self-representation through remediation techniques.
- Participatory action research supports ethical engagement in digital story creation.
- Cultural and political considerations significantly influence the ethics of digital storytelling.

## Open Questions

- How can digital storytelling interventions be made more inclusive and accessible to diverse populations?
