## Social Barriers for People with Disabilities

The concept of 'social barriers' emerges as a critical issue when considering the experiences and perceptions surrounding people with disabilities. Source 1 highlights how stereotypes and lack of awareness can create significant social barriers that hinder full participation and inclusion in society. The source suggests transformative approaches, such as experiential exercises, aimed at changing attitudes and behaviors towards disability through education and direct experience, thereby reducing social barriers.

## Key Findings

- Stereotypes and societal ignorance contribute to substantial social barriers for people with disabilities.
- Experiential learning can be effective in transforming negative perceptions about disabilities.

## Open Questions

- How scalable are experiential approaches for education on disability awareness, and what are the long-term impacts of such methods?
