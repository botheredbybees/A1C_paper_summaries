## Ethnographic Fieldwork Methods

The sources emphasize the importance of principles and practices in ethnographic fieldwork. Both texts highlight the 'verbatim principle' as crucial for accurately representing participant statements, ensuring a faithful representation without interpretation. The 'concrete principle' is also key, advocating for detailed descriptions of settings and events to provide context and richness to the data collected. Cultural themes are identified through these methodologies, providing insights into societal norms and values. While both sources converge on the foundational principles, they diverge slightly in application contexts: one focuses broadly on ethnographic fieldwork while the other specifically addresses audio-visual documentation within ethnomusicology.

## Key Findings

- The verbatim principle is essential for accurate representation of participant statements.
- The concrete principle enhances the detail and context richness of field notes.
- Cultural themes are discernible through effective ethnographic practices.

## Open Questions

- How do differences in application contexts (broad ethnography vs. ethnomusicology) impact the identification and interpretation of cultural themes?
