## The Exposure Triangle

The Exposure Triangle is a fundamental concept in photography that involves balancing three key elements—shutter speed, aperture, and ISO—to control the exposure of an image. Across different sources, it is consistently presented as a crucial aspect for photographers to understand and master. However, there are varying emphases on how these elements interact with each other based on the desired outcome such as depth of field or freezing motion in a photograph. While some sources focus on the technical aspects like adjusting ISO sensitivity for light conditions, others highlight creative decisions that can be made by manipulating these settings to achieve unique artistic effects.

## Key Findings

- The Exposure Triangle balances shutter speed, aperture, and ISO.
- Understanding the interaction between these elements allows photographers to control exposure.
- Manipulation of these elements enables creative expression in photography.
