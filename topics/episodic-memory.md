## Episodic Memory in Emotional and Identity Contexts

The concept of episodic memory is crucial for understanding both emotional responses to music and the formation of personal identity. In the context of emotional responses, episodic memory underlies the recall of past experiences that contribute to a listener's current emotional state (Source 1). This mechanism is often tied with 'emotional contagion,' suggesting that musical experiences can evoke strong emotions by triggering stored memories of significant life events. However, the interplay between music and emotion through episodic memory has not been fully explored, indicating gaps in understanding how different types of memories influence emotional reactions to music (Source 1). On the other hand, the role of episodic memory in personal identity formation is examined through the 'two-component model,' which posits that a sense of self involves the integration of episodic and semantic memories into a coherent narrative. The concept of 'quasi-memory' also emerges as an intriguing possibility for explaining how individuals perceive their own memories as part of their identity (Source 2). Despite these insights, the precise mechanisms by which episodic memory contributes to emotional responses and personal identity remain unclear.

## Key Findings

- Episodic memory plays a critical role in both emotional reactions to music and the formation of personal identity.
- The integration of past experiences through episodic memory influences emotional contagion when listening to music (Source 1).
- Personal identity is shaped by the combination of episodic memories with semantic knowledge, suggesting an intricate relationship between memory types and self-perception.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which episodic memories trigger emotional responses during musical experiences?
