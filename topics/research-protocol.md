## Writing A Research Protocol

The process of writing a research protocol is pivotal in the scientific and academic research domains. Both sources emphasize the importance of developing a comprehensive rationale that clearly articulates the justification for conducting the study, including anticipated contributions to knowledge and potential benefits to society or specific populations (fxa100-week04-writing-a-research-protocol). However, ensuring internal consistency within the protocol is equally critical; it refers to the logical coherence of the research design and methods described in the document. Sources also highlight ethical considerations as paramount, necessitating a thorough evaluation of all potential risks to participants and measures for their protection (fxa301-week01-writing-a-research-protocol). The feasibility aspect underlines the practicality of executing the study within given constraints such as resources and time frame. While these sources align on several fundamental aspects, there is variability in emphasis which may reflect differing research contexts or methodologies.

## Key Findings

- The rationale section must clearly articulate the purpose and justification for the study.
- Internal consistency involves ensuring logical coherence of design and methods throughout the protocol.
- Ethical considerations are integral to the development of a robust research protocol.
