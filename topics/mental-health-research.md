## Visual Art Therapies in Mental Health

Visual art therapies have emerged as potent interventions within mental health research, particularly through methods such as therapeutic photography and photovoice. These approaches leverage visual media to enhance self-esteem, resilience, and self-efficacy among participants, reflecting an emancipatory perspective that emphasizes the power dynamics inherent in therapeutic interviews. The visual dimension of these methodologies not only captures subjective realities but also facilitates a deeper exploration of personal narratives through various creative techniques like collage-making and reflexive photography. While both sources highlight the transformative potential of these interventions, there remains a nuanced debate regarding their applicability across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Visual art therapies significantly enhance self-esteem and resilience among participants.
- Photovoice and therapeutic photography are effective in capturing subjective realities.
- Creative techniques like collage-making deepen understanding of personal narratives.

## Open Questions

- How do visual art therapies address the needs of diverse mental health populations?
