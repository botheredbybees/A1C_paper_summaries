## Human Rights Scrutiny

The concept of human rights scrutiny focuses on ensuring that people with disabilities have their rights upheld as per the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD). This scrutiny emphasizes the importance of reasonable accommodation, a principle that requires the modification or provision of support to enable persons with disabilities to enjoy their rights and fundamental freedoms equally. Across sources, there is agreement about the need for human-rights-scrutiny to address issues where disability intersects with various areas of life, including education, employment, and social inclusion. However, tensions arise when implementing reasonable accommodation policies due to varying interpretations and practical challenges in different settings.

## Key Findings

- The Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD) is a cornerstone for ensuring human rights scrutiny for people with disabilities.
- Reasonable accommodation is critical for enabling persons with disabilities to participate fully in society.
- Human-rights-scrutiny plays a crucial role in addressing systemic barriers and inequalities faced by individuals with disabilities.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of reasonable accommodation be effectively implemented across diverse contexts, considering cultural, legal, and socio-economic differences?
