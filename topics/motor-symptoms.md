## Dancing as Therapy for Motor Symptoms

Research indicates that engaging in dancing can offer significant therapeutic benefits, particularly for individuals suffering from motor symptoms associated with Parkinson's disease. Source 1 highlights the cognitive and memory improvements that occur when participating in creative arts therapies like dance, emphasizing its potential to enhance executive function. This aligns with Source 2's findings which detail a preliminary longitudinal study showing that multisensory dance learning can slow the progression of motor symptom severity in patients with Parkinson's disease as measured by the MDS-UPDRS (Movement Disorder Society Unified Parkinson's Disease Rating Scale). Both studies underscore the multifaceted benefits of dance, including its neurological impact and potential for long-term therapeutic efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Dancing can improve cognitive functions such as executive function and memory in individuals with motor symptoms like those associated with Parkinson’s disease.
- Multisensory dance learning shows promise in slowing the progression of motor symptom severity in Parkinson's patients over a three-year period.
- Creative arts therapies, including dancing, offer therapeutic benefits beyond traditional medical treatments for motor symptoms.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts and sustainability of dance therapy compared to other forms of physical activity or medication?
