## Empathy Manipulation and Emotional Responses

The sources delve into the role of cognitive empathy in shaping emotional responses through different mediums. Source 1 explores how manipulating cognitive empathy affects music-induced emotions via psychophysiological reactions, suggesting that enhanced cognitive empathy can lead to more profound emotional engagement with musical stimuli. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on an intervention using art therapy, specifically puppet making, aimed at promoting both affective and cognitive empathy in adolescents with autism. The study indicates that this expressive therapeutic method not only enhances emotional understanding but also facilitates the development of cognitive empathy through creative expression. However, while both sources highlight the importance of cognitive empathy for deepening emotional experiences and enhancing interpersonal understanding, they differ significantly in their approach, context, and target audience.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive empathy is crucial for an enhanced emotional response to art and music.
- Expressive therapies like puppet making can be effective in promoting both affective and cognitive empathy in adolescents with autism.
- Manipulating cognitive empathy through specific interventions (e.g., psychophysiological studies or therapeutic activities) can lead to significant changes in emotional engagement.

## Open Questions

- How do different forms of expressive therapy compare in their effectiveness for developing cognitive empathy, particularly across various populations?
