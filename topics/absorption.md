## Absorption

The concept of absorption is central to understanding both spiritual experiences and emotional responses in various contexts, such as music. Absorption encompasses a state of deep engagement or immersion where an individual's focus narrows intensely on the object of their attention, leading to a suspension of self-awareness. In the realm of spirituality, absorption manifests in heightened states of consciousness during religious practices or meditation (Source 1). A specific scale, Tellegen Absorption Scale, measures this phenomenon, indicating its relevance across different spiritual traditions and individuals' susceptibility to such experiences. Similarly, in music consumption, absorption correlates with stronger emotional responses to musical stimuli, where listeners report feeling deeply moved by the music they encounter (Source 2). This state of absorption involves both positive and negative emotions, particularly when engaging with emotionally intense or traumatic music pieces (Source 4), suggesting a broader spectrum of psychological benefits associated with such experiences. From a neurological perspective, areas of the brain like the limbic system, dorsal striatum, and basal ganglia are implicated in mediating these processes, providing neurobiological underpinnings for the absorption phenomenon (Source 3). Despite its importance, the exact mechanisms through which absorption translates into emotional or spiritual experiences remain speculative. Research also points towards dissociative tendencies as a related factor contributing to higher levels of absorption during music listening (Source 4), hinting at complex interplays between cognitive states and psychological outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Absorption is a state of deep engagement linked with both spiritual and emotional experiences.
- The Tellegen Absorption Scale measures individual differences in susceptibility to absorbing states.
- Neurological structures like the limbic system play a role in mediating absorption during music listening.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neurobiological mechanisms that underpin the transition from cognitive absorption to emotional or spiritual experiences?
