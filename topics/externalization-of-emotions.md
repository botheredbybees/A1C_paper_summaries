## Externalization Of Emotions

The concept of externalizing emotions is central to both sources provided, emphasizing its role as a therapeutic intervention in creative arts and play therapy. Both documents highlight the technique's application through symbolic representation and emotional regulation strategies. The use of 'symbolic clients' or objects serves as a mediator between the individual and their emotions, facilitating distance and objectivity that can be crucial for understanding and managing complex feelings. This process aids individuals in expressing and processing their emotions more effectively, often leading to improved mental wellbeing. However, while both sources underscore the benefits of externalizing emotions, they do not delve deeply into the mechanisms by which symbolic representation leads to emotional regulation or the nuances involved in different therapeutic settings.

## Key Findings

- Externalization of emotions is a core practice in creative arts and play therapy.
- Symbolic representation through objects helps clients distance themselves from their emotions.
- The process aids in enhancing emotional regulation skills.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific psychological mechanisms that enable symbolic representation to lead to improved emotional regulation?
