## Multisensory Experiences

The concept of multisensory experiences highlights the importance of engaging multiple senses to enhance emotional well-being and cognitive processing. In heritage settings, creative facilitators play a pivotal role in unlocking sensory-stimulating activities that enable individuals with dementia to connect emotionally and intellectually with their environment (Source 1). These interventions not only provide therapeutic benefits but also foster relational-perspectives through shared experiences. The Portuguese Azulejo craft workshop exemplifies how cultural cognition and personality types can influence emotional arousal mechanisms, further underscoring the role of sensory engagement in emotional regulation and personal growth (Source 2). Both sources illustrate that multisensory interventions offer significant potential for enhancing quality of life through targeted sensory-stimulation.

## Key Findings

- Creative facilitators enhance engagement with heritage settings by unlocking sensory experiences
- Multisensory activities contribute to the well-being of individuals living with dementia
- Cultural and personality factors influence emotional responses in multisensory arts

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences impact the effectiveness of multisensory interventions for different populations?
