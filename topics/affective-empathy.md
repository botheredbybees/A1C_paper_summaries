## Art Therapy and Affective Empathy

The sources collectively emphasize the role of art therapy in enhancing emotional or affective empathy among individuals with conditions such as autism. Art therapeutic techniques like puppet making are explored for their capacity to facilitate an understanding and sharing of feelings, which is distinct from cognitive empathy (understanding another's perspective). The Expressive Therapies Continuum (ETC) framework underscores the progression from nonverbal expression through verbalization and reflection towards personal integration. These interventions show promise in improving social interactions by fostering emotional understanding.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy, particularly through methods like puppet making, can enhance affective empathy in individuals with autism.
- The Expressive Therapies Continuum (ETC) supports the therapeutic process of moving from nonverbal expression to personal integration.
- There is a clear distinction between cognitive and affective empathy, with art therapy focusing on emotional understanding.
