## Co-Creation in Dementia Care

The concept of co-creation is pivotal in enhancing the quality of life for individuals with dementia. Sources underscore the significance of engaging people with dementia in creative activities, such as sculpting and other forms of art, which not only facilitate self-expression but also promote social interaction and recognition of their inherent creativity. Co-creation emphasizes a collaborative approach where both caregivers and patients actively participate in generating meaningful experiences. This process is vital for maintaining dignity and fostering community connections among individuals living with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Co-creation through creative activities like sculpting has substantial benefits for people with dementia, including enhanced self-expression and social interaction.
- Recognizing the creative potential of those with dementia can lead to more dignified care practices that focus on capability rather than disability.
- A collaborative approach involving both patients and caregivers is essential for effective co-creation in dementia care.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of sustained co-creative activities on cognitive health and social well-being among people with dementia?
