## Embodied Therapy

The concept of embodied therapy is explored across various sources, emphasizing its roots in a person-centered approach. This therapeutic intervention highlights the interconnectedness of physical and emotional experiences to foster identity formation, connection with others, and intrinsic motivation. Sources underscore how this holistic view integrates bodily sensations and movements as key elements in understanding human psychology and enhancing therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Embodied therapy is grounded in a person-centered approach that values the integration of physical experience and emotional well-being.
- This intervention fosters deeper connections between individuals by addressing both their physical and psychological needs simultaneously.
- The therapeutic process within embodied therapy helps clients build a stronger sense of identity through self-awareness gained from bodily experiences.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of embodied therapy be measured when subjective experience plays such a crucial role?
