## Therapeutic Interventions for Autism Spectrum Disorders

Research on autism spectrum disorders (ASD) highlights the efficacy of various therapeutic interventions in addressing diverse aspects of ASD. Studies focused on temporal brain connectivity underscore differences in cognitive processing speed and neural network dynamics between individuals with ASD and typically developing peers, linking these disparities to symptom severity. In parallel, art therapy programs such as 'Images of Self' aim at enhancing sense-of-self, emotion-regulation, flexibility-in-behavior, and social-skills among children diagnosed with ASD. Repeated case studies further demonstrate the evolution in both participants' and therapists' behaviors over time within these therapeutic frameworks. Additionally, the use of art therapy is seen to facilitate emotional expression through creative means and provide psychoeducational benefits for childhood autism. Complementing this approach, music therapy employs improvised sound exchanges and structured sessions as non-verbal communication tools to engage individuals with ASD on a different sensory level. Collectively, these interventions suggest a multifaceted approach to therapeutic intervention that incorporates visual and auditory modes of expression to improve social interaction, emotional regulation, and self-awareness in children diagnosed with ASD.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy programs like 'Images of Self' enhance sense-of-self and emotion-regulation among children with autism spectrum disorders.
- Music therapy sessions facilitate non-verbal communication through improvised sound exchanges and structured music activities.
- Therapeutic interventions show improvements in cognitive processing speed and neural network dynamics for individuals diagnosed with ASD.

## Open Questions

- How do different therapeutic modalities (art, music) interact or complement each other in the context of autism spectrum disorder treatment?
