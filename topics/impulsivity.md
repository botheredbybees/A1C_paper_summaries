## Impulsivity in Adolescents

The study of impulsivity in adolescents highlights the complexity and variability within this age group. Neural connections continue to develop during adolescence, particularly in areas associated with decision-making and self-regulation, which can contribute to increased impulsivity. Gender differences are noted, indicating that males may be more prone to engage in risky behaviors due to heightened levels of impulsivity compared to females. This developmental stage also correlates with higher vulnerability to substance use among adolescents, suggesting a critical period for intervention. Despite these common findings across research, the mechanisms underlying gender-specific patterns of behavior and neural development remain areas of continued exploration.

## Key Findings

- Adolescent brain development includes heightened impulsivity.
- There are gender differences in adolescent developmental patterns.
- Increased impulsivity correlates with substance use vulnerability.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms behind gender differences in adolescent impulsivity?
