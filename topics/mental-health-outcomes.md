## Creative Arts and Play-Based Interventions for Mental Health

The integration of creative arts and play-based interventions has shown promising mental health outcomes across various populations, including children exposed to traumatic events. Sources emphasize the importance of culturally-engaged public health initiatives that utilize these methods systematically, guided by rigorous frameworks like PRISMA for reviewing and analyzing intervention effectiveness. Play-based therapies specifically target post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), externalizing problems, anxiety levels, and negative moods in children with adverse childhood experiences. However, there are discrepancies regarding the long-term efficacy of such interventions compared to traditional therapy approaches. While arts-based methods engage participants actively and creatively, they require further investigation into their sustained impact over time.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts and play-based interventions positively influence mental health outcomes in children exposed to trauma.
- Play-based therapies specifically address PTSD symptoms, externalizing issues, anxiety, and negative moods effectively in affected youth.
- The PRISMA guidelines are crucial for systematically reviewing the efficacy of creative arts and play-based interventions.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term impact and sustainability of benefits derived from creative arts and play-based interventions compared to traditional therapeutic approaches?
