## Daily Digital Practices for Well-Being

The concept of using daily digital practices as a form of self-care is explored in the context of photography and everyday well-being. The theory posits that engaging with technology, specifically through capturing moments via photography, can enhance one's sense of relational well-being. This theoretical framework suggests that these digital activities are not merely passive consumption but active participation that fosters a deeper connection to daily life experiences. However, there is an undercurrent of tension regarding the potential for such practices to become routine and potentially less meaningful over time.

## Key Findings

- Daily digital routines can serve as a form of self-care.
- Photography used in everyday contexts enhances relational well-being.
- Active engagement with technology can foster deeper connections to daily experiences.

## Open Questions

- How might the novelty and effectiveness of daily digital practices diminish over time?
