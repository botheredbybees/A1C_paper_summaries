## Phototherapy and Therapeutic Photography

The concept of using photography as a therapeutic tool, known as Phototherapy or Therapeutic Photography, emerges from an intersection between visual arts and mental health practices. Sources highlight the use of photography for emotional expression (Source 2), ethical considerations in therapy application (Source 3), and the broader framework of Health Humanities which includes natural-light photography and wabi-sabi aesthetics (Source 4). Despite varying approaches to integrating photography into therapeutic contexts, there is a consensus on the potential for phototherapy to serve as an effective method for enhancing mental well-being through visual communication. The introduction to photography as self-therapy provides a practical guide for beginners, emphasizing emotional expression and creative freedom, while more comprehensive academic literature offers insights into research methodologies and ethical considerations pertinent to therapeutic applications of photography.

## Key Findings

- Phototherapy utilizes photography to enhance mental health by providing a medium for emotional expression
- There is interdisciplinary interest in the application of phototherapy within Health Humanities
- Ethical guidelines are crucial when implementing phototherapy as an intervention
