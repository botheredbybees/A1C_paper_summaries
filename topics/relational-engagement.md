## Relational Engagement

The concept of relational engagement emerges as a critical component in therapeutic interventions, particularly in the context of music therapy for depression. Sources highlight interactive music therapy and its dual focus on aesthetic and physical engagements to enhance relational connections between therapist and patient. These elements are pivotal in facilitating emotional expression, fostering a sense of community, and improving mental health outcomes. However, while there is consensus regarding the importance of these components, questions remain about the specific mechanisms by which relational engagement promotes therapeutic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Relational engagement in music therapy involves interactive processes that include aesthetic and physical elements.
- Enhanced emotional expression through relational engagement can lead to improved mental health outcomes.
- Facilitating a sense of community is an important aspect of effective therapeutic interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which relational engagement in music therapy leads to better mental health outcomes?
