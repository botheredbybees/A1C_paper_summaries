## Reminiscence-Based Benefits

The synthesis of studies on reminiscence-based interventions highlights their broad range of positive impacts, particularly in stimulating creativity and cognitive functions. Across sources, there is consensus that engaging with past experiences through reminiscence activities can enhance mental well-being by reducing feelings of isolation and depression among older adults. However, while some research underscores the creative stimulation aspect of reminiscence therapy, others focus more on its benefits for memory enhancement and overall quality of life. There remains a need to clarify the specific mechanisms behind these diverse effects.

## Key Findings

- Reminiscence-based interventions have multiple beneficial outcomes including improved cognitive functions and reduced feelings of isolation among older adults.
- There is evidence suggesting that reminiscence activities can stimulate creativity in participants, contributing to their overall well-being.
- While widely accepted for its benefits, the specific mechanisms by which reminiscence impacts mental health remain an area of active research.

## Open Questions

- What are the underlying mechanisms through which reminiscence-based interventions exert their positive effects on cognitive and creative functions?
