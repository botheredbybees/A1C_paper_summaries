## Recreational Arts Engagement and Mental Wellbeing

This synthesis explores the impact of health behaviors on mental wellbeing, focusing specifically on how engaging in recreational arts can influence mental health. Source 1 evaluates the Short Warwick Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (SWEMWBS) to gauge associations between health behaviors and mental wellbeing, contributing to an understanding of mental health metrics. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the direct relationship between recreational arts engagement and mental well-being in the general population, identifying a dose-response relationship where greater participation correlates with enhanced mental health outcomes. Both sources highlight the importance of empirical measures for understanding the complex interplay between lifestyle activities and mental wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Health behaviors such as engaging in recreational arts significantly impact mental well-being.
- The Short Warwick Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (SWEMWBS) is a valid tool for assessing mental health across various health behaviors.
- There exists a dose-response relationship indicating that higher levels of engagement in activities like art have positive correlations with improved mental health.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which recreational arts engagement positively affects mental well-being?
