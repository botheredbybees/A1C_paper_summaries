## Story-Based Ethics in Dementia Care

The concept of 'story-based ethics' emerges as a central theme across sources addressing ethical practices within dementia care. Both sources emphasize the importance of understanding and respecting the narratives, histories, and unique identities of individuals with dementia to inform moral decision-making processes in caregiving contexts. The approach underscores the significance of recognizing each person's storied past, thus fostering an ethics that is personalized rather than generalized. This perspective contrasts traditional ethical frameworks by placing value on character development through storytelling, enabling caregivers to better appreciate the individuality and dignity of those with dementia. By prioritizing personal stories over generic guidelines, this narrative approach aims to enhance empathy and tailor care strategies more effectively.

## Key Findings

- Story-based ethics in dementia care emphasizes the importance of understanding individuals' life histories
- Narrative approaches prioritize personalized care over generalized ethical standards
- Character development through storytelling enhances caregivers' empathetic engagement
