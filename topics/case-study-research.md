## Case Study Research

The two case studies provided offer insights into the application and utility of case study research in understanding complex human experiences related to illness and technology intervention. The first source explores how creative expression through art-making influences self-identity and community-building among individuals dealing with illness, while the second examines the impact of social robots on resilience in people suffering from dementia. Both studies highlight the importance of qualitative data and personal narratives in uncovering deeper meanings and implications of therapeutic interventions or technological aids. Despite differing contexts, they share a focus on individual stories as a means to understand broader societal impacts and challenges.

## Key Findings

- Case study research can provide nuanced insights into how individuals cope with illness and technology through creative expression and social interaction.
- Personal narratives are crucial for understanding the multifaceted effects of interventions in healthcare settings.

## Open Questions

- How do case studies balance individual specificity with generalizable conclusions?
