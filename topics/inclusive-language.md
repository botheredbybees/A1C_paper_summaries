## Person-Centered Language

The concept of person-centered language focuses on recognizing individuals' inherent worth and uniqueness by using respectful terminology that emphasizes their humanity rather than their conditions or identities. This approach aims to reduce stigma and promote a positive view of the individual's capabilities and autonomy (Source 1). The guidelines for inclusive language highlight the importance of prioritizing personhood, which means considering each person as an individual with distinct needs and aspirations. By adopting this perspective, professionals and communities can foster more supportive environments that respect human dignity and encourage equitable interactions.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered language promotes a respectful approach to individuals by focusing on their humanity rather than their conditions or identities.
- Inclusive-language guidelines emphasize the importance of prioritizing personhood in communication practices.
- The use of inclusive language contributes to reducing stigma associated with various conditions and enhances positive views about individual capabilities.
