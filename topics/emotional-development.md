## Emotional Development Through Creative Arts

This synthesis examines interventions and theories related to emotional development through creative arts. Source 1 discusses the use of talking picture books in phototherapy as a means to facilitate communication, narrative creation, and emotional expression. It highlights how these materials can aid individuals in understanding their emotions better by visualizing them through storybooks. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the broader impact of cultural activities on public health, specifically focusing on emotional competence as facilitated through artistic engagement. This source mentions physiological changes such as amygdala function and mirror neuron activation due to participation in creative arts, which suggests that these activities can improve emotional regulation and empathy. Both sources emphasize the role of visual and narrative elements (talking picture books) and broader cultural practices (creative arts) in enhancing emotional development.

## Key Findings

- Talking picture books are a valuable tool for phototherapy as they help individuals express and process emotions.
- Creative arts activities can enhance emotional competence by engaging amygdala function and mirror neurons.
- Visual elements and narrative creation play crucial roles in facilitating emotional understanding.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term impact of creative arts engagement on emotional development?
