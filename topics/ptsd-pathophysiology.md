## Pathophysiology of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

The sources explore the pathophysiological aspects and current treatment options for Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD). PTSD is characterized by heightened emotional responses to trauma, involving dysregulated amygdala activity. Traditional interventions include trauma-focused therapies aimed at modifying maladaptive cognitive processes related to traumatic events. However, there remains a significant population of individuals with medically unresponsive epilepsy who do not benefit from conventional treatments, necessitating exploration into alternative methods such as amygdala ablation in severe refractory cases.

## Key Findings

- PTSD involves dysregulated amygdala activity leading to heightened emotional responses to trauma
- Trauma-focused therapies remain the cornerstone of PTSD treatment by targeting maladaptive cognitive processes
- A subset of individuals with medically unresponsive epilepsy may require innovative treatments like amygdala ablation

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and ethical implications of amygdala ablation for treating refractory cases of PTSD?
