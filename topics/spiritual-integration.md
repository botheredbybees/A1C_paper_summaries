## Spiritual Integration in Palliative Care

The integration of spirituality into palliative care aims to provide holistic support for patients facing life-limiting illnesses. The 'Song of Life' (SOL) study protocol highlights the emotional, spiritual, and psychobiological benefits of music therapy in this context. It underscores how interventions like music therapy can enhance spiritual well-being, emotional comfort, and overall psychological quality of life for palliative care recipients. While the SOL study focuses on specific therapeutic techniques, there is a broader discussion in literature about the importance of integrating spirituality into palliative care practices to address not only physical but also emotional and existential needs of patients.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can enhance spiritual integration among patients receiving palliative care.
- Interventions that focus on emotional support are crucial for improving psychological quality of life in palliative care settings.
- Spiritual well-being, as assessed through various dimensions including spiritual activities and beliefs, is significantly influenced by holistic interventions like music therapy.

## Open Questions

- What specific elements of music therapy contribute most effectively to spiritual integration among patients with diverse backgrounds?
