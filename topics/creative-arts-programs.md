## Creative Arts Programs in Youth Justice

The integration of creative arts programs within youth justice systems is highlighted as a beneficial intervention to promote self-expression and mental well-being. Both sources emphasize the importance of such programs for young offenders, with Source 1 focusing on the specific implementation through Artsmark initiatives in Sandwell Youth Offending Service. This program facilitates opportunities for participants to engage in artistic expression, which can contribute positively to their personal development and rehabilitation. Source 2 provides a broader framework for designing and delivering creative arts programs within youth justice contexts, stressing the necessity of involving participant feedback in service design and delivery to ensure relevance and effectiveness. The sources collectively underscore the potential of these programs as tools for engaging young people meaningfully in their own rehabilitation processes.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts programs are beneficial interventions in youth justice systems promoting self-expression and mental well-being.
- Integrating participant feedback is crucial for designing effective creative arts programs tailored to young offenders' needs.
