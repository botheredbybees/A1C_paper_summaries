## Randomized Controlled Trials

The utilization of Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) in evaluating interventions such as music therapy for children with autism spectrum disorder highlights both the strengths and limitations of this methodological approach. RCTs are particularly valued for their ability to establish causality and offer high internal validity, but they also face critiques related to generalizability and ecological validity. In the context of music therapy studies, these trials have been crucial in providing quantitative data on symptom severity and social reactions, yet the specific settings and standardized procedures required by RCTs can sometimes conflict with the inherently flexible nature of therapeutic interventions like music therapy.

## Key Findings

- Randomized controlled trials are a primary method for evaluating intervention effectiveness
- RCTs offer high internal validity but may suffer from limitations in external validity

## Open Questions

- How can ecological validity be improved while maintaining RCT standards?
