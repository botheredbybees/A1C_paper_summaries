## Constitutive Accounts Of Memory

The exploration of memory's nature reveals a significant tension between causal and constitutive accounts. Causal theories focus on the processes that lead to memory formation, often emphasizing brain functions or physical mechanisms. Constitutive theories, however, emphasize external factors such as social interaction and environmental influences in shaping what is remembered and how it is remembered. The text 'Memory: Inner or Outer?' argues for a constitutive account of memory, suggesting that memory cannot be solely an internal process but is fundamentally connected to the outside world through constant interplay with external environments and contexts. This perspective challenges traditional views that often isolate memory within individual cognitive frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Constitutive accounts emphasize the role of social interaction in shaping memory.
- Memory processes are influenced by external factors as much as internal ones.
- Challenges traditional causal theories which focus on individual brain functions

## Open Questions

- How do constitutive and causal accounts coexist or conflict with each other in explaining memory?
