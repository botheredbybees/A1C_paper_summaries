## Left-Right Brain Dominance Myth

The concept of left-right brain dominance, which suggests that one hemisphere of the human brain is more dominant in controlling certain cognitive functions than the other, has long been a subject of interest and debate. Recent research challenges this binary view, suggesting instead that both hemispheres work together to process information related to music perception, such as pitch, timbre, and rhythm. This integration highlights the complexity of neural mechanisms involved in auditory processing rather than attributing specific functions solely to one side of the brain. The myth's persistence despite scientific evidence against it underscores the need for public education about how the brain actually operates.

## Key Findings

- The left-right brain dominance theory is a persistent but scientifically unfounded belief.
- Both hemispheres are involved in processing auditory information such as pitch, timbre, and rhythm.
- There is a lack of scientific evidence supporting the notion that one side of the brain controls music perception exclusively.
