## Policy-Making in Social Services

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights the importance of both empirical evidence and lived experience in policy-making within social services. Source 1 emphasizes the need for rigorously assessing the quality of research findings through criteria such as Risk of Bias (RoB) and GRADE to inform 'what works' policies effectively. It underscores the critical role of systematic evaluation frameworks to ensure that policies are based on solid evidence, thereby enhancing their credibility and effectiveness in addressing social issues. Conversely, Source 2 introduces a contrasting yet complementary perspective by advocating for the inclusion of lived experience in policy-making processes. This source argues for incorporating authentic voices and fostering partnerships between individuals with direct experience of services and those involved in service design to enhance the relevance and applicability of policies. The integration of both evidence-based practices and experiential insights presents a more holistic approach to crafting effective social policies that are not only empirically sound but also deeply meaningful and relatable to their intended beneficiaries.

## Key Findings

- Empirical evidence is essential for informing robust policy-making in social services.
- Incorporating lived experiences enhances the authenticity and relevance of policies.
- Partnerships between stakeholders and individuals with direct experience can improve service design.

## Open Questions

- How to effectively balance empirical research with lived experience inputs in policy formulation?
