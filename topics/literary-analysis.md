## Literary Analysis in Historical Context

The analysis of literature through the lens of historical contexts reveals how narrative techniques and material knowledge shape understanding. Source one examines snapshot photography as a mode of expression and its influence on Henry James's novel 'What Maisie Knew,' emphasizing the importance of tangible objects in conveying deeper meanings within stories. In contrast, source two delves into ancient Greek perspectives on blindness, highlighting both literal and figurative interpretations, which suggest that physical limitations can paradoxically lead to heightened insight or imagination. Source three explores how classical Athenians conceptualized abstract ideas through cultural representations, aligning with the idea of using material elements to explore intellectual concepts. Collectively, these sources underscore the role of materiality in shaping literary narratives across different historical periods and cultures.

## Key Findings

- Narrative techniques are influenced by contemporaneous technologies such as snapshot photography.
- Blindness can be seen not only as a physical limitation but also as a metaphor for deeper understanding or insight.
- Material objects play a crucial role in the conceptualization of abstract ideas within literature.

## Open Questions

- How does the historical context of blindness and imagination differ between classical Greece and the modern era depicted in 'What Maisie Knew'?
