## Sensory Experience in Creative Arts and Play

The concept of sensory experience is explored across two distinct but related contexts within the realm of creative arts and play. In the first source, 'My daddy and me: Navigating the Japanese gardens at the Royal Tasmania Botanical Gardens,' sensory experiences are documented through a creative-arts lens as a father and child interact with the garden environment. This interaction highlights how sensory elements like sight, touch, smell, and sound contribute to enriching the experience of the natural world. In contrast, the second source, 'Week 2 Overview - FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing,' delves into the broader application of sensory experiences within therapeutic play settings aimed at enhancing self-regulation skills, motor skills, cognitive abilities, and fine-motor control among children. Despite the difference in context—natural vs. structured environment—the synthesis reveals a common thread: sensory engagement is integral to both facilitating emotional bonding between caregiver and child (first source) and supporting developmental milestones and mental well-being through play (second source). However, there is a noticeable tension regarding how structured these sensory experiences should be for optimal outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Sensory experiences in natural settings promote emotional bonding between caregivers and children.
- Structured sensory-based activities support cognitive and motor development among children.
- Both contexts highlight the significance of sensory engagement in enhancing well-being.

## Open Questions

- What is the optimal balance between structured and unstructured sensory play for maximizing developmental benefits?
