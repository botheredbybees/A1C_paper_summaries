## Hormonal Changes in Adolescence

The synthesis of the provided sources reveals a complex interplay between neurodevelopment and hormonal changes during adolescence. Neurodevelopment involves critical periods of synaptic pruning, myelination, and increased functional connectivity in regions such as the prefrontal cortex, which are crucial for cognitive maturation and emotional regulation. Hormonal changes play a significant role in this developmental process, influencing both brain structure and function. Socioeconomic factors also exert considerable influence on adolescent neurodevelopment, potentially exacerbating or mitigating the effects of hormonal fluctuations. However, there is variability in how these factors interact, highlighting the need for further research to elucidate their specific impacts.

## Key Findings

- Synaptic pruning and myelination are critical aspects of adolescent brain development.
- Hormonal changes during adolescence significantly impact neurodevelopmental processes.
- Socioeconomic status modulates the effects of hormonal changes on neurodevelopment.

## Open Questions

- How do specific socioeconomic factors interact with hormonal changes to influence adolescent neurodevelopment?
