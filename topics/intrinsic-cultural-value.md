## Intrinsic Cultural Value

The concept of intrinsic cultural value is explored through the lens of how older people's experiences in theater-making contribute to their well-being and community engagement. This review highlights that such activities offer significant health benefits, including psychological and social improvements. The intrinsic cultural value derived from these activities extends beyond personal benefit to encompass a broader contribution to societal culture and heritage preservation. However, there is an undercurrent of tension regarding the quantification of intrinsic value against more tangible outcomes. While the cultural enrichment provided by theater-making is recognized as inherently valuable, challenges arise in articulating this value in terms comprehensible to policy makers or funding bodies.

## Key Findings

- Theater-making activities provide significant health benefits for older people.
- Intrinsic cultural values derived from such activities contribute positively to societal culture and heritage preservation.

## Open Questions

- How can the intrinsic value of cultural activities like theater-making be effectively communicated and quantified to stakeholders?
