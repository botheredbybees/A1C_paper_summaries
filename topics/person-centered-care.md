## Person-Centered Care

Person-centered care emphasizes tailoring healthcare approaches to the unique needs and preferences of individuals with dementia. The concept is supported across various sources, including reminiscence therapy (Source 2) and creative engagement strategies like music therapy (Sources 3 & 4). These interventions aim to enhance memory recall, emotional connection, and overall quality of life through personalized techniques such as musical evoked autobiographical memories and tailored music playlists. Additionally, interactive experiences in museum settings (Source 8) illustrate how engaging environments can be adapted to support personhood while reducing stigma (Sources 5 & 6). However, effective implementation requires not only creative engagement but also a holistic approach that addresses the caregiver’s needs (Source 15), ensuring they possess both emotional and intellectual resourcefulness. The Veder Contact Method is highlighted as an example of a theater-based communication tool designed to facilitate social contact among dementia patients (Sources 11 & 16). Despite these promising approaches, challenges remain in integrating person-centered care into standard practices due to systemic barriers and the complexity of managing both neurological impairment and psychosocial factors.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered care enhances quality of life for individuals with dementia by focusing on their unique needs and preferences.
- Creative engagement techniques such as music therapy and reminiscence therapy are effective in improving memory recall and emotional connection.
- Holistic approaches that address caregiver support and emotional resilience can contribute to the successful implementation of person-centered care.

## Open Questions

- How can existing healthcare systems better integrate creative and personalized interventions into standard dementia care practices?
