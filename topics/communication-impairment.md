## Music Therapy

Music therapy is highlighted as a rehabilitative intervention for individuals with varying degrees of dementia and communication impairments. Across the sources, it emerges as a method that activates neurological processes to soothe and comfort patients, enhancing their quality of life by addressing both emotional well-being and cognitive function. However, while music therapy shows promise in alleviating symptoms and improving daily living for those with dementia, there is variability in how it impacts communication impairments across different populations and contexts.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy activates neurological processes to improve comfort and soothe patients with dementia.
- It serves as a rehabilitative intervention that enhances the quality of life for individuals suffering from communication impairments.
- There is evidence supporting the use of music therapy in addressing both emotional well-being and cognitive function.

## Open Questions

- How does the efficacy of music therapy vary across different types of communication impairments?
