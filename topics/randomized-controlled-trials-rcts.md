## Randomized Controlled Trials

The sources collectively highlight the significance of Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) in evaluating healthcare interventions. They emphasize RCTs' role in providing a basis for decision-making and their necessity in assessing complex interventions. However, there are tensions regarding the balance between evaluation and research within these trials, particularly in the context of measuring outcomes for arts-based health initiatives.

## Key Findings

- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) are essential for evaluating healthcare interventions.
- RCTs provide a robust basis for making decisions about complex interventions.
- Evaluation through RCTs is crucial for assessing the efficacy and impact of interventions, especially in the context of arts in health.

## Open Questions

- How can RCTs effectively balance evaluation with research, particularly when dealing with complex or subjective outcomes like those found in arts-based healthcare initiatives?
