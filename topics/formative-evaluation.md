## Formative Evaluation in Intervention Implementation

The concept of formative evaluation is integral to the successful implementation and refinement of interventions. Sources highlight its role in providing ongoing feedback that enables iterative improvement before full-scale deployment. This approach contrasts with summative evaluation, which focuses on outcomes after intervention completion. Formative evaluation's strength lies in its ability to address practical challenges early, enhancing both effectiveness and efficiency. However, sources also note the challenge of obtaining timely and accurate formative data due to limited resources or participant engagement issues.

## Key Findings

- Formative evaluation is crucial for refining interventions before full-scale implementation.
- Pilot projects play a key role in providing initial data that guides iterative refinement processes.
- Ongoing feedback from formative evaluation helps address practical challenges early.
