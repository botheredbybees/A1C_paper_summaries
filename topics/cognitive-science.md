## Introduction to Cognitive Neuroscience

The provided sources offer a foundational understanding of the brain's structure and its relationship with music. Source one introduces concepts such as absorption, limbic system, dorsal striatum, and basal ganglia, focusing on how these neurological components interact with musical stimuli to influence emotional responses and cognitive processes. The discussion emphasizes the role of specific areas in processing auditory information and linking it to emotional regulation and memory formation. In contrast, source two broadens the scope by discussing general brain functionalities and protection mechanisms such as the blood-brain barrier while touching on various neurological diseases that affect cognition and behavior. Despite differences in focus, both sources highlight the complexity of neural pathways involved in cognitive functions, suggesting a need for further investigation into how different types of stimuli (like music) interact with these pathways to influence mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- The limbic system plays a significant role in processing emotional responses to music.
- Different areas of the brain, such as the basal ganglia and dorsal striatum, are engaged during musical experiences.
- Understanding neural processes involved in cognitive functions requires examining both specific pathways related to stimuli (like music) and general neurological mechanisms.

## Open Questions

- How do various types of stimuli, including music, interact with specific brain regions and impact overall cognitive health?
