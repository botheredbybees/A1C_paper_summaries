## Societal Context In Dementia Experience

The provided sources emphasize the critical role of societal contexts in understanding and addressing the experiences of individuals with dementia. Personhood, as a key concept, highlights the importance of recognizing each individual's unique identity beyond their condition. A holistic approach to dementia care underscores the need for comprehensive support that addresses not just medical needs but also social, emotional, and psychological aspects. These insights challenge existing frameworks that may marginalize or dehumanize individuals with dementia by failing to acknowledge their ongoing personhood throughout the progression of the disease.

## Key Findings

- Personhood recognition is fundamental in enhancing quality of life for individuals with dementia
- Societal attitudes and institutional practices significantly influence how persons with dementia are perceived and cared for
- A holistic approach to dementia care is essential, integrating medical, social, emotional, and psychological support

## Open Questions

- How can societal contexts be effectively addressed in the development of person-centered care models?
