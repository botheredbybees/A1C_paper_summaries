## Arts Therapies for Eating Disorders

Art and other forms of creative expression are increasingly recognized as valuable adjunctive therapies in the treatment of eating disorders. Sources highlight the use of art therapy, museum-based experiences, and collaborative programs to address issues such as body image concerns and emotional expression. Empirical evidence supports the effectiveness of arts-based interventions for enhancing patient satisfaction and fostering positive outcomes. However, while these therapeutic approaches show promise, there is a need for more comprehensive research to establish robust empirical foundations. The integration of symbolic language and group therapy benefits further underscores the multidimensional support these therapies can provide.

## Key Findings

- Art and creative expression are recognized as effective adjunctive treatments in eating disorder recovery.
- Empirical evidence supports positive therapeutic outcomes from arts-based interventions, including improved body image and emotional expression.
- Collaborative art therapy programs offer a supportive environment for patients to engage with their condition.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term effectiveness of arts therapies in treating eating disorders compared to traditional treatments?
