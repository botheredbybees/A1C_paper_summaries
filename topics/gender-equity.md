## Gender Equity in Creative Ageing and Arts-Based Research

The exploration of gender equity within creative ageing initiatives highlights disparities that affect men's participation. Source 1 suggests that participatory arts programs, specifically tailored for older adults, often neglect male engagement due to preconceived notions about the role and interests of elderly males in society. This exclusionary trend not only limits access to beneficial creative activities but also perpetuates stereotypes regarding masculinity and aging. Conversely, source 2 introduces a broader context by examining how arts-based methods can enhance research data quality while fostering gender equity across various populations, including those experiencing trauma or social vulnerability. It underscores the importance of community engagement in creating equitable environments where diverse voices are heard and valued equally, suggesting that integrating participatory arts into research methodologies may help bridge gaps observed in Source 1.

## Key Findings

- Participatory arts programs often fail to cater adequately to male participants in creative ageing initiatives due to societal stereotypes.
- Arts-based methods can improve the quality of research data and promote gender equity, highlighting a potential pathway for addressing underrepresentation.
- Community engagement plays a crucial role in fostering inclusive environments that value diverse voices, thereby enhancing both program effectiveness and participant satisfaction.

## Open Questions

- How can participatory arts programs be redesigned to better include men while maintaining inclusivity for all genders?
