## Decision-Making Difficulties in Adolescents

Adolescence is a period marked by significant neurological changes that influence cognitive development and behavior. Synaptic pruning and neural circuitry reorganization during this phase can lead to decision-making difficulties due to the brain's ongoing maturation process. Identity formation, a critical aspect of adolescent development, complicates decision-making further as teens grapple with their emerging sense of self in various social contexts.

## Key Findings

- Adolescent brains undergo significant synaptic pruning and neural circuitry reorganization affecting cognitive functions including decision-making.
- Identity formation during adolescence contributes to complex decision-making scenarios due to the evolving sense of personal identity.
- Decision-making difficulties observed in adolescents are linked to ongoing brain development processes.
