## Ethics In Healthcare And Research

The examination of ethics within healthcare and research reveals a complex interplay between the rights and well-being of individuals and the responsibilities of professionals conducting studies. Source one emphasizes the concept of 'situated embodied agent' (SEA), which underscores the importance of recognizing patients as whole persons with emotional connections, even in conditions like dementia where personhood is challenged. This perspective aligns with ethical considerations that prioritize respect for autonomy and dignity. In contrast, source two delves into practical ethical dilemmas faced by researchers, such as ensuring participant benefits and managing conflicts of interest. Both sources highlight the necessity of maintaining integrity while balancing competing interests and obligations.

## Key Findings

- Ethics in healthcare must acknowledge patients' emotional connections and personhood beyond physical conditions
- Researchers face multiple ethical challenges including conflict-of-interest and ensuring participant-benefits

## Open Questions

- How can the concept of 'situated embodied agent' be practically applied to resolve ethical issues encountered in research?
