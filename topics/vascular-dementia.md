## Vascular Dementia

The provided sources offer a comprehensive overview of dementia and its forms, focusing on the prevalence and characteristics of vascular dementia. Source 1 highlights that vascular dementia is the second most common type after Alzheimer's disease, with distinct risk factors such as cardiovascular diseases. Sources 2 and 3 provide detailed insights into various types of dementia including Lewy body dementia and frontotemporal dementia, positioning vascular dementia within this broader spectrum. Notably, Source 3 explores an intriguing case study involving a sculptor who retains artistic abilities despite suffering from vascular dementia, suggesting that specific neural pathways for creative tasks may remain intact even as other cognitive functions deteriorate.

## Key Findings

- Vascular dementia is the second most prevalent form of dementia after Alzheimer's disease.
- Risk factors for vascular dementia include cardiovascular diseases and stroke, differentiating it from other forms such as Lewy body or frontotemporal dementia.
- Individuals with vascular dementia may retain specific cognitive abilities like artistic skills due to distinct neural pathways.
