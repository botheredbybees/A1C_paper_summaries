## Working Memory

The study of interventions targeting working memory highlights the importance of specific techniques in enhancing cognitive functions. Visual art training, as explored by the randomized controlled trial on dementia patients, shows promise in improving delayed recall and working memory capacities. This intervention underscores the adaptability of non-traditional methods like visual arts to support brain health and cognitive function.

## Key Findings

- Visual art training can enhance working memory in individuals with dementia.
- Non-pharmacological interventions such as art training offer viable options for supporting cognitive functions.
- Delayed recall is positively influenced by engaging activities that challenge the mind, indicating broader potential benefits beyond immediate application contexts.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of visual art training on working memory and overall cognitive health?
