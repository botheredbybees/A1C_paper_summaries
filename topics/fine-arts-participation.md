## Fine Arts Participation

The provided sources explore the relationship between fine arts participation and self-determination among individuals with developmental disabilities. Both studies emphasize that engaging in fine arts activities can contribute positively to an individual's sense of autonomy and control over their life circumstances, a concept rooted in self-determination theory. The studies highlight internal locus of control as a critical factor; participants who exhibit stronger internal locus of control tend to derive more benefits from fine arts engagement, experiencing increased feelings of personal efficacy and empowerment. However, there is also an acknowledgment that external factors such as societal support systems play significant roles in facilitating access to fine arts programs for this population. Despite the shared focus on self-determination, the nuances in operationalizing 'locus of control' and measuring its impact are noted, suggesting a need for more refined methodologies.

## Key Findings

- Fine arts participation can enhance self-determination among individuals with developmental disabilities.
- A stronger internal locus of control correlates positively with greater benefits from fine arts activities.

## Open Questions

- How can societal and environmental factors be better integrated into the framework for understanding the impact of fine arts on self-determination?
