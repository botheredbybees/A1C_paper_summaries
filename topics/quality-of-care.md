## Quality Of Care

The concept of 'quality of care' is explored through diverse lenses in these sources. The NOAH Code of Ethics (2023) emphasizes the ethical principles and respect for individuals receiving healthcare, underlining that quality of care includes not only medical competence but also ethical considerations. In contrast, Clown Doctors Australia's impact summary uses a Social Impact Measurement (SIM) framework to measure the social return on investment (SROI), indicating that quality of care extends beyond clinical outcomes to include emotional and psychological well-being. The Social Impact Report from 2025 further reinforces this by highlighting patient receptivity to treatment and emotional safety as key indicators of quality healthcare provision, thereby integrating both ethical standards and holistic health considerations.

## Key Findings

- Quality of care encompasses not only medical competence but also ethical principles, respect for patients' autonomy, and their psychological well-being.
- Ethical principles in the NOAH Code of Ethics are fundamental to ensuring that quality of care is patient-centered and respectful.
- The Social Return on Investment (SROI) methodology recognizes emotional safety and patient receptivity as crucial elements in assessing quality of care.

## Open Questions

- How can ethical standards be effectively integrated with methodologies like SROI to comprehensively measure the quality of care?
