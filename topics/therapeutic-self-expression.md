## Therapeutic Self-Expression

The concept of therapeutic self-expression is prominently featured in the context of music therapy for dementia patients. Across various sources, it's evident that engaging with meaningful music can serve as a powerful tool to enhance self-esteem and emotional well-being. Sources highlight how participants experience increased self-worth when they are able to express themselves through art and music, which aligns with the broader therapeutic goal of fostering a sense of identity and personal value in those affected by dementia. Despite its benefits, there remains some tension regarding the extent to which this intervention can be universally applied across different cultural contexts and individual preferences.

## Key Findings

- Engaging in therapeutic self-expression through music enhances participants' self-esteem.
- Meaningful music plays a central role in promoting emotional well-being for dementia patients.
- Therapeutic self-expression helps preserve a sense of identity for those with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic self-expression be adapted to cater to diverse cultural and individual musical preferences?
