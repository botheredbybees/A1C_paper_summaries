## Meaningful Involvement

The concept of 'meaningful involvement' within the realm of arts-based health research highlights critical ethical considerations such as confidentiality and the need for genuine engagement with participants. Across sources, there is agreement on the importance of ensuring that individuals are not merely passive recipients but active contributors to the research process. However, tensions arise in defining what constitutes meaningful participation, especially when balancing the therapeutic benefits against potential risks. The emphasis on ethical challenges underscores the necessity for robust methods and guidelines to ensure authentic involvement without compromising participant welfare.

## Key Findings

- Meaningful involvement is crucial for arts-based health research ethics.
- Balancing therapeutic benefits with risk management remains a challenge.
- Ethical frameworks need to be dynamic to accommodate diverse contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers ensure that 'meaningful involvement' does not become superficial or tokenistic?
