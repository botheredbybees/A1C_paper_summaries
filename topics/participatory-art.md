## Participatory Art

Participatory art, as highlighted by the Active Energy Project aimed at elderly wellbeing, emphasizes the role of community engagement and collective creation in enhancing quality-of-life. This form of intervention underscores the intrinsic value of participatory activities beyond mere aesthetic or therapeutic outcomes, focusing on the social dynamics that emerge from shared artistic experiences.

## Key Findings

- Participatory art interventions can significantly improve elderly wellbeing by fostering a sense of community and purpose.
- Quality-of-life improvements are associated with active engagement in creative arts projects that emphasize collaboration and participation.
- Community value is recognized as an essential component of participatory art, contributing to social cohesion and individual empowerment.

## Open Questions

- How can the broader societal impact of participatory art be measured beyond immediate participant benefits?
