## Excitatory-Inhibitory Balance

The concept of excitatory-inhibitory balance (EIB) emerges as a fundamental theoretical framework in understanding neurochemical processes, particularly in the context of autism spectrum disorder (ASD). This theory suggests that an imbalance between excitatory and inhibitory neurotransmission may underlie several neurological disorders. Source 1 discusses how alterations in neurotransmitters contribute to EIB, which is implicated in the pathophysiology of ASD. While this source focuses on neurochemical aspects, it highlights a critical relationship between neurotransmitter dynamics and broader neurological function relevant to ASD.

## Key Findings

- Excitatory-inhibitory balance (EIB) is central to understanding the neurochemical underpinnings of autism spectrum disorder (ASD).
- Alterations in neurotransmitters are linked to disruptions in EIB contributing to symptoms of ASD.

## Open Questions
