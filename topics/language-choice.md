## Language Choice in Mental Health Recovery

The sources highlight the critical role of language choice in promoting mental health recovery and preventing stigma. They emphasize that the words used to describe individuals with mental illness can either empower or disempower them, thus influencing their journey towards recovery. By adopting a 'recovery-oriented' language, which focuses on hope, dignity, and strength rather than pathology and limitation, professionals and communities can foster a supportive environment conducive to recovery. However, there is variability in the extent to which these principles are applied consistently across different settings and cultures.

## Key Findings

- Language choice has significant implications for mental health recovery.
- Recovery-oriented language emphasizes hope, dignity, and strength over pathology and limitation.
- There is a need for consistent application of recovery-oriented language across various contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of recovery-oriented language be adapted to diverse cultural contexts?
