## The Body-Subject Experience in Dementia

The exploration of the body-subject experience in dementia emphasizes the importance of understanding individuals with dementia beyond their cognitive decline. Sources highlight that dementia significantly impacts one's phenomenal experiences, altering how the body is perceived and experienced by the individual (Source 1). This perspective underscores a shift from traditional medical models focusing solely on brain pathology to recognizing the person as a whole being embedded in bodily experiences and social interactions.

## Key Findings

- Dementia alters individuals' phenomenal experience of their bodies, impacting how they perceive and interact with themselves and others.
- Recognizing the body-subject perspective is crucial for providing holistic care that respects the lived experience of people with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare providers integrate the body-subject approach into daily practice to enhance quality of life for individuals with dementia?
