## Self-Agency in Health and Wellness

The concept of self-agency emerges as a critical component in the design of inclusive projects aimed at enhancing health and wellbeing outcomes. Self-agency refers to an individual's capacity to act independently, make choices, and take responsibility for their own development and health. Across sources, it is evident that fostering self-agency among participants is not just about empowering individuals but also involves creating environments that support collaborative engagement. This collaboration is essential as it allows individuals to share experiences, learn from each other, and collectively drive the change they wish to see in their lives. Sources highlight a tension between promoting individual autonomy while ensuring collective action and mutual respect, suggesting that self-agency should be understood within a social context where community involvement and shared decision-making are valued.

## Key Findings

- Self-agency is crucial for enhancing health outcomes through empowering individuals to make choices about their own well-being.
- Collaborative engagement is necessary to support individual self-agency by fostering an environment of mutual respect and shared learning.

## Open Questions

- How can the balance between promoting individual autonomy and collective action be effectively managed in projects aimed at improving health and wellness outcomes?
