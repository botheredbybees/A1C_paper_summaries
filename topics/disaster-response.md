## Disaster Response and Trauma Recovery

The sources provided highlight various aspects of disaster response and trauma recovery interventions. Source 1 emphasizes the role of creative arts and play in facilitating mental wellbeing and healing after traumatic events through case studies that demonstrate how storytelling, adventure playgrounds, and trauma-informed design can be used to aid recovery processes. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on psychological first aid as an immediate intervention for providing timely psychosocial support to individuals affected by disasters, addressing both emotional and physical reactions crucial in the aftermath of traumatic events. Lastly, Source 3 underscores the personal experiences of individuals coping with anxiety post-disaster, indicating a strong correlation between sense-of-control and mental health outcomes during recovery periods. While these sources suggest diverse intervention strategies for trauma recovery, they collectively stress the importance of tailored approaches that consider psychological needs alongside physical safety measures.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts and play interventions are effective in supporting mental wellbeing after traumatic events.
- Timely psychosocial support through psychological first aid is crucial during disaster response efforts.
- Individuals experiencing a sense-of-control post-disaster exhibit better mental health outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts and play interventions be effectively integrated with psychological first aid protocols to enhance trauma recovery?
