## Types of Dementia

The lecture notes from FXA100 course provide an overview of various types of dementia, emphasizing the role of arts-based interventions in improving cognitive processing and managing symptoms. These include Alzheimer’s disease, vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, fronto-temporal dementia, alcohol-related dementia, HIV-associated dementia, Down syndrome related Alzheimer's, and chronic traumatic encephalopathy (CTE). The Dementia Australia transcript specifically highlights vascular cognitive impairment (VCI), a condition where blood vessel damage leads to oxygen deprivation in the brain. VCI can progress to vascular dementia, often co-occurring with other forms of dementia like Alzheimer’s disease or Lewy body disease as mixed dementia. Both sources discuss common symptoms such as cognitive decline, memory problems, speech difficulties, and mood disorders, underscoring the need for tailored interventions that address specific symptomatology.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions are essential in managing symptoms of various types of dementia by improving cognitive processing and well-being.
- Vascular cognitive impairment (VCI) can progress to vascular dementia and often occurs alongside other forms of dementia such as Alzheimer’s disease or Lewy body disease, leading to mixed dementia.
- Symptoms of different types of dementia vary based on the affected brain areas but commonly involve cognitive decline, memory problems, speech difficulties, behavioral disturbances, and mood disorders.
