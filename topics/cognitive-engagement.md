## Cognitive Engagement Through Creative Activities

The integration of creative activities such as listening to music and engaging in art enhances cognitive engagement for individuals with dementia. The 'Music and Memory' program highlights the therapeutic benefits of rhythmic entrainment, which can lead to dopamine release, improving mood and memory recall in patients. Source two further underscores the broader health benefits associated with creativity, emphasizing its positive impact on brain health. These sources collectively support creative interventions as a means for cognitive engagement among dementia patients.

## Key Findings

- Listening to music through programs like Music and Memory can enhance mood and memory recall.
- Creative activities, including art-making, offer therapeutic benefits by improving overall brain health.
- Rhythmic entrainment in musical therapy aids in dopamine release, benefiting individuals with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of sustained creative interventions compare to those of more structured cognitive training programs?
