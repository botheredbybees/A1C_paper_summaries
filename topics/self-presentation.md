## Self-Presentation Through Therapeutic Photography

Therapeutic photography as an intervention focuses on enhancing individuals' self-presentation skills and mental health through the act of taking photographs. This method not only aids in personal expression but also promotes mindfulness, encouraging participants to be more present in their everyday experiences. Across multiple studies highlighted within this context, therapeutic photography is shown to have positive impacts on mental well-being by enabling better self-awareness and emotional regulation. However, there are discrepancies regarding the specific mechanisms through which these benefits are achieved, suggesting a need for further exploration into how different photographic techniques contribute to varied psychological outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography can improve individuals' self-presentation skills and mental health.
- Photography as an intervention promotes mindfulness and emotional regulation.
- Lack of standardized methods for assessing therapeutic impact limits comparison across studies.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which photographic interventions influence psychological outcomes?
