## Associative Thinking

The concept of associative thinking is deeply intertwined with the narrative technique known as 'stream of consciousness', which gained prominence in modernist literature. This approach aims to replicate the natural flow of human thought, often characterized by its non-linear and associational nature. It allows readers an intimate look into a character's mental processes and emotions, reflecting the intricate web of connections that define human cognition. Across various sources, there is consensus on the importance of associative thinking in understanding narrative techniques and cognitive processes, yet there remains a gap regarding how this concept translates beyond literature to other domains such as psychology and neurology.

## Key Findings

- Associative thinking is a fundamental aspect of stream-of-consciousness writing in modernist literature.
- Stream of consciousness aims to replicate the natural flow of human thought processes, often non-linear and associational.
- This narrative technique provides insights into cognitive patterns and emotional experiences.
