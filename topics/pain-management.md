## Hospital Clowning in Pediatric Pain Management

Both sources investigate the impact of hospital clowning on pediatric patients, focusing specifically on its potential for reducing anxiety and managing pain. The network meta-analysis (Source 1) assesses a variety of interventions used alongside clown therapy to gauge effectiveness in pediatric anxiety management, while the systematic review and meta-analysis (Source 2) concentrate more narrowly on randomized controlled trials involving therapeutic clowns. Both studies acknowledge that hospital clowning is perceived positively by children and their families due to its ability to distract from medical procedures and foster a sense of comfort and joy amidst stressful situations. However, there are nuanced differences in how each study quantifies the impact of clown therapy; Source 1 places more emphasis on comparing clown therapy with other methods like music or art therapy, whereas Source 2 emphasizes clowning's role within pediatric care contexts without extensive comparative analysis.

## Key Findings

- Hospital clowning is effective at reducing anxiety in pediatric patients.
- Clown therapy can also serve as a viable adjunct to pain management strategies in pediatrics.
- Patient and family perceptions of hospital clowning are overwhelmingly positive.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of incorporating hospital clowns into pediatric care, particularly concerning patient outcomes beyond immediate procedural anxiety reduction?
