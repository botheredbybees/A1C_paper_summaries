## Symbolic Play

The concept of symbolic play is deeply intertwined with the development and application of interventions such as play therapy. This form of play allows children to act out their experiences in a metaphorical manner, which helps them understand and express emotions they might not be able to articulate verbally. The summary from Source 1 underscores the importance of understanding Piaget's cognitive stages when engaging in symbolic play with children, as it informs the therapist on how to best facilitate this process based on the child’s developmental stage. Additionally, the role of the therapist, particularly adhering to the principles outlined by Virginia Axline in her work on non-directive play therapy, is highlighted as crucial for effective therapeutic outcomes through symbolic play.

## Key Findings

- Symbolic play helps children express emotions and experiences metaphorically.
- Understanding Piaget's cognitive stages informs the facilitation of symbolic play.
- Principles of non-directive play therapy are essential in conducting effective symbolic play sessions.
