## Documentary Photography in Aged Care

Both sources emphasize documentary photography and photovoice as tools for exploring the lived experiences of aging within aged care settings. Photovoice is highlighted as a participatory method that empowers elderly participants to express their voices through visual storytelling. This approach aligns with hermeneutic phenomenology, which seeks to interpret subjective meanings from lived experiences. Both sources advocate for creative arts interventions and emotive research to capture the complexities of aging beyond traditional narrative forms. The integration of documentary photography into aged care research enriches understanding by providing a visual representation of participants' realities.

## Key Findings

- Documentary photography facilitates the expression of elderly individuals' lived experiences in aged care settings.
- Photovoice is a participatory method that empowers older adults to share their perspectives through visual narratives.
- Creative arts interventions, including documentary photography and poetry, offer emotive insights into aging.
