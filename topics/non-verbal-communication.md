## Non-Verbal Communication in Music Therapy

Music therapy emerges as a potent non-verbal communication tool for individuals with dementia, emphasizing improvisation and personalized approaches. Sources highlight the RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial which underscores the benefit of music therapy in reducing agitation through creative-music-making (Source 2). Another key element is the use of structured music sessions that foster improvised sound exchanges, enhancing therapeutic engagement without reliance on verbal communication (Source 3). The integration of puppet manipulation further enhances this approach by focusing on eye contact, head movements, and other non-verbal cues to convey emotional expression effectively (Source 4), enriching the communicative aspect of therapy beyond the sonic domain.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy serves as a robust non-verbal communication method for dementia patients.
- Improvisation and personalized approaches in music therapy are beneficial for managing agitation among dementia sufferers.
- Puppet manipulation, with its focus on eye contact and body language, complements the sonic elements of music therapy to enhance emotional expression.

## Open Questions

- What is the comparative effectiveness of music therapy versus other non-verbal therapeutic interventions in treating agitation among dementia patients?
