## Non-Verbal Expression

The sources highlight the therapeutic benefits of non-verbal expression through various modalities such as dance and puppetry. Source 1 emphasizes how body memory can serve as a conduit for emotional release, while source 2 explores the role of puppets in providing psychological catharsis and diagnosing underlying issues without verbal communication. Both interventions underscore the importance of physical activity in expressing emotions that might be difficult to articulate verbally.

## Key Findings

- Non-verbal expression through dance can serve as an effective therapeutic intervention by accessing body memory for emotional release.
- Puppets can function as diagnostic tools, enabling individuals to express psychological issues without direct verbal confrontation.
- Both methods of non-verbal expression contribute significantly to emotional catharsis and wellbeing.

## Open Questions

- How do the cultural contexts surrounding dance and puppetry influence their effectiveness in non-verbal therapy?
