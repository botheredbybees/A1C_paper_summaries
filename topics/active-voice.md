## Active Voice in Creative Writing

The concept of active voice is fundamental to the art of storytelling and narrative construction within creative writing. Sources emphasize its importance for engaging readers through directness and clarity, as opposed to passive constructions which can obscure actors or actions. Active voice enhances narrative momentum by clearly identifying who does what in a story, thus maintaining reader engagement. This approach complements the use of five-W questions (who, what, when, where, why) to ensure that narratives are robustly structured and visually compelling through effective caption writing.

## Key Findings

- Active voice is essential for engaging readers with clear narrative construction.
- The use of active voice enhances storytelling by identifying actors and actions directly.
- Combining five-W questions with creative captions strengthens the visual narrative.
