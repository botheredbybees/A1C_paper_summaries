## Coping Strategies

The concept of coping strategies is examined across various therapeutic and educational contexts. Person-centered care in dementia care emphasizes individualized approaches to help individuals manage their symptoms effectively (Source 1). This aligns with the use of object relations theory and projection techniques through puppet play, which facilitates children's ability to cope by externalizing their feelings (Source 2). In addition, sustainable puppet making is highlighted as a coping mechanism within therapeutic contexts, indicating that creative arts can serve dual roles in both expression and practical skill-building for mental wellbeing (Source 3). Despite the diverse applications of coping strategies, there remains an emphasis on creating supportive environments to enhance effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Coping strategies are integral to person-centered care.
- Object relations theory and projection techniques through puppet play aid children's emotional regulation.
- Creative arts activities can serve as both therapeutic expressions and practical skill-building exercises.

## Open Questions

- How do sustainable puppet-making practices contribute to the long-term efficacy of coping strategies?
