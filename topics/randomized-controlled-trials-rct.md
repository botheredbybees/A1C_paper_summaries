## Randomized Controlled Trials

The sources emphasize the role of Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs) as a method for evaluating healthcare interventions, particularly in assessing the efficacy and effectiveness of complex interventions. These trials are fundamental to evidence-based practice and clinical decision-making. They serve as the gold standard for establishing causality between an intervention and its outcomes, providing critical data that supports informed health care policy and practice.

## Key Findings

- RCTs provide a rigorous method for evaluating healthcare interventions
- Complex interventions require careful design and evaluation methodologies like RCTs to account for their multifaceted nature
- RCTs are crucial in establishing evidence-based practices
