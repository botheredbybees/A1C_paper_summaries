## Vicarious Trauma

The sources discuss the intervention of using creative arts like puppetry and clowning to address mental well-being issues associated with vicarious trauma. Puppetry in particular is highlighted for its therapeutic benefits, including non-verbal communication methods which can facilitate emotional expression without direct confrontation or verbal articulation. The concept of vicarious trauma, often experienced by therapists or caregivers who are exposed indirectly to clients' traumatic experiences through their work, underscores the importance of such interventions aimed at alleviating secondary stress and promoting mental health among helping professionals.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies like puppetry offer significant therapeutic benefits for managing vicarious trauma.
- Non-verbal communication techniques used in creative arts can help individuals express emotions and process traumatic experiences indirectly.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of using creative arts interventions to manage vicarious trauma?
