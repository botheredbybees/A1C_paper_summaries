## Ventromedial Prefrontal Cortex Function

The ventromedial prefrontal cortex (VMPFC) plays a crucial role in the processing of emotions and social cognition, including reward responses linked to music. Across various studies, the VMPFC is highlighted for its engagement in activities that elicit emotional arousal, indicating its importance in emotional processing pathways. Notably, when adolescents listen to music, the VMPFC shows activity patterns consistent with those observed during reward anticipation and receipt, suggesting a direct link between musical stimuli and neural reward systems. This suggests that the VMPFC may have broader implications for understanding how humans respond emotionally to rewarding activities beyond just auditory experiences.

## Key Findings

- The ventromedial prefrontal cortex is engaged in emotional processing and social cognition.
- Adolescent brains show activity in the VMPFC during music listening, correlating with reward anticipation and receipt.
- Music listening activates neural circuits associated with pleasure and reward.

## Open Questions

- How does the role of the ventromedial prefrontal cortex in emotional processing change across different developmental stages?
