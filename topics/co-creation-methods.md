## Co-Creation Methods In Healthcare

The concept of 'co-creation' in the context of healthcare and museum spaces emphasizes collaborative efforts between stakeholders to develop tailored interventions that enhance well-being. Source 1 underscores the importance of holistic approaches, integrating physical, mental, and social health through co-created initiatives within museums. Green wellbeing projects are highlighted as a central component of these efforts, reflecting a broader trend towards nature-based solutions in promoting public health. While there is agreement on the value of such participatory methods for fostering community engagement and inclusivity, questions remain about scalability and sustainability across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Co-creation methodologies involve active collaboration between healthcare professionals, patients, and community members to address holistic well-being needs.
- Green wellbeing initiatives play a critical role in co-created health programs by leveraging natural settings for therapeutic benefits.
- Museums serve as unique spaces for implementing such strategies due to their ability to facilitate interdisciplinary approaches.

## Open Questions

- How can co-creation methods be effectively scaled up while maintaining cultural sensitivity and inclusivity across different demographic groups?
