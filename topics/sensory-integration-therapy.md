## Sensory Integration Therapy

Sensory integration therapy is a specialized form of intervention designed to help individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) improve their adaptive behavior and sensory processing. It incorporates elements like play therapy, aiming to enhance expressive communication in children through structured activities that stimulate the senses. However, limitations exist regarding its efficacy across diverse ASD presentations, suggesting varied implications for clinical application.

## Key Findings

- Sensory integration therapy can help improve adaptive behaviors
- It enhances expressive communication skills in children with autism
- The role of play therapy within sensory integration is crucial

## Open Questions

- What are the limitations and effectiveness across different types of ASD presentations?
