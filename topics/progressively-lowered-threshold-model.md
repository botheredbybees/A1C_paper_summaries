## Progressively Lowered Threshold Model

The Progressively Lowered Threshold Model (PLT) is a theoretical framework that aims to understand how the physical environment can support individuals with dementia as their cognitive abilities decline. Sources emphasize the PLT model's focus on tailoring environments to compensate for reduced individual capabilities, thereby maintaining residents' independence and quality of life. The Individual Competence and Environmental Press (ICEP) Model and the Model of Place are also referenced in these sources, reinforcing the importance of adaptive environmental design that considers both the resident’s functional ability and the physical space's demands. Both chapters highlight that as dementia progresses, the environment must adapt to increasingly support individuals, ensuring that they can still engage meaningfully with their surroundings.

## Key Findings

- The Progressively Lowered Threshold Model (PLT) is a critical theoretical framework for designing environments suitable for people with dementia.
- As individual competence diminishes due to dementia progression, environmental adaptations become more necessary to support independence and quality of life.
- Models like Individual Competence and Environmental Press (ICEP) and the Model of Place work in conjunction with PLT, emphasizing holistic approaches to care environment design.
