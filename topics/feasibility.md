## Feasibility in Research

Feasibility is a crucial aspect of research planning that determines whether an intervention or study can be successfully executed within the constraints of time, resources, and ethical considerations. Both sources emphasize the importance of developing a clear rationale for why the proposed project is necessary and feasible given these limitations. The feasibility assessment involves evaluating practical aspects such as participant recruitment, data collection methods, and logistical challenges. Additionally, both texts highlight that approval from an ethics committee is essential before proceeding with any research protocol, underscoring the regulatory framework within which feasibility studies operate.

## Key Findings

- Feasibility studies are crucial for assessing the practicality of proposed interventions.
- Ethics committees play a significant role in reviewing and approving feasibility studies.
- A clear rationale is necessary to justify the feasibility and necessity of research protocols.
