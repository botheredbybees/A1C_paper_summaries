## Cochlear Function

The provided sources delve into the mechanisms of hearing and specifically the role of the cochlea in converting mechanical sound waves into electrical signals. Both sources emphasize the critical function of hair cells within the cochlea, which convert mechanical energy from sound vibrations into neural impulses that are transmitted via the auditory nerve to the brain. These sources illustrate a clear consensus on the fundamental process by which the ear translates acoustic stimuli into perceptible sounds through the intricate interplay between physical structures and neural pathways.

## Key Findings

- The cochlea plays a pivotal role in converting mechanical sound waves into electrical signals for auditory perception.
- Hair cells within the cochlea are essential for transducing vibrations from sound waves into neuronal impulses.
- Auditory nerves carry these electrical signals to the brain, where they are interpreted as sounds.
