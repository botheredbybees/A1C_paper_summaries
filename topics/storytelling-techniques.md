## Storytelling Techniques

The sources highlight various storytelling techniques that utilize different forms of media and cognitive processes to create compelling narratives. 'Week 5 Introduction to Storytelling' introduces image-based storytelling and free-writing as foundational methods for generating content, emphasizing the visual aspect and the importance of streamlining thought into tangible stories through writing exercises (Key Concepts: image-based storytelling, free-writing exercises). In contrast, 'Where do you get your ideas?' delves into mental processes that spark creativity, such as daydreaming and imagining hypothetical scenarios to foster creative curiosity, which serves as a preliminary step in the narrative creation process (Key Concepts: daydreaming, hypothetical-scenarios, creative-curiosity). Lastly, 'The Photo Essay - Storytelling with Image and Text' integrates visual storytelling with text, discussing how narrative progression can be achieved through feedback-driven iterative improvement of content (Key Concepts: narrative-progress, visual-storytelling, feedback-improvement). These sources collectively underscore the diversity of methods available for engaging in storytelling across various mediums and cognitive stages.

## Key Findings

- Image-based storytelling is a fundamental method that relies on visuals to convey narratives effectively.
- Free-writing exercises help translate thoughts into written form, serving as an initial step in storytelling.
- Narrative progression can be enhanced through continuous feedback and iterative improvement.
