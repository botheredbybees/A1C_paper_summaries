## Puppet Therapy

Puppet therapy emerges as a versatile intervention strategy in therapeutic settings, offering unique benefits for children and adults alike. Sources highlight the use of puppetry to externalize problems, enabling clients to discuss painful memories or emotional issues indirectly through symbolic characters (Sources 5, 7). The technique facilitates engagement by providing a safe distance between the client and their emotional challenges, thereby enhancing therapeutic dialogue and fostering emotional regulation (Source 3). Puppet therapy is particularly beneficial for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) as it simplifies social cues and promotes reciprocal interactions, improving attention to social stimuli (Sources 8, 10). Additionally, puppet-assisted play can significantly reduce anxiety and pain in medical procedures, such as blood collection in preschool children (Source 9). The literature underscores the importance of character development and therapeutic engagement when using puppets in therapy sessions. However, while sources acknowledge the benefits, they also highlight the need for further research to establish standardized protocols and best practices.

## Key Findings

- Puppet therapy facilitates emotional regulation and externalization of problems.
- It simplifies social cues and promotes reciprocal interactions in children with ASD.
- Hand puppet-based therapeutic play reduces fear and pain associated with medical procedures.
