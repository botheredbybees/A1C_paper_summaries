## Puppet Therapy

Puppet therapy emerges as a versatile therapeutic intervention, integrating play and creativity to address emotional, social, and cognitive issues in children. The technique leverages the strengths perspective by encouraging imagination and creative thinking (Source 1), which aligns with Torrance's Test of Creative Thinking. Puppet-assisted play is also shown to enhance conflict resolution skills and coping strategies through externalization techniques, where problems are personified as characters or objects allowing for a more objective analysis (Sources 9 & 15). Character development in puppet therapy facilitates therapeutic engagement and helps children cope with various challenges by providing a supportive environment (Source 6), while also aiding in the management of traumatic memories via re-evaluation through symbolic means (Source 7).

Moreover, the use of puppets simplifies complex social cues for children with ASD, enhancing their ability to interact reciprocally and interpret non-verbal communication effectively (Source 12). Additionally, puppet therapy has been proven effective in reducing fear and pain associated with medical procedures among preschool-aged children (Source 13), further highlighting its application across diverse therapeutic settings. However, there is a need for more rigorous research to establish standardized practices and the long-term efficacy of this intervention.

## Key Findings

- Puppet therapy encourages creative thinking and conflict resolution skills in children
- Character development through puppetry enhances emotional regulation and coping strategies
- Puppets simplify social cues, improving interaction among children with ASD

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of using puppet therapy for mental health issues?
