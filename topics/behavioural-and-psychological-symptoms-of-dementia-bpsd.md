## Behavioural And Psychological Symptoms Of Dementia

The study highlights the use of creative activities as a promising intervention for managing BPSD in residential aged care facilities. Creative activities such as art, music, and drama not only provide opportunities for self-expression but also cognitive engagement that helps mitigate symptoms like agitation, depression, and anxiety associated with dementia. The research underscores the importance of personalized care plans tailored to individual residents' needs and interests to maximize benefits. However, it raises questions about the scalability and sustainability of such interventions within resource-constrained environments.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities offer a non-pharmacological approach to managing BPSD in dementia patients.
- Self-expression through creative arts can significantly improve mood and reduce anxiety among residents with dementia.
- Cognitive engagement through tailored activities enhances the overall quality of life for dementia sufferers.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to implementing creative activity programs on a larger scale in aged care facilities?
