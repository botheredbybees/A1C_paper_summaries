## Qualitative Study of Artistic Expression in Rehabilitation and Mental Health

This qualitative synthesis examines the role of visual art as a therapeutic modality for individuals undergoing physical rehabilitation and those with mental health concerns. Source 1, focusing on neurological conditions, highlights how participation in visual arts enhances self-esteem among clients and aids in achieving rehabilitation goals beyond just physical recovery. Patients report emotional benefits such as improved mood and sense of accomplishment, indicating the psychological dimensions of art therapy. In contrast, Source 2 emphasizes artistic expression's role in mental health management, underscoring its utility for emotional regulation and personal well-being enhancement. While both sources recognize the positive impact of creative activities on patients' quality of life, there is a noted gap between acknowledging these benefits and translating them into standard rehabilitation practices.

## Key Findings

- Visual art contributes significantly to self-esteem improvement in individuals undergoing physical rehabilitation.
- Creative arts offer emotional regulation mechanisms crucial for managing mental health challenges.
- The psychological benefits of artistic expression are increasingly recognized beyond the scope of traditional medical treatments.

## Open Questions

- How can qualitative research findings on the benefits of visual art be effectively integrated into mainstream therapeutic and rehabilitative practices?
