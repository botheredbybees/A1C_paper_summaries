## Holistic Well-Being Through Creative Practices

The integration of creative practices such as dance therapy and visual thinking strategies in promoting holistic well-being highlights a growing interdisciplinary approach to rehabilitation and professional development. Dance therapy, as detailed over two decades of bibliometric analysis, has shown significant efficacy in enhancing physical, emotional, and social health among individuals undergoing rehabilitation. Interdisciplinary cooperation is emphasized as crucial for maximizing therapeutic outcomes. Concurrently, the role of art museums in fostering holistic well-being is explored through Visual Thinking Strategies (VTS), which aim to enhance observational skills and empathy among medical professionals. Both sources underscore the importance of creative interventions in achieving a comprehensive sense of well-being that transcends traditional healthcare boundaries.

## Key Findings

- Creative practices such as dance therapy and VTS are effective for holistic well-being
- Interdisciplinary cooperation enhances therapeutic outcomes in rehabilitation
- Visual thinking strategies can foster professional development among medical professionals

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of creative interventions compare across different populations?
