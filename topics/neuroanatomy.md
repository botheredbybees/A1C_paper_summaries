## Human Brain Neuroanatomy

The human brain's neuroanatomy is a complex system with various components that contribute to cognitive functions and perceptions. Source 1 focuses on the thalamus and hippocampus, which play crucial roles in sensory relay and memory formation respectively. The concept of neuroplasticity highlights how these structures can change over time due to environmental factors or experiences, affecting learning and recovery from brain injuries. Source 2 expands this understanding by introducing executive functions managed by the frontal lobe and the limbic system's role in emotional responses. Additionally, sensory processing and artistic perception are discussed, illustrating how different areas of the brain interact to interpret information from our environment. Both sources align in their emphasis on specific brain regions contributing to broader cognitive processes but diverge in detail regarding perceptual functions.

## Key Findings

- The thalamus serves as a critical relay station for sensory and motor signals before they reach other parts of the brain.
- Hippocampus is essential for memory formation, particularly declarative memories related to facts and events.
- Neuroplasticity allows brain structures like the hippocampus and thalamus to adapt and change in response to experiences or injuries.
