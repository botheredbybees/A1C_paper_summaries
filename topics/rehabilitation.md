## Rehabilitation for Cognitive Decline

The sources collectively highlight the importance of cognitive stimulation and social interaction in rehabilitation strategies for individuals with cognitive decline, such as Alzheimer's disease. They underscore the efficacy of engaging activities like crafts and games that not only provide mental engagement but also foster a sense of community and support among participants. These interventions aim to maintain or improve cognitive function, while also addressing the emotional and social needs associated with cognitive decline.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive stimulation through structured activities aids in maintaining brain health for individuals with Alzheimer's disease.
- Social interaction plays a critical role in enhancing quality of life and mental well-being among patients suffering from cognitive decline.
- Crafts and games designed specifically for people with dementia can improve both cognitive function and social engagement.
