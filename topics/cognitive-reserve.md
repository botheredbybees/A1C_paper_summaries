## Cognitive Reserve

The concept of cognitive reserve highlights the brain's ability to adapt and compensate for age-related or disease-induced neurodegeneration. Across sources, it is evident that various activities such as engaging in music, theatre, creative writing, and humor therapy contribute positively to maintaining a high level of cognitive reserve. These activities enhance mental flexibility and emotional intelligence, which are critical components of coping with the challenges associated with aging. Source 1 emphasizes brain plasticity through musical engagement, whereas Source 2 underscores the importance of engaging activities in fostering cognitive resilience. Source 3 introduces humor therapy as an effective playful intervention that not only entertains but also stimulates cognitive reserve in managing frailty among older adults. However, while each source agrees on the efficacy of these interventions, there is a need for more research to understand how different types of interventions may vary in their effectiveness across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Music and engaging activities enhance cognitive reserve by promoting brain plasticity and mental flexibility.
- Humor therapy can serve as an effective playful intervention to manage frailty in aging individuals.
- Activities such as theatre, creative writing, and humor therapy contribute significantly to emotional intelligence.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of cognitive-enhancing activities vary in their effectiveness across diverse populations?
