## Cultural Relevance in Therapeutic Practices

The concept of cultural relevance emerges as a critical factor in therapeutic practices, particularly when engaging older adults. Source 1 highlights how therapeutic clowns can effectively meet the needs of elderly individuals by providing emotional support and fostering creative engagement. This approach underscores the importance of adapting interventions to align with the cultural context and preferences of the target population, thereby enhancing the efficacy and acceptance of such practices.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic interventions are more effective when tailored to meet specific cultural needs
- Creative engagement can significantly enhance emotional well-being in older adults
- Cultural relevance plays a crucial role in the success of therapeutic practices

## Open Questions

- How can other therapeutic practices be adapted to ensure cultural relevance and effectiveness for diverse populations?
