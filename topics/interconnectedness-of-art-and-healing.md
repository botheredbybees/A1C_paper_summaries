## Interconnectedness of Art and Healing

The sources collectively emphasize the potential for arts to support health and wellbeing through an interconnected approach that transcends traditional institutional silos. They highlight the role of artists in engaging with public health, advocating for a more integrated model where artistic interventions are seen as complementary to conventional healthcare practices. This interdisciplinary approach seeks to leverage the unique qualities of art to enhance therapeutic outcomes and patient engagement, challenging existing barriers between the arts sector and healthcare providers. However, challenges persist around defining the specific roles of artists within healthcare settings and ensuring sustained collaboration beyond initial interest or funding cycles.

## Key Findings

- Artistic interventions can significantly support health and wellbeing when integrated into public health frameworks.
- The removal of institutional silos between arts organizations and healthcare providers is crucial for effective implementation of art-based healing practices.
- Engagement of artists in public health initiatives requires clear definitions of roles and responsibilities to ensure meaningful collaboration.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms can be developed to sustain long-term partnerships between the arts sector and healthcare institutions beyond initial project funding?
