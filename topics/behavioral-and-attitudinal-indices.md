## Behavioral And Attitudinal Indices

The utilization of behavioral and attitudinal indices in the cultural sector is primarily aimed at enhancing understanding among policymakers and advocacy groups about arts participation. The National Arts Participation Survey, as highlighted by the Creative Australia initiative, underscores the importance of these indices for gauging public attitudes towards arts engagement. This tool aids in segmenting the population into distinct categories based on their behavior and attitudes toward cultural activities, thereby enabling targeted interventions to increase accessibility and interest. However, while such segmentation models offer valuable insights for advocacy, there remains a need for further research into the direct impact of these indices on policy outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Behavioral and attitudinal indices are crucial for segmenting populations related to arts participation.
- These indices assist in developing targeted advocacy strategies within cultural sectors.
- National surveys contribute significantly to understanding public engagement with art.

## Open Questions

- How do behavioral and attitudinal indices influence actual policy changes or outcomes?
