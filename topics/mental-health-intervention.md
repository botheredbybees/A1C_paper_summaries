## Mental Health Interventions in Educational and Disaster Settings

The provided sources highlight different approaches to mental health interventions that cater to diverse populations facing various challenges. Music therapy, as discussed in Source 1, is presented as a beneficial intervention for students in educational settings, focusing on promoting overall mental health and recovery orientation. This approach leverages the therapeutic benefits of music to address emotional and psychological needs effectively. In contrast, Source 2 explores play therapy, emphasizing its role in aiding children's verbal communication and emotional expression through guided activities designed for their developmental stage. While both interventions aim at enhancing well-being and addressing specific mental health concerns, they operate on different theoretical frameworks and target distinct aspects of youth development. Source 3 shifts focus to psychological first aid (PFA), which is critical for providing timely psychosocial support in disaster-affected communities. PFA aims to alleviate emotional distress and ensure physical safety by offering a structured yet flexible approach that can be adapted based on immediate needs and cultural contexts. These interventions demonstrate the versatility of mental health approaches tailored towards specific settings and populations, each with its unique methodologies and theoretical underpinnings.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances mental health recovery in educational environments by addressing emotional well-being through music-based activities.
- Play therapy supports children's verbal communication and emotional expression within a structured yet flexible framework suitable for developmental needs.
- Psychological first aid offers immediate psychosocial support to disaster-affected individuals, prioritizing safety and emotional stabilization.
