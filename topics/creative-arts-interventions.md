## Creative Arts Interventions

The field of Creative Arts Interventions encompasses a wide range of therapeutic practices aimed at addressing various psychological and emotional needs. Sources highlight the efficacy of these interventions in reducing stress, managing trauma-related symptoms, and fostering resilience among participants. UT MD Anderson's support programs underscore the importance of integrative medicine and holistic patient care through creative arts therapies such as painting or music therapy. These practices are theorized to offer therapeutic benefits by engaging patients holistically, beyond conventional medical treatments.

Research supports this approach: A systematic review on Creative Arts Interventions for stress management reveals significant reductions in anxiety levels among participants, with CATs demonstrating particular effectiveness based on the PICOS principle criteria. Furthermore, the effectiveness of creative arts-based interventions is corroborated by a meta-analysis focusing on children and adolescents exposed to traumatic events. This study shows that such interventions can mitigate negative mood states, externalizing problems, and other post-traumatic stress disorder symptoms.

Neuroscience contributes another layer to this discussion, suggesting that these practices might influence neural plasticity positively. The Afloat project exemplifies how creative expression can build resilience by fostering a sense of place within participants, aiding in their emotional and psychological recovery from trauma. Integrating theoretical frameworks like Erikson's stages of psychosocial development, Piaget’s cognitive developmental theory, and Vygotsky’s social constructivism enhances our understanding of how these interventions cater to diverse developmental needs across age groups.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies effectively reduce stress levels and anxiety in patients
- Integrative medicine approaches that include creative arts contribute to holistic patient care
- Neural plasticity may be positively influenced by engaging in creative arts activities

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of creative arts interventions on neural development?
