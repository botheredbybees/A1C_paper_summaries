## Rules of Composition in Photography

The principles of composition are fundamental to the art and science of photography. Both sources emphasize the importance of the rule-of-thirds for creating balanced images that draw viewers' attention effectively. Additionally, both documents highlight the significance of creative lighting and leading lines in guiding the viewer's eye through the image. Source 2 introduces concepts such as figure-to-ground contrast, pattern repetition, and symmetry which enhance visual appeal and narrative depth. These elements are essential not only for capturing aesthetically pleasing images but also for storytelling within a single frame.

## Key Findings

- The rule-of-thirds is widely regarded as a foundational technique in photographic composition to achieve balance and focal point.
- Leading lines, creative lighting, figure-to-ground contrast, pattern repetition, and symmetry significantly enhance the visual impact of photographs by guiding viewer attention and creating depth.
- Photographic composition rules are not rigid but rather serve as guidelines that photographers can adapt creatively.
