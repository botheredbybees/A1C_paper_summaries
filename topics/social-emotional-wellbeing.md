## Social-Emotional Wellbeing in Indigenous Communities

The exploration of how country, spirituality, music, and arts contribute to the social-emotional wellbeing of Indigenous populations highlights a multifaceted approach to mental health and wellness. Across various sources, there is agreement that these cultural elements play significant roles in fostering attachment to one's ancestral land (country) and promoting resilience against adverse socio-economic conditions. Music, in particular, is identified as a catalyst for change and healing within communities, aiding in the expression of complex emotions and facilitating communal bonding through shared experiences. The sources also emphasize the importance of spiritual practices and artistic expressions in enhancing individual and collective mental health outcomes. However, tensions arise regarding the extent to which these culturally specific interventions can be universally applied or adapted to other contexts without losing their inherent efficacy and cultural significance.

## Key Findings

- Country (ancestral land) plays a central role in fostering social-emotional wellbeing among Indigenous populations.
- Spirituality and artistic expressions significantly contribute to mental health and resilience within communities.
- Music serves as a powerful medium for healing, emotional expression, and community bonding.

## Open Questions

- How can culturally specific interventions be effectively adapted or universally applied without compromising their unique cultural significance?
