## Self-Regulation Skills in Children

The use of a hand puppet named Tony the Turtle to teach young children in domestic violence shelters about self-regulation skills is explored. This intervention aims to enhance emotional safety and facilitate open dialogue, allowing children to express their feelings more freely and learn strategies for managing emotions. Sources indicate positive experiences with this tool but also highlight challenges such as the need for consistent implementation and training for staff using Tony the Turtle effectively.

## Key Findings

- Hand puppets like Tony the Turtle are useful tools in teaching self-regulation skills.
- Open dialogue encouraged by Tony the Turtle helps improve emotional safety among children.
- Consistent use of the hand puppet is essential for its effectiveness in improving self-regulation.

## Open Questions

- What additional training might be necessary to ensure staff can utilize Tony the Turtle effectively?
