## Refugee Support through Creative Arts Programs

Creative arts programs are increasingly recognized as vital interventions to support refugee and immigrant children's emotional well-being. Both sources emphasize the importance of cultural sensitivity in the design of these programs, highlighting that they must be responsive to the unique backgrounds and experiences of participants. Source One discusses how the use of puppets can provide a sense-of-agency for children who might otherwise feel helpless, acting as a coping mechanism that promotes emotional well-being. Meanwhile, Source Two underscores the critical role of cultural context in ensuring that arts programs effectively address social isolation, a common issue among refugees and immigrants. The integration of culturally sensitive art forms not only enhances the therapeutic value but also fosters a sense of belonging.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts programs can significantly enhance emotional well-being for refugee children.
- Cultural sensitivity is crucial in designing effective mental health interventions for immigrant populations.
- Puppets and other creative tools can act as coping mechanisms, providing refugees with a renewed sense of agency.

## Open Questions

- What are the most culturally sensitive forms of art that can be integrated into programs to address social isolation among refugee children?
