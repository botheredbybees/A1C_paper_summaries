## Emotion Regulation in Autism

The provided sources explore the efficacy of an intervention combining Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) and Rhythmic Relating to enhance emotion regulation in individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). Both studies highlight a significant improvement in emotion regulation skills after implementing this combined approach. Notably, both reports emphasize the importance of gender-based considerations while conducting these interventions, suggesting that strategies may need to be adapted for different genders to achieve optimal outcomes. The use of the Emotion Regulation Checklist (ERC) as an assessment tool provides quantitative evidence supporting the positive impact of CCPT and Rhythmic Relating on emotion regulation in children with ASD.

## Key Findings

- Combining Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) and Rhythmic Relating is effective in improving emotion regulation skills among individuals with autism.
- Gender considerations are crucial when implementing interventions for emotion regulation in children with ASD.
- The Emotion Regulation Checklist (ERC) serves as a reliable tool to measure the effectiveness of CCPT and Rhythmic Relating.

## Open Questions

- What specific gender-based adaptations should be made in the implementation of CCPT and Rhythmic Relating for maximum efficacy?
