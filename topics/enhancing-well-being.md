## Enhancing Well-Being Through The Arts

The integration of artistic activities as an intervention for enhancing well-being is supported across multiple sources. Each source emphasizes the benefits of engaging in creative and self-expressive arts, particularly for individuals dealing with dementia or other chronic conditions. A central theme is that participation in these activities can significantly improve mental health, providing a sense of purpose and connection to one's identity. Tensions arise regarding the extent to which these interventions are accessible and tailored to individual needs, highlighting gaps in both research and practice.

## Key Findings

- The arts provide effective therapeutic benefits for individuals with dementia, promoting cognitive function and emotional well-being.
- Self-expression through creative activities can enhance mental health by offering a constructive outlet for emotions and experiences.
- Engagement in the arts is associated with improved quality of life and reduced feelings of isolation.

## Open Questions

- How can access to art-based interventions be increased for underserved populations?
