## Responsibility Shift in Marginalized Communities

The concept of responsibility shift is explored within the context of refugee youth and their adaptation to new roles and responsibilities following migration. Sources emphasize the transformative nature of this process, highlighting how individuals undergo significant changes in role that reflect broader societal shifts in expectations and cultural norms. Through arts-informed research methods, tacit knowledge and symbolic expression play crucial roles in capturing the nuanced experiences and perceptions of these youth. The use of such methodologies not only aids in understanding the complex dynamics of responsibility shift but also serves as a therapeutic intervention for those navigating these changes. However, while sources agree on the importance of recognizing and adapting to new responsibilities, there is less clarity on how best to support individuals through this transition without reinforcing existing power imbalances.

## Key Findings

- Arts-informed research methods provide unique insights into the experiences of responsibility shift among refugee youth.
- Symbolic expression helps in capturing tacit knowledge and the nuanced understanding of role changes within marginalized communities.
- The process of responsibility shift can serve as both a challenge and an opportunity for personal growth and adaptation.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective strategies to support individuals through the challenging process of responsibility shift without perpetuating existing power dynamics?
