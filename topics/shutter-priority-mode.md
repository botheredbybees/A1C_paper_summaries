## Shutter-Priority Mode

The use of shutter priority mode (S or Tv on camera dials) is a crucial photography technique for controlling exposure and capturing motion effectively. Across the provided source, it is highlighted that this semi-automatic shooting mode allows photographers to fix the shutter speed while adjusting aperture automatically to maintain correct exposure, impacting both depth-of-field and potential motion blur in the resulting images. This flexibility empowers users to focus on managing the movement of subjects or camera shake, making it an essential tool for capturing dynamic scenes without compromising lighting conditions.

## Key Findings

- Shutter priority mode enables photographers to control shutter speed independently from aperture settings.
- This setting is pivotal in reducing motion blur by allowing faster shutter speeds
- Aperture adjustments are automated based on the chosen shutter speed and light availability
