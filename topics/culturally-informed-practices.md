## Culturally Informed Practices

The integration of culturally informed practices in mental health and suicide prevention emphasizes a strengths-based approach centered on indigenous communities' unique cultural contexts. Across the sources, there is agreement that these practices are essential for effective intervention strategies, particularly in addressing historical traumas and fostering resilience within affected populations. However, tensions arise regarding the implementation challenges faced by healthcare providers who may lack deep understanding or training in culturally specific methods required to deliver such interventions authentically.

## Key Findings

- Culturally informed practices are vital for effective mental health intervention among indigenous communities.
- A strengths-based approach is crucial when implementing culturally tailored mental health strategies.
- Community-led initiatives play a pivotal role in the success of culturally informed mental health and suicide prevention programs.

## Open Questions

- What training requirements do healthcare providers need to successfully implement culturally informed practices?
