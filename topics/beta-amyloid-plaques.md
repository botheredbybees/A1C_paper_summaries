## Beta-Amyloid Plaques in Alzheimer's Disease

The presence of beta-amyloid plaques and tau protein neurofibrillary tangles are hallmark features of Alzheimer's disease. Both sources emphasize these two elements as key pathological indicators, although they differ slightly on the mechanisms involved. Source 1 focuses more on the role of tau proteins in causing neuronal damage alongside beta-amyloid plaques, while Source 2 discusses potential therapeutic interventions such as acetylcholine inhibitors and immune response training vaccines targeting amyloid-beta. Despite differences in focus, both sources highlight the significance of beta-amyloid plaques in disease progression.

## Key Findings

- Beta-amyloid plaques are a primary pathological feature of Alzheimer's disease.
- Tau protein neurofibrillary tangles and beta-amyloid plaques together contribute to neuronal damage.
- Therapeutic interventions like acetylcholine inhibitors aim to mitigate cognitive decline associated with beta-amyloid pathology.
