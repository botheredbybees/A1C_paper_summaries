## Creative Processes in Dementia

The exploration of the relationship between dementia and artistic creativity highlights a complex interplay where neurodegenerative diseases such as neuronal intermediate filament inclusion disease (NIFID) significantly impact creative processes. This condition, often associated with frontotemporal dementia (FTD), leads to a notable dissolution in an individual's ability to engage in art creation due to the physiological changes within the brain. Across studies focusing on individuals diagnosed with NIFID and FTD, there is a common observation that patients exhibit a marked decline in their capacity for creativity, particularly when it comes to engaging in artistic activities which were previously integral parts of their lives. Despite this significant deterioration, some research hints at the potential therapeutic benefits of art therapy as an intervention for cognitive stimulation and emotional support.

## Key Findings

- Neurodegenerative diseases like NIFID significantly impair creative processes in individuals with dementia
- Frontotemporal dementia (FTD) contributes to the deterioration of artistic creativity
- Art therapy may offer potential benefits as an intervention for cognitive and emotional support

## Open Questions

- How can art therapy be tailored to address specific challenges faced by individuals suffering from NIFID or FTD?
