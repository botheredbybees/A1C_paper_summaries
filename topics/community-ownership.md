## Community Ownership

The concept of community ownership emerges as a critical component in fostering social change and empowerment within communities. Across the sources, it is clear that a holistic approach to intervention strategies necessitates active participation from community members themselves. This proactive involvement is pivotal not only for addressing immediate needs but also for creating sustainable solutions that are tailored to the specific contexts and challenges faced by these groups. Community ownership involves recognizing the intrinsic value of local knowledge and ensuring that decision-making processes reflect the desires and needs articulated by residents, thereby enhancing their sense of agency and fostering a sense of collective responsibility.

## Key Findings

- Community ownership is essential for sustainable social change.
- A holistic approach integrates community input into intervention strategies
- Local knowledge plays a crucial role in effective community interventions

## Open Questions

- How can interventions effectively balance external support with local control?
