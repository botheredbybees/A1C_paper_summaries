## Emotional Safety

The concept of emotional safety is a crucial aspect in both therapeutic and educational contexts. The Social Impact Report highlights the importance of fostering emotional safety to enhance quality of care and patient receptivity to treatment, indicating that when individuals feel emotionally safe, they are more likely to engage positively with their healthcare providers and therapies. This aligns with findings from 'Time for Tony the Turtle', which explores how using a hand puppet can facilitate open dialogue in young children affected by domestic violence, promoting self-regulation skills and emotional safety. Both sources underscore the need for environments that encourage individuals to express themselves freely without fear of judgment or harm.

## Key Findings

- Emotional safety enhances patient receptivity to treatment.
- Facilitating open dialogue through creative means can promote emotional safety in vulnerable populations.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods for creating emotionally safe environments across diverse therapeutic and educational settings?
