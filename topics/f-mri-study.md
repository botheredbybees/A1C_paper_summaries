## Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging Studies of Artistic Brain Activity

The use of fMRI in studying the artistic brain reveals distinct patterns of activity between skilled and novice artists (Source 1) as well as differential effects on functional brain connectivity when engaging in visual art production versus cognitive evaluation of art (Source 2). Both studies highlight the importance of the default-mode network for resilience to stress and cognitive flexibility, indicating that engagement with the arts can lead to positive neuroplastic changes. While Source 1 focuses on the neurological basis of expertise through visual information processing, Source 2 delves into how different aspects of art involvement affect brain connectivity. Despite their focus on distinct methodologies, both studies underscore the potential for artistic activity to influence cognitive and neural processes.

## Key Findings

- Artistic engagement can lead to differential changes in functional brain connectivity depending on whether it involves production or evaluation activities.
- The default-mode network plays a crucial role in resilience to stress and is engaged during art-related tasks.
- Skilled artists demonstrate distinct patterns of neural activity compared to novices when engaging in artistic tasks.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of sustained engagement with visual arts on brain structure and function?
