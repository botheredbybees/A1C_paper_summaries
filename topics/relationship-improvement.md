## Music Therapy in Dementia Care

The integration of music and music therapy into dementia care pathways has been proposed as a beneficial approach to managing symptoms such as agitation while enhancing the overall quality of life for individuals with dementia. The Homeside trial highlighted the potential of using personalized music interventions to reduce behavioral issues in dementia patients, thereby improving relationships between caregivers and patients. However, the effectiveness varies based on individual responses to music, suggesting a need for tailored approaches. Despite these promising findings, there remains a gap in standardizing and embedding music therapy as an integral part of routine care pathways across different healthcare settings.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can be effective in reducing agitation in dementia patients.
- Personalized musical interventions improve caregiver-patient relationships.
- Tailored approaches to music therapy are necessary due to individual differences.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to embedding music therapy as a standard practice in dementia care?
