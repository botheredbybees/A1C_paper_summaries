## Cultural and Public Value of Arts

The concept of the cultural and public value of arts is explored across Australia and Aotearoa New Zealand, focusing on thematic analysis that includes indigenous perspectives. The sources highlight a comprehensive approach to understanding how arts contribute not only economically but also culturally and socially. However, there's a notable emphasis on integrating indigenous views in valuing art forms, underscoring the cultural specificity of these contributions.

## Key Findings

- Arts hold significant public value beyond economic benefits
- Incorporating indigenous perspectives is crucial for a holistic understanding of arts' value
- Cultural specificity influences how arts are valued and supported

## Open Questions

- How can the cultural and public value of arts be measured in ways that respect and include diverse indigenous viewpoints?
