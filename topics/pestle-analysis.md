## Pestle Analysis

The integration of the PESTLE analysis across various sources highlights its role as a robust method for understanding and addressing external factors affecting interventions in healthcare contexts. Source 1 emphasizes the use of PESTLE to plan quality improvement measures within patient-centered care settings, illustrating how it aids in identifying socio-economic and political influences that impact intervention design. Meanwhile, Sources 2 and 3 focus on the application of PESTLE analysis specifically for arts in health initiatives, combining this with Kolb’s Reflective Cycle to deepen understanding through discovery interviews and environmental scans. Despite these shared themes, there is a noticeable tension between the practical application described in Source 1 versus the theoretical and reflective use detailed in Sources 2 and 3.

## Key Findings

- PESTLE analysis serves as an essential tool for identifying external factors influencing healthcare interventions.
- Integration of PESTLE with Kolb’s Reflective Cycle enhances understanding through reflective practice.
- There are practical applications for quality improvement measures versus theoretical uses in arts and health.

## Open Questions

- How can the gap between practical application (quality improvement) and theoretical reflection (arts in health) be bridged to ensure a holistic use of PESTLE analysis?
