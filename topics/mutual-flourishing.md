## Mutual Flourishing in Music Therapy

The concept of 'mutual flourishing' in improvisational music therapy within dementia care homes is explored through the notion of a 'ripple effect'. This phenomenon suggests that engagement in musical activities promotes not only individual well-being but also positive impacts on the broader community and environment. Key themes include shared participation, where patients and caregivers engage actively and collaboratively in music-making sessions, leading to enhanced social connections and emotional support. The ripple effect highlights how these benefits extend beyond immediate participants to influence care practices and relationships within the facility, fostering a supportive ecosystem that nurtures well-being.

## Key Findings

- Improvisational music therapy facilitates mutual flourishing among dementia patients and caregivers through active participation.
- The 'ripple effect' extends the positive impacts of musical engagement beyond individual sessions to affect wider community dynamics.
- Shared participation in music-making activities promotes emotional support and enhanced social connections.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of mutual flourishing be applied to other therapeutic interventions outside of music therapy?
