## Self-Concept Development in Therapeutic Settings

The exploration of self-concept through therapeutic interventions reveals a convergence of methodologies aimed at enhancing individuals' understanding and regulation of their own identity. Expressive writing and art therapy are highlighted as effective practices for developing self-concept, particularly among children with autism spectrum disorders (ASD). Source 1 discusses the expressive writing paradigm, which emphasizes the psychological benefits of creative writing in fostering emotional awareness and expression. This approach moves beyond mere therapeutic value to encompass developmental aspects crucial for personal growth. Meanwhile, Source 2 evaluates an art therapy program named 'Images of Self,' specifically designed to improve ASD children's sense-of-self by enhancing emotion-regulation, behavioral flexibility, and social skills through artistic creation. Both sources underscore the transformative potential of creative interventions in shaping self-concept, highlighting the interplay between emotional expression and developmental milestones.

## Key Findings

- Expressive writing and art therapy are effective interventions for developing self-concept.
- Creative therapies enhance emotion-regulation and social skills among children with ASD.
- Developmental aspects of therapeutic writing contribute to personal growth.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of expressive writing and art therapy on self-concept development in individuals with ASD?
