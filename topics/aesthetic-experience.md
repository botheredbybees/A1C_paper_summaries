## Aesthetic Experience

The concept of aesthetic experience encompasses the neurological and psychological processes that occur during encounters with art. Across these sources, there is a convergence on understanding how individuals respond emotionally and physiologically to art. Source 1 emphasizes embodied simulation and mirror neurons in the generation of empathy through visual motion, suggesting universal mechanisms underlie emotional responses to art. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the activation of the default mode network (DMN), a brain region associated with self-referential thought and social cognition, during intense aesthetic experiences, highlighting individual differences in how people perceive and react to art based on personal and cultural contexts. Meanwhile, Source 3 integrates these findings by providing a unified visual framework that maps emotional responses and physiological reactions to art, further illustrating the complexity of aesthetic experience as it bridges subjective interpretations with neurological activity.

## Key Findings

- Aesthetic experiences involve both universal embodied mechanisms such as mirror neurons and individual-specific neural networks like the default mode network.
- The generation of empathy through art is facilitated by embodied simulation and visual motion in viewers.
- Emotional responses to art can be mapped onto a unified framework that includes physiological reactions.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and personal differences influence the activation patterns within the default mode network during aesthetic experiences?
