## Migrant Wellbeing Through Creative Arts

The integration of creative arts therapies as an intervention for enhancing the mental and cultural adjustment wellbeing of migrants is highlighted across sources. Both documents emphasize that creative therapy can serve dual purposes: it aids in the therapeutic process by helping migrants express and work through their emotional challenges, such as loneliness or stress from cultural dislocation. Moreover, these interventions also foster community engagement by bringing together individuals with shared experiences, thereby reinforcing a sense of belonging and social support networks essential for overall wellbeing. The sources underscore the importance of arts-based research methodologies in documenting and sharing migrant stories, which not only have therapeutic value for participants but also contribute to raising awareness and fostering empathy within broader communities.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapy can be an effective tool for enhancing mental health among migrants by providing a safe space for emotional expression and processing
- Community engagement through creative arts interventions helps build social support networks, crucial for the wellbeing of migrant populations
- Arts-based research methodologies facilitate the sharing of migrant stories, contributing to broader societal understanding and empathy

## Open Questions

- How can cultural adjustment programs be effectively integrated with creative arts therapies to maximize their benefits for migrants?
