## Personhood Maximization

The concept of maximizing personhood in early dementia care highlights the importance of maintaining an individual's identity and sense of self. This approach focuses on preserving personal autonomy, respect, and dignity despite cognitive decline. Across sources, there is a consensus that understanding decision-making capacity is crucial for effective intervention strategies aimed at maximizing personhood. However, there remains tension regarding how to balance the need to support individuals while respecting their right to make decisions independently.

## Key Findings

- Understanding decision-making capacity in early dementia is essential for interventions focused on personhood maximization.
- Maintaining respect and dignity for people with early-stage dementia through personalized care enhances quality of life.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare providers effectively balance support for individuals with early-stage dementia while respecting their autonomy?
