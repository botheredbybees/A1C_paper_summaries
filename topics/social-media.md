## Photography and Digital Health

The integration of photography as a therapeutic intervention in digital health contexts reveals diverse applications and ethical considerations. Source one highlights the use of 'Therapeutic Photography' and 'Phototherapy' to explore personal experiences, emotions, and social issues through visual narratives. The introduction of 'Nomophobia,' which describes the anxiety associated with being without or unable to use one's smartphone, underscores how modern technology influences mental health and well-being. Source two delves deeper into these themes by examining the process of remediation in digital storytelling—a transformation where older media forms are reinterpreted and evolved through new technologies—and discusses self-representation in a digital age. This source raises ethical questions about privacy, consent, and control over personal narratives as people share their stories online. Both sources indicate a growing interest in leveraging photography within social media platforms for therapeutic benefits while also recognizing the potential risks and complexities inherent to these practices.

## Key Findings

- Photography can serve as an effective tool for exploring personal experiences and emotions in a digital health context.
- The concept of 'Nomophobia' demonstrates how contemporary technology influences mental health, particularly within social media interactions.
- Digital storytelling through remediation highlights ethical concerns such as privacy, consent, and control over self-representation.

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic interventions utilizing digital photography address the psychological impact of 'Nomophobia'? What measures are necessary to ensure the ethical use of personal narratives in social media?
