## Social Behavior in Autism Spectrum Disorders Therapy

The provided sources explore the impact of art therapy on children with autism spectrum disorders (ASD), focusing particularly on how this intervention affects their sense-of-self and emotion-regulation abilities. The repeated case study design highlights changes in both the children's behaviors and those of the therapists, emphasizing the flexible nature required by these interactions to maximize therapeutic benefits. A consistent theme across sources is the importance of addressing social-behavior skills, which are crucial for improving quality of life for individuals with ASD.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy interventions can positively influence sense-of-self and emotion-regulation in children with autism spectrum disorders (ASD).
- Flexibility in therapeutic approaches is critical to adapting sessions according to the evolving needs of the child.
- Social-behavior skills are a primary focus for improving quality of life in ASD.

## Open Questions

- How can art therapy be further tailored to address unique social challenges faced by individuals with ASD?
