## Anxiety Reduction Through Puppet Play Therapy

Puppet play therapy emerges as a versatile intervention for anxiety reduction in children across various contexts. Sources highlight its utility in both clinical psychodynamic settings and emergency pediatric dental clinics, suggesting it can be adapted to different environments. The technique facilitates psychological safety by allowing externalization of feelings through the symbolic use of puppets. This method aids in reducing patient anxiety scores as measured by tools like the Venham Anxiety Rating Scale, indicating an effective means of therapy outside traditional talk-based approaches.

## Key Findings

- Puppet play therapy is adaptable to diverse therapeutic settings.
- Externalization of feelings through puppets supports psychological safety.
- Quantitative measures show significant reduction in anxiety levels.
