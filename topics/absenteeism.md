## Workplace Choirs and Absenteeism

The integration of workplace choirs as a form of intervention has been explored for its impact on employee well-being. Sources consistently highlight that such initiatives can enhance work engagement, which is directly linked to reduced absenteeism due to increased resilience and stress reduction. However, there are tensions regarding the generalizability of these benefits across different organizational cultures and demographic groups. While some sources emphasize the broad applicability of workplace choirs in improving mental health outcomes, others question their effectiveness in high-stress industries where work demands might overshadow the positive effects of singing.

## Key Findings

- Workplace choirs can improve employee well-being by enhancing engagement and reducing stress.
- Improved resilience is linked to lower rates of absenteeism among participants in workplace choir programs.
- There are potential barriers to implementing workplace choirs, such as organizational culture and industry type.

## Open Questions

- How does the effectiveness of workplace choirs vary across different occupational sectors?
