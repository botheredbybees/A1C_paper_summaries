## Character Development in Therapeutic Contexts

The integration of story-based ethics and puppet play therapy highlights the multifaceted approach to character development within therapeutic interventions for individuals with dementia or those benefiting from expressive therapies. Both sources emphasize the importance of narrative and role-playing in fostering ethical understanding and personal growth. Story-based ethics (Source 1) advocates for a patient-centered approach where patients' stories are crucial in shaping ethical decision-making processes, enhancing their sense of self-worth and dignity. Puppet play therapy (Source 2), on the other hand, uses the metaphorical distance provided by puppets to facilitate emotional expression and therapeutic engagement, thereby aiding in character development through non-verbal communication and imaginative storytelling. Both interventions underscore the power of narrative and symbolic representation in understanding complex emotions and behaviors.

## Key Findings

- Narrative-based interventions are effective tools for ethical decision-making and personal growth.
- Therapeutic use of puppets can enhance emotional expression and therapeutic engagement.
- Character development is facilitated through story-based ethics and puppet play therapy.
