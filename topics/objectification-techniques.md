## Objectification Techniques in Therapy

The use of objectification techniques such as the externalization of problems through puppetry and narratives is a therapeutic intervention primarily used in narrative therapy. These methods aim to help individuals, particularly children, distance themselves from their issues by personifying them as separate entities. The technique facilitates discussions about personal struggles without the immediate pressure or judgment that comes with addressing oneself directly. However, while sources highlight the potential benefits of these techniques, there is also a noted tension around how such objectification can be ethically navigated to avoid harm and ensure positive outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Objectification techniques in therapy involve externalizing personal problems as separate entities through tools like puppetry.
- Narrative therapy uses objectification methods to help individuals, especially children, better understand and manage their issues.
- The technique can provide a safe space for discussing sensitive topics without immediate personal confrontation.

## Open Questions

- How can therapists ensure that the use of objectification techniques does not inadvertently cause harm or reinforce negative perceptions of oneself?
