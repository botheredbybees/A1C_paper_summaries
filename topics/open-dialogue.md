## Open Dialogue in Domestic Violence Shelters

The integration of 'Time for Tony the Turtle' highlights the utility of employing hand puppets as a therapeutic tool to facilitate open dialogue among children exposed to domestic violence. This method creates an emotionally safe space, enabling young participants to develop self-regulation skills and engage in constructive emotional conversations. While the puppet serves as a mediator between child and therapist, its effectiveness lies in fostering trust and encouraging vulnerable subjects to be discussed openly.

## Key Findings

- Hand puppets can serve as effective mediators for open dialogue with children exposed to domestic violence.
- Creating an emotionally safe environment is crucial for therapeutic interventions involving young victims of domestic violence.
- The use of hand puppets in therapy helps in the development of self-regulation skills among affected children.
