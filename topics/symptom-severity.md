## Symptom Severity in Autism Spectrum Disorder

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights the role of music therapy as an intervention for reducing symptom severity in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The systematic review and meta-analysis emphasize that randomized controlled trials indicate a positive impact of music therapy on symptom reduction. Social reactions, which are part of the broader concept of symptom severity, improve significantly when these interventions are employed. However, while there is evidence supporting its efficacy, inconsistencies remain in the methodologies used across studies, suggesting further research is needed to standardize and enhance the effectiveness of such interventions.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy reduces symptom severity in children with autism spectrum disorder.
- Randomized controlled trials support the use of music therapy for improving social reactions.
- There is evidence indicating that standardized methods could improve the efficacy of music therapy.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective standardization practices to enhance the positive outcomes of music therapy interventions?
