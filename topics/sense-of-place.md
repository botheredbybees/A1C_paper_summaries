## Sense Of Place

The concept of 'sense of place' emerges as a central theme in both the case study of murals revitalizing a declining community and the Afloat Project. Both sources highlight how creative interventions can foster resilience, vitality, and a sense of belonging among community members. The use of reverse graffiti and murals not only beautify the environment but also contribute to the cultural identity of spaces, thereby enhancing residents' emotional connection to their surroundings. This interventionist approach underscores the interplay between physical transformations and social-psychological impacts on communities.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions such as murals and reverse graffiti can significantly enhance a community's sense of place by fostering a stronger connection among its members to their environment.
- Sense-of-place initiatives often serve multiple functions, including enhancing resilience and providing opportunities for creative expression.
- Physical improvements to public spaces through art-based projects have the potential to rejuvenate declining communities and improve overall quality of life.

## Open Questions

- How can sense-of-place interventions be tailored to address specific challenges faced by different types of communities, considering factors such as socio-economic status and cultural background?
