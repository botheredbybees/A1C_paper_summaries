## Story-Based Ethics in Dementia Care

The concept of 'story-based ethics' emerges as a robust framework for understanding ethical decision-making in dementia care. Both sources emphasize the centrality of narrative and individual stories to construct ethical practices that are person-centered rather than abstractly theoretical. This approach recognizes each patient's unique life history and character development, thereby personalizing the ethical considerations within the context of their storied lives. The integration of narrative ethics with practical caregiving underscores a shift from conventional ethical frameworks towards more humanistic and empathetic approaches in dementia care.

## Key Findings

- Story-based ethics is crucial for personalized care in dementia settings
- Narrative and individual stories inform ethical decision-making processes
- Person-centered care emphasizes unique life histories over generalized ethical principles

## Open Questions

- How can story-based ethics be operationalized in diverse clinical scenarios?
