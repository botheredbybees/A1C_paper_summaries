## Creative Arts in Health Interventions

The integration of creative arts into health interventions presents a multifaceted approach to enhancing quality of life among various patient populations. Source 1 emphasizes the use of music as an intervention, noting its positive impact on both mental and physical aspects of health-related quality of life (HRQOL). This is measured through surveys like SF-36 and SF-12, providing empirical data supporting the benefits of music therapy. In contrast, Source 2 explores storytelling with image and text, illustrating how narrative photography serves as a coping mechanism for individuals dealing with chronic illness or trauma. The use of artistic expression here goes beyond mere therapeutic activities to become an integral part of personal healing journeys. Lastly, Source 3 delves into the concept of wisdom poetry, highlighting its transcendental universality in addressing deep emotional and psychological needs through poetic forms. This source underscores the cultural significance and intrinsic value of creative arts in fostering a sense of well-being and self-discovery.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions such as music therapy can significantly improve health-related quality of life (HRQOL), particularly mental and physical components, according to empirical evidence.
- Narrative photography functions not only as an artistic endeavor but also as a powerful coping mechanism for individuals facing chronic illness or personal trauma.
- Poetry and other forms of literary art provide deep emotional healing through their cultural wisdom and transcendent universality.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of various creative arts interventions be standardized across different cultures and healthcare settings?
