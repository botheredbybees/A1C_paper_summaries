## Non-Pharmacological Strategies in Alzheimer's Disease

This synthesis focuses on the non-pharmacological interventions that can regulate autophagy-mediated pathways in Alzheimer's disease. Sources emphasize diet, physical activity, and sleep as crucial factors for brain-specific autophagy markers which play a significant role in mitigating protein aggregation linked to neurodegeneration. While all sources highlight these strategies' potential benefits, there is a need for more research on how different combinations of lifestyle changes can be optimized for individual patient needs.

## Key Findings

- Diet, physical activity, and sleep are critical non-pharmacological interventions that impact autophagy-mediated pathways.
- Autophagy markers in the brain are indicative of neurodegeneration due to protein aggregation.
- Lifestyle modifications offer promising avenues to regulate these pathways without pharmacotherapy.

## Open Questions

- What is the optimal combination of diet, exercise, and sleep for maximizing autophagy in Alzheimer's patients?
