## Sigmund Freud's Psychoanalysis

The theoretical framework of Sigmund Freud's psychoanalysis is a cornerstone in the psychological discourse, emphasizing unconscious processes and conflict resolution. It challenges Cartesian dualism by integrating mind-body connections through its biopsychosocial model. Although the text focuses more broadly on arts in health, Freudian theory underpins much of this interdisciplinary approach by advocating for an understanding of human experience that goes beyond merely physical or emotional states into deeper psychological realms.

## Key Findings

- Freud's psychoanalysis provides a foundational theory integrating mind-body connections.
- The biopsychosocial model complements Freudian ideas, expanding on them to include biological factors alongside psychological ones.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of Cartesian dualism with Freudian theories enhance our understanding and application of arts in health practices?
