## Creative Arts Therapies

The synthesis of the two sources highlights the diverse applications and theoretical underpinnings of creative arts therapies. The first source discusses expressive arts therapy's effectiveness for individuals with autism spectrum disorder, particularly in enhancing social skills through empathy mirroring and tele-dance movement psychotherapy, which facilitates non-verbal communication. This aligns with the second source’s emphasis on trauma-informed care within creative arts therapies, focusing on sensory-based interventions that are essential for treating developmental trauma disorder where traditional verbal therapy may not be effective. Both sources underscore the importance of tailored therapeutic approaches to address unique client needs, whether they stem from autism spectrum conditions or childhood trauma. A tension arises between the specific techniques used for different populations, indicating a need for further research on how creative arts therapies can be effectively adapted across varying contexts and diagnoses.

## Key Findings

- Expressive arts therapy enhances social skills in individuals with autism through empathy mirroring and tele-dance movement psychotherapy.
- Creative arts therapies are effective in treating developmental trauma disorder due to their sensory-based interventions.
- Adaptation of creative arts therapies is necessary for different populations based on unique client needs.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts therapies be effectively adapted and standardized for both autism spectrum disorders and childhood trauma?
