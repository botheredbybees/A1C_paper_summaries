## Anxiety in Adolescents

The provided sources focus on the mental health of adolescents and highlight anxiety as a prevalent concern. Source 1 discusses depression and anxiety alongside strategies for protective environments to support adolescents during their identity exploration stage. It emphasizes the importance of intervention strategies tailored to these developmental needs. In contrast, Source 2 delves into personal narratives that illustrate how a sense of control can mitigate feelings of anxiety, suggesting potential psychological mechanisms at play in adolescent anxiety management. While both sources underscore the significance of intervention and protective environments for adolescents suffering from anxiety, they differ in their approach towards understanding underlying causes and effective coping strategies.

## Key Findings

- Adolescents experiencing anxiety benefit from interventions that provide a sense of control over their environment.
- Identity exploration and mental health support during adolescence are critical for managing anxiety.

## Open Questions

- How do intervention strategies tailored to the developmental needs of adolescents impact long-term outcomes in terms of anxiety management?
