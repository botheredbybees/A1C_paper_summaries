## Photovoice in Aged Care and Mental Health

Photovoice is a qualitative research method that empowers participants to express their experiences through photography. Across aged care and mental health fields, it has been utilized to capture emotive narratives of subjective realities. In aged care settings, photovoice projects highlight the aging experience by engaging seniors in documentary-style photography and poetry creation, fostering deeper community engagement. Additionally, visual methods such as collage-making extend this participatory approach to mental health contexts, offering unique insights into personal narratives that might be difficult to convey through traditional verbal means alone.

## Key Findings

- Photovoice is effective for capturing emotive experiences in qualitative research settings.
- Documentary-style photography and poetry creation help seniors express aging-related emotions and thoughts.
- Visual methods like collage-making provide alternative ways of expressing subjective realities in mental health contexts.
