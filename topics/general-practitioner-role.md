## General Practitioner Role in Disaster Response

The role of general practitioners (GPs) in disaster response is highlighted as crucial for managing immediate and long-term health issues among affected populations. Sources emphasize the need for GPs to adapt their practice beyond routine care, addressing both physical injuries and mental health concerns such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). However, there are tensions between the traditional GP role and the specialized knowledge required during disaster scenarios. Training programs that prepare GPs for disaster management could bridge this gap, ensuring more effective and efficient healthcare delivery in crisis situations.

## Key Findings

- General practitioners play a critical role in providing immediate medical care during disasters.
- Addressing mental health issues like PTSD is essential in the aftermath of disasters.
- Training programs can prepare GPs to better handle disaster scenarios.

## Open Questions

- What additional training and resources are necessary for general practitioners to effectively manage disaster response?
