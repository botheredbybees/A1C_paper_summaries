## Externality Of Mind

The concept of the externality of mind emerges from discussions on how memory and cognitive processes are not solely internal but are influenced by external factors. This perspective challenges traditional views that treat memory as an isolated, personal entity. Causal accounts often focus on the role of physical processes in brain functions, whereas constitutive accounts highlight the interplay between internal mental states and their environmental context. The externality of mind suggests that cognitive processes extend beyond individual boundaries into social and material environments, reflecting a broader understanding of how memory is constructed and maintained through interactions with others and external objects.

## Key Findings

- Causal accounts emphasize physical brain processes in explaining cognition and memory.
- Constitutive accounts stress the interdependence between internal mental states and environmental factors.
- The externality of mind challenges traditional notions by integrating social and material environments into cognitive processes.
