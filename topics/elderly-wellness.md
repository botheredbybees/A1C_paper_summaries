## Creative Interventions for Elderly Wellness

The integration of creative interventions in elderly wellness demonstrates significant therapeutic benefits. Music and visual arts such as sculpture are highlighted as means to foster communication, self-expression, and social interaction among people with dementia. These activities not only support cognitive function but also enhance emotional well-being through positive emotions like laughter. Sources indicate that creative expression can serve multiple functions: it acts as a form of communication for those struggling to articulate their thoughts verbally; it encourages co-creation and intergenerational interactions which are vital for mental health in elderly populations. However, while the benefits of these interventions are evident, there is less emphasis on understanding how such activities might vary in efficacy across different stages of dementia or among individuals with varying levels of cognitive impairment.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression through music and sculpture can enhance communication and self-expression for people with dementia.
- Interventions that promote intergenerational interaction improve emotional well-being and social engagement among the elderly.
- Co-creation in arts activities supports mental health by fostering a sense of community and shared purpose.

## Open Questions

- How do creative interventions vary in effectiveness across different stages or types of cognitive impairment?
