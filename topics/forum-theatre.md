## Forum Theatre

Augusto Boal's Forum Theatre is an innovative form of theatre that aims to empower individuals and communities by providing them with tools for social change. The key concept in this intervention is the 'Theatre of the Oppressed,' which seeks to transform the traditional dynamic between audience and performers into a participatory relationship where spectators become 'spect-actors' who can intervene in the action, propose alternatives, and collectively seek solutions to real-life issues they face. This method is particularly effective in educational settings as it encourages critical thinking, dialogue, and creativity among participants, fostering an environment of empowerment through theatre.

## Key Findings

- Forum Theatre empowers individuals by transforming passive spectators into active 'spect-actors' who engage with the play's narrative to propose solutions for real-life issues.
- The intervention is rooted in the concept of 'Theatre of the Oppressed,' which aims at creating a participatory dynamic where theatre becomes a means for social transformation.
- In educational contexts, Forum Theatre facilitates critical thinking and dialogue among participants.
