## Perceptual Biases in Trauma

The topic of perceptual biases in trauma is approached through the lens of neural rehabilitation and therapeutic intervention. Creative arts therapies are highlighted as effective tools for addressing these perceptual biases by facilitating emotional expression and body movement, which align with neuroscience insights. Sources indicate that traumatic experiences can distort perception, leading to biased interpretations of reality and heightened sensitivity to threat cues. The application of creative arts within trauma therapy aims at neuroplasticity, thereby enabling patients to regain control over their perceptions and manage their reactions more effectively.

## Key Findings

- Traumatic events lead to perceptual biases that can persist long after the traumatic event is over.
- Creative arts therapies help in neural rehabilitation by encouraging emotional expression and body movement.
- The integration of neuroscience into therapeutic practices enhances understanding of trauma's impact on perception.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which creative arts influence neuroplasticity and reduce perceptual biases?
