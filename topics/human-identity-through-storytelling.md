## Human Identity Through Storytelling

The integration of storytelling as an intervention in understanding and reinforcing human identity is a versatile practice seen across various contexts. Applied Theater exemplifies the use of storytelling beyond traditional stages, emphasizing its utility in dementia care interventions where it aids in recapturing personal narratives for patients with declining cognitive abilities. Through this approach, storytelling not only serves as a therapeutic tool but also facilitates social connections and memory recollection among elderly individuals, thereby enhancing their sense of self-worth and identity.

## Key Findings

- Storytelling can serve as an effective intervention to address challenges in dementia care by facilitating personal narrative reconstruction.
- The use of storytelling in dementia interventions promotes social interaction and emotional well-being for patients with cognitive decline.
- Beyond therapeutic applications, storytelling plays a role in maintaining and reconstructing human identity across various stages of life.
