## Interdisciplinary Approaches to Quality of Life Improvement

This synthesis focuses on interdisciplinary research aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals facing health challenges such as dementia. Source 1 explores how visual arts interventions can benefit people with dementia, emphasizing participatory methods that encourage active engagement and expression. This aligns with broader discussions in affective science (Source 2), which examines emotions and moods to understand their influence on well-being. While both sources highlight the potential of artistic and emotional expressions as therapeutic tools, they also suggest the need for more integrated methodologies that combine visual arts therapy with emotional assessment techniques.

## Key Findings

- Visual arts interventions can significantly improve quality of life for people living with dementia through participatory methods.
- The study of emotions is crucial in understanding how artistic activities influence well-being and mental health.

## Open Questions

- How can interdisciplinary approaches better integrate emotional assessment tools into visual arts-based therapies to enhance their effectiveness?
