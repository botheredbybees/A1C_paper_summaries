## Structural And Functional Brain Development

The synthesis of findings from the provided sources highlights the multifaceted nature of structural and functional brain development in early childhood. The research underscores the interplay between genetic and environmental factors in shaping this critical period of neurological growth. Imaging techniques offer a non-invasive approach to mapping these developments, providing insights into neuropsychiatric health issues that arise during this formative stage. However, while imaging technologies are crucial for understanding structural changes, there is less clarity regarding how functional connectivity correlates with behavioral outcomes. The integration of genetic and environmental data alongside brain imaging promises deeper understanding but poses challenges in establishing causality.

## Key Findings

- Structural and functional brain development is significantly influenced by both genetic predispositions and environmental factors in early childhood.
- Imaging techniques, such as MRI and fMRI, are pivotal for tracking changes in the developing brain's structure and function.
- There exists a need to correlate structural and functional neuroimaging findings with behavioral health outcomes.

## Open Questions

- What is the extent of causality between genetic/environmental factors and observed developmental changes as measured by imaging technologies?
