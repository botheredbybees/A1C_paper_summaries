## Kitwood’s Person-Centered Care Model

The concept of person-centered care in dementia management is extensively elaborated upon by Kitwood and emphasizes a holistic approach that respects the inherent dignity, individuality, and personal preferences of each patient. This theoretical framework contrasts with traditional medical approaches which often reduce individuals to their symptoms or diagnosis. The model integrates Maslow's hierarchy of needs to underscore the importance of fulfilling both basic physiological necessities as well as higher-order psychological and social requirements for dementia patients. Despite its foundational impact on modern care practices, some critiques point out a lack of empirical evidence proving its efficacy in all settings, highlighting the need for further research.

## Key Findings

- Kitwood’s model emphasizes the importance of individual dignity and personal preferences.
- It integrates Maslow's hierarchy of needs to address both basic physiological and higher-order psychological/social requirements.
- The model faces critiques due to a perceived lack of empirical evidence supporting its effectiveness in all care settings.
