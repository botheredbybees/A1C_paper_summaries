## Therapeutic Efficacy of Creative Arts in Psychotherapy

The evaluation study on creative arts in psychotherapy for traumatized children underscores the intervention's potential to address behavioral problems and promote posttraumatic growth. Across sources, there is a consistent recognition that creative arts can serve as an effective medium for therapeutic engagement, particularly among populations facing trauma. However, questions persist about the long-term efficacy and sustainability of such interventions without ongoing support or integration with conventional therapy methods. The study highlights the need for further research to establish clear metrics for measuring therapeutic success within diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts in psychotherapy can effectively address behavioral problems in traumatized children
- Posttraumatic growth is a measurable outcome of creative arts interventions
- Cultural context influences the efficacy and implementation of creative arts therapies

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of creative arts-based interventions on mental health?
