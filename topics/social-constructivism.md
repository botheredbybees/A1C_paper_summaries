## Social Constructivism in Dementia Care

The concept of social constructivism is explored through the lens of dementia care, emphasizing a humanistic and contextual approach that interprets memory and forgetting within social contexts. This perspective suggests that the understanding and experience of dementia are co-constructed by individuals, their environment, and society, moving away from a purely medical or individualized view towards a more holistic framework. The integration of this theory in practice highlights the importance of considering personal narratives and social interactions when addressing dementia-related issues.

## Key Findings

- Social constructivism offers a humanistic approach to understanding dementia that goes beyond medical perspectives
- Memory and forgetting are interpreted contextually, highlighting the role of social environments in shaping experiences with dementia
- The theory emphasizes co-construction processes involving individuals, their environment, and society

## Open Questions

- How can social constructivism be effectively applied in practical settings to improve care for those affected by dementia?
