## Self-Induced Laughter

The concept of self-induced laughter as a therapeutic tool is gaining recognition in the field of lifestyle medicine. Sources emphasize the spontaneous nature and potential benefits of inducing laughter intentionally for psychological and physiological health improvement. While there are acknowledged advantages such as stress reduction, enhancing mood, and boosting immune function, challenges remain in standardizing its application across diverse populations. The variability in individual responses to self-induced laughter highlights a need for further research on personalized approaches.

## Key Findings

- Self-induced laughter is proposed as an effective tool for improving mental health.
- Spontaneous and intentional laughter both offer therapeutic benefits.
- Individual response to self-induced laughter varies widely.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal methods for standardizing self-induced laughter practices across different demographics?
