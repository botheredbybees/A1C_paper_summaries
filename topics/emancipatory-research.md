## Emancipatory Research

The concept of emancipatory research is highlighted across the sources as a methodological approach aimed at empowering individuals and communities to understand and transform their social realities. This approach emphasizes participatory involvement, critical consciousness, and action towards societal change. The power dynamics in interviews are particularly noted as crucial considerations within this framework, ensuring that researchers do not exploit or marginalize participants but rather collaborate with them on an equal footing. Visual pedagogy also emerges as a significant component of emancipatory research, where visual methods such as photography serve as tools for both individual self-reflection and collective action. These methods facilitate dialogue about personal experiences and social conditions, fostering the development of critical awareness and practical skills necessary for societal change.

## Key Findings

- Power dynamics in interviews are a key consideration to prevent exploitation and promote equitable collaboration
- Visual pedagogy through methods like photography enhances self-reflection and collective action towards societal transformation
- Emancipatory research aims to empower individuals and communities by fostering critical consciousness

## Open Questions

- How can visual methods be effectively integrated into emancipatory research practices without perpetuating power imbalances?
