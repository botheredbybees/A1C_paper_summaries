## Free-Writing Exercises

The integration of free-writing exercises within various contexts highlights their versatility and adaptability as a therapeutic tool. In the realm of narrative therapy, these exercises are employed to facilitate storytelling through image-based methods, aiming to uncover deeper personal narratives and emotions. Across different applications, free-writing is seen not only as a means to enhance creativity but also as a method for emotional processing and self-discovery. However, there is variation in how these exercises are structured and the specific benefits they offer, indicating that more research could elucidate optimal design principles for various populations.

## Key Findings

- Free-writing exercises serve multiple purposes including therapeutic intervention and creative expression.
- Image-based storytelling enhances the narrative aspect of free-writing exercises, deepening emotional engagement.
- There is variability in how free-writing exercises are implemented across different contexts.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal design principles for effective free-writing exercises that cater to diverse populations?
