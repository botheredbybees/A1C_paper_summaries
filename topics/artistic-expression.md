## Artistic Expression in Dementia Care

The integration of artistic expression into dementia care is a multidimensional approach aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals with dementia. Across various sources, artistic activities are highlighted as beneficial not just for entertainment but also for therapeutic purposes such as cognitive rehabilitation and emotional regulation. Sources emphasize the use of person-centered care that incorporates artistic expression to engage patients on an individual level, focusing on their unique preferences and past experiences. Narrative photography and storytelling through image and text provide a means for individuals with dementia to reconnect with personal narratives and express emotions in ways traditional verbal communication might not allow. Cognitive rehabilitation is also achieved through structured creative arts activities which challenge cognitive functions indirectly while maintaining engagement and interest. However, despite the numerous benefits highlighted by these sources, there remains a need for more standardized approaches and evidence-based guidelines to ensure consistent quality across different settings.

## Key Findings

- Artistic expression in dementia care is effective for emotional regulation and enhancing mental health.
- Person-centered care that includes artistic activities can help individuals with dementia reconnect with personal narratives and express emotions.
- Creative arts interventions, such as narrative photography and storytelling, support cognitive rehabilitation by engaging cognitive functions indirectly.

## Open Questions

- What standardized approaches or evidence-based guidelines are needed to ensure consistent quality in the use of artistic expression for dementia care?
