## Entwinement of Music, Emotion, and Memory

The concept of the entwinement of music, emotion, and memory is explored across multiple sources, indicating a complex interplay where each element influences and shapes the others. The foundational idea posits that listening to music can evoke emotional responses deeply tied to personal memories, suggesting that these three elements are not separate but rather interconnected in human experience. This theory extends beyond mere auditory perception, delving into how rhythmic entrainment plays a crucial role in aligning one's physiological and psychological states with musical rhythms, thereby enhancing the emotional impact of music on memory recall or creation. Additionally, individual differences in musical preferences further complicate this relationship by introducing personal variability that affects how each person experiences and remembers music. Despite broad agreement on the core tenets of the entwinement theory, there remains a gap in understanding the exact mechanisms through which music triggers specific emotional responses linked to particular memories.

## Key Findings

- Music evokes emotions deeply connected to personal memories.
- Rhythmic entrainment aligns physiological and psychological states with musical rhythms, enhancing emotional response.
- Individual differences in musical preferences affect how people experience and remember music.

## Open Questions

- What are the exact mechanisms through which music triggers specific emotional responses tied to particular memories?
