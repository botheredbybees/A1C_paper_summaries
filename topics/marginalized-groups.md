## Marginalized Groups and Arts-Based Interventions

Both sources emphasize the transformative power of arts-based interventions in addressing the needs of marginalized groups. Source 1 focuses on creating art collaboratively with communities to foster cultural sensitivity and enhance community well-being, whereas Source 2 introduces the Creative Change Laboratory (CCoLAB) model that uses arts-based methods to challenge inequalities related to sexual orientation, gender identity, and health issues among marginalized populations. Both documents highlight the importance of critical thinking in these processes, suggesting that arts can serve as a powerful tool for social change when employed with cultural sensitivity.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions enhance community well-being by promoting cultural sensitivity
- Critical thinking is essential in using arts effectively to address inequalities faced by marginalized groups
- Creative Change Laboratory (CCoLAB) model addresses multiple forms of inequality through arts-based methods

## Open Questions

- How can the transformative power of art be measured and quantified in different contexts for marginalized communities?
