## Arts and Dementia Care

The integration of arts-based interventions in dementia care programs, specifically through initiatives like the ARTZ Artists for Alzheimer's Program, underscores a person-centered approach to enhancing quality of life for individuals with dementia. This program emphasizes engaging dementia patients in art creation and appreciation activities within museum settings, fostering a sense of community and personal identity beyond the illness label. Reminiscence therapy is highlighted as a complementary method where past memories are evoked through creative expression or museum visits, potentially alleviating symptoms such as anxiety and depression while promoting cognitive stimulation. Museums are being adapted to accommodate dementia-friendly practices, enhancing accessibility and inclusivity for this population.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions like the ARTZ Artists for Alzheimer's Program provide a person-centered approach in dementia care.
- Museums and art galleries can be transformed into dementia-friendly spaces that enhance engagement and reduce barriers for individuals with dementia.
- Reminiscence therapy through arts activities offers cognitive stimulation and emotional benefits.

## Open Questions

- How can museums further adapt their environments to effectively support all aspects of care for people with dementia?
