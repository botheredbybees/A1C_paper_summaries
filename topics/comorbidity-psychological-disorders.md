## Comorbidity of Psychological Disorders

The provided sources emphasize the complex interplay between psychological disorders and stress or traumatic life events in populations such as those with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). These studies highlight that individuals with ASD often experience comorbid psychological conditions exacerbated by stressful environments, indicating a need for trauma-focused strategies in clinical practice. Stressful life events are shown to increase vulnerability to mental health issues in this population, underscoring the necessity of holistic and integrated therapeutic approaches.

## Key Findings

- Individuals with autism spectrum disorder frequently experience comorbid psychological disorders.
- Stressful life events significantly impact the prevalence and severity of these comorbid conditions.
- Trauma-focused interventions are crucial for addressing mental health issues in individuals with ASD.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms underlie the exacerbation of comorbid psychological disorders due to stress in populations like those with autism spectrum disorder?
