## Inhibitory Control

Inhibitory control is a critical aspect of cognitive function that involves the ability to suppress impulsive responses and engage in goal-directed behavior. One notable condition where inhibitory control is significantly impaired is frontotemporal dementia (FTD), a neurodegenerative disease characterized by protein aggregation in the brain, particularly affecting language functions. Sources discussing FTD highlight how these protein aggregations disrupt neural networks essential for inhibitory control, leading to behavioral and cognitive impairments. This impairment underscores the importance of understanding mechanisms underlying inhibitory control as a therapeutic target or an intervention strategy for managing FTD.

## Key Findings

- Inhibitory control is disrupted in frontotemporal dementia (FTD).
- Protein aggregation in FTD affects neural networks crucial for inhibitory control.
- Impairments in inhibitory control contribute to behavioral and cognitive deficits.
