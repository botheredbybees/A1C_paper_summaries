## Intervention Planning in Arts-Based Interventions

The sources highlight the importance of a structured approach to planning and implementing arts-based health interventions. Both documents emphasize the use of PESTLE analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental) as a foundational tool for assessing external factors that can influence intervention success. This framework helps planners consider the broad context in which their project will operate, from political pressures to technological opportunities and legal constraints.

Kolb’s Reflective Cycle is introduced in Source 1 to complement PESTLE analysis by promoting reflection on past experiences to inform future interventions. Through discovery interviews, stakeholders can share their insights, thereby enhancing the planning process with diverse perspectives and ensuring that all voices are heard and considered. Additionally, stakeholder support emerges as a critical element for success; securing backing from various parties within the community or healthcare system is essential.

While both sources underscore the value of PESTLE analysis in creating robust intervention plans, they also highlight different aspects: Source 1 focuses more on practical implementation through discovery interviews and reflective practice, whereas Source 2 emphasizes the broader framework for conceptualizing interventions, including stakeholder engagement.

## Key Findings

- PESTLE analysis is a crucial component of planning arts-based health interventions by providing a structured way to consider external influences.
- Kolb’s Reflective Cycle enhances PESTLE analysis through reflective practice and discovery interviews, ensuring continuous improvement in intervention design.
- Securing stakeholder support is essential for the success and sustainability of arts-based health interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can PESTLE analysis be effectively integrated with Kolb’s Reflective Cycle to ensure that both strategic planning and reflective practice are incorporated into arts-based interventions?
