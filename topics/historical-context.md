## Historical Context in Visual Storytelling

The sources provided highlight the importance and complexity of historical context in understanding and appreciating visual storytelling. Source 1 discusses Albrecht Dürer through a documentary biography lens, emphasizing the artist's cultural influence within his historical period. This sets a backdrop for examining how artists are perceived within their contemporary contexts and beyond. Meanwhile, Source 2 delves into the origins of photojournalism and its narrative depth, noting that visual storytelling in this medium has deep roots historically, evolving from earlier forms like Dürer’s work to modern-day photography. Both sources underscore the significance of historical context for interpreting visual narratives accurately and appreciating their impact on subsequent developments in art and communication.

## Key Findings

- Historical context is essential for understanding artists' cultural influence.
- Visual storytelling has deep historical roots, evolving from earlier artistic traditions.
