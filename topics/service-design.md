## Service Design

The concept of service design is explored through the lens of lived experience and collaboration. According to source 1, 'Statement on Lived Experience,' emphasizing the authentic voice of those with lived experience in designing services fosters meaningful partnerships that enhance both the quality and relevance of services offered. This approach ensures that service design is not only inclusive but also responsive to real-world needs, highlighting the importance of active participation from individuals who directly benefit from or are affected by these services.

## Key Findings

- Service design should incorporate the authentic voice of those with lived experience.
- Collaborative partnerships between service designers and end-users improve the quality of services.
- Inclusive approaches in service design lead to more relevant and effective outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can barriers to genuine collaboration between service providers and users be systematically addressed?
