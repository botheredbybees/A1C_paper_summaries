## Therapeutic Relationship in Person-Centered and Play Therapies

The therapeutic relationship is a cornerstone of effective therapy, emphasizing the importance of empathy, unconditional positive regard, and self-actualization as central tenets in both person-centered therapy (PCT) and child-centered play therapy (CCPT). Person-centered therapy focuses on fostering an environment where clients can grow through authentic relationships characterized by deep understanding and acceptance. This approach aligns closely with Carl Rogers' principles of promoting the client's sense of self-worth and intrinsic motivation toward personal growth and psychological health. In contrast, CCPT leverages play as a therapeutic medium specifically tailored for children, particularly those on the autism spectrum (ASD), to facilitate emotional expression, social skills development, and communication abilities within a supportive context that mirrors PCT's core values.

## Key Findings

- Empathy, unconditional positive regard, and support for self-actualization are foundational in both person-centered therapy and child-centered play therapy.
- Child-centered play therapy adapts the principles of person-centered therapy to meet the unique needs and developmental stages of children, especially those with ASD.
