## Research Rationale

The concept of research rationale is crucial in the development and approval process of any scholarly study. Both sources emphasize that a solid rationale is foundational for writing an effective research protocol, as it articulates why the proposed research is important and how it contributes to existing knowledge. Feasibility studies are also highlighted as integral parts of this process; they ensure that the research questions can be addressed within practical constraints such as time, budget, and resources. Furthermore, both sources underline the importance of ethical considerations in research protocols, indicating that an ethics committee plays a pivotal role in reviewing the rationale to ensure the study's compliance with ethical standards.

## Key Findings

- Rationale is essential for articulating the significance of proposed research
- Feasibility studies are integral components of developing robust research proposals
- Ethical considerations and review by an ethics committee are critical aspects in validating research protocols

## Open Questions

- How can researchers effectively integrate ethical considerations into their rationale to enhance protocol acceptance?
