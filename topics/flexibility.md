## Flexibility in Autism Spectrum Disorders

The exploration of change in children's and art therapists' behavior through the 'Images of Self' program highlights the critical role of flexibility in addressing challenges faced by individuals with autism spectrum disorders (ASD). Flexibility is crucial for enhancing sense-of-self and emotion-regulation, as well as improving social-behavior. The repeated case study design indicates that a structured yet adaptable intervention can foster significant developmental changes in children diagnosed with ASD. Despite these promising findings, there remains uncertainty about the long-term sustainability of such behavioral modifications without continued therapeutic support.

## Key Findings

- Flexibility is essential for emotion-regulation and social-behavior improvement in individuals with autism spectrum disorders.
- A structured yet adaptable intervention can induce significant developmental changes in children diagnosed with ASD.
- Improvement in flexibility leads to an enhanced sense-of-self, which is vital for therapeutic progress.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of such interventions on the sustained development of flexibility and related social-behavior skills?
