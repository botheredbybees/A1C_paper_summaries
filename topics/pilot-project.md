## Pilot Projects

The concept of a pilot project is crucial in the development and refinement of interventions. It serves as an initial phase where a new intervention or program can be tested on a small scale to assess its feasibility, acceptability, and preliminary efficacy before full-scale implementation (Key Concepts: pilot-project). Sources emphasize that formative evaluation during this stage provides critical insights for iterative refinement, helping practitioners understand the strengths and weaknesses of their approach without risking large-scale negative impacts. This process ensures that adjustments are made based on feedback and evidence to optimize outcomes prior to broader application (Key Concepts: iterative-refinement, formative-evaluation).

## Key Findings

- Pilot projects allow for initial testing of new interventions on a small scale.
- Formative evaluation during the pilot phase is essential for understanding intervention strengths and weaknesses.
- Iterative refinement based on feedback from the pilot project optimizes outcomes before full-scale implementation.
