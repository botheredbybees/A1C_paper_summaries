## Cognitive Enhancement Through Creative and Physical Activities

The integration of various creative and physical activities, such as singing in music therapy, sculpture creation, art therapy, and ballet-based dancing, shows promising results for enhancing cognitive functions in individuals with neurodegenerative diseases. Singing is highlighted for boosting brain activity among Alzheimer's patients by reversing cognitive decline and increasing life satisfaction. Sculpture creation empowers people suffering from dementia through self-expression, social interaction, and tapping into their creative potential to improve their quality of life. Art therapy similarly utilizes creativity as a means to enhance well-being and nurture the heart in individuals with dementia. Furthermore, ballet-based group dancing has been found effective for those with relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis (RRMS), indicating that physical activity can also play a critical role in cognitive enhancement beyond mere physical benefits. These interventions not only address cognitive decline but also emphasize the importance of social interaction and emotional well-being.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities like singing, sculpture creation, and art therapy significantly enhance cognitive functions and quality of life for individuals with neurodegenerative diseases.
- Physical activity in the form of ballet-based dance interventions positively impacts cognitive abilities, particularly among those suffering from RRMS.
- Social interaction and emotional well-being are crucial components of effective cognitive enhancement interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can the positive effects of creative activities on cognitive functions be optimized for broader application across various neurodegenerative diseases?
