## Radial Diffusivity in White Matter

The study of white matter diffusion measures, particularly radial diffusivity (RD) and fractional anisotropy (FA), has revealed nuanced age-related differences in the brain's structural integrity. RD is increasingly recognized as a critical measure for assessing myelin integrity and axonal damage. Studies indicate that while FA may be used to gauge overall white matter tract coherence, RD specifically captures changes related to demyelination or microstructural alterations within axons. These measures are pivotal in understanding neurodevelopmental disorders such as autism spectrum condition (ASC), where RD might serve as an indicator of altered myelin properties and neural connectivity.

## Key Findings

- Radial diffusivity is a crucial measure for assessing myelin integrity and axonal damage in the brain's white matter
- Fractional anisotropy, while useful for overall tract coherence, does not fully capture changes related to demyelination or microstructural alterations
- In neurodevelopmental disorders like autism spectrum condition, RD may provide insights into altered neural connectivity and myelin properties
