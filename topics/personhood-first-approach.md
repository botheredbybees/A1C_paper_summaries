## Personhood-First Approach

The concept of a 'personhood-first' approach is emphasized across the sources as an innovative intervention for individuals with Alzheimer's disease and other forms of dementia. This approach centers on recognizing and valuing each person's unique identity, history, and desires despite cognitive impairments, fostering dignity and meaningful engagement in activities that resonate personally. A key example discussed is the ARTZ Artists for Alzheimer's program, which integrates art therapy into museum settings to enhance self-expression and social interaction among patients while promoting dementia awareness within communities. Despite positive outcomes reported from such initiatives, challenges remain regarding broader implementation due to logistical barriers like resource allocation and specialized training requirements.

## Key Findings

- A personhood-first approach recognizes individual identity beyond cognitive decline in dementia care.
- Art therapy programs tailored for people with Alzheimer's can enhance self-expression and social engagement.
- Integrating such interventions into community settings, like museums, promotes public understanding of dementia.
