## Self-Actualization

The concept of self-actualization is explored primarily within the framework of Person-Centered Therapy (PCT), also known as Rogerian therapy. This approach emphasizes empathy, unconditional positive regard, and the inherent drive towards personal growth and fulfillment. Carl Rogers, a pioneering figure in PCT, posited that individuals possess an innate tendency to develop into self-actualized beings when provided with nurturing conditions such as empathetic listening and acceptance without judgment. The theory underscores the importance of a therapeutic environment where clients can explore their feelings and thoughts freely, fostering self-discovery and leading towards increased self-understanding and personal development.

## Key Findings

- Self-actualization is central to Person-Centered Therapy (PCT).
- Empathy and unconditional positive regard are critical components of PCT.
- The inherent drive for personal growth is a fundamental aspect of human nature according to Rogers.
