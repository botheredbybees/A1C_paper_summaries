## Ballet-Based Group Dance Intervention

The analysis of the feasibility and preliminary effects of a ballet-based group dance intervention in individuals with relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis (RRMS) indicates positive outcomes. The study underscores the potential benefits of such an intervention for enhancing physical capabilities and promoting mental health. However, there is a need to further explore long-term impacts and the generalizability across different patient populations. This research sets a foundation for future studies that could refine and scale up ballet-based group dance as a therapeutic intervention.

## Key Findings

- Ballet-based group dance interventions show potential benefits for individuals with RRMS.
- The intervention is feasible to implement in clinical settings.
- Positive preliminary effects on physical capabilities and mental health were observed.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of ballet-based group dance interventions?
- How can this intervention be adapted for other neurological conditions?
