## Arts Therapies in Mental Health Interventions

The integration of arts-based therapeutic practices into mental health interventions shows promising outcomes, particularly for populations dealing with eating disorders and emotional wellbeing challenges. Source One highlights the role of arts therapies as a valuable intervention for treating eating disorders through various modalities such as music therapy and visual art creation. The study emphasizes the need to explore specific types of arts therapies that yield optimal therapeutic results for this patient group. Conversely, Source Two focuses on broader societal benefits of arts education and self-expression in fostering emotional well-being among young individuals. It introduces the Labyrinth Project, a model demonstrating how the arts can promote social inclusion and personal development. While both sources acknowledge the therapeutic value of artistic expression, they differ in scope—Source One examines specific clinical outcomes for eating disorders, whereas Source Two addresses more general mental health benefits through community-based art programs.

## Key Findings

- Arts therapies are effective interventions for treating eating disorders.
- Self-expression and creativity contribute to emotional wellbeing and social inclusion.
- The Labyrinth Project exemplifies the integration of arts in promoting youth development.
