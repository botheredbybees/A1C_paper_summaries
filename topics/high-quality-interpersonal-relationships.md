## High-Quality Interpersonal Relationships

The sources emphasize the importance of high-quality interpersonal relationships in enhancing care for individuals with neurological impairments like dementia. Person-centered care emerges as a critical approach, emphasizing the maintenance of selfhood and addressing both psychosocial and physical needs to foster better relationships between caregivers and patients. This method underscores the necessity of understanding each individual's unique experiences and preferences to provide effective support that respects their dignity and autonomy.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered care significantly enhances high-quality interpersonal relationships in dementia care.
- Maintaining selfhood is crucial for improving psychosocial well-being in individuals with neurological impairments.
- Addressing both physical and psychological needs concurrently through person-centered approaches yields better outcomes.
