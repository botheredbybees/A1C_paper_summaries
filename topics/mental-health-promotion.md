## Puppetry in Mental Health Promotion

The integration of puppetry within mental health promotion emerges as a versatile intervention across various contexts. Both sources highlight the use of puppets in programs aimed at improving emotional well-being, particularly among immigrant and refugee children (Source 1). Puppets facilitate expression and exploration of emotions in a non-threatening manner, enhancing coping mechanisms and emotional regulation skills. The scoping review by Source 2 further corroborates these benefits, emphasizing puppetry's role in managing anxiety and fostering social interaction. However, while both sources underscore the positive impact on mental health through creative outlets like storytelling and play, there is a need for more empirical evidence to substantiate these qualitative observations.

## Key Findings

- Puppetry enhances emotional well-being among children facing cultural transition or trauma
- Creative expression through puppetry improves coping mechanisms in diverse settings
- Social interaction skills can be positively influenced by engaging with puppets

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of incorporating puppetry into mental health programs?
