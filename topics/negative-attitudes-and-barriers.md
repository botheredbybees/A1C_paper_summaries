## Negative Attitudes and Barriers in Arts Participation

The research overview on arts and disability highlights significant underrepresentation of artists with disabilities and recreational participation barriers. A recurring theme is the existence of negative attitudes that hinder engagement and opportunities for individuals with disabilities within the arts community. These barriers include societal stigma, lack of accessible resources, and inadequate support systems. Despite these challenges, efforts to increase awareness and promote inclusion are ongoing but face significant obstacles.

## Key Findings

- Negative attitudes towards disability in the arts lead to underrepresentation of artists with disabilities.
- Recreational participation in the arts is limited by systemic barriers and lack of access.
- Efforts to overcome these challenges include advocacy for inclusive policies and education on disability awareness.

## Open Questions

- How can negative attitudes be effectively addressed to promote greater inclusivity in the arts?
