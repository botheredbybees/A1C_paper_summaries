## Empathy Mirroring in Expressive Arts Therapy

The use of empathy mirroring as a therapeutic intervention within the context of expressive arts therapies for individuals on the autism spectrum shows promising results. This technique, which involves therapists and clients engaging in mutual, reflective emotional and behavioral exchanges, is particularly evident in tele-dance movement psychotherapy studies. Sources highlight that empathy mirroring helps to foster understanding and connection between therapist and client, enhancing therapeutic outcomes. However, there are differing viewpoints on the extent to which this approach can be universally applied across diverse cultural contexts due to varying expressions of emotion and interaction styles among individuals with autism.

## Key Findings

- Empathy mirroring in expressive arts therapy is beneficial for individuals on the autism spectrum.
- Tele-dance movement psychotherapy incorporates empathy mirroring techniques effectively.
- The use of empathy mirroring can enhance therapeutic outcomes through improved connection.

## Open Questions

- How does cultural context influence the effectiveness and application of empathy mirroring in expressive arts therapy?
