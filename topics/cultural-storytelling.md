## Cultural Storytelling Through Puppetry

The exploration of cultural storytelling through puppetry reveals a multifaceted intervention that leverages the therapeutic potential of creative play and narrative expression. Source 1 discusses the use of puppetry to address vicarious trauma, emphasizing non-verbal communication as a key element in its effectiveness. This aligns with the therapeutic benefits associated with creative play noted in the same source. In contrast, Source 2 provides practical examples through shadow theatre and marionette performance, illustrating how such techniques can be employed for self-care and storytelling. While both sources highlight the versatility of puppetry as a medium for expression and healing, they diverge slightly in their focus: one on trauma and non-verbal communication, the other on specific forms like shadow and marionette theatre. Despite these differences, both underscore the cultural significance and therapeutic value of puppetry across various contexts.

## Key Findings

- Puppetry is a versatile medium for creative play with significant therapeutic benefits.
- Non-verbal communication in puppetry plays an essential role in addressing emotional and psychological needs, particularly related to vicarious trauma.
- Shadow theatre and marionette performance are specific forms of cultural storytelling through puppetry that can serve multiple purposes including self-care.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultural contexts influence the therapeutic applications and effectiveness of puppetry in addressing emotional well-being?
