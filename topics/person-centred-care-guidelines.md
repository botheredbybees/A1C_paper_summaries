## Person-Centred Care Guidelines

The sources collectively emphasize the role of person-centered care guidelines in enhancing dementia care through creative arts therapies. Theatre of the Oppressed techniques and Veder method are highlighted as effective approaches that align with these guidelines, fostering a therapeutic environment where patients can express themselves freely and improve their quality of life. These methods underscore the importance of treating each patient individually, considering their unique needs and preferences. However, there is a need for more detailed guidance on how to implement these guidelines effectively in various cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Theatre of the Oppressed techniques and Veder method align with person-centered care principles.
- Creative arts therapies enhance expression and quality of life in dementia patients.
- Guidelines are necessary but require practical implementation strategies.

## Open Questions

- How can person-centered care guidelines be adapted for diverse cultural settings?
