## Public Health Waves

The concept of 'public health waves' is presented as a framework to understand the evolution and response mechanisms within public health over time. Each wave represents an era marked by unique challenges, solutions, and methodologies tailored to address emergent societal problems. The fifth wave emphasizes the integration of culture and arts into traditional public health practices, highlighting their roles in enhancing community resilience and engagement. While earlier waves focused on sanitation, epidemiology, social reform, and preventive medicine respectively, the current wave underscores the importance of creativity and cultural elements for addressing complex contemporary issues such as mental health, social isolation, and environmental degradation.

## Key Findings

- Public health practices have evolved through distinct 'waves' corresponding to societal challenges.
- The fifth wave emphasizes the integration of arts and culture into public health strategies.
- Each wave builds on previous ones but introduces novel approaches and focuses

## Open Questions

- How can traditional public health stakeholders effectively collaborate with cultural institutions and artists?
