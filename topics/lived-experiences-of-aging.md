## Lived Experiences Of Aging

The topic of 'lived experiences of aging' encompasses the subjective and personal understanding of aging through various methodologies, with an emphasis on arts-based research. Sources emphasize how creative arts interventions like photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry offer unique perspectives into the lived realities of older adults. These methods are grounded in hermeneutic phenomenology, which aims to uncover deeper meanings within individual experiences. The approach highlights aging as a dynamic process characterized by both challenges and opportunities for personal growth. There is recognition that arts-based research can provide a richer understanding of the complexities of aging beyond traditional quantitative measures.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based methods such as photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry are valuable tools in exploring lived experiences of aging
- Hermeneutic phenomenology underpins these creative arts interventions by focusing on the interpretation and meaning-making process within individual narratives
- Aging is portrayed not only through challenges but also opportunities for personal development

## Open Questions

- What are the limitations or biases inherent in using artistic methods to capture the diverse experiences of aging?
