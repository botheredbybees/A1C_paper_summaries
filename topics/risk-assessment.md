## Risk Assessment in Health Care

This synthesis examines the methodological approaches to assessing risks within health care contexts. Source 1 focuses on measuring care risk specifically for older adults with dementia, emphasizing patient-centered assessment and longitudinal validation of care risk dimensions. This highlights the importance of context-specific assessments tailored to the needs of particular patient populations. In contrast, Source 2 introduces the ROB-ME tool designed to assess the risk of bias due to missing evidence in systematic reviews, underscoring the broader applicability of such tools across various health studies and the necessity of addressing selective non-publication issues. While both sources discuss methods for assessing risks, they differ in their scope and application: one is patient-specific while the other is methodological for evaluating research studies.

## Key Findings

- Patient-centered risk assessment is crucial for older adults with dementia.
- Longitudinal validation of care risk dimensions enhances reliability.
- Tools like ROB-ME are essential in assessing biases due to missing evidence.

## Open Questions

- How can patient-specific and methodological approaches to risk assessment be integrated effectively?
