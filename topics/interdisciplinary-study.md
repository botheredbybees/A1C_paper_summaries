## Interdisciplinary Study in Healthcare and Aging

The concept of interdisciplinary study emerges as a critical framework within healthcare and aging research, encapsulating diverse theoretical approaches such as participatory action research and the health humanities. Source 1 delves into understanding dementia through the lens of aging paradigms, advocating for participatory methodologies that involve patients directly in their care planning. Meanwhile, Sources 2 and 3 emphasize the integration of arts and healthcare within narrative medicine and creative expression, suggesting a broader framework where different disciplines collaborate to enhance patient experiences and outcomes. These sources collectively indicate the need for interdisciplinary collaboration to address complex issues like dementia, highlighting the benefits of combining medical expertise with artistic practices.

## Key Findings

- Participatory action research is crucial in aging healthcare to involve patients directly in decision-making processes.
- The health humanities and narrative medicine offer valuable frameworks for integrating arts into healthcare practice.
- Creative expression and interdisciplinary collaboration can enhance the quality of care and patient outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can participatory methodologies be effectively integrated with creative practices to improve dementia care?
