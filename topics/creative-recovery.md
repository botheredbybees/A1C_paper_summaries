## Creative Recovery

The concept of creative recovery emphasizes the role of arts and cultural activities in supporting individuals and communities following traumatic events or disasters. The National Taskforce for Creative Recovery Recommendations highlights how creative practices can facilitate resilience building, inclusive community engagement, and trauma processing through art. This approach underscores a shift towards integrating creativity as a tool for healing and rebuilding after crises. Afloat: Children and Families Community Resilience Art Project exemplifies the practical application of creative recovery principles by fostering community resilience through artistic expression and collaboration among children and families affected by disasters. Both sources converge on the importance of harnessing creativity to foster psychological and social well-being, suggesting a broader acceptance and implementation of creative interventions in disaster management strategies.

## Key Findings

- Creative practices play a crucial role in building community resilience post-disaster.
- Trauma processing through arts is an effective method for recovery.
- Inclusive engagement with diverse groups enhances the effectiveness of creative recovery initiatives.
