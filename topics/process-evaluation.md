## Process Evaluation in Arts-Based Interventions

The provided sources emphasize the importance of process evaluation in assessing and refining arts-based interventions for various populations, particularly those with dementia. Source 1 highlights the implementation of 'Living Room Theatre' activities within nursing homes using a person-centered approach, emphasizing executive support and the Veder Method as integral components. This source underscores that effective intervention execution relies not only on theoretical frameworks but also on supportive administrative structures and practical methodologies. In contrast, Source 2 focuses more broadly on process evaluations in arts-in-health contexts, advocating for ethnographic methods to gather detailed insights into how interventions are received and utilized by participants. It stresses the need for comprehensive outcome assessments that capture both qualitative and quantitative data to fully understand intervention efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Process evaluation is crucial for understanding the implementation of arts-based interventions in healthcare settings, particularly for populations like those with dementia.
- Supportive administrative structures are necessary for the successful execution of person-centered care initiatives, such as Living Room Theatre activities.
- Ethnographic methods offer valuable insights into how arts-based interventions are received and utilized by participants.

## Open Questions

- How can process evaluations better integrate qualitative data to enhance our understanding of intervention effectiveness in diverse healthcare settings?
