## Consensual Qualitative Research

The sources collectively underscore the utility and versatility of consensual qualitative research (CQR) as a methodological framework for capturing rich narrative data within various humanistic contexts. The training of trainers model highlighted in one source illustrates CQR's application in educating mental health professionals on utilizing arts-based therapeutic techniques, particularly following traumatic events like natural disasters. This approach emphasizes the importance of creative processes in trauma recovery and underlines how CQR facilitates this by offering a structured yet flexible framework for analyzing qualitative data.

## Key Findings

- CQR is an effective method for capturing nuanced narrative data from individuals affected by trauma
- Creative processes play a crucial role in psychosocial support and therapeutic interventions post-disaster
- The Training of Trainers model, when coupled with CQR, enhances the dissemination and efficacy of arts-based therapies

## Open Questions

- How can the Training of Trainers model be adapted to incorporate digital or remote learning for broader accessibility?
