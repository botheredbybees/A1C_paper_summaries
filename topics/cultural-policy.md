## Cultural Policy

The documents emphasize the importance of managing conflicts of interest and maintaining artistic integrity within the context of cultural policy. The 'Peer Handbook for Assessment Panels' provides guidelines on ethical practices, such as a code of conduct, to ensure fair evaluations of applications or projects in the cultural sector. Meanwhile, the 'Revive: A Place for Every Story, A Story for Every Place' document outlines Australia's strategic approach towards fostering creativity and innovation within its cultural policy framework over the next five years. It prioritizes the integration of First Nations stories into national narratives while also addressing gaps in infrastructure that support creative industries.

## Key Findings

- Cultural policies are designed to uphold ethical standards through conflict-of-interest management and a commitment to artistic integrity
- There is an emphasis on integrating First Nations stories within broader cultural narratives
- Creative economy initiatives aim at enhancing the environment for artists and creators

## Open Questions

- How can cultural policy effectively balance ethical practices with the promotion of diverse voices in creative industries?
