## Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (Ptsd)

Both sources highlight the use of arts therapy as an intervention for individuals suffering from Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD). Source 1 focuses on integrating arts-based activities into therapeutic settings to address PTSD alongside other mental health issues such as eating disorders. It emphasizes the importance of creative expression in healing and recovery processes. In contrast, Source 2 delves into trauma-informed design within arts therapies, underscoring the significance of creating a safe environment that acknowledges past traumas while promoting resilience and coping mechanisms against long-term health impacts resulting from traumatic events or disasters.

## Key Findings

- Arts therapy is recognized as an effective intervention for PTSD in therapeutic settings.
- Trauma-informed design plays a crucial role in enhancing the effectiveness of arts therapies by creating supportive environments that are sensitive to past traumas.
- Long-term health impacts from traumatic experiences can be mitigated through tailored interventions like those offered by trauma-informed arts therapy.

## Open Questions

- How can arts therapy and trauma-informed design be further integrated into broader mental healthcare systems to address PTSD more comprehensively?
