## Multidisciplinary Collaboration

The concept of multidisciplinary collaboration emerges as a critical element in various therapeutic and research contexts. In music therapy, professionals are encouraged to embrace collaborative roles that facilitate broader research advancements and identity formation within their field (Source 1). Helen Morse's work interpreting dementia through drama highlights the importance of resilience and humor while emphasizing the necessity for interdisciplinary teamwork in addressing complex human conditions (Source 2). Moreover, the challenge of broadening cultural definitions and rehumanizing migrants necessitates a multidisciplinary approach that integrates arts, culture, migration studies, and change (Source 3), indicating a universal need across diverse professional domains to collaborate effectively. This synthesis reveals an overarching theme of collaboration's importance in advancing therapeutic practices, cultural understanding, and resilience among various populations.

## Key Findings

- Multidisciplinary collaboration is crucial for the advancement of music therapy research and identity formation.
- Resilience and humor are essential components when interpreting complex conditions like dementia through interdisciplinary approaches.
- A multidisciplinary approach in studying migration can lead to a rehumanization of migrants, broadening cultural definitions.

## Open Questions

- How can different professional disciplines effectively collaborate to enhance therapeutic practices and cultural understanding without diluting the unique contributions of each field?
