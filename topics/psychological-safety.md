## Psychological Safety

The concept of psychological safety emerges as a crucial element in the therapeutic practice aimed at enhancing child welfare and emotional development. Sources indicate that psychological safety is vital for children to explore their feelings and thoughts through safe, creative mediums such as puppets. These tools externalize emotions, allowing children to express inner conflicts symbolically and non-verbally within a protected environment. The use of play symbolism in psychodynamic therapy highlights the importance of creating an atmosphere where vulnerability can be shared without fear of judgment or retribution, thereby fostering growth and healing.

## Key Findings

- Psychological safety is essential for children to engage openly with their emotions.
- Externalizing feelings through creative tools like puppets aids in non-verbal emotional expression.
- Play symbolism plays a key role in therapeutic interventions aimed at psychological safety.
