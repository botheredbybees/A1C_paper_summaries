## Social-Political Factors in Artistic Production

The synthesis integrates the concept of 'social-political factors' influencing artistic production and the intersection between arts and health. Across sources, there is an agreement on the critical importance of understanding how broader societal and political contexts shape the creation and reception of art in healthcare settings. However, tensions arise when considering the balance between the purity of artistic expression and its instrumentalization for health outcomes. The role of policy in mediating these factors emerges as a key area where further research is needed to ensure that cultural interventions remain meaningful while also contributing effectively to public health goals.

## Key Findings

- Social-political contexts significantly influence how art is produced, consumed, and valued in healthcare settings.
- There is an ongoing debate about the extent to which artistic integrity can be maintained when art is used as a tool for social or health benefits.
- Policy frameworks play a crucial role in shaping both the opportunities and constraints within which arts-based interventions operate.

## Open Questions

- How can policy better support the integration of arts into healthcare while preserving the creative autonomy of artists?
