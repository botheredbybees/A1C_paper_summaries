## Emotional Expression Through Drama

The integration of dramatic arts as an intervention for enhancing emotional expression and promoting well-being is a significant area of study. Source 1 highlights the use of dramatherapy in dementia care, emphasizing its role in person-centered care and cognitive function improvement. Dramatherapy, as an approach, facilitates emotional release through enactment, storytelling, and other theatrical techniques, helping individuals express feelings that might otherwise be suppressed due to illness or life circumstances. The benefits extend beyond emotional expression; it also aids in improving cognitive functions by stimulating memory recall and enhancing social interactions.

## Key Findings

- Dramatherapy is an effective intervention for promoting emotional expression among individuals with dementia, contributing positively to their mental health and well-being.
- Through person-centered care, dramatherapy allows for a tailored approach that respects the individual's unique experiences and needs.
- The use of dramatic arts in therapy significantly contributes to cognitive function improvement by engaging multiple brain functions.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of dramatherapy on emotional expression and cognitive health, especially when used as a part of ongoing care for individuals with chronic conditions such as dementia?
