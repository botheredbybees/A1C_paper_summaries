## Music Therapy in Dementia Care

The provided sources collectively underscore the role of music therapy as an intervention for promoting health and wellbeing. Neuroimaging studies reveal that music engages cortical regions related to motor synchronization, suggesting potential therapeutic benefits for neurological conditions such as dementia (Source 1). Source 2 highlights the application of music interventions in dementia care, noting the discourse surrounding dementia and its impact on patients' daily lives. These musical therapies not only alleviate symptoms but also enhance emotional engagement and cognitive activation through gene-environment interactions (Sources 8 and 2), which can be observed in immune system changes due to physiological responses to sound stimuli (Source 4). Ethical considerations, such as confidentiality and meaningful involvement of participants, are crucial when implementing these interventions (Source 3). Additionally, the design principles for inclusive projects in dementia care emphasize self-agency and collaborative engagement among patients and caregivers (Source 9), reinforcing the importance of culturally aware practices and risk analysis to ensure effective implementation of arts-based health initiatives (Sources 5 and 7).

## Key Findings

- Music therapy positively impacts neurological functions through cortical engagement and motor synchronization.
- Ethical considerations are critical in implementing music interventions for dementia care.
- Collaborative engagement between patients, caregivers, and healthcare providers enhances the effectiveness of music-based health initiatives.
