## Cerebral Cortex Dysregulation in Autism Spectrum Disorder

The cerebral cortex plays a pivotal role in complex cognitive functions and is the site of broad transcriptomic dysregulation observed in individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). Source 2 identifies significant gene expression abnormalities across various cortical regions, indicating that disruptions at the molecular level may underlie the diverse neurological symptoms seen in ASD. However, while this research highlights the importance of the cerebral cortex in ASD pathophysiology, it does not delve into specific neuronal structures like the hippocampus mentioned in Source 1, which is critical for memory and learning processes.

## Key Findings

- Autism spectrum disorder involves broad transcriptomic dysregulation across multiple regions of the cerebral cortex.
- Gene expression abnormalities play a crucial role in ASD pathophysiology.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neuronal structures involved in ASD and how do they interact with broader cortical dysregulation?
