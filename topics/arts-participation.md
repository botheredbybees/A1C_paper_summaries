## Arts Participation

The examination of arts participation reveals a multifaceted understanding across different communities and contexts. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of creative engagement for well-being, particularly noting the impact on First Nations individuals who actively participate in arts activities, highlighting the cultural and social significance of such engagement for this population's health and identity. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on broader demographic trends as observed through a national survey, which underscores various dimensions of arts participation including community-engagement initiatives and educational outcomes. Both sources highlight digital arts as an emerging domain with significant potential to enhance access and inclusivity in arts engagement. While there is agreement on the benefits of arts participation for personal well-being and social cohesion across diverse populations, tensions arise regarding equitable access and representation, particularly concerning underrepresented groups such as indigenous communities.

## Key Findings

- Arts participation contributes significantly to individual well-being and community cohesiveness.
- Digital platforms offer new avenues for increasing accessibility and inclusivity in arts engagement.
- First Nations individuals benefit uniquely from their cultural engagement through artistic practices.

## Open Questions

- How can digital arts initiatives better serve underrepresented populations while maintaining cultural integrity?
