## Participatory Arts-Based Research

The sources emphasize the potential of participatory arts-based research (PAR) as a method to investigate complex social issues such as migration and cultural change. PAR is highlighted for its capacity to foster engagement among diverse groups, including migrants and refugees, thereby providing rich qualitative data that traditional methods might overlook. While multidisciplinary approaches are encouraged for their ability to address the multifaceted nature of these topics, tensions arise regarding how best to integrate arts-based practices with rigorous academic methodologies without compromising either.

## Key Findings

- Participatory Arts-Based Research (PAR) is a valuable tool for engaging diverse populations in research related to migration and cultural change.
- Visual studies within PAR offer unique insights into the subjective experiences of individuals affected by social changes such as migration.
- Multidisciplinary approaches are necessary to fully capture the complexity of issues like migration and its impact on arts and culture.

## Open Questions

- How can participatory arts-based research maintain academic rigor while embracing creative and expressive methodologies?
