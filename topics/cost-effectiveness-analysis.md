## Cost-effectiveness Analysis of Arts and Creativity Interventions

The provided sources explore the cost-effectiveness analysis of arts and creativity interventions in health contexts. Source 1, a systematic literature review, focuses on older adults and evaluates economic aspects alongside health improvements from creative activities. It highlights challenges such as variable intervention costs and difficulty in quantifying long-term benefits. In contrast, Source 2 discusses broader implications of implementing arts-based social prescribing across diverse populations, emphasizing its potential to address health inequalities and align with sustainable development goals. Both sources converge on the need for robust cost-effectiveness data but diverge in their scope—Source 1 is narrower focusing solely on older adults while Source 2 extends to wider community benefits.

## Key Findings

- Arts and creativity interventions show promise for improving health outcomes, particularly among older adults.
- Cost-effectiveness analysis of these interventions faces challenges due to variability in implementation costs and difficulty quantifying long-term benefits.

## Open Questions

- How can the cost-effectiveness of arts-based social prescribing be better understood when applied across diverse populations beyond just older adults?
