## Spiritual Experience in Choir Singing

The study of choir singing as an intervention reveals a multifaceted impact on participants' mental health and social well-being. Sources highlight emotional regulation and spiritual experience as key benefits, with participants reporting a sense of unity and transcendence during performances that enhance their self-perception and group identity. However, the variability in individual spiritual experiences within the choir setting introduces complexity in measuring these effects universally.

## Key Findings

- Choir singing enhances emotional regulation
- Participation fosters a strong group identity
- Spiritual experience is a significant benefit

## Open Questions

- How do varying personal definitions of spirituality impact the effectiveness and reception of choir interventions?
