## Supporting Youth Mental Health Through Arts-Based Strategies

The integration of arts-and-culture-based interventions in supporting youth mental health is a global phenomenon that leverages local cultural practices to enhance the efficacy and relevance of these programs. Both sources emphasize the importance of elevating the voices of young people, enabling them to contribute meaningfully to their own care through creative activities such as music, visual art, and drama. This approach not only addresses traditional mental health disorders but also taps into core outcome measures that can be adapted across diverse cultural contexts. However, there are tensions between maintaining culturally specific practices and ensuring universal applicability of the interventions.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based strategies are effective in supporting youth mental health on a global scale
- Local cultural practices enhance the relevance and effectiveness of arts-and-culture-based interventions
- Elevating youth voice through creative participation is crucial for intervention success

## Open Questions

- How to balance culturally specific approaches with universal applicability in arts-based mental health interventions?
