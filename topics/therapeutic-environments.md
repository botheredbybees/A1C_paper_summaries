## Therapeutic Environments

The concept of therapeutic environments is explored through the lens of two distinct yet complementary perspectives. Source 1 discusses public baths as a historical architectural and mental health intervention, emphasizing the integration of natural elements and personal memories within these spaces to foster well-being. In contrast, Source 2 delves into modern aesthetic principles that underpin innovative designs for health facilities, focusing on sensory experiences and neuroaesthetic theories to create environments conducive to recovery and wellness. Both sources highlight how physical spaces can be designed or utilized in a way that positively impacts mental health by engaging with natural elements and creating meaningful sensory interactions. However, there is a noticeable gap in the discourse regarding how historical practices might inform contemporary design principles to enhance therapeutic effects.

## Key Findings

- Natural elements and personal connections to place significantly influence mental well-being
- Sensory experiences and aesthetic environments are critical for health recovery
- Historical and modern approaches both leverage architecture as a tool for therapy

## Open Questions

- How can historical therapeutic practices be integrated into contemporary design principles?
