## Historical Medical Texts

The historical context of medical texts is pivotal for understanding the evolution of medical theories and practices. Source 1 discusses Hippocratic ethics as interpreted by Galen, emphasizing the ethical relationship between doctors and patients. This interpretation underpins a significant portion of medieval and early modern medical practice. Meanwhile, Source 2 highlights how Galen's influence extended beyond theoretical ethics into practical areas such as dissection studies and the development of medical illustrations. Lithography, for instance, became an essential tool in conveying anatomical knowledge derived from Galenic traditions. Despite their divergent focuses—one on ethical theory and patient care, the other on visual representation of anatomy—both sources underscore the enduring impact of ancient Greek and Roman medical thought on later periods.

## Key Findings

- Galen's interpretations of Hippocratic ethics played a crucial role in shaping medieval and early modern medical practice.
- Lithography facilitated the spread of anatomical knowledge derived from Galenic dissection studies.
- Both ethical theory and visual representation were influenced by ancient Greek and Roman medical thought.

## Open Questions

- How did the integration of Hippocratic ethics with Galenic practices affect the development of modern medicine?
