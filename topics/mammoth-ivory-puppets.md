## Mammoth Ivory Puppets

The exploration of mammoth ivory puppets as a form of puppetry highlights the historical and cultural significance of these objects. Mammoth ivory, sourced from extinct woolly mammoths, provides an intriguing material for creating durable and detailed puppets. This unique medium is discussed in relation to its use in various forms of puppetry, including Bunraku theatre, where intricately carved wooden puppets are a hallmark. The synthesis across sources reveals the rich history of puppetry and the diverse materials used over time. Mammoth ivory puppets stand out due to their rare material and historical context, offering an intersection between archaeology, craftsmanship, and theatrical performance.

## Key Findings

- Mammoth ivory is used in creating detailed and long-lasting puppets.
- Bunraku theatre utilizes intricately carved wooden puppets.
- Puppets made of mammoth ivory hold both cultural and historical significance.
