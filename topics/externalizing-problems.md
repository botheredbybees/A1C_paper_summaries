## Creative Arts-Based Interventions for Externalizing Problems

The systematic review and meta-analysis highlight the effectiveness of creative arts-based interventions in addressing various issues among children and adolescents exposed to traumatic events, including post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), negative mood, externalizing problems, and anxiety levels. Across multiple studies, these interventions are shown to provide significant benefits by reducing symptoms such as aggression, hyperactivity, conduct problems, and emotional dysregulation often categorized under externalizing behaviors. However, the review also notes a variability in intervention types, modalities, and durations that complicates drawing definitive conclusions about specific efficacy profiles of individual arts-based methods. There is agreement on the potential therapeutic benefits but an acknowledged need for further research to delineate optimal dosing and mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts-based interventions can effectively reduce externalizing problems in children and adolescents who have experienced trauma.
- Interventions vary widely in their approach, which complicates direct comparisons of efficacy between different methods.
- There is a consensus on the beneficial impact of these interventions despite methodological differences.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal durations and frequencies for creative arts-based interventions to maximize benefits for externalizing problems?
