## Motor Synchronization in Music Therapy

The sources collectively highlight the role of motor synchronization in music therapy and its implications for health promotion. Neuroimaging studies reveal enhanced cortical engagement during musical activities, supporting the therapeutic benefits of synchronizing movements to music. This synchronization enhances neural connectivity and promotes neuroplasticity, contributing to improvements in motor function and cognitive processes. However, there is a noted variability in how different populations respond to motor-synchronization interventions, indicating the need for tailored approaches.

## Key Findings

- Motor synchronization in musical activities leads to increased cortical engagement as observed through neuroimaging techniques.
- Enhanced neural connectivity and neuroplasticity are linked with motor synchronization, contributing positively to cognitive and motor function improvements.
- Responses to motor-synchronization interventions vary across different populations.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms behind individual differences in response to motor-synchronization interventions?
