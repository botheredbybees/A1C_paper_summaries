## Adaptive Behavior in Children with Autism

The research on play therapy for children with autism highlights its role in fostering adaptive behaviors and improving communication skills. Adaptive behavior encompasses various areas such as self-help, social interaction, and functional living skills. Play therapy is noted to enhance these aspects through activities tailored to the child's developmental stage. Sensory integration therapy complements this by addressing sensory processing issues that may impede engagement in play-based interventions. Despite its benefits, there are limitations regarding how effectively these therapies cater to individual differences among autistic children, and more research is needed to optimize therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Play therapy can enhance adaptive behaviors in children with autism.
- Sensory integration therapy supports the effectiveness of play-based interventions by addressing sensory needs.
- Improvements in expressive communication are linked to better engagement in play therapies.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences among autistic children influence the efficacy of play and sensory integration therapies?
