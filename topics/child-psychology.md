## Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy for Children

Puppet-assisted play therapy emerges as a versatile intervention in child psychology, integrating metaphorical conflict resolution, character development, and emotional safety. Sources highlight the use of puppets to externalize feelings, manage vicarious trauma, and foster psychological safety for children with diverse needs including Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and those exposed to domestic violence. The technique supports divergent thinking and self-regulation, while also aiding in psychoeducation and addressing symptoms associated with trauma. However, the effectiveness of puppet-based interventions varies based on cultural sensitivity and the child's developmental stage, emphasizing the need for tailored approaches.

## Key Findings

- Puppet-assisted play therapy promotes emotional safety and psychological well-being in children.
- Externalization of feelings through puppets is beneficial for trauma-related symptom management in middle childhood.
- Character development and metaphorical conflict resolution are integral components enhancing therapeutic engagement.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal conditions under which puppet-assisted play therapy should be applied to ensure cultural sensitivity and effectiveness across diverse populations?
