## Photojournalism in Therapeutic Applications

The synthesis integrates themes from photojournalism's narrative styles and its use as a therapeutic intervention. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of both linear and non-linear narratives in visual storytelling, highlighting how different structures can effectively convey messages about complex issues such as postnatal depression, as seen in Source 2. The therapeutic benefits of photography are highlighted through the practice of mindfulness and coping mechanisms for those experiencing mental health challenges. Both sources suggest that photojournalism is not only a form of communication but also an intervention that offers individuals a way to explore their experiences deeply.

## Key Findings

- Photojournalism can be used as a therapeutic tool to help individuals cope with mental health issues such as postnatal depression.
- Both linear and non-linear narrative structures are effective in visual storytelling, each providing unique insights into the complexities of human experience.
- Engaging in photojournalistic practices like mindfulness through photography can offer coping mechanisms for those dealing with stress or trauma.
