## Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging

The application of Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) is explored through its ability to reveal the neural mechanisms underpinning expertise and information processing. Source 1 uses fMRI to distinguish brain activities between skilled and novice artists, highlighting specific areas involved in visual information processing that differ based on experience level. The findings underscore the methodological potential of fMRI in elucidating the neurological basis of expertise but also pose questions about the specificity and generalizability of such neural signatures.

## Key Findings

- fMRI can differentiate brain activities between skilled and novice individuals
- Visual information processing shows different neural patterns based on expertise level

## Open Questions

- How specific are fMRI findings to particular domains or tasks?
