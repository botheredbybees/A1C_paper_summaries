## Trait Empathy and Music

The relationship between music and empathy is a multifaceted topic with implications for both psychological theory and neurophysiological processes. Source 1 discusses how individuals high in trait empathy are more likely to experience deep emotional responses, including being moved by unfamiliar sad music. This suggests that empathy may influence the way people perceive and engage emotionally with musical stimuli. Source 2 delves deeper into this relationship, identifying specific neural mechanisms such as sensorimotor activation during music listening among those with high trait empathy. Both sources highlight a significant correlation between levels of trait empathy and emotional responses to music, indicating that individuals who are naturally more empathetic tend to have stronger emotional reactions when exposed to musical pieces evoking sadness or other strong emotions.

## Key Findings

- Individuals high in trait empathy experience heightened emotional responses to unfamiliar sad music
- Sensorimotor activation is correlated with higher levels of trait empathy during music listening
- Trait empathy influences the way people perceive and emotionally engage with musical stimuli

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and contextual factors affect the relationship between trait empathy and emotional response to music?
