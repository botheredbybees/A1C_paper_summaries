## Roger Sperry's Split-Brain Research

Roger Sperry’s seminal work on split-brain patients fundamentally altered our understanding of brain lateralization. His findings suggested that the left hemisphere is predominantly responsible for language and logical reasoning, while the right hemisphere manages spatial abilities and emotional expression. However, subsequent research using advanced neuroimaging techniques such as functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) has revealed a more nuanced picture where both hemispheres contribute to various cognitive functions in an interdependent manner. This shift underscores the complexity of brain function and challenges earlier notions of strict lateralization.

## Key Findings

- Roger Sperry's split-brain research provided foundational insights into hemispheric specialization, though it has been critiqued for overemphasizing compartmentalized functions.
- Subsequent neuroimaging studies have revealed the interdependence and interconnectedness of brain hemispheres in processing complex tasks.
- The myth of left-brained or right-brained dominance persists in popular culture despite scientific evidence challenging this notion.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural and educational practices influence individual differences in cognitive processes related to hemispheric function?
