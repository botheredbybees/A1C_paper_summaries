## Research Ethics in Arts in Health Interventions

The integration of research ethics into arts in health interventions highlights the necessity of addressing several critical areas: informed consent, professional conduct principles, and ethical data handling. Across sources, there is a strong emphasis on ensuring participants' anonymity and transparency in reporting practices, which are essential for maintaining trust and credibility in the field. The Vancouver Protocol emerges as a key framework for authorship responsibilities, reinforcing the importance of ethical conduct among researchers. However, while sources stress the need for informed consent and data protection, they also reveal gaps in how these principles are applied in diverse cultural contexts, posing challenges to their uniform implementation.

## Key Findings

- The informed consent process is crucial for establishing trust between participants and researchers in arts in health interventions.
- Anonymity of participants must be preserved through various methods to ensure ethical standards are met during data collection and dissemination.
- Adherence to the Vancouver Protocol provides a clear guideline for authorship responsibilities, contributing to transparent research practices.

## Open Questions

- How can research ethics in arts in health interventions be adapted to respect cultural differences while maintaining global standards?
