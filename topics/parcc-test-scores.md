## Impact of Extracurricular Activities on Academic Performance

Research indicates that participation in extracurricular activities positively influences academic performance, as measured by PARCC test scores. However, the benefits are not uniformly distributed across demographic groups; disparities exist particularly for students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. Studies suggest that while extracurricular involvement can enhance attendance and engagement, these outcomes may be less pronounced or even negative for students eligible for free and reduced lunch programs.

## Key Findings

- Extracurricular activities positively impact PARCC test scores.
- Participation in extracurriculars correlates with improved academic attendance.
- Demographic disparities exist in the benefits of extracurricular involvement, especially for economically disadvantaged students.

## Open Questions

- How can schools ensure that all demographic groups benefit equitably from extracurricular participation?
