## Laughter-Care Framework

The Laughter-Care framework is an intervention that employs the use of humor and joy to improve patient outcomes, particularly through the Clown Doctors Program in Australia. This strategy aims to incorporate laughter into medical treatment as a means to reduce stress and anxiety among patients. The theory-of-change underlying this approach posits that by engaging with clowns and promoting laughter, individuals can experience physiological benefits such as reduced cortisol levels and enhanced immune response, thereby improving their overall health outcomes. Sources agree on the potential psychological and physical benefits of laughter, yet there is a gap in understanding its long-term effects compared to traditional care.

## Key Findings

- The Laughter-Care framework utilizes humor as an intervention for enhancing patient well-being.
- Clown doctors engage patients through comedy to reduce anxiety and improve mood.
- Physiological benefits of laughter include reduced stress hormones.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of incorporating laughter care into standard medical treatment?
