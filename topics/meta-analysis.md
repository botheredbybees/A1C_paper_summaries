## Effectiveness of Therapeutic Interventions in Specific Populations

The meta-analyses explore the effectiveness of dance and music interventions for falls prevention in older adults and autism spectrum disorder (ASD) in children, respectively. Source 1 examines how structured dance programs reduce fall risk through enhancing functional abilities, while Sources 2 and 3 delve into the efficacy of music therapy for ASD. All three sources highlight the need for rigorous evaluation methods such as randomized controlled trials to establish intervention effectiveness. They agree on the importance of measuring outcomes like social behavior, symptom severity, and physical function. However, there is a divergence in focus: Source 1 emphasizes functional measures related to falls prevention, whereas Sources 2 and 3 consider biological mechanisms and neural connectivity underlying improved ASD symptoms through music therapy.

## Key Findings

- Structured dance interventions can significantly reduce fall risk in older adults.
- Music therapy shows promise in improving social behavior and symptom severity in children with autism spectrum disorder.
- Randomized controlled trials are crucial for evaluating the efficacy of therapeutic interventions across different populations.

## Open Questions

- How do the underlying biological mechanisms involved in music therapy for ASD compare to those potentially affecting fall risk reduction through dance intervention?
