## Experience Sampling Method

The experience sampling method (ESM) is a powerful tool for capturing real-time data on individuals' experiences and mental states in their natural environments. Both studies utilize ESM to explore different aspects of subjective experience—music-induced involuntary imagery and emotional reactions to music, respectively. One study employs Bayesian network modeling alongside ESM to investigate how environmental and mental conditions influence the occurrence of involuntary musical imagery (mind-wandering involving music). The other study focuses on understanding the complexity of emotional responses to music by considering both listener characteristics and situational contexts. While these studies highlight the versatility of ESM in capturing dynamic, real-world phenomena, they also underscore the method's sensitivity to various influencing factors.

## Key Findings

- Experience sampling method (ESM) is effective for studying subjective experiences under naturalistic conditions.
- Environmental and mental states significantly impact involuntary musical imagery according to one study employing Bayesian network modeling.
- Emotional reactions to music are influenced by both listener characteristics and contextual elements, indicating the complexity of such reactions.

## Open Questions

- How can ESM be further optimized to better integrate multiple influencing factors (e.g., environmental conditions, mental states) in studies examining involuntary musical imagery or emotional responses?
