## Emotional Sensitivity

The concept of emotional sensitivity is examined through the lens of media use and brain development during adolescence. The sources highlight how increased exposure to digital media can impact adolescents' cognitive control and reflective processing abilities, which in turn influence their emotional sensitivity. There is a consensus that excessive media engagement might exacerbate emotional sensitivity by reducing the ability for reflective processing, thereby affecting an adolescent's emotional regulation capabilities. However, there is a tension around the specific mechanisms through which media use influences these developmental stages, with varying theoretical frameworks offering differing explanations.

## Key Findings

- Excessive digital media use during adolescence can lead to heightened emotional sensitivity due to impaired cognitive control and reflective processing.
- There are divergent theories on how exactly media consumption impacts brain development related to emotional regulation.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neural pathways through which media exposure affects adolescents' emotional sensitivity?
