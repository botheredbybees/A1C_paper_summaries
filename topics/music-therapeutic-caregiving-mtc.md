## Music Therapeutic Caregiving

The integration of singing by caregivers into daily care routines for individuals with dementia, a practice known as music therapeutic caregiving (MTC), has been explored in various studies. These investigations highlight the potential of MTC to influence emotional expressions and reduce resistance during morning care situations. Studies suggest that when caregivers incorporate singing into their interactions, they can evoke positive emotions such as joy and relaxation in the individuals with dementia, thereby enhancing the quality of care provided. However, there are nuances where some patients exhibit mixed responses or no change despite the caregiver's efforts to engage through music, indicating a need for further investigation into individual preferences and the optimal use of MTC.

## Key Findings

- Music therapeutic caregiving can reduce resistance in morning care situations.
- Incorporating singing by caregivers evokes positive emotions in individuals with dementia.
- Responses to MTC vary among patients, suggesting personal preference impacts effectiveness.

## Open Questions
