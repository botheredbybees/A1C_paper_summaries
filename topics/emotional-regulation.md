## Emotional Regulation

The synthesis integrates various aspects of emotional regulation across diverse contexts and methodologies. Sources highlight the critical role of emotional intelligence in therapeutic interventions for children (Source 3), emphasizing the importance of play therapy and symbolic communication in fostering self-regulation skills. Additionally, sources reveal how humor can serve as a buffer against stress and promote resilience through positive psychology approaches (Source 2). The use of puppetry is also proposed as an effective tool for anxiety management and emotional expression (Sources 6 & 7), showcasing the potential of creative arts in promoting mental health. Sensory experiences are highlighted as crucial components in enhancing motor skills, cognitive abilities, and self-regulation among children (Source 4), reinforcing the need for holistic interventions that cater to both physical and psychological needs.

## Key Findings

- Play therapy is essential for developing emotional intelligence and self-regulation in children through symbolic communication techniques.
- Humor serves as a protective factor against stress by fostering resilience and character strengths.
- Creative arts, such as puppetry, play a significant role in managing anxiety and facilitating the externalization of emotions.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of creative arts interventions like puppet therapy on emotional regulation and mental well-being?
