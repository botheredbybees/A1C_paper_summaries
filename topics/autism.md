## Autism

The topic of autism is explored through the lens of neurodiversity and the social model of disability. Each source emphasizes different aspects of understanding autism as a neurological condition rather than a disease or defect (neurodiversity). The concept of the social model of disability, which views barriers in society as disabling rather than impairments themselves, provides insight into how societal structures can affect individuals on the autism spectrum. Integrating these perspectives highlights both the biological aspects of autism and the broader sociocultural context in which autistic people live. However, there is a tension between focusing solely on neurodiversity and considering the systemic challenges faced by those with autism due to societal attitudes and accessibility issues.

## Key Findings

- Autism is increasingly understood through the concept of neurodiversity rather than as a deficit or disease.
- The social model of disability emphasizes that societal barriers are disabling factors, not individual impairments.
- Understanding autism requires integrating both neurological perspectives and sociocultural contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can society better accommodate the needs of individuals on the autism spectrum while addressing structural barriers?
