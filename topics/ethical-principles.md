## Ethical Principles in Healthcare

The NOAH Code of Ethics from 2023 outlines fundamental ethical principles that guide the provision of healthcare services. Central to this code is the principle of respect for persons, which emphasizes the importance of recognizing and protecting individual autonomy and dignity. This principle underscores the necessity of informed consent, patient confidentiality, and the right to make autonomous decisions about one's health care. Another key concept highlighted by the NOAH Code is quality-of-care, asserting that ethical practice necessitates a commitment to providing high-quality healthcare services tailored to individual needs. While there are significant agreements on foundational ethical principles such as respect for persons, differences may arise in interpreting or implementing these principles across diverse cultural contexts and patient populations.

## Key Findings

- Ethical-principles such as respect-for-persons are central to healthcare ethics
- Quality-of-care is a key component of ethical practice
- There is broad agreement on the need for informed consent
