## Shadow Theatre

The concept of shadow theatre emerges as an intervention that utilizes puppets and self-care techniques to address psychological and emotional needs. Source one highlights the therapeutic use of shadow theatre and marionette performance through the application of therapeutic puppets, which can be employed in a variety of settings for individual or communal healing. This form of expression offers individuals an indirect yet profound way to confront personal issues by projecting their struggles onto lifeless objects that come alive on stage.

## Key Findings

- Shadow theatre uses therapeutic puppets as an intervention
- Marionette performance can serve psychological and emotional benefits
- Therapeutic value lies in indirect confrontation of personal issues
