## Journaling as a Practice

The sources emphasize journaling as an intervention to enhance creativity and prevent cognitive decline among older adults. Source two highlights the role of journaling in supporting mental wellbeing through documentation of creative processes. Both sources underscore the potential for journaling to serve as a tool that enhances engagement with materials in unconventional ways, thereby fostering cognitive flexibility and artistic expression.

## Key Findings

- Journaling can enhance creativity by encouraging the use of materials in innovative manners.
- Creative process journaling is beneficial for mental wellbeing and may contribute to slowing cognitive decline.
- The practice of journaling supports cognitive flexibility, which is crucial for aging individuals.

## Open Questions

- What specific elements of creative process journaling contribute most effectively to mental health benefits?
