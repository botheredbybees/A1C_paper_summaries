## Components Of Visual Storytelling

The lectures on components of visual storytelling emphasize the importance of narrative structure in shaping visual narratives. Both sources highlight the distinction between linear and non-linear storytelling, underscoring how different structures can impact viewer engagement and comprehension. Key concepts such as shot listing and establishing shots are discussed to illustrate how these elements contribute to a cohesive narrative. While both sources agree on the fundamental differences between linear and non-linear narratives, there is less detail provided about practical applications and audience reception.

## Key Findings

- Linear and non-linear storytelling approaches offer distinct benefits in visual narratives.
- Shot listing and establishing shots are critical for maintaining narrative clarity.

## Open Questions

- How do different audience demographics respond to linear versus non-linear story structures?
