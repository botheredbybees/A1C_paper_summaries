## Sustained Interaction

The concept of sustained interaction emphasizes the importance of ongoing and continuous engagement with individuals in various contexts. This intervention is seen as a critical component for achieving person-centered care, which places the needs and preferences of patients or clients at the heart of healthcare delivery. Across studies, sustained interaction is not merely about prolonged conversations but involves deepening understanding through repeated engagements that foster trust and intimacy between providers and recipients. However, there are tensions regarding how to operationalize sustained interaction in practice, especially within constrained healthcare systems where time pressures may hinder its implementation.

## Key Findings

- Sustained interaction is integral to achieving person-centered care
- Trust and intimacy develop over repeated engagements
- There is a challenge in implementing sustained interaction due to system constraints

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare systems better support the implementation of sustained interaction?
