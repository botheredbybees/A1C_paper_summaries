## Identity Maintenance in Chronic Illness

The role of art-making as an intervention for identity maintenance among individuals with chronic illnesses such as cancer is highlighted through multiple case studies. These studies underscore the therapeutic benefits of engaging in creative expression, suggesting that it helps patients maintain a sense of self and cope with the psychological impact of their illness. Art therapy emerges as a potent tool for preserving personal identity amidst life-altering health challenges, facilitating emotional processing and narrative construction around illness experiences.

## Key Findings

- Art-making serves as an effective intervention for individuals coping with chronic illnesses such as cancer.
- Creative expression through art helps in maintaining and reinforcing personal identity during periods of significant physical and psychological stress.
- Engaging in art therapy can aid in emotional processing and narrative construction around illness experiences.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of art therapy on identity maintenance for individuals with chronic illnesses?
