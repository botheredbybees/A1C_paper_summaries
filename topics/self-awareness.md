## Self-Awareness Through Creative and Expressive Practices

Creative and expressive practices, such as drama therapy for dementia patients and journaling for general audiences, emerge as effective methods to enhance self-awareness and mental well-being. Drama therapy specifically aims to improve the quality of life among older people with dementia by fostering a sense of identity and emotional expression through dramatic role-playing activities. These interventions not only provide therapeutic benefits but also contribute to cognitive stimulation and social interaction. On the other hand, journaling offers a straightforward yet profound avenue for individuals to articulate their thoughts, emotions, and experiences in written form, aiding in processing complex feelings and enhancing psychological resilience. Despite differences in target populations (older adults with dementia vs. general population), both methods emphasize self-awareness as a critical component of mental well-being, illustrating the versatility and universality of creative expression in therapeutic contexts.

## Key Findings

- Creative and expressive practices like drama therapy and journaling significantly enhance self-awareness across different populations.
- These interventions contribute to improved quality of life by fostering emotional expression and cognitive engagement.
- Both methods demonstrate efficacy in promoting mental well-being through diverse mechanisms, such as social interaction and reflective thinking.
