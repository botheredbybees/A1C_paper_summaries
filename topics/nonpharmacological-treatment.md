## Individualized Music-Based Intervention

The study titled 'An Individualized Music-Based Intervention for Acute Neuropsychiatric Symptoms in Hospitalized Older Adults With Cognitive Impairment' explores a nonpharmacological treatment approach using personalized music to alleviate acute neuropsychiatric symptoms. This intervention is tailored specifically to the individual needs and preferences of each patient, aiming to provide a more personalized form of care compared to standard pharmacological treatments. The findings suggest that such an individualized music-based approach can be effective in managing symptoms without relying on medications, thereby offering potential benefits for older adults suffering from cognitive impairment. However, further research is required to establish the long-term efficacy and broader applicability of this intervention.

## Key Findings

- Individualized music-based interventions can effectively manage acute neuropsychiatric symptoms in hospitalized patients with cognitive impairment.
- Nonpharmacological treatments provide an alternative approach for symptom management compared to conventional drug therapies.
- Personalizing the treatment based on individual patient needs and preferences enhances its effectiveness.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of using music-based interventions in managing neuropsychiatric symptoms in patients with cognitive impairment?
