## Narrative Development

The sources provide insights into the intervention of narrative development through various approaches such as shared reading groups and expressive writing therapy. These interventions aim to enhance engagement with literature and creative writing, focusing on therapeutic benefits and communal experiences in reading and storytelling. There is agreement that both literary engagements serve as therapeutic tools, aiding participants in personal expression and emotional processing. However, there are tensions regarding the specific mechanisms through which narrative development impacts mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Narrative development interventions such as shared reading groups provide a platform for communal engagement with literature.
- Expressive writing therapy is identified as an effective method to enhance personal expression and emotional processing.
- Both interventions offer therapeutic benefits, supporting mental health through narrative creation and sharing

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which narrative development impacts mental health outcomes?
