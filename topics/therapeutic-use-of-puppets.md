## Therapeutic Use Of Puppets

The concept of the therapeutic use of puppets is explored across two sources with a focus on indirect expression and its efficacy in acute paediatric settings. Source one introduces the broader context where puppets serve as a means for indirect expression, aiding patients in conveying emotions or experiences that are difficult to articulate directly. Source two delves into a specific application within an acute paediatric unit, highlighting how nurses utilize puppets as part of the Pup-Ed framework to reduce child anxiety. Both sources agree on the potential of puppets to facilitate therapeutic dialogue and emotional release but differ in their level of specificity regarding practical implementation. The overview provides a foundational understanding while the second source offers concrete examples of nurse interactions with patients, emphasizing the indirect nature of expression facilitated by puppetry.

## Key Findings

- Puppets can serve as an effective medium for indirect expression and communication.
- The therapeutic use of puppets is beneficial in reducing anxiety among pediatric patients.
- Nurses play a crucial role in facilitating therapeutic interactions through puppetry.
