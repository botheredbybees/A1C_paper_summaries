## Brain Mapping

The exploration of brain mapping through neuroimaging techniques reveals significant insights into functional reorganization and reward system engagement. Source 1 discusses the primary visual cortex's activation in blind individuals during Braille reading, highlighting cortical plasticity and sensory-deprivation adaptability. This phenomenon suggests that areas typically dedicated to vision can be recruited for tactile processing, indicating a remarkable level of brain flexibility in response to sensory deprivation. Conversely, Source 2 examines the engagement of reward systems in early adolescents listening to music, emphasizing regions such as the medial orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortex, which are associated with pleasure and reward processing. This research underscores the dynamic nature of brain function across different developmental stages and sensory experiences.

## Key Findings

- Sensory-deprivation leads to functional reorganization within the brain's visual system for tactile processing in blind individuals.
- Music listening engages specific neural circuits involved in reward processing, particularly in early adolescence.

## Open Questions

- How do sensory deprivation and developmental stage interact to influence brain plasticity and function?
