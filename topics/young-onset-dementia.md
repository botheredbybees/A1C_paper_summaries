## Young-Onset Dementia

The population under consideration is individuals diagnosed with young-onset dementia (YOD), a condition that affects people under the age of 65. The literature highlights significant challenges faced by this group, including early-stage diagnosis difficulties, societal stigma, and lack of tailored support services. Prevalence rates indicate YOD represents approximately 4-10% of all cases but may be underreported due to diagnostic delays. Across studies, there is a recognition that individuals with YOD often face unique challenges related to employment loss, maintaining relationships, and managing daily activities, yet the specific needs and experiences of this group are not always adequately addressed in policy or practice frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Young-onset dementia (YOD) represents between 4-10% of all dementia cases, indicating a significant but underreported demographic.
- Individuals with YOD often face unique challenges such as employment loss and relationship management that differ from those experienced by older adults with the condition.
- There is recognition in literature for the need to address societal stigma and improve support services tailored specifically towards individuals diagnosed early-onset dementia.
