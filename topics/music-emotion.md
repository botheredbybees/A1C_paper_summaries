## Music and Induced Emotion

The investigation of how music influences emotion is a complex field that integrates psychological and physiological measures. Source 1 examines the effectiveness of two models for measuring emotions induced by music: discrete (such as Geneva Emotional Music Scale - GEMS) and dimensional models, revealing biases related to personality traits and emotional intensity. Meanwhile, Source 2 delves into the structural elements of music, like harmonic complexity and rhythmic articulation, and their impact on psychophysiological measures. These sources highlight the interplay between musical structure and physiological responses. Additionally, Source 3 contributes by studying the temporal dynamics of emotional reactions to music, suggesting that emotions are not static but evolve over time, requiring dynamic measurement techniques.

## Key Findings

- Different models for measuring emotion induced by music yield varying insights depending on personality biases and emotional intensity.
- Structural elements of music, such as harmonic complexity and rhythmic articulation, correlate with specific physiological responses.
- Emotional reactions to music are not static but evolve over time.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of discrete and dimensional emotion models enhance our understanding of complex emotional experiences induced by music?
