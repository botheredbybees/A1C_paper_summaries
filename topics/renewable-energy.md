## Active Energy Project in Renewable Energy

The Active Energy project focuses on integrating renewable energy practices within communities through creative engagement with older people. Sources highlight the use of democratization-of-technology and intergenerational-partnerships to promote social innovation, fostering a participatory approach that enhances community value and contributes positively to elderly wellbeing. Quality-of-life improvements are noted as key outcomes of participants' involvement in this innovative project, indicating the potential for such interventions to be impactful beyond immediate technological adoption.

## Key Findings

- The Active Energy Project uses technology democratization to engage older people in renewable energy practices.
- Intergenerational partnerships enhance social innovation and community value through participatory art initiatives.
- Participatory approaches in renewable energy projects positively impact elderly quality of life.

## Open Questions

- How scalable are the outcomes observed from the Active Energy Project to other demographic groups?
