## Improv Theatre

The practice of improv theatre, as illustrated through the historical context of Commedia dell'Arte, emphasizes the importance of spontaneous interaction between performers and the audience. This form of theatrical performance relies heavily on improvisation where actors are expected to create dialogue and situations spontaneously based on a few given characters or scenarios. The key concepts highlighted in the provided sources underscore the dynamic nature of improv theatre, its roots embedded in historical performances like Commedia dell'Arte, and how these practices have evolved over time. However, there is tension regarding the extent to which modern improvisational techniques deviate from traditional methods.

## Key Findings

- Improv theatre involves spontaneous interaction between actors and relies on a framework of characters and scenarios for structure
- Historical performance traditions like Commedia dell'Arte provide foundational elements that influence contemporary improv practices

## Open Questions

- How do modern improvisational techniques differ from the traditional methods established in historical performance contexts such as Commedia dell'Arte?
