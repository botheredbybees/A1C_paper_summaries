## Cognitive Recovery Post-Stroke

The synthesis of literature on cognitive recovery post-stroke reveals the efficacy and potential mechanisms of arts-based interventions like music therapy. These methods are noted for their ability to enhance sensory processing, which in turn can support broader aspects of cognitive function after neurological injury such as those incurred by stroke victims. While specific case studies highlight positive outcomes from listening to or creating music following a stroke, the exact neurobiological pathways that enable these recoveries remain under investigation.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy aids in sensory processing recovery
- Arts-based interventions can enhance cognitive function post-stroke
- Further research is needed on specific mechanisms of cognitive recovery

## Open Questions

- What are the underlying neural mechanisms for music's role in cognitive recovery post-stroke?
