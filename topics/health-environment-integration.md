## Health-Environment Integration Through The Arts

The integration of arts into public health frameworks has been advocated as a means to improve community well-being and address various societal challenges. The National Arts and Health Framework emphasizes the use of arts not only for therapeutic purposes but also for general engagement, highlighting its broader benefits beyond clinical applications. This shift towards a more holistic approach underscores the potential for arts-based interventions in promoting health-environment integration. However, tensions arise regarding the distinction between art therapy (focusing on healing and recovery) and general arts engagement (aimed at community building and societal enrichment). Despite these nuances, both approaches are seen as vital components of comprehensive public health strategies.

## Key Findings

- The National Arts and Health Framework promotes a broad integration of arts in public health beyond traditional clinical applications.
- Arts-based interventions can serve dual purposes: healing through art therapy and community enrichment through general engagement with the arts.
- Health-environment integration is facilitated by arts practices that bridge individual wellness and communal well-being.

## Open Questions

- How can distinctions between art therapy and general arts engagement be clearly delineated while maintaining their complementary roles in public health strategies?
