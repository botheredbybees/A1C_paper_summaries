## Longitudinal Studies in Health Interventions

This synthesis integrates findings from longitudinal studies across various health interventions and populations. The first source examines the impact of a visual arts program on quality of life, communication skills, and well-being among people living with dementia over time. This study highlights the positive effects of such programs in enhancing quality of life for this demographic. The second source develops a novel benchmark method to identify best practices in home care across several European countries using the interRAI Home Care instrument. It emphasizes the importance of systematic approaches in evaluating and improving home care services. Lastly, the third source investigates how multisensory dance learning can slow down motor symptom progression in Parkinson's disease patients over three years, demonstrating the potential benefits of such interventions for neurological conditions. Despite differing methodologies and target populations, all studies underscore the value of longitudinal research in assessing long-term outcomes of health interventions.

## Key Findings

- Longitudinal investigations provide valuable insights into the long-term impacts of various health interventions on quality of life and well-being across different patient populations.
- Visual arts programs can improve communication skills and overall well-being for individuals with dementia, highlighting the role of creative therapies in enhancing care outcomes.
- Systematic approaches to identifying best practices in home care are crucial for improving services and ensuring effective support for older adults.

## Open Questions

- How can findings from longitudinal studies on visual arts programs be applied to other therapeutic interventions targeting dementia patients?
