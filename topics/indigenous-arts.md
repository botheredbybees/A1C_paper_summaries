## Indigenous Arts in Therapy and Mental Health

The integration of Indigenous arts within therapeutic practices holds significant value for mental health interventions. Both sources emphasize the importance of cultural pride and identity reclamation, highlighting that such art forms can aid individuals in reclaiming their heritage while promoting healing from historical traumas unique to Indigenous communities. Ethical practice is a recurring theme, advocating for approaches that respect Indigenous knowledge systems and involve co-design with community members. The concept of 'The Dreaming' underscores the cultural significance and spiritual dimensions inherent in these arts, further enriching therapeutic settings.

## Key Findings

- Indigenous arts are vital tools for mental health interventions within Indigenous communities.
- Ethical practice requires respecting Indigenous knowledge systems and involving co-design with community members.
- The cultural pride and identity reclamation facilitated by Indigenous arts contribute to healing from historical trauma.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of 'The Dreaming' in therapeutic practices be further developed and standardized?
