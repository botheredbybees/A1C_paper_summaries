## Personhood in Dementia Care

The concept of personhood in dementia care is multifaceted and crucial for understanding the holistic needs of individuals with dementia. Source 1 emphasizes that despite cognitive decline, a person's essence remains intact, focusing on their identity as a situated embodied agent (SEA). This perspective challenges traditional views by highlighting emotional connections and personal narratives as central to preserving individuality. Similarly, Source 2 underscores the importance of inclusive language in reducing stigma and enhancing quality care, promoting a dignified approach that respects each patient’s unique history and preferences. However, both sources acknowledge potential tensions when societal attitudes do not align with these person-centered perspectives (Source 3). This source highlights how institutional settings can undermine personal dignity through neglectful practices or inadequate support systems for emotional and psychological well-being. In contrast, Source 4 offers insights into therapeutic interventions aimed at maintaining personhood, emphasizing the role of biographical knowledge in providing meaningful care.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered approaches are essential for preserving individual identity in dementia care.
- Inclusive language and respectful communication reduce stigma and improve patient dignity.
- Emotional connections and personal narratives play a critical role in maintaining personhood.

## Open Questions

- How can societal attitudes be changed to better support personhood-focused dementia care?
