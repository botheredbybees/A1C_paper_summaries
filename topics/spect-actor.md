## Spect-Actor in Theater

Augusto Boal's concept of 'spect-actor' emerges from his broader framework known as the Theatre of the Oppressed. This intervention aims to empower individuals and communities by transforming passive audiences into active participants who can challenge social injustices through theatre. In Forum Theatre, a subset of this approach, spectators are invited to intervene in scenes portraying oppressive situations, becoming 'spect-actors' capable of suggesting solutions or alternative outcomes. The synthesis across sources highlights how Boal's work aims not just at entertainment but at fostering critical thinking and collective action towards social change.

## Key Findings

- The concept of the spect-actor challenges traditional roles in theatre by encouraging active participation rather than passive observation.
- Forum Theatre is a practical application of the Theatre of the Oppressed, serving as a method for community empowerment and political activism.
- Spect-actors are pivotal in transforming oppressive scenarios into opportunities for collective problem-solving.
