## Normal Breathing

The concept of normal breathing is explored across various sources, focusing on the mechanics and functions of pulmonary ventilation. Sources highlight that normal breathing involves efficient exchange of gases without undue effort or strain. Key themes include the importance of nasal breathing for optimal air filtration and warming before entering the lungs, the role of diaphragmatic breathing in maintaining lung health, and the interplay between respiratory rate, tidal volume, and minute ventilation to ensure adequate oxygenation and carbon dioxide removal. However, there are inconsistencies regarding what constitutes 'normal' among sources, with some emphasizing physiological parameters while others focus on behavioral aspects.

## Key Findings

- Nasal breathing is crucial for optimal air filtration and warming before it reaches the lungs.
- Diaphragmatic breathing contributes to lung health by promoting efficient gas exchange.
- Normal breathing involves balancing respiratory rate, tidal volume, and minute ventilation.

## Open Questions

- What are the physiological parameters that define 'normal' breathing across different populations?
