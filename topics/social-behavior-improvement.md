## Social Behavior Improvement in Children with Autism

Both sources explore interventions aimed at improving social behavior and functionality in children with autism. Source 1 examines the effects of dance practice on functional independence, focusing particularly on non-verbal communication skills among autistic individuals requiring various levels of need support. In contrast, Source 2 reviews literature on Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) as an intervention for children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), highlighting its effectiveness in enhancing social behavior and emotional regulation through a play-based approach. Despite the difference in methodologies—dance practice versus play therapy—both interventions share common goals of fostering better communication skills, improving social interaction capabilities, and promoting overall functional independence among autistic children.

## Key Findings

- Both dance practice and child-centered play therapy aim to improve non-verbal communication skills.
- Functional independence is a key outcome for both intervention methods in treating autism.
- Social behavior improvement is a core objective of interventions targeting autistic individuals.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of dance practice compare with those of child-centered play therapy in improving social behaviors and functional independence among children with autism?
