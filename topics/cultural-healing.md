## Cultural Healing

The sources explore interventions that utilize expressive arts for healing and intergenerational dialogue. Puppet theatre is highlighted as a medium where radical listening and storytelling can occur in safe spaces, facilitating the expression of painful or silenced experiences across generations. In contrast, a dance project within youth detention centers focuses on self-expression and trauma-healing through cultural representation, empowering young individuals to articulate their stories physically. Both interventions underscore the importance of culturally relevant practices in therapeutic settings and highlight the potential for arts-based methods to address historical and contemporary traumas.

## Key Findings

- Expressive arts can serve as effective mediums for intergenerational dialogue and healing from trauma.
- Culturally-specific artistic forms such as puppet theatre and dance projects offer unique pathways for individuals to process and express their experiences.
- Safe spaces facilitated by expressive arts interventions are crucial in enabling radical listening and storytelling across diverse age groups.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of culturally specific therapeutic interventions be measured and standardized?
