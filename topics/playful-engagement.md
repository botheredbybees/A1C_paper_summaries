## Playful Engagement in Dementia Care

Sources indicate that playful engagement is a valuable approach in dementia care, emphasizing person-centered treatment and incorporating various theatrical and creative methods. These interventions are rooted in Roger Caillois's four forms of play, which categorize different types of ludic activities to enhance cognitive stimulation and emotional well-being for individuals with dementia. The Playful Engagement Project exemplifies such practices by integrating storytelling, improvisation, and other theatrical techniques that resonate with the personal histories and preferences of patients. Additionally, initiatives like Sundowner Theatre Production, Timeslips Program, and Alzheimer's Poetry Project have been developed to cater specifically to the needs of persons with dementia through arts-based therapeutic interventions, providing them a platform for self-expression and social interaction despite cognitive decline.

## Key Findings

- Playful engagement is beneficial in enhancing emotional well-being and cognitive stimulation among individuals with dementia.
- Theatrical and creative methods can be effectively used to implement person-centered care principles in dementia settings.
- Specific programs such as Sundowner Theatre Production, Timeslips Program, and Alzheimer's Poetry Project have demonstrated positive outcomes for persons living with dementia.

## Open Questions
