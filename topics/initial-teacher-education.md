## Initial Teacher Education in Music

The provided sources emphasize the critical role of initial teacher education (ITE) in shaping future educators' proficiency and enthusiasm for teaching music. These documents highlight significant challenges in current ITE programs, such as inadequate training resources, insufficient professional development opportunities, and a decline in dedicated music education provision within these frameworks. A recurring theme across all sources is the need for comprehensive support systems to ensure that new teachers feel prepared and motivated to incorporate high-quality music instruction into their classrooms. Despite shared concerns about the state of music education preparation in ITE programs, there are indications from some studies suggesting potential pathways forward through innovative curricula and collaborative professional development models.

## Key Findings

- Inadequate music education provision is a significant challenge within current initial teacher education frameworks.
- There is a recognized need for enhanced professional development opportunities for educators focusing on music instruction.
- Potential exists for improvement in ITE programs through the adoption of innovative teaching methodologies and collaborative learning environments.

## Open Questions

- How can educational institutions effectively integrate comprehensive music education into initial teacher training programs to ensure quality and sustainability?
