## Timeslips Program in Dementia Care

The Timeslips program emerges as a significant intervention for individuals living with dementia, focusing on fostering creativity and play through narrative storytelling. Both sources emphasize the importance of humanities-based approaches that prioritize engagement through activities like TimeSlips, which facilitates collective story creation without fear of memory loss or recollection accuracy. Such interventions are grounded in the concept of 'homo ludens', underscoring the inherent human drive towards playful expression as a therapeutic and expressive tool. Both sources highlight similar benefits from these programs: improved social interaction, enhanced sense of self-worth, and a means to connect emotionally with others despite cognitive challenges. However, there is less discussion on how such interventions can be scaled or standardized across diverse settings, reflecting an ongoing challenge in their widespread adoption.

## Key Findings

- TimeSlips program enhances creativity and social interaction among individuals living with dementia through narrative storytelling.
- Humanities-based activities like TimeSlips foster emotional connections and self-worth, independent of memory accuracy.
- Playful Engagement Projects and similar initiatives are recognized as valuable interventions for dementia care.

## Open Questions

- How can playful engagement programs such as Timeslips be effectively scaled or standardized across different settings to ensure broad accessibility?
