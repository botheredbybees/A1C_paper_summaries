## Migrant Mothers

The topic of migrant mothers is primarily explored through the lens of their experiences and challenges as a distinct population. A significant source material comes from an assignment titled 'The Assignment I'll Never Forget,' which delves into the life and struggles of a migrant mother captured in the iconic photograph by Dorothea Lange for the Farm Security Administration (FSA) during the Great Depression. This narrative highlights the dire economic conditions faced by migrant workers, particularly women, who were forced to travel across the country seeking agricultural jobs due to economic hardship. The document provides insights into the specific hardships endured by these mothers, including poverty and hunger, which are exacerbated by their transient lifestyle. However, there is a paucity of detailed analysis regarding the long-term impacts on this population's social and economic status post-Great Depression era.

## Key Findings

- Migrant mothers faced extreme poverty and hardship during the Great Depression era as documented through FSA projects like Dorothea Lange’s work.
- The transient lifestyle of migrant workers significantly impacted their ability to maintain stable family conditions.
- Economic instability was a primary driver pushing families into migration in search of agricultural labor opportunities.

## Open Questions

- What were the long-term social and economic impacts on the lives of migrant mothers following the Great Depression?
