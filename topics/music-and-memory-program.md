## Music And Memory Program

The Music and Memory program is highlighted as an effective non-pharmacological intervention for dementia care in nursing homes. Both sources emphasize the positive behavioral impacts of personalized music listening among individuals with dementia, suggesting that individualized musical experiences can reduce agitation and improve mood. Source 1 presents empirical evidence from a study evaluating the effectiveness of this program in reducing behavioral problems associated with dementia. Meanwhile, Source 2 delves into the physiological benefits of music engagement, such as increased dopamine release through rhythmic entrainment. Despite these positive outcomes, both sources underscore the necessity for individualized approaches and personal connections to music that resonate deeply with each person.

## Key Findings

- The Music and Memory program effectively reduces behavioral problems in dementia patients
- Music therapy can enhance mood and decrease agitation in those suffering from dementia
- Personalized musical experiences are more beneficial than generic music programs
