## Illness Metaphors in Dementia Discourse

The concept of illness metaphors in dementia discourse highlights the importance of language and framing in understanding and treating dementia. Sources discuss how dementia is often framed as a disease rather than an aspect of human frailty, influencing both societal perceptions and palliative care approaches. There is agreement across sources on the need for a rethinking of dementia discourse to better address the holistic needs of individuals with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Dementia is frequently framed within medical models as if it were a disease similar to other illnesses.
- Framing dementia solely through an illness metaphor can oversimplify and misrepresent its nature, focusing too much on pathology rather than personhood.
- A reframing of dementia towards human frailty acknowledges the complex interplay between physical decline and mental health.

## Open Questions

- How does shifting from disease-based to human-frailty-oriented metaphors impact the quality of care for individuals with dementia?
