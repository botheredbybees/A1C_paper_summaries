## Pivotal Response Therapy

Pivotal Response Therapy (PRT) is a behavioral intervention designed to address autism spectrum disorder by focusing on specific pivotal areas of development. Play and arts-based therapies, including music therapy and child-centered play therapy, are often used alongside PRT to enhance its effectiveness in engaging children with neurodevelopmental disorders. These complementary methods leverage the natural motivation of children through activities they enjoy, thereby facilitating learning and social skills improvement. However, while there is consensus on the benefits of combining these approaches, questions remain about the optimal integration and individualized tailoring for maximum efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Pivotal Response Therapy (PRT) targets key areas that lead to widespread improvements in behavior
- Play and arts-based therapies complement PRT by engaging children through preferred activities
- Combining interventions can enhance learning and social skills development

## Open Questions

- What is the optimal combination of Pivotal Response Therapy with play and arts-based therapies for individualized treatment?
