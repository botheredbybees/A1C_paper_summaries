## Displacement

This topic explores the concept of displacement both as a broad phenomenon affecting individuals and groups and through specific therapeutic interventions. Source 1 discusses the global displacement of people, often due to conflict or environmental factors, highlighting the personal and societal impacts of such movements on refugees' lives. In contrast, Source 2 delves into Puppet Play Therapy (PPT) with displaced children, using symbolic representations via puppets to facilitate expression of emotions that might be difficult to articulate verbally. PPT serves as a therapeutic intervention aimed at helping children cope with their experiences of displacement and other life stressors by providing an outlet for emotional projection and processing through the use of symbolism.

## Key Findings

- Displacement can have profound effects on individuals, particularly refugees, impacting their personal lives and communities.
- Puppet Play Therapy provides a symbolic medium for children to project and process complex emotions related to displacement experiences.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term psychological impacts of displacement differ between adults and children, and how can interventions be tailored accordingly?
