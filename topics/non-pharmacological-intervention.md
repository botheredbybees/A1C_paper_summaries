## Non-pharmacological Interventions for Dementia Care

The synthesis examines two non-pharmacological interventions aimed at enhancing quality of life and addressing behavioral symptoms in dementia patients: the Wisconsin Music & Memory Program and elder-clowning. The Music & Memory Program emphasizes personalized music therapy to improve resident well-being, while elder-clowning focuses on alleviating Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD). Both approaches suggest that targeted interventions can provide significant benefits beyond traditional pharmacological treatments. However, the specific mechanisms through which these non-pharmacological strategies exert their influence remain underexplored in empirical studies.

## Key Findings

- Personalized music therapy improves resident well-being in dementia care settings.
- Elder-clowning intervention helps alleviate BPSD symptoms among dementia patients.

## Open Questions

- What are the underlying mechanisms that make non-pharmacological interventions effective for managing BPSD and enhancing well-being?
