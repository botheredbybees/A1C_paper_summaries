## A Framework for Arts in Health Programmes

The Aesop framework presented across these sources aims to standardize the development and research of arts in health programs. It draws heavily on Medical Research Council guidelines, emphasizing the importance of rigorous evaluation methodologies such as Participatory Action Research (PAR), ethnography, and grounded theory for a comprehensive understanding of how art impacts various facets of healthcare. While there is agreement on utilizing participatory methods to ensure that arts-based interventions are relevant and effective within local contexts, differences arise in the application of these principles across diverse populations and settings.

## Key Findings

- The Aesop framework emphasizes rigorous evaluation methodologies for arts in health programs.
- Participatory Action Research (PAR), ethnography, and grounded theory are integral to understanding arts-based interventions
- Standardized approaches aim to enhance the efficacy and relevance of arts-in-health initiatives.

## Open Questions

- How can participatory methods be effectively adapted for diverse cultural and social contexts within arts in health programs?
