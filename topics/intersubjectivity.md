## Intersubjectivity

The concept of intersubjectivity emerges as a critical framework in understanding the experience and personhood of individuals with dementia. Intersubjectivity refers to the shared mental processes that occur between people during social interactions, which is crucial for building mutual understanding and support. In the context of dementia, this theoretical lens highlights the importance of acknowledging the subjective experiences of patients, emphasizing their ongoing need for recognition and care in a way that respects their personhood despite cognitive decline. Across multiple sources discussing the experience of dementia, intersubjectivity emerges as a foundational concept for fostering meaningful human connections within caregiving relationships. However, tensions arise regarding how to maintain subjective identity while also addressing the practical challenges posed by progressive memory loss and diminished communication capabilities.

## Key Findings

- Intersubjectivity is essential in understanding dementia from both a care-giving perspective and an individual's experience of their own personhood.
- The concept bridges philosophical insights about human interaction with practical implications for healthcare practices, particularly in recognizing the subjective experiences of individuals with dementia.
- Maintaining intersubjective relationships can provide emotional support and quality of life enhancements for people living with dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do we balance respect for an individual's subjective experience of personhood against the pragmatic realities and limitations imposed by dementia?
