## Hand Puppet-Based Therapeutic Play

The synthesis of research on hand-puppet-based therapeutic play reveals a consistent trend in its efficacy for reducing fear and pain associated with medical procedures among preschool-aged children. This intervention utilizes the engaging nature of hand puppets to provide emotional support, distraction, and reassurance during potentially distressing experiences like blood collection. Studies indicate that this method can significantly lower anxiety levels and improve cooperation from young patients. However, there is variability in how effectively different puppet characters resonate with various cultural backgrounds, suggesting a need for customization based on demographic specifics.

## Key Findings

- hand-puppet-based therapeutic play reduces fear and pain associated with medical procedures
- the intervention increases children's cooperation during blood collection
- customization of hand puppets based on cultural context enhances effectiveness

## Open Questions

- how to effectively customize hand puppet characters for diverse cultural backgrounds
