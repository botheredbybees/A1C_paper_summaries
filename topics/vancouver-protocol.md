## Vancouver Protocol

The Vancouver Protocol is a widely recognized guideline for the ethical conduct of research, emphasizing the importance of maintaining participant anonymity and transparency in data analysis and reporting. Across sources, there is agreement on the protocol's core principles but also notable gaps regarding its application across diverse contexts. The protocol underscores the significance of preserving anonymity to protect participants' identities and fosters a commitment to transparent reporting practices. However, discrepancies arise when considering how these principles are applied in different research settings, such as arts-in-health initiatives where creative expression can complicate traditional notions of confidentiality.

## Key Findings

- The Vancouver Protocol prioritizes the preservation of participant anonymity.
- It mandates transparency in data analysis and reporting.
- Differences exist regarding its applicability in various research contexts.

## Open Questions

- How does the Vancouver Protocol address the unique challenges posed by creative expression in arts-in-health research?
