## Perceived Social Support

This synthesis examines the impact of interventions aimed at enhancing perceived social support among individuals with multiple sclerosis (MS). Sources highlight that creative art programs significantly influence self-esteem, hope, and self-efficacy in MS patients, underscoring a strong correlation between these psychological factors and perceived social support. However, while these findings indicate positive outcomes, there is limited exploration of the specific mechanisms through which such interventions affect perceived social support directly. Additionally, despite evidence for the benefits of creative art programs, the sustainability and scalability of these interventions remain underexplored.

## Key Findings

- Creative art programs positively impact self-esteem, hope, and self-efficacy in individuals with MS.
- Perceived social support is closely linked to psychological well-being outcomes like self-esteem and hope.
- Interventions designed to enhance perceived social support may indirectly benefit other aspects of psychological health.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which creative art programs influence perceived social support?
