## Child-Centered Play Therapy

The sources explore the efficacy of Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) combined with rhythmic relating as an intervention for improving emotion regulation in children diagnosed with autism. Both studies focus on CCPT's role in enhancing emotional intelligence and social skills among autistic children, emphasizing its non-verbal nature which aligns well with the communication challenges faced by this population. However, there are discrepancies regarding the specific measures used to assess improvements: while one study employs qualitative methods to gauge changes in emotion regulation behavior, another highlights the utility of a standardized Emotion Regulation Checklist (ERC) for quantifying such advancements. These sources underscore CCPT's potential as an effective therapeutic approach but also indicate a need for more robust measurement tools.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy and rhythmic relating can improve emotion regulation in children with autism
- The non-verbal nature of CCPT aligns well with communication challenges faced by autistic children
- Standardized measures like the Emotion Regulation Checklist are useful for quantifying improvements

## Open Questions

- What is the optimal combination of qualitative and quantitative assessments to evaluate the effectiveness of CCPT in improving emotion regulation?
