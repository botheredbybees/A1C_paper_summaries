## Regulatory Behaviors in Social Contexts

The concept of regulatory behaviors is explored through the lens of musical play, where children engage in self-regulating and co-regulating activities. These behaviors include cognitive monitoring, emotionally-motivational monitoring, and socially-shared regulation. Sources highlight how these behaviors are influenced by environmental factors and social interactions. The integration of music as a context for regulation offers unique affordances that enhance emotional and cognitive development. Despite the benefits observed in musical settings, questions remain about how regulatory behaviors translate across different contexts and populations.

## Key Findings

- Regulatory behaviors include cognitive monitoring, emotionally-motivational monitoring, and socially-shared regulation.
- Musical play provides a context for enhanced cognitive and emotional development through these regulatory mechanisms.
- Environmental factors significantly influence the expression of regulatory behaviors in children.

## Open Questions

- How do regulatory behaviors observed in musical contexts transfer to other settings or populations?
