## Emotional and Cognitive Resources

The concept of emotional and cognitive resources emerges as a critical factor in understanding the impact of music on public health. From a salutogenic perspective, which focuses on maintaining well-being and promoting recovery rather than addressing illness (Source 1), these resources represent an individual's ability to manage their emotions and engage effectively with complex information or tasks. While Source 1 emphasizes the role of music as a facilitator in enhancing emotional-and-cognitive-resources, thereby contributing positively to public health outcomes, there is a need for further exploration into how these resources can be quantified and operationalized within various cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Music plays a crucial role in enhancing individuals' emotional and cognitive capacities, supporting overall well-being according to the salutogenic approach
- There is a clear link between improved emotional-and-cognitive-resources and better public health outcomes

## Open Questions

- How can emotional and cognitive resources be effectively measured and operationalized across diverse cultural settings?
