## Dramatherapy

Dramatherapy is a form of psychotherapy that utilizes theatrical techniques to facilitate emotional expression and personal development. Across sources, it is highlighted as an intervention for both dementia care and autism spectrum disorder (ASD), emphasizing the role of nonverbal communication, therapeutic relationships, and person-centered approaches. Source 1 introduces Theatre of the Oppressed methods alongside Veder’s approach to demonstrate how dramatherapy can address power dynamics and personal narratives in a supportive manner. Sources 2 and 4 corroborate that dramatherapy enhances non-verbal expression and fosters therapeutic bonds, which are crucial for individuals with ASD where verbal communication might be challenging. Both dementia care (source 1) and ASD contexts (source 4) utilize dramatherapy to promote coping strategies and emotional engagement through the arts. The integration of person-centered care guidelines in source 3 underscores the importance of individualized treatment plans that respect the unique needs of patients, aligning with both therapeutic settings.

## Key Findings

- Dramatherapy supports non-verbal communication as a primary means of expression for individuals facing verbal communication challenges.
- Person-centered approaches are integral to successful dramatherapy interventions in dementia care and autism spectrum disorder (ASD).
- Theatre techniques, such as Theatre of the Oppressed and Veder's method, enhance emotional engagement by addressing personal narratives and power dynamics.

## Open Questions
