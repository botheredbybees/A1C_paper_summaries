## Neural Networks in Aesthetic and Humorous Experiences

The exploration of neural networks involved in aesthetic and humorous experiences reveals a complex interplay between individual differences, emotional response, and cognitive processing. Source 1 delves into how the default mode network (DMN) is activated during intense aesthetic experiences, indicating that this network plays a significant role in subjective appreciation and emotional engagement with art. This activation underscores the DMN's function beyond its traditional roles of self-reflection and mind-wandering to include evaluative judgments about sensory inputs. In contrast, Source 2 examines humor processing through the lens of incongruity resolution and the reward system, highlighting that perceiving humor triggers a series of cognitive processes that lead to positive affect and neural activation in areas associated with anticipation and satisfaction. This indicates that while both sources investigate emotional engagement, they focus on different types of stimuli (aesthetic vs. humorous) and highlight distinct but interconnected brain networks involved in these experiences.

## Key Findings

- The default mode network is activated during intense aesthetic experiences, suggesting a role in evaluative judgments beyond self-reflection.
- Humor perception involves incongruity resolution and activation of the reward system, indicating cognitive engagement and positive affect.
- Both sources highlight how emotional responses to stimuli are mediated by complex neural networks.

## Open Questions

- How do overlapping neural activations during aesthetic and humorous experiences contribute to a broader understanding of emotional processing?
