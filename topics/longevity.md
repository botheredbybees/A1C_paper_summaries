## Longevity

The concept of longevity is explored through the lens of both quality of life and cultural engagement in two distinct sources. Source one examines how research on dementia influences our understanding of ageing and quality of life, with a focus on ethical considerations related to elderly care that impact long-term health outcomes. In contrast, source two delves into the role of creative arts and music in enhancing psychological well-being and cognitive development among older adults, suggesting these activities contribute positively to longevity by providing mental stimulation and emotional support. While both sources highlight the importance of quality over quantity in life expectancy, they diverge in their primary focus areas: ethical care versus cultural enrichment as contributors to prolonged healthy living.

## Key Findings

- Quality of life is a critical factor influencing longevity among elderly populations.
- Creative arts and music can enhance cognitive development and psychological well-being in older adults.
- Ethical considerations play a significant role in how we approach the care of aging individuals.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural activities compare to other forms of intervention, such as medical treatment or physical exercise, in promoting longevity?
