## Functional Outcomes in Respiratory Health

The topic of functional outcomes, particularly in the context of respiratory health for individuals with emphysema, is explored through diaphragmatic breathing techniques. Source 1 discusses how enhancing breath control and efficiency can improve both functional outcomes and subjective quality of life. The use of singing as a therapeutic modality to promote better breathing patterns provides an innovative approach for treatment beyond conventional medical interventions.

## Key Findings

- Diaphragmatic breathing improves respiratory function in patients with emphysema.
- Singing therapy enhances breath control, contributing to functional outcomes.
- Subjective quality of life is positively impacted by improved respiratory health.

## Open Questions
