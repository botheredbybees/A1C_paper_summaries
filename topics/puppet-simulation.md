## Puppet Simulation

The use of interactive puppet simulation in nursing education and children's healthcare serves as a potent tool for communication and therapeutic expression. Across the sources discussed, this method facilitates an effective three-way communication between child patients, nurses, and caregivers or parents. It enables expressive therapeutics by allowing participants to articulate feelings and scenarios through puppets, thus reducing psychological barriers and enhancing emotional engagement in pediatric settings. This simulation technique not only aids in educational training for nursing professionals but also significantly improves the therapeutic relationship with young patients, making it an innovative intervention.

## Key Findings

- Interactive puppet simulation enhances three-way communication among children, nurses, and caregivers or parents.
- The method facilitates expressive therapeutics, helping participants to openly express emotions and address sensitive issues through puppets.
- Puppet simulation is beneficial both in training nursing students and in clinical settings involving pediatric patients.
