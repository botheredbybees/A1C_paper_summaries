## Music Therapy in Dementia Care

The Music and Memory (M&M) program stands out as a non-pharmacological intervention for enhancing the quality of life among nursing home residents with dementia. Despite mixed results regarding cognitive improvement, consistent positive outcomes are observed in reducing behavioral problems and emotional distress such as anxiety. Personalized music playlists appear to be a key component of this effectiveness, fostering engagement and emotional well-being through familiar songs that trigger memories and evoke emotions. The Wisconsin Music & Memory Program's implementation survey reveals significant reductions in anti-psychotic medication use and reliance on bed alarms, indicating broader benefits beyond just behavioral improvements. These findings align with theoretical frameworks emphasizing the role of music therapy in dementia care, leveraging aural memory, rhythmic entrainment, emotional responses, and dopamine release to enhance overall quality of life. However, there is a need for more rigorous evaluations to validate long-term efficacy and standardize best practices.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy reduces behavioral problems among nursing home residents with dementia.
- Personalized playlists improve engagement and well-being in dementia care settings.
- Implementation surveys show reductions in anti-psychotic medication use and reliance on bed alarms.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of music-based interventions for dementia patients?
