## The Five-Ws in Creative Writing

The concept of the five Ws—Who, What, When, Where, and Why—emphasizes comprehensive storytelling that covers all key aspects of an event or situation. This approach ensures that narratives are rich with context and detail, enabling readers to fully understand and engage with the story. The integration of this theory in creative writing, as exemplified by the photo essay, highlights its utility in structuring compelling visual and textual compositions. Active voice is recommended for clarity and engagement, while creative captions can serve to enhance or alter the narrative perspective introduced by the image alone, providing a deeper layer of storytelling.

## Key Findings

- The five Ws are crucial elements that must be addressed in any comprehensive narrative.
- Active voice contributes significantly to engaging readers through clear and direct language.
- Creative captions can add depth or modify the story told by images.
