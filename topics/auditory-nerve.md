## Auditory Nerve Function

The auditory nerve plays a crucial role in transmitting sound information from the inner ear to the brain. Sources describe the pathway starting with sound waves entering the outer ear and traveling through the middle ear, where they cause vibrations of the eardrum. These vibrations are then transmitted via three tiny bones (ossicles) to the cochlea, an organ within the inner ear shaped like a snail. Inside the cochlea, thousands of hair cells convert these mechanical movements into electrical signals that travel along the auditory nerve to the brain for interpretation as sound. The process highlights the importance of each component's function in hearing; any damage or malfunction can significantly impair this complex system.

## Key Findings

- The auditory nerve transmits sounds from the inner ear to the brain.
- Hair cells within the cochlea convert mechanical energy into electrical signals that travel along the auditory nerve.
