## Person-Centred Practice

The concept of person-centred practice is emphasized in the context of dementia care through a creative communication-based reminiscence work model. This approach prioritizes understanding and valuing each individual's unique identity, history, preferences, and needs to enhance their quality of life and emotional well-being. Sources highlight that a key aspect of this intervention involves engaging individuals with dementia in activities that draw upon their past experiences and interests through creative means such as storytelling, art, or music. This method not only fosters meaningful engagement but also strengthens the therapeutic relationship between caregivers and patients by focusing on personhood rather than disease. Despite its benefits, there is a need to better understand how these practices can be effectively integrated into diverse care settings.

## Key Findings

- Person-centred practice in dementia care emphasizes understanding and valuing each individual's unique identity.
- Creative communication-based approaches are used to engage individuals with dementia by drawing on their past experiences and interests.
- Effective person-centred interventions strengthen the therapeutic relationship between caregivers and patients.

## Open Questions

- How can creative communication-based reminiscence work be effectively adapted for use in different care settings?
