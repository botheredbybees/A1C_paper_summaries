## Adolescent Brain Development

The adolescent brain undergoes significant structural changes characterized by cortical gray matter thinning and white matter maturation. Source 1 highlights heterochronic developmental patterns during this period, while Sources 2 and 3 delve into the specific regional gray matter volume and white matter tract developments related to calendar age and pubertal status. Myelination and synaptic pruning are key processes in brain maturation, contributing to structural plasticity as outlined by Source 3. The integration of these neurobiological changes with behavioral outcomes is evident in Source 4, which discusses the implications of neural connectivity on impulsivity and substance use vulnerability, noting gender differences in development.

## Key Findings

- Adolescent brain development includes significant changes in gray matter volume and white matter maturation.
- Calendar age and pubertal status influence regional gray matter volume and white matter tract developments during adolescence.
- Myelination and synaptic pruning are critical processes contributing to structural plasticity and brain maturation.

## Open Questions
