## Child-Centered Play Therapy for Autism

The integration of multiple studies on autism interventions highlights the efficacy and unique benefits of child-centered play therapy (CCPT). Studies indicate that CCPT can positively impact the neurobiological processes in children with autism, as evidenced by changes in EEG activity following intervention. This suggests an improvement in cognitive functioning that is not merely behavioral but reflects a deeper neurological adjustment. While one study emphasizes the role of reflective practice and its potential to enhance therapeutic outcomes through increased self-awareness and appreciation for individual differences among clients, another underscores the measurable impact of CCPT on brain function as assessed through rigorous RCT methodologies. These findings collectively suggest that CCPT is not only effective in observable behavioral changes but also shows promise in modifying underlying neural processes associated with autism spectrum disorders.

## Key Findings

- Child-centered play therapy (CCPT) leads to measurable improvements in EEG activity for children with autism, indicating neurobiological benefits beyond mere behavioral change.
- Reflective practice enhances the therapeutic efficacy of CCPT by fostering a deeper understanding and appreciation among therapists for individual differences.
- Randomized controlled trials confirm the effectiveness of CCPT interventions.

## Open Questions
