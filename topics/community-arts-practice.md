## Community Arts Practice

The integration of creative arts into public health initiatives, known as community arts practice, has gained traction globally. According to the World Health Organization's definition of health, which emphasizes social and personal resources, community arts programs aim to enhance overall wellbeing by fostering cultural and communal ties. These practices are diverse in scope, reflecting regional cultures and socio-economic contexts, yet share a common goal: to improve mental and physical health through artistic expression and collaboration. However, the field faces challenges such as inadequate funding, limited evidence of long-term impact, and variable quality control among programs.

## Key Findings

- Community arts practice aims at enhancing overall wellbeing by fostering cultural and communal ties.
- The practices are diverse in scope reflecting regional cultures and socio-economic contexts
- There is a lack of standardized evaluation metrics for assessing the effectiveness of community arts interventions

## Open Questions

- What are effective methods to ensure consistent quality control across different types of community arts programs?
