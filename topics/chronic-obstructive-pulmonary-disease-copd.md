## Singing as a Therapy for Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

The use of singing in COPD therapy is emerging as an innovative intervention. Both sources highlight the potential benefits of singing on respiratory function and quality of life for individuals with COPD, though each emphasizes different aspects. Source 1 focuses on social engagement and its impact on overall well-being, suggesting that group singing activities can improve mental health and reduce feelings of isolation among patients. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the technical side of singing as a therapy, detailing how it aids in assessing respiratory function and enhancing breathing techniques through vocal exercises.

## Key Findings

- Singing improves quality of life for COPD patients by fostering social engagement
- Respiratory function can be assessed and improved through singing-based interventions

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of regular singing therapy on respiratory health in COPD patients?
