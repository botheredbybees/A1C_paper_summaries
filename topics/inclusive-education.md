## Inclusive Education

The concept of inclusive education emphasizes the importance of accommodating diverse needs within educational settings. Sources highlight various approaches to fostering inclusivity through art and creative activities, disability awareness training, and understanding neurodiversity. Contemporary-art is seen as a means for engagement and cultural inclusion (Source 1), while Source 2 underscores the significance of breaking down stereotypes and social barriers related to disabilities. Music therapy and emotional regulation are integral parts of an inclusive environment, facilitating social justice and mental wellbeing among neurodiverse individuals (Sources 3-4). There is an underlying theme across sources that creative arts interventions can play a pivotal role in promoting inclusivity, supporting the social model of disability, and enhancing quality of life for marginalized groups. However, practical implementation and effectiveness of these interventions remain points of discussion.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts and cultural inclusion enhance engagement among diverse learners
- Disability awareness training helps dismantle stereotypes and social barriers
- Music therapy supports emotional regulation and mental wellbeing in neurodiverse individuals

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods to integrate creative arts interventions into regular education curricula?
