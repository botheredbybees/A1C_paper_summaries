## Therapeutic Use of Arts in Multiple Sclerosis

The integration of therapeutic arts programs for individuals with multiple sclerosis highlights their potential impact on various dimensions of quality of life. Both sources indicate that creative art interventions can positively influence self-efficacy and emotional well-being. Source one focuses more broadly on self-esteem, hope, perceived social support, and self-efficacy in a pilot study setting, while source two elaborates on the enhancement of motor control alongside emotional-well-being and self-efficacy. Despite these benefits, there is a need for further research to understand how specific art forms contribute uniquely to different aspects of health improvement. The findings suggest that arts therapy could be a valuable adjunctive intervention in MS care.

## Key Findings

- Creative art programs can positively impact self-esteem and hope in individuals with multiple sclerosis.
- Therapeutic use of the arts can enhance emotional well-being and motor control.
- Self-efficacy is consistently seen to improve through various forms of therapeutic arts.

## Open Questions

- How do specific types of art-based interventions uniquely contribute to different aspects of health improvement in multiple sclerosis?
