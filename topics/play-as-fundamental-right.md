## Play as a Fundamental Right

The concept of 'play as a fundamental right' is explored through the lens of hospital clowning as an intervention in healthcare settings. This approach emphasizes interactive play and humor to promote emotional expression, resilience-building among patients, and recognizes play's intrinsic value in enhancing quality of life and well-being. Despite its benefits, the implementation of such interventions faces challenges related to cultural acceptance, medical prioritization, and operational integration within healthcare frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Hospital clowning enhances patient well-being through interactive play and humor.
- Play is recognized as a fundamental right contributing to resilience and emotional health.
- Interventions like hospital clowning face practical challenges in healthcare settings.

## Open Questions

- How can the concept of 'play as a fundamental right' be integrated into diverse cultural contexts?
