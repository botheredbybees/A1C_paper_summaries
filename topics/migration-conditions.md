## Health and Well-being of Male International Migrants

The cross-sectional follow-up study highlights the health disparities faced by male international migrants compared to non-migrants in Bangladesh. Key findings indicate higher prevalence rates for depressive symptoms, overweight/obesity risk, and hypertension among migrants. These conditions are likely exacerbated by migration-related stressors such as poor living conditions, social isolation, and economic hardships. However, self-rated health assessments often do not reflect these objective medical findings, suggesting a potential gap between perceived well-being and actual health status.

## Key Findings

- Male international migrants exhibit higher prevalence rates of depressive symptoms compared to non-migrants.
- Overweight/obesity risk and hypertension are more prevalent among male migrants due to migration-related stressors.
- Self-rated health assessments may not accurately reflect the objective medical conditions faced by migrants.
