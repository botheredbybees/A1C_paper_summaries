## Self-Reflection Through Creative Therapies

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights self-reflection through various creative therapeutic interventions. Art therapy and visual storytelling are emphasized as effective means for individuals, particularly those with dementia or chronic pain, to engage in internal dialogue and communicate outwardly about their experiences. Sources also point out the use of photography and journaling as personal practices that foster emotional introspection and mindfulness. Furthermore, puppetry is introduced as a method for non-verbal expression and psychological catharsis. Despite varied therapeutic techniques employed across sources, there is a consistent theme: creative activities aid in processing complex emotions, providing coping mechanisms through expressive means. Each form of therapy explored offers unique insights into the emotional landscape of participants, facilitating healing and personal growth.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapies such as art, music, photography, journaling, and puppetry are used for self-reflection and emotional processing.
- These therapeutic interventions aid in external communication and internal dialogue, especially beneficial for individuals with dementia or chronic pain conditions.
- Non-verbal expression through creative activities facilitates psychological catharsis and emotional healing.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of different creative therapies be measured and compared in terms of their impact on self-reflection and emotional well-being?
