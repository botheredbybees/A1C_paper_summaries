## Nomophobia Questionnaire (NMP-Q)

The Nomophobia Questionnaire (NMP-Q) is a tool designed to assess the fear of being without one's mobile phone, or nomophobia. This method provides a structured approach to understanding and quantifying this modern phobia by addressing situational specific fears associated with smartphone use. The questionnaire captures various aspects of how individuals relate to their smartphones in different settings, such as social situations or travel scenarios, reflecting the complex nature of digital dependence today. Sources suggest that while NMP-Q is useful for identifying nomophobia, cognitive-behavioral therapy might be an effective intervention for those diagnosed with this condition.

## Key Findings

- The Nomophobia Questionnaire (NMP-Q) serves as a valid and reliable tool to assess the fear of being without one's mobile phone.
- Nomophobia is characterized by anxiety specific to various situational contexts involving smartphone use, indicating its complexity.
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy may offer therapeutic benefits for individuals suffering from nomophobia.
