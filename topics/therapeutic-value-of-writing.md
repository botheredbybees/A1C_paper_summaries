## Therapeutic Value Of Writing

The therapeutic value of writing has been explored through various paradigms and models, notably the expressive writing paradigm and more recent developments in developmental creative writing. Expressive writing focuses on the process of writing about traumatic or stressful experiences to promote emotional healing and psychological health. However, this approach has limitations when addressing the broader spectrum of human emotions and life circumstances. Consequently, newer models of developmental creative writing have emerged that aim to enhance personal growth through storytelling and narrative techniques. While expressive writing remains a robust therapeutic tool for dealing with trauma, there is a growing recognition of the benefits of creativity in writing as a means to foster self-awareness and emotional resilience.

## Key Findings

- Expressive writing offers significant psychological health benefits by helping individuals process traumatic experiences.
- Developmental creative writing extends the therapeutic scope beyond trauma management to include personal growth and narrative construction.
- Narrative techniques are increasingly recognized for their capacity to foster self-awareness and emotional resilience.
