## Health-Related Quality of Life

The evaluation and measurement of health-related quality of life (HRQOL) are central themes across multiple sources. HRQOL is a multidimensional concept that encompasses physical, mental, emotional, and social well-being, reflecting the impact of illness and treatment on an individual's daily functioning and satisfaction with life. Two prominent tools for assessing HRQOL include the SF-36 and SF-12 surveys, which measure both physical (PCS) and mental component scores (MCS), providing a comprehensive view of health status. Additionally, the EQ-5D-3L is highlighted as another critical tool for HRQOL assessment, focusing on five dimensions: mobility, self-care, usual activities, pain/discomfort, and anxiety/depression. While these tools are crucial in capturing different facets of HRQOL, there remains a need to integrate qualitative data to better understand individual experiences and preferences, especially when using quantitative measures.

## Key Findings

- SF-36 and SF-12 surveys measure both physical (PCS) and mental component scores (MCS), providing comprehensive health status evaluation.
- The EQ-5D-3L is a critical tool for HRQOL assessment, focusing on mobility, self-care, usual activities, pain/discomfort, and anxiety/depression.
- HRQOL is a multidimensional concept encompassing physical, mental, emotional, and social well-being.
