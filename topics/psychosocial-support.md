## Psychosocial Support

The integration of psychosocial support through various interventions is explored in two distinct contexts. Source 1 discusses the use of Body Mapping and THRiVE (Transformative Health Responses, Innovations, Visual Expressions) to address post-disaster mental health needs by allowing individuals to visually represent their emotional responses to trauma. This method offers a non-verbal yet profound means for survivors to communicate their experiences, aiding in healing processes and facilitating community support. Source 2 examines the role of clowning in healthcare settings during the COVID-19 pandemic, highlighting how medical clowns engage patients with humor and artistic interventions to alleviate stress and improve patient morale. Both sources emphasize the importance of creative and emotionally supportive methods in enhancing mental health outcomes for individuals facing significant life challenges.

## Key Findings

- Body mapping serves as a therapeutic tool for expressing trauma-related emotions without verbal articulation.
- Medical clowns use humor and artistic interventions to boost patient morale and reduce stress.
- Creative psychosocial interventions play crucial roles in healing and improving mental health outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultures interpret and utilize creative therapies such as body mapping and clowning for emotional support?
