## Social Connection Through Music and Movement

Both sources highlight the importance of social connection through engaging in communal activities that involve music and movement. Source 1 discusses 'Singing for the Brain', which emphasizes the role of communal singing in fostering a sense of belonging and emotional release among participants, particularly those suffering from dementia-related illnesses. In contrast, Source 2 introduces 'Octaband Dance for Connection', an intervention focused on sensory stimulation through dance to enhance social interactions and improve quality of life. Despite their different modalities—singing versus dancing—both interventions underscore the therapeutic value of group dynamics in creating supportive environments that promote mental health and emotional well-being.

## Key Findings

- Communal singing can significantly enhance a sense of belonging among participants, especially those with dementia-related conditions.
- Sensory-stimulation through dance enhances social interactions and quality of life by engaging multiple senses and promoting group dynamics.

## Open Questions

- What are the comparative effects of communal singing versus dance on emotional well-being and social connectedness?
