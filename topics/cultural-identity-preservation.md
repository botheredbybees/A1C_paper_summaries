## Cultural Identity Preservation

The concept of cultural identity preservation is central to both sources on arts collaborations with refugee communities and lectures on arts and social inclusion. Both emphasize the use of artistic expression as a therapeutic tool for preserving cultural heritage among displaced or marginalized populations. These interventions not only serve an emotional healing purpose but also play a crucial role in maintaining the historical, linguistic, and traditional elements that define these groups' identities. Intercultural understanding and social inclusion are significant outcomes of such initiatives, fostering community cohesion and personal well-being. However, there is some ambiguity regarding how effectively arts-based therapies can address broader structural inequalities affecting refugee populations.

## Key Findings

- Arts collaborations with refugees help preserve cultural identity.
- Artistic expression serves as a therapeutic tool for emotional healing.
- Intercultural understanding and social inclusion are important outcomes of these interventions.

## Open Questions

- How do arts-based therapies address broader structural inequalities affecting refugee populations?
