## Drama Therapy

The concept of Drama Therapy emphasizes an 'embodied practice' that engages individuals through the use of role-playing and storytelling. This therapeutic approach uses dramatic techniques to facilitate emotional expression, healing, and personal growth in a non-verbal manner, allowing participants to explore their experiences and emotions beyond traditional talk therapy. The sources highlight how drama therapy can be particularly effective for those who struggle with verbal articulation or are seeking alternative modes of self-expression. However, the integration of role-playing and storytelling into therapeutic practice poses challenges regarding its applicability across diverse populations and the need for tailored methodologies.

## Key Findings

- Drama Therapy is an embodied intervention that utilizes dramatic techniques like role-playing and storytelling.
- This form of therapy aims to facilitate emotional expression and personal growth, offering alternatives to traditional talk therapy.

## Open Questions

- How can drama therapy be effectively adapted for diverse populations with varying needs?
