## Arts Engagement in Australia

The synthesis of data from the National Arts Participation Survey and other initiatives reveals significant insights into arts engagement across diverse segments of the Australian population. Source 1 outlines the use of a Population Segmentation Model alongside Behavioral and Attitudinal Indices to understand varying levels of participation in creative activities. This model aids in identifying specific groups within the population that exhibit different patterns regarding their interaction with and appreciation for the arts. Source 2 further explores how these behaviors were affected by the pandemic, highlighting shifts in engagement patterns due to social distancing measures and economic changes. Both sources emphasize the importance of measuring artistic wellbeing as a metric alongside traditional participation rates, suggesting that qualitative aspects such as personal satisfaction from creative activities are crucial indicators of health for both individual well-being and community vitality.

## Key Findings

- Population Segmentation Model identifies distinct segments based on their engagement with arts activities
- Behavioral and attitudinal indices provide deeper understanding beyond just participation rates
- Pandemic had a significant impact, shifting patterns in creative activity participation

## Open Questions

- How can qualitative measures of artistic well-being be effectively integrated into broader policy frameworks?
