## Creative Arts Interventions For Stress Management

The synthesis of studies on creative arts interventions for stress management reveals a consistent emphasis on the efficacy and versatility of various artistic modalities in alleviating stress. Creative arts therapies (CATs), including music, dance/movement, drama, visual arts, and poetry therapy, are shown to have positive impacts across different populations, from students to corporate workers. Studies utilizing the PICOS principle for systematic review highlight the need for rigorous methodological approaches in evaluating CATs' effectiveness. While there is agreement on the benefits of these interventions, there remains a gap in understanding how specific artistic activities influence distinct physiological and psychological stress indicators.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies are effective in reducing stress levels across various populations
- The PICOS principle aids in structuring systematic reviews for CATs research
- Diverse creative arts modalities, such as music therapy, dance/movement therapy, drama therapy, visual arts therapy, and poetry therapy, contribute to stress reduction

## Open Questions

- What are the specific physiological and psychological mechanisms through which different forms of creative arts therapies impact stress?
