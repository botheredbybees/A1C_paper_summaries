## Organizational Effects of Pubertal Hormones

The synthesis integrates findings from the single provided source focusing on the theory of how pubertal hormones organize adolescent brain development and behavior. The text underscores that organizational effects during puberty are crucial for shaping neural circuits, which in turn influence future behavioral patterns. These biological transformations lay the groundwork for psychological and social developments throughout adolescence and beyond, highlighting the complex interplay between endocrine systems and neurodevelopment.
