## Discrimination Experience Among Youth

The Youth Survey Report 2024 reveals significant insights into the experiences of young people across various dimensions including mental health and climate change concerns. However, it notably underscores the pervasive issue of discrimination experienced by youth. The report highlights that economic pressures such as the cost-of-living crisis exacerbate feelings of isolation and marginalization among youth. These factors are compounded by direct experiences of discrimination, which include biases based on race, gender, sexuality, or socioeconomic status. This intersectionality complicates the understanding of how young people navigate their environments, leading to unique challenges that require tailored support systems. The report’s findings emphasize a need for comprehensive policies and interventions aimed at addressing both economic disparities and social inequalities to improve mental health outcomes and foster inclusive communities.

## Key Findings

- Youth experience multiple layers of discrimination intersecting with socioeconomic factors such as cost-of-living pressures.
- Mental health issues among youth are exacerbated by experiences of discrimination and economic hardships.
- Climate change concerns further compound the psychological burdens faced by youth.

## Open Questions

- What are effective methods to integrate climate change education and activism into anti-discrimination efforts for youth?
