## Psychological First Aid

The synthesis of the concept of Psychological First Aid (PFA) reveals its critical role in providing immediate mental health support following traumatic events. Both sources highlight PFA as a foundational intervention that aims to mitigate emotional distress and promote psychological well-being among individuals affected by disasters or other crises. Source 1 emphasizes creative interventions such as arts-based activities, play therapy, and community recovery programs as vital components of the broader psychological first aid framework. These creative approaches are seen as ways to foster resilience and support mental health in a holistic manner, beyond traditional verbal therapies. In contrast, Source 2 focuses more on the structured, timely psychosocial support aspects of PFA, underscoring its importance in addressing immediate emotional and physical reactions to disaster situations. However, while both sources acknowledge the value of PFA as an initial intervention, there is a tension regarding the integration of creative approaches within traditional frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Psychological First Aid (PFA) is essential for providing early mental health support post-trauma.
- Creative interventions such as art therapy and play therapy can be integral parts of PFA to foster resilience and psychological well-being.
- Timely psychosocial support provided by PFA helps mitigate immediate emotional and physical reactions in disaster-affected individuals.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts-based approaches be effectively integrated into traditional psychological first aid frameworks?
