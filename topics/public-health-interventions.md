## Arts in Public Health Interventions

Public health interventions employing arts and cultural engagement aim to improve individual and community health outcomes, incorporating diverse methodologies such as art therapy, creative writing workshops, and museum-based initiatives. Across the sources, there is agreement on the importance of social inclusion and holistic healthcare approaches facilitated through culturally engaging practices. Source 1 highlights the significance of designing public health interventions that integrate arts into healthcare systems to promote cultural engagement. Sources 2 and 4 emphasize Community Cultural Development (CCD) as a foundational approach for enhancing community health and wellbeing, while source 5 underscores the specific benefits of Arts on Prescription (AoP) in enhancing psychosocial wellbeing. These sources collectively indicate that arts interventions can effectively address public health issues when integrated into existing healthcare frameworks through co-creation methods and policy reform.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions are effective tools for promoting social inclusion and community well-being
- Holistic approaches incorporating cultural engagement, such as Community Cultural Development (CCD), support broader public health initiatives
- Specific forms of arts integration, like Arts on Prescription (AoP), can significantly enhance individual psychosocial wellbeing

## Open Questions

- How can public health systems best integrate arts-based interventions to maximize their impact and sustainability?
