## Expressive Therapy

Expressive therapy emerges as a vital intervention for children who have experienced trauma. The use of creative techniques within this therapeutic approach fosters emotional healing and the development of abstract thinking skills, essential tools in managing traumatic experiences. Sources emphasize how expressive therapy allows children to articulate their emotions through non-verbal means, thereby bypassing verbal limitations that can hinder traditional talk therapies. A common theme across studies is the recognition that trauma exposure profoundly affects cognitive and emotional functioning, necessitating interventions like expressive therapy which address these impacts directly.

## Key Findings

- Expressive therapy facilitates emotional healing in children who have experienced trauma by allowing them to express feelings non-verbally through creative techniques.
- Creative expression aids in the development of abstract thinking skills in traumatized children.
- Trauma exposure significantly impacts both cognitive and emotional functioning, highlighting the need for specialized interventions like expressive therapy.
