## Well-Being Outcomes

The research conducted in aged care settings highlights the significance of therapeutic interventions for enhancing residents' well-being outcomes. Sources emphasize the importance of considering individual vocal capabilities when implementing such interventions, as these capabilities are closely tied to social and emotional well-being among elderly individuals. However, there is a need to explore how varying levels of vocal function influence intervention effectiveness across diverse aged care populations.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic interventions are crucial for enhancing the well-being outcomes in aged care settings.
- Individuals' vocal capabilities play a significant role in their ability to benefit from these interventions.
- Interventions should be tailored considering residents' varying levels of vocal function.

## Open Questions

- How do differences in individuals' vocal capabilities impact the efficacy and design of therapeutic interventions?
