## Sense Of Belonging

The sources collectively highlight the role of music and communal singing in fostering a sense of belonging among individuals. Singing for the Brain, as described in Source 1, illustrates how communal activities can enhance emotional release and social connectedness. Similarly, the Brisbane Pub Choir Movement detailed in Source 2 emphasizes community-building through regular choir practices, indicating that ongoing engagement in musical activities can strengthen interpersonal bonds and a sense of belonging within communities. In contrast, Source 3 describes personal narratives from participants at the Arts Gymnasium project in Coventry, which demonstrates how such interventions not only provide emotional support but also contribute to participants' self-confidence, thereby reinforcing their sense of place and identity within the community.

## Key Findings

- Communal singing fosters a sense of belonging
- Regular musical activities enhance social connectedness and interpersonal bonds
- Music-based interventions can positively impact individual self-confidence

## Open Questions

- How do long-term effects of music-based interventions on sense-of-belonging compare to those of other community-building initiatives?
