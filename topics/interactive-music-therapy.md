## Interactive Music Therapy

Interactive music therapy emerges as a promising intervention for individuals struggling with depression. The approach involves three types of engagements: aesthetic, physical, and relational. Aesthetic engagement focuses on the emotional response to music; physical engagement includes direct interaction such as playing instruments or singing; while relational engagement underscores the importance of interpersonal connections within therapeutic settings. Although interactive music therapy shows positive outcomes in alleviating depressive symptoms, the exact mechanisms behind its efficacy are not fully understood. Sources indicate that the combination of these three engagements may contribute to improved mood and reduced depression severity, but more research is needed to elucidate specific pathways and optimal approaches.

## Key Findings

- Interactive music therapy encompasses aesthetic, physical, and relational engagements.
- There is evidence supporting the effectiveness of interactive music therapy for reducing depressive symptoms.
- Combining multiple engagement types may enhance therapeutic outcomes.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which interactive music therapy affects depression?
