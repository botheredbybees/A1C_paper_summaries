## Self-Determination Theory

The sources under review explore the concept of Self-Determination Theory (SDT) in the context of individuals with developmental disabilities participating in fine arts. Both documents emphasize SDT as a critical framework for understanding how autonomy, competence, and relatedness can enhance psychological well-being. They highlight that internal locus of control, alongside self-determination, is crucial for these individuals to actively engage and benefit from fine arts activities. The integration of SDT with the concept of locus of control in such contexts reveals a nuanced interplay where personal agency and environmental support work together to foster meaningful participation.

## Key Findings

- Self-Determination Theory (SDT) is central for understanding engagement in fine arts among individuals with developmental disabilities
- Internal locus of control correlates positively with self-determined behavior and enhances the benefits derived from participating in fine arts activities

## Open Questions

- How do external factors such as societal support systems impact the internalization of SDT principles by individuals with developmental disabilities?
