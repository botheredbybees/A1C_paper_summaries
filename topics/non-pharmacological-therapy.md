## Non-Pharmacological Therapies for Dementia Care

The provided sources highlight various non-pharmacological interventions aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals with dementia. Cognitive stimulation therapy (CST) is one approach that focuses on cognitive exercises to improve memory and thinking skills, which are often compromised in dementia patients. Reminiscence therapy, another form of intervention, leverages past experiences through conversation and activities like looking at old photographs or listening to favorite music from their youth to evoke positive emotions and social interaction. The second source introduces the concept of 'Demenz-Clown', a unique therapeutic approach that uses humor as a method to engage patients and cultivate joy in dementia care settings, emphasizing the importance of emotional well-being alongside cognitive stimulation. These interventions collectively suggest a holistic view of dementia care that addresses not only cognitive decline but also emotional and social needs.

## Key Findings

- Non-pharmacological therapies offer alternatives to traditional medication for managing symptoms of dementia.
- Cognitive stimulation therapy and reminiscence therapy focus on improving memory and enhancing quality of life through engagement with past experiences.
- Humor-based interventions like 'Demenz-Clown' aim to engage patients emotionally, contributing positively to their overall well-being.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of non-pharmacological therapies in managing cognitive decline and improving emotional health among dementia patients?
