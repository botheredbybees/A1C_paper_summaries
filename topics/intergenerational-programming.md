## Intergenerational Programming

The synthesis of the provided source highlights intergenerational programming as an intervention designed to bridge generational gaps through personalized music therapy and dementia awareness. The Wisconsin Music & Memory Program exemplifies this approach by integrating music tailored to individual preferences, which significantly enhances quality of life for older adults with dementia. This initiative not only offers therapeutic benefits but also fosters understanding and empathy between younger volunteers and elderly participants, enriching both generations' lives.

## Key Findings

- Intergenerational programming leverages personalized music therapy to enhance the well-being of individuals with dementia.
- Volunteering in intergenerational programs increases awareness and sensitivity towards dementia among young people.
- Programs like the Wisconsin Music & Memory Program serve as models for effective intergenerational engagement through music therapy.
