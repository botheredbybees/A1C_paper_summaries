## Quality Of Life Assessment

The evaluation of quality of life (QoL) has evolved as a critical dimension in healthcare beyond traditional medical outcomes. The EQ-5D-3L, introduced and detailed in the user guide, serves as a standardized tool to assess health-related QoL across various conditions. This instrument focuses on dimensions such as mobility, self-care, daily activities, pain/discomfort, and anxiety/depression, aiming for simplicity and consistency across different populations and cultures. Meanwhile, examining key issues from the 1990s highlights the increasing importance of patient-centered care in quality of life assessments, emphasizing that QoL measures should not only focus on physical health but also incorporate psychological and social aspects. Both sources underscore the necessity for comprehensive and adaptable methods to capture diverse dimensions of patients' experiences.

## Key Findings

- EQ-5D-3L is a standardized tool for assessing health-related quality of life.
- Quality of life assessments should consider patient-centered care principles.
- Dimensions beyond physical health, such as psychological and social aspects, are crucial in QoL evaluations.
