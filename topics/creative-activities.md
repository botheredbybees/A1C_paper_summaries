## Creative Activities for Persons with Dementia

The integration of creative activities in dementia care emphasizes their potential to enhance quality of life through various dimensions such as social inclusion, memory enhancement, and self-expression. Source 1 underscores the importance of identifying personal purposes and planning arts-based projects that cater to individual preferences, which aligns with the practical application emphasized in Source 2 regarding sensory-based writing and object-based reminiscence techniques. These interventions aim at eliciting life stories and enhancing communication among persons with dementia, promoting a sense of identity and connection to past experiences. Moreover, Source 3 highlights empirical evidence showing that creative activities can mitigate Behavioural and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD), further underlining their therapeutic value in residential aged care settings. However, while sources agree on the positive impacts of these activities, there is variability in how such interventions are structured and implemented across different environments.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities for persons with dementia include arts-based projects that enhance self-expression, social inclusion, and memory enhancement.
- Life-story work, sensory-based writing, and object-based reminiscence techniques are practical approaches to facilitate communication among individuals suffering from dementia.
- Engaging in creative pursuits can reduce Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD) observed in residential care facilities.

## Open Questions

- What standardized criteria should guide the implementation of creative interventions across diverse settings?
