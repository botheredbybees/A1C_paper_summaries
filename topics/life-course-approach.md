## Life-Course Approach

The concept of the life-course approach emphasizes understanding health and well-being across an individual's lifespan rather than focusing solely on specific age groups or stages. Key to this theory is recognizing the influence of social determinants throughout different phases of life, including early childhood development, education, employment, and retirement. Sources highlight the importance of proportionate universalism, which suggests that public health interventions should be universally applied but tailored in intensity according to need. This approach underscores that addressing health issues requires policies and practices that are responsive to changes at various stages throughout a person's life, thereby impacting daily living conditions positively from early stages through late adulthood.

## Key Findings

- The life-course approach emphasizes the influence of social determinants on health outcomes across an individual's lifespan.
- Proportionate universalism is highlighted as crucial for tailoring interventions to address needs at different life stages effectively.
- Interventions aimed at improving daily living conditions should be responsive to changes throughout various life phases.
