## Applied Theatre

Applied theatre emerges as an intervention that leverages the transformative power of performance arts to address societal issues and improve mental health. Sources highlight its application in diverse settings such as dementia care, cyberbullying prevention, and cross-disciplinary educational frameworks. The method's flexibility allows for a continuum of approaches, ranging from traditional dramatic therapy techniques like Theatre of the Oppressed to puppetry and dance therapy methods. These interventions often emphasize nonverbal communication and active listening skills, fostering emotional resilience and cognitive flexibility among participants. However, while applied theatre shows promise in various contexts, challenges remain in operationalizing its principles into structured psychotherapy practices that adhere to person-centred care guidelines.

## Key Findings

- Applied theatre is a versatile intervention with applications ranging from mental health support to societal issues like cyberbullying prevention and dementia care.
- Techniques such as Theatre of the Oppressed, Veder method, and puppetry are employed in applied theatre practices across different contexts.
- Nonverbal communication and active listening are emphasized as key components for effective therapeutic outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can applied theatre principles be more effectively integrated into structured psychotherapy practice while maintaining their transformative power?
