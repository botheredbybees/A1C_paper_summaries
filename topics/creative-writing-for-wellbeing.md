## Creative Writing for Wellbeing

The sources collectively highlight the therapeutic value of creative writing in promoting mental and emotional health. Both drama workshops and shared reading groups are discussed as interventions that foster a sense of community, self-expression, and personal growth. Creative writing, specifically, is emphasized for its ability to enhance well-being through narrative creation and reflection. However, there is a notable gap in the discussion regarding standardized methodologies or criteria for evaluating the effectiveness of creative writing programs in clinical settings.

## Key Findings

- Creative writing as an intervention can improve mental health outcomes.
- Shared reading groups and drama workshops are complementary interventions that enhance community engagement and self-expression.
- Narrative creation through writing is a powerful tool for personal reflection and emotional processing.

## Open Questions

- What standardized methodologies or criteria exist to evaluate the effectiveness of creative writing programs in clinical settings?
