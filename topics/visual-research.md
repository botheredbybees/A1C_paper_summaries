## Visual Research Methods

The integration of visual research methods within therapeutic contexts highlights a shift towards more inclusive and multimodal approaches to understanding human experiences. Source 1 delves into the theoretical underpinnings and early stages of phototherapy, emphasizing the importance of epistemological considerations and ontological perspectives in shaping photographic interventions. It introduces methodologies such as photo elicitation and grounded theory, which aim at capturing nuanced personal narratives through visual data collection and analysis. Conversely, Source 2 focuses on journaling as a reflective practice that encourages critical thinking and divergent thought processes. This approach complements phototherapy by providing individuals with additional tools for self-expression and introspection beyond the use of photographs alone.

## Key Findings

- Visual research methods like photo elicitation are valuable in capturing personal narratives and therapeutic insights.
- Journaling as a method supports critical thinking and divergent thought processes, enhancing reflection and understanding.
- Phototherapy integrates multiple theoretical frameworks to inform its practice and analysis.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of visual research methods enhance traditional therapeutic practices beyond their current applications?
