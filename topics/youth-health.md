## Youth Health Through Arts and Dance

The integration of arts activities and dance as therapeutic interventions in youth health demonstrates significant physiological and psychological benefits. A systematic review focusing on children and adolescents highlights the positive impact of dance on physical health, such as improved cardiovascular function and strength, alongside psychological enhancements including reduced stress and anxiety levels (Source 1). Similarly, a rapid literature review underscores the role of arts activities, encompassing various forms like music, visual arts, and drama, in fostering resilience and mental wellbeing among young people (Source 2). Both studies converge on the notion that engaging in creative outlets offers youths a multifaceted approach to health maintenance and enhancement. However, discrepancies arise regarding the specific mechanisms through which these interventions achieve their outcomes, with one focusing more on physiological markers while the other emphasizes psychological resilience.

## Key Findings

- Engaging in dance can improve both physical fitness and mental health among children and adolescents.
- Arts activities contribute to developing resilience and enhancing mental wellbeing in youth.
- Creative therapies offer a holistic approach to addressing diverse health needs of young people.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific physiological mechanisms through which arts activities and dance influence psychological resilience and mental well-being?
