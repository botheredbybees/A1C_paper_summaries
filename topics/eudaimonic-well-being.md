## Eudaimonic Well-being

The concept of eudaimonic well-being encompasses a deep sense of fulfillment and purpose derived from the realization of one's potential. Sources highlight the distinction between hedonia (pursuit of pleasure) and eudaimonia (flourishing through virtuous activities). Maslow's hierarchy, particularly self-actualization at its apex, underscores personal growth, peak experiences, and transcendence beyond individual needs to serve others. Self-determination theory also integrates intrinsic motivation, autonomy, and competence as essential drivers for psychological well-being. The interplay between hedonic and eudaimonic aspects is critical; while pleasure contributes to happiness, fulfilling one's true potential through self-transcendence leads to a deeper sense of satisfaction. Existential living and fully functioning individuals are portrayed in these sources as embodying characteristics like Gemeinschaftsgefuhl (feeling connected with the community) and subjective well-being derived from virtuous activities.

## Key Findings

- Eudaimonic well-being involves personal growth, peak experiences, and transcendence beyond individual needs to serve others
- Self-determination theory emphasizes intrinsic motivation, autonomy, and competence for psychological well-being
- Existential living and fully functioning individuals embody characteristics such as Gemeinschaftsgefuhl

## Open Questions

- How do hedonic and eudaimonic dimensions of well-being interact in real-life contexts to enhance overall life satisfaction?
