## Art Therapies in Dementia Care

The integration of visual arts projects and other forms of art therapies in dementia care serves as a cognitive-stimulation intervention aimed at engaging patients. These therapeutic activities are designed to provide cognitive stimulation, which is crucial for maintaining the cognitive function and overall well-being of individuals living with dementia. Visual arts projects contribute positively by enhancing engagement, reducing isolation, and promoting self-expression among dementia patients. Despite these benefits, there remains a need to further explore how different art therapy methods can be tailored to address specific needs of various stages and types of dementia.

## Key Findings

- Art therapies significantly enhance cognitive stimulation in dementia care.
- Visual arts projects improve engagement and reduce isolation for dementia patients.

## Open Questions

- How can art therapy interventions be better tailored to meet the varying needs across different types and stages of dementia?
