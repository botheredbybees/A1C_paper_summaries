## Trauma Treatment through Expressive Arts

Expressive arts therapy encompasses a variety of approaches for treating trauma, including breathing exercises, body-mapping, puppet theatre, and traditional art therapy. Breathing techniques and body-mapping are employed to engage the vagal system and autonomic nervous system (ANS), helping individuals regulate their physiological responses to stress and anxiety. Puppet theatre provides a metaphorical space where participants can enact traumatic experiences and intergenerational narratives through 'radical listening,' which fosters understanding and empathy among group members. Art therapy, reviewed in several studies, demonstrates effectiveness in mitigating symptoms of psychological trauma and depression in adults, though specific mechanisms are less clear. The value-evidence framework for art therapy suggests that diverse approaches tailored to individual needs yield the best outcomes, emphasizing structured interventions within the therapeutic process.

## Key Findings

- Expressive arts therapies utilize breathing techniques and body-mapping to engage physiological regulation of stress responses.
- Puppet theatre facilitates radical listening and intergenerational dialogue as a means to address trauma through metaphorical enactment.
- Art therapy is effective in reducing symptoms of depression and psychological trauma among adults.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which different forms of expressive arts interventions influence recovery from psychological trauma?
