## Interactive Puppet Simulation in Nursing Education

The use of interactive puppet simulation as an educational tool in nursing and children's healthcare highlights its potential to enhance communication skills among practitioners. Studies suggest that this method can serve as a three-way communication tool, bridging the gap between nurses, patients (often represented by puppets), and their families or guardians. It also fosters a therapeutic environment where expressive therapeutics become an integral part of care delivery. However, while there is evidence supporting the effectiveness of interactive puppet simulation in improving patient outcomes and nursing skills, questions remain about its scalability beyond clinical settings and how it can be adapted to diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Interactive puppet simulation enhances communication between nurses and patients.
- Puppets act as intermediaries in three-way communication, facilitating dialogue with families.
- Expressive therapeutics are integral to the use of interactive puppets.

## Open Questions

- How can interactive puppet simulation be adapted for diverse cultural contexts?
