## Emotional Coping Strategies

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights various therapeutic interventions aimed at facilitating emotional resolution and coping mechanisms. Source 1 explores therapeutic photography as a means for individuals to process and manage their emotions through visual storytelling, emphasizing its role in personal reflection and growth. This form of intervention uses case studies to illustrate how participants engage with their photographs to address unresolved emotional issues. In contrast, Source 2 delves into interactive puppet play specifically designed for children, employing object relations theory to facilitate the projection of internal conflicts onto external objects (puppets). The supportive environment created through this method encourages children to develop coping strategies by safely expressing and resolving their emotional distress through imaginative play. Both interventions share a common thread in providing structured yet creative outlets for individuals to confront and work through emotionally challenging situations, highlighting the versatility of non-traditional therapeutic methods.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography offers a visual medium for personal reflection and emotional resolution.
- Interactive puppet therapy uses object relations theory to help children project internal conflicts onto external objects, aiding in emotional processing.
- Both interventions provide structured but creative environments that support the development of coping strategies.
