## Emotional Well-Being Through Creative Interventions

The sources explore various creative interventions aimed at enhancing emotional well-being across diverse populations. Source 1 examines the therapeutic use of arts in patients with Multiple Sclerosis (MS), focusing on how self-efficacy and motor control are influenced, thereby contributing to better emotional well-being. Similarly, Source 2 discusses a school-based program that integrates puppets into storytelling and art for immigrant and refugee children, aiming to empower these youth through increased sense-of-agency and improved coping mechanisms. Lastly, Source 3 introduces the concept of therapeutic clowning as an intervention within healthcare settings involving life-threatened children, illustrating its role in emotional well-being and complementary health care practices. Common across all sources is the emphasis on creative interventions enhancing not only physical but also psychological resilience and emotional stability.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions significantly enhance self-efficacy and coping mechanisms among participants.
- Interventions like therapeutic clowning, puppet-based storytelling, and arts therapies contribute positively to emotional well-being across various health conditions and life stages.
- These creative practices complement traditional healthcare approaches, supporting both physical and psychological aspects of patient care.

## Open Questions
