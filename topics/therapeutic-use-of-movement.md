## Therapeutic Use Of Movement

The sources collectively highlight the therapeutic potential of various forms of bodily and expressive movements. They emphasize that dance/movement therapy can serve as an effective psychological intervention for a range of mental health conditions, integrating creative expression with physical movement to foster emotional well-being. Both sources underscore the importance of non-verbal communication in this form of therapy and its unique ability to address unconscious processes. However, there is variability noted between sources regarding specific techniques or populations most suitable for these therapies.

## Key Findings

- Dance/movement therapy integrates physical movement with psychological expression, offering a holistic approach to mental health care.
- Non-verbal communication plays a crucial role in therapeutic sessions that incorporate bodily movements.
- The effectiveness of dance and movement therapies can extend across different mental health issues.
