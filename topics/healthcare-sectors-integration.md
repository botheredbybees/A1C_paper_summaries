## Healthcare Sectors Integration

The synthesis of literature on the integration of healthcare sectors reveals a multidimensional approach that seeks to bridge gaps between traditional healthcare services and other domains such as social care, community support, and arts programs. Sources emphasize the importance of financial viability alongside clinical outcomes, suggesting that integrating non-traditional elements like arts programs can enhance patient wellbeing while also providing potential economic benefits through improved efficiency and reduced long-term costs. However, there is a need for more concrete evidence on how such integrations affect diverse populations and whether they are scalable across different healthcare settings.

## Key Findings

- Healthcare sectors integration involves combining traditional medical services with social care and community support to improve patient outcomes.
- Financial returns from arts investment within healthcare frameworks can contribute positively to the economic viability of healthcare systems.
- Integration efforts require addressing logistical, financial, and operational challenges to ensure sustainability.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of integrating arts programs on diverse populations' health outcomes?
