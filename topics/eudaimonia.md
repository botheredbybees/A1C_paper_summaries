## Eudaimonic Approach to Well-being

The concept of eudaimonia is explored across multiple sources as a philosophical and psychological framework distinct from hedonistic notions of happiness. Eudaimonia emphasizes the development of virtues, personal growth, and living in accordance with one's true nature, as opposed to seeking pleasure (hedonia) alone. Sources highlight that eudaimonic well-being involves engaging in meaningful activities, pursuing excellence, and developing a sense of purpose and fulfillment, rather than simply experiencing positive emotions or avoiding negative ones. There is agreement on the importance of self-knowledge and personal growth towards achieving eudaimonia, although the operationalization and measurement of this concept remain challenging.

## Key Findings

- Eudaimonia focuses on living virtuously and fulfilling one's potential rather than merely seeking pleasure.
- Self-knowledge and understanding one's true nature are crucial for attaining eudaimonic well-being.
- Measurement of eudaimonic well-being is more complex compared to hedonic approaches due to its focus on growth, purpose, and virtue.

## Open Questions

- How can the concept of eudaimonia be operationalized in a way that accurately reflects its theoretical underpinnings?
