## Family Rejection Among LGBTQ Youth

The sources collectively highlight the profound impact of family rejection on the mental health of lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer (LGBTQ) youth. Both sources underscore that family rejection is a significant risk factor contributing to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality among LGBTQ individuals. Minority stress theory, as discussed in Source 2, provides an explanatory framework for these mental health issues by considering the chronic social stressors faced by marginalized groups, including discrimination and stigma. Family support acts as a protective factor against such negative outcomes; however, rejection exacerbates psychological distress. The sources also emphasize the critical role of connectedness and resilience-building interventions to counteract the adverse effects of family rejection.

## Key Findings

- Family rejection is a major risk factor for mental health issues in LGBTQ youth.
- Minority stress theory explains how social stigmas and discrimination contribute to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality among marginalized groups.
- Connectedness and resilience-building interventions can mitigate the negative effects of family rejection on mental health.

## Open Questions

- How effective are resilience-building interventions in countering the adverse impacts of family rejection for LGBTQ youth?
