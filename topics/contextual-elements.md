## Contextual Elements in Emotional Reactions to Music

The research collectively highlights the intricate relationship between music and listeners' emotions, emphasizing the role of both personal characteristics of the listener and situational factors. The studies underscore that emotional responses are not solely a product of the intrinsic qualities of musical pieces but are also significantly influenced by external contexts and individual dispositions. While there is agreement on these fundamental influences, tensions arise regarding how to quantify and integrate contextual elements effectively in models predicting emotional outcomes from music listening.

## Key Findings

- Emotional reactions to music are heavily influenced by both the characteristics of the listener and situational factors.
- The intrinsic qualities of musical pieces alone do not fully explain emotional responses; personal dispositions and external contexts play significant roles.

## Open Questions

- How can contextual elements be effectively quantified and integrated into models predicting emotional outcomes from music listening?
