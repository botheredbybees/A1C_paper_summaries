## Risk Of Bias Assessment

The evaluation of research quality through the lens of risk of bias is critical for ensuring that findings from randomized and non-randomized studies are reliable. The Cochrane Collaboration's tool, designed specifically for randomized trials, employs a systematic approach to assess potential biases based on pre-defined criteria. In contrast, ROBINS-I Version 2 (ROBINS-I V2) targets non-randomized studies of interventions by considering confounding factors and the concept of target trial equivalence, advocating for per-protocol effect analyses. Both methods emphasize the importance of a structured approach to identify and mitigate biases that can distort study outcomes, thereby enhancing the validity and reliability of research findings in both randomized and non-randomized contexts.

## Key Findings

- Both ROBINS-I V2 and Cochrane's tool employ systematic approaches to assess risk of bias
- ROBINS-I V2 specifically addresses confounding factors and per-protocol analysis in non-randomized studies
- Cochrane's tool is designed for randomized trials, focusing on predefined criteria for bias assessment
