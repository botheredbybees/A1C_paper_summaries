## Voxel-Based Morphometry in Neurodevelopmental Disorders

Voxel-based morphometry (VBM) has emerged as a critical neuroimaging method for investigating structural brain differences associated with various developmental disorders. A study focusing on autism, ADHD, and developmental dyslexia highlights the utility of VBM in identifying distinct gray matter decreases within specific regions of the cerebellum across these conditions. The anatomical likelihood estimate meta-analysis technique further refines the application of VBM by providing a statistical framework to pinpoint exact brain areas affected consistently across multiple studies. This approach underscores the significance of the cerebellum in neurodevelopmental processes, revealing that abnormalities in gray matter volume correlate with symptom severity and functional impairments characteristic of these disorders.

## Key Findings

- Voxel-based morphometry is a valuable tool for identifying regional brain changes in developmental disorders
- The cerebellum shows consistent structural differences across autism, ADHD, and dyslexia
- Anatomical likelihood estimate meta-analysis enhances the specificity and reliability of VBM findings

## Open Questions

- How do gray matter reductions in the cerebellum correlate with specific symptoms or functional impairments within each disorder?
