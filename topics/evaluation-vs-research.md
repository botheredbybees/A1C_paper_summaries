## Evaluation Versus Research in Healthcare

The distinction between evaluation and research is a critical aspect of healthcare practice and policy-making. According to the provided sources, evaluation primarily aims at assessing the effectiveness and impact of complex interventions within specific contexts, often with an eye towards immediate application. In contrast, research seeks broader generalizable knowledge through rigorous methodologies such as randomized controlled trials (RCTs). While both processes aim to improve patient outcomes, they differ in their approach and goals. Evaluation tends to be more pragmatic and context-specific, whereas research is theoretically grounded and seeks universal applicability.

## Key Findings

- Evaluation focuses on the immediate application of knowledge within specific contexts.
- Research aims for broader generalizability through methods such as RCTs.
- Complex interventions are best suited for evaluation due to their multifaceted nature.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare practitioners balance the need for context-specific evaluation with the pursuit of universally applicable research findings?
