## Empowerment Through Theatre

The concept of empowerment through theatre is a significant intervention that seeks to engage and empower individuals by leveraging the transformative power of theatre. Augusto Boal's Forum Theatre, rooted in his Theatre of the Oppressed methodology, offers a unique platform for participants (referred to as spect-actors) to address social issues and enact change within their communities. This approach not only allows for the exploration of societal problems but also encourages active participation from those affected by these issues, thereby fostering a sense of empowerment and collective action. Across various contexts, including educational settings such as schools and universities, Forum Theatre has proven to be an effective tool in promoting social justice and personal growth.

## Key Findings

- Forum Theatre is a powerful intervention for empowering individuals and communities through active engagement with social issues
- Theatre of the Oppressed methodologies like Forum Theatre are widely applicable across diverse contexts, including educational settings
- Spect-actors play a crucial role in transforming passive observation into participatory action

## Open Questions

- How can Forum Theatre be adapted to address specific cultural and social contexts where traditional theatre practices may face resistance or lack relevance?
