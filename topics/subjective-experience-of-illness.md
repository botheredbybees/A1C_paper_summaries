## Subjective Experience Of Illness

The subjective experience of illness is a complex interplay between the physical manifestations of disease and the patient's perception and interpretation of their condition. The source material highlights this through the lens of Alzheimer's disease, focusing on an artist's personal journey as depicted in self-portraits that capture the decline of cognitive function. This narrative underscores the importance of understanding the subjective experience as it provides insights into how individuals cope with and perceive their illness over time. However, while visual arts offer a unique perspective, there remains a challenge in fully capturing and translating such subjective experiences through traditional medical frameworks.

## Key Findings

- The subjective experience of illness varies widely among individuals due to personal factors like cognitive function decline
- Visual arts can provide valuable insights into the subjective experience of Alzheimer's disease
- Subjective experiences offer unique perspectives on coping mechanisms and perception over time

## Open Questions

- How can medical professionals better integrate patients' subjective experiences in treatment plans?
