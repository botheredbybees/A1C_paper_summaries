## Respect For Persons

The concept of 'respect for persons' is a foundational principle in ethics and bioethics that emphasizes the inherent worth and dignity of every individual. Across sources, it is clear that respect for persons goes beyond mere compliance with laws or regulations; it involves actively recognizing and valuing individuals as autonomous agents capable of making informed decisions about their own lives. This principle encourages healthcare providers to listen attentively, communicate transparently, and ensure patients understand all aspects of their care before consenting to treatment. However, there is tension between the ideal of respecting persons and practical challenges such as power imbalances in patient-provider relationships or situations where a person's decision may be perceived as harmful.

## Key Findings

- Respect for persons involves recognizing individuals' autonomy and dignity.
- Effective communication and transparency are key to upholding respect for persons.
- Ethical principles like the NOAH Code of Ethics promote respect for persons.

## Open Questions

- How can providers navigate ethical dilemmas when a patient's decision is potentially harmful?
