## Meet Me At Moma Program

The Meet Me at MoMA program is an intervention designed to make art accessible and engaging for individuals with dementia. Sources highlight the creation of a supportive environment within museums that fosters interaction, inclusivity, and meaningful engagement among participants. This initiative aims not only to provide educational benefits but also social and emotional support by encouraging dialogue about artwork and personal experiences in a non-judgmental space.

## Key Findings

- The Meet Me at MoMA program creates an inclusive environment for people with dementia to engage with art
- Art-based interventions like Meet Me at MoMA can offer significant social, educational, and emotional benefits to individuals living with Alzheimer's disease or other forms of dementia
- Dialogue about art within the context of dementia support programs fosters personal connections and meaningful conversations

## Open Questions

- How do cultural institutions beyond museums incorporate similar interventions for people with dementia?
