## International Migration Studies

The discourse on international migration encompasses both health and social dimensions. Source 1 delves into the physical and mental well-being of male international migrants in Bangladesh, highlighting issues such as self-rated health, depressive symptoms, and obesity risks. This study underscores the impact of migration conditions on health outcomes. In contrast, Sources 2 and 3 focus on the arts and cultural aspects of migration through interdisciplinary research methodologies. They advocate for visual studies and participatory arts-based approaches to understand broader social changes induced by migration, emphasizing the rehumanization of migrants and the necessity for multidisciplinary collaboration. Despite differing focuses, these sources collectively contribute to a more nuanced understanding of international migration's multifaceted impacts.

## Key Findings

- International migration significantly affects the health outcomes of male migrants in Bangladesh, including self-rated health, depressive symptoms, and obesity risks.
- Multidisciplinary approaches involving visual studies and participatory arts-based research can provide deeper insights into social changes caused by international migration.
- There is a need for rehumanization of migrant populations through broadened cultural definitions and multidisciplinary collaboration in migration studies.

## Open Questions

- How do the health impacts observed in Source 1 align with findings from other regions or demographic groups, and what are the implications for global public health policies?
