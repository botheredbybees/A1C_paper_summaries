## Personalized Interventions for Dementia Care

The sources highlight the importance of personalized interventions in dementia care, emphasizing both music therapy and playful engagement to enhance quality of life. Source 1 discusses the RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial, which shows that creative-music-making can reduce agitation among individuals with dementia, suggesting a tailored approach through specific therapeutic activities. Conversely, Source 2 focuses on playfully engaging people living with dementia by fostering meaningful interactions in social settings, aiming to alleviate social isolation and improve daily life experiences through personalized care methods.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can significantly reduce agitation symptoms among individuals with Alzheimer's disease.
- Playful engagement strategies enhance the quality of life for those living with dementia.
- Personalized interventions are crucial in addressing specific needs and improving outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences affect the efficacy and acceptance of personalized interventions such as music therapy or playful engagement activities?
