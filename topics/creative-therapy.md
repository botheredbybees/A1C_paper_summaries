## Creative Therapy

Creative therapy is an innovative approach to enhancing self-expression and communication among individuals facing challenges such as dementia or schizophrenia. Source 1 highlights the importance of person-centered care through self-expression, emphasizing a holistic view that respects individual needs and preferences. Similarly, Source 2 underscores facilitated creativity and non-verbal communication as vital components in engaging persons with dementia positively. This source also notes cultural perceptions of dementia, suggesting a need for culturally sensitive interventions. Interestingly, Source 3 introduces Creative Puppet Therapy (CPT) as a specific intervention to reduce hallucinations in patients diagnosed with schizophrenia. While CPT is not explicitly mentioned in Sources 1 and 2, the underlying principles of self-expression and facilitated creativity are consistent across all three sources, indicating that creative therapies can be beneficial for diverse patient populations.

## Key Findings

- Creative therapy facilitates self-expression and positive participation among patients facing cognitive challenges.
- Facilitated creativity is an effective approach to engaging individuals with dementia or schizophrenia.
- Cultural sensitivity is important in the implementation of therapeutic interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can creative therapies, such as Creative Puppet Therapy, be adapted for use across different cultural contexts and patient populations?
