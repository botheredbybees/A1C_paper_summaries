## Preschool Children's Blood Collection Experience

The use of hand puppet-based therapeutic play for reducing fear and pain associated with blood collection in preschool-aged children is a promising intervention. Source 1 reports positive outcomes from employing this playful method to mitigate the distress commonly experienced by young patients during medical procedures. While the study focuses specifically on hand puppets, it suggests broader applications for creative interventions aimed at making healthcare settings less intimidating and painful for young children.

## Key Findings

- Hand puppet-based therapeutic play reduces fear and pain in preschool-aged children undergoing blood collection.
- Creative interventions can positively impact the medical experience of young patients.

## Open Questions

- What are the comparative effects of different forms of creative intervention (e.g., story-telling, role-play) on reducing anxiety during medical procedures?
