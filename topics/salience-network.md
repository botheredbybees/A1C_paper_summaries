## Salience Network in Brain Health

The salience network's role in brain health is increasingly recognized through various studies focusing on its activation patterns. Notably, research indicates that music can activate regions of the brain spared by Alzheimer's disease, highlighting the salience network as a potential target for intervention. The use of personalized music interventions shows promise in engaging and stimulating this network, which plays a critical role in emotional regulation and cognitive function. This intervention strategy leverages the unique ability of music to bypass damaged neural pathways and activate healthier regions, offering hope for improving quality of life for individuals with neurodegenerative diseases.

## Key Findings

- Music can activate spared brain regions in Alzheimer's patients, engaging the salience network.
- Personalized music interventions stimulate healthy brain networks and improve cognitive function.
- The salience network is crucial for emotional regulation and cognitive processes.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of personalized music interventions on the salience network in Alzheimer's patients?
