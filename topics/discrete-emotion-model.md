## Discrete Emotion Model in Music-Induced Emotions

The Discrete Emotion Model and the Dimensional Emotion Model offer contrasting frameworks for understanding music-induced emotions. The study comparing these models through the Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS) suggests that while both models are useful, the discrete model may better capture specific emotional responses elicited by music due to its focus on distinct emotional categories such as joy, sadness, anger, and fear. However, the dimensional approach offers a more nuanced perspective by considering the intensity and valence of emotions, potentially explaining gradations within these discrete categories. The analysis also highlights personality biases affecting individuals' perception of emotion in music, which could influence the validity of each model's application.

## Key Findings

- The Discrete Emotion Model is effective for identifying specific emotional responses to music.
- Personality biases play a significant role in how emotions are perceived and measured.
- The Dimensional Emotion Model offers insight into the intensity and valence of emotions, providing a more detailed analysis.

## Open Questions

- How do personality traits interact with the applicability of discrete versus dimensional models in measuring music-induced emotions?
