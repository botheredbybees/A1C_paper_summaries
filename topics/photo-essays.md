## Photo Essays

Photo essays are a powerful form of visual storytelling that combine images and text to create narrative depth. Their historical roots in photojournalism have evolved into a more creative medium, leveraging the five Ws (who, what, when, where, why) for active voice captions and reflective practices. The structure of a photo essay often begins with an establishing shot or lead photo, followed by body shots that develop the narrative, and concludes with a closing shot that resonates with the opening image. Editing processes include journaling to capture initial thoughts, caption-editing to enhance visual story elements, and peer feedback for refinement.

## Key Findings

- Photo essays effectively use images combined with text to tell stories in a visually engaging way
- Reflective practice and journaling are essential parts of the photo essay creation process
- The structural components of a photo essay include an establishing shot, body shots, and a closing shot
