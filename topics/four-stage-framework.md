## Four-Stage Framework For Research Process

The sources collectively discuss the application of a structured four-stage framework to the research process. This framework emphasizes the importance of scientific rigor in creativity and stakeholder engagement, guiding researchers through a systematic approach that enhances both the quality and applicability of their work. There is agreement on the utility of such a methodological structure for organizing and executing research effectively. However, there are tensions regarding how much flexibility should be allowed within these rigid stages to accommodate diverse research contexts.

## Key Findings

- The four-stage framework provides a systematic approach to conducting research that enhances both scientific rigor and creativity.
- Engagement of stakeholders is critical in all phases of the research process according to the framework
- There is consensus on the utility of the four-stage framework for organizing research activities.

## Open Questions

- What are the implications of adhering strictly to a four-stage framework when faced with complex, dynamic research contexts?
