## Personhood In Dementia Care

The concept of personhood in dementia care emphasizes the importance of recognizing each individual's unique identity beyond their medical condition. Across multiple sources, there is a consensus on the need for holistic approaches to caregiving that address not only physical needs but also emotional and intellectual aspects of care. Caregivers are required to be emotionally and intellectually resourceful to meet these diverse needs effectively. However, while the importance of maintaining personhood in dementia care is widely acknowledged, there remains a gap in translating this understanding into consistent practice across different caregiving settings.

## Key Findings

- Personhood in dementia care emphasizes recognizing individual identity beyond medical condition
- Holistic approaches to caregiving are necessary for effective dementia care
- Caregivers need emotional and intellectual resourcefulness

## Open Questions

- How can the concept of personhood be more effectively integrated into everyday dementia care practices?
