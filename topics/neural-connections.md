## Neural Connections in Adolescent Development

The adolescent brain undergoes significant changes, marked by the development and pruning of neural connections. This period is characterized by enhanced impulsivity and heightened vulnerability to substance use, particularly influenced by gender differences during this developmental stage. The emergence of these traits underscores a critical window for intervention but also highlights the complexity in understanding how specific neural connections influence behavior.

## Key Findings

- Neural connections are actively developing and pruning during adolescence.
- Adolescents exhibit increased impulsivity due to changes in neural connectivity.
- Gender differences play a role in substance use vulnerability among adolescents.

## Open Questions

- How do gender-specific neural pathways affect the risk of substance abuse?
