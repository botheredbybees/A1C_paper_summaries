## Sustainable Practices

The integration of puppetry as a therapeutic tool for emotional expression highlights its potential for both immediate benefits and long-term sustainability. Across the provided sources, non-verbal communication through puppetry is presented as an effective means to facilitate mediated expression, enabling individuals to communicate emotions in a controlled and creative manner. The concept of sustainable practices emerges as pivotal in maintaining such interventions over time, suggesting that emotional expression through puppetry can serve not only therapeutic purposes but also educational and community-building functions. However, the variability in how these interventions are implemented across different contexts raises questions about their uniformity and effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Non-verbal communication is a key component of emotional expression through puppetry.
- Mediated expression via puppetry facilitates therapeutic benefits.
- Sustainable practices play an important role in maintaining long-term efficacy of such interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can the variability in implementation affect the effectiveness and sustainability of puppetry-based interventions?
