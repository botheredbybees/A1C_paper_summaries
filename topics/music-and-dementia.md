## Music Therapy in Dementia Care

The integration of music therapy within dementia care has been shown to yield significant benefits across multiple sources. Music therapy is recognized as a powerful intervention that enhances the quality of life for individuals with dementia, providing therapeutic support and fostering supportive relationships (Source 2). This form of therapy involves engaging patients in musical activities tailored to their individual needs, addressing both auditory processing challenges due to hearing mechanisms affected by aging or disease (Source 1) and promoting autonomy through creative expression within structured environments like musical theatre groups (Source 3). By positioning dementia patients as 'spect-actors' who actively participate rather than passively observe, music therapy can empower individuals with limited verbal communication abilities to express themselves creatively, thereby enhancing their sense of self-worth and social connection. However, there is a notable gap in understanding the long-term efficacy and sustainability of such interventions when integrated into routine care settings.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances the quality of life for individuals with dementia by fostering supportive relationships and providing therapeutic support.
- The 'spect-actors' framework promotes autonomy among dementia patients through active participation in musical activities.
- Hearing mechanisms affected by aging or disease impact the effectiveness of music-based interventions, necessitating tailored approaches.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term efficacy and sustainability of integrating music therapy into routine care settings for individuals with dementia?
