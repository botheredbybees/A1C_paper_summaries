## Psychological Resilience

The concept of psychological resilience is explored across the sources provided. The first source highlights therapeutic benefits of nature in promoting well-being and joy amid lockdown conditions, underscoring how engaging with natural environments can foster a sense of control and stability during stressful times (therapeutic-benefits-of-nature). This ties closely to the broader theme of psychological resilience, which involves adapting successfully in the face of adversity, trauma, tragedy, threats, or significant sources of stress. The source also introduces creative hobbies as an effective pathway towards enhancing mental health and resilience (creative-hobbies-for-wellbeing), reflecting a practical application of resilience theory that encourages individuals to develop coping strategies through engagement with meaningful activities.

## Key Findings

- Nature exposure is beneficial for psychological well-being, fostering resilience in challenging times such as during a pandemic.
- Engaging in creative hobbies can significantly contribute to mental health and overall resilience.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of nature exposure on building and sustaining psychological resilience beyond immediate crisis situations?
