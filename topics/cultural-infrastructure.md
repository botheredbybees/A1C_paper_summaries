## Cultural Infrastructure

The concept of cultural infrastructure is examined through the lens of Australia's 'Revive' policy, which seeks to integrate a vibrant creative economy with an emphasis on First Nations stories and narratives. This policy highlights the significance of physical spaces that house cultural activities as well as the digital platforms that enable cultural exchange. The document underscores the importance of storytelling within communities, suggesting that every place should have a story and every story its place. There is a recognition of the need for equitable access to cultural resources, particularly for First Nations peoples whose stories are foundational yet often marginalized in broader national narratives.

## Key Findings

- Cultural infrastructure includes both physical and digital spaces essential for community engagement with culture.
- Storytelling plays a pivotal role in defining and revitalizing places through narrative.
- Equitable access to cultural resources is critical, especially for First Nations peoples.

## Open Questions

- How can policy effectively balance the representation of diverse narratives within limited physical spaces?
