## Color Theory

The concept of color theory is explored across various contexts such as photography and medical art therapy. In photographic applications, sources emphasize the use of hue, saturation, brightness, complementary colors, and analogous schemes to evoke specific visual effects or emotional responses in viewers (Source 1, Source 2). These principles are integral to achieving aesthetic appeal or narrative impact within a photograph by leveraging color interactions. Additionally, there is an exploration of how warm and cool colors affect emotions differently in street photography compositions (Source 2). The theoretical framework also extends into medical applications where abstract color theory is utilized as a therapeutic tool for treating nervous disorders through the use of carefully selected color environments (Source 3), suggesting that beyond visual arts, color theory has significant implications in healing contexts by manipulating emotional and physiological states.

## Key Findings

- Color theory principles such as hue, saturation, brightness, complementary colors, and analogous schemes are used to influence viewer perception and emotion in both photographic compositions and medical settings.
- Warm and cool colors elicit distinct emotional responses from viewers in street photography contexts.
- Abstract color theory is applied in therapeutic environments to aid patients suffering from nervous disorders.
