## Subjective Quality of Life

The concept of subjective quality of life (SQoL) is multifaceted and highly personalized. It goes beyond objective measures such as physical health or social status to encompass personal perceptions of well-being, satisfaction with life domains, and the overall sense of living a good life. One key example highlighting SQoL's significance can be found in interventions focusing on diaphragmatic breathing for individuals suffering from emphysema. Such treatments not only aim at improving functional outcomes but also consider how these improvements affect patients' subjective experiences. This dual focus underscores the importance of integrating physical health measures with psychological and social dimensions to fully capture an individual’s SQoL.

## Key Findings

- Diaphragmatic breathing interventions can enhance both functional outcomes and subjective quality of life for individuals with emphysema.
- Subjective quality of life integrates personal perceptions of well-being beyond objective health metrics.
- Interventions targeting physical conditions should consider the impact on overall quality of life, including psychological and social aspects.

## Open Questions

- How can we better measure and integrate subjective experiences in evaluating the effectiveness of interventions for chronic respiratory diseases?
