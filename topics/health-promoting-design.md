## Health-Promoting Design

Health-promoting design as an intervention is a critical framework that seeks to enhance the well-being of individuals by intentionally integrating health-focused considerations into architectural and museographic designs. Across various sources, health-promoting design is seen as instrumental in fostering environments that are not only visually appealing but also conducive to physical and mental health. The concept is grounded in the understanding that spaces can have a profound impact on people's behavior, emotions, and overall wellness. Key trends highlighted include enhancing inclusion and accessibility, with an emphasis on creating spaces that cater to diverse needs and abilities. Furthermore, environmental psychology plays a pivotal role in shaping these designs by providing insights into how the built environment influences human health and well-being. While there is agreement on the importance of health-promoting design principles such as daylight exposure, green spaces, and accessibility features, tensions arise regarding their implementation and effectiveness in different cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Health-promoting design integrates architectural and museographic considerations to improve physical and mental wellness.
- Inclusion and accessibility are central themes in health-promoting design to cater to diverse needs.
- Environmental psychology informs the creation of spaces that positively impact human well-being.

## Open Questions

- How can cultural differences influence the effectiveness of health-promoting design principles?
