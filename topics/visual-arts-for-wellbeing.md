## Visual Arts for Wellbeing

The use of visual arts as a therapeutic intervention to promote wellbeing is explored across the provided sources. The primary focus is on how engaging in creative activities can have positive impacts on mental health and social connections. Sources highlight that art therapy, by providing a non-verbal outlet for expression, can be particularly beneficial for individuals who struggle with verbal communication or traditional talk therapies. Additionally, it fosters social bonding among participants, enhancing community support networks. However, there are gaps in understanding the long-term efficacy of visual arts interventions and their applicability across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Visual arts can serve as a non-verbal therapeutic outlet for individuals with communication difficulties.
- Engaging in creative activities through art therapy enhances social connections and community support networks.
- Art therapy is beneficial for mental health improvement.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of visual arts interventions on wellbeing?
