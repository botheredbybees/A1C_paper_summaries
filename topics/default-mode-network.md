## The Default Mode Network in Art Experience

Research on the impact of art experiences on brain activity emphasizes the role of the default mode network (DMN) as a central component in both the creation and evaluation of visual art. Source one highlights how engaging in visual art production or cognitive appraisal of artwork can differentially influence functional connectivity within the DMN, suggesting that these activities have distinct neurobiological underpinnings but share common activation patterns indicative of mental processes associated with self-referential thought and social cognition. Meanwhile, source two focuses on individual variability in aesthetic response, showing that the DMN is activated during intense aesthetic experiences, which can be quantified through subjective ratings provided by participants exposed to various artistic stimuli. Together, these studies underscore the importance of the DMN as a neural substrate for processing complex aesthetic information and suggest its critical role in mediating psychological benefits such as stress resilience.

## Key Findings

- The default mode network is crucial in both creating and appreciating art
- Different activities related to art engage distinct but overlapping brain networks within the DMN
- Aesthetic experiences activate the DMN, reflecting individual differences in perception

## Open Questions

- How do varying levels of artistic expertise impact DMN engagement during aesthetic evaluations?
