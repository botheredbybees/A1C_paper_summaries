## Inclusive Theatre

The concept of inclusive theatre emerges as a transformative intervention that prioritizes community involvement and co-production. Source one highlights the 'theatre of wandering,' which emphasizes the importance of collaborative processes in devising theatrical performances, aiming to challenge societal attitudes towards care by involving diverse communities actively. Meanwhile, source two underscores the significance of disability-led theatre, asserting its role in fostering inclusion, enhancing societal understanding, and building confidence among marginalized groups. Both sources converge on the idea that inclusive theatre is not merely a performance but an extensive social engagement practice that seeks to empower individuals often excluded from mainstream cultural activities.

## Key Findings

- Inclusive theatre fosters community involvement through co-production processes.
- Disability-led theatre challenges societal attitudes and promotes inclusion.
- Theatre initiatives aimed at marginalized groups enhance individual confidence.

## Open Questions

- How can inclusive theatre practices be effectively scaled up to reach a broader audience?
