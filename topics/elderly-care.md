## Elderly Care Interventions

The synthesis focuses on elderly care interventions including personalized risk profiling for Alzheimer's disease, therapeutic interventions enhancing vocal capabilities and well-being outcomes, music therapy for managing depression in older adults, and drama therapy aimed at improving quality of life. Key sources discuss the importance of modifiable risk factors and cognitive assessments like the Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MCA) for early intervention in Alzheimer's disease prevention. Therapeutic interventions are highlighted for their impact on vocal capabilities and well-being outcomes in aged care settings. Music therapy is noted for its effectiveness in managing depression through scales such as the Montgomery Aasberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS). Additionally, drama therapy shows promise in enhancing self-awareness and overall quality of life among older adults with dementia when evaluated using mixed-method approaches.

## Key Findings

- Personalized risk profiling is crucial for early intervention in Alzheimer's disease prevention.
- Therapeutic interventions significantly improve vocal capabilities and well-being outcomes in elderly care settings.
- Music therapy effectively manages depression among older adults as measured by the Montgomery Aasberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS).
- Drama therapy positively impacts self-awareness and quality of life for individuals with dementia.
