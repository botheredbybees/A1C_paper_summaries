## Court Jesters

The concept of court jesters as a historical figure in royal courts offers insights into the role of humor and wit as an intervention for stress relief and entertainment. The therapeutic benefits associated with laughter are highlighted, suggesting that court jesters played a crucial role in alleviating tensions through their comedic acts. This function overlaps with modern practices like clown therapy or laughter therapy, indicating a historical precedent for using humor to improve mental health and social well-being.

## Key Findings

- Court jesters provided therapeutic benefits through humor
- Historical use of laughter as an intervention is reflected in contemporary practices
