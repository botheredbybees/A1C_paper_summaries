## Externalization Of Feelings

The use of puppets in psychodynamic child therapy exemplifies the intervention of externalizing feelings. This method leverages psychological safety and symbolic play to facilitate emotional expression. By utilizing puppets, children can project their internal experiences onto tangible objects, thereby distancing themselves from raw emotions and making them more manageable for discussion. The practice aligns with theories that emphasize the therapeutic benefits of externalizing internal conflicts as a means to process and understand one’s own psyche.

## Key Findings

- Puppets enable children to externalize feelings in a safe environment.
- Symbolic play is an effective method for discussing difficult emotions.
- Externalization through puppets helps distance emotional experiences from the self, facilitating easier exploration.
