## Artistic Process

The artistic process encompasses both the neurological and experiential dimensions of creating art. Source 1 uses functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) to explore brain activities in skilled versus novice artists, highlighting the neurological basis of expertise and how visual information processing differs between individuals with varying levels of practice. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on papermaking as a sensory-based integration process that promotes personal expression and well-being, emphasizing the therapeutic and integrative aspects of the artistic process from a practical standpoint.

## Key Findings

- The neurological basis of expertise in visual arts can be observed through distinct patterns of brain activity between skilled and novice artists.
- Papermaking as an artistic practice facilitates sensory-based integration and personal expression, contributing positively to mental health and well-being.

## Open Questions

- How do the neurological changes associated with becoming a skilled artist correlate with or influence the therapeutic benefits experienced through the creative process?
