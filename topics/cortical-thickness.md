## Cortical Thickness

Both sources contribute to the theoretical understanding of brain plasticity and development through the lens of cortical thickness. Source one explores age-related changes in gray matter density, volume, mass, and cortical thickness from childhood to young adulthood, highlighting sex differences in these measures. It underscores that changes in cortical thickness are not uniform across sexes but rather display varying trajectories over time, suggesting a complex interplay between biological development and environmental influences.

Source two focuses on the effects of childhood music training on brain structure, providing longitudinal evidence for the impact of early musical experience on cortical thickness and other measures such as fractional anisotropy. This research indicates that engaging in activities like musical training can induce structural changes in the brain, particularly in areas associated with auditory processing and motor skills.

These sources collectively suggest that both natural developmental processes and environmental factors such as structured learning experiences influence cortical thickness, contributing to individual differences in brain structure.

## Key Findings

- Changes in cortical thickness are not uniform across sexes.
- Early musical training can induce structural changes in the brain, including increased cortical thickness.
- Cortical thickness is influenced by both natural developmental processes and environmental factors.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of structured learning experiences beyond music training impact cortical thickness?
