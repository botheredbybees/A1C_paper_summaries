## Positive Psychology and Well-Being Theory

The theory of positive psychology emphasizes the importance of positive emotions as a pathway to overall well-being. Source 1 highlights the 'broaden-and-build' theory, which posits that positive emotions expand one's momentary thought-action repertoires and build personal resources over time, contributing significantly to resilience against stressors. This is complemented by the 'undo hypothesis,' suggesting that positive emotions help mitigate physiological arousal from negative emotional experiences. Source 2 broadens this perspective by introducing concepts such as character strengths and virtues, which are integral components of well-being according to positive psychology. It also touches upon posttraumatic growth (PTG), a phenomenon where individuals experience personal development after struggling with highly challenging life circumstances, further reinforcing the role of positivity in enhancing resilience and coping mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Positive emotions play a crucial role in building psychological resources that enhance well-being.
- Character strengths and virtues contribute significantly to an individual's sense of well-being according to positive psychology theory.
- Posttraumatic growth highlights how individuals can experience personal development following adversity.
