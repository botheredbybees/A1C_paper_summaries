## Music Therapy and Wellbeing

Research into music therapy highlights its multifaceted benefits for enhancing wellbeing, particularly in dementia care settings. The 'ripple effect' concept emphasizes mutual flourishing through shared participation in improvisational music therapy, fostering not only individual but also communal health improvements. Across various studies, the therapeutic potential of music is recognized through emotional engagement and neurological impacts, contributing to mental health outcomes and long-term benefits such as improved genetic expression. Additionally, specific interventions like group singing are shown to effectively reduce mental distress and improve overall wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Improvisational music therapy in dementia care fosters mutual flourishing among participants.
- Music has significant therapeutic potential through emotional engagement and neurological effects.
- Group singing interventions effectively mitigate mental health issues and enhance general wellbeing.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of sustained participation in improvisational music therapy for individuals with dementia?
