## Emotional Responses to Music

The study of emotional responses to music involves the characterization and classification of these reactions as well as understanding their time course. Source One discusses the Geneva Emotional Music Scale, which provides a framework for measuring emotions evoked by musical sounds. This scale is crucial in quantifying subjective experiences elicited by different genres or compositions of music. On the other hand, Source Two delves into how emotional responses to music unfold over time using multidimensional scaling techniques. These sources collectively contribute towards a comprehensive understanding of how humans perceive and react emotionally to music, highlighting both temporal dynamics and categorization methods.

## Key Findings

- The Geneva Emotional Music Scale offers a reliable method for measuring the emotions triggered by musical sounds.
- Multidimensional scaling helps in mapping out the time course of emotional responses to music, illustrating how these reactions develop over different stages.

## Open Questions

- What are the limitations and cross-cultural applicability of the Geneva Emotional Music Scale?
