## Creative Music Making

The integration of various sources on the topic of 'creative music making' highlights its efficacy as an intervention in enhancing cognitive and emotional well-being. Particularly within dementia care, creative music-making activities have been shown to reduce agitation among patients, as evidenced by the RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial. The intervention is not only beneficial for elderly populations but also demonstrates potential in engaging individuals across various age groups through creativity and expression. However, while sources agree on its positive outcomes, there remains a lack of standardized protocols or metrics for evaluating such interventions effectively, indicating areas for further research.

## Key Findings

- Creative music making significantly reduces agitation among dementia patients according to the RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial.
- There is widespread agreement on the positive effects of creative music-making across different populations.
- Standardization of protocols and evaluation metrics remains a challenge in assessing the impact of creative music interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal methods for evaluating and standardizing creative music making as an intervention?
