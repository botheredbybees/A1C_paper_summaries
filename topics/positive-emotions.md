## Positive Emotions in Healthcare

The sources highlight the role of interventions aimed at eliciting positive emotions among individuals receiving healthcare. Caregivers' singing (Source 1) and clowning (Source 2) are introduced as methods to induce positive emotional states in patients, particularly those with dementia. These practices can mitigate resistance during care procedures and foster a more pleasant interaction experience. The Humour Foundation's laughter care program (Source 3) underscores the importance of intergenerational interactions for enhancing positive emotions among participants. However, while singing and clowning interventions aim to address emotional responses in specific populations like dementia patients, the humour foundation's approach appears more generalizable across various age groups and healthcare settings.

## Key Findings

- Caregivers' singing can reduce resistance and enhance positive emotions during care procedures for individuals with dementia.
- Clowning is recognized as a professional practice in healthcare settings that promotes psychological well-being among adults.
- Intergenerational interactions through laughter care programs contribute to the enhancement of positive emotional states across diverse age groups.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultural contexts influence the effectiveness and acceptance of interventions aimed at fostering positive emotions in healthcare settings?
