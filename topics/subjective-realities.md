## Subjective Realities in Qualitative Research

The exploration of subjective realities through qualitative participatory art-based research methods highlights the importance of visual mediums such as photovoice and collage-making in understanding mental health issues. These methods contribute to a deeper comprehension of personal experiences by allowing individuals to express their unique perspectives visually, which can offer insights not easily captured through traditional interview or survey methods alone. This approach emphasizes the subjective nature of reality experienced by participants, fostering a more nuanced appreciation of individual narratives within research contexts.

## Key Findings

- Photovoice and collage-making are effective tools in qualitative participatory art-based research for exploring mental health issues.
- Visual expressions provide unique insights into personal experiences that text alone might not capture adequately.
- The subjective nature of reality is central to understanding individual narratives within the context of mental health.
