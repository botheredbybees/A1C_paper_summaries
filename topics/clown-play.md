## Therapeutic Clowning

The effectiveness of therapeutic clowning as an intervention is explored through various studies and applications. One study focuses on the potential of online therapeutic clowning to maintain its efficacy in a virtual healthcare setting, indicating that 'clown-play' can be adapted to digital platforms without losing its therapeutic benefits. The concept of therapeutic clowning encompasses the use of humor, playfulness, and non-verbal communication to enhance patient engagement, reduce anxiety, and improve overall mood in clinical settings. While traditional clowning takes place in physical healthcare environments, recent advancements have seen this intervention being translated into online formats as a response to increasing virtual healthcare needs due to various challenges such as the global pandemic. However, there is still a need for further research to understand how best to adapt therapeutic practices like clowning for digital delivery and ensure their continued effectiveness.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic clowning can be effective in reducing patient anxiety and improving mood in clinical settings.
- Online therapeutic clowning has the potential to maintain efficacy in virtual healthcare environments.
- Humor, playfulness, and non-verbal communication are key elements of therapeutic clowning.

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic clowning be effectively adapted for online delivery while maintaining its therapeutic benefits?
