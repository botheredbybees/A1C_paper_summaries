## Dementia Care Interventions

The integration of creative arts and technology in dementia care reveals diverse approaches to improving the quality of life for individuals with cognitive impairments. Sources highlight the use of tactile art materials like clay (source 1) and digital tools such as computers and mobile phones (source 2), alongside traditional therapies including music, dance, and visual arts (sources 4, 6). These interventions aim to foster emotional expression, social interaction, and cognitive engagement through varied mediums. Music therapy in particular emerges as a prominent area of focus across sources 7-13, with studies showing positive impacts on mood, behavior, and even reduction in medication usage among nursing home residents (sources 10, 12). Dance is another therapeutic avenue explored for its ability to evoke memories and enhance emotional well-being through spontaneous movement (source 3). Cultural sensitivity and personalized approaches are crucial themes throughout these sources, emphasizing the need for tailored interventions that respect individual preferences and backgrounds. Despite promising findings, open questions remain regarding the long-term efficacy of such programs and their scalability in diverse care settings.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts and technology can enhance emotional expression and social interaction among individuals with dementia.
- Music therapy shows significant benefits in improving mood and reducing challenging behaviors in dementia patients.
- Personalized interventions tailored to individual preferences yield better outcomes compared to generalized approaches.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of personalized music and arts-based interventions on quality of life for individuals with dementia?
