## Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses of Creative Interventions

Three systematic reviews and meta-analyses highlight the effectiveness of creative interventions such as music and play-based therapies for enhancing health-related quality of life (HRQOL) and mental well-being. The first study focuses on the association between music interventions and HRQOL, using standardized surveys like SF-36 and SF-12 to measure both physical and mental component scores. A second review examines play-based interventions' impact on mental health outcomes, adhering to PRISMA guidelines for rigorous analysis. Lastly, a third review scrutinizes creative arts-based interventions in treating children and adolescents exposed to traumatic events, focusing on indicators such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), negative mood, externalizing problems, and anxiety levels. Despite differences in intervention types, all studies converge on the positive impact of creative therapies, underscoring their utility across various mental health issues.

## Key Findings

- Music interventions improve both physical and mental components of health-related quality of life (HRQOL).
- Play-based interventions are effective for enhancing mental health outcomes in individuals.
- Creative arts-based interventions significantly reduce symptoms associated with trauma exposure in youth.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of creative therapies compare in terms of efficacy and applicability across diverse populations?
