## Social Change Through Art

The integration of art forms such as theatre and music in social interventions highlights their transformative power to effect change. Applied theatre operates on a continuum from traditional performance arts to more interactive practices, utilizing the 'Theatre of the Oppressed' methodology developed by Augusto Boal. This approach includes Forum Theatre where participants engage as 'spect-actors', combining spectatorship with active participation to explore and challenge oppressive societal norms. Music similarly plays a pivotal role in social change through its ritualistic significance and ability to mobilize communities around shared values, often transcending linguistic barriers. Both art forms emphasize empowerment and communal engagement, facilitating dialogue and action towards social justice.

## Key Findings

- Theatre of the Oppressed by Augusto Boal provides a framework for interactive theatre that aims at societal change.
- Music's ritualistic significance can be harnessed to mobilize communities around common goals and values.
- Both applied theatre and music emphasize empowerment through participatory practices.

## Open Questions

- How do the specific cultural contexts influence the effectiveness of applied theatre or music-based social interventions?
