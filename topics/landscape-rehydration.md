## Landscape Rehydration

The Capertee Valley Hydrology Project emphasizes the importance of landscape rehydration as a critical intervention for ecological restoration. This project underscores how community participation and sustainable land use practices can enhance catchment restoration efforts, aiming to recharge underground aquifers and rejuvenate surface water bodies. The initiative highlights that landscape rehydration is not merely about technical hydrological processes but also involves social dimensions such as engaging local communities in decision-making processes and ensuring long-term sustainability through adaptive management strategies.

## Key Findings

- Landscape rehydration initiatives are crucial for ecological restoration efforts, aiming to recharge underground aquifers and rejuvenate surface water bodies.
- Community participation is essential for the success of landscape rehydration projects.
- Sustainable land use practices enhance catchment restoration outcomes.
