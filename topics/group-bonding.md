## Group Bonding Through Creative Activities

The provided sources highlight the importance of group bonding as an outcome of engaging in creative activities such as singing and writing. Both sources emphasize that these interventions not only enhance emotional support but also serve as a means for self-expression, fostering stronger social connections within groups. Singing for the Brain, discussed in Source 1, is described as a method to provide emotional support while facilitating group bonding through shared musical experiences. In contrast, the Creative Writing Workshop Experiences in Jurby, Isle of Man (Source 2), underscores how creative writing can act as a powerful tool for mental health support and self-expression, further reinforcing the role of group activities in building community bonds. Despite their differences in format and setting, both sources indicate that regular engagement with such programs contributes to an individual’s sense of belonging and mutual support.

## Key Findings

- Group bonding is significantly enhanced through creative activities like singing and writing.
- Creative interventions provide emotional and mental health support for participants.
- Self-expression plays a crucial role in fostering stronger social connections.
