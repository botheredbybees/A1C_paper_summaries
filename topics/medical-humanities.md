## Medical Humanities

The field of medical humanities is characterized by its integrative approach to the human experience in healthcare and illness. Sources discuss various models and concepts that aim to define the relationship between arts, medicine, and health, such as the Arts in Health Diamond and continua model from Source 1, which propose multi-faceted ways to understand this intersection. Other sources emphasize empirical research (Source 2) and holistic understanding (Source 3), highlighting the importance of evidence-based practice within the humanities framework. Sources also explore narrative medicine (Source 7) as a critical reflection on how storytelling can inform patient-centered care, while incorporating historical perspectives and creativity in health through concepts like medical humanism (Source 5). Furthermore, spirituality and religion are increasingly recognized components of healing practices (Source 8), suggesting the socio-spiritual identity plays a role in enhancing patient care. The integration of arts within medicine is seen as essential for healthcare professional development (Sources 6 and 9) and for fostering empathy and ethical understanding among medical practitioners.

## Key Findings

- Medical humanities adopt an interdisciplinary approach to explore human experiences in health and illness, integrating the arts into clinical practice.
- Narrative medicine emphasizes storytelling as a critical aspect of patient-centered care, informing both therapeutic practices and healthcare policy.
- Spirituality and religious studies are emerging areas within medical humanities, offering new perspectives on healing and socio-spiritual identity.

## Open Questions

- How can the medical humanities effectively balance its theoretical foundations with practical implementation in clinical settings?
