## Distributed Neural Networks

The concept of distributed neural networks highlights the brain's ability to process information across multiple interconnected regions rather than relying on isolated modules. This theory supports the idea that cognitive functions are not localized but spread out in a networked fashion, enhancing flexibility and adaptability. Source 1 emphasizes how these networks enable transmodal creativity, where signals cross modalities leading to innovative outcomes, and also influence the brain's reward system, suggesting a broader impact on human behavior beyond cognition alone.

## Key Findings

- Distributed neural networks facilitate transmodal creativity.
- Cognitive functions are processed across interconnected regions in the brain.
- Activation of the brain's reward system is influenced by distributed neural network activity.
