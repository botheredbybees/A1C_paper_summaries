## Educational Drama

The systematic review on school-based drama interventions highlights the effectiveness of educational drama in promoting health among children and adolescents. The integration of theatrical elements into curricula fosters a unique interactive learning environment that encourages self-reflection, active participation, and personal development. Educational drama leverages its inherent interactive nature to engage learners more deeply with subject matter, allowing for better retention and application of knowledge. However, while the review provides evidence supporting the positive impact on health outcomes, it also points out variability in the efficacy across different contexts and populations due to differences in implementation quality.

## Key Findings

- Educational drama fosters interactive learning that enhances self-reflection.
- Drama interventions can positively influence children's and adolescents' health outcomes.
- Implementation quality significantly affects the success of educational drama programs.

## Open Questions

- How does variability in implementation impact the efficacy of educational drama across different demographic groups?
