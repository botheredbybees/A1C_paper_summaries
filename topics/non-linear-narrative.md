## Non-Linear Narrative

The concept of narrative in visual storytelling is crucial for understanding how stories are conveyed through images and sequences. Sources indicate that traditional approaches to visual storytelling often rely on linear narratives, which follow a clear sequence of events leading from an initial state to a final resolution. However, the development of non-linear narratives has expanded the possibilities of storytelling by allowing authors to explore complex themes, multiple perspectives, and fragmented realities. Non-linear narratives in visual media can offer unique ways to engage audiences, challenge conventional narrative structures, and reflect contemporary cultural phenomena.

## Key Findings

- Linear narratives are traditionally used for clarity and chronological progression
- Non-linear narratives provide opportunities for creative expression and complexity
- The adoption of non-linear narratives challenges readers' expectations and engagement

## Open Questions

- How does the shift from linear to non-linear narrative impact audience comprehension and retention?
