## Drama Therapy

The sources collectively underscore drama therapy as a versatile intervention with applications across diverse populations and contexts. In older adults with dementia (Sources 1-2), the approach aims to enhance quality of life through self-awareness and engagement in activities that transcend daily routines, potentially promoting autonomy and emotional well-being. The spect-actors framework (Source 3) highlights how theatre can empower individuals by providing a platform for creative expression and agency despite cognitive impairments. Meanwhile, drama therapy extends its reach beyond dementia care, engaging young Black men to promote health and self-esteem through critical awareness and safe spaces (Source 5). Additionally, clown therapy illustrates an innovative use of drama techniques in addressing addiction and existential challenges, leveraging paradox reconciliation and the transcendent function to foster personal growth (Source 6). Throughout these applications, common threads include the importance of embodied practice, storytelling, role-playing, and creating supportive environments that facilitate emotional exploration and social connection.

## Key Findings

- Drama therapy enhances quality of life in older adults with dementia by fostering self-awareness and autonomy.
- The spect-actors framework empowers individuals with dementia through creative expression and agency within a theatre setting.
- Beyond traditional therapeutic roles, drama therapy addresses issues such as addiction and supports marginalized youth in building self-esteem.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of drama therapy be further adapted to cater to diverse cultural backgrounds and specific mental health needs?
