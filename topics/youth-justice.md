## Creative Arts Programs in Youth Justice

The integration of creative arts programs within the youth justice system represents a shift towards more holistic and rehabilitative approaches. Sources highlight the importance of service design and delivery that incorporates participant feedback, emphasizing the need for tailored interventions. For instance, 'Developing Creative Approaches in Youth Justice' underscores the significance of involving young offenders directly in the development of creative arts programs to enhance their relevance and effectiveness. Similarly, Bell Shakespeare's youth justice program utilizes workshops based on Shakespearean texts not only as a means of engaging young people but also for fostering community building among participants. Both sources agree that these interventions have potential restorative impacts beyond immediate incarceration or court proceedings by providing therapeutic benefits and social skills training.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts programs like those utilizing Shakespearean texts provide unique opportunities for rehabilitation in youth justice settings.
- Incorporating participant feedback is crucial to the success of creative arts interventions within youth justice systems.
- Workshops and performances can serve as effective tools for community building among young offenders.

## Open Questions

- How do we measure the long-term effectiveness of creative arts programs in reducing recidivism rates among juvenile delinquents?
