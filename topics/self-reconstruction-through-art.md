## Self-Reconstruction Through Art

The synthesis integrates the concept of self-reconstruction through art as a method for individuals to process grief and engage in emotional coping. Sources highlight therapeutic photography, such as the visual storytelling at Largs Pier, as an intervention that facilitates healing by enabling personal narratives and self-expression. This approach not only aids in understanding one's emotions but also offers a pathway towards rebuilding a sense of self.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography is used to facilitate emotional coping mechanisms.
- Visual art can serve as a tool for storytelling about grief.
- Engaging with artistic practices supports the process of self-reconstruction following trauma.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of using therapeutic photography in self-reconstruction?
