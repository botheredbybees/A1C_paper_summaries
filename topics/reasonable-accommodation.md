## Reasonable Accommodation

The concept of reasonable accommodation is a central component in ensuring that people with disabilities can enjoy their human rights on an equal basis with others. As highlighted across the sources, reasonable accommodation serves as a foundational principle under the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), which mandates that states take appropriate measures to ensure that individuals with disabilities have access to all aspects of society without discrimination. The CRPD emphasizes that reasonable accommodation is a dynamic process, requiring ongoing adaptation and implementation based on individual needs rather than being static or one-size-fits-all solutions. However, the extent to which this principle is effectively implemented varies widely across different contexts and jurisdictions.

## Key Findings

- The Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities mandates reasonable accommodation as a key human right for individuals with disabilities.
- Reasonable accommodation is a dynamic process requiring continuous adaptation based on individual needs.
- Implementation of reasonable accommodation varies significantly across different regions and institutions.
