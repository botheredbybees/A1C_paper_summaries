## National Arts and Health Framework

The National Arts and Health Framework is a strategic initiative aimed at integrating arts into public health practices. Sources highlight its role in enhancing health outcomes through art engagement, emphasizing the importance of evidence-based policy development and sector engagement. The framework seeks to address social prescribing and health-environment integration by fostering collaboration between healthcare providers and creative artists. While there is agreement on the necessity for a structured approach to leverage arts for public health benefits, sources also highlight the need for clearer definitions between therapeutic art interventions and general artistic engagements within this context.

## Key Findings

- The National Arts and Health Framework aims to integrate arts into public health practices.
- Evidence-based policy development and sector engagement are crucial components of the framework.
- Social prescribing is a key area where the framework seeks to improve health outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can the distinction between art therapy and general artistic engagement be clearly defined within the National Arts and Health Framework?
