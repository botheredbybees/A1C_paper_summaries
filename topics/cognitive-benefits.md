## Cognitive Benefits

The sources collectively highlight the significant cognitive benefits derived from engaging in arts activities during adolescence. The concept of Arts on Prescription is introduced as a strategic intervention aimed at leveraging the therapeutic and developmental advantages of art-making. This approach underscores the role of arts in fostering identity formation, enhancing emotional resilience, and developing socio-emotional skills. However, there are nuances regarding the specific types of cognitive improvements associated with different forms of artistic engagement, which adds complexity to understanding its broader impact.

## Key Findings

- Arts on Prescription is an intervention that promotes cognitive benefits.
- Engagement in arts activities contributes significantly to identity formation and emotional resilience during adolescence.
- Socio-emotional skills are strengthened through various forms of art-making.

## Open Questions

- What specific types of cognitive improvements correlate with particular forms of artistic engagement?
