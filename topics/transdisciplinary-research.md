## Transdisciplinary Research

The sources highlight the challenges and opportunities inherent in conducting transdisciplinary research within the realm of international migration studies. Both texts emphasize the importance of visual and participatory arts-based methodologies, suggesting that these approaches can offer unique insights into cultural phenomena associated with migration by engaging diverse communities directly in the research process. A consistent theme across both documents is the need for multidisciplinary collaboration to effectively address complex issues surrounding migration and change. However, there is a noted tension regarding how to balance disciplinary expertise while maintaining openness to new forms of knowledge production that go beyond traditional academic boundaries. This transdisciplinary approach aims at rehumanizing migrants by shifting away from reductionist views and broadening cultural definitions to include more diverse perspectives.

## Key Findings

- Transdisciplinary research in migration studies benefits from visual and participatory arts-based methodologies.
- Multidisciplinary collaboration is essential for addressing complex issues related to international migration.
- Rehumanization of migrants through transdisciplinary approaches challenges traditional academic boundaries.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers effectively balance disciplinary expertise with openness to new forms of knowledge production in transdisciplinary research?
