## Creative Arts Wellbeing

The integration of creative arts in wellbeing programs, as exemplified by the Active Energy Project for older adults, underscores a multifaceted approach to enhancing quality of life. This project highlights the community value of experience and suggests that engaging in creative activities can lead to significant improvements in psychological health and social connectedness among seniors. The findings align with broader literature indicating that creative arts interventions not only address individual needs but also foster a sense of belonging within communities. However, there is inconsistency across studies regarding the long-term efficacy of such programs without sustained community engagement.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions improve psychological health and social connectedness in older adults.
- Community value of experience plays a crucial role in the success of creative arts programs for wellbeing.
- Engagement in creative activities contributes to enhanced quality of life models.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of creative arts programs without sustained community involvement?
