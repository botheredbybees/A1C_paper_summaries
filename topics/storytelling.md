## Storytelling Across Domains

The concept of storytelling is explored across various domains in the provided sources. In the legal context (Source 1), storytelling serves as an essential editing technique and feedback mechanism for developing persuasive claims, highlighting its role in communication and persuasion. Moving to healthcare (Source 2), storytelling emerges as a powerful tool in dementia care interventions, fostering human identity through narrative therapy that enhances quality of life for individuals suffering from memory loss. In the realm of child psychology (Source 3), storytelling methods, such as puppet shows, are investigated as therapeutic tools aimed at addressing children's behavioral problems using a quasi-experimental design framework to assess their effectiveness. Despite differences in application—legal persuasion, narrative therapy for dementia care, and behavioral interventions for youth—storytelling consistently serves as an intervention enhancing communication, empathy, and therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Storytelling is widely recognized across various fields as a powerful communicative tool.
- In healthcare settings, storytelling aids in the identification of personal identity among dementia patients.
- For children with behavioral issues, storytelling can be an effective intervention method.

## Open Questions
