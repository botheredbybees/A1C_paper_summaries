## Homelessness

The experiences and effects of homelessness are multifaceted, involving emotional and social dimensions. Source 1 explores the concept through a photo essay focusing on Donna's journey, highlighting themes of emotional regulation, creative expression, and self-acceptance as she navigates her situation. This source underscores how individuals dealing with homelessness often must cope with profound psychological impacts while finding ways to express themselves creatively. In contrast, Source 2 shifts focus towards the impact of community support through a choir for homeless people (Choir of Hard Knocks), emphasizing community building and personal transformation. It demonstrates that structured group activities can offer significant emotional and social benefits, such as fostering a sense of belonging and providing platforms for individual growth.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression serves as an essential coping mechanism for individuals experiencing homelessness.
- Community-building initiatives play a critical role in the personal transformation of homeless populations.

## Open Questions

- How do community-based interventions like choirs contribute to long-term stability and integration of formerly homeless individuals into society?
