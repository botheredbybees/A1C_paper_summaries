## Integrated Care

The concept of integrated care emerges as a crucial intervention in healthcare settings, emphasizing the coordination and collaboration between various services to address both medical and non-medical needs. Social prescribing, specifically highlighted in Source 1, serves as an integral component of integrated care by connecting patients with community-based resources that can help manage long-term conditions beyond traditional medical treatment. The report underscores the importance of addressing socioeconomic factors that influence health outcomes through tailored interventions such as social prescribing. While there is a consensus on the potential benefits of integrating these services to improve patient-centered care, challenges remain in effectively implementing and scaling up integrated care models across diverse populations and healthcare systems.

## Key Findings

- Social prescribing can enhance access to community resources that address non-medical needs affecting health outcomes.
- Integrated care aims to coordinate medical and social support services for comprehensive patient management.
- Addressing socioeconomic factors is critical in the design of effective integrated care interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific barriers to implementing social prescribing within existing healthcare systems, and how can these be overcome?
