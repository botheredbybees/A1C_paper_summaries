## Long-Term Benefits of Arts in Health and Wellbeing

The exploration of arts as an intervention for enhancing health and wellbeing reveals a consistent emphasis on its ability to yield long-term benefits, particularly in mental health outcomes. Sources highlight the potential for sustained positive impacts beyond immediate engagement with artistic activities, such as improved mood, reduced anxiety, and enhanced resilience against stress-related disorders. Moreover, there is evidence suggesting that arts interventions can influence genetic expression in ways that promote better overall health status over extended periods. However, while these studies demonstrate promising results, they also underscore the need for further research to understand more precisely how and why these long-term benefits occur.

## Key Findings

- Arts interventions are associated with improved mental health outcomes
- Long-term benefits of arts in health may extend beyond immediate effects
- Influence on genetic expression suggests broader health benefits

## Open Questions

- What mechanisms explain the long-term impacts of arts on health and wellbeing?
