## Violence Prevention Through Movement

The concept of preventing violence through movement is explored across various sources that emphasize the therapeutic benefits of dance and physical activity. Donna Newman Bluestein's work on Dance Therapy underscores the importance of integrating personality in treatment approaches to address underlying issues that may contribute to violent behavior. This intervention aims at enhancing empathy-building skills among individuals, which can be crucial for reducing aggressive tendencies within communities. The integration of movement as a medium for emotional and psychological expression offers a unique way to facilitate non-verbal communication and self-awareness, thereby serving as an effective preventive measure against violence.

## Key Findings

- Movement-based interventions like dance therapy are effective in preventing violent behavior by addressing underlying issues through empathy-building exercises.
- Integration of personality within movement therapies helps tailor prevention strategies that resonate with individual psychological needs.
