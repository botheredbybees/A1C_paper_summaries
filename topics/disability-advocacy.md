## Disability Advocacy

Sources highlight two dimensions of disability advocacy: the linguistic approach and the narrative empowerment. The PWDA Language Guide emphasizes the importance of language choices that reflect respect for individuals with disabilities, promoting person-first or identity-first language over stigmatizing 'ableist' terms. This underscores a broader movement in disability rights to control narratives about their own experiences rather than having them dictated by external societal perceptions. Conversely, the story of the French horn player highlights individual resilience and creativity in overcoming physical challenges, showcasing how people with disabilities can defy stereotypes through exceptional achievements that challenge traditional views on capability and limitation.

## Key Findings

- The choice of language used to discuss disability significantly impacts societal perceptions and respect for individuals with disabilities
- Personal stories that highlight achievement and resilience among individuals with disabilities serve as powerful tools in advocating for greater acceptance and understanding within society

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective strategies for integrating linguistic best practices into mainstream media and public discourse to promote positive perceptions of disability?
