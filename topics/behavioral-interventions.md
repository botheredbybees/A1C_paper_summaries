## Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy

The synthesis of the provided sources reveals that Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy (ICPT) is a specialized form of therapy designed for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The pilot study discussed demonstrates its potential efficacy in addressing behavioral challenges faced by these children. ICPT stands out as an intervention that is child-led, focusing on play to facilitate communication and social skills development. Despite promising preliminary results, there remains a need for further research into the long-term impacts of such interventions.

## Key Findings

- Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy is specifically tailored for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder.
- This therapy utilizes child-led play as a primary method to engage and support social and communication skills in children with ASD.
- Initial studies suggest that ICPT can effectively address behavioral challenges faced by children on the autism spectrum.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy on the behavior, communication, and social skills development of children with Autism Spectrum Disorder?
