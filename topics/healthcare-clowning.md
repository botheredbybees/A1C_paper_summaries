## Healthcare Clowning

The concept of healthcare clowning, a form of artistic intervention in medical settings, has evolved from initial initiatives like the 'Clown Care' program to independent organizations that focus on delivering humor and joy during patient care. This therapeutic approach aims to improve the emotional well-being and overall experience for patients, their families, and hospital staff. Source 2 highlights the adaptability of healthcare clowning during challenging times such as the COVID-19 pandemic, demonstrating its relevance in diverse medical contexts across Europe. Meanwhile, Source 3 emphasizes the role of participatory action research and facilitative communication to enhance collaboration between various stakeholders involved in long-term dementia care settings. Despite differences in application contexts, all sources underscore healthcare clowning's potential to serve as a supportive intervention that complements traditional medical treatments by fostering positive emotional climates.

## Key Findings

- Healthcare clowning has evolved from initial programs like 'Clown Care' into diverse independent organizations across Europe.
- The intervention adapts well to challenging times, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, demonstrating its versatility and ongoing relevance in healthcare.
- Facilitative communication and participatory action research are crucial for enhancing collaboration between staff, family members, and patients.

## Open Questions
