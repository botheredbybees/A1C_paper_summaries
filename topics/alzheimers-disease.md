## Alzheimer's Disease Management and Prevention

The study of Alzheimer’s disease (AD) management and prevention encompasses a broad spectrum of interventions ranging from pharmacological to lifestyle modifications. Source 1 highlights the potential of antidiabetic drugs in mitigating AD through their effects on tau pathology, neuroinflammation, and cognitive function based on preclinical studies. However, there is a call for further clinical research to confirm these promising findings.

Source 2 introduces personalized risk profiling as an approach to identify modifiable factors contributing to AD among asymptomatic individuals. Key identified risks include sleep disorders, low physical activity levels, poor diet, and high blood pressure, emphasizing the importance of lifestyle changes in reducing disease progression risk.

Source 3 elaborates on how diet, physical activity, and sleep can influence autophagy-mediated pathways critical for neuronal health, suggesting non-pharmacological strategies as viable options to delay AD onset or progress. Yet, there is a need for better understanding of brain-specific markers to measure intervention effectiveness accurately.

Source 4 reviews the therapeutic potential of natural products in treating AD by mitigating oxidative stress and inflammation while enhancing synaptic plasticity. Findings from herbal medicines and plant-derived compounds like curcumin and ginsenosides indicate promising benefits, particularly when used in conjunction with conventional treatments.

Lastly, Source 5 provides foundational knowledge about AD mechanisms, including beta-amyloid plaques and tau protein tangles that disrupt neural communication leading to cognitive decline. Current treatment focuses on slowing disease progression rather than curing it, necessitating a comprehensive approach combining multiple interventions.

## Key Findings

- Antidiabetic drugs show potential in mitigating Alzheimer's disease through preclinical studies but require further clinical research for confirmation.
- Personalized risk profiling identifies modifiable factors such as lifestyle changes that can reduce the progression of Alzheimer’s disease among asymptomatic individuals.
- Non-pharmacological strategies like diet, exercise, and sleep regulation influence autophagy-mediated pathways critical to neuronal health and AD prevention.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective combinations of pharmacological treatments and lifestyle interventions for delaying or preventing Alzheimer's disease progression?
