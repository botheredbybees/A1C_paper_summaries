## Anonymity Preservation

The concept of anonymity preservation is crucial across research methodologies and data analysis in arts and health studies. The Vancouver Protocol, aimed at protecting the confidentiality of participants' identities during dissemination, highlights a significant ethical consideration when reporting on studies involving sensitive or private information. While there is agreement about the importance of preserving anonymity to protect participants, there are also tensions regarding transparency versus privacy, suggesting that researchers must carefully balance these aspects to ensure both ethical integrity and scientific rigor in their work.

## Key Findings

- Anonymity preservation is a critical component of ethical research practices in arts and health studies.
- The Vancouver Protocol provides guidance on maintaining participant confidentiality during the reporting process.
- Balancing transparency with privacy presents a significant challenge for researchers.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers maintain both transparency and anonymity effectively when dealing with sensitive information?
