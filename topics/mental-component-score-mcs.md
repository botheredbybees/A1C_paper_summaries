## Health-Related Quality Of Life

The sources collectively explore how health-related quality of life (HRQOL) is influenced by various factors, particularly focusing on the mental and physical aspects as measured by surveys like SF-36 and SF-12. The mental component score (MCS), a key metric in these assessments, reflects psychological well-being and emotional health, while the physical component score (PCS) evaluates physical health status. Music interventions have been shown to improve HRQOL through enhanced emotional well-being and reduced stress, aligning with findings that highlight improvements in MCS but varying outcomes for PCS.

## Key Findings

- Music interventions can significantly enhance mental health as measured by the Mental Component Score (MCS)
- Health-related quality of life is assessed using surveys such as SF-36 and SF-12
- There are inconsistent results regarding the impact of music interventions on physical health

## Open Questions

- What additional factors influence the differential impact of music interventions on mental versus physical component scores?
