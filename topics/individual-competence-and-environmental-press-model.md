## Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model

The Individual Competence and Environmental Press (ICEP) model posits that the relationship between an individual's capabilities and their environment significantly influences behavior, particularly in contexts of reduced cognitive function such as dementia care. This theory contrasts with earlier models like the Progressively Lowered Threshold Model which emphasizes age-related decline rather than environmental impact. The ICEP model suggests designing environments to accommodate varying levels of individual competence, thereby maintaining functional independence for longer periods. Sources highlight how this theoretical framework provides a nuanced understanding of the interaction between people and their surroundings in care settings.

## Key Findings

- The Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model emphasizes the interaction between individuals' capabilities and their environment to influence behavior and maintain function.
- Designing environments that align with the ICEP model can support individuals with reduced cognitive abilities, promoting independence and quality of life.
- This model offers a more dynamic perspective compared to models focusing solely on age-related decline.

## Open Questions

- How does the Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model account for cultural differences in perceptions of competence and environmental design?
