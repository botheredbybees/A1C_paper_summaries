## Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model

The Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model emphasizes the interaction between an individual's capabilities and the demands placed on them by their environment. Both sources highlight that this model is crucial in designing dementia care settings, as it aids in understanding how physical environments can either support or challenge residents' functional abilities. The model underscores the importance of considering both the inherent competencies of individuals with dementia and the environmental factors that influence these competencies. This theoretical framework complements other models such as the Model of Place and the Progressively Lowered Thresholds for Success, suggesting a holistic approach to assessing and improving dementia care settings is necessary.

## Key Findings

- The Individual Competence and Environmental Press Model highlights the critical relationship between individual abilities and environmental demands in dementia care.
- This model supports a person-centered design philosophy in creating dementia-friendly spaces.
- Complementary models such as Model of Place and Progressively Lowered Thresholds for Success offer additional perspectives on optimizing dementia care environments.
