## Long-term Care Interventions

The synthesis of the provided sources highlights two key long-term care interventions aimed at enhancing the quality of life for residents with dementia. Source 1 focuses on the implementation and results of the Music & Memory Program, which uses personalized music listening to improve residents' quality of life in long-term care facilities. In contrast, Source 2 examines person-centered versus traditional visual arts activities, emphasizing their impact on well-being indicators such as stigma reduction and overall resident happiness. Both sources underscore the importance of tailored approaches that recognize individual preferences and histories, suggesting a shift towards more personalized and holistic care practices within long-term care settings.

## Key Findings

- Personalized music listening significantly enhances residents' quality of life in long-term care facilities.
- Person-centered care activities lead to improved well-being indicators for individuals with dementia, reducing stigma and enhancing happiness.

## Open Questions

- What are the comparative benefits between personalized music therapy and person-centered visual arts activities in terms of overall resident satisfaction and health outcomes?
