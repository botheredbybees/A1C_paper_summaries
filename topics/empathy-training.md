## Empathy Training in Medical Education

Empathy training is a growing intervention in medical education that aims to enhance doctor-patient relationships through various arts-based methods. Sources reveal the integration of comics, visual art instruction, and humanities into curricula as effective tools for empathy development. 'Graphic Medicine' highlights how comics can serve as a powerful medium for community-building and storytelling in healthcare settings, fostering deeper understanding between medical professionals and patients. Another source emphasizes that incorporating cultural sensitivity and ethical reflection through arts and humanities education helps cultivate empathetic care among future doctors. Visual thinking strategies (VTS) are also shown to be beneficial; they encourage detailed observation skills which can translate into better patient communication and understanding of biases. The inclusion of such courses in medical schools not only enhances emotional intelligence but also leads to improved patient satisfaction by developing robust communication skills.

## Key Findings

- Comics, visual arts, and humanities education are effective tools for empathy training in medical students.
- Enhancing cultural sensitivity and ethical reflection through arts-based methods improves empathetic care practices.
- Visual thinking strategies (VTS) aid in the development of observation and communication skills crucial for patient interaction.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of these interventions be measured longitudinally to assess their impact on clinical practice?
