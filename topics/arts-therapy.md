## Arts Therapy

The concept of arts therapy is explored across several sources, highlighting its benefits for diverse populations such as persons with dementia and children/young people dealing with mental health issues. Sources identify self-expression and social inclusion as central to enhancing quality of life through art, music, and visual interventions. Notably, engagement in structured group arts activities supports the development of resilience and emotional regulation among younger participants while providing memory enhancement for those living with dementia. However, there are varying approaches and methods used across different age groups and conditions, indicating a need for tailored strategies.

## Key Findings

- Self-expression through various art forms can improve social inclusion and mental well-being in diverse populations.
- Visual arts interventions are beneficial for the quality of life of people living with dementia.
- Structured group arts activities contribute to resilience-building among children and young people dealing with mental health issues.

## Open Questions

- What specific art forms and methods are most effective for enhancing therapeutic outcomes in different demographic groups?
