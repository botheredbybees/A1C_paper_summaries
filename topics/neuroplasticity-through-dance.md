## Neuroplasticity Through Dance

The sources collectively highlight the use of dance and music as therapeutic methods to promote neuroplasticity and enhance overall health. These art-based programs are described in various contexts, including live music interventions and structured dance routines. The evidence underscores the potential for these activities to positively influence brain function through physical movement and cognitive engagement. However, there is a need to further investigate the specific mechanisms by which dance induces neuroplastic changes, as well as its applicability across different populations.

## Key Findings

- Dance and music are effective in inducing neuroplasticity due to their integrative nature involving both physical movement and cognitive processing.
- Live music interventions complement structured dance programs, enhancing their therapeutic benefits.
- There is a growing interest in incorporating art-based therapies into health programmes for their mental and physical health benefits.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neurobiological mechanisms by which dance promotes neuroplasticity?
