## Neurotransmitter Pathways in Mental Health

The exploration of neurotransmitter pathways in mental health disorders such as Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) highlights the complex interplay between biological and psychological factors. Source 1 discusses how PTSD affects the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, leading to alterations in key neurotransmitters involved in stress response regulation like cortisol and norepinephrine. These changes are part of a broader neurobiological impact that includes genetic predispositions influencing the susceptibility or resilience to developing PTSD after traumatic events.

## Key Findings

- Alterations in neurotransmitter pathways play a critical role in the development and maintenance of PTSD.
- The HPA axis is significantly impacted in individuals suffering from PTSD, indicating dysregulation within stress response systems.
- Genetic factors contribute to individual differences in susceptibility or resilience to PTSD following trauma exposure.
