## Verbal Association in Musical Aesthetics

The study of verbal association in the context of music aesthetics explores how individuals describe and conceptualize their experiences with music. The key concept of 'aesthetic experience' underlines the subjective nature of musical appreciation, suggesting that people's emotional responses to music are not only sensory but also involve cognitive processes such as verbalization and interpretation. This investigation highlights the role of language in capturing the nuances of musical beauty, indicating that verbal associations play a crucial part in shaping personal perceptions of music. The findings suggest that there is a significant variation in how individuals articulate their aesthetic experiences, pointing to the complexity of the relationship between music perception and linguistic expression.

## Key Findings

- Verbal association is an integral component of aesthetic experience with music.
- The use of language varies significantly among individuals when describing musical beauty.
- Subjective interpretation of music through verbal means influences personal appreciation.

## Open Questions

- How does cultural background affect the ways in which people verbally associate their emotional responses to music?
