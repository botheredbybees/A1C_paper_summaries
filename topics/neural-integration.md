## Neural Integration in Early Development

The study of neural integration during early development is crucial for understanding how brain networks form and function. The source focuses on the use of electroencephalography (EEG) to assess functional connectivity, a key aspect of neural integration, in infants at risk for Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). This methodological approach highlights the importance of studying neural processes as they develop before behavioral symptoms manifest. By examining EEG data from the first year of life, researchers can identify potential biomarkers or patterns that differentiate between typical and atypical development trajectories. The research underscores the complexity of neural integration, which involves the coordination and communication between different brain regions to perform cognitive functions.

## Key Findings

- Functional connectivity measured through EEG is a valuable tool for studying early neural integration in infants.
- Identifying patterns of neural integration in infancy can provide insights into the development of neurodevelopmental disorders like ASD.
- EEG studies in this age group offer unique opportunities to explore the precursors and predictors of later developmental outcomes.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms underlying altered functional connectivity in infants at risk for ASD, and how do these translate into behavioral symptoms over time?
