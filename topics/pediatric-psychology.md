## Puppet Play Therapy and Fear of Clowns in Pediatric Psychology

Pediatric psychology interventions aim to address the emotional and psychological needs of children in healthcare settings. Two distinct therapeutic approaches have been explored: puppet play therapy and medical clown intervention. Puppet play therapy, as discussed in one study conducted within an emergency pediatric dental clinic, employs a 'tell-show-do' technique to assist children with managing their anxiety during procedures, using puppets to model the experience. This method is evaluated through the Venham Anxiety Rating Scale (VARS), which effectively measures the reduction of anxiety levels among young patients. In contrast, another study examines the fear of clowns in hospitalized children and evaluates the effectiveness of medical clown interventions on psychological well-being. While both methods involve engaging therapeutic practices designed to alleviate distress, they differ significantly in approach, with puppet play therapy focusing more on procedural education through play while medical clown intervention seeks to uplift spirits and provide comfort through humor.

## Key Findings

- Puppet play therapy effectively reduces anxiety levels measured by the Venham Anxiety Rating Scale among pediatric dental patients
- Medical clown interventions can positively impact psychological well-being in hospitalized children despite some having a fear of clowns

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences and individual preferences affect the efficacy of puppet play therapy versus medical clown intervention?
