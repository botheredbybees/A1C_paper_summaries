## Puberty

The onset of puberty marks a significant phase in human development where the interplay between hormonal changes and brain maturation becomes crucial. Sources highlight that during this period, there is substantial neural circuitry maturation which influences cognitive functions such as decision-making, impulse control, and emotional regulation. Puberty's impact on the adolescent brain is multifaceted, with evidence suggesting differential effects based on gender and timing of puberty onset. Despite these insights, several gaps remain regarding the long-term consequences of pubertal changes on mental health and cognitive development.

## Key Findings

- Puberty triggers significant neural circuitry maturation.
- Cognitive functions such as decision-making are influenced by hormonal changes during puberty.
- Gender differences exist in how the adolescent brain responds to pubertal hormones.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of pubertal changes on cognitive and mental health outcomes?
