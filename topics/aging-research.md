## Personalized Risk Profiling And Physical Activity In Aging Research

This synthesis integrates findings from two aging-related research studies. The first study focuses on personalized Alzheimer's disease risk profiling among healthy middle-aged adults, revealing significant modifiable factors such as sleep disorders and low physical activity levels contributing to ADRD risk. Conversely, the second study delves into the relationship between physical activity, cognitive function, brain morphology, and AD biomarkers in older adults, highlighting that higher cardiorespiratory fitness (CRF) correlates with greater hippocampal subfield volumes, better cognitive performance, and lower neurofilament light chain levels, suggesting a protective role of exercise. Both studies underscore the importance of personalized approaches and lifestyle interventions to mitigate cognitive decline associated with aging.

## Key Findings

- Personalized risk profiling identifies significant modifiable factors contributing to Alzheimer's disease risk in healthy middle-aged individuals.
- Higher cardiorespiratory fitness levels are associated with better cognitive performance, greater hippocampal subfield volumes, and lower neurofilament light chain levels among older adults engaged in moderate-to-vigorous physical activity.
- Modifiable lifestyle interventions such as addressing sleep disorders and increasing physical activity can potentially mitigate Alzheimer's disease risk.

## Open Questions

- How do the specific modifiable factors identified through personalized risk profiling interact with genetic predispositions to affect Alzheimer's disease risk?
