## Music Therapy in Dementia Care

The integration of music therapy across dementia care interventions highlights its multifaceted benefits. Source 1 emphasizes the use of musical memory and reminiscence bump to enhance emotional wellbeing, suggesting that nostalgic songs can evoke positive emotions associated with past experiences, which is particularly beneficial for individuals with limited current memory capacity due to dementia. Conversely, Source 2 delves into the social aspect of music therapy by highlighting singing as a means for emotional expression, cognitive stimulation, and stress reduction. Both sources illustrate how engaging in musical activities not only triggers personal memories but also fosters social interactions and mental health improvements, underscoring the holistic approach that music therapy can offer to dementia patients.

## Key Findings

- Musical memory and reminiscence bump improve emotional wellbeing by evoking positive past experiences.
- Singing enhances emotional expression, cognitive function, and stress reduction in dementia care.

## Open Questions

- How does the effectiveness of music therapy vary based on cultural background or personal musical preferences?
