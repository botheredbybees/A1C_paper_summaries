## Emotional Expression Through Therapeutic Interventions

The integration of emotional expression through various therapeutic interventions reveals a nuanced landscape where non-verbal communication plays a crucial role in facilitating emotional articulation. Sources highlighting the use of singing and music-based interventions (Source 2) emphasize their ability to reduce stress hormones, increase melatonin levels, and foster community support networks. These effects are paralleled by puppetry as a medium for emotional expression, with sources detailing how puppets can serve as diagnostic tools or enable psychological catharsis (Sources 4-9). Puppet therapy appears particularly adept at engaging non-verbal communication and mediated expression techniques that cater to diverse cultural sensitivities, thereby enhancing therapeutic engagement among participants. However, while these interventions demonstrate significant promise in facilitating emotional articulation and supporting mental wellbeing, there is a noted tension regarding the extent to which such methods can effectively address deep-seated emotional dysregulation without verbalization.

## Key Findings

- Music-based interventions reduce stress hormones and foster community support networks.
- Puppet therapy facilitates non-verbal communication and psychological catharsis.
- Non-verbal expression techniques are culturally sensitive and adaptable across populations.

## Open Questions

- Can emotional dysregulation be effectively addressed through non-verbal therapeutic methods alone?
