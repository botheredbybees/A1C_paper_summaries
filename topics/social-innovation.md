## Social Innovation Through Creative Thinking

The Active Energy project highlights the potential of creative thinking to engage older people and foster social innovation. This initiative leverages democratization-of-technology by making technology accessible for diverse age groups, thereby breaking down traditional barriers that limit access to such tools primarily for younger generations. The project also promotes intergenerational partnerships as a means to enhance social cohesion and creativity within communities. However, there is an underlying tension regarding the sustainability of these initiatives beyond the initial project timeline, especially considering the rapid pace at which technology evolves and the need for continuous engagement strategies.

## Key Findings

- Creative thinking initiatives can effectively engage older people in technological advancements through democratization-of-technology.
- Intergenerational partnerships fostered by such projects enhance social cohesion within communities.
- Sustainability of social innovation initiatives remains a challenge beyond project timelines.

## Open Questions

- How to sustain intergenerational partnerships and the benefits they bring long-term?
