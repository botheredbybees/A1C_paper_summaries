## Lifestyle-Based Interventions for Mental Health and Neurodegenerative Diseases

The sources highlight the significant role of lifestyle interventions in managing major depressive disorder (MDD) and neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease. Source 1 emphasizes non-pharmacological strategies, particularly diet, physical activity, and sleep regulation, for enhancing autophagy-mediated pathways which can mitigate protein aggregation—a key factor in Alzheimer’s progression. Conversely, source 2 focuses on lifestyle-based mental health care within a biopsychosocial-cultural framework to address MDD. Both sources underscore the importance of integrating multiple factors (diet, exercise, sleep) into intervention strategies but differ in their specific application: one targets physical and biochemical pathways, while the other emphasizes psychological and social dimensions.

## Key Findings

- Lifestyle interventions such as diet, exercise, and sleep can be effective non-pharmacological treatments for both Alzheimer's disease and major depressive disorder.
- Autophagy-mediated pathways are crucial in mitigating protein aggregation associated with neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's.

## Open Questions

- How do lifestyle interventions that target autophagy pathways interact with psychological approaches to mental health care?
