## Child-Centered Therapy

Child-centered therapy emphasizes a client-led approach where the therapist provides a supportive environment for children to express themselves and explore issues. Puppet play therapy, as discussed in 'Puppet Play Therapy: A Practical Guidebook,' is one such modality that uses puppets to facilitate communication between children and therapists. This technique can be particularly effective with younger clients who may find traditional talk therapies less engaging or challenging. The book outlines a series of sessions designed around the Berkeley Puppet Interview, which helps in understanding the child's perspective on various issues. While solution-focused brief therapy shares some goals like empowering the client to make changes, it differs significantly in its focus and methods compared to puppet play therapy.

## Key Findings

- Puppet play therapy is a form of child-centered intervention that uses puppets as a tool for communication between children and therapists.
- The Berkeley Puppet Interview is an integral part of puppet play therapy sessions aimed at understanding the child's viewpoint on issues being addressed.
- Child-centered therapy aims to provide a supportive space where the child can freely express themselves, fostering growth and healing.
