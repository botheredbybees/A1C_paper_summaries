## Arts and Cultural Value

The value of arts is explored through cultural and public perspectives in Australia and Aotearoa New Zealand, emphasizing thematic analysis and indigenous viewpoints. This theoretical framework highlights the intrinsic worth of artistic expression within communities. Additionally, participants from an arts gymnasium at Belgrade Theatre Coventry demonstrate how engaging with the arts can enhance self-confidence and a sense of belonging. The research on arts, culture, migration, and change further elaborates on multidisciplinary approaches to understanding cultural transitions and the role of participatory arts-based methodologies in studying these phenomena.

## Key Findings

- The intrinsic value of arts is recognized from both public and cultural perspectives
- Participation in arts activities can improve individual self-esteem and social integration
- Multidisciplinary research approaches are necessary to understand complex interactions between art, culture, migration, and societal change

## Open Questions

- How do multidisciplinary approaches integrate indigenous perspectives on the value of arts?
