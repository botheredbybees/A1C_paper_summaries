## Promises Of Marriage

The concept of 'promises of marriage' discussed in the literature explores how young individuals commit to each other through informal engagements or vows. These promises are often made with high expectations and involve pledges regarding personal values such as virginity until marriage. However, this notion contrasts sharply with realities like societal norms, educational gaps, particularly around sexual health education, which can affect the fulfillment of these commitments. The literature emphasizes the importance of cultural context in understanding these promises, highlighting how they are perceived differently within various communities.

## Key Findings

- Promises of marriage among young individuals often involve vows regarding personal values like virginity until marriage.
- Cultural and societal norms play a significant role in shaping perceptions around premarital sexual relationships and commitments.
- There is a lack or inadequacy in sexual health education which can lead to unrealistic expectations about marriage.

## Open Questions

- How do variations in sexual health education programs across different regions impact the realization of 'promises of marriage'?
