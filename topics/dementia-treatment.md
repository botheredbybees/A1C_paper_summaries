## Therapeutic Interventions for Dementia

Recent studies have highlighted the potential benefits of creative and physical interventions in improving the quality of life (QoL) for individuals with dementia. Source 1 examines drama therapy, noting its impact on self-awareness among older adults suffering from dementia. This form of expressive therapy is suggested to enhance emotional expression and social interaction, contributing positively to QoL. Meanwhile, Source 2 presents a randomized controlled trial focusing on dance movement therapy (DMT) and physical exercise for those with mild dementia. It highlights that both DMT and physical exercises can yield significant psychophysiological benefits, such as reduced stress levels and improved cognitive function, but it also suggests that the specific therapeutic effects may differ between these approaches.

Despite the positive outcomes reported in both studies, there is a need to further investigate how different forms of therapy interact with varying stages of dementia. The mixed-method approach employed in Source 1, combining qualitative and quantitative analysis, offers a nuanced understanding compared to the clinical trial design used in Source 2, which focuses more on measurable physiological changes.

## Key Findings

- Drama therapy enhances self-awareness and QoL among older adults with dementia.
- Both dance movement therapy and physical exercise offer significant psychophysiological benefits for those with mild dementia.
- The efficacy of therapeutic interventions may vary depending on the severity and progression of dementia.

## Open Questions

- How do different stages of dementia influence the effectiveness of drama therapy and other creative therapies?
