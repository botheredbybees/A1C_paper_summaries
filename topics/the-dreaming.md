## The Dreaming in Indigenous Arts and Mental Wellbeing

The Dreaming is a central concept within Indigenous Australian culture that encompasses spiritual beliefs, creation stories, and cultural practices. It serves as a framework for understanding the world and guiding behavior, including mental health interventions. Sources highlight its role in mental wellbeing through artistic expressions such as painting, storytelling, and dance. These arts not only provide therapeutic benefits but also facilitate healing from historical trauma by reclaiming cultural identity and fostering community connection. However, there is a need to ensure that these practices are developed collaboratively with Indigenous communities (co-design) to respect their unique cultural contexts and avoid misrepresentation.

## Key Findings

- The Dreaming plays a vital role in mental health interventions for Indigenous Australians.
- Artistic expressions related to The Dreaming can serve as therapeutic tools.
- Co-design approaches are essential when implementing interventions based on The Dreaming.

## Open Questions

- How can non-Indigenous practitioners effectively engage with and support the integration of The Dreaming in mental health practices without cultural insensitivity?
