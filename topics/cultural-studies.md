## Cultural Studies and Applied Music

This synthesis explores the intersection of cultural studies with applied music research. Source 1 emphasizes the role of ethnomusicology and performance studies in understanding sustainability within music cultures, suggesting a need for arts-based community development initiatives that foster local culture's longevity. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on empirical research advances in the field of 'flow,' particularly Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi’s theory applied to various disciplines including music performance and education. While these sources diverge in their primary focus—one being qualitative cultural studies and the other quantitative empirical studies—they converge on the importance of practical application, whether it be through community engagement or improving individual musical experiences. Both underscore the dynamic nature of studying human interactions with music within cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Ethnomusicology plays a pivotal role in understanding and preserving local music cultures.
- Research in 'flow' enhances our comprehension of optimal experience during music performance.
- Arts-based community development is crucial for sustainable cultural practices.

## Open Questions

- How can empirical research on 'flow' be integrated with qualitative studies from ethnomusicology to provide a more comprehensive understanding of musical experiences?
