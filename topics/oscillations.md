## Oscillatory Rhythms in Motor Function

This review synthesizes research on the impact of rhythmic auditory cueing (RAC) on gait function in individuals with Parkinson's disease. Studies highlight β oscillations as a key neural signature associated with motor inhibition, suggesting that RAC may modulate these oscillatory patterns to enhance movement. The role of motor imagery is also discussed, emphasizing how cognitive strategies can complement rhythmic cues to improve gait performance. However, the exact mechanisms through which RAC influences β oscillations and subsequent improvements in motor function remain underexplored.

## Key Findings

- Rhythmic auditory cueing enhances gait function in Parkinson's disease.
- β oscillations are associated with motor inhibition in Parkinson's patients.
- Motor imagery can complement rhythmic cues to improve movement performance.

## Open Questions

- What is the precise mechanism through which RAC influences β oscillatory patterns and subsequent improvements in motor function?
