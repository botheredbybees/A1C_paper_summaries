## Sensory Processing Recovery

The integration of arts-based interventions such as listening to music post-stroke aims to enhance sensory processing recovery. This approach seeks to address not only the physical and cognitive aspects of stroke recovery but also social responsiveness in individuals with autism, highlighting a broader applicability beyond neurology. However, while these interventions show promise in enhancing sensory processing abilities, there remains a need for more comprehensive research exploring their long-term effectiveness and underlying mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can enhance sensory processing recovery post-stroke.
- Arts-based interventions may have broader applications than neurological recovery alone.
- Enhancing social responsiveness is an additional benefit of certain sensory-processing interventions.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects and mechanisms of arts-based interventions for sensory processing recovery?
