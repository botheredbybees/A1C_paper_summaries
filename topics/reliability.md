## Reliability in Psychometric Properties

Sources one and two highlight the foundational role of reliability within psychometrics. Both sources underscore that reliability refers to the consistency or repeatability of a measurement tool, such as a psychological test or survey. The first source delineates various types of reliability (e.g., test-retest, inter-rater, internal consistency) while emphasizing its importance alongside validity for ensuring sound instrument validation. The second source presents a case study on the Chinese version of a specific assessment tool, illustrating how cultural translation impacts reliability and validity. Despite cultural adaptation challenges highlighted in the latter, both sources concur that rigorous statistical analysis is crucial to establish reliability in diverse contexts.

## Key Findings

- Reliability measures the consistency of an assessment tool over time or across different raters.
- Cultural adaptations can affect the reliability of psychometric instruments.
- Statistical methods play a critical role in establishing and validating reliability.
