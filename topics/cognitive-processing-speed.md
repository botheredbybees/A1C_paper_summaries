## Cognitive Processing Speed

The evaluation of differences in temporal synchrony between brain regions reveals significant disparities in individuals with autism compared to those with typical development. This research highlights the importance of functional brain connectivity and neural network dynamics in understanding cognitive processing speed. Studies indicate that variations in temporal synchrony are linked to autism symptom severity, suggesting a direct relationship between these neurobiological factors and cognitive function. While there is agreement on the impact of altered neural synchrony on cognitive processing, gaps remain regarding how specific interventions can enhance or normalize this process.

## Key Findings

- Variations in temporal synchrony significantly affect cognitive processing speed in individuals with autism compared to those without.
- Neural network dynamics play a critical role in modulating cognitive processing speed and are linked to symptom severity.
- Alterations in functional brain connectivity impact the efficiency of cognitive processes.

## Open Questions

- What specific interventions can effectively improve temporal synchrony and cognitive processing speed in individuals with autism?
