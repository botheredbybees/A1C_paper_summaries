## Social Determinants Of Health From An Indigenous Perspective

The discourse on Social Determinants of Health (SDH) in the context of Indigenous communities highlights a need to move beyond conventional SDH frameworks. Source 1, emphasizing Indigenous epistemologies and community sovereignty, critiques current approaches as insufficient when considering historical contexts and cultural specificity of Indigenous health issues. It argues for integrating Indigenous knowledge systems to address SDH more effectively. While there is agreement on the importance of understanding local contexts in shaping health outcomes, discrepancies arise regarding how best to incorporate traditional wisdom into contemporary public health frameworks.

## Key Findings

- The social determinants of health framework needs modification when applied to Indigenous communities due to unique cultural and historical factors.
- Incorporating Indigenous epistemologies is crucial for a holistic understanding of SDH in these contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of Indigenous knowledge systems be effectively achieved within existing social determinants of health frameworks?
