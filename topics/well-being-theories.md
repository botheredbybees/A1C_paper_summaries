## Theories of Well-being

The theories surrounding well-being are multifaceted and culturally influenced. Sources highlight two dominant traditions in the study of well-being: hedonic and eudaimonic perspectives. Hedonia, focusing on pleasure and absence of pain, contrasts sharply with eudaimonia which emphasizes personal growth and fulfillment aligned with one's potential (Sources 1 & 4). These theories are interwoven within various models like PERMA and complete mental health, aiming to integrate both hedonic and eudaimonic elements for a comprehensive understanding of well-being (Source 3). The cultural context also plays a crucial role; for instance, collectivist cultures might place more emphasis on eudaimonic happiness compared to individualist societies (Source 2). While these models offer nuanced views, they are not without critique, particularly regarding potential cultural biases in the research and application of well-being theories (Sources 3 & 4).

## Key Findings

- Hedonia focuses on pleasure maximization while eudaimonia emphasizes personal growth.
- Models integrating hedonic and eudaimonic elements provide a more holistic view of well-being.
- Cultural contexts influence the prioritization between hedonic and eudaimonic happiness.

## Open Questions

- How can cultural biases be minimized in the research and application of well-being theories?
