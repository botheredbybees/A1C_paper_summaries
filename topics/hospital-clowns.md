## Hospital Clowns

The practice of using clowns in healthcare settings, often referred to as clown therapy or therapeutic clowning, has been explored for its potential benefits in reducing stress and anxiety among patients. The use of physical comedy and social barriers transcendence, such as engaging with those who might otherwise be isolated due to their medical condition, highlights the unique role clowns can play in healthcare environments. Despite these promising applications, concerns around professionalization and the maintenance of a therapeutic clown's credibility while adhering to ethical guidelines have been raised.

## Key Findings

- Clown therapy is increasingly recognized for its ability to improve patient morale and reduce anxiety.
- Physical comedy used by clowns can help patients transcend social barriers and connect with others.
- Professionalization of therapeutic clown roles raises questions about maintaining the integrity and effectiveness of this intervention.

## Open Questions

- How does professionalization affect the credibility and impact of hospital clowns in healthcare settings?
