## Peer Education

The concept of peer education is explored in various contexts, highlighting its effectiveness and versatility across different applications such as theater and drama engagement. The case studies reviewed underscore the transformative power of involving peers as educators, particularly within marginalized or oppressed communities. Theater of the Oppressed is a powerful method that not only entertains but also educates and mobilizes community members to take action against social injustices. Laughter Therapy, another form of peer education, leverages humor to address serious issues, reducing stigma and fostering engagement among participants. These methods suggest that peer education can be adapted to fit various cultural and societal contexts while maintaining its core objective of empowering individuals through shared knowledge.

## Key Findings

- Peer education is an effective method for engaging marginalized communities in social change initiatives.
- Theater-based interventions, like Theater of the Oppressed, serve dual purposes of entertainment and education.
- Laughter Therapy can be used to address sensitive topics by reducing barriers such as stigma and fear.

## Open Questions
