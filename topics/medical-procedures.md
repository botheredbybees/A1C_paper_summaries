## Hospital Clowning in Pediatric Medicine

Sources highlight hospital clowning as an intervention to reduce pediatric anxiety and pain. The network meta-analysis (Source 1) demonstrates that this method effectively alleviates patient anxiety, with notable benefits observed across various medical contexts such as anesthesia induction, surgery preparation, and post-operative recovery phases. Source 2 further corroborates these findings by emphasizing the role of therapeutic clowns in engaging children through distraction therapy, thereby enhancing overall pain management outcomes and potentially reducing parental stress associated with pediatric procedures.

## Key Findings

- Hospital clowning significantly reduces anxiety levels among pediatric patients undergoing medical procedures.
- Therapeutic clowns employ distraction techniques to improve pain management in young patients.
- Interventions by hospital clowns can also mitigate anxiety felt by parents accompanying their children.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term psychological impacts of incorporating therapeutic clowning into pediatric medical practices?
