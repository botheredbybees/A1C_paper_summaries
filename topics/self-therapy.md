## Self-Therapy

The concept of self-therapy emerges from the notion that individuals can engage in therapeutic practices independently to improve their mental health. This approach is grounded in the idea that one's environment and personal interactions with nature (such as through the biophilia hypothesis) have significant impacts on psychological well-being. The sources suggest that by actively engaging in activities that foster a connection to nature or personal growth, individuals can enhance their resilience and emotional stability without external intervention. However, there is a tension between the efficacy of self-therapy and its potential limitations, particularly regarding complex mental health issues.

## Key Findings

- Self-therapy involves individuals actively engaging in therapeutic practices independently for better mental health.
- The biophilia hypothesis supports the notion that nature has a restorative effect on mental well-being, which is integral to self-therapy.
- Activities fostering connection with nature and personal growth enhance resilience and emotional stability.

## Open Questions

- How effective is self-therapy for individuals suffering from severe or complex mental health issues?
