## Creativity in Education

The integration of creative interventions within educational settings is explored through two distinct lenses. Source 1 discusses the teachability and benefits of creativity in education, emphasizing that fostering creativity can be achieved through various pedagogical techniques such as reflective practice and therapeutic tools like creative writing exercises. This source posits that creativity serves not only to enhance academic performance but also to enrich personal development and social skills among students. On the other hand, Source 2 delves into the role of puppets in education, highlighting them as powerful instruments for communication generation and fostering a positive classroom climate. The use of puppets is seen to encourage cooperative learning and facilitate attitude change towards learning, suggesting an innovative approach to traditional teaching methods. Both sources underscore the importance of non-traditional educational tools that can stimulate creative thinking, yet differ in their specific application and methodology.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions such as reflective practice and therapeutic tools like creative writing enhance personal development and social skills among students.
- Puppets serve as a pedagogical tool to generate communication and establish a positive classroom atmosphere
- Both sources highlight the potential of non-traditional methods in encouraging cooperative learning.

## Open Questions

- How do the specific creative tools, such as puppets or reflective writing, compare in their impact on long-term educational outcomes?
