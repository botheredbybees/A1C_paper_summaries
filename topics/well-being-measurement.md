## Measurement of Well-Being

The evaluation of quality of life and mental well-being involves various approaches to ensure comprehensive assessments. The 1990s saw a pivotal shift towards patient-centered care, emphasizing the importance of subjective assessment from individuals themselves rather than purely clinical measures (Source 1). This approach aligns with modern evaluations that integrate therapeutic interventions such as art therapy for promoting mental well-being and social inclusion (Source 2). While both sources highlight the significance of participant satisfaction and involvement in assessments, there is a noted gap regarding standardized methodologies across different cultural contexts to effectively measure these subjective experiences. The integration of arts-based methods into well-being measurement initiatives reflects a growing trend towards holistic evaluation strategies that account for emotional and social dimensions of health.

## Key Findings

- Shift towards patient-centered care in the assessment of quality of life
- Incorporation of arts-based therapies to promote mental well-being
- Emphasis on subjective participant satisfaction

## Open Questions

- Standardization of well-being measurement methodologies across diverse cultural settings
