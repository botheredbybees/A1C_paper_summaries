## Simplified Social Stimuli in Autism Interventions

Research indicates that simplified social stimuli, such as puppets used to facilitate attention to social cues in children with ASD, are effective in enhancing reciprocal interactions. Studies highlight the potential of these tools in reducing complexity and anxiety typically associated with direct human-to-human communication, thus promoting engagement and learning. However, there is a need for more research on how such interventions can be adapted across various developmental stages and cultural contexts to ensure broader applicability.

## Key Findings

- Simplified social stimuli like puppets help in capturing attention towards social cues in children with ASD.
- Puppets facilitate reciprocal interactions by lowering the barriers associated with direct human engagement.
- The use of simplified stimuli can enhance learning and communication skills among children with autism.

## Open Questions

- How can interventions using simplified social stimuli be adapted to cater to diverse developmental stages and cultural backgrounds?
