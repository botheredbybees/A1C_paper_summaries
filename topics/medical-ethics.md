## Philosophy and Ethics in Medical Practice

Both sources explore the role of philosophical inquiry in understanding medical practice from a humanistic perspective. Source 1 discusses how philosophy addresses existential questions relevant to healthcare, emphasizing the importance of valuing human life through an ethical lens. It highlights that a philosophical toolkit can aid practitioners in making morally sound decisions and appreciating the complexities of patient care. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on narrative medicine, illustrating how storytelling and patient narratives enrich clinical practice by providing a holistic view of patients beyond their medical conditions. Both sources highlight the necessity for healthcare providers to engage with humanistic elements, such as ethical frameworks and personal narratives, to enhance patient-centered care.

## Key Findings

- Philosophical inquiry helps address existential questions in medicine
- Narrative medicine enriches clinical practice by focusing on holistic listening and storytelling
- A philosophical toolkit supports making ethically sound decisions

## Open Questions

- How can narrative medicine be integrated with ethical frameworks to enhance patient-centered care?
