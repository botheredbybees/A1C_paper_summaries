## Community-Led Initiatives

The sources emphasize the significance of community-led initiatives in addressing mental health and suicide prevention among Indigenous populations. Each source underscores the need for an intervention approach that is culturally informed, leveraging local strengths and resources to foster resilience and well-being. There is consensus on adopting a strengths-based framework which prioritizes indigenous knowledge and practices over external interventions. This collaborative model empowers communities to define their needs and solutions, thereby enhancing effectiveness and sustainability of initiatives. However, the sources highlight challenges in scaling such localized efforts while maintaining cultural integrity and community autonomy.

## Key Findings

- Community-led initiatives are critical for effective mental health interventions among Indigenous populations.
- A strengths-based approach is essential to foster resilience and well-being within these communities.
- Culturally informed practices enhance the efficacy of community-driven programs.

## Open Questions

- How can large-scale policy frameworks support small, localized community-led initiatives while preserving cultural uniqueness?
