## Training of Trainers Model

The Training of Trainers (ToT) model emerges as a significant framework for capacity building and dissemination of knowledge within various communities, particularly those recovering from traumatic events. This approach is highlighted in the context of psychosocial support after natural disasters like the Yolanda typhoon in the Philippines. The ToT model leverages creative processes to foster resilience and recovery among affected populations through arts-based interventions. Key concepts such as Consensual Qualitative Research underscore the importance of qualitative methodologies for evaluating the efficacy of these training programs.

## Key Findings

- The Training of Trainers model is effective in capacity building within communities recovering from trauma.
- Creative processes, including arts-based interventions, are integral to trauma recovery and psychosocial support.
- Qualitative research methods such as Consensual Qualitative Research provide valuable insights into the implementation and impact of ToT models.

## Open Questions

- How can the Training of Trainers model be adapted for use in different cultural contexts where arts-based interventions may not be readily accepted?
