## Self-Care Practices

The synthesis of self-care practices across the two sources highlights diverse approaches to fostering well-being and resilience. Source 1 emphasizes the use of daily digital routines, such as photography, to enhance personal well-being through relational connections with others and oneself. Conversely, Source 2 explores the application of applied theatre techniques and puppetry within healthcare settings, focusing on emotional skills development for both practitioners and patients. Both sources underscore the importance of engaging in consistent practices that promote mental health and resilience; however, they diverge in their methodologies. While digital photography provides a solitary yet communal approach to self-care, applied theatre integrates social interaction and creative expression as core elements of its therapeutic framework.

## Key Findings

- Self-care can be effectively practiced through both individual and communal means.
- Digital practices like photography contribute to relational well-being by fostering connections with others and oneself.
- Cross-disciplinary approaches in healthcare, such as applied theatre, enhance emotional resilience through active listening and creative expression.

## Open Questions

- How do the solitary and communal benefits of digital self-care compare to those provided by interactive therapeutic practices like applied theatre?
