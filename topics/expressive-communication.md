## Expressive Communication in Autism

The provided sources highlight the significance of play therapy and other therapeutic approaches like sensory integration in enhancing adaptive behavior and expressive communication skills among children with autism. Play therapy, as an intervention method, is recognized for its ability to foster development in a natural, child-led setting that can facilitate better communication outcomes. However, the limitations and challenges associated with these interventions are also noted, suggesting a need for tailored approaches based on individual needs and progress.

## Key Findings

- Play therapy supports adaptive behavior and expressive communication skills in children with autism.
- Sensory integration therapy complements play therapy by addressing sensory processing issues which can impact communication abilities.
- Interventions require customization to match the unique developmental stages and challenges of each child.
