## Hippocratic Corpus

The Hippocratic Corpus presents a foundational theoretical framework in ancient Greek medical thought. It employs metaphors of aggressive disease, reflecting pre-Hippocratic concepts where illness is personified as an enemy or predator. This aggressive view contrasts with the corpus's holistic approach to treatment and diagnosis, which emphasizes balancing bodily humors and considers disease within a broader context than just physical symptoms. The 'hiera nosos' concept, or sacred disease, exemplifies this tension between traditional religious views of illness as divine punishment and emerging rational medical explanations. These themes highlight the Corpus's integration of diverse cultural understandings and its shift towards more scientific inquiry in medicine.

## Key Findings

- The Hippocratic Corpus integrates pre-existing concepts such as aggressive-disease metaphors with novel approaches to holistic treatment based on humoral theory.
- Emerging medical theories within the Corpus challenged traditional beliefs, notably through distinguishing between sacred (hiera nosos) and non-sacred diseases.
- Holistic medicine in the Corpus aims at balancing bodily humors, reflecting a comprehensive view of health beyond symptom management.

## Open Questions

- How did the shift from personifying disease as an aggressive entity to viewing it through humoral imbalance influence public perceptions of illness and medical practices?
