## Behavioral And Psychological Symptoms Of Dementia In Lewy-Body Dementia

The provided sources highlight the complexity of managing behavioral and psychological symptoms (BPSD) in dementia, particularly focusing on Lewy-body dementia. Source 1 introduces musical engagement as a therapeutic activity for people with dementia, emphasizing emotional memory recall which can evoke positive responses and mitigate negative behaviors often associated with BPSD. This aligns with the multifactorial etiology discussed in Source 2, where non-cognitive symptoms are explored beyond their behavioral manifestations to include psychological distress. Both sources indicate that addressing these symptoms requires a comprehensive approach that integrates both behavioral interventions and emotional support.

## Key Findings

- Musical engagement can mitigate negative behaviors associated with BPSD.
- A multifactorial etiology underpins the development of BPSD in dementia.
- Emotional memory recall through music can positively impact psychological well-being in individuals with Lewy-body dementia.
