## Uncertainty and Harm Theory

The Uncertainty-Harm theory posits that feelings of anxiety and fear stem from a perceived threat or harm coupled with uncertainty about the nature or extent of such threats. Source 1, 'Fear of Clowns,' delves into coulrophobia as an example of this phenomenon, focusing on how clowns' unpredictable behavior and media portrayals contribute to individuals’ fears. Across different contexts, the theory highlights the role of ambiguity and unpredictability in amplifying fear reactions due to perceived threats. The sources collectively reinforce the theory's applicability across various scenarios by illustrating how elements like media representations can exacerbate uncertainties surrounding potential harms.

## Key Findings

- Uncertainty-Harm Theory explains fear responses as a result of perceived harm coupled with uncertainty about the threat.
- Media portrayals and behavior unpredictability are significant factors in generating feelings of fear according to the theory.
