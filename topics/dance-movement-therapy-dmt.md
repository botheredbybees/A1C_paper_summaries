## Dance-Movement Therapy

The provided sources highlight the growing interest and research in Dance-Movement Therapy (DMT) as a therapeutic intervention. Source 1 focuses on the benefits of DMT for people with dementia, noting its positive impact on neurocognitive functions through techniques such as Laban Movement Analysis. This approach not only aids physical health but also supports cognitive engagement, offering non-pharmacological treatment options that enhance quality of life. In contrast, Source 2 addresses broader advancements in creative arts therapies, including DMT, emphasizing the methodological developments in assessing emotional recognition and therapeutic outcomes. Both sources underscore the potential of DMT to address diverse psychological and physical needs through movement-based practices.

## Key Findings

- DMT can significantly improve neurocognitive functions for people with dementia.
- Techniques like Laban Movement Analysis are utilized within DMT to foster cognitive engagement and emotional recognition.
- Methodological advancements in creative arts therapies, including DMT, enhance the understanding of therapeutic benefits.

## Open Questions

- How can methodological advancements be integrated into practice to maximize the benefits of DMT for various conditions beyond dementia?
