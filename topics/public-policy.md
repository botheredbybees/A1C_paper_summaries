## Creative Arts in Public Policy for Health

The integration of creative arts into public health policy is emerging as a significant avenue to enhance holistic well-being across various life stages. Sources highlight the potential of art therapy and participatory art programs in fostering better mental health outcomes (Source 1). The Creative Health Review Report emphasizes the role of creativity not only as an indicator but also as a determinant of health, advocating for preventive healthcare approaches that integrate arts initiatives within community settings to improve both physical and psychological wellness (Sources 2 & 3). Non-medical, holistic care methods through arts engagement are noted for their cognitive benefits and potential to address mental health issues effectively (Source 4). These sources align in recognizing the value of arts-based interventions for public health, yet there remains a gap between policy recommendations and practical implementation. Additionally, while sources focus on positive aspects of creative therapies, they lack in-depth exploration of challenges such as ensuring equitable access to these programs and addressing potential barriers to their widespread adoption.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts initiatives have significant potential for enhancing mental health and overall well-being across different life stages.
- Integration of arts-based interventions into public health policy can lead to preventive healthcare measures, contributing positively to holistic care approaches.
- Non-medical creative therapies offer effective cognitive and emotional benefits.

## Open Questions

- How can public policies ensure equitable access to arts-based health initiatives for all segments of the population?
