## Image Composition

The synthesis of image composition techniques reveals a blend of artistic and technical elements aimed at enhancing visual communication. Negative space, as discussed in the first source, acts as a fundamental principle that guides photographers to create balance and draw attention through minimalism and implied boundaries. Conversely, positive space emphasizes subjects and their interactions within an environment, contributing to a sense of completeness and context. The second source introduces technical methods for image manipulation using Photoshop, focusing on layer-blending modes and masking techniques, which enable the merging of images seamlessly while maintaining distinct visual identities. These methods offer tools to enhance or alter composition elements described in earlier concepts like negative space. The third source explores storytelling through a structured approach involving three pictures that together create a cohesive narrative, engaging viewers by leading them from one conceptually linked image to another. This method underscores the importance of creating a visual hierarchy and viewer engagement by effectively balancing both positive and negative spaces within each frame and across an entire series.

## Key Findings

- Negative space is crucial for drawing attention and establishing balance in images.
- Layer-blending techniques enhance the merging of distinct images to create cohesive compositions.
- A three-picture technique can be used to construct a narrative that engages viewers effectively.

## Open Questions
