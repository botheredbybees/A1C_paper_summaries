## Creative Therapies in Mental Health

The exploration of creative therapies such as photography and play offers unique insights into treating depression and enhancing emotional intelligence. Photography is highlighted for its therapeutic benefits, allowing individuals to express emotions visually and process traumatic experiences through self-reflection. In contrast, play therapy focuses on children's development, emphasizing stages of social play and the use of symbolic communication to address emotional needs. Both interventions encourage introspection and emotional expression but cater to different age groups and contexts.

## Key Findings

- Photography can serve as a therapeutic tool for individuals dealing with depression by facilitating visual expression of emotions and personal experiences.
- Play therapy is an effective method for enhancing emotional intelligence in children, particularly through symbolic communication during various stages of play development.
- Both photography and play therapy promote self-reflection and introspection, aiding individuals in processing their feelings and understanding themselves better.

## Open Questions

- How do the therapeutic benefits of visual art (photography) compare with those of symbolic play for adults experiencing emotional distress?
