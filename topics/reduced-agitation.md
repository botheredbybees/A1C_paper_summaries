## Music Therapy for Reduced Agitation

Music therapy has been shown to effectively reduce agitation in dementia patients according to the RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial and other related studies. Creative music making is highlighted as a key element of these interventions, contributing significantly to patient engagement and behavioral outcomes improvement. While music therapy demonstrates significant benefits, there are still questions regarding its long-term efficacy and how it compares with pharmacological treatments.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy reduces agitation in dementia patients
- Creative music making is a crucial component of effective interventions
- The RESCTUE-Alzheimer trial supports the use of music therapy

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits and drawbacks of using music therapy compared to pharmacological treatments?
