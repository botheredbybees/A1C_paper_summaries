## Photo Therapy

Photo therapy has emerged as a valuable practice for enhancing mental health and well-being through the use of photography. Sources indicate that photo therapy encompasses more than just capturing images; it involves mindfulness, storytelling with images and text, and even image editing as therapeutic processes (Source 1, Source 2). The integration of biophilia, an innate human inclination to connect with nature, highlights how engaging with natural imagery can further enrich the healing potential of photography. While both sources emphasize the positive impact on mental health and well-being, there is a notable gap in specific methodologies or guidelines for practitioners wishing to incorporate photo therapy into their practice.

## Key Findings

- Photo therapy supports mental health and enhances well-being through mindfulness and storytelling with images and text.
- Therapeutic photography can involve engaging with nature through biophilia to further enhance its benefits.
