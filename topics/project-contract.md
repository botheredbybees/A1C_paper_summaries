## Project Contracts in Arts and Health

The process of drawing up contracts for arts-in-health projects is crucial to ensure clarity and alignment between all stakeholders. These contracts not only define the terms of collaboration but also address intellectual property rights, ethical considerations, and support structures necessary for project success. Across sources, there is a consensus on the importance of contractual agreements in protecting artists' work while ensuring that healthcare providers can utilize these artistic resources ethically and effectively. However, tensions arise regarding how to balance legal requirements with the creative freedom essential to art-making processes within health settings.

## Key Findings

- Contracts are critical for defining roles and responsibilities in arts-in-health projects.
- Intellectual property rights must be clearly delineated in contracts to protect artists' work.
- Ethical guidelines should inform contract terms to ensure responsible use of artistic resources in healthcare contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can project contracts balance legal requirements with the need for creative flexibility and autonomy?
