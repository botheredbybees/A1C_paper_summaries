## Biophilia Hypothesis

The biophilia hypothesis is a theoretical framework suggesting that there is an inherent human need to connect with the natural world. Sources one and two highlight this concept in relation to therapeutic practices involving nature imagery, indicating a connection between exposure to natural scenes and psychological well-being. Both sources emphasize the application of the biophilia hypothesis in various forms of therapy such as therapeutic photography and self-therapy through editing images that evoke natural settings. The integration of these concepts underscores the theory's applicability across different therapeutic modalities aimed at leveraging human affinity for nature.

## Key Findings

- The biophilia hypothesis underpins the use of nature imagery in therapeutic contexts.
- Therapeutic practices involving the manipulation and viewing of natural scenes are supported by the biophilia hypothesis.
- Self-therapy through image editing is a practical application of the biophilia hypothesis.
