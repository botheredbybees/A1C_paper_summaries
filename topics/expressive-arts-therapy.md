## Expressive Arts Therapy

Expressive Arts Therapy encompasses a range of creative modalities including dance and visual arts to facilitate emotional expression and healing. Source 1 highlights the benefits of Dance Therapy, emphasizing its capacity for memory stimulation through movement and enhancing emotional vitality in participants. This aligns with broader therapeutic goals within Expressive Arts Therapy. Conversely, Source 2 delves into trauma-specific applications, focusing on breathing techniques that engage the vagal system to modulate the autonomic nervous system (ANS). Additionally, body-mapping is introduced as a method for visually representing and addressing traumatic experiences. While these approaches differ in their specific therapeutic objectives, they share an underlying principle: utilizing creative expression to navigate and integrate difficult emotions and memories.

## Key Findings

- Expressive Arts Therapy includes various modalities like dance therapy and visual arts that facilitate emotional healing and memory stimulation.
- Breathing techniques can engage the vagal system in trauma-focused expressive arts therapy, aiding ANS regulation.
- Body-mapping is an effective method for addressing traumatic experiences through creative expression.
