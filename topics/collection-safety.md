## Collection Safety in Healthcare Settings

The integration of museum collections into healthcare settings is an innovative approach aimed at enhancing patient welfare and therapeutic outcomes. However, this practice raises concerns about the safety and management of these collections within clinical environments. Heritage in Health: A Guide to Using Museum Collections in Hospitals provides a comprehensive framework for ensuring collection safety while also emphasizing the importance of participant welfare and program evaluation. The guide underscores the necessity of integrating security measures to protect historical artifacts from potential damage or theft, which can impact both the healthcare institution's reputation and the value of its cultural heritage assets. Additionally, it highlights the need for regular assessments to evaluate the effectiveness of these collections in achieving their intended therapeutic benefits.

## Key Findings

- Ensuring collection safety is paramount when integrating museum artifacts into healthcare settings.
- Security measures must be implemented to protect historical items from damage or theft.
- Regular program evaluations are necessary to assess the therapeutic impact and efficacy.
