## Laughter Therapy

Laughter therapy is an intervention that leverages humor and laughter to improve mental and physical health. The case studies in source 1 highlight the application of this therapy within the context of Theater of the Oppressed, a technique which aims at social change through participatory theater. These studies showcase how laughter therapy can be integrated into drama and theatre engagement programs to address psychological issues among participants. A common finding across these sources is that laughter therapy can serve as a powerful tool for enhancing mental health and fostering social connections in diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Laughter therapy improves both physical and mental well-being.
- Integration of laughter therapy with Theater of the Oppressed enhances therapeutic outcomes.
- The use of drama and theatre to deliver laughter therapy is effective among varied demographics.
