## Accelerated Brain Growth

Research on the characteristics of brains in Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) suggests a pattern of accelerated brain growth early in life. This finding is supported by studies indicating that children with ASD often have larger head circumferences at birth and during infancy compared to typically developing peers. However, this accelerated growth appears to plateau or even decrease later in childhood. Additionally, the altered connectivity patterns observed in individuals with ASD are thought to be a result of this abnormal developmental trajectory. The structural differences, such as gray matter abnormalities, further support the theory that there is an underlying biological mechanism driving these changes, which may contribute to the diverse symptomatology seen across the spectrum.

## Key Findings

- Accelerated brain growth during early development in individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) has been documented.
- Altered connectivity patterns and gray matter abnormalities are consistent findings in ASD research.
- The observed structural differences in ASD brains may be linked to an abnormal developmental trajectory.

## Open Questions

- What mechanisms underlie the transition from accelerated growth to potential deceleration or stabilization of brain development in children with ASD?
