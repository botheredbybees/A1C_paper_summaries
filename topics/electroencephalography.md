## Electroencephalography

Electroencephalography (EEG) is a non-invasive method for measuring brain activity with high temporal resolution. The study 'Functional Connectivity in the First Year of Life in Infants at Risk for Autism Spectrum Disorder: An EEG Study' highlights how EEG can be used to investigate functional connectivity and neural integration patterns, particularly during critical developmental periods like infancy. This source underscores the utility of EEG as a methodological tool to explore the dynamic interactions within and between brain networks, making it valuable not only for clinical applications but also for advancing theoretical understanding of brain function.

## Key Findings

- EEG is a non-invasive method with high temporal resolution.
- Functional connectivity studies using EEG provide insights into neural integration patterns in infants at risk for autism spectrum disorder.
- EEG enables the exploration of dynamic interactions within and between brain networks.
