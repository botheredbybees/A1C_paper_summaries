## Self-Perception in Choir Singing

The study of choir singing among disadvantaged adults highlights several interconnected benefits that positively influence participants' self-perception. Choirs provide a structured environment where individuals can engage in communal activities, leading to enhanced social connections and improved mental health outcomes. Participants report emotional regulation as a significant benefit, suggesting that engaging in group singing helps manage stress and negative emotions. Furthermore, the spiritual experiences reported by choir members contribute to an overall sense of well-being and positivity, reinforcing their self-perception. The formation of group identity through shared musical experiences also plays a crucial role in boosting participants' self-esteem and social integration. However, while these benefits are widely acknowledged, there is a need for more detailed exploration into how specific aspects of choir participation, such as emotional regulation and spiritual experiences, uniquely contribute to changes in self-perception.

## Key Findings

- Choir singing offers significant mental health benefits including improved emotional regulation and reduced stress among participants.
- Spiritual experiences within choirs positively influence overall well-being and contribute to a more positive self-perception.
- The formation of group identity through choir participation enhances social integration and boosts self-esteem.

## Open Questions

- What specific elements of choir singing (e.g., emotional regulation, spiritual experience) have the most significant impact on enhancing self-perception?
