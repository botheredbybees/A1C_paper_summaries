## Expressive Therapeutics

The use of expressive therapeutics as an intervention spans various fields including nursing education and children's healthcare. Interactive puppet simulation emerges as a distinctive form within this therapeutic framework, functioning not just as entertainment but also as a powerful tool for communication and expression. Puppet-based interventions provide a unique avenue for patients to express emotions and thoughts that might be too difficult or uncomfortable to articulate directly, thus facilitating better therapeutic outcomes in settings where direct verbal communication is challenging. The literature suggests a positive impact on patient engagement and educational effectiveness when such tools are integrated into care strategies.

## Key Findings

- Interactive puppet simulation serves as an effective form of expressive therapeutics for enhancing patient communication.
- This method facilitates improved emotional expression among participants, making it particularly valuable in settings involving children or those with communication difficulties.

## Open Questions

- How can the efficacy of interactive puppet simulation be further optimized to cater to diverse patient needs across different cultures and backgrounds?
