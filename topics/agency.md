## Agency in Young Black Men's Health and Wellbeing

The sources discuss the role of community-based drama as an intervention to promote health and wellbeing among young Black men. This approach aims to enhance their self-esteem, critical awareness, and sense of agency through peer trust-building in a safe space environment. The findings from these studies indicate that when individuals are provided with supportive structures, they can develop a stronger sense of control over their lives and improve their mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Community-based drama is an effective intervention for promoting health and wellbeing among young Black men
- Enhancing self-esteem and critical awareness contributes to increased agency in participants
- Creating safe spaces facilitates trust-building among peers, which supports psychological resilience

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of community-based drama interventions on the mental health outcomes for young Black men?
