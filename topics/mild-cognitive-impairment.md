## Mild Cognitive Impairment

The concept of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) is central across the provided sources, serving as a critical transitional state between normal cognitive aging and dementia or neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's disease. Source 1 introduces the Clinical Dementia Rating (CDR) scale for staging dementia, which aids in clinical assessment and differentiation of MCI from other stages of cognitive decline. Sources 2 and 3 further explore practical implications and interventions focused on MCI patients, particularly emphasizing dance movement therapy as a potential therapeutic approach to support neurodegenerative diseases while also highlighting the importance of timely diagnosis through biomarker detection for effective management. However, there is a tension regarding how best to integrate these varied approaches to ensure comprehensive care for individuals with MCI.

## Key Findings

- Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) serves as an important intermediary state between normal cognitive aging and dementia or neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's disease.
- The Clinical Dementia Rating (CDR) scale is a valuable tool in the staging of MCI to distinguish it from other stages of cognitive decline.
- Timely diagnosis through biomarker detection is crucial for effective management of symptomatic Alzheimer’s disease and MCI.

## Open Questions

- How can dance movement therapy be effectively integrated into clinical practices alongside established diagnostic tools such as the CDR scale to support individuals with MCI?
