## Sector Engagement in Policy Development

The sources discuss the critical role of sector engagement in the development of comprehensive policies aimed at integrating arts and health initiatives. The National Australian Arts and Health Framework highlights how effective policy-making requires deep collaboration between various stakeholders within the health and arts sectors, including artists, healthcare providers, researchers, and policymakers. This engagement not only enriches the evidence base but also ensures that the policies are grounded in practical realities and community needs. However, challenges such as varying levels of expertise among participants and potential silos between disciplines can hinder collaborative efforts.

## Key Findings

- Sector engagement is essential for developing effective arts and health policies.
- Collaboration across different sectors enriches policy-making processes by incorporating diverse perspectives.
- Challenges exist in bridging the gap between theoretical frameworks and practical implementation.

## Open Questions

- How can sector engagement be optimized to overcome disciplinary silos and enhance interdisciplinary collaboration?
