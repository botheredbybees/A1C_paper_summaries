## Role Change in Marginalized Populations

The concept of role change is extensively explored through the experiences of refugee youth who undergo significant alterations in their societal roles and responsibilities due to migration. The use of arts-informed research methods provides a unique lens into understanding these transformations, capturing nuances that may be missed by traditional methodologies. This approach allows for the expression of tacit knowledge and symbolic meanings inherent in role changes, highlighting the complex dynamics within marginalized communities facing such shifts.

## Key Findings

- Arts-informed research offers a powerful tool to understand role changes experienced by refugee youth.
- Role change often involves significant shifts in responsibilities and societal positioning for individuals from marginalized backgrounds.
- Symbolic expression through arts can reveal deeper, non-verbal aspects of the psychological impacts associated with changing roles.

## Open Questions

- How do different cultural contexts influence the nature and extent of role changes among refugee populations?
