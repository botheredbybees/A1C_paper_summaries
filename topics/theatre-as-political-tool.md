## Theatre as a Political Tool

Augusto Boal's work emphasizes the use of theatre as an intervention to give voice to underrepresented groups and promote political activism. Across various sources, there is agreement on the transformative potential of theatre in empowering marginalized communities through participatory practices. However, some tensions arise concerning the scalability and cultural specificity of these methods. While theatre can serve as a powerful medium for social change, questions remain about its effectiveness across different contexts and how to sustain engagement over time.

## Key Findings

- Theatre can act as an effective tool for political activism by empowering marginalized communities.
- Participatory practices in theatre allow individuals to express their perspectives and experiences.
- There is a need to adapt theatrical interventions to diverse cultural contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can the effectiveness of using theatre as a political tool be sustained over time?
