## Arts Education and Adolescent Development

The integration of arts into adolescent education emerges as a multifaceted intervention with profound impacts on emotional wellbeing, identity formation, cognitive benefits, and socio-emotional skills. Source one highlights 'Arts on Prescription,' an initiative focusing on the therapeutic use of arts to enhance emotional resilience in adolescents. This is complemented by source two's exploration of specific arts therapies and their role in promoting self-expression and social inclusion among young people through projects like the Labyrinth Project. Both sources converge in emphasizing the arts' potential to foster identity formation, with each highlighting different aspects such as cognitive development (source one) and therapeutic benefits for marginalized youth (source two). However, there is a tension regarding the specific mechanisms of how these interventions achieve their outcomes, suggesting that further research is needed to clarify effective practices.

## Key Findings

- Arts education enhances emotional resilience in adolescents
- Art-based interventions support identity formation and self-expression among young people
- Specific arts therapies such as 'Labyrinth Project' promote social inclusion

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which art-based interventions achieve their outcomes?
