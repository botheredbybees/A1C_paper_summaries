## Neuronal Intermediate Filament Inclusion Disease

Neuronal Intermediate Filament Inclusion Disease (NIFID) is associated with a progressive decline in cognitive and neurological functions, particularly affecting frontotemporal dementia patients. The disease's impact extends to the dissolution of artistic creativity, highlighting its broader influence beyond traditional clinical symptoms. However, research on NIFID and its relationship with creative processes remains limited, leading to gaps in understanding how the condition specifically affects these aspects.

## Key Findings

- Neuronal Intermediate Filament Inclusion Disease is linked to frontotemporal dementia
- The disease impacts cognitive functions and can lead to a loss of artistic creativity

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which NIFID affects creative processes?
