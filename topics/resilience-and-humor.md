## Resilience and Humor in Dementia Care

The integration of humor and resilience as therapeutic strategies for individuals with dementia has been explored through various multidisciplinary collaborations. Helen Morse's personal experience of interpreting dementia through drama suggests that humor can be a significant coping mechanism, fostering a positive outlook despite the profound changes in identity (change-in-person) often experienced by those living with dementia. Across different sources, there is agreement on the importance of these interventions in enhancing quality of life and emotional well-being among patients and caregivers alike.

## Key Findings

- Humor and resilience can serve as effective coping mechanisms for individuals dealing with changes due to dementia.
- Multidisciplinary collaborations can enhance the understanding and application of humor and resilience techniques in dementia care.
- Interventions that foster a positive outlook despite cognitive decline are crucial in improving quality of life.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms underlie the effectiveness of humor as a therapeutic tool in dementia?
