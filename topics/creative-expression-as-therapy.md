## Creative Expression As Therapy

The provided sources discuss creative expression as a therapeutic intervention aimed at enhancing resilience and emotional well-being. While the first source highlights how art workshops can serve as a means for children to explore their emotions and develop coping strategies, it underscores the importance of creativity in fostering psychological resilience. Despite varied approaches across different contexts, there is an underlying agreement that creative expression provides a non-verbal method for individuals to articulate complex feelings and experiences, making it particularly effective for those who find traditional talk therapies challenging or unsuitable.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression through activities like art workshops can enhance psychological resilience in children.
- Non-verbal methods such as drawing, painting, and sculpture are beneficial therapeutic interventions, especially for individuals reluctant to engage in verbal communication.
- There is a consensus on the efficacy of creative therapies in addressing emotional challenges and improving mental health outcomes.
