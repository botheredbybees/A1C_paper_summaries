## Cultural Identity Through Art and Intergenerational Relationships

The sources highlight two distinct but interrelated interventions that support cultural identity through art. Source 1 examines the role of language and culture in maintaining intergenerational relationships, particularly focusing on how dementia can affect these ties. In contrast, Source 2 discusses the revitalization of a community's sense-of-place using murals as a form of reverse graffiti to combat urban decay. Both sources underscore the importance of cultural expression in reinforcing community identity and well-being.

## Key Findings

- Cultural identity is sustained through intergenerational relationships that are often mediated by language and shared experiences.
- Community art projects, such as murals, play a crucial role in revitalizing declining neighborhoods by fostering a sense of place among residents.

## Open Questions

- How do intergenerational relationships influenced by cultural identity contribute to the success or effectiveness of community arts initiatives like murals?
