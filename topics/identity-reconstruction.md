## Identity Reconstruction

The concept of identity reconstruction is examined through the lenses of resilience and self-exploration in individuals who have undergone significant life changes or trauma. Source 1 emphasizes the role of dance therapy in helping participants build a resilient sense-of-self, which involves understanding personal strengths and vulnerabilities to foster psychological recovery. In contrast, Source 2 highlights the use of art therapy as part of holistic treatment plans for those with traumatic brain injury (TBI), focusing on neuroplasticity and self-exploration to aid patients in reconstructing their identity post-injury. Both sources underscore the importance of therapeutic interventions that encourage individuals to engage deeply with their experiences, thereby facilitating a process of rebuilding and redefining their sense of self.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic interventions like dance therapy and art therapy support resilience and personal growth in individuals facing significant life challenges or trauma.
- Identity reconstruction involves processes of neuroplasticity and self-exploration, which are key components in therapeutic approaches for individuals with TBI.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of creative therapies compare in their effectiveness at facilitating identity reconstruction?
