## Design Thinking

The concept of Design Thinking is explored in the provided sources as a versatile and creative approach to solving complex problems. It emphasizes empathy with users, ideation, prototyping, and iterative testing. The method encourages inviting open responses from stakeholders to foster innovation and address challenges through collaborative problem-solving. Problem tree analysis is highlighted as a complementary tool within this framework, aiding in mapping the root causes of issues and identifying entry points for intervention. Systems thinking is also mentioned, suggesting that Design Thinking operates effectively when viewed as part of larger systemic changes, requiring an understanding of interconnectedness and holistic approaches to tackling complex problems.

## Key Findings

- Design Thinking emphasizes empathy with users, ideation, prototyping, and iterative testing to address complex challenges.
- Problem tree analysis is used alongside Design Thinking to map root causes and entry points for intervention.
- Systems thinking complements Design Thinking by encouraging an understanding of interconnectedness in addressing systemic issues.
