## Insulin Signaling Disruption

The sources collectively highlight the critical role of insulin signaling disruption in various diseases. Insulin resistance is noted as a significant factor contributing to Alzheimer's disease pathology, with antidiabetic drugs showing promise in mitigating this condition. However, there remains uncertainty around the specific mechanisms by which these drugs exert their effects on neuronal health and tau-pathology. The interplay between oxidative stress and insulin signaling disruption also emerges as an area of interest for further research.

## Key Findings

- Insulin resistance is a key factor in Alzheimer's disease pathology
- Antidiabetic drugs may play a role in mitigating the effects of insulin resistance on neuronal health
- The interplay between oxidative stress and insulin signaling disruption impacts neuronal function

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which antidiabetic drugs affect tau-pathology and neuronal health?
