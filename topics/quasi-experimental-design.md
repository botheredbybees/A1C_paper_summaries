## Quasi-Experimental Design

The sources collectively explore the utility and efficacy of quasi-experimental designs in various research contexts. These studies are crucial for scenarios where randomization is not feasible or ethical, allowing researchers to make more nuanced causal inferences. The focus on the puppet show and storytelling methods highlights the therapeutic value these interventions can have when applied effectively. Despite methodological challenges posed by the absence of random assignment, quasi-experimental designs offer a valuable approach to assessing interventions' impact without compromising ethical standards. However, tensions arise regarding the extent to which such designs can produce generalizable findings.

## Key Findings

- Quasi-experimental designs are useful for making causal inferences when randomized controlled trials are not feasible.
- Interventions like puppet shows and storytelling have therapeutic value but require careful design considerations.
- The use of quasi-experimental methods necessitates robust analytical techniques to address potential biases.

## Open Questions

- To what extent do quasi-experimental designs offer generalizable findings compared to randomized controlled trials?
