## Health Equity Through Cultural Programs

The synthesis of the provided sources underscores how cultural programs and interventions can significantly impact health equity across diverse populations. Both 'Arts for EveryBody: The One Nation/One Project Impact Report' and 'Mind, Body, Spirit: How Museums Impact Health and Wellbeing' highlight the role of mixed-method research and holistic-health approaches in understanding these impacts. While the former emphasizes evidence-based engagement through arts programs to address community needs, the latter focuses on cultural interventions facilitated by museums as part of a broader framework for health and wellbeing. Both sources converge on the idea that cultural participation can be an effective public health strategy, particularly in addressing social determinants of health.

## Key Findings

- Cultural programs have measurable positive impacts on community health and well-being.
- Mixed-method research provides valuable insights into the effectiveness of cultural interventions for promoting health equity.
- Arts engagement and museum visits can act as holistic-health strategies, addressing both physical and mental health needs.

## Open Questions

- How can barriers to access be overcome to ensure that cultural programs reach all segments of the population?
