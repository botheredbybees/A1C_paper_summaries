## Community Support Networks

The sources highlight the significance of community support networks in enhancing well-being and fostering a sense of identity. Source 1 examines singing-based interventions that not only reduce stress hormones but also increase melatonin levels, indicating a positive physiological impact on participants. These music-based interventions serve as a platform for building supportive relationships within communities. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on the broader concept of community support networks in Aboriginal contexts, emphasizing how these networks are integral to cultural identity and drug prevention strategies. Both sources underscore the role of community engagement in improving mental health outcomes through various forms of intervention.

## Key Findings

- Music-based interventions can enhance physiological well-being by reducing stress hormones and increasing melatonin levels.
- Community support networks play a crucial role in maintaining cultural identity, particularly among Aboriginal populations.
- Effective drug prevention strategies are often underpinned by strong community support systems.

## Open Questions

- How do music-based interventions integrate with broader cultural practices to enhance the effectiveness of community support networks?
