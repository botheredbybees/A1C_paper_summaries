## Music Therapy and Executive Function Improvement

The integration of music therapy into rehabilitation practices highlights its potential in enhancing executive functioning through mechanisms such as neural plasticity and rhythmic auditory stimulation. Sources emphasize the importance of personalized therapeutic interventions to address deficits in executive function after brain injuries, leveraging advances in neuroscience to tailor treatments effectively. Despite promising findings, there is a need for further research to delineate specific parameters for successful application across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can improve executive functioning through neural plasticity.
- Rhythmic auditory stimulation plays a critical role in enhancing cognitive functions post-brain injury.
- Personalized therapeutic interventions are crucial for effective executive function improvement.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal parameters and duration of music therapy sessions to maximize benefits for executive function recovery?
