## Nomophobia

Sources highlight the concept of nomophobia, a fear of being without one's mobile phone. The Nomophobia Questionnaire (NMP-Q) assesses this condition by measuring anxiety and behavioral patterns related to smartphone use. Situational specific phobias are identified as underlying factors, suggesting that cognitive-behavioral therapy may be an effective intervention for addressing nomophobia. Meanwhile, the concept of therapeutic photography is introduced as a method to improve mental health through self-presentation exercises, which might also contribute indirectly to managing nomophobic behaviors by fostering mindfulness and emotional regulation.

## Key Findings

- Nomophobia is characterized by anxiety related to being without one's smartphone.
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy may be an effective intervention for treating nomophobia.
- Therapeutic photography can improve mental health through mindfulness and self-presentation.
