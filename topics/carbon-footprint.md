## Carbon Footprint and Sustainable Well-being

The Happy Planet Index (HPI) of 2024 emphasizes the importance of aligning high life expectancy with low environmental impact. The HPI measures the sustainability of well-being by integrating self-reported wellbeing, life expectancy, and carbon footprint indicators. Across multiple countries, it reveals varying degrees of success in balancing these elements, suggesting that while some nations achieve high levels of happiness and longevity with minimal ecological disruption, others struggle to maintain a sustainable balance between quality of life and environmental responsibility. This index highlights the necessity for holistic approaches in policy-making that address both human welfare and planetary health.

## Key Findings

- The HPI integrates multiple indicators to assess national sustainability and well-being
- There is variability among nations in achieving high levels of wellbeing while maintaining low carbon footprints
- High life expectancy combined with a minimal ecological footprint is achievable but uncommon

## Open Questions

- How can policy-makers effectively balance the promotion of human welfare with environmental conservation goals?
