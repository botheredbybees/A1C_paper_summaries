## Projection in Therapy

Sources discuss the application of object relations theory to develop coping strategies for children through projection in therapy. Both sources highlight the use of interactive puppet play as a technique to help children externalize their emotions and understand interpersonal dynamics, which is crucial for emotional regulation and personal growth. The method fosters a supportive environment that aids in the expression of feelings and enhances therapeutic outcomes by addressing cultural sensitivity and emotional dysregulation.

## Key Findings

- Interactive puppet play is an effective intervention to help children externalize emotions through projection
- Object relations theory underpins the use of projection techniques in therapy for children
- Creating a supportive environment aids children's coping strategies and emotional regulation
