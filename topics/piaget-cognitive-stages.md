## Piaget's Cognitive Stages

Jean Piaget's theory of cognitive development is a foundational framework for understanding how children's mental processes evolve over time. The theory delineates four stages: sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, and formal operational. Each stage represents distinct cognitive abilities and limitations. Symbolic play emerges in the preoperational stage and is crucial for cognitive growth as it allows children to manipulate ideas through symbolic representations. Play therapy practitioners often apply Piaget's theory to guide therapeutic interventions by understanding clients' current developmental levels and facilitating their progress toward more advanced stages of thinking.

## Key Findings

- Symbolic play in the preoperational stage is critical for cognitive development.
- Piaget's theory provides a framework for understanding children's mental processes.
- Play therapy practitioners use Piaget's theory to guide therapeutic interventions.
