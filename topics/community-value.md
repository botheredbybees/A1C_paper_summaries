## Community Value Through Participatory Art

The Active Energy Project exemplifies the profound impact of participatory art on elderly wellbeing and community value. This initiative underscores how engaging in creative arts can enhance quality of life for older adults, fostering a sense of belonging and contribution to their communities. Sources highlight the therapeutic benefits of such interventions, including improved mental health and social connections. However, there is also an acknowledgment of challenges, such as accessibility barriers and varying levels of engagement among participants.

## Key Findings

- Participatory art enhances quality of life for elderly individuals.
- Engagement in creative arts fosters a sense of community and belonging.
- Therapeutic benefits include improved mental health and social connections.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to accessibility and how can they be addressed?
