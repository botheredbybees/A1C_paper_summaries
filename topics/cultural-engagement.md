## Cultural Engagement in Health and Well-Being

The sources collectively highlight the importance of cultural engagement as an intervention for improving public health outcomes, mental health recovery, community cohesion, and overall well-being. Cultural engagement is not only seen to foster stronger community ties but also to serve as a critical pathway for Indigenous self-determination and health improvement through mixed-methods research approaches. The evidence underscores the necessity of culturally sensitive designs in arts and health initiatives that engage diverse populations effectively.

## Key Findings

- Cultural engagement enhances public health outcomes by fostering mental health recovery and community cohesion.
- Mixed methods research provides a robust framework for evaluating cultural interventions among Indigenous communities.
- Self-determination through cultural participation improves well-being and health outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can the design of arts in health initiatives be further refined to better engage diverse populations and maximize public health benefits?
