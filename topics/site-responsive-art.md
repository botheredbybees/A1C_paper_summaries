## Site-Responsive Art

The concept of site-responsive art emerges as a dynamic intervention that bridges the gap between traditional artistic practices and contemporary interdisciplinary methodologies. Dr Annalise Rees's project in Antarctica exemplifies this approach, integrating drawing with broader environmental and social contexts through an expanded field perspective. The interviews highlight the importance of context-specific engagement, which fosters unique dialogues between art and science. This synthesis reveals a consistent theme: site-responsive art serves as a powerful medium for fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and enhancing public understanding of complex issues such as climate change and cultural heritage.

## Key Findings

- Site-responsive art integrates various disciplines beyond traditional boundaries.
- Context-specific engagement fosters unique dialogues between art and science.
- Artists use expanded field concepts to create meaningful, context-driven works.

## Open Questions

- How can site-responsive art better engage with diverse audiences and communities?
