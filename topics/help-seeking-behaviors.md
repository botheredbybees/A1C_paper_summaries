## Help-Seeking Behaviors

The integration of cultural strengths in support programs for Aboriginal young people has shown significant improvements in their social and emotional wellbeing. These initiatives focus on resilience building, which encourages help-seeking behaviors by fostering a supportive environment where individuals can express their needs without fear of judgment. However, there is a noted gap between the cultural relevance of these interventions and the broader accessibility to such culturally-specific resources for Aboriginal youth outside specialized programs.

## Key Findings

- Cultural strengthening programs improve social and emotional wellbeing
- Resilience building initiatives encourage help-seeking behaviors

## Open Questions

- How can culturally specific support be scaled up for wider impact?
