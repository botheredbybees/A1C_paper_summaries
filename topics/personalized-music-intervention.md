## Personalized Music Intervention

The use of personalized music interventions has shown promising results in activating specific regions of the brain affected by Alzheimer's disease. Studies utilizing functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) have identified that music can activate the salience network, which is often spared from the effects of neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer’s. These findings suggest a potential therapeutic benefit for personalized music interventions in improving cognitive and emotional functioning in individuals with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Personalized music activates regions of the brain that are typically unaffected by Alzheimer's disease.
- The salience network, which is involved in attention, emotion, and motivation, can be activated through tailored musical experiences.
- Functional MRI studies provide evidence for the neurological benefits of personalized music interventions.
