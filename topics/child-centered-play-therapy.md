## Child-Centered Play Therapy

Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) is recognized as a significant intervention for children with diverse needs. It is particularly effective in addressing the social and emotional challenges faced by children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Studies indicate that CCPT not only helps improve behavior but also leads to measurable changes in brain activity, as observed through EEG studies during randomized controlled trials (RCTs). Moreover, systematic reviews highlight its efficacy in managing trauma-related symptoms stemming from adverse childhood experiences. The intervention is rooted in creating a safe and nurturing environment where children can express themselves freely and develop better coping mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) is effective for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).
- CCPT leads to observable changes in brain activity measured through EEG studies.
- The intervention helps manage trauma-related symptoms in children.
