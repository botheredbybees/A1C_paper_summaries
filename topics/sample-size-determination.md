## Sample Size Determination

The topic of sample size determination is crucial for ensuring the statistical validity and reliability of behavioral science studies. Jacob Cohen's seminal work 'Statistical Power Analysis for the Behavioral Sciences' provides foundational insights into determining appropriate sample sizes through power analysis, which considers factors such as effect size, intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC), and desired level of statistical power. Both sources emphasize the importance of understanding these concepts to avoid Type II errors—failing to detect an existing effect—and to optimize resource allocation in research design.

## Key Findings

- Sample size determination is critical for achieving adequate statistical power in behavioral science studies.
- Understanding intraclass correlation coefficients (ICC) and effect sizes are essential components of conducting a proper power analysis.
- Cohen's work provides foundational methodology for determining sample sizes through power analysis.
