## Non-verbal Communication in Puppetry

The sources highlight the profound influence of non-verbal communication in puppetry, particularly within therapeutic and educational contexts. This mode of expression through puppets allows for nuanced, subtle conveyance of emotions and narratives without words. One source emphasizes the role of creative play facilitated by puppetry as a means to address vicarious trauma among caregivers. Puppetry acts as a bridge between the conscious mind and unconscious feelings, allowing individuals to express themselves in ways that direct verbal communication might inhibit due to the complexity or sensitivity of topics. The methodological aspect of non-verbal communication is also noted for its ability to engage diverse audiences who may have language barriers, thus making puppetry an inclusive tool across different cultures and age groups.

## Key Findings

- Non-verbal communication through puppetry serves as a therapeutic mechanism in addressing traumatic experiences.
- Puppetry acts as a medium that bridges conscious and unconscious feelings, facilitating deeper emotional expression.
- The use of puppets promotes inclusivity by transcending language barriers.
