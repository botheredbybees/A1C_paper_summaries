## Transcriptomic Dysregulation in Autism Spectrum Disorder

The provided sources collectively emphasize the importance of understanding transcriptomic dysregulation in individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The studies highlight the broad impact of dysregulated gene expression across various brain regions, particularly within the cerebral cortex. This dysregulation involves multiple genes and pathways, affecting neural development and function. Despite these common findings, there is a gap in translating this understanding into targeted interventions or therapies for ASD.

## Key Findings

- Transcriptomic dysregulation is widespread across the cerebral cortex in individuals with autism spectrum disorder.
- Dysregulated gene expression impacts multiple brain regions and pathways involved in neural development.
- Understanding gene expression patterns may provide insights into the biological mechanisms underlying ASD.

## Open Questions

- How can knowledge of transcriptomic dysregulation be translated into effective interventions for managing symptoms of autism spectrum disorder?
