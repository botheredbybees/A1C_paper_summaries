## Collective Subjectivity in Dementia Care

The concept of collective subjectivity as it pertains to dementia care is explored through the lens of situational awareness and how meaning-making processes are embodied. This theoretical framework highlights the importance of recognizing the shared experiences and understandings that emerge within caregiving environments, where the individual subjective experience intersects with communal understanding. The body serves not just as a passive recipient of care but as an active participant in articulating these collective meanings.

## Key Findings

- The body is central to the expression of meaning in dementia care
- Care settings foster collective subjectivity through shared experiences

## Open Questions

- How can caregiving practices better incorporate and understand the role of the body in conveying collective meaning
