## Sustainable Land Use

The Capertee Valley Hydrology Project highlights the importance of comprehensive interventions in achieving sustainable land use. This project emphasizes catchment restoration and landscape rehydration as fundamental components for revitalizing degraded landscapes. Community participation is identified as a critical element, underscoring the necessity for local involvement in planning and execution to ensure ecological sustainability and community buy-in. The integration of these elements demonstrates an integrated approach towards transforming unsustainable land-use patterns into models that support both environmental resilience and social equity.

## Key Findings

- landscape rehydration and catchment restoration are crucial components of sustainable land use
- community participation is essential for successful implementation of sustainable practices
