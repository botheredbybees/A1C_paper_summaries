## Health-Related Quality Of Life Surveys

The SF-36 and SF-12 health-related quality of life surveys are widely recognized for their utility in evaluating both mental and physical components of HRQOL. These tools offer a concise yet comprehensive assessment, capturing multiple dimensions including physical functioning, role limitations due to physical health problems, bodily pain, general health perceptions, vitality, social functioning, role limitations due to emotional problems, and mental health. Despite their widespread use, the application across diverse populations remains an area of ongoing investigation.

## Key Findings

- The SF-36 and SF-12 surveys are instrumental in assessing HRQOL.
- These surveys measure both mental (MCS) and physical (PCS) components of quality of life.
- Applications of these tools continue to evolve with research.

## Open Questions

- How do the SF-36 and SF-12 surveys perform across different cultural contexts?
