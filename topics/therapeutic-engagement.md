## Therapeutic Engagement

The concept of therapeutic engagement is highlighted in both sources as a fundamental aspect of enhancing patient care and quality of life. In the context of dementia patients, music therapy serves as an engaging intervention to tap into preserved musical memories, thus improving their cognitive function and overall well-being (Source 1). Similarly, puppet play therapy focuses on character development through interactive storytelling, which fosters therapeutic engagement by making treatment sessions more relatable and enjoyable for young children. Both interventions underscore the importance of personalized care plans that resonate with patients' unique backgrounds and interests to maximize therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic engagement is critical in enhancing patient well-being through tailored interventions.
- Music therapy leverages preserved musical memories to improve cognitive function in dementia patients.
- Puppet play therapy uses character development and interactive storytelling to engage children emotionally.

## Open Questions

- How can therapeutic engagement principles be effectively adapted for diverse populations, such as elderly individuals with advanced dementia or non-verbal children?
