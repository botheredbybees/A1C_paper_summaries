## Analogous Color Schemes in Photography

The study of color theory, specifically the utilization of analogous and complementary color schemes, enhances visual impact in photography. Key concepts such as hue, saturation, and brightness are crucial for understanding how colors interact within an image. Analogous color schemes involve using colors that are adjacent on the color wheel to create a harmonious and visually appealing effect, whereas complementary colors (opposite each other on the wheel) provide contrast. While both techniques aim to enhance aesthetic qualities in photography, their implementation can vary widely depending on the photographer's intent and context.

## Key Findings

- Analogous color schemes use adjacent hues for a cohesive look.
- Complementary colors are used for high visual impact due to contrast.
- Key concepts like hue, saturation, and brightness guide effective color usage in photography.
