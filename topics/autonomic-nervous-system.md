## Autonomic Nervous System Responses in Therapeutic Clowing

The study explores the physiological and emotional responses of disabled children to therapeutic clowning, focusing on changes within their autonomic nervous system. Key findings indicate that such interventions can lead to both physical relaxation (as evidenced by changes in heart rate variability) and positive emotional states among participants. This pilot research highlights a potential non-pharmacological approach for enhancing patient care. However, it also reveals the complexity of measuring short-term versus long-term effects on children's autonomic responses.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic clowning can lead to physical relaxation in disabled children.
- Positive emotional states correlate with physiological changes indicative of parasympathetic activation.
- Potential for therapeutic clowning as a non-pharmacological intervention to improve patient care

## Open Questions

- How do the autonomic responses measured in this pilot study translate into long-term health benefits?
