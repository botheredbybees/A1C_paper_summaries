## Ethical Considerations in Therapeutic Practices

The ethical considerations in therapeutic practices are illuminated through the lenses of phototherapy and therapeutic photography (Source 1) as well as a music and health perspective on music's perceived 'goodness' (Source 2). Both sources emphasize the importance of intrinsic value and integrity within therapeutic contexts. Source 1 underscores the significance of ethics in therapy, highlighting the necessity for ethical conduct in visual communication through phototherapy and photography practices. This includes considerations such as informed consent, confidentiality, and respect for individual autonomy. Conversely, Source 2 explores how music's perceived goodness can be compromised when it is commodified or its intrinsic value diminished by commercial interests, thereby impacting therapeutic benefits. Both sources reveal a tension between maintaining ethical standards in therapy and the challenges posed by external factors such as market forces and commodification.

## Key Findings

- Intrinsic value is crucial to both phototherapy practices and music's therapeutic roles.
- Maintaining confidentiality and obtaining informed consent are key ethical considerations in visual and auditory therapies.
- Commodification can diminish the perceived goodness of music, impacting its therapeutic benefits.

## Open Questions

- How do external factors such as market forces impact the ethical practice of phototherapy and the therapeutic use of music?
