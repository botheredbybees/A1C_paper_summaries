## Social Prescribing

The concept of social prescribing is highlighted across sources as an intervention aimed at enhancing mental well-being and resilience through structured activities, particularly in vulnerable populations. Source 1 introduces visual storytelling as a form of social prescribing that fosters resilience among its participants, emphasizing the narrative and creative process. Sources 3 and 5 delve into Arts on Prescription (AoP) as a specific implementation of social prescribing, showing it to have positive impacts on mental health issues such as anxiety and depression. Source 2 reviews the role of arts activities in developing resilience, indicating that these activities contribute significantly to both mental well-being and resilience among young people. However, while AoP is effective, its reach and accessibility are influenced by socioeconomic factors (Source 4), suggesting a need for broader integration into healthcare systems to maximize benefits across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Social prescribing, including Arts on Prescription (AoP), significantly improves mental well-being and resilience among participants.
- Visual storytelling through social prescribing contributes positively to the development of personal resilience.
- Structured arts activities play a crucial role in enhancing mental health and resilience in children and young people.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to implementing Arts on Prescription (AoP) across diverse socioeconomic backgrounds, and how can these be addressed?
