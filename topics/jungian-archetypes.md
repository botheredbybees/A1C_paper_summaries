## Jungian Archetypes in Therapy

The integration of Jungian archetypes into therapeutic interventions is highlighted through the use of self-portraiture and color psychology. Source one underscores how archetypes can provide a symbolic framework for understanding personal trauma, enabling individuals to visualize their internal struggles through artistic expression. This approach leverages the power of imagery and symbolism inherent in Jung's theories to facilitate deeper emotional processing and healing.

## Key Findings

- Jungian archetypes offer a rich psychological language that can be used in therapeutic contexts.
- Therapeutic self-portraiture using color psychology aids in personal trauma reimagining.
- Symbolic representation through art can enhance understanding of deep-seated emotional issues.
