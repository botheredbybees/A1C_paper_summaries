## Lifestyle Factors in Neurodegeneration Prevention

The reviewed literature highlights the critical role of lifestyle factors in mitigating neurodegeneration and enhancing cognitive health. Specifically, two studies focus on autophagy-mediated pathways influenced by diet, physical activity, sleep (Source 1), and playing a musical instrument (Source 2) as protective against dementia and Alzheimer's disease. Autophagy is identified as a key cellular process where its regulation through lifestyle interventions can significantly influence neurodegeneration by reducing protein aggregation characteristic of diseases like AD. Meanwhile, engaging in musical activities demonstrates an additional modifiable risk factor that may independently protect against cognitive decline. Both studies underscore the importance of balanced autophagic responses and non-pharmacological strategies for disease prevention. However, limitations such as the need for better biomarkers to measure brain-specific autophagy in Source 1 and potential confounders like socio-economic status or genetic predisposition not fully addressed by twin studies (Source 2) suggest avenues for further research.

## Key Findings

- Autophagy-mediated pathways can be regulated through diet, physical activity, sleep, and musical activities to prevent neurodegeneration.
- Playing a musical instrument is associated with reduced risk of dementia and cognitive impairment independent of sex, education level, and physical activity.
- Balanced autophagic responses are crucial in delaying or preventing Alzheimer's disease progression.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal thresholds for non-pharmacological interventions such as diet, exercise, sleep quality, and music engagement to maximize neuroprotective benefits?
