## Innate Musicality

The concept of innate musicality suggests a universal human capacity for music that extends beyond learned skills or environmental influences. Sources indicate that this inherent trait manifests in various spontaneous interactions, such as babbling and body movement to rhythm, which can be therapeutic. However, there is variability in the extent to which these natural inclinations are harnessed for health benefits, suggesting a gap between innate capabilities and realized outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Innate musicality is viewed as an intrinsic human trait rather than a learned skill.
- Spontaneous interactions with music reveal potential therapeutic impacts independent of formal training.
- The therapeutic effects of music are contingent on the recognition and engagement of innate musical behaviors.

## Open Questions

- How can environments be designed to better support the expression and enhancement of innate musicality for health outcomes?
