## Clowning as an Intervention

The sources highlight clowning as a versatile intervention in creative arts and play therapy contexts. While puppetry also plays a role, the primary focus is on clowning's unique capacity to engage participants emotionally and physically through humor and playfulness. The intervention addresses mental wellbeing but raises concerns about vicarious trauma for practitioners.

## Key Findings

- Clowning is used as an intervention in creative arts therapy settings.
- Puppetry, alongside clowning, serves therapeutic purposes but clowning offers distinct benefits.
- Vicarious trauma can be a significant concern for practitioners engaging with traumatic client stories through clowning.
