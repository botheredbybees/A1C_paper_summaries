## Functional Brain Connectivity

The concept of functional brain connectivity explores how different regions of the brain communicate and interact during various activities. Source 1 focuses on visual art production and cognitive evaluation's impact on the default-mode network, highlighting its role in resilience to stress through enhanced connectivity. Conversely, Source 2 delves into temporal synchrony variations between individuals with autism and those without, suggesting that altered neural network dynamics correlate with autism symptom severity and potentially influence cognitive processing speed. Both studies underscore the dynamic nature of functional brain connectivity but differ in their primary focus areas: one on artistic engagement's impact on resilience, while the other examines differences rooted in developmental conditions like autism.

## Key Findings

- Functional brain connectivity varies depending on the activity or condition being assessed (e.g., visual art production vs. cognitive evaluation)
- Enhanced connectivity within the default-mode network is linked to improved resilience against stress
- Individuals with autism exhibit altered temporal synchrony between brain regions, which may correlate with symptom severity

## Open Questions

- How does functional brain connectivity change in response to therapeutic interventions aimed at improving cognitive processing speed and social functioning in individuals with autism?
