## Risk Of Bias In Non-Randomized Studies

The evaluation of non-randomized studies faces significant challenges in ensuring the validity and reliability of outcomes due to inherent biases such as confounding factors. Sources emphasize the importance of tools like ROBINS-I, which is designed specifically for assessing risk of bias in these types of studies. Both sources highlight that a thorough understanding of potential confounders and methodological rigor are crucial for accurate assessment, particularly through per-protocol effect analyses to understand intervention effects under ideal conditions.

## Key Findings

- ROBINS-I provides a structured approach to assess risk of bias in non-randomized studies
- Per-protocol analysis helps understand intervention effectiveness as intended
- Confounding factors and methodological rigor are critical for accurate study evaluation

## Open Questions

- How can the impact of unmeasured confounders be minimized or accounted for in non-randomized studies?
