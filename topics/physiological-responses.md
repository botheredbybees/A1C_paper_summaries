## Physiological Responses to Music and Sound

The exploration of physiological responses to music and sound stimuli reveals a multifaceted impact on human physiology. Source 1 discusses immune system changes and the emotional underpinnings of musical experiences, highlighting how music can influence biological processes beyond simple auditory perception. This aligns with findings in Source 2, which delves into psychophysiological studies showing that empathy manipulation significantly alters music-induced emotions, indicating a complex interplay between cognitive processes and physiological responses. Additionally, Source 3 introduces the concept of therapeutic clowns affecting autonomic nervous system responses in disabled children, further illustrating how specific interventions can modulate emotional and physical states through tailored sensory inputs like clowning activities.

## Key Findings

- Music and sound stimuli have significant impacts on immune system functioning and emotional well-being.
- Empathy manipulation influences the physiological outcomes of music-induced emotions, demonstrating a cognitive-emotional-physiological nexus.
- Therapeutic interventions such as clown therapy can alter autonomic nervous system responses in children with disabilities.
