## Elderhood in the Context of Art Therapy and Creative Aging

The integration of art therapy within the framework of creative aging acknowledges the complexities of elderhood, focusing on both the challenges of biological decline and the opportunities for continued personal development. Sources highlight how engaging in artistic activities can enhance cognitive functions, emotional well-being, and social interactions among older adults. The intervention addresses not only physical and mental health issues common to elderly populations but also fosters creativity and a sense of purpose that contributes positively to aging experiences.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy is an effective tool for enhancing cognitive and emotional resilience in the elderhood population.
- Creative activities can mitigate some effects of biological decline, supporting physical and mental health maintenance among seniors.
- Engaging with art therapies encourages social interaction and community involvement, combating isolation commonly associated with aging.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of sustained participation in creative arts therapy for individuals experiencing elderhood?
