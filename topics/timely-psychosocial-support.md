## Timely Psychosocial Support

The provision of timely psychosocial support following disasters is a critical intervention that aims to mitigate the immediate psychological impact on affected individuals. The concept of Psychological First Aid (PFA) emphasizes the importance of emotional and physical care in the aftermath of traumatic events, such as natural disasters. PFA involves providing comfort, reducing distress, fostering social connections, and ensuring basic needs are met to help victims feel safe and secure immediately after a disaster. This approach contrasts with traditional first aid which is primarily focused on addressing physical injuries, underscoring the need for integrated care that includes mental health support right from the start. Across multiple sources, there is consensus about the necessity of immediate psychological interventions but also recognition of ongoing needs beyond the initial response phase.

## Key Findings

- Timely psychosocial support is crucial for addressing both emotional and physical distress in disaster-affected populations.
- Psychological First Aid (PFA) provides a framework that includes emotional comfort, reducing distress, and promoting social connections in disaster scenarios.
- The integration of mental health interventions with traditional first aid approaches enhances overall recovery and resilience among affected individuals.
