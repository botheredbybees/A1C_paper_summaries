## Therapeutic Goals in Medical Clowning

The research on medical clowns highlights the multifaceted role these performers play beyond mere entertainment. They use a range of specialized communication skills to engage with patients, aiming at achieving specific therapeutic goals that enhance patient well-being and recovery processes. These goals include reducing anxiety and pain, increasing social interaction among patients, promoting positive mood states, facilitating coping mechanisms for stress, and supporting the overall healing environment in medical settings. Despite their clear impact on these aspects of patient care, there remains a need to further delineate how specific communication techniques translate into measurable therapeutic outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Medical clowns use specialized communication skills to achieve therapeutic goals.
- Therapeutic goals include reducing anxiety and pain, increasing social interaction among patients, promoting positive mood states.
- Facilitating coping mechanisms for stress is a key goal of medical clowning interventions.

## Open Questions

- How do specific communication techniques employed by medical clowns translate into measurable improvements in patient outcomes?
