## Emotional Reactions to Music

The examination of emotional reactions to music reveals a complex interplay between individual characteristics and contextual factors. Source 1 highlights the role of personalized music choice in evoking specific emotions, demonstrating that listener personality—particularly openness to experience—affects how individuals respond emotionally to musical stimuli. Conversely, Source 2 emphasizes the importance of personal-emotion-experience and sound-and-rhythm as pivotal elements in understanding emotional reactions without focusing on personality traits. Together, these sources suggest a dual influence of both individual predispositions and the intrinsic qualities of music itself on emotional responses. Despite this agreement on the multifaceted nature of emotional reactions to music, there is an open question regarding how external social contexts might further modulate these internal dynamics.

## Key Findings

- Personalized music choice can induce specific emotions based on listener personality traits.
- The intrinsic qualities of sound and rhythm are critical in shaping personal-emotion-experience.

## Open Questions

- How do external social contexts influence emotional reactions to music beyond individual personality and musical qualities?
