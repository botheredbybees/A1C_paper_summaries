## Narrative Tense

The concept of narrative tense is explored in the provided sources, focusing on the use of past and present tenses to convey different aspects of storytelling. Past tense is often used to recount events that have already happened, offering a retrospective view with clear temporal boundaries. In contrast, present tense creates an immediacy and directness, making the narrative feel more immediate and involving for readers or viewers. However, the choice between past and present tenses can impact how effectively a story is told, influencing reader engagement and comprehension. Visual storytelling complements these textual narratives by adding layers of meaning through images and visual cues that are not explicitly described in text alone.

## Key Findings

- The use of past tense provides context and clarity about events that have already transpired.
- Present tense enhances the immediacy and direct involvement of the audience with the narrative.
- Visual storytelling can enhance textual narratives by adding visual cues and imagery.
