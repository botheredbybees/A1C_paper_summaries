## Depth Of Field

The concept of depth of field (DoF) in photography is a critical aspect that influences the aesthetic and technical quality of images. Sources emphasize the role of aperture settings as a primary means to control DoF, alongside considering focal length. In aperture-priority mode, photographers can directly manipulate the size of the aperture to achieve desired levels of sharpness from foreground to background within their frame. Conversely, shutter priority mode offers less direct influence over depth of field but enables adjustment for controlling motion blur and exposure time. Across these methods, achieving a shallow DoF involves selecting wider apertures (lower f-stop numbers) while longer focal lengths contribute to narrowing the range of sharp focus in front-to-back direction.

## Key Findings

- Aperture settings are crucial for manipulating depth of field
- Longer focal lengths result in narrower depths of field
- Shutter priority mode focuses primarily on motion blur and exposure, not directly on DoF
