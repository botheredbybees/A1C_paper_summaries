## Neurodiversity and Social Justice in Music Therapy

The integration of neurodiversity principles into arts-based therapies such as music therapy presents a robust framework for promoting social justice. By recognizing the inherent value of neurological differences rather than viewing them through a deficit model, practitioners can tailor interventions to honor individual strengths and preferences. This approach not only enhances therapeutic outcomes but also challenges societal norms that marginalize neurodivergent individuals. However, implementing this paradigm shift requires significant educational reform within healthcare professions to ensure widespread adoption. The interplay between artistic expression and social justice is evident in the use of play and creativity as therapeutic tools for advocating inclusivity.

## Key Findings

- Neurodiversity emphasizes the value and strengths inherent in neurological differences, challenging deficit models often prevalent in clinical settings.
- Music therapy offers a platform to integrate neurodiversity principles into practice through personalized and strength-based interventions.
- Social justice in therapeutic contexts is facilitated by arts-based approaches that promote inclusivity and challenge societal norms.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to widespread adoption of neurodiversity frameworks within professional training programs for music therapists?
