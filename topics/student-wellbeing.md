## Student Wellbeing

The synthesis of student wellbeing interventions highlights the therapeutic functions of art as a means to promote emotional healing and support mental health among students. Art therapy not only serves as an individual tool for self-expression but also integrates into collaborative educational environments, fostering a supportive community atmosphere conducive to overall well-being. Additionally, university settings play a critical role in enhancing student welfare by offering inclusive spaces that align with Seligman's theory dimensions of positive emotions, engagement, and meaning. These features are particularly important for marginalized groups such as LGBTQ+ students studying creative arts, suggesting that institutional support is integral to the effectiveness of art-based therapeutic interventions.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy contributes significantly to emotional healing through creativity.
- Collaborative teaching approaches enhance the supportive community environment in universities.
- Inclusive university environments are crucial for the well-being of marginalized student groups.

## Open Questions

- How can institutions better integrate art therapy into broader mental health support frameworks?
