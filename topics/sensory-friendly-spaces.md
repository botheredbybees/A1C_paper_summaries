## Sensory-Friendly Spaces

The integration of sensory-friendly spaces in various environments is highlighted as a beneficial intervention aimed at improving accessibility and well-being for diverse populations. Museums, among other public institutions, are increasingly adopting interdisciplinary approaches to create such spaces, which cater specifically to individuals with sensory sensitivities or disabilities. These initiatives often involve collaboration between designers, therapists, educators, and community members to ensure that the environments are inclusive and supportive. However, there is a noted tension in balancing the need for tailored interventions with the desire to maintain the aesthetic and educational integrity of these spaces.

## Key Findings

- Sensory-friendly spaces improve accessibility for individuals with sensory sensitivities or disabilities
- Interdisciplinary approaches enhance the effectiveness of creating inclusive environments
- Collaboration between various stakeholders is crucial in designing supportive spaces

## Open Questions

- How can institutions balance the creation of sensory-friendly spaces while maintaining their educational and aesthetic missions?
