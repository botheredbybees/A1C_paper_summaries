## Network Meta-Analysis in Healthcare Research

Network meta-analysis (NMA) is a statistical method used to synthesize evidence from multiple randomized controlled trials to compare the efficacy of different interventions simultaneously. Source 1 discusses NMA applied to various art therapies for patients with dementia, focusing on improvements in cognitive function and agitation behavior. It highlights that while traditional pairwise meta-analyses can only compare two treatments at once, NMAs allow for a more comprehensive evaluation across several therapeutic modalities. Similarly, Source 2 examines the impact of hospital clowning on pediatric anxiety and pain through NMA, showing how this technique offers greater flexibility in assessing multiple interventions within the same framework. Both sources underscore that NMA can provide nuanced insights into comparative effectiveness and safety profiles among competing therapies, aiding clinicians and researchers in making more informed decisions.

## Key Findings

- Network meta-analysis enables comparisons of multiple treatments simultaneously, offering a comprehensive evaluation beyond pairwise comparisons.
- NMAs are useful for assessing the comparative efficacy of various therapeutic modalities in different healthcare settings.
- The application of NMA can provide deeper insights into treatment effectiveness and safety profiles.
