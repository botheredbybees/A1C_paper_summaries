## Community-Based Participatory Arts

The integration of community-based participatory arts activities offers a robust intervention that supports both dementia patients and older individuals by fostering person-centered care and encouraging in-the-moment experiences. These activities not only enhance participation and communication but also facilitate intergenerational collaboration through the democratization of technology, as seen in various case studies like The Active Energy Project. However, while these practices bring numerous benefits such as improved mental health and social engagement, there are challenges related to sustaining long-term engagement and ensuring equitable access across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Community-based participatory arts improve communication and participation for dementia patients
- Intergenerational collaboration through technology democratization enhances creative thinking among older adults
- Person-centered care is a cornerstone of effective community-based participatory arts programs

## Open Questions

- How can long-term engagement be sustained in community-based participatory arts activities?
