## Ethnomusicology

Ethnomusicology encompasses a broad spectrum of research and practice methodologies aimed at understanding the role of music in communities. Sources highlight that ethnomusicologists engage deeply with local arts to foster community development, health, well-being, and sustainability. The method emphasizes ethical engagement and relationship-building as foundational principles. Key concepts like 'verbatim principle' and 'concrete principle' underscore the importance of accurate documentation and identification of cultural themes in fieldwork. Additionally, there is an emphasis on applying these methods not just for academic purposes but also to promote better community life through arts-based initiatives. However, sources suggest that despite its potential, ethnomusicology faces challenges in ensuring long-term sustainability and broadening its impact beyond traditional research settings.

## Key Findings

- Ethnomusicologists engage with local communities to foster social benefit through arts
- Accurate documentation principles are crucial for effective fieldwork
- Ethical engagement and relationship-building form the core of ethnomusicology practice

## Open Questions

- How can ethnomusicological practices be made more sustainable in various contexts?
