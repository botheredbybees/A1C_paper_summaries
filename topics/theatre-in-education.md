## Theatre-in-Education

The integration of theatre in education as a therapeutic intervention highlights its profound impact on dementia care. Sources consistently emphasize the enhancement of dignity and identification with characters through aesthetic experiences, which mitigate personhood-undermining elements often found in malignant social psychology contexts (Source 1). The Veder Contact Method, an active engagement technique within theatre, encourages tacit knowledge sharing, aligning with the principle of person-centered care to enhance life quality for individuals living with dementia. However, while some studies underscore the transformative potential of theatre in education for dementia care, others highlight methodological challenges and the need for further exploration into how specific theatrical elements contribute effectively to improving quality of life (Source 3).

## Key Findings

- Theatre-in-education interventions can enhance dignity and person-centered care for individuals with dementia.
- Active engagement techniques like the Veder Contact Method foster tacit knowledge sharing among participants, promoting a sense of identity and community.
- Aesthetic experiences through theatre can help mitigate the undermining effects of malignant social psychology on persons with dementia.

## Open Questions

- What specific theatrical elements are most effective in enhancing quality of life for individuals with dementia?
