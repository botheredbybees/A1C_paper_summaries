## Geneva Emotional Music Scale

The Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS) is a tool designed to measure the emotional responses induced by music. It operates within both discrete and dimensional models of emotion, aiming to capture the complexity of musical emotions. The GEMS demonstrates utility in comparing different emotion models and understanding personality biases influencing emotional reactions to music. However, there are tensions between the theoretical underpinnings of these models and practical applications in measuring specific emotional responses.

## Key Findings

- The GEMS is effective for capturing both discrete and dimensional emotions induced by musical stimuli.
- Personality factors can significantly influence individual emotional responses to music as measured by the GEMS.
- There are valid comparisons between discrete emotion models and dimensional approaches in assessing music-induced emotions.

## Open Questions

- What are the limitations of applying discrete vs. dimensional models when measuring specific musical emotions using the GEMS?
