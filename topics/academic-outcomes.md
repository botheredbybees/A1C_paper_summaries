## Impact of Extracurricular Activities on Academic Outcomes

The integration of extracurricular activities into the educational landscape has been shown to have significant impacts on academic outcomes and attendance, particularly among students from disadvantaged backgrounds. Source 1 highlights how participation in such activities can lead to improved performance on standardized tests like PARCC, despite demographic disparities that might otherwise hinder student success. Moreover, these effects are not uniform across different socioeconomic groups, with free and reduced lunch eligibility indicating a potential gap in benefits received by less privileged students. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the specific realm of instrumental music education and its role in academic attainment at age 16, presenting it as an intervention that can positively influence school performance. While both sources underscore the importance of extracurricular engagement for academic success, they also point to variations in how different types of activities might benefit students differently based on their socioeconomic status.

## Key Findings

- Participation in extracurricular activities correlates with improved academic outcomes and test scores.
- Instrumental music education has a positive impact on academic attainment at age 16.
- Demographic disparities influence the effectiveness of extracurricular interventions.

## Open Questions

- How do different types of extracurricular activities (e.g., sports vs. arts) vary in their effects on academic outcomes across diverse socioeconomic backgrounds?
