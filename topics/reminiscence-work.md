## Reminiscence Work

The integration of personhood maintenance and reminiscence work reveals an intervention aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals with dementia by leveraging their biographical knowledge. Both sources highlight the importance of creating supportive environments that facilitate sensory engagement, which helps in stimulating memories from the past. The therapeutic approach centers around using reminiscence as a tool to connect residents to earlier periods of their lives and thus reinforce their sense of self and personal history. However, while one source underscores the role of biographical knowledge in personhood maintenance, the other focuses more on practical methods such as sensory stimuli to evoke memories. Despite this distinction, both emphasize the necessity for trained professionals who can effectively guide reminiscence sessions, ensuring that each interaction supports the unique needs and experiences of individuals with dementia.

## Key Findings

- Reminiscence work is a therapeutic intervention aimed at improving quality of life for individuals with dementia by engaging their biographical knowledge.
- Creating supportive environments that utilize sensory stimuli is crucial in evoking memories effectively during reminiscence sessions.
- Personhood maintenance and the reinforcement of personal identity are core objectives when conducting reminiscence work.
