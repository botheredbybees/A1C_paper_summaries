## Effect Size in Behavioral Sciences

Cohen's 'Statistical Power Analysis for the Behavioral Sciences' emphasizes the importance of effect size in research methodology, advocating for its use to determine statistical power and enhance study design. The book argues that understanding the magnitude of an effect is crucial beyond merely identifying if there is a statistically significant difference or relationship between variables. In contrast, 'What Works for Whom? A Critical Review of Psychotherapy Research' underscores the relevance of effect sizes in evaluating psychotherapy interventions but with a focus on personalized treatment approaches and contextual considerations. This work highlights how effect sizes can vary significantly across different populations, suggesting that understanding individual differences is imperative when applying general research findings to specific cases.

## Key Findings

- Effect size is crucial for conducting power analysis in behavioral science studies.
- Understanding the magnitude of an intervention's impact (effect size) helps refine personalized treatment approaches.
- Variability in effect sizes across different populations highlights the importance of contextual factors in psychotherapy research.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers more accurately determine and apply effect sizes that are representative of diverse populations?
