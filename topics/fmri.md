## Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (Fmri)

The sources provided offer insights into the application and implications of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) technology. Source 1 discusses the concept of brain lateralization and debunks myths surrounding left-brain/right-brain dominance, highlighting how fMRI can reveal more nuanced patterns of brain activity rather than simplistic dichotomies. In contrast, Source 2 delves into specific neurological profiles associated with autism spectrum disorder (ASD), utilizing fMRI to analyze synchronized brain activity that correlates with social dysfunction in ASD individuals. Together, these sources illustrate the versatility and depth of information that can be gleaned from fMRI studies—ranging from general cognitive functions to specific neural signatures linked to developmental disorders.

## Key Findings

- fMRI provides a non-invasive means to map brain activity patterns associated with various mental processes and neurological conditions.
- Myths about left-brain/right-brain dominance do not align with the complex, interconnected nature of human cognitive functions as revealed by fMRI studies.

## Open Questions

- How can the detailed insights from fMRI be effectively translated into practical interventions for individuals with autism or other neurological conditions?
