## Gestalt Play Therapy

Gestalt play therapy is a therapeutic intervention aimed at helping children manage and address symptoms associated with trauma. The therapy emphasizes the use of play to enhance self-awareness, emotional expression, and self-regulation skills in middle childhood. While it shows promise in alleviating distress and promoting resilience among traumatized children, there are gaps in understanding its long-term efficacy and mechanisms of action beyond symptom reduction.

## Key Findings

- Gestalt play therapy helps address trauma-related symptoms in children through enhanced emotional expression and self-awareness
- Self-regulation skills improve with the use of gestalt play therapy in middle childhood
- There is a growing body of evidence supporting the effectiveness of gestalt play therapy for trauma-related issues in children

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of gestalt play therapy on trauma recovery and emotional well-being?
