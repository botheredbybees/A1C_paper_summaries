## Mental Health Services

The integration of lived experience and trauma-informed practice in mental health services highlights a collaborative approach towards improving care quality. Source one emphasizes the importance of involving individuals with lived experience in service design, advocating for their authentic voice to ensure that services are relevant and effective. This perspective underscores the need for partnerships between mental health professionals and those who have directly experienced the system. Conversely, source two introduces trauma-informed practice as a critical framework for organizational assessment and improvement. The toolkit presented suggests an audit process aimed at identifying gaps in current practices to better support individuals affected by traumatic experiences. Together, these sources suggest that an effective mental health service should not only be informed by both professional expertise and client experience but also address the unique challenges posed by trauma.

## Key Findings

- The involvement of people with lived experience enhances the authenticity and effectiveness of mental health services.
- Trauma-informed practice is crucial for assessing and improving organizational responses to clients' needs.
- Service design benefits from a partnership approach between professionals and individuals who have experienced mental healthcare.

## Open Questions

- How can mental health organizations effectively integrate trauma-informed practices with the authentic voice of lived experience?
