## Berkeley Puppet Interview

The Berkeley Puppet Interview is an innovative form of therapy that utilizes puppetry to facilitate communication between children and therapists. This approach is rooted in child-centered therapy, which prioritizes the child's perspective and aims to build a supportive therapeutic relationship. Additionally, it incorporates principles from solution-focused brief therapy (SFBT), emphasizing positive outcomes and goal-oriented strategies. The Puppet Play Therapy: A Practical Guidebook outlines various techniques for using puppets as a tool for intervention, highlighting its utility in enhancing self-expression and emotional processing among children. While the use of puppetry is shown to be particularly effective with children who might feel anxious or reluctant about traditional therapy methods, there remains an ongoing need to further explore its efficacy across diverse populations and cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- The Berkeley Puppet Interview integrates child-centered and solution-focused therapeutic approaches
- Puppet play therapy enhances emotional expression and reduces anxiety in children during sessions
- There is a growing interest in utilizing puppetry as a tool for mental health interventions with young clients
