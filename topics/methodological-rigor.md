## Methodological Rigor in Non-Randomized Studies

The evaluation of non-randomized studies requires a robust approach to assessing methodological rigor and the risk of bias. The ROBINS-I tool provides a systematic way to evaluate the quality and reliability of such studies, addressing issues inherent in their design that might affect outcomes. Conversely, the Arts and Health Evaluation Toolkit emphasizes specific health outcome measures like mental health and quality of life, but does not explicitly address methodological rigor or risk assessment for non-randomized studies. This highlights the need for a combined approach where both the toolkit's focus on outcome evaluation and ROBINS-I’s detailed bias assessment can be integrated to provide a comprehensive methodology.

## Key Findings

- ROBINS-I is essential for assessing risk of bias in non-randomized study designs
- Specific health outcomes, such as mental health and quality of life, should also be evaluated
- Integration of ROBINS-I with outcome-focused evaluations improves overall methodological rigor

## Open Questions

- How can the Arts and Health Evaluation Toolkit integrate risk-of-bias assessment methods from tools like ROBINS-I?
