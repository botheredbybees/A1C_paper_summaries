## Perspective-Taking in Infants

The concept of perspective-taking emerges as a pivotal developmental milestone during infancy and early childhood. Sources emphasize the importance of understanding how infants begin to cognitively grasp the viewpoints of others, which is fundamental for their social-emotional development. Trauma-informed practices suggest that perspective-taking can be nurtured through play-based interventions designed to foster empathy and social understanding in young children. Play, particularly dramatic or pretend play, serves as a platform where infants practice recognizing and responding appropriately to different emotional states expressed by others. However, sources highlight the need for further exploration into how early-life stressors might affect this critical developmental process.

## Key Findings

- Perspective-taking is an essential skill that develops during infancy and childhood.
- Play-based interventions are effective in fostering perspective-taking skills in infants.
- Trauma-informed practices emphasize the importance of nurturing social-emotional development through play.

## Open Questions

- How do early-life stressors impact the development of perspective-taking abilities in infants?
