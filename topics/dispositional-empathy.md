## Dispositional Empathy

The concept of dispositional empathy plays a significant role in understanding emotional responses to live music performance. Research suggests that individuals with higher levels of dispositional empathy experience stronger emotional engagement and deeper connections during musical performances, linking their personal emotional states with the performer's expression. This phenomenon is further influenced by individual differences in visual imagery, which can enhance or detract from empathetic emotional experiences based on one’s capacity to visualize scenes or emotions vividly. However, while dispositional empathy contributes positively to such engagements, other factors like sublimity (the feeling of being overwhelmed by beauty) also play critical roles, sometimes overshadowing the direct impact of empathy.

## Key Findings

- Dispositional empathy significantly influences emotional engagement during live music performances.
- Individual differences in visual imagery can modulate the effect of dispositional empathy on emotional experiences.
- Sublimity, the sense of being overwhelmed by beauty or grandeur, interacts with empathy to shape aesthetic responses.

## Open Questions

- How do varying levels of sublimity experienced during live music performances affect the relationship between dispositional empathy and emotional engagement?
