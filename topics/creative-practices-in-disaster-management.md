## Creative Practices In Disaster Management

The integration of creative practices into disaster management strategies is increasingly recognized for its potential to build community resilience and facilitate post-disaster recovery. The National Taskforce's recommendations emphasize the importance of incorporating artistic activities such as music, visual arts, and storytelling in disaster response plans to foster inclusive community engagement and trauma processing. These initiatives not only aid in psychological healing but also enhance social cohesion among affected populations by creating a platform for shared experiences and collective memory formation.

## Key Findings

- Creative practices can serve as an effective means of building resilience within communities facing the threat of natural disasters.
- Inclusive community engagement through creative arts activities helps in trauma processing and psychological healing post-disaster.
- Facilitating access to artistic expression aids in strengthening social networks and fostering a sense of unity among community members.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods for integrating creative practices into existing disaster management frameworks without disrupting established protocols?
