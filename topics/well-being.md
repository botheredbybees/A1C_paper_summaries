## Well-Being Through Creative Arts

The integration of creative arts as an intervention to improve well-being is a recurring theme across multiple sources. Engaging in artistic activities such as music listening, theater-making, and writing has been shown to enhance various aspects of health and personal development. Sources highlight the role of creative arts in improving quality of life for older adults through engagement with the arts, promoting identity formation, and enhancing emotional states among adolescents. Additionally, these interventions contribute positively towards cultural sustainability by fostering community well-being and supporting institutional transformations such as those seen in museum learning evaluations. However, there is a tension between understanding the intrinsic value of artistic experiences and their measurable health benefits, suggesting that while creative activities are widely recognized for promoting mental health and personal agency, further research is needed to quantify their broader societal impacts.

## Key Findings

- Engagement with the arts can significantly improve quality of life in older adults
- Creative writing has therapeutic effects on mental health by aiding cognitive processing and emotional regulation
- Artistic activities contribute positively towards cultural sustainability and institutional transformation

## Open Questions

- How do we balance the intrinsic value of artistic experiences with their measurable health benefits?
