## Arts-Based Social Prescribing

Arts-based social prescribing has emerged as a promising intervention to address health inequalities and contribute to the Sustainable Development Goals. This approach integrates creative activities into healthcare plans, aiming to improve mental and physical well-being through engagement in arts-related projects or hobbies. Key benefits highlighted across studies include enhanced quality of life, reduced stress levels, and community-building effects. However, there are also challenges such as limited evidence on long-term efficacy and the need for robust cost-effectiveness analysis to support broader implementation.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based social prescribing can reduce health inequalities.
- Creative activities improve mental well-being and enhance quality of life.
- There is a lack of empirical data on long-term effectiveness.

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods for evaluating cost-effectiveness in arts-based interventions?
