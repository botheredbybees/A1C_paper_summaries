## Psychophysiological Effects of Creative Arts

The integration of psychophysiology with creative arts reveals significant impacts on emotional and physiological health. Dance movement therapy and physical exercise in older adults with mild dementia yield positive psychophysiological effects, enhancing both mental and physical well-being (Source 1). Cultural activities similarly bolster public health by improving emotional competence through the modulation of amygdala function and mirror neuron activity, indicating a broader benefit beyond specific therapeutic practices (Source 2). Poetry's influence on neurobiology extends this exploration further, demonstrating how aesthetic experiences are mediated through brain structures like the nucleus accumbens, contributing to peak emotion experiences and overall psychological health (Source 3). These studies collectively suggest that creative arts interventions can have profound psychophysiological benefits across various populations, yet there is a need for more research into the specific mechanisms and long-term effects.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies improve emotional and physical well-being in older adults with mild dementia.
- Cultural activities enhance public health by improving emotional competence through neurobiological pathways.
- Poetry engages brain structures associated with pleasure and reward, contributing to peak emotion experiences.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of creative arts interventions on mental and physical health?
