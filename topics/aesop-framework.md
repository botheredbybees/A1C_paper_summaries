## Aesop Framework for Arts in Health

The Aesop framework is presented as a standardized approach to developing and researching arts in health programs. It emphasizes the importance of structured methodologies that can be applied across various contexts, aiming to provide clarity and rigor to an otherwise diverse field. Both sources highlight the AESOP framework's role in facilitating co-production between stakeholders such as artists, healthcare providers, and patients. This collaborative process is crucial for the successful implementation and evaluation of arts-based health interventions. Despite its comprehensive nature, there are nuances in how this framework can be operationalized within different settings, reflecting a need for flexibility while maintaining methodological consistency.

## Key Findings

- The Aesop framework aims to standardize the development and research of arts in health programs.
- Co-production is an integral part of the AESOP framework's approach to arts-based interventions.
- There is a necessity for adaptability within the standardized methodology offered by AESOP.

## Open Questions

- How can the AESOP framework be effectively adapted to cater to diverse cultural and social contexts in health settings?
