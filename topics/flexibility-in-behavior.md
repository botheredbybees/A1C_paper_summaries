## Flexibility in Behavior

The evaluation of the 'Images of Self' art therapy program for children with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD) highlights the importance of interventions aimed at enhancing flexibility in behavior. This therapeutic approach seeks to improve participants' sense of self and emotion regulation, which are foundational skills that underpin behavioral adaptability. The study underscores the role of visual arts as a medium through which individuals can express themselves, thereby facilitating an enhanced understanding of their emotions and social interactions. However, while the program shows promise in fostering flexibility in behavior among children with ASD, there is variability in the extent to which individual participants benefit from such interventions.

## Key Findings

- Art therapy programs can enhance a sense of self and emotion regulation in children with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD)
- Visual arts serve as an effective medium for expressing emotions and understanding social interactions
- Interventions that foster flexibility in behavior are crucial for improving social skills among individuals with ASD

## Open Questions

- What factors contribute to the variability in individual responses to interventions aimed at enhancing flexibility in behavior?
