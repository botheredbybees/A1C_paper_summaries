## Self-Esteem Building

The concept of enhancing self-esteem through various therapeutic interventions is explored in two distinct but related sources. Source 1 discusses the use of photography as a tool for emotional healing and self-efficacy, illustrating how visual arts can serve as an alternative communication method to express personal narratives and foster therapeutic progress. This aligns with the broader goal of increasing self-confidence and resilience by enabling individuals to articulate their experiences in a creative way. Source 2 further supports this approach by highlighting the importance of creating safe spaces within youth-oriented programs, emphasizing activities that promote emotional healing and self-esteem building through alternative means like visual arts and other non-verbal communication techniques. Both sources underscore the potential of creative interventions to address deeper psychological issues related to low self-worth or poor self-image, suggesting that such approaches can be effective in diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Photography as a therapeutic tool can enhance emotional healing and self-efficacy among individuals seeking to improve their self-esteem.
- Safe spaces within youth programs are critical for fostering an environment conducive to emotional and psychological growth through alternative communication methods.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of using creative interventions such as photography on participants' overall mental health and sustained self-esteem levels?
