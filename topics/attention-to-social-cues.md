## Attention to Social Cues

The sources collectively suggest that interventions employing simplified social stimuli can enhance children's ability to attend to and process social cues. Specifically, the use of puppets in therapeutic settings is highlighted as an effective method for facilitating reciprocal interactions among children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). This approach simplifies complex social environments, making it easier for these children to focus on relevant social signals. However, while there is evidence supporting the efficacy of such interventions, questions remain about their long-term impacts and applicability across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Interventions using simplified social stimuli can improve attention to social cues in children with ASD.
- Puppets are effective tools for facilitating reciprocal interactions in therapeutic settings.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of interventions using simplified social stimuli on children's ability to attend to complex social cues?
