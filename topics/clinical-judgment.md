## Clinical Judgment in Neurocognitive Disorders

The integration of clinical judgment into the diagnostic process for neurocognitive disorders as outlined in the DSM-5-TR supplement highlights a nuanced approach to diagnosis, where medical etiologies and diagnostic criteria serve as foundational elements but are supplemented by professional discretion. Clinical judgment plays a pivotal role in differentiating between various types of neurocognitive disorders based on clinical presentation, progression patterns, and response to interventions, beyond what is strictly outlined in the diagnostic criteria.

## Key Findings

- Clinical judgment complements medical etiologies in diagnosing neurocognitive disorders.
- Diagnostic criteria serve as a basis but require professional discretion for accurate diagnosis.
- Differentiation between types of neurocognitive disorders relies heavily on clinical presentation and progression patterns.
