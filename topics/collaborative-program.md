## Collaborative Program for Eating Disorders

The examination of a collaborative multiple-visit program aimed at supporting individuals with eating disorders highlights the importance of interdisciplinary approaches and art therapy in fostering positive body image. This study underscores that effective interventions should not only address psychological aspects but also incorporate creative techniques to empower patients in their recovery journey. The use of a collaborative model allows for personalized care, integrating diverse expertise and therapeutic modalities tailored to individual needs.

## Key Findings

- Collaborative programs enhance patient outcomes by integrating multiple disciplines and perspectives
- Art therapy is beneficial for fostering positive body image among individuals with eating disorders
- Personalized treatment plans that include creative interventions are more effective

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of collaborative programs on recovery rates?
