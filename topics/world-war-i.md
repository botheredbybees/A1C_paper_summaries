## Shell Shock and Treatment Innovations in World War I

World War I witnessed the emergence of shell shock as a significant psychological condition among soldiers. Historically constructed and initially poorly understood, shell shock was later recognized as an early form of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). The diagnosis marked a shift in medical perceptions of psychological trauma caused by warfare. Concurrently, innovative treatment methods were developed to address the burgeoning mental health crisis. One such method was the color treatment introduced at the Red Cross Russell Lea Nerve Home, which utilized abstract color theory and art as part of therapy for nervous disorders, reflecting an interdisciplinary approach combining medicine with art to treat shell shock patients.

## Key Findings

- Shell shock emerged during World War I as a psychological condition among soldiers.
- Color treatment was employed as an innovative therapeutic intervention for nervous disorders in WWI veterans.
- Art and medical practices converged in the form of color therapy at specialized nerve homes.
