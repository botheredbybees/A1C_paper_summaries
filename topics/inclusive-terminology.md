## Inclusive Terminology for Dementia

Sources highlight the importance of using inclusive and non-stigmatizing language when discussing dementia. Both documents emphasize the use of person-first language (e.g., 'person with dementia' rather than 'demented person'), which is aimed at shifting societal perception and treatment of individuals affected by dementia towards a more respectful, compassionate framework. Cultural sensitivity is also noted as crucial in adapting communication practices to reflect diverse cultural contexts and values regarding aging and disability. The impact of language on public perception and understanding of dementia underscores the need for ongoing education and guideline dissemination.

## Key Findings

- Person-first language improves societal perception and treatment of individuals with dementia.
- Cultural sensitivity is critical in developing inclusive terminology guidelines.
- Language significantly influences public attitudes towards those living with dementia.
