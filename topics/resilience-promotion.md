## Resilience Promotion

The concept of resilience promotion is central to the discussion on how communities, especially children in post-disaster contexts, can maintain and enhance their ability to cope with adversity. Source 1 introduces a SPIT (Space, People, Interventions, Time) model that emphasizes the interconnectedness between environmental conditions, social interactions, specific interventions, and temporal dynamics in fostering resilience among children during recovery periods after disasters. This holistic approach suggests that resilient outcomes are not solely dependent on individual factors but also significantly influenced by environmental and relational support structures.

## Key Findings

- Resilience-promotion interventions should consider the interplay between physical environments, social networks, specific actions, and time frames.
- Children's resilience in post-disaster contexts can be supported through structured outdoor play activities that leverage natural spaces and community resources.
- Temporal dynamics are crucial for effective resilience promotion strategies, as they account for both immediate needs and long-term developmental considerations.

## Open Questions

- How can the SPIT model be adapted to address diverse cultural contexts and varying types of disasters?
