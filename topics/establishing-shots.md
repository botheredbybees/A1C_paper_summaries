## Establishing Shots in Visual Storytelling

The concept of establishing shots within visual storytelling is emphasized across the provided sources. Both sources discuss how establishing shots serve to set the stage for a narrative, providing context and grounding viewers in time and space before delving into more detailed scenes or actions. These opening visuals are crucial for informing audiences about setting, mood, and potential themes that will recur throughout the story. Additionally, these sources highlight the importance of understanding different types of narratives—both linear and non-linear—which can influence how establishing shots are used to convey information efficiently. Shot-listing is also mentioned as a technique that filmmakers use to plan and strategically place establishing shots within their narrative structures.

## Key Findings

- Establishing shots provide essential context at the beginning of visual narratives.
- Understanding both linear and non-linear story structures informs shot placement.
- Shot-listing is a useful planning tool for integrating establishing shots effectively.
