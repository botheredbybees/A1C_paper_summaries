## Clown Doctors Program

The Clown Doctors Program is a unique intervention designed to provide psychosocial support through the medium of clowns. The program's theoretical framework, known as the Laughter Care Framework, seeks to enhance patient well-being and reduce stress through humor and playfulness. This strategy aligns with a theory-of-change model that posits laughter and joy can facilitate healing and comfort in clinical settings. However, while some sources highlight the positive impact of clown interventions on patient morale and staff engagement, others note a need for more rigorous evaluation of outcomes to substantiate claims about its efficacy.

## Key Findings

- The Clown Doctors Program aims to improve patient well-being through humor and play.
- A theory-of-change model underpins the program's approach, emphasizing laughter as a therapeutic tool.
- There is evidence supporting positive impacts on staff engagement and morale.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of clown interventions on patients' physical and mental health outcomes?
