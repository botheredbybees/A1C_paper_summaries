## Creativity In Dementia

The integration of creative practices into dementia care highlights a multifaceted approach to understanding and supporting individuals with dementia. Source 1 emphasizes the concept of embodied selfhood, which posits that an individual's identity is deeply connected to their bodily experiences and expressions. This perspective underlines the importance of recognizing patients' continued capacity for self-expression despite cognitive decline, particularly through creative activities like arts participation. Similarly, Source 2 elaborates on how engaging in artistic endeavors can serve as a means of self-expression and enhance well-being among people with dementia, reinforcing the idea that creativity offers more than just an outlet; it supports emotional and social engagement crucial to quality of life.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities offer significant benefits for individuals living with dementia, including opportunities for continued self-expression.
- The concept of embodied selfhood underscores the importance of recognizing patients' identity through their ongoing physical and creative expressions.
- Engaging in arts can enhance emotional well-being by providing a sense of connection and purpose.

## Open Questions

- What specific forms of creativity are most effective for supporting dementia care, given individual differences?
