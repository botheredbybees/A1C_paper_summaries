## Art Programs in Aged Care

The integration of artistic activities into aged care settings has been shown to have multifaceted benefits for residents, including enhanced quality of life and engagement. Source 1 highlights the 'Artists in the House Program', which aims to provide individuals with Alzheimer's Disease opportunities for self-expression through neural plasticity stimulation. This program underscores the importance of arts-based interventions as a means to improve mental health and cognitive function among elderly patients suffering from dementia-related conditions.

Source 2 discusses the CALE Research Aged Care Deloraine Partnership, focusing on how engagement with the arts contributes significantly to enhancing quality of life for aged care residents. This study emphasizes that such activities not only provide entertainment but also foster social connections and stimulate cognitive abilities among participants.

Finally, Source 3 introduces the MAC.ART program, which employs a person-centered approach through art therapy and communal art projects to cater to individual needs within an aging population. It demonstrates how tailored creative interventions can effectively promote psychological well-being and reduce feelings of isolation amongst elderly individuals residing in care facilities.

## Key Findings

- Art-based programs enhance cognitive stimulation and mental health for residents with dementia or Alzheimer's Disease.
- Engagement with the arts improves quality of life and social interaction among aged care facility residents
- Person-centered art therapy supports individualized care that addresses unique needs and preferences

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of sustained participation in art programs on cognitive health and overall well-being?
