## Group Singing

Group singing emerges as a multifaceted intervention with potential benefits across psychological and physiological health dimensions. Source 1 highlights the stress hormone reduction and melatonin level increase associated with group singing, underscoring its therapeutic impact on the body's response to stress. Additionally, it mentions the role of music-based interventions in fostering community support networks, emphasizing the social dimension of this activity. Source 2 delves into a systematic review focusing on mental health outcomes for adults with mental health conditions who participate in community choir singing. This source identifies group singing as a viable intervention that can improve mental well-being and suggests that it might be more effective when participants are embedded within supportive social structures like community choirs.

## Key Findings

- Group singing reduces stress hormones and increases melatonin levels, indicating physiological health benefits.
- Community support networks formed through group singing contribute to psychological well-being.
- Adults with mental health conditions may experience improved mental health outcomes from participating in community choir activities.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of sustained participation in group singing for individuals' mental and physical health?
