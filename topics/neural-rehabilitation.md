## Neural Rehabilitation

Sources highlight the concept of neural rehabilitation through the lens of neuroplasticity and targeted brain training. Both texts emphasize the brain's adaptability to change after injury or disease, with a focus on recovery mechanisms that can be harnessed for therapeutic purposes. The use of brain mapping is crucial in understanding how specific areas of the brain contribute to different functions and how these maps can be restructured through interventions. This approach not only aids in enhancing perception but also supports personal triumphs over injuries or illnesses, underscoring the potential for personalized treatment plans based on individual neural mappings.

## Key Findings

- Neuroplasticity offers a foundation for recovery from brain injury and disease.
- Brain mapping is essential for understanding and treating neural deficits.
- Targeted interventions can restructure brain function and perception.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in neuroplasticity impact the effectiveness of rehabilitation strategies?
