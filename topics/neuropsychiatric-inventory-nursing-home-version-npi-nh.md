## Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Nursing Home Version (Npi-nh)

The Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Nursing Home version (NPI-NH) is a standardized assessment tool designed to evaluate the behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) in individuals residing in long-term care facilities. It serves as a crucial instrument for healthcare providers, particularly in pilot studies focusing on innovative interventions such as elder-clowning. The NPI-NH's comprehensive nature allows it to measure various aspects of BPSD, including delusions, hallucinations, agitation/aggression, depression/dysphoria, anxiety, elation/euphoria, apathy/indifference, disinhibition, irritability/lability, motor disturbance, and nighttime behaviors. In a pilot study involving elder-clowning in long-term dementia care, the NPI-NH was employed to gauge the impact of this intervention on reducing BPSD symptoms among participants. The results highlighted its utility in quantifying changes in patients' neuropsychiatric conditions over time.

## Key Findings

- The NPI-NH is a standardized tool for assessing behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) in nursing home residents.
- It includes a wide range of symptom categories, such as delusions, hallucinations, agitation/aggression, depression/dysphoria, anxiety, elation/euphoria, apathy/indifference, disinhibition, irritability/lability, motor disturbance, and nighttime behaviors.
- Elder-clowning can be evaluated using the NPI-NH to measure its impact on reducing BPSD symptoms.
