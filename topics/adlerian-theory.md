## Adlerian Theory

Adlerian theory emphasizes the importance of social connectedness and personal responsibility in fostering individual growth and well-being. This theory aligns with the concept of resilience, which is the capacity to thrive despite adversity. In examining immigrant and refugee youth experiences through a community-based arts practice, resilience and hope emerge as pivotal factors influenced by Adlerian principles. These findings highlight how supportive social environments can nurture resilience among individuals facing significant challenges, such as migration or displacement. The integration of Adlerian theory into interventions targeting these populations underscores the value of promoting interpersonal relationships and collective empowerment.

## Key Findings

- Adlerian theory highlights the role of personal responsibility and social connectedness in individual development.
- Community-based arts practices can foster resilience among immigrant and refugee youth, aligning with Adlerian principles.
- Supportive environments that encourage hope and connection are essential for promoting resilience.

- How can Adlerian theory be further adapted to address the unique challenges faced by diverse populations such as immigrants and refugees?
