## Humanistic Medicine

The integration of humanistic approaches into medical education and practice highlights the importance of empathy, open-ended questioning, and active listening. Source 1 discusses how Penn State Medical School integrates lessons from historical texts to teach students the 'art of medicine', focusing on enhancing communication skills such as active listening and empathetic engagement with patients. Meanwhile, Source 2 introduces a pedagogical framework for teaching observational skills that are crucial in empathy-driven healthcare. Both sources emphasize that fostering these interpersonal competencies is essential not only for effective patient care but also for nurturing a deeper understanding of the human condition among medical practitioners.

## Key Findings

- Humanistic medicine emphasizes empathetic communication and active listening between doctors and patients
- Incorporating lessons from historical texts helps modern medical students understand the 'art of medicine'
- Observational skills are fundamental to developing empathy in healthcare professionals

## Open Questions

- How can medical educators effectively integrate humanistic learning into the busy curriculum without compromising technical training?
