## Harmonic Complexity in Music

The investigation into the relationship between musical structure and emotional responses highlights harmonic complexity as a critical element that influences psychophysiological measures of emotion. Harmonic complexity, alongside rhythmic articulation, is examined for its effect on physiological responses, such as heart rate variability (HRV) and skin conductance levels. Across studies, it is observed that increased harmonic complexity can evoke more intense emotional reactions, correlating with higher physiological arousal. However, there are varying degrees of response depending on individual musical preferences and familiarity with the genre or style being analyzed. This suggests a need for further exploration into how cultural context influences the perception and physiological reaction to harmonic complexity in music.

## Key Findings

- Harmonic complexity is linked to heightened emotional responses and increased physiological arousal.
- Rhythmic articulation, when combined with harmonic complexity, can significantly affect psychophysiological measures of emotion.
- Individual differences, including musical preferences and cultural context, play a role in how harmonic complexity is perceived and responded to physiologically.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences such as cultural background and prior musical experience influence the physiological responses to harmonic complexity?
