## Connection To Culture

The concept of 'connection to culture' emerges as a critical determinant of health and well-being in Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander communities. Across the reviewed literature, themes such as family/community connections, self-determination, and cultural identity consistently recur. These elements collectively reinforce the importance of maintaining strong ties with cultural heritage for promoting mental and physical health outcomes. The narrative overview highlights that while there is a substantial body of research underscoring these relationships, inconsistencies in operational definitions and methodologies pose challenges to achieving consensus on precise mechanisms through which culture impacts health.

## Key Findings

- Family/community connections are integral to the cultural determinants of health for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people.
- Self-determination is a key factor in improving health outcomes within indigenous populations.
- A robust connection to one's cultural heritage significantly contributes to overall well-being.

## Open Questions

- What specific mechanisms link cultural identity and self-determination to improved health outcomes?
