## Memory Retention

The integration of photography in daily digital practices is explored across the provided sources. One study discusses how photography can foster mindfulness and enhance community interaction, thereby positively influencing wellbeing (Source 1). Another source delves into how smartphone photography impacts memory retention, suggesting that it acts as a form of cognitive offloading where visual memories are stored externally through photos, potentially affecting internal visual memory retention processes (Source 2). Both sources highlight the dual nature of photography's impact on mental health and memory: while it can promote mindfulness and social cohesion, there is also concern over its role in altering how we remember personal experiences.

## Key Findings

- Photography as a daily digital practice promotes mindfulness and enhances community interactions positively impacting wellbeing.
- Smartphone photography leads to cognitive offloading where visual memories are stored externally in photos rather than internally.
- There is evidence that using photography can influence both mental health outcomes and memory retention processes.

## Open Questions

- How does the reliance on external storage of visual memories through smartphone photography affect long-term internal memory retention?
