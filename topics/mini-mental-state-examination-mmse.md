## Mini-Mental State Examination (Mmse)

The Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) is a widely used cognitive screening tool to assess various aspects of mental status, particularly in individuals with dementia. The study on dance and movement therapeutic methods reports improvements in visuospatial abilities, planning skills, and self-care ability among participants after engaging in this therapy, as measured by the MMSE. Similarly, the systematic review and meta-analysis examining game therapy's effects in people living with dementia found that while game therapy showed positive rehabilitation effects, these benefits were assessed primarily using cognitive tests like the MMSE alongside assessments of depressive symptoms such as the Cornell Scale for Depression in Dementia. However, neither study provides extensive details on how specific MMSE scores translate to real-world functional improvements or long-term care needs.

## Key Findings

- The Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) is a key tool used to assess cognitive functions in individuals with dementia.
- Dance and movement therapy can improve various aspects of mental status, including visuospatial abilities, planning skills, and self-care ability as measured by the MMSE.
- Game therapy also demonstrates positive rehabilitation effects for people living with dementia when evaluated using cognitive assessments like the MMSE.

## Open Questions

- How do changes in MMSE scores correlate with real-world functional improvements and long-term care needs?
