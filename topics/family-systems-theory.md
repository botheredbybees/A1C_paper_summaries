## Family Systems Theory

The sources emphasize the importance of Family Systems Theory in understanding and addressing mental health conditions within a familial context. This theoretical framework posits that individuals are interconnected with their family members, and each member's psychological issues can be influenced by the dynamics of the entire system rather than being isolated incidents. Source 1 explores how identity formation is affected by these systemic interactions for an individual diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The inclusion of OCD alongside ASD highlights the complexity of diagnosing and treating conditions within the family unit, as one member's mental health can impact others and be impacted by them in return.

## Key Findings

- Family Systems Theory provides a lens to understand how individual mental health issues are intertwined with familial dynamics.
- Identity formation is significantly influenced by interactions within the family system for individuals with ASD.
- Mental health conditions such as OCD and ASD can be better understood when considering their systemic context.
