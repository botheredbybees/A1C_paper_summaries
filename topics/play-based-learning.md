## Play-Based Learning

Play-based learning is a pedagogical approach that integrates play into educational settings to enhance child development. Source 1 highlights the critical-period theory and longitudinal studies as important methodologies in understanding how early experiences shape brain development through creative arts activities. Brain imaging techniques further support this, indicating specific neural pathways are established during these formative years. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on the mental health benefits of play-based learning for neurodiverse children, employing a social model of disability that emphasizes inclusion and accommodation over deficit models. This source introduces concepts like creative arts interventions and emotional regulation, which align with therapeutic benefits observed in play environments. Both sources underscore the importance of play as an intervention, though they differ in their primary focus areas: one on developmental neuroscience and the other on mental health and social inclusivity.

## Key Findings

- Play-based learning enhances cognitive development during critical periods of brain growth.
- Creative arts interventions are beneficial for emotional regulation among neurodiverse children.
- Longitudinal studies support the positive impact of play on child development.

## Open Questions

- How do longitudinal studies and brain imaging contribute to understanding the long-term benefits of play-based learning in mental health?
