## Healthy Cities

The concept of healthy cities is explored across various theoretical frameworks such as the social model of disability and dementia-friendly communities. These approaches emphasize the importance of creating inclusive environments that support health and well-being for all residents, particularly those with disabilities or chronic conditions like dementia. The focus on compassionate city initiatives highlights efforts to promote understanding and support among community members towards individuals with these challenges. However, while there is a strong emphasis on building supportive infrastructures, questions arise regarding the scalability of such initiatives across different urban contexts.

## Key Findings

- Healthy cities theory promotes inclusive environments for all residents.
- Dementia-friendly communities emphasize social inclusion for those affected by dementia.
- Compassionate city initiatives aim to foster community understanding and support towards individuals with disabilities.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of healthy cities be effectively scaled up in diverse urban settings?
