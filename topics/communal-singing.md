## Communal Singing

Communal singing is highlighted as an intervention that offers a range of benefits, including the enhancement of social connections and emotional health. Across various sources, communal singing is noted for its role in fostering a sense of belonging among participants. This activity encourages emotional release through music, contributing positively to mental well-being. However, there are differing views on how effective communal singing is as an intervention compared to individual therapeutic approaches, with some studies suggesting that the benefits might be more profound when singing is done within a group setting.

## Key Findings

- Communal singing fosters social connections and sense of belonging among participants
- Singing in groups provides emotional release through music
- Communal singing contributes to mental health improvements

## Open Questions

- How do the benefits of communal singing compare with those of individual therapeutic approaches?
