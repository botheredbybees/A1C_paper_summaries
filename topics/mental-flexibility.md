## Mental Flexibility

The provided sources collectively suggest that engaging in creative activities such as theatre and creative writing significantly enhances mental flexibility. These activities are associated with the development of cognitive reserve, which provides a buffer against cognitive decline by increasing neural adaptability and synaptic plasticity. Emotional intelligence also plays a crucial role; through these arts-based interventions, individuals can develop better emotional regulation skills that contribute to their overall mental resilience and adaptability. However, while there is agreement on the benefits of such activities for enhancing cognitive functions, the exact mechanisms linking creative engagement with improved mental flexibility remain underexplored.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions like theatre and creative writing can enhance cognitive reserve, supporting mental flexibility
- Engaging in these activities also develops emotional intelligence, which is integral to overall mental resilience
- The relationship between creative activities and mental flexibility suggests a broader applicability of arts-based therapies

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neural mechanisms through which theatre and creative writing enhance mental flexibility?
