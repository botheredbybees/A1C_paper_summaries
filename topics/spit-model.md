## Spit Model For Understanding Outdoor Play

The SPIT (Space, People, Interventions, and Time) model is introduced as a framework to understand children's outdoor play in post-disaster contexts. This model highlights the interplay between environmental factors, social interactions, intentional interventions aimed at promoting resilience, and temporal considerations relevant for assessing long-term impacts. While focusing primarily on resilience-promotion within recovery settings following natural disasters, it underscores the importance of tailored approaches that consider both immediate needs and sustained engagement strategies over time to foster resilient play behaviors among children.

## Key Findings

- The SPIT model provides a structured approach to understanding complex interactions influencing children's outdoor play in post-disaster scenarios.
- Resilience-promotion through targeted interventions is crucial for supporting the mental health and well-being of young individuals in challenging environments.

## Open Questions

- How can the SPIT framework be adapted or applied in contexts beyond disaster recovery, such as urban planning or community development?
