## Inclusive Arts Practices in Disability Studies

The integration of inclusive arts practices within disability studies is characterized by a shift from traditional therapeutic approaches to more socially integrative models. Source 1 emphasizes the social model of disability, which views disability as a societal issue rather than an individual medical condition, and highlights the importance of inclusive arts in fostering social inclusion and empowerment among individuals with disabilities. This perspective is further elaborated upon in Source 2, where 'inclusive practices' are discussed alongside 'creative empowerment', underlining the transformative potential of art to challenge societal barriers and promote a sense of belonging for marginalized communities. The practical application of these theoretical frameworks is evident in Source 3's mention of the Art Beyond Sight program and its collaboration with organizations such as the Lab For Learning (L4L) and the Art and Disability Institute (ADI). These initiatives showcase how inclusive arts can bridge gaps between disabled individuals and mainstream society, fostering a more equitable environment through creative expression.

## Key Findings

- The social model of disability advocates for inclusive practices that empower marginalized communities.
- Inclusive arts serve as a catalyst for social inclusion by challenging societal barriers and promoting equality.
- Practical programs like Art Beyond Sight exemplify the application of theoretical frameworks in fostering meaningful engagement among people with disabilities.

## Open Questions

- How can traditional therapeutic models be adapted to incorporate the principles of inclusive arts practices without compromising individualized care?
