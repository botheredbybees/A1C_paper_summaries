## Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy

The integration of sources on cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) and its applications reveals both specific therapeutic techniques and broader implications for psychological interventions. Source 2 highlights CBT as a primary method for treating conditions such as post-traumatic stress disorder, complemented by advanced methods like eye movement desensitization and reprocessing (EMDR). Meanwhile, Source 1 introduces an innovative approach to traditional CBT through the use of puppet-assisted play therapy, emphasizing divergent thinking and metaphorical conflict resolution. While these sources underscore the versatility of CBT in addressing complex psychological issues, they also reveal the ongoing exploration of adjunctive methods that enhance its efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is a widely used intervention for treating post-traumatic stress disorder and other mental health conditions.
- Adjunctive therapies like EMDR can complement traditional CBT, enhancing therapeutic outcomes.
- Puppet-assisted play therapy represents an innovative application of divergent thinking within the framework of CBT.
