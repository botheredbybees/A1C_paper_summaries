## Theatrical Therapy

Theatrical therapy emerges as a versatile intervention with applications ranging from general social issues to specific healthcare contexts like dementia management. Source 1 outlines the transformative power of theatre in addressing societal problems and enhancing personal development, underscoring its potential across diverse populations and settings. This source also maps out a continuum of applied theatre, suggesting its broad utility and adaptability. In contrast, Source 2 narrows the focus to living room theatre activities for people with dementia within nursing home environments. Here, theatrical therapy is integrated into person-centered care frameworks, highlighting its role in enhancing patient engagement and quality of life. The study also emphasizes the importance of executive support for successful implementation, a critical aspect often overlooked in broader theoretical discussions.

## Key Findings

- Theatrical therapy has significant potential for personal transformation and societal impact.
- Person-centered care can be enhanced through theatrical activities designed to engage individuals with dementia.
- Executive-level support is crucial for the effective implementation of theatrical interventions.

## Open Questions
