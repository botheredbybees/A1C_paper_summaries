## Music Therapy

The integration of music into therapeutic practices has been shown to have numerous benefits for individuals with dementia. The sources highlight that auditory processing in older adults may deteriorate over time, yet the response to musical stimuli can remain intact, making it a valuable intervention tool. Music therapy is particularly beneficial as it engages multiple cognitive domains and promotes emotional expression, social interaction, and memory recall, even when other forms of communication are impaired. This therapeutic approach utilizes the unique characteristics of music such as rhythm, melody, and harmony to improve quality of life for those suffering from dementia.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy benefits individuals with dementia by enhancing cognitive functions and emotional well-being.
- Auditory processing remains relatively preserved in older adults compared to other sensory modalities.
- Engaging musical interventions can facilitate social interaction among people with dementia.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of music therapy on memory retention for individuals with dementia?
