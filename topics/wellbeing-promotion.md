## Wellbeing Promotion Through Arts and Drama

The integration of arts into health and wellbeing strategies is explored in both sources, highlighting diverse methodologies and benefits. Source one advocates for the removal of institutional silos between art and public health sectors to enhance interdisciplinary collaboration, emphasizing that the interconnectedness of art and healing can lead to innovative solutions for supporting health and wellbeing across the United States. In contrast, source two focuses on a specific intervention using community-based drama to promote the health and wellbeing of young Black men in New York City. This approach builds self-esteem and critical awareness through the creation of safe spaces that encourage peer trust-building and foster a sense of agency among participants. Both sources underscore the potential for arts interventions to address systemic challenges by engaging artists directly within public health initiatives, thereby creating unique opportunities for healing and empowerment.

## Key Findings

- Arts integration can enhance interdisciplinary collaboration between art and public health sectors.
- Community-based drama is effective in promoting self-esteem and critical awareness among young Black men.
- Safe spaces created through arts interventions foster trust-building and agency.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences influence the efficacy of arts-based wellbeing promotion strategies?
