## Focus Groups in Evaluating Community Programs

The utilization of focus groups as a qualitative research method is highlighted across both sources. Source 1 discusses the application of focus groups to understand the experiences and benefits derived by working adults participating in regular choral activities, emphasizing aspects such as self-improvement, sense-of-community, positive-self-identity, and healing-and-happiness. Similarly, Source 2 employs focus groups to evaluate an arts and mental health project, focusing on enhancing mental well-being and social inclusion through art therapy. Both studies underscore the effectiveness of focus groups in gathering detailed insights into participants' experiences within community programs aimed at improving quality of life. Despite variations in the specific domains (choral activities vs. arts-based interventions), both sources agree that focus groups offer a valuable means to explore personal narratives and communal impacts, contributing to a holistic understanding of program efficacy.

## Key Findings

- Focus groups effectively capture nuanced participant experiences within community programs.
- Both choral activities and art-based interventions demonstrate positive outcomes in enhancing participants' sense-of-community and self-improvement.
- Participant satisfaction is a critical indicator for assessing the success of mental well-being and social inclusion initiatives.

## Open Questions

- How can focus group methodologies be further adapted to account for diverse cultural backgrounds among participants?
