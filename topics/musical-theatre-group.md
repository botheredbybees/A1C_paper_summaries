## Musical Theatre Groups for Dementia Patients

The inclusion of individuals with dementia as 'spect-actors' in a community-based musical theatre setting offers unique benefits and challenges. This model emphasizes the autonomy and active participation of those living with dementia, integrating them into creative arts practices alongside students from the broader community. While the concept provides an innovative platform for enhancing social engagement and promoting dignity through performance, it also raises questions about the extent to which such interventions can be universally applied due to varying degrees of cognitive impairment among participants.

## Key Findings

- Musical theatre groups offer a unique intervention approach for dementia patients.
- The spect-actors framework is designed to promote autonomy in individuals with dementia.
- Community involvement through student participation enriches the therapeutic benefits of musical theatre interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can the spect-actors model be adapted for varying levels of cognitive ability among dementia patients?
