## Improvisational Music Therapy

The reviewed literature on music therapy for children with neurodevelopmental disorders, including Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), highlights the effectiveness of improvisational music therapy. This intervention involves spontaneous creation and interaction in musical experiences to foster social communication skills and emotional regulation among participants. The studies indicate that improvisational elements are crucial as they allow therapists to adapt sessions based on individual needs, making this approach particularly beneficial for children with ASD. However, there is a need for further research to explore how these methods can be tailored effectively across diverse populations within neurodevelopmental disorders.

## Key Findings

- Improvisational music therapy is effective in enhancing social communication and emotional regulation among children with neurodevelopmental disorders, especially those with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD).
- Educational-music-therapy approaches may benefit from incorporating improvisation to enhance therapeutic outcomes.
- Adaptability of sessions based on individual needs through improvisation is a key feature that distinguishes its efficacy.

## Open Questions

- How can improvisational music therapy be further tailored and standardized for diverse populations within neurodevelopmental disorders?
