## Creativity Development

The development of creativity is a multifaceted process that involves both practical and theoretical components. Source 1 highlights the importance of journaling as a documentation practice to capture the creative process, which can help individuals reflect on their creative experiences and enhance self-awareness. This intervention aids in understanding personal patterns and fostering new ideas through reflection. In contrast, Source 2 delves into the neuroscience behind creativity, suggesting that processes such as synaesthesia play a significant role in how the brain generates novel ideas and connects seemingly unrelated concepts. Both sources underscore the complexity of creative development by emphasizing different aspects: one focusing on practical tools for nurturing creativity (journaling) while the other addresses underlying neural mechanisms.

## Key Findings

- Journaling serves as an effective tool for reflecting on and understanding personal patterns in the creative process.
- Synaesthesia is linked to enhanced creativity through the brain's ability to make novel connections between disparate concepts.

## Open Questions

- How do practical interventions like journaling interact with neurological processes such as synaesthesia to influence creativity?
