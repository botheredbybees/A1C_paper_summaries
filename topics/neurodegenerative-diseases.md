## Dementia and Its Forms

The provided sources delve into the diverse forms of dementia, highlighting their unique presentations and impacts. Source 1 introduces several types of dementia including Alzheimer's disease, vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, and frontotemporal dementia (FTD). These conditions vary in terms of underlying pathology, symptomatology, and progression patterns. Notably, FTD is distinguished by its early onset behavioral changes compared to cognitive impairments typical in other dementias like Alzheimer’s. In contrast, Source 2 shifts focus towards the therapeutic aspects, emphasizing how individuals with certain forms of dementia, particularly those associated with preserved creativity, can engage productively in artistic activities. Visual arts are posited as a viable therapeutic avenue for FTD patients, providing both cognitive stimulation and emotional expression opportunities.

## Key Findings

- Dementia encompasses multiple subtypes, each characterized by distinct clinical features and pathologies
- Frontotemporal dementia (FTD) is noted for its early behavioral symptoms over cognitive decline
- Artistic activities can serve as therapeutic interventions for individuals with specific types of dementia

## Open Questions

- How effective are artistic therapies in managing the diverse symptom profiles of different dementias?
