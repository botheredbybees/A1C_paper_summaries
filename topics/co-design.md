## Co-Design in Indigenous Arts and Mental Wellbeing

The concept of co-design emerges prominently as a vital methodology within the realm of indigenous arts and mental health practices. The process centers on engaging participants, particularly those from indigenous communities who have experienced historical trauma, to actively collaborate in designing interventions or projects that address their unique cultural and social needs. This approach underscores the importance of incorporating the wisdom and lived experiences of community members to ensure that programs are not only culturally relevant but also effective in promoting mental wellbeing among these populations. The inclusion of The Dreaming, a significant concept in many indigenous cultures representing the spiritual dimension and ancestral laws, highlights the necessity of integrating traditional knowledge into modern practices as part of co-design initiatives.

## Key Findings

- Co-design methodology facilitates culturally relevant mental health interventions for indigenous communities
- Incorporating historical trauma awareness is crucial in designing effective mental wellbeing programs
- The Dreaming plays a pivotal role in shaping the cultural and spiritual aspects of co-designed projects

## Open Questions

- How can co-design methodologies be adapted to better address the diverse needs across different indigenous communities?
