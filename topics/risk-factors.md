## Risk Factors for Dementia

Both sources highlight the multifaceted nature of dementia risk factors in Australia. The 'Dementia in Australia' report emphasizes lifestyle choices and health behaviors, such as physical activity, smoking cessation, obesity management, high blood pressure control, hearing loss treatment, and depression management, as modifiable factors to reduce the likelihood of developing dementia. It also underscores the importance of national initiatives like the National Dementia Action Plan 2024-2034 in addressing the growing health challenge posed by dementia. The Cedars-Sinai Memory & Healthy Aging Program (MHAP) study complements these findings with personalized risk profiling for Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias among healthy middle-aged individuals, identifying additional modifiable factors like sleep disorders, physical activity levels, socialization levels, obstructive sleep apnea, elevated LDL cholesterol, poor diet, high blood pressure, traumatic brain injury history, and depressive mood. Both studies stress the importance of early intervention through risk profiling and lifestyle modifications to mitigate dementia risks.

## Key Findings

- Modifiable risk factors such as physical activity, smoking cessation, obesity management, high blood pressure control, untreated hearing loss, depression treatment, sleep disorders, low socialization levels, obstructive sleep apnea, elevated LDL cholesterol, poor diet, and traumatic brain injury history contribute significantly to the development of dementia.
- Personalized risk profiling can effectively identify individual modifiable factors contributing to Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias.
- National initiatives aimed at improving care and services for people with dementia are crucial in addressing the growing health challenge posed by dementia.
