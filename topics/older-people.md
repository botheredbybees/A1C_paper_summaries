## Theater and Older People

This synthesis explores the impact of theater on older adults, focusing on both health benefits and cultural enrichment. Sources highlight that engaging in theatrical activities can have significant positive effects on physical and mental well-being among elderly participants (Health Benefits). Additionally, involvement in theatre challenges negative stereotypes about aging and offers a platform for self-expression and creativity (Creative Participation, Stereotypes of Aging). However, the intrinsic cultural value of such experiences is also noted, suggesting that older people's engagement with theater goes beyond mere health interventions to encompass deeper societal contributions. Despite these benefits, there are concerns regarding access and inclusivity in theatre programs aimed at seniors.

## Key Findings

- Theater participation offers significant health benefits for older adults.
- Engaging in theatrical activities challenges negative age stereotypes.
- The intrinsic cultural value of theater experiences for older people is recognized.

## Open Questions

- How can we improve accessibility and inclusivity for older individuals in theatre programs?
