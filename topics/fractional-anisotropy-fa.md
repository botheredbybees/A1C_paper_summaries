## Fractional Anisotropy in White Matter Diffusion

The study of fractional anisotropy (FA) and radial diffusivity (RD) within white matter diffusion measures is critical for understanding age-related changes in brain structure. Across various sources, there are consistent findings related to the use of FA as a key measure to assess white matter integrity and connectivity patterns over time. However, some studies highlight discrepancies between expected results based on traditional neurodevelopmental theories and observed outcomes in specific populations such as those with autism spectrum condition (ASC). These discrepancies raise questions about the generalizability of FA measures across different clinical groups and developmental stages.

## Key Findings

- Fractional anisotropy is a critical measure for assessing white matter integrity over time.
- Age-related changes in fractional anisotropy can provide insights into neurodevelopmental processes.
- Studies on autism spectrum condition reveal unexpected patterns of fractional anisotropy, challenging conventional theories.

## Open Questions

- What are the implications of inconsistent findings between expected and observed outcomes for clinical applications using fractional anisotropy?
