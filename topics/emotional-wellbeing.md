## Music Therapy for Emotional Wellbeing

The integration of music therapy in enhancing emotional wellbeing is a recurring theme across the sources. Music's capacity to evoke memories and emotions through rhythmic entrainment and cultural nuances highlights its therapeutic value in dementia care (Sources 2, 3, 4). These findings align with studies showing how spontaneous musical interactions and self-expression can have profound therapeutic impacts on individuals (Source 9). Visual storytelling and detailed observation are also noted as effective methods for emotional health during challenging times like lockdowns (Sources 5, 7), reinforcing the broader concept of sensory engagement in therapy. Expressive writing and journaling further extend these practices by providing structured emotional processing tools that enhance mindfulness and gratitude (Source 6). The role of creative arts beyond music is discussed through case studies focusing on art-making as a means for self-identity exploration and community-building among the ill, illustrating how various forms of creativity contribute to mental wellbeing (Sources 8, 10). However, there are discrepancies in addressing the unique needs of disabled children, where clown therapy emerges as an alternative therapeutic intervention highlighting the importance of tailored approaches beyond traditional music therapy.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy enhances emotional and cognitive stimulation for dementia patients through rhythmic entrainment and memory evocation.
- Spontaneous musical interactions and self-expression offer significant therapeutic benefits by evoking positive emotions and memories.
- Visual storytelling, expressive writing, and journaling contribute to emotional processing and mindfulness during challenging times.

## Open Questions

- How can music therapy be tailored for disabled children to address their unique physiological and emotional needs?
