## Understanding Stress

The concept of stress is multifaceted and encompasses both internal psychological processes and external environmental factors. Source 1 discusses the Holmes Rahe Scale (SRRS) and daily hassles, emphasizing that chronic low-level stressors can be as detrimental to health as significant life events. In contrast, Source 2 introduces the biopsychosocial model of stress, which broadens the perspective beyond the traditional biomedical approach by considering psychological, social, and biological factors. This integrative framework provides a more comprehensive understanding of how various elements interact to influence an individual's experience of and response to stress.

## Key Findings

- The Holmes Rahe Scale identifies significant life events as major sources of stress.
- Daily hassles can have cumulative negative effects on health similar to those caused by major life events.
- The biopsychosocial model offers a holistic view of stress, incorporating biological, psychological, and social factors.

## Open Questions

- How do daily hassles interact with significant life events in the context of an individual's overall experience of stress?
