## Refugee Youth and Their Role Changes

The exploration of refugee youth's experiences reveals a complex interplay between role changes, resilience, hope, and culturally inclusive practices. Arts-informed research methods are highlighted as effective tools for understanding the shifting responsibilities and roles that young refugees experience, which often go unnoticed or unacknowledged in traditional research settings (Source 1). This approach not only captures the tacit knowledge and symbolic expressions of refugee youth but also provides a platform for marginalized communities to voice their experiences. The concept of resilience is further explored through community-based arts practices, underscoring how such initiatives can foster hope and psychological well-being among immigrant and refugee youths by applying Adlerian theory (Source 2). Additionally, the 'Younger Stories' multicultural arts project demonstrates the potential of trauma-informed care in creating culturally inclusive environments that promote healing and expression for diverse youth groups (Source 3). Despite these promising interventions, a tension exists between understanding young refugees' needs through symbolic expressions versus providing structured support systems based on resilience theory.

## Key Findings

- Arts-informed research methods offer unique insights into the role changes experienced by refugee youth.
- Community-based arts practices can enhance psychological well-being among immigrant and refugee youths, fostering hope and resilience.
- Trauma-informed care in multicultural arts projects supports healing and cultural inclusivity.

## Open Questions

- How do symbolic expressions through arts-informed research align with structured support systems based on resilience theory?
