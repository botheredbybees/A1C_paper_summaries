## Sistema Scotland

Sistema Scotland is an intervention model inspired by Venezuela's El Sistema that aims to engage children and young people in music education programs with the broader goal of enhancing their educational performance, health, wellbeing, emotional development, and social skills. Both sources highlight the Big Noise programme as central to Sistema Scotland’s efforts. Source 1 focuses primarily on educational outcomes while also touching upon health and wellbeing improvements through musical engagement. In contrast, Source 2 delves deeper into evaluating the specific impact of the Big Noise programme in Douglas, with an emphasis on emotional wellbeing and social skills development among participants. Despite differences in focus areas between the two sources, both underscore the transformative potential of music education within a structured, supportive community framework.

## Key Findings

- Sistema Scotland's Big Noise programme aims to enhance educational performance, health, and overall wellbeing through music education.
- Participants exhibit improved emotional wellbeing as a result of involvement in the Big Noise programme.
- The program contributes significantly to social skills development among its participants.
