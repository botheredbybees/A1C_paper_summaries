## Patient Experience in Healthcare Interventions

The exploration of various healthcare interventions aimed at enhancing the quality of life for patients reveals a convergence and divergence across different methods. Music therapy and clown doctor programs serve distinct yet complementary roles in alleviating psychological symptoms, fostering positive emotions, and improving ward atmospheres within hospital settings. Grounded theory and thematic analysis are employed to understand patient experiences from multiple perspectives, indicating that interventions like music therapy focus on enhancing quality of life through structured therapeutic practices. In contrast, clowning interventions aim at creating a lighter atmosphere and promoting fun levels, contributing to a positive mood among patients and staff alike. These methods highlight the importance of both professional recognition for healthcare professionals delivering such services and the subjective perception of benefits from the patient's viewpoint. Despite differences in approach, all studies underscore the critical role of psychological support in managing symptoms and improving patient well-being.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy and clown doctor programs contribute to enhancing patients' quality of life.
- Both interventions focus on fostering positive emotions among patients but differ in their implementation methods.
- Professional recognition for healthcare providers delivering such services is crucial for the effectiveness of these interventions.

## Open Questions

- How can we standardize the measurement of patient engagement and mood across different types of interventions like music therapy and clown doctor programs?
