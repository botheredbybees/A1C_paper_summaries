## Master-Apprentice Programs

Master-apprentice programs are designed to preserve and pass down cultural knowledge from one generation to the next. These programs aim to strengthen community ties by fostering mentorship relationships where experienced masters impart their traditional skills, languages, or arts to younger apprentices. The effectiveness of these interventions lies in their ability to engage youth in culturally significant activities that promote a sense of belonging and identity within their communities. However, there are challenges such as the availability of qualified masters and ensuring the sustainability of these programs over time.

## Key Findings

- Master-apprentice programs aim to preserve cultural heritage
- These programs foster intergenerational relationships and community bonds
- There is a need for sustaining these programs in the long-term

## Open Questions

- How can the shortage of qualified masters be addressed to ensure continuity of master-apprentice programs
