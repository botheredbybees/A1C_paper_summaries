## Positive and Negative Space

The concept of positive and negative space is a fundamental aspect within the realms of visual arts including photography. While 'negative space' generally refers to unoccupied areas around a subject that give it more prominence, 'positive space' designates the area occupied by objects or subjects themselves. In photography, utilizing these spaces effectively can enhance visual hierarchy and convey meaning beyond mere aesthetics. The interplay between positive and negative space is crucial for creating balance in compositions; however, varying interpretations of how to optimize both elements exist among different photographers.

## Key Findings

- Positive and negative space are essential components for effective composition.
- Balancing these spaces can enhance visual hierarchy and convey deeper meanings.
- Photographers have differing opinions on the optimal use of positive and negative space.
