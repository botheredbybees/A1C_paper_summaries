## Neurodiversity Paradigm

The neurodiversity paradigm posits that neurological differences like autism and ADHD are a result of normal variation in the human genome rather than errors or defects to be corrected. This perspective emphasizes acceptance, respect, and celebration of these differences as valuable aspects of human diversity. Source summaries highlight how arts-based therapies can align with this philosophy by providing inclusive and adaptive environments where individuals can express themselves without judgment and enhance their self-esteem and social skills.

## Key Findings

- The neurodiversity paradigm advocates for the acceptance and celebration of neurological differences as part of natural human diversity
- Arts-based therapies offer a framework that complements the principles of the neurodiversity paradigm, fostering inclusive environments where individuals can express themselves freely
- Through arts-based practices, people with neurological differences may experience enhanced self-esteem and social skills

## Open Questions

- How do cultural perspectives influence the application and acceptance of the neurodiversity paradigm in different societies?
