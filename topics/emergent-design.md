## Emergent Design in Arts and Health

Emergent design is a fluid, responsive methodology within arts and health that emphasizes adaptability and co-creation. Sources highlight its application through educational case studies (Ulrich) and servant leadership approaches, aiming to bridge gaps between disciplines by fostering an ecology of process. This approach acknowledges the person-in-role's affective responses as pivotal in shaping outcomes, underscoring a holistic view where design evolves organically rather than being rigidly predefined.

## Key Findings

- Emergent design is inherently adaptive and responsive, facilitating interdisciplinary collaboration.
- The method leverages educational case studies to illustrate its application effectively.
- Person-in-role's affective responses are critical in shaping emergent outcomes.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of servant leadership principles enhance the effectiveness of emergent design methodologies?
