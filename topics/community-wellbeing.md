## Community Wellbeing Through Arts Engagement

The synthesis of the sources highlights a broad range of arts-based interventions aimed at enhancing community wellbeing. Key concepts such as 'artistic-engagement' and 'arts engagement programs' are recurrent, underscoring the central role of creative activities in promoting social cohesion and mental health outcomes. Sources emphasize safe, supportive environments that encourage personal growth through art and cultural expression, leading to improved cognitive skills and a sense of connection among community members. The integration of arts into healthcare settings, as seen in 'The Role of the Arts in Healthcare', suggests a holistic approach to wellbeing beyond traditional medical treatments. Additionally, the development of policies on arts (such as the National Arts and Health Framework) aims to institutionalize these practices across sectors, ensuring equitable access to creative health initiatives. However, while there is significant agreement on the benefits of arts engagement for community wellbeing, sources also highlight ongoing challenges in implementing such programs at scale and integrating them effectively into existing healthcare frameworks.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions contribute significantly to improving mental health outcomes and social cohesion within communities.
- Safe and supportive environments that foster personal growth through art are critical for engaging diverse populations.
- Cross-sector strategies and policy development are essential for institutionalizing arts engagement programs in healthcare frameworks.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific challenges in scaling up arts-based interventions to ensure equitable access across different communities?
