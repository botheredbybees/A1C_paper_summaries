## Altered Connectivity Patterns

Research focusing on the characteristics of brains in individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) has identified several key aspects including accelerated brain growth, altered connectivity patterns, and gray matter abnormalities. These studies suggest that ASD is marked by differences in how various parts of the brain connect and communicate with each other, rather than isolated regions being affected. Altered connectivity patterns are particularly noted to impact both structural and functional connectivity within neural networks, contributing significantly to the diverse symptomatology observed across individuals diagnosed with ASD. Despite these findings indicating the complexity of neural interactions in ASD, there is still a need for further research into how specific brain alterations relate to clinical symptoms and developmental trajectories.

## Key Findings

- Accelerated brain growth occurs early in development.
- Altered connectivity patterns impact both structure and function.
- Gray matter abnormalities are present within certain regions.

## Open Questions

- How do altered connectivity patterns specifically influence the diverse symptomatology seen in ASD?
