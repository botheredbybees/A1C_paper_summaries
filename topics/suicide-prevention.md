## Suicide Prevention

The integration of suicide prevention strategies in the context of adolescent and indigenous mental health highlights both universal and culturally specific approaches. Source 1 emphasizes common issues such as depression, anxiety, identity exploration, and protective environments, advocating for intervention strategies that focus on youth resilience and holistic care within a supportive environment. Conversely, Source 2 underscores the importance of strengths-based and culturally informed practices to address the unique challenges faced by indigenous communities, stressing the efficacy of community-led initiatives in fostering mental health. Both sources acknowledge the complexity of mental health issues but diverge in their emphasis: while one centers on broad adolescent needs, the other focuses intensely on the cultural and communal aspects relevant to indigenous populations. Despite these differences, there is a shared commitment to developing protective environments through tailored intervention strategies.

## Key Findings

- Depression and anxiety are common among adolescents and require comprehensive mental health interventions.
- Community-led initiatives and culturally informed practices are critical for effective suicide prevention in indigenous communities.
- Supportive environments play a crucial role in enhancing resilience and preventing suicidal behavior.

## Open Questions

- How can intervention strategies be effectively adapted to address the unique cultural needs of diverse populations while maintaining a broad focus on adolescent mental health?
