## Disaster Response Strategy

The topic of disaster response strategy emphasizes the importance of learning from past incidents to better prepare for future calamities. Across sources, there is a consensus on the need for multi-faceted approaches that include immediate medical and psychological interventions. However, there is a tension between the urgency required during a crisis and the time needed to implement long-term strategies effectively. Sources highlight the role of general practitioners in providing post-disaster care, such as addressing post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) among affected populations. Integrating lessons from previous events underscores the importance of continuous improvement and adaptation in disaster management planning.

## Key Findings

- Disaster response strategies benefit from learning and adapting lessons from past incidents.
- General practitioners play a critical role in providing post-disaster healthcare, especially for mental health issues like PTSD.
- Immediate medical interventions are crucial alongside long-term psychological support.

## Open Questions

- How can disaster response plans be made more adaptable to incorporate lessons learned while maintaining emergency readiness?
