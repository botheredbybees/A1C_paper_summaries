## Elderly Engagement

The topic of elderly engagement is highlighted through interventions designed to improve quality of life and cognitive function among older adults. Two key approaches are explored: aged care outreach focusing on reminiscence therapy, cognitive stimulation, social interaction, and sensory stimulation; and the creation of memory trees and boxes aimed at personal identity preservation and dialogic engagement. While both methods emphasize the importance of personalized experiences for elderly individuals through interactive activities and sensory inputs, there is a tension in balancing individualized support with broad applicability across diverse elderly populations. This synthesis identifies common themes such as cognitive enhancement and social connection but also highlights the need for adaptable interventions that can cater to varying levels of physical and mental capacity among older adults.

## Key Findings

- Reminiscence therapy is a widely accepted approach in aged care outreach, improving cognitive function through recollection of personal histories.
- Memory trees and boxes offer an alternative method for elderly engagement by focusing on sensory stimulation and dialogic interaction to enhance personal identity preservation.
- Both interventions emphasize the significance of social interaction as integral to enhancing quality of life among older adults.

## Open Questions

- How can aged care outreach programs effectively balance personalized support with broad applicability across diverse elderly populations?
