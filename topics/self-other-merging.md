## Self-Other Merging in Social Bonding

The concept of 'self-other merging' is explored across several studies as a mechanism through which social bonds are strengthened. This process involves the psychological and neurological integration of the self with others, facilitated by various factors including endorphin release induced by music. Across these sources, there is consensus that musical activities can significantly enhance this merging experience, promoting deeper interpersonal connections and shared emotional states. However, tensions arise regarding the specificity of neurochemical mechanisms involved and how they differ across diverse social settings.

## Key Findings

- Self-other merging enhances social bonding through increased psychological integration.
- Music plays a significant role in facilitating endorphin release, which supports self-other merging.
- The process of self-other merging involves both neurological and emotional components.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neurochemical mechanisms behind self-other merging in different contexts?
