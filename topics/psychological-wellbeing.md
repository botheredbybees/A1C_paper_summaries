## Psychological Wellbeing Interventions

This synthesis explores various interventions aimed at enhancing psychological wellbeing. Two key sources highlight different approaches to achieving this goal: regular choral activities and the use of laughter prescriptions. Both studies indicate that these interventions can foster positive self-identity, a sense of community, and overall happiness among participants. The study on choral activities underscores the role of group involvement in promoting healing and self-improvement, whereas the laughter prescription approach focuses more on spontaneous and self-induced laughter as therapeutic tools. While both methods offer distinct pathways to psychological wellbeing, they share a common theme: enhancing mental health through active engagement and communal support.

## Key Findings

- Regular choral activities can significantly improve participants' sense of community and positive identity.
- Laughter prescriptions are effective therapeutic tools that promote healing and happiness.
- Both interventions contribute to psychological wellbeing by fostering social connections.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term effects of choral activities compare with those of laughter prescriptions in promoting mental health?
