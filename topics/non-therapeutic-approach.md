## Non-Therapeutic Approach in Creative Arts Therapy

The concept of a non-therapeutic approach within creative arts therapy is explored across the provided sources. This method emphasizes using art and other creative activities as mediums for personal expression, social engagement, and general well-being rather than focusing on therapeutic outcomes or clinical intervention goals. The sources highlight how this approach can facilitate participant autonomy, foster creativity in various settings such as workshops and community programs, and promote a sense of flow where individuals are fully immersed in the creative process without pressure to meet specific treatment objectives. Despite its benefits, there is acknowledgment that defining clear boundaries between therapeutic and non-therapeutic approaches presents challenges, affecting how practitioners apply this method.

## Key Findings

- Non-therapeutic approaches prioritize participant autonomy and creativity over clinical goals.
- Art workshops and other creative activities are central to implementing a non-therapeutic approach.
- Promoting flow in the creative process is an important aspect of effective non-therapeutic practice.

## Open Questions

- How can practitioners clearly delineate between therapeutic and non-therapeutic approaches within creative arts therapy?
