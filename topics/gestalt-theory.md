## Gestalt Theory

Gestalt theory emphasizes the importance of perception and its affective components in understanding human cognition. Necker's smile exemplifies how early perceptual processes can have immediate emotional impacts, a concept that aligns with gestalt principles which posit that perception is not just sensory input but also involves organizing and interpreting information based on meaningful patterns or 'wholes'. This interplay between perception and emotion underscores the theory's broader implications for cognitive psychology. However, the theory's focus on immediate perceptual experiences contrasts with some other psychological theories that emphasize longer-term learning or behavior modification processes.

## Key Findings

- Gestalt theory focuses on how humans perceive and organize visual information into meaningful patterns.
- Perception is intertwined with affective responses, as demonstrated by Necker's smile.
- Early perceptual processes have immediate emotional consequences.
