## Social Presence Theory

Social presence theory emphasizes the importance of perceived social interaction and relational aspects in online learning environments. According to Brooker's study, enhancing student engagement through this theoretical framework requires focusing on factors such as instructor immediacy, the use of multimedia tools for communication, and fostering a sense of community among participants. While motivation theories also play a role in understanding why students engage in educational activities, social presence theory specifically addresses how online interaction quality can lead to higher levels of learner satisfaction and participation.

## Key Findings

- Social presence theory focuses on the perceived relational aspects in online environments.
- Instructors' immediacy and multimedia use positively impact student engagement.
- Combining social presence with motivation theories provides a more comprehensive understanding of online learning engagement.

## Open Questions

- How can social presence be effectively measured and operationalized in diverse online educational contexts?
