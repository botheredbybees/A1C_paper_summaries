## Sundown Syndrome in Geriatric Psychiatry

Sundown syndrome is a significant issue for individuals with dementia and involves symptoms that exacerbate during the late afternoon and evening. Source 1 highlights the role of circadian rhythmicity, particularly degeneration within the suprachiasmatic nucleus, which regulates daily biological rhythms including sleep-wake cycles, and reduced melatonin production contributing to this syndrome. Understanding these physiological changes can inform targeted interventions such as light therapy or medication adjustments aimed at stabilizing circadian rhythms and alleviating symptom severity. However, Source 2 introduces a methodological challenge by validating the Chinese version of sundown syndrome assessment tools, emphasizing the need for culturally sensitive instruments that maintain high reliability and validity across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Sundown syndrome symptoms in dementia are linked to circadian rhythm disruptions
- Degeneration of the suprachiasmatic nucleus affects melatonin production and sleep-wake cycles
- Culturally adapted assessment tools for sundown syndrome must balance reliability and validity

## Open Questions

- How can culturally sensitive interventions be developed that address both physiological and environmental factors influencing sundown syndrome?
