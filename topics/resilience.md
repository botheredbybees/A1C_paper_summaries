## Resilience

The concept of resilience is a central theme across multiple sources. Resilience refers to the capacity for an individual or community to recover from adversity and thrive in challenging circumstances. Sources discuss diverse methods of fostering resilience, including social prescribing through visual storytelling (Source 1), self-expression through arts activities in both adults (Source 4) and children/young people (Sources 3, 5), and leveraging hope within immigrant/refugee communities via community-based arts practice (Source 6). Each source highlights the unique role of resilience in mental wellbeing (Sources 3-5, 6) and sense-of-self (Source 2). Additionally, resilience is linked to work engagement and stress reduction through workplace choirs (Source 4), further illustrating its applicability across different contexts. However, while arts activities appear instrumental in cultivating resilience, particularly among vulnerable populations like immigrant/refugee youth, there remains a need for more specific methodologies addressing the diverse needs of these groups.

## Key Findings

- Resilience is critical for mental wellbeing and can be enhanced through various interventions.
- Visual storytelling and structured group arts activities are effective methods to foster resilience across different age groups.
- Arts-based approaches help in developing self-efficacy, work engagement, stress reduction, and hope among diverse populations.

## Open Questions

- How do specific cultural or social contexts influence the effectiveness of resilience-building interventions through arts activities?
