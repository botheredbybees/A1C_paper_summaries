## Theatre as a Political Tool

Augusto Boal's work in Theatre of the Oppressed highlights how theatre can serve as a political tool to empower underrepresented groups and give them a voice. This intervention uses theatrical performance not just for entertainment, but as a platform for social change by engaging directly with societal issues and providing marginalized communities an opportunity to express their struggles and advocate for their rights on stage. Boal's approach emphasizes the transformative power of participatory theatre where spectators become actors in real-life scenarios, fostering dialogue about inequalities and oppression, thus enabling underrepresented voices to be heard.

## Key Findings

- Theatre can serve as a powerful political tool to give voice to marginalized groups.
- Augusto Boal's Theatre of the Oppressed allows for direct engagement with social issues through theatrical means.
- Participants are encouraged to become active agents in addressing and resolving societal problems via theatre

## Open Questions

- How can Theatre of the Oppressed be effectively implemented beyond its cultural origins to address global inequalities?
