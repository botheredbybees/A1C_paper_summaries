## Fall Risk Reduction Through Dance

The integration of dance as a method to reduce fall risk in older adults highlights its multifaceted benefits. The systematic review and meta-analysis indicate that regular dance participation significantly improves balance, which is crucial for reducing the likelihood of falls. Additionally, dance enhances functional capacity, enabling better performance of daily activities without the risk of falling. While these findings are promising, there remains a need to establish standardized protocols for dance interventions tailored to different age groups and health conditions within older populations.

## Key Findings

- Dance is effective in improving balance among older adults
- Regular participation in dance can enhance functional capacity
- Meta-analysis supports the efficacy of dance as an intervention

## Open Questions

- How can standardized protocols for dance interventions be developed to address varying health conditions and age groups?
