## Mixed-Methods Research in Cultural Programs

The utilization of mixed-method research in evaluating the impact and effectiveness of cultural programs, such as those outlined in 'Arts for EveryBody: The One Nation/One Project Impact Report,' underscores its versatility and comprehensiveness. This approach integrates quantitative data with qualitative insights to provide a more nuanced understanding of program outcomes. While evidence-based engagement methodologies highlight the importance of rigorous data collection and analysis, they also recognize the value of participant perspectives in assessing cultural initiatives' broader social impacts.

## Key Findings

- Mixed-method research provides a comprehensive evaluation by combining quantitative and qualitative approaches.
- Cultural programs benefit from mixed-methods for understanding both measurable outcomes and subjective experiences.
- Evidence-based engagement strategies are crucial for enhancing the impact of cultural initiatives.

## Open Questions

- How can mixed-method research be further optimized to capture the full breadth of cultural program impacts?
