## Reducing Child Anxiety

The integration of various interventions aimed at reducing child anxiety reveals a multifaceted approach that includes the use of puppets and other therapeutic tools. The Pup-Ed framework, highlighted in one study, underscores the significant role that non-verbal communication through puppetry can play in alleviating children's distress within an acute pediatric setting. While there is clear evidence supporting the effectiveness of this intervention, gaps remain in understanding how to scale such practices across different healthcare environments and populations.

## Key Findings

- Puppet-based interventions are effective for reducing child anxiety in clinical settings.
- The Pup-Ed framework facilitates a structured approach to using puppets as therapeutic tools.

## Open Questions

- How can the use of puppetry be adapted and scaled across various pediatric healthcare environments?
