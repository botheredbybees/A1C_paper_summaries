## Patient Satisfaction

The integration of adjunctive museum-based art therapy for women with severe eating disorders highlights the importance of non-traditional therapeutic settings in enhancing patient satisfaction. This approach underscores the value of self-expression and addressing body image concerns through an immersive, culturally rich environment. While this specific intervention focuses on a particular population dealing with severe eating disorders, its success lies in creating spaces where patients can express themselves freely and positively impact their mental health outcomes.

## Key Findings

- Adjunctive museum-based art therapy significantly improves patient satisfaction among individuals with severe eating disorders.
- Self-expression through creative activities in non-clinical settings is beneficial for addressing body image concerns.
- Integration of cultural and artistic elements into therapeutic practices enhances engagement and satisfaction.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles behind museum-based art therapy be adapted to other populations facing different mental health challenges?
