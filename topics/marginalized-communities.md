## Marginalized Communities Through an Arts-Informed Lens

The utilization of arts-informed research methods to study the experiences and social roles of marginalized groups, particularly refugee youth and migrants, reveals profound shifts in personal responsibility and societal role as individuals navigate new cultural landscapes. Sources highlight how refugees often bear a tacit knowledge about their evolving identities within host societies, yet this nuanced understanding is frequently underrepresented or overlooked by traditional research approaches. Through symbolic expression via arts-based methodologies, these communities can articulate complex experiences related to identity formation, resilience in the face of adversity, and adaptation to new norms. This method allows marginalized individuals to reclaim a voice and share their stories authentically, thus challenging broader societal perceptions about migration, displacement, and integration.

## Key Findings

- Arts-informed research methodologies enable refugee youth and migrants to express complex emotions and experiences related to role changes within host communities more effectively than traditional methods.
- Tacit knowledge held by marginalized individuals regarding their evolving social roles is a critical yet underutilized source of insight into the dynamics of migration and adaptation processes.
- Symbolic expression through arts-based interventions facilitates deeper understanding of resilience among refugees and migrants as they navigate new cultural environments.

## Open Questions

- How can researchers better integrate tacit knowledge and symbolic expressions from marginalized communities to inform policy-making in fields like education, healthcare, and social services?
