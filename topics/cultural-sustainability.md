## Cultural Sustainability Through Arts and Wellbeing

The concept of cultural sustainability emerges as a critical framework for community resilience and social well-being in both 'Make Arts for a Better Life' and the 'Happy Museum Learning Evaluation Report'. Both sources underscore the importance of artistic communication and community engagement to foster sustainable cultural practices. The first source highlights how arts facilitate dialogue and collective action within communities, enhancing local identities and mutual support networks. Meanwhile, the second report examines institutional efforts towards cultural sustainability through innovative well-being initiatives that aim at transforming museums into more inclusive and responsive public spaces. Despite these complementary approaches, there is a gap in directly linking community-led arts projects with broader structural changes required for sustainable development.

## Key Findings

- Artistic communication and community engagement are central to promoting cultural sustainability.
- Community-based arts initiatives enhance local identities and support networks.
- Institutional transformations through well-being initiatives contribute to cultural sustainability.

## Open Questions

- How can community-led arts projects be effectively integrated with institutional changes for broader impact on cultural sustainability?
