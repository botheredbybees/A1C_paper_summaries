## Creative Arts in Health Education

The integration of creative arts into health education and practice presents a novel approach to enhancing both the therapeutic experience and the educational environment. Source one discusses how incorporating creative arts can foster non-normative ethics and values, thereby enriching compassion in healthcare practice through a creative curriculum. This aligns with source two's emphasis on school-based drama interventions (educational-drama) as an interactive-learning tool for health promotion among children and adolescents, highlighting the potential of self-reflection theory to deepen understanding and engagement with health-related issues. Both sources underscore the value of non-traditional educational methods in promoting ethical practice and fostering personal growth through direct experience.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts enhance compassion and ethics in healthcare education
- Educational drama supports interactive learning and self-reflection for health promotion among youth
- Non-normative values can be cultivated through creative curriculum approaches

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of educational-drama be effectively applied to adult populations in health education contexts?
