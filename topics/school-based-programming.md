## School-Based Creative Arts Therapy Programs

The integration of creative arts therapies in school settings demonstrates a promising approach to supporting the well-being and development of immigrant and refugee children. Source 1 highlights how puppetry within an Art & Storytelling program enhances students' sense-of-agency and emotional-well-being by providing effective coping-mechanisms. In contrast, Source 2 evaluates a broader Creative Arts Therapy Program designed for adolescents from refugee backgrounds, emphasizing improvements in behavioral-difficulties, emotional-symptoms, and cognitive-development. Both sources underscore the versatility of creative arts therapies in addressing various developmental needs and challenges faced by children and adolescents from immigrant or refugee families, suggesting that these programs foster resilience and mental health support within educational environments.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapy programs enhance emotional well-being through improved coping mechanisms.
- These interventions help mitigate behavioral difficulties and emotional symptoms among refugee youth.
- School-based creative arts therapies contribute to cognitive development in adolescent refugees.

## Open Questions

- How can school-based creative arts therapy programs be adapted for diverse cultural backgrounds?
