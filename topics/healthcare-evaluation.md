## Healthcare Evaluation

Healthcare evaluation encompasses the assessment of complex interventions and processes to enhance decision-making in healthcare delivery. Both sources emphasize different aspects of evaluation methodologies. Source 1 discusses the distinction between evaluation and research, focusing on the importance of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) for assessing intervention effectiveness. In contrast, Source 2 highlights process evaluations and outcome research, with an emphasis on ethnographic studies to understand how interventions are implemented in real-world settings. While both sources underscore the need for rigorous evaluation methods to inform practice and policy, there is a tension between the idealized controlled environments of RCTs and the practical realities captured through ethnography.

## Key Findings

- Healthcare evaluation distinguishes between process evaluations and outcome research
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) are crucial for assessing intervention effectiveness
- Ethnographic studies provide unique insights into real-world implementation challenges

## Open Questions

- How can the strengths of ethnographic studies be integrated with RCT methodologies to enhance healthcare evaluation?
