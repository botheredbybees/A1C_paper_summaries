## Adolescent Neurodevelopment

The adolescent neurodevelopment theory emphasizes significant brain changes during the teenage years, particularly in the prefrontal cortex. This region undergoes synaptic pruning and myelination, enhancing functional connectivity and executive functions such as decision-making and impulse control. Hormonal changes play a crucial role alongside socioeconomic factors influencing these processes. Across studies, there is agreement on the importance of these developmental stages but also tension regarding the extent to which external influences shape neural development.

## Key Findings

- The prefrontal cortex undergoes significant synaptic pruning and myelination during adolescence.
- Hormonal changes are integral to adolescent brain development.
- Socioeconomic factors can influence neurodevelopment in adolescents.

## Open Questions

- What is the extent of external influences, such as socioeconomic status, on neural development?
