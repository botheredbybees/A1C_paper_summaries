## Social Media Impact on Mental Health and Wellbeing

The Mission Australia Youth Survey highlights the multifaceted influence of social media on young people's mental health and wellbeing. The survey reveals that while social media can offer a platform for expression, connection, and information sharing among youth, it also contributes significantly to issues such as bullying and negative body image. This dual nature suggests both potential benefits and detrimental impacts tied directly to the use of social media by adolescents. Despite its role in enhancing communication channels, there is an undeniable correlation with deteriorating mental health indicators linked to excessive screen time and constant online engagement.

## Key Findings

- Social media has a significant impact on young people's mental health and wellbeing
- Increased social media use correlates with higher incidences of bullying and negative body image issues among youth
- While offering benefits such as enhanced connectivity, social media also poses risks to mental health

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of prolonged exposure to social media on adolescent mental health?
