## Creative Arts As Resilience Building Tools

The integration of creative arts into therapeutic practices is highlighted across multiple sources as a critical intervention for building resilience in various populations facing mental health challenges such as eating disorders and trauma. These interventions leverage the symbolic language inherent in artistic expression to provide an alternative mode of communication beyond verbal articulation, enabling deeper emotional exploration and healing. Group therapy within this framework fosters community support and shared experiences, enhancing individual resilience through collective strength. However, while the benefits are evident, there is a need for more standardized protocols and further research into the long-term efficacy and broad applicability of creative arts as therapeutic tools.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts offer symbolic language facilitating emotional expression in therapy
- Group therapy involving creative arts enhances resilience through community support
- Leveraging artistic expression improves healing processes for trauma and eating disorders

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term benefits of using creative arts as therapeutic tools compared to traditional methods?
