## Therapeutic Photography Practices

Therapeutic photography is an innovative intervention that leverages the power of visual imagery and self-expression to aid individuals in healing from trauma. The concept of therapeutic photography encompasses a range of practices, including creating self-portraits as a means for individuals to reframe their traumatic experiences through a lens of empowerment and personal growth. This approach integrates elements of Jungian archetypes and color psychology, enhancing the therapeutic potential by delving into symbolic meanings and emotional responses tied to colors and images. While this method offers unique insights and benefits in trauma recovery, it also highlights the necessity for further exploration regarding its application across diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic photography interventions like self-portraiture can aid individuals in transforming their perceptions of trauma into narratives of resilience and empowerment.
- Integrating Jungian archetypes and color psychology enriches the therapeutic value by providing symbolic frameworks for emotional processing.
- There is a growing recognition of the potential benefits of therapeutic photography as an alternative or supplementary approach to traditional talk therapy.

## Open Questions

- How can cultural sensitivity be incorporated into therapeutic photography practices to ensure effective application across diverse populations?
