## Successful Aging Through Creative Expression

The concept of successful aging is explored through the lens of creative expression in visual arts for people living with dementia. Sources emphasize the importance of ecopsychosocial theory, which integrates ecological and social factors to support mental health and resilience among elderly individuals. The role of creative activities such as painting or drawing is highlighted as an intervention that not only provides a therapeutic outlet but also fosters community engagement and self-expression, contributing to a sense of purpose and well-being in later life. However, while there is consensus on the benefits of engaging with visual arts for emotional resilience, questions arise regarding the scalability and accessibility of such programs.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression through visual arts contributes to mental health and resilience among elderly individuals.
- Ecopsychosocial theory supports the integration of social and ecological factors in interventions aimed at successful aging.
- Community engagement facilitated by creative activities can enhance quality of life for older adults.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to implementing visual arts programs as a widespread intervention for promoting successful aging?
