## Institutional Silos Removal

The concept of removing institutional silos is central to realizing the potential of arts in supporting health and wellbeing. This involves fostering a collaborative environment where arts, healthcare, and other sectors work together seamlessly. The key challenge highlighted across studies is the need for greater interconnectedness between disciplines, which can be hindered by traditional organizational structures that promote isolation rather than collaboration. Sources emphasize the importance of artist engagement in public health initiatives as a means to bridge gaps between these silos, suggesting that arts practitioners play a critical role in this process. However, while the potential benefits are clear, there remains a gap in understanding how such cross-sector collaborations can be effectively implemented and sustained.

## Key Findings

- Interconnectedness between arts and health sectors is crucial for effective interventions
- Artist engagement is essential to bridge gaps in institutional silos
- Traditional organizational structures often impede collaborative efforts

## Open Questions

- How can cross-sector collaboration be effectively sustained over time?
