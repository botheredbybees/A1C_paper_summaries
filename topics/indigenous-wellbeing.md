## Indigenous Wellbeing in Australia

This synthesis explores the cultural and environmental determinants of wellbeing among Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples. Source 1 highlights the importance of family/community connections, self-determination, and connection to culture as essential components for health outcomes among these communities. It underscores that social support networks and a sense of identity rooted in traditional practices are crucial for addressing socio-economic disparities. Conversely, Source 2 emphasizes how engagement with Country, spirituality, music, and arts can significantly contribute to mental health and wellbeing. Music, in particular, is highlighted as a powerful catalyst for community cohesion and individual expression, reinforcing the holistic nature of Indigenous wellness. Both sources converge on the significance of cultural continuity and environmental ties but diverge slightly in their focus; one prioritizing structural aspects like self-determination while the other focuses more on creative and spiritual expressions.

## Key Findings

- Family/community connections significantly contribute to health outcomes for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples.
- Self-determination and connection to culture are vital components of overall wellbeing among Indigenous communities.
- Engagement with Country, spirituality, music, and arts can positively impact mental health and social-emotional wellbeing.

## Open Questions

- How do structural determinants like self-determination interact with cultural expressions such as music and art in fostering holistic wellness for Indigenous populations?
