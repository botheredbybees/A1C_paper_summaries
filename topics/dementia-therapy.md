## Dementia Therapy Through Creative Arts

The sources illustrate the use of creative arts as a therapeutic intervention for individuals with dementia. Source 1 emphasizes the tactile and sensory benefits of working with ceramics, which can provide a medium for emotional expression and personal creativity. The experience of crafting clay offers unique opportunities for engagement that are distinct from other forms of communication or therapy. In contrast, Source 2 discusses dance as a form of movement-based therapy focusing on non-verbal expression through the body's memory. Both sources highlight the importance of sensory experiences in dementia care and suggest that creative arts interventions can cater to diverse needs by allowing participants to communicate through alternative means.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts therapies offer significant benefits for individuals with dementia, including emotional expression and non-verbal communication.
- Tactile activities such as ceramics provide unique sensory experiences that aid in personal expression.
- Movement-based therapies like dance engage body memory and support non-verbal forms of self-expression.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of multiple creative arts modalities enhance dementia therapy outcomes?
