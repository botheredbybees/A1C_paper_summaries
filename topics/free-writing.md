## Free-Writing as a Creative Process

The concept of free-writing is presented across various sources as a method that enhances creativity and spontaneity in writing. Emphasized in LAW122 Legal Systems Week 5 Activities, free-writing serves to break down barriers between the conscious and unconscious mind, allowing for fluid expression without self-censorship or overthinking (fxa202-week06-lesson-fxa202-week06-page-4). This technique is often utilized alongside other creative practices such as storytelling and using images as prompts. Despite its benefits, there are differing opinions on the most effective ways to incorporate free-writing into a broader writing process.

## Key Findings

- Free-writing enhances creativity by allowing for spontaneous expression without self-censorship.
- Creative techniques like image-based prompts and story-telling can complement free-writing.
- There is debate over how best to integrate free-writing into the overall creative process.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal conditions and contexts for incorporating free-writing into a writer's routine?
