## Green Wellbeing Initiatives

The integration of natural elements into urban environments and healthcare practices has been identified as a critical component in enhancing mental and physical health. Museums, as outlined in the 'Museums as Spaces for Wellbeing' report, have adopted green wellbeing initiatives by incorporating co-creation methods that involve community input and holistic approaches to healthcare. These initiatives emphasize the importance of nature in therapeutic settings. However, while museums lead the way with innovative practices, there is a gap in understanding how these principles can be widely implemented across other institutions such as hospitals and schools.

## Key Findings

- Green wellbeing initiatives incorporate natural elements into urban environments to improve health outcomes.
- Museums are adopting co-creation methods to enhance community engagement in green wellbeing practices.
- Holistic healthcare approaches are gaining prominence through the integration of nature and cultural spaces.

## Open Questions

- How can museums' successful green wellbeing initiatives be translated into other institutional settings?
