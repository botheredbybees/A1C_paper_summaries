## Blood-Brain Barrier

The Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) is a critical anatomical structure that plays a vital role in maintaining the central nervous system's homeostasis by selectively regulating the entry of substances from the bloodstream into brain tissue. It is particularly important for preventing harmful substances and pathogens from entering neural tissues, thereby protecting neurons and glial cells. This barrier serves as a physical and functional interface between blood vessels and the brain parenchyma, comprising specialized endothelial cells that are tightly joined by tight junctions, thus restricting paracellular transport. Research has highlighted its significance in understanding and treating neurological diseases such as Alzheimer's disease, multiple sclerosis, and traumatic brain injuries. However, there is a tension around how to effectively bypass or modify the BBB for therapeutic purposes without compromising its protective functions.

## Key Findings

- The Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB) selectively regulates substance entry from blood into the brain tissue
- It plays an essential role in protecting neural tissues and cells by preventing pathogens and harmful substances from entering
- Understanding the BBB is crucial for treating neurological diseases
