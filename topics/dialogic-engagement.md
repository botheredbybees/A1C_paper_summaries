## Dialogic Engagement

The concept of dialogic engagement is explored through the use of memory trees and boxes as tools for sensory stimulation. These resources are designed to evoke recollections and foster a sense of personal identity in individuals. Memory trees serve as visual representations that connect past experiences with current thoughts, while memory boxes contain physical items that trigger memories and enhance emotional connections. Across different contexts, these methods facilitate meaningful dialogue and interaction by encouraging participants to engage deeply with their own histories and those of others. However, the effectiveness of dialogic engagement techniques can vary depending on individual cognitive abilities and willingness to participate.

## Key Findings

- Memory trees and boxes are effective tools for sensory stimulation in dialogic engagement.
- These methods help individuals connect past experiences with current identity formation.
- Dialogic engagement enhances personal narrative expression through meaningful interaction.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences impact the effectiveness of dialogic engagement techniques?
