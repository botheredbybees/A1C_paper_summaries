## Creative Self-Expression Through Digital and Performing Arts

This synthesis explores creative self-expression through digital photography and musical theatre as interventions for dementia patients and general well-being. Source 1 presents a case study of people with dementia participating in a musical theatre group, leveraging the spect-actors framework to empower them through performance arts. This intervention highlights autonomy and personal expression among participants. In contrast, Source 2 introduces daily digital practices using photography as a form of self-care and well-being. It emphasizes relational aspects of well-being and the therapeutic benefits of regular creative engagement with technology. Both sources underscore the potential for creative activities to enhance quality of life but approach it through different modalities: physical performance versus digital creation.

## Key Findings

- Creative interventions like musical theatre can empower dementia patients by fostering autonomy and personal expression.
- Regularly engaging in daily digital practices, such as photography, offers a form of self-care that enhances well-being through relational engagement.
- Both performing arts and digital art offer unique pathways to support mental health and overall quality of life.

## Open Questions

- How do the effects of creative self-expression interventions differ between digital and physical performance contexts?
