## Expressive Writing Therapy

Expressive writing therapy is a psychological intervention that involves the expression of thoughts and feelings through writing about traumatic or stressful events. Sources indicate that this therapeutic approach can lead to improvements in physical health outcomes, such as reduced symptoms of asthma and rheumatoid arthritis (Source 1). Additionally, expressive writing has been associated with enhanced self-awareness and mental well-being, offering individuals a platform for reflection and emotional processing (Source 2). The practice emphasizes the importance of articulating personal experiences, which can facilitate cognitive restructuring and lead to positive psychological changes.

## Key Findings

- Expressive writing therapy aids in improving physical health outcomes.
- Enhances self-awareness and mental well-being.
- Involves expressing thoughts and feelings about traumatic or stressful events through writing.
