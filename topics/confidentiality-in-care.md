## Confidentiality In Care

The concept of confidentiality in care is a critical aspect that transcends various domains such as cultural norms and religious practices. Sources indicate the necessity of maintaining patient privacy to foster trust between healthcare providers and patients, particularly among marginalized communities facing socioeconomic barriers. However, balancing confidentiality with the need for family or community involvement poses challenges due to diverse cultural expectations regarding disclosure and decision-making.

## Key Findings

- Confidentiality in care is essential for building trust between healthcare providers and patients.
- Maintaining confidentiality can be challenging when accommodating different cultural norms around privacy and sharing of personal health information.
- Socioeconomic barriers exacerbate the difficulties in ensuring confidentiality due to limited access to private healthcare settings.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare systems effectively balance patient confidentiality with cultural expectations that value communal involvement in decision-making processes?
