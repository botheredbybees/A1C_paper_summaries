## Dance as an Intervention

The studies reviewed highlight the versatile benefits of dance interventions across various populations and health outcomes. For female adolescents, engaging in contemporary dance has been shown to improve aerobic capacity and self-esteem while also enhancing upper-body strength. In older adults, the systematic review indicates that participating in dance can significantly reduce fall risk by improving balance and functional capacity. Additionally, for individuals with Parkinson's disease, a ballet-based intervention not only enhances gait variability but also boosts balance confidence. These findings collectively suggest that dance interventions are effective across age groups and health conditions, providing significant physical and psychological benefits.

## Key Findings

- Dance interventions improve self-esteem and aerobic capacity in female adolescents
- Participation in dance reduces fall risk and improves functional capacity in older adults
- Ballet-based dance enhances gait variability and balance confidence in individuals with Parkinson's disease

## Open Questions

- How do the specific types of dances (e.g., contemporary, ballet) differ in their efficacy for various health outcomes?
