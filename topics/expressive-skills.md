## Expressive Skills in Therapy

The integration of play and arts-based therapies emphasizes the importance of expressive skills for enhancing therapeutic outcomes. Sources discuss child-centered play therapy, pivotal response therapy, and their roles in developing expressive skills such as emotional regulation. These interventions highlight a child's capacity to express themselves through various mediums like art, drama, and music, which can aid in reducing behavioral issues and improving overall well-being. However, there is an ongoing debate regarding the most effective methods for integrating these therapies into clinical practice.

## Key Findings

- Expressive skills are crucial for emotional regulation and therapeutic outcomes
- Child-centered play therapy and pivotal response therapy effectively enhance expressive capabilities in children
- Integrating arts-based modalities can provide a platform for children to express themselves

## Open Questions

- What are the most effective methods for integrating expressive skills interventions into clinical practice?
