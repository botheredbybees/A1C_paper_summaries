## Research and Evaluation in Social Policy

The synthesis integrates the critical methodologies employed in assessing 'what works' in social policy with specific focus on tools for evaluating mental health programs. Source One introduces Risk of Bias (RoB) and GRADE as essential components to evaluate the strength of evidence in social interventions. These criteria help assess the reliability, validity, and applicability of research findings in determining effective policies. Conversely, Source Two delves into practical applications through The THRivE Toolkit, which emphasizes tools such as mental health assessments and quality-of-life measurements specifically for arts and heritage projects that aim to enhance public well-being. Despite these distinct approaches, both sources highlight the importance of robust evaluative criteria to ensure credible conclusions about interventions' impacts on societal issues.

## Key Findings

- Risk of Bias (RoB) is a crucial component in assessing the reliability of research findings in social policy evaluation
- The THRivE Toolkit provides specific tools and methodologies for evaluating mental health programs within arts and heritage contexts
- Both sources emphasize the importance of comprehensive evaluative criteria to establish effective interventions
