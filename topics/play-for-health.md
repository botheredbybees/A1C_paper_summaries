## Play For Health

The concept of using play as a therapeutic and developmental tool is widely supported across various sources. The use of play to enhance health outcomes is particularly pronounced in infancy through the promotion of perspective-taking, an essential cognitive skill that fosters social understanding and empathy. Trauma-informed practices further emphasize the importance of supportive environments during play to ensure all participants feel safe and engaged, promoting healing and growth rather than re-traumatization. These sources collectively highlight how play can serve as a versatile intervention for diverse health needs, from early childhood development to therapeutic contexts.

## Key Findings

- Play is an effective tool in promoting cognitive skills such as perspective-taking in infants.
- Trauma-informed practices during play create safe and supportive environments.
- Play serves multiple roles including healing and developmental enhancement across various stages of life.

## Open Questions
