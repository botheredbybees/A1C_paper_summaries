## Agitation Management in Dementia

The management of agitation in dementia through non-pharmacological interventions has been a focus across various studies. Music therapy stands out as an effective intervention compared to general recreational activities, with significant benefits observed for individuals suffering from dementia and associated agitation. A systematic review further highlights that non-pharmacological approaches such as person-centered care, communication skills training, and adapted dementia care mapping can offer substantial improvements in managing behavioral issues. Additionally, the Sydney Multisite Intervention of LaughterBosses and ElderClowns (SMILE) study introduces humor therapy as a novel intervention, which not only targets agitation but also addresses related conditions like depression and enhances quality of life through increased social engagement.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy is effective in reducing agitation compared to general recreational activities in people with dementia.
- Non-pharmacological interventions such as person-centered care, communication skills training, and adapted dementia care mapping are beneficial for managing agitation.
- Humor therapy can improve quality of life and address behavioral disturbances beyond just agitation.

## Open Questions

- How do different non-pharmacological interventions compare in their efficacy for reducing agitation over extended periods?
