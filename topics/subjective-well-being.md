## Subjective Well-Being

The integration of the findings from the provided sources highlights a significant link between music listening and subjective well-being in late adolescents. The concept of self-determined motivation plays a crucial role, suggesting that when individuals listen to music for intrinsic reasons rather than external pressures, they experience higher levels of happiness and overall life satisfaction. This insight underscores the importance of personalized, intrinsically motivated activities like music listening as potential avenues for enhancing subjective well-being among young people.

## Key Findings

- Self-determined motivation in engaging with music correlates positively with global happiness in late adolescents.
- Subjective well-being can be influenced by the intrinsic versus extrinsic motivations driving musical engagement.

## Open Questions

- What are the mechanisms through which self-determined motivation enhances subjective well-being, and how might this extend to other cultural contexts?
