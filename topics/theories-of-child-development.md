## Theories of Child Development

Child development theories encompass a range of perspectives that explain the processes and stages through which children grow and develop. Critical period theory is one such perspective highlighted in the sources, emphasizing specific time windows during early childhood when certain developmental milestones must be met for optimal cognitive and neural architecture development. This theory underscores the importance of timely interventions to support healthy child development. Theories like these are crucial in shaping educational and therapeutic practices aimed at nurturing mental wellbeing through creative arts and play.

## Key Findings

- Critical period theory emphasizes specific windows during early childhood for developmental milestones.
- Theories of child development guide practices that enhance mental well-being and cognitive development.
