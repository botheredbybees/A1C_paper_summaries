## Brain Connectivity in Autism and Art Production

The investigation of brain connectivity spans various methodologies including functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and analysis of temporal synchrony between brain regions. Source 1 explores the impact of visual art production on functional brain connectivity, highlighting changes within the default-mode-network that contribute to resilience against stress. In contrast, sources 2 through 4 focus on understanding neural differences in individuals with autism spectrum condition (ASC), revealing age-related variations in white matter diffusion measures and evaluating temporal synchrony between brain regions. Despite divergent methodologies, a common thread emerges: altered connectivity patterns are observed in ASC, affecting cognitive processing speed and social interaction abilities. While art production shows potential for enhancing connectivity and neural resilience, the underlying mechanisms remain less explored in individuals with ASC, suggesting an opportunity to bridge these areas of research.

## Key Findings

- Art production can alter functional brain connectivity, impacting mental health resilience.
- Individuals with autism spectrum condition exhibit altered white matter diffusion measures that vary with age.
- Temporal synchrony between brain regions differs in individuals with autism compared to those without.

## Open Questions

- How does art therapy impact neural network dynamics and functional connectivity in individuals with autism?
