## Personalized Medicine for Alzheimer's Disease

The synthesis integrates insights from a clinical and research program focusing on personalized risk profiling for Alzheimer’s disease (AD) among asymptomatic adults at Cedars-Sinai. This intervention targets modifiable risk factors contributing to AD, including sleep disorders, low physical activity levels, poor diet, and high blood pressure. In parallel, Daniel Levitin's discussion in his book 'I Heard There Was a Secret Chord' highlights the therapeutic potential of music as an adjunctive treatment for AD patients. Both sources underscore personalized approaches for enhancing brain health and mitigating risk factors associated with neurodegenerative diseases like AD.

## Key Findings

- Personalized profiling can identify modifiable risk factors contributing to Alzheimer’s disease in healthy individuals.
- Music therapy has significant therapeutic potential by engaging multiple parts of the brain, aiding conditions such as Alzheimer's and PTSD.
- Therapeutic interventions targeting cognitive reserve through lifelong musical engagement or personalized risk assessment show promise.

## Open Questions

- How can the integration of music therapy with conventional medical approaches improve outcomes for patients undergoing personalized AD risk profiling?
