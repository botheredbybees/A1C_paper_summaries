## Laughter Prescription in Lifestyle Medicine

Both sources explore the use of laughter as a tool within lifestyle medicine, focusing on its potential health benefits and therapeutic applications. Source 1 introduces the concept that both spontaneous and simulated laughter can be beneficial for one's well-being, underscoring the idea that motion does indeed create emotion, suggesting physical activity related to laughing could enhance emotional states positively. In contrast or rather in complement, Source 2 elaborates on how self-induced laughter can serve as a therapeutic tool, offering patients and practitioners another means of promoting health through lifestyle changes. Despite some overlap in terminology and application, the sources seem to diverge slightly in their primary focus: one emphasizing the act of laughing itself while the other highlights the intentional induction of laughter for its benefits.

## Key Findings

- Both spontaneous and simulated laughter are considered beneficial tools within lifestyle medicine.
- Laughter as a therapeutic tool is seen as capable of inducing positive emotional states and health benefits.

## Open Questions

- How does self-induced laughter differ in efficacy from naturally occurring laughter?
