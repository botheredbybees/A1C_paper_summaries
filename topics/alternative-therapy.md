## Alternative Therapy Through Dance

The systematic review on the physiological and psychological benefits of dance for children and adolescents highlights dance as a versatile form of alternative therapy. Sources concur that dance not only enhances physical health but also contributes significantly to mental well-being, making it an effective intervention in various therapeutic settings. The integration of dance into healthcare and educational programs is seen as beneficial due to its non-verbal nature, which can facilitate communication among individuals who might struggle with verbal expression. However, there are tensions regarding the standardization of dance therapy protocols and the need for more empirical evidence on long-term effects.

## Key Findings

- Dance serves multiple therapeutic functions, impacting both physical health and mental well-being.
- Non-verbal communication facilitated through dance can be particularly beneficial in settings where verbal expression is challenging.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal protocols for integrating dance therapy into clinical practice to maximize efficacy?
