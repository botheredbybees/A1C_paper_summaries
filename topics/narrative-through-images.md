## Narrative Through Images

The concept of 'narrative through images' emphasizes the use of visual elements to convey stories and meanings beyond literal representation. The source primarily discusses diptychs, which are pairs of related photographs that together create a narrative or symbolize an idea more powerfully than either image could alone (diptychs). This practice relies heavily on contextual symbolism, where viewers interpret images based not only on the visual elements but also on their broader understanding and context (contextual-symbolism). The underlying theme is how images can transcend literal depiction to evoke emotions, tell stories, or symbolize complex ideas, effectively making metaphors from photos. While each source offers unique insights into different aspects of narrative photography, they collectively highlight the power and potential of visual storytelling.

## Key Findings

- Diptychs are a powerful method for creating narratives through image pairing.
- Contextual symbolism is crucial in interpreting images as stories or metaphors.
- Visual elements can transcend literal representation to evoke deeper meanings.
