## Refugee Youth in Vulnerable Populations

The study of refugee youth highlights the significant role that migration plays in altering their social standing within both origin and destination communities. Arts-informed research methodologies provide a unique lens for understanding how these young individuals navigate changing roles and responsibilities post-migration. This approach not only captures emotional and psychological experiences but also offers insights into how cultural identities are reshaped amidst new environments. Across the sources, there is agreement on the importance of recognizing refugee youth as active agents who actively negotiate their positions within complex social structures, rather than passive victims of circumstances.

## Key Findings

- Refugee youth experience significant changes in roles and responsibilities after migration due to shifts in cultural contexts and familial dynamics.
- Arts-informed research methodologies are particularly effective for capturing the subjective experiences of refugee youth who may struggle with verbalizing complex emotions or traumatic memories.
- Understanding refugee youth as active agents rather than passive victims enriches our comprehension of their resilience and agency.

## Open Questions

- What specific forms of support, beyond traditional psychological interventions, can most effectively aid in the social reintegration and development of roles for refugee youth?
