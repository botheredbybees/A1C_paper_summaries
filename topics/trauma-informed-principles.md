## Trauma-Informed Principles

The principle of trauma-informed care (TIC) highlights the importance of integrating awareness about the impact of trauma on individuals into every aspect of service delivery. Source 1 discusses how applying TIC in creative arts and play projects is essential to ensuring a person-centered approach that respects each participant's unique experiences and needs, emphasizing adaptability and responsiveness. This aligns with the broader scope presented in Source 2, which offers an organizational toolkit aimed at fostering a sustainable culture of trauma-informed practices through quality improvement plans. Both sources underscore the necessity for service providers to be attuned to individual histories of trauma while also implementing systemic changes that promote healing and resilience across various settings.

## Key Findings

- Trauma-informed principles are crucial for adopting a person-centered approach in creative arts and play projects
- Organizations can foster sustainable cultural change through the implementation of quality improvement plans based on TIC principles
- Systemic changes are necessary to support healing and resilience alongside individualized care
