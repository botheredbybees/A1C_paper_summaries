## Music Therapy

The integration of music therapy across various neurological conditions underscores its profound impact on cognitive function, emotional well-being, and quality of life. Sources highlight that music can evoke vivid memories and emotions in individuals with dementia (Sacks, 2006), suggesting its effectiveness as a reminiscence tool. Neuroscientist Daniel Levitin discusses the therapeutic potential of music, noting its ability to alleviate pain through natural opioids (Levitin, n.d.). Contemporary research supports these insights by demonstrating that musical activities engage different parts of the brain, aiding recovery from traumatic brain injuries (Vik et al., 2019). The concept of neuroplasticity is emphasized in studies showing enhanced activity within the orbitofrontal cortex after music-supported therapy for TBI patients (neuroplastic-effects-in-patients-with-traumatic-brain), indicating music's potential to facilitate cognitive and behavioral rehabilitation. However, a twin study suggests that playing musical instruments may serve as a protective factor against dementia but does not significantly improve cognitive or memory outcomes in those already diagnosed with the condition (playing-a-musical-instrument-as-a-protective-factor). The Music and Memory Program further illustrates personalized music therapy's role in reducing behavioral problems among nursing home residents with dementia, though improvements in medication reduction and overall quality of life were observed.

## Key Findings

- Music evokes vivid memories and emotions, aiding reminiscence for individuals with neurological conditions like dementia (Sacks).
- Music activates various brain areas, potentially enhancing cognitive function and motor skills post-TBI (Vik et al., 2019; neuroplastic-effects-in-patients-with-traumatic-brain).
- Engaging in musical activities may serve as a protective factor against dementia but shows limited effect on reversing cognitive decline once it has occurred.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of personalized music therapy on patients with established neurological conditions, and how does its impact compare to pharmacological interventions?
