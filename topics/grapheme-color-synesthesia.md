## Grapheme-Color Synesthesia

The study of grapheme-color synesthesia reveals a complex interplay between neural connectivity and sensory perception. Source 1 explores the functional connectivity in individuals with this form of synesthesia, highlighting enhanced connections within the inferior parietal lobule. This suggests that these regions play a critical role in integrating visual and color information. In contrast, Source 2 delves into age-related changes in grapheme-to-color associations over the adult lifespan, indicating variability in how consistent these associations remain throughout life. Despite differences in approach, both sources underscore the neural basis of synesthetic experiences and their potential to vary with developmental stages.

## Key Findings

- Grapheme-color synesthesia involves enhanced functional connectivity within specific brain regions like the inferior parietal lobule.
- Synesthetic grapheme-to-color associations can remain consistent over time but may also show variability, especially as individuals age.

## Open Questions

- How do developmental changes influence the stability and intensity of synesthetic experiences?
