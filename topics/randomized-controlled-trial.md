## Randomized Controlled Trials

The sources highlight the utility and multifaceted application of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) in evaluating therapeutic interventions such as dance movement therapy for dementia patients. Source 1 specifically investigates the impact of dance movement therapy on individuals with dementia, comparing it against standard care through an RCT framework. The study emphasizes the structured nature of RCTs in providing robust evidence regarding intervention efficacy. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on the broader evaluation methodologies used to assess health and wellbeing benefits, including both qualitative research methods and quantitative approaches like RCTs. This source underscores the importance of integrating diverse methodological strategies to capture a comprehensive understanding of therapeutic outcomes beyond what RCTs alone can provide.

## Key Findings

- RCTs are crucial for evaluating the efficacy of interventions such as dance movement therapy in dementia care
- Integrating qualitative and quantitative research methods offers a more holistic approach to assessing health benefits
