## Non-Normative Ethics in Healthcare

The concept of non-normative ethics within healthcare is explored through its application in creative arts and person-centered care approaches. Non-normative ethics challenges traditional ethical frameworks by focusing on the uniqueness of individual patient experiences rather than adhering strictly to established norms or guidelines. This perspective is particularly relevant for complex interventions where standardized protocols may not adequately address the multifaceted needs of patients. Sources highlight how adopting non-normative ethics can lead to more personalized and effective healthcare practices, emphasizing flexibility over rigid adherence to conventional ethical doctrines.

## Key Findings

- Non-normative ethics emphasizes individual patient experiences over standard ethical norms.
- Person-centered care is facilitated by the application of non-normative ethics.
- Complex interventions benefit from the adaptability offered by non-normative ethical approaches.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare professionals balance flexibility in non-normative ethics with adherence to legal and regulatory standards?
