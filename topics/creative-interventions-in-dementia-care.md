## Creative Interventions in Dementia Care

The sources collectively illustrate the multifaceted impact of arts-based interventions on dementia care. Visual and creative arts programs significantly improve quality of life, communication skills, and overall well-being among individuals with dementia (Source 2). These programs are not only enriching but also foster a sense of cultural inclusivity and engagement with contemporary art, enhancing social interaction and appreciation for the environment (Source 3). However, critiques exist regarding the methodological rigor in assessing these interventions, highlighting issues such as randomization and potential confounding factors that may influence outcomes (Source 4). Despite these challenges, arts-based activities like color therapy and photography are shown to offer therapeutic benefits, enhancing both hedonic and eudaimonic well-being through reflective practice (Sources 6 & 7). The broader application of creative practices extends beyond dementia care into disaster recovery and resilience building, demonstrating the versatility and wide-reaching benefits of such interventions across diverse populations and contexts.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions significantly improve quality of life and communication skills in people with dementia.
- Creative activities enhance both hedonic and eudaimonic well-being through reflective practice.
- The methodological rigor of assessing arts-based interventions is a critical area for future research.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms by which arts-based interventions improve mental well-being in dementia patients?
