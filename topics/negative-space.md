## Negative Space in Photography

The concept of negative space in photography is pivotal for creating visually compelling images. Both sources emphasize the importance of utilizing empty or unoccupied areas to draw attention to the main subject, a technique that enhances visual hierarchy and balance within an image. The use of negative space promotes minimalism, ensuring that the focus remains on what matters most—often leading to more impactful storytelling through fewer distractions. Source 2 introduces its counterpart, positive space, explaining how the interplay between these two elements can significantly influence composition techniques. This duality highlights a broader compositional approach in photography, advocating for intentional design choices rather than cluttered shots.

## Key Findings

- Negative space is crucial for creating balance and visual hierarchy in photographs.
- Intentional use of negative space promotes minimalism in photographic storytelling.
- The interplay between positive and negative spaces enriches compositional techniques.
