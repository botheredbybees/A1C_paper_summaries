## Disabilities

The concept of disabilities encompasses not only the physical or mental impairments that individuals face but also the societal perceptions and stereotypes that create significant social barriers. Across sources, there is a consistent emphasis on transforming perceptions through educational interventions and experiential learning to challenge preconceived notions about disability. These efforts highlight the importance of understanding disabilities beyond mere medical definitions, emphasizing the role of societal attitudes in shaping experiences and opportunities for individuals with disabilities.

## Key Findings

- Disabilities are more than just physical or mental impairments; they also include social barriers created by stereotypes and societal perceptions.
- Educational interventions aimed at transforming perceptions about disabilities can play a crucial role in reducing stigma and improving inclusivity.
- Understanding the broader context of disability, including its intersection with societal attitudes and norms, is essential for effective support and policy-making.

## Open Questions

- How can educational programs effectively address and change deeply rooted stereotypes about individuals with disabilities?
