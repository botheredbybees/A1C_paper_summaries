## Puppetry Therapy

The use of puppetry in therapeutic contexts emerges as a versatile intervention aimed at enhancing mental and emotional well-being. Source one emphasizes the integration of creativity through puppet play to address vicarious trauma and foster emotional development among individuals, suggesting that puppets serve as a medium for expressing emotions non-verbally and creatively. This aligns with source two's application of puppets within an educational setting for immigrant and refugee children, where puppets facilitate the expression of helplessness and empower children by providing them with coping mechanisms through storytelling. Both sources highlight puppetry's capacity to bridge emotional articulation gaps, enabling participants to engage in therapeutic activities that enhance their sense of agency and well-being.

## Key Findings

- Puppetry therapy offers a creative outlet for expressing emotions non-verbally.
- Use of puppets can help mitigate vicarious trauma by facilitating emotional expression and development.
- In educational settings, puppetry enhances coping mechanisms and a sense of agency in children.

## Open Questions

- How does the efficacy of puppetry therapy vary across different cultural contexts?
