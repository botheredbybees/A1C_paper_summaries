## Holistic Understanding in Healthcare

The concept of a 'holistic understanding' in healthcare emerges from the integration of person-centered care and sustained interaction (Source 1) with medical humanism and evidence-based medicine (Source 2). Both sources advocate for a transformative approach that prioritizes the whole patient, including their emotional and social needs alongside their clinical requirements. However, there is a tension between the emphasis on individualized care versus standardized practices in evidence-based medicine. While Source 1 highlights the necessity of sustained interaction to build trust and understanding with patients, Source 2 introduces the challenge of integrating subjective humanistic values within objective medical practice.

## Key Findings

- Person-centered care requires sustained interaction between healthcare providers and patients
- Medical humanism complements evidence-based medicine by addressing emotional and social needs of patients
- A holistic approach to patient care involves a transformative shift in clinical practice

## Open Questions

- How can medical humanism be effectively integrated into the framework of evidence-based medicine?
