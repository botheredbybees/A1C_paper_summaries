## Arts and Crafts Therapy

The integration of arts and crafts activities into therapeutic settings demonstrates a multifaceted approach to enhancing both mental and physical well-being. In the context of photography sessions with Mary Stringer, lighting techniques emerge as crucial elements in achieving high-quality photographic outcomes that can also serve as a means for academic achievement through creative expression. Meanwhile, successful crafts and game ideas designed for Alzheimer's activities highlight the cognitive-stimulation benefits these interventions offer, alongside fostering social interaction which is critical for mental health and rehabilitation among patients with dementia. Both sources underscore the therapeutic potential of arts-based activities in enhancing various aspects of patient care, from improving visual skills to promoting social engagement.

## Key Findings

- Lighting techniques enhance academic achievement through creative expression.
- Arts and crafts promote cognitive stimulation for individuals with Alzheimer's disease.
- Social interaction is a critical component of rehabilitation and mental health support.
