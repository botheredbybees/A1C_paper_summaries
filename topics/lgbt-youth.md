## Mental Health Among Lgbt Youth

The mental health of LGBT youth is a critical area of study, characterized by significant risks and challenges. Both sources highlight the application of minority stress theory to understand the elevated rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality among this population. Distal factors like societal discrimination and proximal stressors such as internalized homonegativity exacerbate mental health issues. While protective factors play a role in mitigating these risks, there is an ongoing need for effective interventions tailored specifically to LGBT youth.

## Key Findings

- Minority stress theory explains elevated rates of depression, anxiety, and suicidality among LGBT youth.
- Distal and proximal stressors contribute significantly to mental health issues experienced by LGBT youth.
- Protective factors can mitigate the risks associated with being an LGBT youth.
