## Sundowner Theatre Productions

The sources discuss sundowner theatre productions as a significant intervention in dementia care. This form of play and performance is aimed at engaging individuals with dementia through creative arts, specifically focusing on activities that occur during the late afternoon and early evening periods when symptoms like agitation or confusion often peak (sundowning). Sources highlight various programs such as Playful Engagement Project and Timeslips Program, each emphasizing the importance of creating a supportive environment for cognitive stimulation and emotional expression. Sundowner theatre productions are seen not just as entertainment but also as therapeutic interventions that can improve quality of life by fostering social interaction, enhancing mood, and reducing stress among participants.

## Key Findings

- Sundowner theatre productions offer a unique form of therapy for individuals with dementia.
- These programs aim to reduce symptoms associated with sundowning through creative engagement.
- Social interaction and emotional expression are key components of sundowner theatre's benefits.
