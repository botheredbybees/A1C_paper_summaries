## Poetry in Research

The integration of poetry into research methodologies within the context of aged care has been explored through multiple studies. Sources highlight the use of arts-based interventions like photovoice and documentary photography as means to capture lived experiences and aging processes with depth and nuance, often employing a hermeneutic-phenomenological approach. This qualitative methodology allows for emotive research that reflects personal narratives and emotional dimensions not easily captured through traditional methods. Both sources emphasize the role of poetry in enriching these artistic methodologies by providing a medium to express complex feelings and insights succinctly yet profoundly.

## Key Findings

- Poetry enhances arts-based research by capturing intricate emotions and experiences.
- Photovoice and documentary photography are effective for documenting lived aging experiences
- A hermeneutic-phenomenological approach aids in understanding emotional dimensions of aging

## Open Questions

- How does the use of poetry within these methodologies affect generalizability of research findings?
