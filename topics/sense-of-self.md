## Sense of Self

The concept of 'sense of self' emerges consistently across the three sources as a central theme in understanding individual identity and development. In the context of resilience and emotional regulation, Source 1 highlights how individuals can maintain a positive sense of self despite challenges. This idea is further explored in Sources 2 and 3, which focus on an art therapy program for children with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD). These sources emphasize that enhancing a child's sense of self through creative expression not only fosters emotional regulation but also promotes flexibility in behavior and social skills. The integration of therapeutic practices such as those described can be pivotal in helping individuals navigate their identities, especially when faced with developmental challenges.

## Key Findings

- Sense-of-self is critical for resilience in the face of personal challenges.
- Enhancing sense-of-self through creative therapies supports emotion-regulation and flexibility-in-behavior.
- Improvement in social-skills correlates with an enhanced sense-of-self in individuals diagnosed with ASD.

## Open Questions

- How do different therapeutic approaches impact the development of a positive sense-of-self in diverse populations?
