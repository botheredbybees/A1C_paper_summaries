## Visual Imagery in Emotional Experiences

The concept of visual imagery is pivotal across multiple studies within the context of emotional experiences and individual differences such as dispositional empathy. These sources emphasize how visual imagery contributes to the perception of sublimity, a sense of awe and beauty often experienced during live music performances. Individual differences in these areas suggest that people who score higher on measures of visual imagery might experience deeper or more vivid emotional responses to music due to their enhanced ability to mentally picture scenes described through auditory cues like those found in music lyrics or the atmosphere created by live performance settings. However, there is a tension regarding how exactly visual imagery interacts with other cognitive and affective processes during such experiences.

## Key Findings

- Visual imagery significantly influences emotional responses during live music performances.
- Individuals high in dispositional empathy tend to have higher visual imagery capabilities.
- The perception of sublimity is strongly linked with the ability for vivid mental imagery.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neural mechanisms by which visual imagery enhances emotional experiences during live music performances?
