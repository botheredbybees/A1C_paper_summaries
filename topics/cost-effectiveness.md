## Cost-effectiveness of Art-based Interventions in Healthcare

The integration of arts into healthcare practices has been shown to offer a range of benefits including symptom management and increased self-efficacy, as highlighted in the summary of 'Is There Compelling Evidence for Using the Arts in Health Care?'. However, while these outcomes are promising, they must be balanced against the practical challenges of implementation such as cost-effectiveness. The article 'Implementing Creative Health' discusses this issue directly, advocating for a rigorous analysis that goes beyond qualitative benefits to include economic viability. It emphasizes the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration and the alignment with broader goals like health equity and sustainable development. A tension arises between the demonstrated positive impacts of arts-based interventions on patient outcomes and their cost-effectiveness when scaled up in healthcare systems.

## Key Findings

- Art-based interventions offer significant benefits for symptom management and self-efficacy in patients.
- Interdisciplinary collaboration is essential for integrating arts into health practices effectively.
- Cost-effectiveness analysis is crucial before scaling up art-based health initiatives.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term economic impacts of implementing large-scale, sustainable creative health programs?
