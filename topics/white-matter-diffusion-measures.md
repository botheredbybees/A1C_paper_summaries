## White Matter Diffusion Measures

The study of white matter diffusion measures in the context of neurodevelopmental conditions such as autism spectrum condition (ASC) reveals consistent age-related differences in key metrics like fractional anisotropy (FA) and radial diffusivity (RD). Across various studies, these diffusion characteristics are found to be altered compared to typical development. The integration of advanced imaging techniques with behavioral assessments enhances understanding of neural maturation processes. However, there remains a need for longitudinal investigations to clarify the temporal dynamics of white matter changes and their implications on cognitive function.

## Key Findings

- Fractional anisotropy (FA) and radial diffusivity (RD) measures in white matter show significant age-related differences in individuals with ASC compared to typical development.
- Advanced imaging techniques combined with behavioral assessments enhance the understanding of neural maturation processes in ASC.
- White matter diffusion characteristics are altered across the lifespan in individuals with ASC, indicating disrupted neurodevelopmental trajectories.

## Open Questions

- What is the temporal dynamics of white matter changes and their implications on cognitive function over the lifespan?
