## Found Spaces Vs Constructed Spaces

The analysis of 'found' and 'constructed' spaces in the context of children's outdoor play reveals a nuanced interplay between natural and human-induced disaster resilience. Found spaces, which are naturally occurring areas not specifically designed for recreational use, contrast with constructed spaces purposefully created for play and social interaction. Research highlights that both types offer unique benefits; found spaces tend to foster greater creativity due to their unstructured nature, while constructed ones provide safety through design elements like playground equipment and clear boundaries. However, the effectiveness of these spaces in disaster contexts is under scrutiny, particularly regarding accessibility, durability against environmental stressors, and adaptability for diverse play needs.

## Key Findings

- Found spaces promote greater creativity among children due to their unstructured nature.
- Constructed spaces provide safety features which are important in enhancing playground security and user confidence.
- The role of found vs. constructed spaces becomes more complex during disaster scenarios, highlighting the need for resilient design considerations.

## Open Questions

- How can natural spaces be effectively utilized to enhance resilience without compromising on safety?
