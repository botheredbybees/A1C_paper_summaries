## Food Memories

The role of food in shaping family dynamics and individual identity is explored through the concept of intergenerational transmission in both sources. Both emphasize how food serves as a conduit for cultural-historiography, embedding historical narratives and personal histories within familial bonds. Food acts not only as a means to sustain life but also as a medium that facilitates resilience and love. Source 1 highlights this aspect by demonstrating how certain foods act as symbolic representations of family connections and emotional health. Meanwhile, source two delves into the therapeutic value of food memories through an arts-in-healthcare lens, showing how these memories contribute to personal identity formation and cognitive development among individuals. Despite their shared focus on the importance of food in familial contexts, there is a nuanced difference between the sources: one focuses more on the emotional and cultural significance of food whereas the other examines the therapeutic role of these memories.

## Key Findings

- Food serves as a significant medium for intergenerational transmission and family bonding.
- The role of food in familial contexts extends beyond sustenance to include cultural-historiography, resilience, and love.
- Therapeutic use of food memories can aid in personal identity formation and emotional health.

## Open Questions
