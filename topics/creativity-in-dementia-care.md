## Creativity in Dementia Care

The integration of creative activities as an intervention for individuals with dementia highlights a shift from purely pharmacological approaches towards more holistic and person-centered care methods. Sources emphasize the importance of non-pharmacological interventions to improve quality of life, cognitive function, and emotional well-being in those affected by dementia. The inclusion of artistic endeavors such as painting, music, dance, and storytelling is seen not just as a form of entertainment but also as a therapeutic medium that fosters social engagement and personal expression. However, while there is broad agreement on the benefits of creative interventions, implementation challenges persist due to resource limitations and varying levels of caregiver education regarding these methods.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities are beneficial non-pharmacological interventions for individuals with dementia.
- Such interventions improve cognitive function, emotional well-being, and social engagement in people living with dementia.
- Artistic pursuits can serve as therapeutic mediums that foster personal expression.

## Open Questions

- What are the barriers to implementing creative activities widely across different care settings?
