## Situational Awareness

The concept of situational awareness emerges prominently in the discourse on dementia care, particularly in understanding how individuals with dementia experience and communicate their environment. The key theme across sources is the notion of 'collective subjective' as a means to articulate meaning within the context of dementia care settings. This involves recognizing the interplay between an individual's internal mental state and external environmental factors, which collectively contribute to situational awareness. Sources highlight the importance of caregivers being attuned not only to patients' verbal expressions but also to their non-verbal cues and the broader situational context in which these interactions occur. However, there is a tension around how much reliance should be placed on subjective experiences versus objective measures in assessing situational awareness.

## Key Findings

- Situational awareness in dementia care involves recognizing both internal mental states and external environmental factors.
- The collective subjective approach emphasizes the integration of patient's non-verbal cues with their verbal expressions.
- Caregivers need to be attuned to multiple dimensions of the environment to understand patients' situational awareness effectively.

## Open Questions

- How can caregivers balance subjective experiences with objective measures in evaluating situational awareness?
