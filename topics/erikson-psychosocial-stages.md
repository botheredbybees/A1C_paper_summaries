## Erikson's Psychosocial Stages

Erik Erikson's theory of psychosocial development posits a series of eight stages through which an individual progresses throughout their lifespan. Each stage is characterized by a specific psychological conflict that must be resolved to achieve healthy personality growth and identity formation. The theory emphasizes the importance of social interaction in resolving these conflicts, with each stage building on previous ones to foster continual personal development. Despite its influence, critiques point out inconsistencies and lack of empirical validation for some stages.

## Key Findings

- Erikson's theory outlines a series of eight psychosocial stages from infancy through late adulthood.
- Each stage is marked by the resolution of specific conflicts crucial to identity formation and social interaction.
- Critics note limitations in empirical evidence supporting certain aspects of the theory.
