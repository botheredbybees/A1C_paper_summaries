## Art-Science Collaboration

The integration of artistic and scientific methodologies in the study of human conditions such as dementia highlights a holistic approach that transcends traditional disciplinary boundaries. Source one emphasizes the use of an interpretative, humanistic framework to understand dementia through social constructivism, suggesting that memory and forgetting are contextual phenomena influenced by personal and societal experiences. This perspective aligns with the interdisciplinary nature highlighted in source two, where site-responsive art and drawing serve as tools for engaging with environments and communities in a manner deeply intertwined with scientific inquiry. Both sources underscore the value of collaboration between artists and scientists to explore complex human experiences from multiple angles, offering new insights into how memory and perception are shaped within specific contexts.

## Key Findings

- Art-science collaborations provide a nuanced understanding of human conditions through contextual interpretation and social constructivism
- Site-responsive art enhances the interdisciplinary nature of research by engaging directly with environmental and community factors
- Interdisciplinary approaches can foster a more holistic view of memory, forgetting, and perception

## Open Questions

- How can the methodologies used in site-responsive arts be further integrated into scientific studies to enhance our understanding of complex human conditions?
