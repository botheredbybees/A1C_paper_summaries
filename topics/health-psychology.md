## Music and Health Interventions

The integration of music in health interventions highlights its therapeutic potential beyond traditional paradigms like expressive writing. Source 1 underscores the emotional engagement and neurological effects of music, suggesting it can significantly impact mental and physical wellbeing through direct physiological responses and psychological benefits. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on the expressive writing paradigm as a therapeutic intervention but also discusses evolving models of developmental creative writing that go beyond mere expression to include more comprehensive therapeutic approaches. While both sources acknowledge the therapeutic value of creative expressions such as music and writing, there is an implied divergence in their methodologies and focus areas: one emphasizes auditory stimuli (music) while the other explores written forms of self-expression.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy shows significant emotional engagement and neurological benefits.
- Expressive writing has long been recognized for its therapeutic value.
- Creative interventions such as music and expressive writing are integral to health psychology

## Open Questions

- What is the comparative efficacy between music-based interventions and written expression therapies in clinical settings?
