## Psychological Well-Being through Creative and Expressive Activities

The integration of creative activities such as music, expressive writing, and therapeutic clowning has shown positive impacts on psychological well-being across different populations. Source 1 emphasizes the influence of cultural activities like music in enhancing cognitive development and longevity, contributing to a broader sense of mental health. In contrast, Source 2 highlights that expressive writing can improve immune system functioning alongside psychological well-being, underscoring the therapeutic benefits of creative expression through written forms. Similarly, Source 3 investigates the use of medical clowns as an intervention for hospitalized children experiencing fear, revealing how this form of creative interaction positively affects their mental health by providing comfort and reducing anxiety levels.

## Key Findings

- Creative activities such as music and writing can enhance psychological well-being.
- Expressive writing improves immune system functioning in addition to alleviating stress.
- Medical clown interventions reduce fear and improve psychological well-being in hospitalized children.
