## Nonpharmacological Treatments

The integration of multiple sources focusing on nonpharmacological treatments reveals a consistent emphasis on the use of supportive and engaging activities to manage symptoms in individuals with conditions like Alzheimer's disease. Sources highlight interventions such as music therapy, art therapy, and structured daily routines as effective ways to improve quality of life. However, there is variability in the specific types of activities recommended across different sources, indicating a need for further research on the most efficacious treatments.

## Key Findings

- Nonpharmacological treatments are crucial in managing symptoms of Alzheimer's disease.
- Structured daily routines and engaging activities can significantly improve quality of life.
- The ARTZ program is highlighted as an effective nonpharmacological intervention.

## Open Questions

- What specific types of nonpharmacological interventions are most effective for different stages of Alzheimer's disease?
