## Core Curriculum For Arts In Health Professionals

The integration of arts into health professions has been a focus on professionalizing the field through structured frameworks and standards. Both sources highlight the importance of establishing a Core Curriculum for Arts in Health Professionals to ensure that practitioners have a foundational understanding and skills relevant to their practice. The curriculum's development aims at promoting systemic capacity building, which supports the overall growth and credibility of arts-in-health practices. Additionally, the establishment of a Code of Ethics alongside a certification exam process underscores the commitment to maintaining high professional standards within this interdisciplinary field. However, there is an implied challenge in standardizing such comprehensive frameworks across diverse healthcare settings while accommodating the unique contributions of various art forms.

## Key Findings

- A Core Curriculum for Arts in Health Professionals is essential for skill and knowledge development among practitioners.
- Systemic capacity building is crucial for enhancing the professional standing of arts-in-health practices.
- Establishing a Code of Ethics alongside a certification exam process supports professionalization.

## Open Questions

- How can diverse healthcare settings effectively integrate and standardize the Core Curriculum while accommodating unique artistic contributions?
