## Earworms

The concept of earworms spans several dimensions of human experience including involuntary musical imagery (INMI), emotional contagion, and empathy. Earworms are characterized by repetitive cycles of a song or melody playing continuously in one's mind despite the lack of auditory stimulation. Sources indicate that these phenomena can be triggered by various factors such as emotional states, attentional conditions, and specific brain functions involving the limbic system, dorsal striatum, and basal ganglia. The BRECVEMA model proposes an integrated framework for understanding earworm triggers based on context (B), repetition (R), emotions (E), conditions (C), values (V), meaning (M), and other music-related activities (A). Musical obsessions or stuck-song syndrome can sometimes indicate underlying mental health issues such as obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) necessitating tailored coping strategies.

## Key Findings

- Earworms are involuntary musical imagery triggered by emotional states and attentional conditions.
- The brain's limbic system, dorsal striatum, and basal ganglia play crucial roles in generating earworms.
- Musical obsessions may indicate underlying mental health issues such as OCD.
