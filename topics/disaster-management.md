## Creative Arts and Disaster Management

The integration of creative arts into disaster management interventions emerges as a multifaceted approach to support survivors' mental health and resilience building. Sources highlight the importance of trauma-informed design, which emphasizes understanding the effects of traumatic experiences on individuals and communities and designing recovery efforts that are sensitive to these impacts (Source 1). The National Taskforce for Creative Recovery Recommendations underscores the role of creative practices in facilitating trauma processing and fostering community engagement through inclusive arts initiatives (Source 2). Both sources converge on the need for holistic approaches that go beyond immediate emergency response to include long-term mental health support, recognizing the chronic stress and PTSD that can follow disasters. However, there is a noted gap regarding how these interventions are tailored to specific cultural contexts and individual needs.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions in disaster management promote trauma processing and resilience.
- Trauma-informed design principles guide effective recovery efforts.
- Inclusive community engagement through creative practices strengthens social cohesion.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts interventions be culturally adapted to meet diverse survivor needs?
