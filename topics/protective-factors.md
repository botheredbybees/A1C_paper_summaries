## Protective Factors in Mental Health

Both sources highlight the critical role of protective factors in mitigating mental health issues. Source 1 discusses art therapy as a resilience-building tool that provides psychological relief to individuals at the border, suggesting that creative expression can serve as a protective factor against stress and trauma. This aligns with the broader understanding from Source 2 about how certain interventions within the LGBT community, such as affirming environments and supportive networks, act similarly as protective factors, reducing risks like depression, anxiety, and suicidality. Both sources emphasize the importance of tailored approaches that recognize unique challenges faced by different populations to effectively address mental health needs.

## Key Findings

- Protective factors significantly mitigate psychological distress.
- Creative expression through art therapy is a resilience-building intervention.
- Affirming environments and supportive networks reduce risk factors for LGBT youth.
