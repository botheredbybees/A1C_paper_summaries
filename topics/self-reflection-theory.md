## Self-Reflection Theory

The concept of Self-Reflection Theory is crucial in understanding how individuals can critically examine their thoughts and behaviors. This theory is explored within the context of school-based drama interventions, which serve as a practical application for enhancing self-reflection skills among children and adolescents. The integration of educational-drama techniques in health promotion activities highlights the importance of interactive learning in fostering an environment where participants engage deeply with themselves and others through role-play and reflective exercises.

## Key Findings

- Self-Reflection Theory enhances personal development by encouraging individuals to critically assess their own thoughts, feelings, and actions.
- School-based drama interventions utilize educational-drama techniques to promote self-reflection among young people.
- Interactive learning is a cornerstone in implementing Self-Reflection Theory effectively within educational settings.
