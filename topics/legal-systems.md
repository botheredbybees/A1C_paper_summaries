## Legal Systems Through Visual Storytelling

The provided sources outline a course on the legal systems that emphasizes visual storytelling as a method for understanding and communicating legal concepts. The course progresses through various techniques including camera use, composition, caption writing, creative storytelling with images, and image editing. Throughout these activities, students engage in free-writing exercises to explore their ideas visually, learn about ethical considerations like photo consent forms, and refine both their visual skills and written narratives based on feedback. This method of learning legal systems integrates technology (e.g., camera phones) with artistic expression and critical thinking.

## Key Findings

- The course utilizes visual storytelling as a core methodology to teach legal concepts
- Ethical considerations such as obtaining photo consent forms are addressed alongside technical skills
- Feedback loops are integral in refining both visual images and written narratives

## Open Questions

- How effective is the use of visual media, particularly photography, compared to traditional methods for teaching legal systems?
