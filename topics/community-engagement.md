## Community Engagement

Community engagement is a multifaceted intervention aimed at fostering participation and collaboration among diverse groups within a community. The sources highlight the importance of artistic expression in driving social change, educational outcomes, and cultural sustainability (Sources 2, 3, 5). Photovoice and documentary photography serve as powerful tools for storytelling and advocacy, particularly in aged care settings where they facilitate residents' active participation and representation of their needs and experiences (Source 1). Artistic engagement also plays a pivotal role in promoting collective memory, re-enchantment, and cultural revitalization by enabling communities to address issues such as PTSD among veterans or historical disenfranchisement faced by migrants (Sources 9, 10). The initiative 'Cork As Canvas' illustrates how murals can become platforms for civic engagement, fostering a sense of belonging and collective identity. Furthermore, community-based arts initiatives have been shown to enhance educational attainment, digital literacy, and lifelong learning opportunities among various demographics including seniors and veterans (Sources 5, 8, 9). However, despite these promising outcomes, challenges persist in ensuring equitable access to such programs, necessitating ongoing research into inclusive methodologies that cater to diverse cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Artistic expression is a powerful tool for community engagement, driving social change and fostering collective identity.
- Photovoice and documentary photography are effective methods for empowering marginalized groups in aged care settings.
- Community-based arts initiatives can improve educational outcomes and digital literacy among various demographics.

## Open Questions

- How can inclusive methodologies be developed to ensure equitable access to community engagement programs across diverse cultural contexts?
