## Engagement with Music and Dance in Historical Contexts

The historical perspective on the use of music and dance as therapeutic interventions is explored through two sources. Source one highlights the role of quadrivium, an ancient concept involving arithmetic, geometry, astronomy, and music, suggesting a structured approach to engaging with music for holistic development and spiritual elevation. Trance-inducing rituals are noted as part of this historical context, indicating the use of dance in altering consciousness states for communal or individual healing purposes. Source two moves forward into more recent history by discussing various modes of engagement such as participatory, experiential, and reflective practices that emphasize the therapeutic potential of engaging with music and dance within community settings. Healing practices are also mentioned, reflecting on how neural interactions are influenced positively when participating in these activities.

## Key Findings

- Historically, quadrivium has provided a structured approach to incorporating musical experiences for both educational and spiritual purposes.
- Trance-inducing rituals involving music and dance have been used as interventions for altering consciousness states.
- Recent therapeutic applications of music and dance emphasize participatory engagement for healing and neural interaction benefits.
