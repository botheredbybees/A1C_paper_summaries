## Puppet Theatre

Puppet theatre is an engaging and interactive intervention that can serve multiple purposes in theatrical performances as well as therapeutic contexts. Sources highlight the versatility of rod puppetry in fostering creative expression, with techniques detailed for sustainable puppet making to encourage long-term engagement (Source 2). In therapeutic settings, puppet theatre helps participants develop coping strategies and enhances mental wellbeing through non-judgmental interactions based on appearance and confidence building exercises (Sources 2 & 3). These activities facilitate emotional exploration and social skills development in a safe environment. However, while the benefits are clear, there is limited discussion on specific methods to assess long-term effectiveness of puppet theatre interventions.

## Key Findings

- Puppet theatre offers versatile creative expression opportunities
- Sustainable puppet making encourages prolonged engagement
- Therapeutic settings use non-judgmental interactions for emotional exploration

## Open Questions

- What methods are used to assess the long-term effectiveness of puppet theatre interventions?
