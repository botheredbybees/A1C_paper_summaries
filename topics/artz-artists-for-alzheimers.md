## Artz - Artists For Alzheimer's

ARTZ, also known as 'Artists for Alzheimer's,' is an innovative intervention program that leverages the power of art to engage individuals with Alzheimer's and their caregivers. The initiative operates within museum settings, facilitating a unique form of reminiscence therapy where participants can connect emotionally and cognitively through the exploration of art pieces. Across various sources, there is consensus on the effectiveness of ARTZ in improving quality of life for those affected by Alzheimer's disease. However, questions remain about the scalability of such programs within diverse cultural institutions and their broader impact on dementia care practices.

## Key Findings

- ARTZ provides a unique form of reminiscence therapy through museum-based art engagement.
- The intervention shows promise in enhancing emotional well-being for individuals with Alzheimer's disease.
- Museum settings offer an enriching environment for both participants and their caregivers.

## Open Questions

- How can the ARTZ model be adapted to cater to diverse cultural institutions and broader demographics?
