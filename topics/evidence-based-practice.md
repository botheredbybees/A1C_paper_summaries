## Evidence-Based Practice in Music Therapy

The integration of evidence-based practices into music therapy interventions for individuals with disabilities is a significant focus. Both sources highlight the importance of using established techniques by registered professionals to enhance community participation and overall well-being. Source 1, 'Music Therapy Disability Evidence Summary 2024,' emphasizes practical application through specific music therapy methods under professional supervision. Conversely, Source 2 introduces broader implications for policy development within a national framework aimed at integrating arts and health initiatives across sectors in Australia. Despite the focus on different levels—practical intervention versus overarching policy frameworks—both sources underscore the necessity of evidence-based approaches to improve therapeutic outcomes and inform future practice and legislation.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy techniques, when applied by registered therapists, significantly contribute to community participation among individuals with disabilities.
- Evidence-based practices are crucial for both practical interventions in music therapy and policy development at a national level.
- Sector engagement is vital in shaping evidence requirements that support arts and health initiatives.

## Open Questions

- How can the specific findings from practical music therapy applications be effectively translated into broader policy frameworks to maximize their impact?
