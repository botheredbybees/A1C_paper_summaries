## Creative Arts Interventions for Disadvantaged Youth

The integration of arts-based interventions for disadvantaged youth is highlighted across both sources as a critical method to support mental health. Both emphasize the importance of trauma-informed practices that recognize the unique challenges these youth face due to their socio-economic and psychological circumstances. The creative process, according to source two, is framed not only as therapeutic but also as a means for young people to regain agency over their lives, suggesting that the act of creating can serve multiple functions beyond mere expression or catharsis. However, while both sources agree on the importance of arts-based interventions and trauma-informed care, they do not fully articulate how these elements interact in practice nor address potential barriers to implementation.

## Key Findings

- Arts-based interventions are effective for supporting mental health among disadvantaged youth.
- Trauma-informed practices are essential when working with this population.
- The creative process can serve as a tool for empowering disadvantaged youth.

## Open Questions

- How do arts-based interventions and trauma-informed care interact in practice to provide comprehensive support?
