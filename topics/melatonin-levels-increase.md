## Melatonin Levels Increase Through Singing

The integration of findings from the provided sources underscores the beneficial effects of singing as an intervention on physiological and psychological health. Across these studies, it is evident that engaging in singing activities leads to a reduction in stress hormones while concurrently increasing melatonin levels. This dual effect suggests that singing not only aids in mitigating adverse stress responses but also supports better sleep regulation through hormonal balance. Additionally, the concept of music-based interventions highlights the therapeutic potential of such practices beyond traditional medical treatments. Furthermore, the development of community support networks around singing activities enhances social cohesion and mutual emotional support among participants, which is a critical component for sustained mental well-being.

## Key Findings

- Singing leads to an increase in melatonin levels.
- Music-based interventions reduce stress hormones.
- Community participation through singing boosts overall psychological health.
