## Autism Symptom Severity

The study of differences in temporal synchrony between brain regions in individuals with and without autism highlights the complexity of neurological functioning associated with autism symptom severity. Functional brain connectivity, particularly concerning cognitive processing speed and neural network dynamics, plays a crucial role in understanding these discrepancies. While the direct impact on intervention strategies is not explicitly detailed, the identification of specific patterns in neural activity could inform future targeted therapeutic approaches aimed at improving communication and social skills through modulation of neural synchrony.

## Key Findings

- Functional brain connectivity varies significantly between individuals with autism and those without.
- Temporal synchrony differences are linked to cognitive processing speed variations in autism.
- Understanding neural network dynamics can aid in the development of targeted interventions.

## Open Questions
