## Linear Storytelling

The provided source material discusses the concept of linear storytelling as a fundamental aspect of traditional narrative structures. Linear storytelling is characterized by a chronological progression and clear beginning, middle, and end stages. This contrasts with non-linear storytelling, which may present events out of time order or through multiple perspectives simultaneously. The lecture emphasizes that while linear storytelling offers clarity and coherence, it can limit the complexity and depth of narratives compared to more flexible non-linear approaches.

## Key Findings

- Linear storytelling provides a clear narrative structure with distinct phases.
- Non-linear storytelling is an alternative approach that offers greater narrative flexibility.
- Both forms have their merits depending on the story's intent and audience engagement.
