## Clinical Empathy

The concept of clinical empathy is explored through two distinct but complementary perspectives. The activist perspective emphasizes the importance of emotional engagement and understanding the social context in which patients live (Source 1). This view aligns with a holistic approach to medical humanities, which seeks to integrate humanistic values into healthcare practices beyond the mere application of evidence-based medicine (Source 2). Both sources highlight the need for clinicians to develop deeper connections with their patients by recognizing and addressing the broader social determinants of health. While Source 1 focuses on practical strategies to enhance empathy through active listening and emotional engagement, Source 2 advocates for a more systemic change within healthcare institutions, promoting medical humanism as an essential component in training future healthcare professionals.

## Key Findings

- Clinical empathy involves both emotional engagement with patients and understanding their social context.
- There is a growing emphasis on integrating humanistic values into clinical practice beyond evidence-based medicine alone.
- Medical humanities can play a crucial role in enhancing the holistic understanding of patient care.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare institutions effectively integrate the principles of medical humanism into their training programs and daily practices?
