## Expanded Field of Drawing

The 'expanded field of drawing' concept encompasses a broad and evolving understanding of artistic practice that extends beyond traditional media to include site-specific installations, interdisciplinary collaborations, and conceptual frameworks. Dr Annalise Rees's interview highlights the application of this method in an innovative arts project within Antarctica. The project integrates various techniques traditionally associated with drawing but expands them into larger-scale, environmentally responsive works. This approach underscores the importance of location and context as integral components of the creative process, blending art creation with scientific observation and documentation. The discussion reveals how artists are pushing boundaries by engaging directly with remote environments to produce artwork that not only captures visual elements but also conveys experiential narratives unique to specific sites.

## Key Findings

- The expanded field of drawing transcends conventional artistic mediums, incorporating diverse forms such as site-responsive installations and interdisciplinary collaborations.
- Environmental contexts significantly influence the creative output in expanded field practices, with artists often engaging directly with natural settings to create meaningful work.
- Interdisciplinary research plays a crucial role in contemporary art projects, especially those involving remote or extreme environments.

## Open Questions

- How can the principles of the expanded field be effectively applied and adapted across different cultural contexts?
