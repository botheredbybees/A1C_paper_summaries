## Natural Light Photography

The concept of natural light photography emphasizes the use of ambient lighting in capturing images, often incorporating elements of wabi-sabi aesthetics that focus on imperfection and transience. Anna Mackintosh's critical reflection highlights the integration of natural-light-photography within the framework of health-humanities, particularly in how it can serve as a form of phototherapy. This method not only enhances artistic expression but also has therapeutic benefits for practitioners through its meditative and reflective aspects.

## Key Findings

- Natural light photography is characterized by its reliance on ambient lighting conditions rather than artificial sources.
- The aesthetic principles of wabi-sabi are often employed in natural-light-photography, emphasizing the beauty in imperfection and simplicity.
- Photographic practices like natural light photography have therapeutic benefits beyond their artistic value.
