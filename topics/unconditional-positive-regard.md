## Unconditional Positive Regard

The concept of Unconditional Positive Regard (UPR) is central to the Person-Centered Therapy framework developed by Carl Rogers. UPR involves providing individuals with acceptance and support without judgment, fostering an environment where they can explore their feelings and experiences freely. This approach emphasizes the therapist's role in creating a non-judgmental space that enables clients to grow and self-actualize, highlighting key concepts like empathy and emotional health. In the context of community health and education, UPR is extended into creative arts practices as a means for social inclusion and flourishing, reflecting its broader applicability beyond therapy settings.

## Key Findings

- Unconditional Positive Regard fosters an environment where individuals can explore their feelings without judgment.
- Person-Centered Therapy emphasizes the therapist's role in providing acceptance and support.
- The application of UPR extends beyond traditional therapeutic settings to areas like community health and education.
