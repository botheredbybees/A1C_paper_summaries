## Refugee Youth

The studies on refugee youth highlight the multifaceted challenges these adolescents face, particularly in cognitive development, emotional symptoms, and behavioral difficulties. Creative arts therapy has been evaluated as a potential intervention for addressing some of these issues within school settings, indicating its utility in supporting mental health and cognitive growth. Meanwhile, using arts-informed research to understand changes in roles and responsibilities offers an innovative approach to studying the vulnerabilities specific to refugee youth due to migration experiences.

## Key Findings

- Refugee adolescents face significant behavioral difficulties and emotional symptoms.
- Creative arts therapy can serve as a beneficial intervention for cognitive development among refugee youth.
- Arts-informed research methods provide valuable insights into the changing roles and responsibilities of refugee youth.

## Open Questions

- How can creative arts interventions be adapted to address the unique challenges faced by refugee youth in different cultural contexts?
