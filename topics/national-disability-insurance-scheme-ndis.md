## Music Engagement and the NDIS

The integration of music therapy into the framework of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) is gaining traction as a beneficial intervention for individuals with intellectual disabilities. Sources indicate that music engagement, through either structured therapy or unstructured listening activities, offers various functional benefits when accessed via NDIS funding. The inclusion of music therapy under NDIS services provides these participants greater access to tailored interventions that can enhance their quality of life and address specific needs such as social interaction, emotional regulation, and cognitive function. However, there remains a need for more empirical evidence from studies utilizing advanced neuroimaging techniques like fMRI to substantiate the neurological impact of music engagement on individuals within NDIS.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy is increasingly available through the NDIS as an intervention for people with intellectual disabilities.
- Structured and unstructured forms of music engagement can provide functional benefits such as emotional regulation, social interaction, and cognitive enhancement.
- The integration of neuroimaging studies could further substantiate the efficacy of music therapy in treating individuals within the NDIS.

## Open Questions

- What specific neurological mechanisms underpin the observed therapeutic effects of music engagement among participants with intellectual disabilities?
