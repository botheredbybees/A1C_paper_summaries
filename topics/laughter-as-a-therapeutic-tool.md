## Laughter as a Therapeutic Tool

The concept of laughter as a therapeutic tool is explored in two sources, both emphasizing the utility and benefits of spontaneous and simulated laughter for enhancing physical and mental health. Both sources highlight the 'motion creates emotion' theory, suggesting that even feigned laughter can lead to genuine positive emotional responses and physiological benefits such as reduced stress levels and improved immune function. The integration of laughter into lifestyle medicine practices is advocated, with simulations often employed when natural laughter is not sufficient or feasible.

## Key Findings

- Spontaneous and simulated laughter are both beneficial for mental and physical health.
- 'Motion creates emotion' theory indicates that feigned laughter can lead to genuine positive emotional responses.
- Laughter prescription is a viable tool within lifestyle medicine practices.

## Open Questions

- What is the long-term efficacy of simulated laughter compared to spontaneous laughter in therapeutic contexts?
