## Universal Membership in Awe Experiences

The concept of awe as explored across several studies suggests that it is a profound and transformative emotional state that can lead to shifts in personal identity and broader existential considerations. This phenomenon, encapsulated by the key concepts of cognitive accommodation, self-diminishing effects, and universal membership, indicates how awe experiences not only challenge an individual's existing views but also prompt a re-evaluation of one’s place within the world at large. Cognitive accommodation suggests that individuals adapt their mental frameworks to incorporate novel or challenging information from awe-inducing stimuli. The resultant 'self-diminishing effect' often leads individuals to feel smaller in comparison to vast, majestic scenes, fostering a sense of humility and interconnectedness. This state can subsequently enhance feelings of universal membership, whereby people perceive themselves as part of something greater than their personal identities, thus expanding the scope of social identity from local to global or cosmic.

## Key Findings

- Awe experiences prompt cognitive accommodation, leading individuals to reassess and expand their mental models.
- The self-diminishing effect associated with awe can induce a sense of humility and connectedness to the world beyond oneself.
- Feeling part of something larger than one’s individual identity contributes to a sense of universal membership.

## Open Questions

- How do cultural differences influence the experience of awe and its subsequent effects on personal identity?
