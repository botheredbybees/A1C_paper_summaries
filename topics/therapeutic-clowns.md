## Therapeutic Clowns

Therapeutic clowns constitute an innovative intervention across various patient populations, primarily children and older adults. In pediatrics, therapeutic clowns are employed to reduce anxiety and manage pain (Key Concepts: clown-therapy, anxiety-reduction, pain-management). A systematic review underscores their efficacy in pediatric settings, with significant positive outcomes reported for these areas. Similarly, among older adult patients in nursing homes, creative engagement techniques used by clowns contribute positively to emotional support and cultural relevance, enhancing the quality of life (Key Concepts: creative engagement, cultural relevance, emotional support). In disabled children's care, therapeutic clowns have shown both physiological benefits through autonomic nervous system regulation and emotional enhancements, indicating their broad applicability in reducing stress responses in healthcare settings. However, despite these positive findings, there remains a need for more robust evidence to establish standardized practices and outcomes across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Therapeutic clowns effectively reduce anxiety and pain in pediatric patients.
- In older adults, therapeutic clowning enhances emotional support and cultural relevance.
- Physiological benefits are observed through autonomic nervous system regulation in disabled children.

## Open Questions

- What standardized practices should be established for therapeutic clowning across different healthcare settings?
