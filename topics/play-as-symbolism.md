## Play as Symbolism in Therapy

The utilization of puppets and other symbolic representations in psychodynamic child therapy exemplifies the concept of 'play as symbolism'. This approach hinges on the principle that through play, children can externalize their feelings and thoughts, creating a safer psychological environment where they are more willing to express deep-seated emotions or fears. The symbolic nature of play allows therapists to engage with the patient's psyche in non-verbal ways that are often more accessible than direct verbal confrontation. Across studies and therapeutic contexts, 'play as symbolism' emerges not just as a technique but as an integral part of the healing process for children, highlighting its value beyond the specific modality it is employed in.

## Key Findings

- 'Play as symbolism' facilitates psychological safety in therapy
- Externalization of feelings through play aids therapeutic progress
- Symbolic representations like puppets are effective tools for expression

## Open Questions

- How does the effectiveness of 'play as symbolism' vary across different cultural contexts?
