## Creative Arts Benefits for Disabilities

The integration of creative arts as an intervention strategy within disability support programs highlights its multifaceted benefits. Sources emphasize the social model of disability, focusing on societal barriers rather than individual impairments, and advocate for creative activities to enhance quality of life and personal development among individuals with disabilities. The approach underscores the importance of tailored artistic experiences that cater to diverse needs, fostering a sense of community engagement and inclusion. Notably, while there is broad consensus on the positive outcomes associated with arts-based interventions, tensions arise regarding the scalability and accessibility of these programs in various cultural contexts.

## Key Findings

- Creative arts interventions positively impact the quality of life for individuals with disabilities
- The social model of disability informs a more inclusive approach to creative arts programming
- Tailored artistic experiences cater to diverse needs, enhancing personal development

## Open Questions

- How can arts-based interventions be scaled and made accessible in different cultural contexts?
