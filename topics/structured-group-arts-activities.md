## Structured Group Arts Activities

The review of literature highlights the significant role that structured group arts activities play in developing resilience and enhancing mental well-being among children and young people. These interventions are noted to provide a supportive environment for personal growth, emotional expression, and social interaction. The research collectively indicates that engaging in such activities fosters a sense of community and mutual support, contributing to increased self-esteem and confidence. However, the review also underscores the need for more detailed exploration into how specific types of arts-based interventions can be tailored to address unique developmental needs across diverse populations.

## Key Findings

- Structured group arts activities are effective in enhancing mental well-being and resilience among children and young people.
- These activities provide a platform for emotional expression, social interaction, and personal development.
- There is a need for more detailed research on the tailoring of specific types of arts-based interventions to meet diverse developmental needs.

## Open Questions

- What are the optimal conditions (e.g., group size, duration) for structured group arts activities to maximize their benefits?
