## Collaborative Care Models

The exploration of collaborative care models in healthcare settings reveals a convergence of diverse approaches aimed at enhancing patient-centered care and treatment outcomes. Heritage-informed practices, such as the use of museum collections within hospitals, exemplify an innovative intervention that integrates cultural heritage with medical practice to foster healing environments and therapeutic connections. These models emphasize collaboration between various stakeholders including patients, healthcare providers, historians, curators, and community members, creating a comprehensive support network that addresses both physical and psychosocial needs. However, while the integration of such unique interventions offers potential benefits in terms of patient satisfaction and holistic treatment approaches, there remains a need for further empirical validation regarding their efficacy across different contexts.

## Key Findings

- Collaborative care models enhance patient-centered approaches by integrating various stakeholders
- Integration of cultural heritage elements like museum collections can create healing environments
- More research is needed to validate the efficacy of such interventions in diverse settings

## Open Questions

- What are the specific mechanisms through which collaborative care models and heritage-informed practices improve patient outcomes?
