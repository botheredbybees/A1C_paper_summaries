## Layer Masking

Layer masking is a versatile technique in digital image editing that allows for the selective modification of individual layers within an image without permanently altering them. This method enables editors to blend multiple images or elements seamlessly, providing flexibility and control over the final composition. The process involves creating a mask on one layer which hides parts of it while revealing underlying layers, effectively merging visuals based on specific selections made by tools like the Quick Selection Tool in Photoshop.

## Key Findings

- Layer masking allows for non-destructive editing techniques that enable selective visibility and blending of image elements.
- Selection tools such as the Quick Selection Tool are crucial for accurately defining areas to be masked, ensuring high-quality merges and composites.
- Photoshop offers a comprehensive set of features and layer-masking capabilities that facilitate complex image manipulations
