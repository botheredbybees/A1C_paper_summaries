## Self-Diminishing Effects of Awe

The concept of 'self-diminishing effects' in awe experiences suggests that encountering awe-inspiring stimuli can lead to a temporary diminishment of the self, often associated with feelings of insignificance or smallness. This effect is part of a broader theory on the nature and impact of awe, which includes cognitive accommodation, the process by which individuals adjust their mental frameworks to incorporate new experiences. Key sources discuss how awe-inducing events can challenge existing beliefs and lead to a reappraisal of self-concept, potentially fostering a sense of interconnectedness and belonging (universal membership) beyond individual identity. However, while there is agreement on the transformative potential of awe in reshaping personal perspectives, tensions exist around the mechanisms through which this occurs and the long-term impacts on individuals' psychological well-being.

## Key Findings

- Awe experiences can lead to a temporary diminishment of self-concept (self-diminishing effects).
- Cognitive accommodation is central in processing awe-inducing events, leading to potential shifts in personal beliefs and identity.
- Awe may foster feelings of universal membership, enhancing perceptions of interconnectedness beyond individual existence.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term psychological impacts of self-diminishing effects in awe experiences on individuals' well-being?
