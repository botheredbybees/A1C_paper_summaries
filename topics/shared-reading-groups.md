## Shared Reading Groups

Shared reading groups are a therapeutic intervention that integrates literature and narrative development to enhance personal growth and emotional recovery. Sources highlight the social connectivity fostered through these groups, which contribute significantly to participants' well-being. Expressive writing therapy is often incorporated into shared reading sessions, offering an additional avenue for self-expression and healing. Additionally, the application of theatre elements in creative writing workshops within shared reading contexts can further enrich participants' experiences by providing diverse forms of engagement and expression.

## Key Findings

- Shared reading groups foster emotional recovery through narrative development and expressive writing.
- Social connectivity is a key benefit derived from participation in shared reading groups.
- Incorporating theatre elements into creative writing workshops enhances the therapeutic value of shared reading groups.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of shared reading group interventions on participants' mental health and social integration?
