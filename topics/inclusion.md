## Inclusion in Disability-Led Theater

The sources highlight the critical role of disability-led theater as an intervention for fostering societal understanding and building confidence among individuals with disabilities. By taking control over their narrative, actors and artists can challenge stereotypes and promote a more inclusive society. This approach not only enhances self-esteem but also educates the broader community about the capabilities and experiences of people with disabilities.

## Key Findings

- Disability-led theater plays a vital role in promoting societal understanding of disability issues.
- Theater interventions centered on individuals with disabilities help build confidence within this population.
- Narrative control given to those with disabilities through arts-based practices can challenge and transform public perceptions.
