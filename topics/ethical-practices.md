## Ethical Practices in Art Therapy

The exploration of ethical considerations within art therapy and the practical application of these principles through community-based art projects reveal a complex interplay between theoretical frameworks and real-world implementation. Source one, 'Exploring Ethical Considerations in Art Therapy,' emphasizes critical ethical concerns such as informed consent and dual relationships, while also delving into philosophical underpinnings like utilitarianism and deontology to guide decision-making processes. In contrast, source two, 'Making Art with Communities - A Work Guide,' focuses on practical aspects of ethical practice by discussing the transformative power of art in communities, cultural sensitivity towards diverse populations, and strategies for managing risks inherent in communal art practices. Both sources underscore the importance of ethical considerations but diverge in their approach: one prioritizing theoretical foundations to inform ethical decision-making, while the other emphasizes culturally sensitive risk management strategies.

## Key Findings

- Ethical considerations in art therapy include informed consent and navigating dual relationships.
- Philosophies such as utilitarianism and deontology provide foundational guidance for ethical practice.
- Community-based art projects benefit from an understanding of cultural sensitivity and risk management.

## Open Questions

- How can the theoretical ethical frameworks be effectively applied in community-based art therapy settings?
