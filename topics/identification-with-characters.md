## Identification With Characters

The concept of 'identification with characters' emerges across various contexts as a means to enhance personal experience and well-being. In the realm of dementia care, it serves as an intervention to bolster dignity and quality of life by fostering emotional connections through engagement with narratives or performances. Sources suggest that such identification can create meaningful aesthetic experiences for individuals living with dementia, offering them a sense of continuity and value within social and cultural contexts. However, while this approach has shown promise in enhancing the lives of those affected by cognitive decline, there remains a need to explore its broader applications and effects on diverse populations beyond dementia-specific interventions.

## Key Findings

- Identification with characters can enhance dignity and quality of life for individuals living with dementia.
- Engagement through narratives or performances fosters meaningful aesthetic experiences for those with dementia.
- The concept holds potential beyond dementia care, though its broader applications require further exploration.

## Open Questions

- How might the intervention of identification with characters be effectively applied to diverse populations and conditions?
