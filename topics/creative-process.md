## Creative Process

The creative process is a complex and multifaceted theory that involves various cognitive states and challenges. Source 1 highlights Leonardo da Vinci's paradoxical genius, which includes difficulties in focus and completion despite his immense creativity. This source points to the inherent tension between broad curiosity and the need for sustained attention to produce tangible results. Conversely, Source 2 emphasizes the role of daydreaming and hypothetical scenarios as sources of creative ideas. Both sources underscore the importance of curiosity but highlight different pathways: one through disciplined effort in overcoming distractions and the other through relaxed mental states that allow for serendipitous connections.

## Key Findings

- Creative geniuses often face internal challenges such as difficulties with focus and completion, despite their broad range of interests.
- Daydreaming and engaging in hypothetical scenarios can be crucial components in the generation of creative ideas.

## Open Questions

- How do different cognitive states (focused vs. relaxed) contribute to varying aspects of creativity, from idea generation to implementation?
