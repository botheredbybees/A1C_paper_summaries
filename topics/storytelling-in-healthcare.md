## Storytelling in Healthcare

The integration of storytelling into healthcare practice through the concept of Narrative Medicine highlights its potential to transform patient-centered care. Source 1 emphasizes the importance of holistic listening and engaging with patients' narratives as a means for doctors to become healers beyond just treating symptoms. This perspective is corroborated by Source 2, which offers a systematic review that supports the use of personal narratives in healthcare, grounded in the PRISMA guidelines, indicating a scientific validation of storytelling's impact on patient care. However, while both sources underscore the benefits and applications of narrative medicine, they also hint at the need for further research to standardize practices and fully understand its long-term effects.

## Key Findings

- Storytelling in healthcare enhances patient-centered care by allowing doctors to engage more holistically with patients' narratives.
- Narrative Medicine's application is supported by systematic reviews, indicating growing scientific interest in its efficacy.
- Both sources highlight the importance of personal and holistic listening as a cornerstone for effective narrative practice.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term impacts of integrating storytelling into healthcare practices?
