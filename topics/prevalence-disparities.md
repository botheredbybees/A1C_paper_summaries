## Prevalence Disparities in Dementia

The examination of dementia's impact on diverse populations highlights significant disparities in its prevalence and how it is managed culturally. Sources emphasize that late-onset Alzheimer’s disease disproportionately affects certain groups, such as indigenous communities. Cultural care practices play a crucial role not only in the perception but also in the management of dementia within these communities, leading to variations in reported incidence and prevalence. However, there are tensions between traditional healing methods and Western medical approaches, indicating a need for more culturally sensitive research methodologies and interventions.

## Key Findings

- Late-onset Alzheimer’s disease disproportionately affects certain populations, particularly indigenous groups.
- Cultural care practices significantly influence the management and perception of dementia within communities.
- There are significant disparities in the reported prevalence of dementia across different cultural contexts.

## Open Questions

- How can research methodologies be adapted to better understand and address the unique needs of diverse populations affected by dementia?
