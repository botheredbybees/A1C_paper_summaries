## Cognitive Accommodation

The concept of cognitive accommodation plays a pivotal role in understanding how individuals adjust their mental frameworks to incorporate new information and experiences. Across the provided sources, this theory is used to explain phenomena such as awe elicitors, appraisals, and their effects on self-concept. It aligns closely with Piaget's original theoretical framework but extends its application into contemporary psychological contexts like those experiencing awe-inspiring moments. These moments can lead individuals to reassess their personal beliefs and identities through a process of cognitive accommodation, often resulting in what is termed 'self-diminishing effects,' where self-importance seems reduced relative to the vastness or complexity encountered. This theoretical model underscores the dynamic nature of human cognition and its continuous adaptation to novel stimuli, suggesting that it serves as a mechanism for personal growth and psychological transformation.

## Key Findings

- Cognitive accommodation is a key process in how individuals adapt their mental schemas to new experiences.
- Awe-inspiring moments often trigger self-diminishing effects due to cognitive accommodation.
- Universal membership, or the feeling of being part of something larger than oneself, can be an outcome of cognitive accommodation.

## Open Questions
