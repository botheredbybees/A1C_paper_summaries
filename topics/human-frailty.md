## Human Frailty in Palliative Care

The concept of 'human frailty' is deeply intertwined with the discourse on dementia and its management through palliative care. This notion challenges traditional illness metaphors by shifting focus from a cure-centric approach to one that embraces human vulnerability and recognizes the inevitability of decline in certain contexts, such as end-of-life scenarios or severe cognitive impairment like dementia. Across sources, there is an agreement that palliative care approaches can be more effective when they incorporate an understanding of human frailty, emphasizing comfort, quality of life, and dignity over aggressive treatment aimed at extending life. However, there remains a tension between the medical community's push for curative treatments and the recognition by some scholars that this paradigm fails to address the holistic needs of individuals facing severe cognitive decline or terminal conditions.

## Key Findings

- Human frailty is increasingly recognized as an important concept in palliative care discourse, particularly in managing conditions like dementia.
- Palliative care approaches are more effective when they move beyond traditional illness metaphors to embrace the inevitability of decline and focus on quality of life and dignity.
- There is a notable shift towards recognizing human frailty as a crucial aspect for improving end-of-life care.

## Open Questions

- How can healthcare systems better integrate palliative care principles that acknowledge human frailty, especially in treating conditions like dementia?
