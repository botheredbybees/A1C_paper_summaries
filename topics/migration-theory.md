## Migration Theory

The sources provided focus on the concept of migration theory through archaeological findings and anthropological studies. The use of carbon-dating techniques reveals early musical instruments that challenge established theories about human migration patterns, suggesting a more complex narrative involving cultural exchanges and interactions between different populations. This evidence supports the idea that migration is not solely driven by environmental factors but also includes socio-cultural influences.

## Key Findings

- Migration theory incorporates both environmental pressures and cultural exchange.
- Earliest musical instruments indicate earlier and more widespread human migrations than previously thought.

## Open Questions
