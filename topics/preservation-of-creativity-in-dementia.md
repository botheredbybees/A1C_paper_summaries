## Preservation of Creativity in Dementia

The concept of preserving creativity in individuals with dementia highlights the complex interplay between cognitive decline and creative expression. Source 1 underscores the unique case study of an artist diagnosed with frontotemporal dementia, revealing that despite significant cognitive impairment, their artistic abilities remain relatively intact. This preservation of creativity challenges traditional views on the progressive nature of neurodegenerative diseases like dementia, suggesting avenues for therapeutic interventions aimed at maintaining or enhancing creative capacities in affected individuals.

## Key Findings

- Creativity can persist even when other cognitive functions are severely compromised by dementia
- Artistic expression may serve as a form of communication and self-expression for those with dementia
- Preservation of creativity might offer therapeutic benefits to individuals living with dementia

## Open Questions

- How do specific types of artistic activities influence the preservation or decline of cognitive functions in dementia patients?
