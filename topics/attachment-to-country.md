## Attachment to Country

The concept of 'attachment to country' emerges as a critical factor in the mental health and social-emotional wellbeing of Indigenous populations. This attachment is deeply intertwined with spirituality, music, and arts, each acting as a catalyst for change and enhancement in cultural identity and communal support systems. Across various sources, there is an agreement on how these elements contribute to strengthening community bonds and fostering psychological resilience among Indigenous groups. However, tension exists regarding the varying levels of effectiveness and accessibility of these culturally-specific practices across different regions and communities.

## Key Findings

- Attachment to country enhances social-emotional wellbeing in indigenous populations
- Music, spirituality, and arts contribute to a sense of cultural identity and community support among Indigenous individuals
- Culturally specific practices are essential for mental health interventions targeting indigenous groups

## Open Questions

- How do regional differences affect the impact and accessibility of culturally-specific practices such as music and art in promoting attachment to country?
