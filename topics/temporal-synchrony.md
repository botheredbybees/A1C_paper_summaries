## Temporal Synchrony in Brain Function

The evaluation of differences in temporal synchrony between brain regions highlights the significance of this methodological approach in understanding neurodevelopmental conditions such as autism. Across multiple studies, researchers have found that assessing temporal synchrony can provide insights into how different brain networks communicate and function over time. In individuals with autism, deviations from typical patterns of temporal synchrony may correlate with variations in cognitive processing speed and symptom severity. However, there are methodological challenges in precisely measuring these dynamics, particularly when attempting to disentangle the effects of neurodevelopmental conditions from individual differences in neural network functioning.

## Key Findings

- Temporal synchrony is a valuable measure for understanding functional brain connectivity
- Individuals with autism exhibit differential patterns of temporal synchrony compared to those without
- Temporal synchrony can be linked to cognitive processing speed and symptom severity

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in neural network dynamics influence the measurement and interpretation of temporal synchrony?
