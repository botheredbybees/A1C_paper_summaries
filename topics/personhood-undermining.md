## Personhood Undermining in Dementia Care

The topic of personhood undermining highlights a critical issue within dementia care where practices and approaches may inadvertently devalue or negate the personal identity of individuals living with dementia. Source 1 discusses how traditional models of dementia care can be seen as 'personhood-undermining,' suggesting that conventional methodologies might fail to recognize and uphold the intrinsic worth and individuality of patients, instead focusing on their deficits. This critique points towards a need for more affirming and person-centered approaches like the Veder Contact Method, which seeks to engage with individuals based on their current capabilities while respecting their dignity. Tensions arise when balancing practical care needs against preserving personal identity and autonomy.

## Key Findings

- Traditional models of dementia care are often criticized as 'personhood-undermining', failing to recognize the intrinsic worth of those living with dementia.
- There is a growing need for person-centered approaches that affirm individual identity in dementia care.
- The Veder Contact Method offers an alternative approach, focusing on engaging patients based on their current capabilities.

## Open Questions

- How can care practices be reformed to balance the practical needs of caregiving with the preservation of personal dignity and autonomy for individuals with dementia?
