## Wabi-Sabi in Phototherapy

Anna Mackintosh's critical reflection on her photographic essay highlights the intersection of wabi-sabi philosophy with natural light photography and its therapeutic benefits. Wabi-sabi, an aesthetic concept originating from Japanese culture, emphasizes imperfection, impermanence, and incompleteness, fostering a deeper appreciation for the beauty in nature and everyday life. This philosophy is paralleled in Mackintosh's use of natural light to capture ordinary moments with authenticity and depth, showcasing her practice within health humanities and phototherapy. The therapeutic power of wabi-sabi in photography lies in its ability to promote mindfulness and enhance emotional well-being through the contemplation of subtle beauty.

## Key Findings

- Wabi-sabi enhances the appreciation for natural light and imperfection in photographic art
- Phototherapy benefits from incorporating aesthetic principles like wabi-sabi
- Health humanities can be enriched by integrating traditional Japanese philosophies

## Open Questions
