## Life Story Work

The concept of life story work emerges as a therapeutic intervention aimed at enhancing the quality of life for individuals with dementia through personalized storytelling and reminiscence. Source 1, 'Creativity and Communication in Persons with Dementia,' underscores the role of sensory-based writing and object-based reminiscence within this framework, suggesting that such activities are not only engaging but also deeply meaningful for patients. These practices help to maintain a sense of self by connecting individuals to their past through tangible memories and experiences. Source 2 discusses audience feedback on a performance named 'Inside Out of Mind,' which highlights the importance of sensitivity in care when implementing life story work methods, indicating that it is crucial for practitioners to approach these activities with empathy and respect for the unique history and present state of each individual.

## Key Findings

- Life story work enhances quality of life by connecting individuals with dementia to their past through personalized storytelling and reminiscence.
- Sensory-based writing and object-based reminiscence are effective methods within life story work that engage patients deeply on an emotional level.
- Sensitive implementation is essential in care settings when conducting life story work, ensuring respect for individual histories.

## Open Questions

- How can practitioners effectively integrate sensory-based writing and object-based reminiscence into everyday care routines without causing undue stress or discomfort to individuals with dementia?
