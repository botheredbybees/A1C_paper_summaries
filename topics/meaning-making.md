## Meaning Making in Dementia Care

The concept of 'meaning making' within the context of dementia care emphasizes the importance of creating meaningful experiences for individuals with dementia and their caregivers through engagement activities, such as museum programs. These initiatives aim to foster a sense of continuity and identity by evoking memories linked to personal histories. Museums offer unique environments where sensory stimuli and historical artifacts can trigger reminiscence, thereby enhancing emotional well-being. However, the effectiveness of these interventions varies based on individual differences in dementia progression and personal engagement with past experiences.

## Key Findings

- Meaning making through museum programs can positively impact the quality of life for individuals with dementia by evoking memories and fostering a sense of identity.
- Reminiscence therapy, often facilitated within museums, plays a crucial role in enhancing emotional well-being among those affected by dementia.
- Museum-based interventions require customization to address individual differences in dementia progression.

## Open Questions

- What are the long-term effects of museum-based meaning making activities on cognitive decline and overall quality of life for individuals with dementia?
