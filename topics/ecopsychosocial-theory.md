## Ecopsychosocial Theory

The sources collectively explore the role of creative expression within an ecopsychosocial framework, emphasizing its impact on resilience and well-being across different populations. Source 1 highlights the application of visual arts in supporting people with dementia in care homes, underscoring how such interventions align with broader theories that consider psychological, social, and ecological dimensions. This suggests a holistic approach where creative activities contribute not only to individual health but also to environmental and community resilience.

## Key Findings

- Creative expression through the visual arts enhances resilience among individuals living with dementia.
- Ecopsychosocial theory integrates psychological, social, and ecological aspects of well-being.
- Successful aging is facilitated by engaging in meaningful creative activities that promote mental and physical health.

## Open Questions

- How can ecopsychosocial approaches be effectively applied to diverse populations beyond those with dementia?
