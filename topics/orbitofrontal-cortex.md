## Orbitofrontal Cortex in Neuroplasticity and Cognitive Rehabilitation

The role of the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) in neuroplasticity and cognitive rehabilitation is highlighted across studies, specifically regarding its influence on behavioral changes after traumatic brain injury. Source 1 discusses how music-supported therapy induces neuroplastic effects in patients with TBI, particularly noting improvements in OFC function linked to enhanced cognition and behavior. Meanwhile, Source 2 delves into the broader functions of limbic structures, including the OFC and hippocampus, emphasizing their roles in emotional regulation and memory processing. Both sources underscore that while there is no single 'limbic system', these interconnected regions collaboratively support critical brain functions; however, Source 1 focuses more narrowly on how therapeutic interventions can enhance neuroplastic changes within the OFC, thereby impacting cognitive recovery.

## Key Findings

- Music-supported therapy induces neuroplastic changes in the orbitofrontal cortex of patients with traumatic brain injury.
- The orbitofrontal cortex plays a significant role in emotional regulation and memory processing alongside the hippocampus.
- Therapeutic interventions can improve cognitive and behavioral outcomes post-TBI through enhanced neuroplasticity in OFC.

## Open Questions

- How do specific therapeutic techniques beyond music-supported therapy influence neuroplastic changes in the orbitofrontal cortex for patients with TBI?
