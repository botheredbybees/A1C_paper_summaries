## Federated Identity Management

Federated Identity Management (FIM) is a methodology that allows users to log in and gain access to various services across different organizations using their existing credentials from another trusted service provider. The concept of FIM is primarily about enabling secure, seamless user authentication without the need for additional account creation or password management on each individual system. Across sources, there's agreement on its role in enhancing security by reducing the exposure of passwords and centralizing identity information management. However, sources also highlight challenges such as interoperability between different systems and ensuring data privacy and compliance with regulations like GDPR across jurisdictions.

## Key Findings

- Federated Identity Management enhances user experience through single sign-on capabilities.
- Interoperability is a critical challenge in implementing FIM across diverse organizational environments.
- Security benefits include reduced password exposure and centralized identity management.
