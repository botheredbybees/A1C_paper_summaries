## Artistic Wellbeing

The concept of artistic wellbeing has been explored in the context of its impact on individuals during and after the pandemic. The National Arts Participation Survey highlights how engagement in creative activities can significantly influence mental health positively, indicating that artistic practices serve as an intervention promoting psychological resilience and personal growth. Despite the significant disruptions caused by the pandemic, there is a noted increase in community participation in various forms of art, suggesting that people are turning to creative outlets as a form of coping mechanism and self-expression. This finding suggests a broader acceptance of arts not just for enjoyment but also as an essential tool for maintaining mental health and overall wellbeing.

## Key Findings

- Engagement in artistic activities can significantly enhance individual psychological resilience during challenging times such as the pandemic.
- There is growing evidence supporting the role of creative practices in promoting mental health and emotional stability, indicating a shift towards viewing art not only from an aesthetic perspective but also for its therapeutic value.
- The National Arts Participation Survey highlights increased participation in arts activities post-pandemic, reflecting broader societal recognition of the benefits of creative outlets.

## Open Questions

- How can we further integrate and promote artistic practices as a standard intervention within healthcare settings to address mental health issues?
