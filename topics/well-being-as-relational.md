## Well-Being As Relational

The concept of 'well-being as relational' emerges strongly from the integration of sources focused on self-care practices within digital contexts. Each source emphasizes a distinct yet interconnected aspect of this framework, highlighting how individual actions and behaviors contribute to broader social dynamics that influence personal well-being. For example, one study explores the use of photography as a daily routine to foster self-care, illustrating how such practices can be both personally enriching and socially engaging. These findings collectively underscore the importance of considering relational elements in theories about well-being and highlight how digital media practices facilitate new forms of social interaction that support individual health.

## Key Findings

- Relational aspects play a crucial role in theories of well-being, suggesting that individual practices are embedded within broader social contexts.
- Digital routines, such as photography practice, serve dual functions: promoting personal growth and fostering social connections.
- The integration of daily digital activities into self-care routines can enhance overall well-being by providing avenues for creative expression and community engagement.

## Open Questions

- How do varying cultural contexts influence the effectiveness of relational practices in enhancing individual well-being?
