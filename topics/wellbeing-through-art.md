## Wellbeing Through Art

The integration of art as a therapeutic intervention for mental health issues is explored in two sources. Source one focuses on the therapeutic benefits of photography specifically as a tool to aid individuals with depression, highlighting its utility in emotional expression and trauma processing. This aligns closely with broader interventions that use creative arts therapies. However, source two delves into the cultural and public value of art from an indigenous perspective within Australia and Aotearoa New Zealand, suggesting a different role for art beyond individual therapy; it encompasses community engagement and cultural preservation. While both sources acknowledge the transformative power of art on wellbeing, they differ in scale—ranging from individual to communal benefits.

## Key Findings

- Photography can serve as a therapeutic tool for emotional expression and trauma processing in individuals with depression.
- Art plays a significant role beyond personal therapy by fostering cultural preservation and community engagement.

## Open Questions

- How do the therapeutic benefits of art extend from individual to communal levels?
