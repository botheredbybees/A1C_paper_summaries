## Sensitive Period Hypothesis in Brain Development

The Sensitive Period Hypothesis posits that specific periods during development are crucial for acquiring certain skills or traits due to heightened brain plasticity. Adolescence, with its unique neurodevelopmental processes like synaptic pruning and myelination, is considered a sensitive period where environmental influences significantly shape the maturation of cognitive, emotional, and social functions. Across studies, there's consensus on adolescence being marked by both vulnerability and opportunity for positive change due to heightened neural plasticity. However, tensions arise regarding the precise timing and duration of these periods as well as how they vary across different brain regions and functionalities.

## Key Findings

- Adolescence is recognized as a sensitive period in brain development with unique neuroplasticity characteristics.
- Environmental influences play a critical role during this period, impacting long-term cognitive and emotional outcomes.

## Open Questions

- What are the specific neural mechanisms that underlie variability in developmental trajectories across individuals?
