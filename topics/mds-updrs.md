## Multisensory Dance Learning and Parkinson's Disease

The study examines the impact of a unique intervention called Multisensory Dance Learning (MDL) on the progression of motor symptoms in individuals with Parkinson's disease over a period of three years. Using the Movement Disorder Society - Unified Parkinson's Disease Rating Scale (MDS-UPDRS), which is an extensive tool for assessing various aspects of Parkinson’s disease, including motor function and daily living activities, the study found that MDL may slow down the progression of motor symptoms in people with Parkinson's disease. The findings suggest that integrating dance into a therapeutic regimen can be beneficial, possibly due to its multisensory engagement and complex movement patterns which challenge multiple neural pathways.

## Key Findings

- Multisensory Dance Learning slows down the progression of motor symptoms in individuals with Parkinson's disease over time.
- The Movement Disorder Society - Unified Parkinson's Disease Rating Scale (MDS-UPDRS) is a comprehensive tool used to assess various aspects of Parkinson’s disease, including motor function and daily living activities.

## Open Questions
