## Strengths-Based Approach

The strengths-based approach emphasizes the importance of recognizing and building upon an individual's or community's inherent capabilities rather than focusing on deficits. Source 1 highlights this through its focus on recovery-oriented language that prevents stigma and promotes positive mental health outcomes by empowering individuals to take charge of their recovery journey. This shift in language is crucial as it not only supports personal resilience but also contributes to broader systemic changes that foster a more supportive environment for recovery. In contrast, Source 2 focuses on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people, identifying cultural determinants of health such as family/community connections and self-determination which are critical elements within their communities. These aspects reflect a strengths-based perspective by valuing traditional cultural practices and community ties that enhance overall well-being and resilience against adverse circumstances.

## Key Findings

- Recovery-oriented language is essential in promoting mental health recovery by preventing stigma and fostering empowerment.
- Family/community connections and self-determination are vital for the health and resilience of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people.
- A strengths-based approach can be effectively applied across different cultural contexts to enhance overall well-being.

## Open Questions

- How can recovery-oriented language be universally adopted and adapted in diverse socio-cultural settings to ensure its efficacy?
