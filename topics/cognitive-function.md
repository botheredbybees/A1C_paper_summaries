## Cognitive Function Enhancement Through Lifestyle Factors

The integration of lifestyle factors such as diet, physical activity, and sleep in the regulation of autophagy-mediated pathways presents a promising avenue for cognitive function enhancement. Source 1 highlights how these lifestyle choices can positively influence neuronal health by modulating protein aggregation critical to Alzheimer's disease (AD) pathology. The IGNITE study from Source 2 provides empirical evidence that increased cardiorespiratory fitness and moderate-to-vigorous physical activity correlate with better cognitive performance, preserved brain morphology, and reduced neurodegeneration biomarkers among older adults at risk of AD. Notably, high levels of aerobic exercise are associated with lower neurofilament light chain (NfL) levels in individuals positive for amyloid-beta, suggesting a potential mechanism by which physical activity mitigates neurodegenerative changes early on. In contrast, Source 3 introduces the therapeutic power of music as an intervention to evoke memories and emotional responses in dementia care, underscoring the diverse range of non-pharmacological strategies available for cognitive function enhancement.

## Key Findings

- Diet, physical activity, and sleep influence autophagy-mediated pathways crucial for neuronal health.
- Higher cardiorespiratory fitness and MVPA are linked to better cognitive performance and reduced neurodegenerative biomarkers in older adults at risk of AD.
- Music therapy can evoke emotional responses and memories in individuals with dementia, enhancing quality of life and cognitive function.

## Open Questions

- How do lifestyle interventions like diet, exercise, and sleep interact to optimally support cognitive health in aging populations?
