## Roger Caillois' Four Forms of Play

The concept of Roger Caillois' four forms of play is a theoretical framework that delineates different types of playful activities. In the context of dementia care, as suggested by the source on Theatre and Play in Dementia Care, integrating these forms can enhance person-centered care strategies. The article underscores how understanding these categories—such as agon (competitive play), alea (gambling or games of chance), mimicry (mimetic behavior like acting or disguise), and ilinx (disturbance seeking)—can provide caregivers with a nuanced approach to engaging dementia patients in meaningful activities that cater to individual needs and preferences. This theoretical framework offers a structured yet adaptable model for creating therapeutic interventions designed to improve quality of life through play.

## Key Findings

- Roger Caillois' four forms of play offer a comprehensive theory for categorizing different types of playful behaviors.
- Integrating these forms into dementia care enhances person-centered approaches by tailoring activities to individual patient needs and preferences.
