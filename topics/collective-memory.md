## Collective Memory

The concept of collective memory is intricately linked with the idea of civic engagement and social identity within communities. The Shandon Big Wash Up murals in Cork provide a vivid example of how public art can serve as a medium for re-enchanting communal spaces, thereby enriching the sense of citizenship among its residents. This interplay between the visual representation of community history and the active participation of citizens in shaping their collective memory underscores the dynamic nature of this theoretical construct. The murals not only reflect past events but also engage current inhabitants in discussions about their heritage and shared future, reinforcing a collective identity that transcends individual narratives.

## Key Findings

- Public art can serve as a conduit for engaging citizens in the creation and perpetuation of collective memory.
- Community-engagement initiatives like mural projects can re-enchant urban spaces by fostering a sense of belonging and civic pride.
- The intersection of citizenship and collective memory is enhanced through tangible, visual representations of shared history.

## Open Questions

- How does the ephemeral nature of public art (e.g., murals) impact the long-term preservation and transmission of collective memory?
