## Research Rationale

The concept of research rationale is integral to structuring a comprehensive and purposeful investigation. It provides the foundation for understanding why a particular study should be conducted, addressing gaps in existing knowledge or proposing innovative methodologies. Sources emphasize the importance of ethical approval processes, highlighting that these protocols are not mere bureaucratic hurdles but critical steps in ensuring the integrity and relevance of research. The necessity for rigorous justification underpins both theoretical frameworks and practical applications in diverse fields such as arts in health.

## Key Findings

- Research rationale provides a clear foundation for why studies should be conducted.
- Ethical approval processes play a crucial role in validating the research protocol's legitimacy and integrity.
