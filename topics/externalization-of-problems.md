## Externalization Of Problems

The concept of externalizing problems is a therapeutic approach widely applied in both narrative therapy and other modalities aimed at enhancing personal resilience. In therapeutic photography, this technique involves clients using photographs to document and reflect upon their challenges, thereby moving the problem out of internal mental dialogue and into an observable format. Similarly, the use of puppets in expression activities facilitates externalization by allowing individuals to personify their issues, making it easier for them to review these problems from a distance and gain new perspectives on how to manage or overcome them. Both methods highlight the power of creating space between oneself and one's difficulties, which can significantly aid in emotional regulation and the re-evaluation of painful memories.

## Key Findings

- Externalizing problems helps individuals move beyond internal mental struggles by transforming issues into observable external objects (e.g., photographs or puppets).
- Creating distance from personal challenges through externalization techniques aids in developing new strategies for coping and addressing these difficulties.
- Photo elicitation and puppet use are effective means to achieve emotional regulation, particularly when dealing with traumatic experiences.
