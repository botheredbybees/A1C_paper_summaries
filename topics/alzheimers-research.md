## Creative Therapies for Alzheimer's Patients

Research indicates that creative therapies such as music and art can significantly enhance the quality of life for individuals suffering from Alzheimer's disease. Music therapy has been shown to boost brain activity, potentially reversing cognitive decline and enhancing patients' life satisfaction (Source 1). Similarly, the MoMA Alzheimer's Project illustrates how structured art programs like 'Meet Me at MoMA' provide inclusive environments that engage people with dementia in meaningful artistic experiences, promoting mental well-being and social interaction. Both interventions highlight the therapeutic benefits of creative activities for cognitive health and emotional support.

## Key Findings

- Music therapy can boost brain activity and improve life satisfaction among Alzheimer's patients.
- Structured art programs offer inclusive environments that enhance the quality of life for individuals with dementia.
- Creative therapies such as music and art contribute to cognitive and emotional well-being in Alzheimer's disease management.

## Open Questions

- How do the long-term impacts of creative interventions compare between various forms, such as music versus visual arts?
