## Creative Thinking Across Generations

The sources discussed the role of creative thinking interventions across different age groups. The Active Energy project highlighted how democratization of technology can facilitate intergenerational partnerships and social innovation among older people, emphasizing the potential for creative engagement to enrich lives regardless of age. In contrast, Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy focuses on enhancing creativity in children through puppetry, utilizing tools like the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking to measure effectiveness. Both interventions underscore the importance of fostering imaginative capacities early and throughout life, albeit with different emphases on technology use and conflict resolution strategies.

## Key Findings

- Creative thinking interventions can be adapted for various age groups, from children to older adults.
- Intergenerational partnerships in creative projects offer social innovation benefits.
- Puppetry and other imaginative activities enhance creativity and conflict resolution skills in young individuals.

## Open Questions

- How do the effectiveness measures of creative thinking interventions differ between children and older adults?
