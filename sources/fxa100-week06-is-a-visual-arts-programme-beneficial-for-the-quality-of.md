## Is a Visual Arts Programme Beneficial for the Quality of Life of People Living with Dementia?

This symposium addresses the growing interest in utilizing arts programs to enhance dementia care, focusing on a longitudinal mixed-method study involving N=126 individuals living with varying stages of dementia. The research explores the impact of visual art interventions on their quality of life and well-being. Key findings include improved communication and reconnection to communities through participatory visual arts activities, which are particularly beneficial for those with advanced dementia who face significant challenges in traditional qualitative data collection methods. Challenges discussed involve understanding unique benefits of creative approaches and developing methodologies that account for memory and communication difficulties. The research also highlights the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration between arts and science to address current evidence limitations around the effectiveness of visual art activities in dementia care.
