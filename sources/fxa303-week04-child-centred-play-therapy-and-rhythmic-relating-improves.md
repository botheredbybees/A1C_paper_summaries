## Child-Centred Play Therapy and Rhythmic Relating improves emotion regulation in autism: A single-N pilot intervention study

This study investigates the impact of a therapeutic combination of Child-Centred Play Therapy (CCPT) and Rhythmic Relating on emotion regulation in autistic children. The research focuses on a single case study of a 5-year-old boy over ten months, involving weekly sessions to assess improvements in emotion regulation through co-regulatory experiences. Key findings indicate statistically significant improvements in the participant's emotion regulation compared to a control group of non-interventional autistic boys without cognitive impairment or additional therapies. The study suggests that CCPT, emphasizing nonverbal communication and play, can be an effective intervention for improving emotional well-being and overall mental health outcomes in children with autism. This approach is particularly relevant as it addresses the unique challenges faced by autistic individuals in social timing and co-regulation.
