## Applied theatre, puppetry and emotional skills in healthcare: A cross-disciplinary pedagogical framework

This article explores the emotional competencies and skills required by artists, particularly puppeteers, working in healthcare settings such as hospitals. The study highlights the importance of self-care, empathy, emotional resilience, active listening, and self-reflection for professionals to effectively engage with emotionally vulnerable patients. It argues that traditional training methods focusing solely on artistic abilities are insufficient; instead, a cross-disciplinary pedagogical framework is necessary to equip artists with the skills needed to manage their own emotions while providing care in stressful healthcare environments. The research emphasizes the need for ongoing emotional support and resilience training to ensure that artists can sustain their roles as vital contributors to patient well-being.
