## Storytelling with Image and Text: A Photo Essay on Donna's Journey

Lauren Pothof's photo essay explores the therapeutic benefits of art for neurodivergent individuals through the story of Donna, an autistic artist. The project aims to support the thesis that engagement with arts enhances health and wellbeing (Sajnani & Nils Fietje, 2023). Through carefully curated text and images, Pothof documents how Donna uses art both as a coping mechanism for trauma and daily challenges associated with her multiple psychiatric diagnoses. The essay highlights the unique storytelling power of combining photography and narrative in conveying complex personal experiences (Douglis, 2003).

Pothof's choice to focus on Donna reflects her interest in understanding autism beyond diagnosis. This project provides a therapeutic benefit not only for Donna but also for Pothof herself by fostering self-awareness about assumptions around disability (Burn, 2019). The process of creating the photo essay allowed Donna to communicate more freely and feel proud of her artistic expression (Weiser, 2019).

The use of photography as an intermediary in interviews helped facilitate open communication between Pothof and Donna, who finds verbal exchanges challenging. By integrating Donna’s own words into captions, Pothof ensured the narrative resonated authentically with her subject's voice and experiences (Moran, 1974). The photo essay aims to empower Donna by bringing attention to her art within a contextualized framework of lived experience.

Pothof's flexible approach allowed for creative problem-solving when initial concepts did not align with the realities encountered. Ultimately, this flexibility led to a more powerful piece that captures Donna’s complexity and talent while acknowledging the role of art in coping mechanisms (Wu & Koutstaal, 2022). The project concludes by affirming the photo essay as an effective medium for advocacy in creative arts and health.
