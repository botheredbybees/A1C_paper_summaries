## Refugee Art Project

The Refugee Art Project is an initiative founded by a collective of academics and artists concerned with the welfare of refugees and asylum seekers in Australia, particularly those housed in detention centers. Since its inception in late 2010, the project has facilitated the creation and exhibition of over 500 artworks by these individuals. The core objective of this project is to support self-expression and creativity among refugees and asylum seekers, providing them with a platform to share their diverse stories and challenges through art workshops and public exhibitions. Through showcasing the artistic talents of those who are often marginalized or misunderstood in society, Refugee Art Project aims to educate the broader community about the realities faced by refugees and challenge existing misconceptions. The project also provides an essential activity for individuals within detention centers who may have limited access to creative resources and social interactions.
