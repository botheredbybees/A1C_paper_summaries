## Using Creative Arts in Trauma Therapy: The Neuroscience of Healing

This article examines the role of creative arts therapies in trauma treatment, emphasizing their impact on brain function and healing processes. It highlights that traumatic experiences can lead to perceptual biases affecting future assessments and responses. The authors advocate for an understanding of neuroscience principles in choosing therapeutic methods, suggesting that counselors should integrate these principles with theories like cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT). Creative arts activities provide a non-threatening approach for clients to process trauma through body movement and emotional expression, creating corrective experiences that diminish the traumatic impact. This work is particularly relevant for creative arts therapists working in mental health settings, as it underscores the importance of addressing trauma's neurological effects.
