## Moshing with Physics

In this TEDxYale lecture, Jesse Silverberg discusses the physics of mosh pits in heavy metal concerts. He explores how collective behavior and emergent phenomena observed in such gatherings can be modeled using principles from statistical mechanics and fluid dynamics. Through analyzing YouTube videos and developing numerical simulations, Silverberg demonstrates that the chaotic motion of people in a mosh pit mirrors the random movements of gas molecules. This research also reveals the formation of ordered vortex-like structures under certain conditions. The implications extend beyond entertainment to potential applications in crowd management during emergencies.
