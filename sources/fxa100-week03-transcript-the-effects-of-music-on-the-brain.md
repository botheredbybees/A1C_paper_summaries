## The Effects of Music on the Brain

Oliver Sacks, a neurologist and writer, explores how the brain reacts to music through an experiment at Columbia University. He undergoes a scan while listening to music by Bach and Beethoven. The results show that Bach activates more regions of his brain, including the amygdala, which is important for emotional processing, compared to Beethoven. Despite Sacks being unable to differentiate between the two composers in one segment, his brain activity still revealed a preference for Bach.
