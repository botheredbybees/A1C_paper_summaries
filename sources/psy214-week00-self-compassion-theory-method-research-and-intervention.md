## Self-Compassion: Theory, Method, Research, and Intervention

This article by Kristin D. Neff delves into the concept of self-compassion, which involves being supportive and kind towards oneself during times of personal suffering or pain, whether from personal mistakes or external challenges. The author outlines a theoretical model of self-compassion comprising six distinct elements: increased self-kindness, common humanity, mindfulness, reduced self-judgment, isolation, and overidentification. It reviews the research methodology employed in studying self-compassion and highlights empirical evidence indicating its positive effects on mental and physical well-being. The paper also addresses misconceptions about self-compassion, such as it being viewed as weak or selfish, by dispelling these myths through empirical evidence. Interventions like compassion-focused therapy and mindful self-compassion are discussed for enhancing self-compassion levels. Additionally, the review examines limitations in the field of self-compassion research and outlines future directions for study.
