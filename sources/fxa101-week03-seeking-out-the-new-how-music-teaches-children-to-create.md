## Seeking Out the New: How Music Teaches Children to Create and Innovate

The chapter 'Seeking Out the New' by Anita Collins explores how music education fosters creativity and innovation in children through pattern recognition, rule-breaking, and emotional engagement. Using examples like Where's Wally? books and pop songs, Collins illustrates that humans are drawn to patterns but also seek novelty. This duality is reflected in the structure of music, which often involves satisfying predictable sequences while occasionally introducing unexpected elements. The chapter discusses psychological traits like openness and conscientiousness as key predictors for musical proficiency and creative thinking. It argues that the initial stages of learning an instrument require strict adherence to rules, but this discipline can eventually lead to a balanced approach where creativity flourishes within structured boundaries.
