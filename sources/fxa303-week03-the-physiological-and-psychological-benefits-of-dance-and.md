## The Physiological and Psychological Benefits of Dance and its Effects on Children and Adolescents: A Systematic Review

This systematic review examines the physiological and psychological benefits of dance for children and adolescents, emphasizing its potential as an alternative therapy to traditional physical activities. The study synthesizes research from the past two decades, focusing on dance interventions that address various health issues in youth populations. Key findings highlight the feasibility of dance as a substitute for conventional exercise methods and the multifaceted health improvements it offers. These benefits are observed not only in healthy children but also in those with medical conditions such as autism spectrum disorders. The review underscores the importance of incorporating dance into physical education programs to enhance overall health, social connections, and cognitive development among young people.
