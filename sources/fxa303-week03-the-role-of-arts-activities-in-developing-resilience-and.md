## The role of arts activities in developing resilience and mental wellbeing in children and young people: a rapid review of the literature

This paper presents a rapid review of the literature examining how arts activities contribute to resilience and mental wellbeing in children aged 11 to 18 years. The study, conducted as part of an investigation into creative interventions for enhancing health outcomes among young people, synthesizes findings from various databases without date restrictions. Key objectives include assessing community-based arts activities' role in fostering protective factors associated with resilience and positive mental health outcomes.

The review highlights that participating in arts activities can enhance self-confidence, foster social connections, improve a sense of belonging, and build intrinsic factors crucial for resilience such as self-efficacy and empowerment. The authors discuss the growing need for non-traditional approaches to address rising mental health issues among young people, given limitations in current healthcare systems.

The findings indicate that arts-based interventions can offer cost-effective methods to promote social inclusion and positive psychological traits linked to better mental wellbeing. Given the increasing prevalence of mental health conditions during adolescence and early adulthood, these activities present a promising avenue for prevention and support outside formal clinical settings.
