## Photography as Self-Therapy: An Introduction for Beginners

The article 'Photography as Self-Therapy: An Introduction for Beginners' by Ellyn Kail introduces the concept of using photography as a therapeutic tool, tracing its roots back to the late 1970s. It highlights how Dr. Robert Irwin Wolf and Judy Weiser were among the pioneers who began integrating photo-based techniques into psychological therapy. The text underscores that 'PhotoTherapy' involves trained therapists using photos for therapeutic purposes, while 'Photo-Art-Therapy' is a specialized subset practiced by art therapists with additional training. Notably, the efficacy of photography in therapy lies not in creating technically proficient images but in capturing personal and emotional content. The article also discusses how the medium's accessibility, especially with smartphones, makes it an effective tool for individuals across different age groups to express their feelings and experiences.
