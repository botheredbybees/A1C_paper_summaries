## Welcome to Week 2: Using Your Camera

This week’s lecture welcomes students to the second week of the Photo Essay course, focusing on practical photography skills and camera usage. The document advises that any digital device capable of taking photographs is suitable for the tasks at hand. For beginners, it emphasizes patience in learning how to use their cameras effectively and encourages experimentation with different devices to understand their unique features. More experienced students are advised to delve deeper into manual settings such as aperture and shutter speed to enhance their photography skills.

The activities for the week center on exploring color and composition in photographs. Students are encouraged to think about cultural and personal associations with colors and to experiment with breaking traditional rules of color theory. For composition, various techniques are suggested, challenging students to apply at least two new strategies in their work.

The course also highlights the importance of engaging with peers through discussion boards where students can receive feedback from tutors and fellow classmates. Instructions for sharing images are provided along with tips on resizing pictures using basic software tools available on both Mac and Windows systems. Finally, the document reminds students to seek help via a dedicated questions board and encourages them to prioritize capturing quality shots in-camera rather than relying solely on post-processing.
