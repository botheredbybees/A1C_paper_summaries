## The Trauma-Informed Care and Practice Organisational Toolkit (TICPOT): An Organisational Change Resource

The Trauma-Informed Care and Practice Organisational Toolkit (TICPOT) is a comprehensive guide developed by the Mental Health Coordinating Council Inc. aimed at facilitating organisational change towards trauma-informed care practices. It provides detailed frameworks, tools, and resources to support mental health organizations in their journey toward becoming more responsive to the needs of individuals who have experienced trauma.

The document highlights that each organization's path to becoming trauma-informed will be unique, necessitating tailored approaches but with a universal goal: embedding trauma-informed principles into daily practices. The toolkit emphasizes creating a sustainable culture where these principles become second nature for all staff members within the organization.

Key aspects of TICPOT include identifying leaders who are willing to champion change and establishing an Implementation Working Group (IWG) comprising senior management, staff from various departments, consumers, and carers. This group plays a crucial role in prioritizing areas for quality improvement based on audit findings and setting clear goals and timelines.

The toolkit also underscores the importance of fostering governance, leadership, policy development, consumer participation, direct service delivery, workforce health, outcomes evaluation, and continuous monitoring to ensure effective implementation. It includes detailed considerations such as goal-setting, establishing guidelines, and implementing a quality improvement plan that incorporates feedback from all organizational levels, including consumers and key stakeholders.

In summary, TICPOT offers practical guidance for mental health organizations seeking to transform their practices to better serve individuals who have experienced trauma, promoting a holistic approach towards mental well-being through systemic change.
