## Prevalence, Incidence, and Risk Factors for Dementia

This chapter examines the prevalence and incidence of dementia across different age groups and regions, highlighting the doubling effect every five years after age 65. It discusses genetic risk factors such as ApoE e4 allele and lifestyle factors like diet and exercise that influence cognitive decline. The text emphasizes vascular conditions as significant contributors to cognitive impairment and dementia, underscoring the importance of modifiable risk factors for prevention.
