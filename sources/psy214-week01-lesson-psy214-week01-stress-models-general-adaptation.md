## Stress as a Response: The Biology of Stress

This lesson delves into the biological aspects of stress, focusing on the physiological stress response and its impact on various body systems. It covers key concepts such as the fight-or-flight response, General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS), and the roles of the nervous system, endocrine system, and immune system in stress management. The HPA axis and SAM system are highlighted for their crucial roles in triggering and managing the stress response. The lesson also explores how chronic stress affects brain structure and function, including changes to the amygdala, hippocampus, prefrontal cortex, and hypothalamus, leading to potential mental health issues.
