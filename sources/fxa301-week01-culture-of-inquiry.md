## Culture of Inquiry

The chapter explores the role of research in the field of community music and its cultural significance. It emphasizes that reflective practice is integral to the work of community musicians, aligning with philosopher Donald Schön's concept of 'thinking-in-action.' The text highlights various examples of graduate research projects that delve into diverse aspects of community music, such as its impact on veterans and secondary school students, gender roles in relation to teenage guitar players, and the integration of symphonic string players with indie rock bands. These studies underscore the profound impacts of music-making on personal development, social engagement, and cultural transformation. The chapter also discusses the importance of informed practice in community music education, exemplified through the work of Dave Camlin at Sage Gateshead, who emphasizes empathy, musicality, risk-taking, and dialogue as essential qualities for effective community musicians.
