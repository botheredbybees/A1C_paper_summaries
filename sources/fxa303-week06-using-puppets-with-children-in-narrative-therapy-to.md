## Using Puppets with Children in Narrative Therapy to Externalize the Problem

The article explores the use of puppets as a therapeutic tool within narrative therapy for helping children externalize their problems. The authors present narrative therapy principles and highlight its fundamental principle of externalizing the problem, which involves separating the individual from their issues through objectification techniques. They suggest that using puppets in this context can effectively externalize problems by creating distance between the client and their issues, thereby aiding in the deconstruction and redefinition of problematic narratives. The article includes a case example demonstrating how a puppet can be imbued with characteristics representing the problem, allowing the child to engage more actively in therapy sessions. This application underscores the potential for puppets to serve as an effective medium for enhancing client engagement and facilitating the narrative therapy process.
