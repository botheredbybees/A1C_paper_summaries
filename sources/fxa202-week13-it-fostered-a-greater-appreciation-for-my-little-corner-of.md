## It fostered a greater appreciation for my little corner of the world

The study examines the feasibility of a therapeutic photography intervention for enhancing well-being among autistic youth aged 16 to 25 years. The intervention involves participants taking photos and reflecting on them, aiming to promote both hedonic (emotional) and eudaimonic (psychological) aspects of well-being. Findings indicated that the intervention was feasible in terms of demand, implementation, acceptability, practicality, and preliminary effects. Participants reported various benefits and challenges, suggesting the potential for therapeutic photography as an accessible and effective approach to enhance positive mental health among autistic youth.
