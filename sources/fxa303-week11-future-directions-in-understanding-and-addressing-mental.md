## Future Directions in Understanding and Addressing Mental Health among LGBTQ Youth

Jessica N. Fish's article in the Journal of Clinical Child & Adolescent Psychology discusses the persistent mental health disparities among LGBTQ youth despite societal progress and changing attitudes towards LGBTQ rights. The author argues that these disparities persist due to ongoing stigma, discrimination, and minority stressors experienced by LGBTQ individuals. Despite improvements in legal protections and social acceptance over recent decades, mental health issues such as distress, symptomology, and substance use remain elevated compared to their heterosexual counterparts. Fish outlines six future directions for research and practice aimed at addressing these disparities: adopting a life course perspective, expanding the understanding of mental health beyond pathology, considering the complexity of youth's contexts, recognizing heterogeneity in LGBTQ experiences, leveraging community partnerships, and fostering interdisciplinary collaboration through team science approaches.
