## Longitudinal Analysis of Music Education on Executive Functions in Primary School Children

This study, a longitudinal analysis of music education on executive functions in primary school children, aimed to investigate the influence of structured music education programs on cognitive abilities. A total of 147 children were randomized into four groups: two music intervention groups, one visual arts group, and a no-arts control group. The research followed participants for 2.5 years and assessed executive functions such as planning, working memory, and inhibition, along with academic performance data.

Key findings indicate that the music intervention groups showed significant improvement in inhibition, planning, and verbal intelligence compared to both the visual arts and no-arts control groups over time. Notably, children involved in the visual arts program demonstrated better visuospatial memory skills. The study supports a far transfer effect from music education to academic performance mediated by executive sub-functions.

This research contributes to understanding the cognitive benefits of structured music education on executive functions and may have implications for educational policies that aim to enhance learning outcomes through targeted interventions.
