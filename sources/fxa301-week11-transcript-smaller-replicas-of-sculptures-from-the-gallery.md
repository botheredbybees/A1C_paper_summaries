## Tactile Gallery at Louvre Museum

This transcript discusses the tactile gallery within the Louvre Museum's sculpture department, which was established in 1995 to allow blind and visually impaired visitors to explore artworks through touch. It highlights how all audiences can benefit from touching sculptures for a deeper understanding of their materials and design. The space is described as an immersive experience that reduces the symbolic distance between museum-goers and art objects, engaging multiple senses. Preschool and early elementary school children particularly benefit from tactile exploration, enhancing memorization and spatial understanding.
