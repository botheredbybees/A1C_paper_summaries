## Research Related Projects and Problem Tree Development

This chapter discusses strategies for developing arts-based interventions in healthcare settings. It outlines methods for collecting open-ended responses from stakeholders through consultation forms and emphasizes the importance of targeted questions to gather useful data. The text highlights the need for diverse participation across different demographics to ensure comprehensive results. Additionally, it suggests examining existing research studies and current projects within organizations to inform intervention design. A problem tree analysis is proposed to identify key issues and their interconnections, aiding in prioritizing which problems to address. Step 4 of the process involves generating ideas through design thinking, open innovation, and systems thinking to create effective interventions that align with utopian goals. The chapter underscores the importance of considering long-term implications and sustainability when designing these interventions.
