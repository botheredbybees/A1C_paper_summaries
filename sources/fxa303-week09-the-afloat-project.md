## The Afloat Project

The 'Afloat' project, initiated by the City of Hobart following the destructive May 2018 flood in South Hobart, aimed to enhance community resilience through creative arts and play. The project engaged professional artists to work with children, families, and educators using various creative activities such as drawing, storytelling, model-making, music, and movement to help children process their experiences of change and adversity caused by the flood. This initiative was grounded in a growing body of evidence suggesting that creative recovery can improve community well-being, cohesion, resilience, and physical and mental health. Key artists involved included Rosie McKeand and Leigh Tesch, who employed pedagogies like visual literacy to facilitate meaningful engagement with environmental change. The project culminated in a community event celebrating South Hobart's resilience, where residents participated in activities that fostered reflection on the flood experience, community support, and future preparedness.
