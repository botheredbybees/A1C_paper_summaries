## The Emotional Response to Social Dancing and Walks in Persons With Dementia

This study investigates the emotional responses of individuals with dementia during social dance events and walks, using Husserl's philosophy as a basis for analysis. Six participants were observed to understand their engagement, caregivers' interactions, mutual tenderness, and environmental influences on their emotional states. The results suggest that these activities can evoke positive emotions such as joy and amusement, while also providing opportunities for communication and emotional connection. These findings highlight the importance of caregiver sensitivity and tailored interventions in dementia care.
