## Mental Health of Adolescents

The World Health Organization's fact sheet on adolescent mental health highlights that one in seven individuals aged 10–19 years experiences a mental disorder, contributing to 15% of the global disease burden for this age group. Depression, anxiety, and behavioral disorders are leading causes of illness and disability among adolescents, with suicide being the third leading cause of death for those aged 15-29 years. Adolescents face numerous challenges including exposure to adversity, peer pressure, identity exploration, media influence, and socioeconomic problems, which can exacerbate mental health issues. The document emphasizes the importance of protective environments at home, school, and in communities, as well as early detection and treatment of mental health conditions. It also underscores the need for comprehensive interventions targeting risk-taking behaviors such as substance use and unsafe sexual practices. Additionally, the fact sheet discusses World Health Organization initiatives aimed at promoting adolescent mental health through policy strengthening, program development, and training educators to better understand and support adolescent mental health needs.
