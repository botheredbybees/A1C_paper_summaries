## Feasibility and Preliminary Effects of Ballet-Based Group Dance Intervention in Relapsing–Remitting Multiple Sclerosis: A Pilot Study

This pilot study explores the feasibility and preliminary effects of a ballet-based group dance intervention on motor function, cognition, fatigue, and quality of life in individuals with Relapsing–Remitting Multiple Sclerosis (RRMS). The intervention comprised two weekly sessions over ten weeks, focusing on balance, gait, upper limb functions, cognitive measures like information processing speed, attention/executive function, memory, and psychosocial well-being. Results indicated significant improvements in motor tasks such as the Mini-BESTest (+17.5%), 6-minute walk test (+7.3%), and box and block test dominant hand (+6.9%). Cognitive tests also showed notable enhancements including Rey Auditory Verbal Learning Test Immediate Recall (+5.9%) and Symbol Digit Modalities Test (+47.4%). Despite the small sample size, these findings suggest potential benefits of ballet-based dance interventions for RRMS patients. However, the study's limitations, such as a lack of control group, restrict broader generalization. Future research with larger cohorts and randomized controlled designs is recommended to validate these preliminary results.
