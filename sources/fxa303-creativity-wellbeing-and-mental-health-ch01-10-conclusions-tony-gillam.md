## Conclusions

In the concluding chapter of 'Creativity, Wellbeing and Mental Health Practice', Tony Gillam reflects on how contemporary mental health practice has become mechanical, joyless, and uninspiring. This loss of creativity correlates with a reduced sense of wellbeing among practitioners. Gillam revisits key themes from earlier chapters such as the model of creative mental health care and eight wellbeing recommendations aimed at enhancing practitioner well-being and promoting greater creativity in patient care. He emphasizes the importance of fostering a supportive organizational climate, engaging in continuous learning and supervision, and advocating for holistic approaches to mental health care that include physical, social, philosophical, family, and supervisory dimensions.
