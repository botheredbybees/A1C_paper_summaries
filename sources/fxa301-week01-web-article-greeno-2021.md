## Tasmanian Aboriginal Shell Necklaces: A Significant Cultural Practice

This article discusses the cultural significance of Tasmanian Aboriginal shell necklaces, a tradition that dates back over 2600 years and is still practiced today. The necklaces are made from maireener shells (Phasianotrochus irisodontes), native to Tasmanian waters. Since colonial times, these necklaces have been collected by museums worldwide, with notable collections in the Oxford and British Museums. Shell necklace-making has provided economic support for Aboriginal women and serves as a medium to pass down cultural knowledge across generations. However, recent environmental challenges such as global warming, storms, and changes in kelp growth are affecting shell populations, impacting this traditional practice.
