## The Contribution of the Visual in Qualitative Participatory Art-Based Research Methods within the Mental Health Field

Maria Fjellfeldt's study aims to advance understanding of the visual element in arts-based participatory research methods within mental health contexts, particularly focusing on photovoice and collage-making techniques. The paper employs a secondary data analysis from an earlier qualitative study exploring sustainable living environments for individuals with mental illness. Key findings highlight how visual methodologies facilitate new perspectives by enabling participants to document their lived experiences visually, engage in reflective discussions about these images, and share insights gained. Fjellfeldt argues that the visual component of participatory arts-based research allows for deeper exploration and understanding of subjective realities and fosters dialogue around well-being and mental health issues. This approach is particularly relevant to creative arts therapies and contributes to the broader discourse on inclusive and empowering research practices in the field of arts and health.
