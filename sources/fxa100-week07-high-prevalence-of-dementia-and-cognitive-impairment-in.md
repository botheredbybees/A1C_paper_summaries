## High Prevalence of Dementia and Cognitive Impairment in Indigenous Australians

This study investigates the prevalence of dementia and cognitive impairment not dementia (CIND) among Indigenous Australians aged over 45 years in the Kimberley region of Western Australia. Using the Kimberley Indigenous Cognitive Assessment (KICA), researchers found a substantially higher rate of dementia compared to non-Indigenous populations, with a 12.4% prevalence overall. The study highlights significant disparities in health outcomes between Indigenous and non-Indigenous Australians, emphasizing the need for tailored healthcare approaches to address these issues. Environmental factors such as limited education and poor cardiovascular health were identified as contributing factors to the elevated dementia risk among Indigenous communities.
