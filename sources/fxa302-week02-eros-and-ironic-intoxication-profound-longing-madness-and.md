## Eros and Ironic Intoxication: Profound Longing, Madness, and Discipleship in Plato’s Symposium and in Modern Life

Kieran Bonner's article 'Eros and Ironic Intoxication' explores the relationship between desire, beauty, and the good life in Plato’s *Symposium*. The paper argues that for Plato, power, desire, and ethics are interrelated, unlike modern perspectives which often separate these concepts. Bonner focuses on Socrates as a paradigmatic case of this interrelation, particularly examining how his strong teaching inspires profound longing and sometimes misguided devotion among his followers. This analysis highlights the tension between fulfilling desires through satisfaction versus seeking a deeper understanding that transcends superficial pleasures. The article emphasizes the importance of irony and critical reflection in engaging with eros (desire) to avoid the pitfalls of blind adoration or competitive emulation, which can lead to ethical and existential crises. Bonner’s work is relevant for creative arts and health studies by illustrating how deeply rooted cultural narratives about desire and fulfillment shape personal and societal values, influencing well-being and educational practices.
