## Brain development during childhood and adolescence: a longitudinal MRI study

This article by Jay N. Giedd et al., published in Nature Neuroscience, presents findings from a longitudinal Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) study examining brain development during childhood and adolescence. The study involved healthy boys and girls aged between 4.2 and 21.6 years, with multiple scans conducted at approximately two-year intervals for some participants. Key findings include nonlinear changes in cortical gray matter, characterized by an increase before adolescence followed by a decrease after adolescence. Frontal and parietal lobes exhibited peak sizes around age 12, while the temporal lobe peaked later, around age 16. Occipital lobe gray matter continued to grow linearly through age 20 without significant decline. These results support the notion of heterochronic development in human cerebral cortex compared to synchronous development seen in non-human primates.
