## Psychiatry in scripture: sacred texts and psychopathology

The article examines the series 'Psychiatry in the Old Testament' and discusses its attempt to diagnose mental disorders based on biblical texts. While it aims to engage psychiatry with religious literature, the series often interprets spiritual experiences as symptoms of psychopathology without considering cultural context or faith-based interpretations. The author critiques this approach for oversimplifying ancient narratives and disregarding the theological significance of these sacred texts. The article highlights the importance of a more nuanced and constructive dialogue between psychiatry and religion to better understand how religious beliefs can influence mental health.
