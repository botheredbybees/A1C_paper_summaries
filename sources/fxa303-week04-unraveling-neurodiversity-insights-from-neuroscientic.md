## Unraveling Neurodiversity: Insights from Neuroscientiﬁc Perspectives

The article 'Unraveling Neurodiversity: Insights from Neuroscientiﬁc Perspectives' by Hagar Goldberg explores the concept of neurodiversity, a term coined to address and normalize human neurocognitive heterogeneity. The paper delves into how neurological differences are not merely pathologies but natural variations in cognition and communication styles that should be accepted rather than treated as deficits. Goldberg discusses two contrasting perspectives within the neurodiversity movement: normalization (social model) versus acknowledgment of disability or illness (medical model). She also examines the biological basis of neurodiversity, noting how brain characteristics can vary across individuals due to anatomical and functional traits. The article emphasizes a statistical approach to understanding neurodiversity, recognizing that it exists along a spectrum rather than being categorized as binary. This perspective helps in fostering an inclusive society where neurological differences are viewed positively and not stigmatized.
