## The means to flourish: arts in community health and education

The chapter discusses the emergence and impact of community-based arts projects aimed at promoting health and social inclusion. It traces the origins of these initiatives in the UK during the late 1980s, highlighting their growth into a global phenomenon. The author emphasizes the role of participatory arts in addressing mental health issues across different life stages and aging populations. Projects like Bealtaine in Ireland exemplify this trend internationally. Community outreach by cultural institutions also supports these goals. Notable events such as the 2002 Commonwealth Games Closing Ceremony and the 2012 London Olympics Opening Ceremony showcase large-scale expressions of arts in health. The chapter underscores the importance of relationship-based working and creativity for public health, referencing educational initiatives like Every Child Matters and Marmot Review's recommendations. The 'Roots and Wings' project in a socio-economically deprived area is presented as a case study demonstrating how participatory arts can foster social and emotional development among children.
