## Musical Preferences are Linked to Cognitive Styles

The research article explores the relationship between cognitive styles, specifically empathizing and systemizing, and musical preferences. The study consisted of two parts: in Study 1, participants indicated their preference for music from various genres while reporting empathy levels; results showed a significant link between empathy and musical preferences even within specific genres. In Study 2, participants were categorized based on cognitive styles (empathizing vs. systemizing), with type E individuals preferring mellow music characterized by low arousal and negative valence, whereas type S individuals preferred intense music marked by high arousal and positive valence. The study concludes that these findings can be applied in clinical settings to understand musical preferences better among those on the autism spectrum.
