## The Social Emotional Context of Dementia Care

This transcript discusses the importance of the social and emotional environment in dementia care. It emphasizes that individuals with diminishing cognitive abilities are highly attuned to others' emotions and feel emotionally unsafe when judged or ignored. Caregivers should strive to create loving, caring environments through non-verbal communication, such as posture, use of space, and movement rhythms. The text highlights the significance of recognizing non-verbal cues from people with dementia and responding appropriately to foster a sense of safety and connection. It also promotes dance movement therapy as a way to tap into unused resources and build healing relationships.
