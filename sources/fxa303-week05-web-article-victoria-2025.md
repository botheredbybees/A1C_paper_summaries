## A History of Punch & Judy

This article traces the history of Punch and Judy, a traditional British puppet show featuring Mr. Punch and his wife Judy. The origins can be traced back to Italy in the 16th century with Pulcinella, who arrived in England by 1662. Early performances were by marionette artists like Signor Bologna. By the 18th century, Punch became a celebrity character integrated into British puppetry and theatre culture. During the 19th century, glove puppets replaced marionettes, and shows moved to portable booths on streets and seaside resorts. In the early 20th century, the popularity of Punch decreased due to competition from cinema and television but remained a popular attraction at beaches. The article highlights cultural shifts in entertainment preferences while discussing the significance of Mr. Punch as an enduring British figure.
