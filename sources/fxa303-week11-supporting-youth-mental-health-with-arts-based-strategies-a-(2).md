## Supporting youth mental health with arts-based strategies: a global perspective

The article discusses the global challenge of youth mental health, emphasizing the need for accessible and culturally relevant solutions to address this crisis. The authors highlight the potential of arts-based strategies as a viable approach due to their cultural adaptability and cost-effectiveness across diverse communities. They note that while mental health issues among youths are increasing globally, there is a significant shortage in resources and trained professionals, particularly in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs). Engaging youth in local arts practices can promote positive outcomes such as improved self-expression, cultural identity, sense of community, and resilience against discrimination. Furthermore, the article underscores that engaging in creative tasks stimulates brain regions associated with well-being and stress reduction, thereby enhancing mental health support. The authors propose four critical steps for future action: elevating youth voices, developing core outcome measures, identifying successful global models, and securing funding pathways for research and community practice.
