## Humor Assessment and Interventions in Palliative Care: A Systematic Review

This systematic review evaluates the effectiveness of humor interventions and assessments in palliative care for patients with life-limiting illnesses. The study synthesizes existing research, finding positive correlations between humor and improved quality of life, reduced physiological symptoms, enhanced personal connections, and overall wellbeing for patients, their relatives, and healthcare professionals. Despite these benefits, limited empirical evidence exists due to scarcity of rigorous evaluations of interventions. Future research is recommended to establish a solid empirical foundation for integrating humor into regular palliative care activities.
