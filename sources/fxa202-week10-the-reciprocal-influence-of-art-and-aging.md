## The Reciprocal Influence of Art and Aging

This chapter explores the creative potential of older adults, particularly focusing on Rose, Adeena, and Marcia, who demonstrate how aging influences art and vice versa. The text supports theories suggesting that creativity expands with age, as older artists feel more creative and integrate life experiences into their work. It also discusses developmental perspectives such as continuity and adaptation, which argue that people use adaptive strategies throughout adulthood to enhance strengths while minimizing weaknesses. The chapter challenges traditional Eriksonian models of aging by presenting these women as vibrant participants in social and community activities rather than merely reflecting on past lives or preparing for death. Art-making is seen as fostering gerotranscendent behavior, defined as moving towards a cosmic and transcendent view of life alongside the process of aging.
