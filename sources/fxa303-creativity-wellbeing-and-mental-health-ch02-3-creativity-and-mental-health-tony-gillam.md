## Creativity and Mental Health

This chapter examines the historical and contemporary relationship between creativity, mental health, and artistic temperament. It challenges persistent myths about the link between madness and creativity, highlighting that people are more likely to be creative when mentally well-balanced. The text discusses the benefits of arts participation for mental health, including social inclusion and protective factors against stress. Research by scholars such as Rothenberg and Jamison is critically analyzed, suggesting that while there may be a slight correlation in some fields like writing or fine art, overall creativity thrives with positive affect and good mental health.
