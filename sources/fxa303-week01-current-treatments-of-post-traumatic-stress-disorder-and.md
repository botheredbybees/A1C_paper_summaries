## Current Treatments of Post-traumatic Stress Disorder and Amygdala Ablation as a Potential Cutting-Edge Therapy in Its Refractory Cases

This article reviews current treatments for Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) and discusses the potential of amygdala ablation as a novel therapy for refractory cases. PTSD, which can result from traumatic events such as combat or abuse, is characterized by symptoms including intrusion, avoidance, cognitive changes, emotional alterations, and arousal/reactivity changes that persist for over a month and significantly disrupt daily life. Psychological treatments include trauma-focused therapies like Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Prolonged Exposure therapy, and Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), as well as non-trauma-focused approaches such as relaxation techniques and mindfulness. Pharmacological interventions have also been attempted with mixed success. The review highlights the link between PTSD and right-side amygdala activation and suggests that selective laser ablation of the amygdalohippocampal unit may offer a promising surgical intervention for cases unresponsive to other treatments, marking a potential breakthrough in neurosurgical approaches to treating severe PTSD.
