## Poverty, Stress, and Brain Development: New Directions for Prevention and Intervention

This article by Clancy Blair and C. Cybele Raver explores the significant impact of poverty on children's neuroendocrine function, brain development, and cognitive abilities. The authors review evidence indicating that chronic stress related to poverty can adversely affect brain regions crucial for executive functions, such as the frontal cortex and hippocampus. They argue that addressing these negative consequences early in life is critical due to the malleability of developmental processes during childhood. Early interventions are highlighted as essential tools for mitigating the long-term effects of poverty on health and academic achievement. The paper underscores the importance of integrating insights from developmental science, pediatrics, child psychology, and public policy to develop effective strategies that support children's brain development and overall well-being in the context of economic adversity.
