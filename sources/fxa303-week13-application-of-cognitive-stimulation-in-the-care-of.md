## Application of cognitive stimulation in the care of institutionalized elderly people with dementia

This study, a literature review focusing on nursing care for institutionalized elderly people with dementia, identified the importance of cognitive stimulation and physical activities in improving their quality of life. The research included articles from databases such as LILACS, CINAHL, MEDLINE, and SCOPUS between 2009 and 2018, employing a PICo strategy for evidence identification. Seven studies were selected, predominantly published in 2010 and 2016, highlighting the positive impact of cognitive and physical interventions on dementia care. These activities enhance brain functions still preserved in patients with dementia, thereby improving their quality of life. The study concludes that such interventions can be applied by multi-professional teams and may complement pharmacological treatments when necessary.
