## FXA303 Week 8: Clowns and Clowning in Healthcare and Trauma Relief

This week of FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing focuses on the role of clowns and clowning in healthcare and trauma relief. The document traces the historical significance of court jesters who used humor to challenge authority figures while maintaining their protected status. It then moves to modern perceptions of clowns shaped by pantomime and circus traditions, highlighting how these performers use physical comedy and humor to bring joy and healing to children and adults in various settings, including hospitals. The materials explore the development of Western clowning traditions and discuss how social clowning techniques such as mime, humor, tricks, and silliness address serious social issues and transcend barriers of language and culture. Research is cited showing that hospital clowns provide both physical and emotional benefits to those who are ill or anxious. This week also includes Assessment Task 2 details, requiring students to submit photos and a video clip of their puppet alongside written work.
