## “Play” and People Living With Dementia: A Humanities-Based Inquiry of TimeSlips and the Alzheimer’s Poetry Project

This paper presents a humanities-based inquiry into the concept of 'play' in dementia care, drawing on Johan Huizinga's framework of homo ludens ('man the player'). The authors explore how play can be understood and valued in the context of two participatory arts programs: TimeSlips and the Alzheimer’s Poetry Project. These activities were conducted over 10 weeks at residential care facilities for people with dementia, focusing on the potential of play to foster expression, meaning-making, and relationship-building. The study reveals that participants engaged in playful behaviors, found joy in group activities, and demonstrated renewed capacity for imaginative engagement despite cognitive challenges. By emphasizing play rather than solely deficits or limitations, this research underscores a humanistic approach to dementia care, advocating for further exploration of the ludic experience as an integral aspect of personhood.
