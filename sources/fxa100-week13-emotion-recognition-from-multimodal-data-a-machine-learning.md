## Emotion Recognition from Multimodal Data: A Machine Learning Approach Combining Classical and Hybrid Deep Architectures

The paper explores the application of machine learning algorithms to recognize emotions from multimodal data sources for therapeutic purposes, particularly in elderly individuals with dementia. The authors propose a method that integrates artificial intelligence techniques such as random forest and hybrid deep architectures to analyze physiological signals, speech recordings, and facial expressions for emotion recognition. In their experimental evaluations, they achieve high accuracy rates—up to 99% for physiological signals and nearly 80% for speech signals—with the use of Random Forest and more than 82% with a hybrid architecture when analyzing images of facial expressions. The study suggests that these techniques could be instrumental in personalizing therapy interventions by providing emotional feedback, thereby enhancing therapeutic outcomes. This research is particularly relevant to creative arts therapies such as music or art therapy where understanding and modulating patient emotions can significantly improve engagement and treatment efficacy.
