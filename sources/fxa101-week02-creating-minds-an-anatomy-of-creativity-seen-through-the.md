## Creating Minds: An Anatomy of Creativity Seen Through the Lives of Freud, Einstein, Picasso, Stravinsky, Eliot, Graham and Gandhi

Jonathan W. Schooler and Stephen M. Fiore provide a book review of Howard Gardner's 'Creating Minds,' which explores the lives of seven influential individuals from the 20th century: Freud, Einstein, Picasso, Stravinsky, Eliot, Graham, and Gandhi. The reviewers highlight Gardner’s discussion on the importance of child-like thinking in creativity, emphasizing that this perspective facilitates innovation but can also be a hindrance if not balanced with experience. Additionally, they discuss the concept of personal asynchronies or imbalances, suggesting that these contribute to creative individuals' unique abilities and potential flaws. Gardner posits that these elements may help explain why truly innovative figures often have unusual personal traits, such as Freud's vindictiveness or Picasso’s sadistic behavior, which can be both a by-product and an enabler of their creativity.
