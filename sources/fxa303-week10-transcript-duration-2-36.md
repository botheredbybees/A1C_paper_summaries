## Transcript: Connecting Aboriginal Culture, Identity, and Well-being

The transcript discusses a youth program aimed at Aboriginal individuals aged thirteen to twenty-eight from the Blue Mountains area. The initiative focuses on reducing drug and alcohol use by fostering connections with cultural heritage, self-worth, and community support. Through storytelling and teachings about land stewardship, participants are encouraged to address mental health issues including anxiety, depression, and suicide prevention. Success stories highlight personal transformations, such as a young person avoiding juvenile justice due to the program's influence. The program emphasizes values of belonging, respect, humility, and patience, creating a safe space for self-expression and healing.
