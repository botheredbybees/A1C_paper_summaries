## Acquiring Tastes and Loves: What Neuroplasticity Teaches Us About Sexual Attraction and Love

The chapter examines how human sexuality, particularly preferences and attractions, can be highly plastic and influenced by psychological factors rather than being solely biologically driven. The case study of A., a young man who develops an erotic taste for emotionally unstable women despite his moral objections, illustrates the complex interplay between past experiences and current desires. Neuroplasticity is highlighted as playing a significant role in shaping sexual preferences through rewiring of neural pathways influenced by environmental stimuli and personal history. This plasticity extends to changes over time within relationships and across different types of partners, suggesting that sexuality is not fixed but malleable. The chapter also discusses how love can be flexible and adaptable, evolving with societal norms and individual experiences. These insights underscore the brain's capacity for change in response to diverse sexual scripts and emotional contexts, contributing to a nuanced understanding of human relationships within the creative arts and health domains.
