## Enhancing aesthetic appreciation by priming canvases with actions that match the artist’s painting style

This study explores the role of motor activity in aesthetic appreciation, particularly focusing on how priming with specific hand actions influences preference for pointillist-style paintings. The authors present participants with high-quality reproductions of such artworks preceded by images that prime compatible or incompatible hand movements. Results indicate enhanced aesthetic liking when priming is congruent with the painting style, suggesting that covert and involuntary simulation of the artist's gestures contributes to the appreciation of art. This research underscores the importance of motor areas in shaping aesthetic experience and challenges traditional views on artistic preference being solely driven by perceptual and cognitive processes.
