## Biopsychology as a Neuroscience

The text introduces the field of biopsychology, which focuses on understanding the biological basis of behavior and mental processes through neuroscience. Key learning objectives include defining biopsychology, exploring its origins, and discussing various research methods and ethical considerations involved in studying human and nonhuman subjects. The chapter outlines six primary fields of neuroscience relevant to biopsychological inquiry: physiological psychology, psychopharmacology, neuropsychology, psychophysiology, cognitive neuroscience, and comparative psychology. It emphasizes the complexity of brain function and its implications for scientific study, illustrated through the case study of Jimmie G., a patient with severe memory impairment due to Korsakoff's syndrome. The chapter also highlights four major themes: thinking creatively about biopsychological claims, clinical implications, the evolutionary perspective, and neuroplasticity, underlining the importance of these approaches for advancing scientific understanding in the field.
