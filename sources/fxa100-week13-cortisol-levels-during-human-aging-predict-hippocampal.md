## Cortisol Levels During Human Aging Predict Hippocampal Atrophy and Memory Deficits

The study investigates the relationship between cortisol levels and hippocampal atrophy in aging humans, linking prolonged elevation of glucocorticoids to reduced hippocampal volume and memory impairments. Over five years, researchers measured annual basal plasma cortisol levels in healthy elderly volunteers, identifying three subgroup patterns based on cortisol slope dynamics: increasing/high cortisol, increasing/moderate cortisol, and decreasing/moderate cortisol. MRI scans revealed that the group with progressively high cortisol showed a 14% reduction in hippocampal volume compared to those with decreasing/moderate cortisol levels. Memory tests indicated significant impairments in delayed recall and spatial memory for subjects with elevated cortisol. The findings suggest that prolonged exposure to high glucocorticoid levels may contribute to cognitive decline, providing insights into the neurobiological basis of age-related memory deficits.
