## Case Studies of Visual Arts with, and for Communities

The document discusses two case studies involving visual arts engagement projects aimed at fostering community wellbeing and social cohesion. The first case study, 'The Paper Apothecary,' describes a project initiated by the Beaney House in Canterbury where community members were invited to explore museum collections and create 'happiness prescriptions.' This initiative was part of the broader Happy Museum Project founded by Tony Butler, which seeks to redefine museums as spaces that promote well-being through social connections and environmental stewardship. The second case study highlights a mural project at Margaret Jurd College (Shortland) and discusses how community murals can serve both aesthetic and social purposes, fostering pride among residents and reducing vandalism in disadvantaged neighborhoods. These projects underscore the potential of visual arts to enhance community bonds and well-being, reflecting broader trends in cultural institutions shifting towards more inclusive and participatory roles.
