## The rationale for rating risk of bias should be fully reported

The letter discusses the importance of transparently reporting the rationale behind risk-of-bias (ROB) assessments in medical research studies, especially when using domain-based tools for various study types such as randomized clinical trials and non-randomized studies. The author argues that while these tools offer flexibility through multiple response options (e.g., yes, probably yes [PY], probably no [PN], no), they can also introduce variability between assessors due to subjective interpretation of the data. This heterogeneity in ROB ratings can affect the inclusion criteria for meta-analyses and thus influence treatment effect estimates. To address this issue, Faggion suggests that researchers should clearly report their rationales for assessing each signaling question related to ROB. Such transparency would enable readers to make informed judgments about the appropriateness of different approaches used in ROB assessments. This recommendation aims at enhancing methodological rigor and reproducibility in evidence synthesis.
