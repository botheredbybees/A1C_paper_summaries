## Using the Arts as a Therapeutic Tool for Counselling – An Australian Aboriginal Perspective

Liz Cameron's paper discusses the use of arts as a therapeutic tool for counseling from an Australian Aboriginal perspective, addressing cultural and communication barriers between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples in Australia. The article highlights how traditional practices such as storytelling and art contribute to healing and understanding within Indigenous communities. It emphasizes that Indigenous culture is holistic, encompassing social, physical, mental, emotional, spiritual, and cultural aspects of well-being. The arts serve as a means to bridge these differences by providing a culturally safe environment for communication and self-expression.

Cameron argues that the arts can be used to facilitate better understanding and collaboration between health care professionals and Indigenous clients. She suggests that artistic expression offers a neutral ground where traditional practices can be incorporated into counseling sessions without causing offense or misunderstanding. The article also explores the importance of cultural protocols, such as appropriate introductions and acknowledgments of connections to land and family, in building trust and rapport.

The central argument posits that by integrating Indigenous cultural understandings with Western therapeutic methods, health care professionals can provide more effective and culturally sensitive support for Indigenous peoples. This approach respects Indigenous beliefs about healing and well-being while addressing contemporary mental health challenges.
