## What is dementia and how might it present?

This lesson introduces students to the concept of dementia, its symptoms, progression, and types. It explains that dementia is a collection of symptoms resulting from brain disorders, typically progressive with deteriorating cognitive functions including memory, thinking, behavior, and daily activities. The content elaborates on four common types of dementia: Alzheimer's disease, vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, and frontotemporal dementia. Each type is characterized by specific neurological changes such as amyloid plaques in Alzheimer's, vascular damage leading to reduced blood flow in vascular dementia, protein deposits known as Lewy bodies in Lewy body dementia, and predominantly behavioral and linguistic challenges in frontotemporal dementia. The lesson also provides statistics about the prevalence of dementia in Australia and suggests ways for students to engage with the material through note-taking and quizzes.
