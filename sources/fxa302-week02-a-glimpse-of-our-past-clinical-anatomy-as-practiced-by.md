## A Glimpse of Our Past: Clinical Anatomy as Practiced by Ancient Egyptians

This article explores the historical contributions of ancient Egypt to clinical anatomy, spanning from around 3000 BCE to 250 BCE. It highlights the anatomical knowledge and practices documented in papyri such as the Edwin Smith and Ebers papyri, which contain detailed surgical procedures and medical treatments. The authors discuss how Egyptian embalmers and physicians contributed significantly by dissecting bodies for religious purposes, thereby advancing anatomical understanding. Additionally, they examine the work of Greek anatomists like Herophilus and Erasistratus who practiced dissection in Alexandria, contributing further to the field of anatomy. This historical review underscores Egypt's foundational role in the development of anatomical sciences, providing insights that are still relevant today.
