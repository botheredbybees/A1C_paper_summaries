## Taking Out-of-Focus Pictures

In this article, photographer Jim Richardson discusses the intentional use of out-of-focus techniques in photography to create dramatic images where the subject stands out clearly against a blurred background. He explains that controlling depth of field through aperture settings is only part of the equation; getting close to the subject and using longer lenses are also crucial for achieving this effect. The article emphasizes the importance of understanding lens physics, relative distances between subjects and backgrounds, and creative angles such as shooting from a low perspective. Richardson provides tips on selecting appropriate camera equipment and background features that enhance the out-of-focus technique, while cautioning against altering photographs to represent them falsely as nonfiction.
