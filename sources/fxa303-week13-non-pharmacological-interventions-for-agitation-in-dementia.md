## Non-pharmacological interventions for agitation in dementia: systematic review of randomised controlled trials

The article presents a systematic review of non-pharmacological interventions for managing agitation in dementia, focusing on randomized controlled trials (RCTs). Agitation is prevalent among individuals with dementia and can lead to significant distress for both patients and caregivers. The study aimed to evaluate the effectiveness of various interventions over immediate and long-term periods. Key findings indicate that person-centered care, communication skills training, and adapted dementia care mapping significantly reduced symptomatic agitation in care home settings, showing efficacy not only immediately but also up to six months post-intervention. Additionally, protocol-driven music therapy was found effective in decreasing overall agitation levels. However, aromatherapy and light therapy did not demonstrate similar benefits. The review emphasizes the importance of tailored strategies for different stages of agitation severity and highlights the need for further research on interventions applicable to individuals living at home rather than institutional settings.
