## Apples Never Fall.....

Jane Crosswell's document 'Apples Never Fall.....' is a poignant narrative that explores the intergenerational legacy of culinary traditions within her family. The text weaves together memories and anecdotes of her ancestors, particularly focusing on the role of women who nourished their families through simple yet meaningful meals. Nanna’s raspberries during the Great Depression, Mum's crusty bread and sticky marmalade, and Aunt's choux buns with custard are all vividly described, each highlighting the resilience and love embedded in these everyday acts of care.

The document also touches on personal milestones like mastering a pavlova recipe from a family friend and discovering her passion for pastry through high school cooking classes. These experiences underscore Crosswell's own journey in embracing her culinary heritage and aspirations to become a professional pâtissière or chef.

Moreover, the text reflects on cherished traditions such as Christmas visits to the family shack where memories of simpler times are evoked through familiar scents and rituals like baking pies and preparing for Christmas pudding. This narrative not only celebrates food but also its role in preserving familial bonds and emotional connections across generations.
