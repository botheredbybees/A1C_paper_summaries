## Exploring Synaesthesia through a Metaphorical Lens

This chapter from the book 'The Many Faces of Creativity' by Sarah Turner and Jeannette Littlemore explores how synaesthesia can provide insights into creativity and metaphorical thinking. The authors discuss the phenomenon of synaesthesia, where individuals experience a blending of sensory perceptions, and its implications for understanding creative expression and metaphor usage. They present findings from their study on how synaesthetes describe sensory experiences, noting that these descriptions often involve rich metaphors and creative language. By examining synesthetic responses to different stimuli, the authors aim to shed light on the cognitive mechanisms underlying creativity and metaphorical thinking in a broader context.
