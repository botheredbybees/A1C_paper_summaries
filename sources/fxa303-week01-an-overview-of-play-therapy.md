## An Overview of Play Therapy

The article provides an overview of play therapy, emphasizing its significance in child development and mental health intervention. Play is fundamental for children to express themselves, explore the world around them, and develop social skills. The review highlights that play therapy uses a theoretical framework to enable trained therapists to utilize play as a therapeutic tool to help children prevent or resolve psychosocial issues and achieve optimal growth. It is particularly effective for preschool and school-aged children who face significant life transitions, developmental challenges, or emotional distress. Play therapy facilitates the expression of emotions and thoughts through symbolic means more accessible than verbal communication for young children. The article also explores historical perspectives on play, including contributions from key figures like Piaget, Freud, Klein, Winnicott, and Axline, each contributing unique insights into how play can be harnessed therapeutically. Overall, the efficacy of play therapy is demonstrated through its ability to reduce negative emotions and behaviors in children undergoing various challenges or developmental issues.
