## Hidden Identities and Concealed Dangers: Visual Art, Transgender Health, and Wellbeing

The paper discusses the challenges faced by transgender individuals in terms of health risks and social discrimination during their transition process. Despite societal shifts towards greater acceptance and legal protections, trans people still encounter significant obstacles that pose both physical and mental dangers throughout their life course. Visual art is proposed as a powerful medium to bring these issues into public discourse and highlight hidden health concerns impacting the transgender community.

The authors argue that while there is an increasing desire for visibility among trans individuals, this often comes with heightened risks due to societal prejudices and institutional barriers. This paradox leads many to opt for incremental invisibility or 'passing'—concealing their true identity in order to navigate safer social spaces. However, such invisibility can also suppress the voices of transgender people within broader cultural narratives, hindering recognition of their needs and struggles.

The paper further explores how visual art can serve as a tool for revealing these concealed health dangers by grounding complex experiences into tangible images that resonate widely. By challenging normative perceptions through provocative imagery, artworks have potential to spark social change necessary for improving transgender people’s access to healthcare services and addressing the unique challenges they face.
