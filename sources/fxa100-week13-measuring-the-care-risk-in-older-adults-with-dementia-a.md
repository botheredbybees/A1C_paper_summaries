## Measuring the care risk in older adults with dementia: a scoping review

This scoping review aims to elucidate the concept of care risk in older adults with dementia and assess current tools for measuring this risk. The study identifies four primary dimensions of care risk: physical health, caregivers, social support, and environment. It highlights that existing tools require longitudinal validation and emphasizes the need for patient-centered assessment approaches. The research underscores the importance of comprehensive risk assessment to mitigate adverse outcomes in dementia care. However, due to definitional ambiguities and operational challenges, further standardized evaluation methods are essential.
