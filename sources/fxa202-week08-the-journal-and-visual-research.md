## The Journal and Visual Research

The document 'The Journal and Visual Research' by the University of Tasmania provides a comprehensive guide for students on how to maintain an effective visual journal. It emphasizes the importance of documenting processes, methods, techniques, and practices used in creative projects. The guide also highlights the role of journals as research tools that help students keep track of their ideas and influences over time. Students are encouraged to engage with critical texts and contextual sources, such as historical and contemporary artworks and exhibitions. Visual note-taking through sketchnoting is promoted as a method for enhancing memory and understanding by combining text and imagery. The document stresses the necessity of proper source documentation using Harvard referencing style and includes tips on analyzing artwork through pictorial analysis. It serves as an essential tool for fostering independent research, critical thinking, and project development in creative arts.
