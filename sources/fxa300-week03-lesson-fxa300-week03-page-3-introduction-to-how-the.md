## Music and Mood

The lesson explores universal responses to music, cultural influences on emotional interpretation, and the psychological mechanisms that underpin musical emotions. Research by Dr. Thomas Fritz suggests humans may recognize emotional expressions in unfamiliar music, indicating potential universality. The discussion delves into how different cultures associate moods with musical scales, the historical context of the Doctrine of the Affections during the Baroque era, and modern research on tuning systems like equal temperament. Additionally, it examines how restaurants manipulate music to influence customer behavior and taste perception. Finally, Juslin's BRECVEMA model is introduced, outlining eight psychological processes through which people respond emotionally to music.
