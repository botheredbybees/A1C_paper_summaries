## Eastern Conceptualizations of Happiness: Fundamental Differences with Western Views

This review article contrasts Western and Eastern conceptualizations of happiness and optimal functioning, identifying six fundamental differences between the two perspectives. It argues that contemporary Western notions of happiness are rooted in individualistic values such as autonomy and self-determination, while Eastern traditions like Hinduism, Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism, and Sufism emphasize unity with the divine, self-discovery, and transcendence beyond material existence. The article critiques the Western approach for being overly culture-specific and calls for a more inclusive research methodology that incorporates indigenous frameworks to better understand happiness across diverse cultural contexts.
