## Exploring the Experiences of a Person with Dementia and Their Spouse Who Attended a Telehealth Music Therapy Programme: Two Case Examples from Ireland

This paper presents an exploratory phenomenological study examining the experiences of two couples who participated in a six-week telehealth music therapy program for individuals with dementia and their spouses in Ireland. The research aimed to contribute to the growing evidence base on telehealth music therapy outside of the COVID-19 era when traditional in-person services were available. Through detailed case studies, the study revealed three main themes: social isolation exacerbated by a dementia diagnosis in rural areas; song singing and writing enabling revisiting happy memories; and telehealth delivery increasing access due to geographical barriers. The findings highlight that telehealth music therapy has potential for expanding accessibility to those who otherwise lack access or are unable to attend community support services due to cognitive decline, mobility issues, or remote locations. Despite the benefits observed, successful implementation relies on several factors including digital literacy, appropriate technology availability, caregiver support, and level of cognitive impairment.
