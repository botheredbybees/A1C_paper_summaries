## Arts, Health and Well-Being: A Critical Perspective on Research, Policy and Practice - Introduction

The introduction of 'Arts, Health and Well-Being: A Critical Perspective on Research, Policy and Practice' by Norma Daykin discusses the growing interest in the intersection between arts and health over the past two decades. It highlights the significant contributions from artists, researchers, health professionals, and policymakers who have collaborated to explore the connections and evidence base for arts’ impact on health and well-being. The text acknowledges challenges that need addressing before a cohesive integration of arts into healthcare can be fully realized.

Daykin emphasizes the idea that combining arts with healthcare is seen as a potential solution to demographic and social changes, which often reveal limitations in traditional medical approaches. She critiques the notion that scientific medicine alone can address all health issues and introduces the concept that artistic knowledge, like healthcare knowledge, is shaped by societal conditions and cannot exist independently of them.

The book’s two main premises are outlined: first, the influence of political governance on healthcare development; second, the social and political embedding of artistic production and reception. These foundational ideas set up the framework for understanding how arts and health can collaboratively address broader health challenges and inequalities in society.
