## Moshing with Physics | Jesse Silverberg | TEDxYale – Video transcript

In this video transcript of 'Moshing with Physics' by Jesse Silverberg at TEDxYale, the speaker explores the behavior of crowds during heavy metal concerts through a scientific lens. He describes how mosh pits, chaotic and seemingly unordered spaces where concertgoers collide and move randomly, can be analyzed using principles from physics. Specifically, he uses Newton's second law to describe forces acting on individuals in mosh pits, including propulsion, repulsion, flocking behavior (following others), and noise/randomness due to distraction or intoxication. By simulating these interactions computationally, Silverberg finds that the statistical patterns of movement in real-world mosh pit videos match those predicted by their model. Moreover, as they adjust parameters like the strength of flocking behavior in their simulations, they observe transitions from chaotic states to more ordered formations such as circular groupings. These findings are relevant beyond music events; understanding collective human behavior could have practical applications for managing large gatherings and ensuring public safety during emergencies.
