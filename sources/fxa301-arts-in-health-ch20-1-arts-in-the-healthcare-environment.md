## Does a Model Exist?

This chapter explores the existence of a model for categorizing arts in health activities and discusses the challenges and benefits of such models. It highlights how bespoke projects tailored to specific needs have rendered traditional modeling difficult but necessary for descriptive purposes. The chapter presents seven categories of arts in health activities, illustrating their development over time with examples like Paintings in Hospitals and De Hogeweyk dementia village. These initiatives demonstrate the importance of art in healthcare environments and participatory programs for patient groups, emphasizing a holistic approach to improving patient care through arts integration.
