## Interventions for Dementia Care

This document outlines various pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions used in the care of individuals with dementia. Pharmacological interventions primarily involve drugs like acetylcholinesterase inhibitors and memantine, which aim to slow cognitive decline but do not cure dementia. These medications can have side effects and are expensive, necessitating cost-management strategies for patients. Non-pharmacological interventions include Cognitive Stimulation Therapy (CST), reminiscence therapy, validation therapy, reality orientation, physical exercise, multisensory stimulation through Snoezelen rooms, and aromatherapy. CST aims to engage individuals in activities that stimulate cognitive function and enhance quality of life. Reminiscence therapy leverages long-term memory to improve emotional well-being, while other non-pharmacological methods focus on reducing agitation and improving communication between patients and caregivers. These interventions are crucial for providing holistic care and enhancing the daily lives of people with dementia.
