## The one thing that actually helps: Art creation as a self-care and health-promoting practice amongst youth experiencing homelessness

This study explores the significance and impact of arts creation for youth experiencing homelessness, based on in-depth interviews with 23 participants and shelter staff at a large youth homeless shelter. The research identifies five key ways through which art-making aids these young people: managing mental health challenges; coping with stress and homelessness; recovering from trauma by creating 'safe spaces'; exploring self-expression and discovery; and fostering positive self-esteem and hope for the future. Findings emphasize the personal, strategic use of art as a form of self-care and health promotion. The study underscores the importance of developing arts-based interventions that support youth's existing practices to enhance their mental well-being, resilience, and empowerment in overcoming adversity.
