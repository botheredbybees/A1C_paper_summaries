## The transactional model of stress and coping

This document outlines the transactional model of stress and coping developed by Lazarus and Folkman in 1984. The model challenges traditional views of stress as either a stimulus or a response, instead framing it as an interactive process involving both the individual and their environment. Central to this model is the idea that stress arises from appraisals, which are judgments about whether a situation is threatening, challenging, or harmful to one’s well-being. The document emphasizes the dynamic nature of these appraisals, noting they can be complex and not necessarily linear in progression. However, the text also critiques certain limitations within the model such as its lack of emphasis on biological aspects of stress reactions. This framework is particularly relevant for understanding self-care practices and mindfulness interventions in addressing stress, highlighting the importance of individual perceptions and coping mechanisms.
