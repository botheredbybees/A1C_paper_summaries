## Puppet Play Therapy with Children of All Ages

This chapter discusses the use of puppet play therapy in psychotherapeutic settings with children across different age groups. It explores how puppets can be utilized as therapeutic tools to aid in diagnosis and treatment by providing access to a child's imagination and inner world through dramatic play. The text highlights key psychological mechanisms such as symbolism, projection, displacement, and role reversal, which are employed during puppet therapy sessions. Case studies illustrate the effectiveness of this approach, particularly with children experiencing traumatic events or emotional distress. Puppet play therapy is emphasized for its potential to facilitate healing by allowing children to express emotions and reframe experiences in a controlled and safe environment.
