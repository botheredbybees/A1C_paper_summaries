## Can science account for taste? Psychological insights into art appreciation

This article explores the psychological insights into art appreciation, challenging the notion that aesthetic preferences are unpredictable and inherently subjective. The authors review recent research suggesting that scientific methods can provide significant understanding of factors influencing art appreciation. They examine both bottom-up variables intrinsic to paintings (such as abstraction, form, complexity, and symmetry) and top-down influences such as artwork novelty and viewer expertise. The paper argues that while the full extent of aesthetic preferences may seem unquantifiable, a growing body of research has shed light on various factors reliably affecting art appreciation. This review aims to bridge the gap between subjective aesthetics and objective scientific analysis, contributing to a deeper understanding of how visual art impacts human psychology.
