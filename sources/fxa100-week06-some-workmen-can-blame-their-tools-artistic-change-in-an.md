## Some workmen can blame their tools: artistic change in an individual with Alzheimer’s disease

The article explores the relationship between Alzheimer's disease and artistic ability through a detailed case study of William Utermohlen, an artist diagnosed with probable Alzheimer’s at age 61. The study documents his deterioration in cognitive functions and how this directly correlates with changes in his visual art creations over time. Initially, Utermohlen’s work was precise and reflective of his technical skill; however, as the disease progressed, there were noticeable declines in visuospatial skills, leading to a loss of proportionality and coherence in his later self-portraits. The research highlights how Alzheimer's impacts an artist’s ability to maintain consistency and detail in their artwork, providing insights into the neurological underpinnings of artistic expression and creativity. This case study is significant for understanding the impact of neurodegenerative diseases on cognitive functions that are essential for creative arts.
