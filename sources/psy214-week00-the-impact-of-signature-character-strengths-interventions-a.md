## The Impact of Signature Character Strengths Interventions: A Meta-analysis

This meta-analysis evaluates the impact of interventions focused on using signature character strengths, which are an individual's most prominent personal strengths. The study consolidates results from fourteen articles with twenty-nine effect sizes, examining various outcomes such as positive affect, happiness, depression levels, and life satisfaction. Across nine studies, these interventions significantly increased positive affect or happiness (weighted Hedges' g = 0.32). Seven studies reported significant reductions in depression symptoms (g = 0.21), while seven studies showed an increase in life satisfaction (g = 0.42). The research also highlights a notable impact on the use of signature strengths itself, based on two studies with Hedges' g = 0.55. Despite these promising results, the authors emphasize that further research is needed to fully understand and optimize the efficacy of such interventions.
