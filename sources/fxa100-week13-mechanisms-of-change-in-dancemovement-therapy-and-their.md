## Mechanisms of change in dance/movement therapy and their neural correlates: A theoretical framework

The article presents a theoretical framework for dance/movement therapy (DMT) by identifying unique therapeutic factors and their neural correlates, aiming to establish a common language in the field. The study involves an extensive literature review to delineate five main mechanisms of change: connection through movement, emotional regulation through movement, body sensing and being, creative movement expression, and meaning making through movement. Each mechanism is analyzed with subcategories that explore the interplay between DMT practices and neurobiological processes. The authors argue for interdisciplinary collaboration to integrate somatic and creative modalities within neuroscience research, thereby advancing understanding of therapeutic benefits in health and well-being contexts such as mental health issues, medical conditions, and psychological trauma.
