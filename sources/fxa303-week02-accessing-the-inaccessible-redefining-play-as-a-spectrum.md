## Accessing the Inaccessible: Redefining Play as a Spectrum

The article 'Accessing the Inaccessible: Redefining Play as a Spectrum' by Jennifer M. Zosh and colleagues challenges traditional views on defining play, suggesting that it should be seen as a spectrum rather than a rigidly defined concept. The authors argue that previous attempts to define play have been inconsistent due to its complexity and broad range of manifestations. They propose viewing play along a continuum from free play (completely child-led with no goals) to guided play and games (which include adult involvement aimed at learning objectives but retain playful elements). This spectrum approach aims to capture the essence of play's role in various developmental aspects, including social interactions and academic learning. The paper highlights how this redefined concept of play can inform educational practices better by clarifying distinctions between types of play and their specific outcomes.
