## Community perspectives on the appropriateness and importance of support goals for young autistic children

The article explores the perspectives of autistic adults, parents, and professionals on support goals for young autistic children in New Zealand and Australia. It reveals that all groups prioritize goals related to adult support, reducing harmful behaviors, and improving quality of life. However, autistic adults give lower priority ratings to play skills, autism characteristics, and participation goals compared to parents and professionals, who find these areas more appropriate and important. The study highlights the need for further research to understand why there are differences in priorities among these groups. The article also discusses how differing models of disability—medical model versus neurodiversity/social model—affect perceptions of support goals.
