## The impact of a visual arts program on quality of life, communication, and well-being of people living with dementia: a mixed-methods longitudinal investigation

This study evaluates the impact of a visual arts program on quality of life, communication, and well-being for individuals living with dementia through a mixed-methods longitudinal investigation. Researchers recruited 125 participants across three settings in England and Wales to participate in a 12-week art program. The findings indicate significant improvements in domains such as attention, pleasure, self-esteem, and negative affect compared to an alternative non-art activity group. While proxy-reported quality of life improved over the study period, participant reports did not show similar changes. However, qualitative interviews highlighted the program's value for social connectedness, well-being, and inner strength. The study underscores the potential benefits of incorporating art activities into dementia care practices.
