## Identity, Connection, and Motivation

The chapter explores the role of art therapy in enhancing the quality of life for older individuals, particularly those facing challenges such as dementia or loss. It highlights a shift from traditional psychodynamic approaches to a more person-centered and embodied approach that focuses on supporting the capacities and strengths of elderly people rather than attempting to fix their ailments. Central themes include fostering an artist identity, promoting social connection, and instilling motivation. Through case studies like Mary’s, who uses art to cope with her husband's death and declining vision, the author illustrates how art therapy can help individuals maintain a sense of self and purpose despite life changes. The text emphasizes the importance of adapting therapeutic environments to suit individual needs and encourages exploration of various artistic mediums as tools for personal expression and resilience.
