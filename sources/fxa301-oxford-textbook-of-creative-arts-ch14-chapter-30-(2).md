## Case Study: Engaging Older People in Creative Thinking - The Active Energy Project

This chapter presents a case study of the Active Energy project, an initiative that engages older people in creative thinking and technological innovation through participatory art projects. The project started with a commission from SPACE Studios London to work with members of the Geezers Club, an organization established to reduce isolation among retired men over 50 years old in Tower Hamlets. Initially, the author approached the group expecting limited engagement but was surprised by their enthusiasm and innovative ideas regarding tidal power generation for community benefit. Through workshops, research trips, and mentorship programs with local schools, the Geezers Club members developed plans for improved turbine designs and presented their findings at academic symposiums. The project expanded internationally to Pittsburgh, connecting UK and US senior groups through technology-driven collaborations focused on health issues such as Alzheimer's research.
