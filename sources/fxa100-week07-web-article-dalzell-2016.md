## Art Alleviates Anxiety for People with Dementia, New Research Finds

A study commissioned by the Art Gallery of New South Wales has found that viewing art creates a heightened sense of joy and reduces anxiety in individuals living with dementia. The gallery's art access program invites people with dementia and their carers to view iconic works, allowing participants to engage in the present moment rather than struggling to recall memories. This program, which has been running since 2010, provides emotional benefits for those affected by dementia while also enhancing the interaction between patients and their caregivers.
