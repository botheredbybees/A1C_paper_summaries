## Role of Art Therapy in the Promotion of Mental Health: A Critical Review

The article by Shukla et al. provides a comprehensive review of the role and effectiveness of art therapy in promoting mental health, particularly focusing on visual art therapy for adult mental health rehabilitation. The authors explore how engaging in artistic activities can help individuals express themselves more freely, improve their mental health, and enhance interpersonal relationships. They highlight that art therapy is grounded in the idea that people can achieve recovery and emotional well-being through creative expression.

The review critically evaluates existing literature on art therapy interventions such as painting, drawing, and crafting, emphasizing its benefits for conditions like mood disorders, anxiety, depression, schizophrenia, dementia, and Alzheimer's disease. The authors argue that despite numerous causes of mental illness, including environmental, brain-related, and genetic factors, incorporating art therapy can be a valuable adjunct treatment to improve patient outcomes.

Shukla et al. also discuss the historical context of using arts for healing purposes and how it has evolved into formalized therapeutic practices since 1940. They examine various modes of communication through kinaesthetic, sensory, perceptual, and symbolic means that art therapy facilitates beyond traditional verbal communication. The review concludes by emphasizing the importance of continued research on art therapy's mechanisms to better understand its role in mental health recovery and rehabilitation.
