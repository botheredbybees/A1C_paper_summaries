## The Time Course of Emotional Responses to Music

This study investigates the temporal dynamics of emotional responses to music, focusing on how quickly listeners can identify and categorize emotions conveyed by musical excerpts. The research includes two empirical studies: one where participants grouped similar emotional meanings from short (25 seconds) and very brief (1 second) musical clips using multidimensional scaling methods; and another where participants rated the emotional impact of increasing durations of musical pieces ranging from 250 milliseconds to 20 seconds on a subjective scale. The findings suggest that listeners can discern complex emotional responses in less than one second, indicating an intricate psychological and neurophysiological process involved in music perception. These results are significant for understanding how music influences emotions and cognitive processes, contributing valuable insights into the creative arts and health.
