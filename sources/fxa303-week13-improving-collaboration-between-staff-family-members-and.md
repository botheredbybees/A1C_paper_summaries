## Improving Collaboration Between Staff, Family Members, and Artists in Long-Term Dementia Care: A Participatory Action Research Project Into Health Care Clowning

The study explores the impact of participatory action research (PAR) on health care clowning in dementia care, focusing on the collaborative processes between staff, family members, and artists. It highlights that while arts-based interventions like clowning can have positive effects on individuals with dementia, their success is contingent upon effective collaboration within healthcare settings. The research underscores the need for a supportive environment that facilitates open dialogue and clear organizational structures to implement such practices effectively. Key findings indicate that elements of PAR, including arts-based methods and facilitative communication, serve as catalysts for successful boundary work between art and care sectors, fostering sustainable impacts in long-term dementia care.
