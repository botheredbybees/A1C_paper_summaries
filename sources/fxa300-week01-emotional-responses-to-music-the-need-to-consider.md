## Emotional responses to music: The need to consider underlying mechanisms

This article discusses the challenge of explaining emotional responses to music, a topic that has intrigued experts and laypeople for centuries. The authors argue that existing research on musical emotions is inconsistent due to an inadequate focus on the underlying mechanisms through which emotions are induced by music. They propose a theoretical framework featuring six mechanisms: brain stem reflexes, evaluative conditioning, emotional contagion, visual imagery, episodic memory, and musical expectancy. Each mechanism differs in terms of its information focus, developmental stage, key brain regions, cultural impact, induction speed, degree of volitional influence, modularity, and dependence on musical structure. The authors suggest that understanding these mechanisms can help resolve previous disagreements within the field and provide new paradigms for emotion induction research.
