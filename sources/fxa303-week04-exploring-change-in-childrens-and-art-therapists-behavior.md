## Exploring Change in Children’s and Art Therapists’ Behavior during ‘Images of Self’, an Art Therapy Program for Children Diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorders: A Repeated Case Study Design

This study evaluates the 'Images of Self' (IOS) art therapy program for children with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD), focusing on changes in behavior related to sense of self, emotion regulation, flexibility, and social behavior. Twelve children diagnosed with ASD participated alongside seven trained therapists who monitored their own actions using two observation tools: OAT (Observation of a child with autism in Art Therapy) and EAT (Evaluation of Art Therapist’s behavior when working with a child with autism). The results indicated that ten out of twelve children showed significant improvement in these areas, particularly social behavior. During therapy sessions, therapists focused on supporting emotion regulation, flexibility, and social behavior. While specific one-on-one relationships between changes in children's behavior and therapist actions were not clearly identified, the study highlights the potential for art therapy to enhance communication skills and therapeutic relationships.
