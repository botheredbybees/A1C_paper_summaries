## Adolescent Neurodevelopment

The article discusses notable alterations in the adolescent brain, focusing on both regressive and progressive changes that refine brain functional connectivity. The review highlights synaptic pruning and myelination as key processes shaping the adolescent brain, influencing its efficiency and connectivity. The study explores how these neurodevelopmental transformations interact with hormonal changes and socio-economic factors to impact adolescent behavior. Key regions of interest include the prefrontal cortex (PFC) and subcortical areas such as the amygdala and ventral striatum. These regions are crucial for emotional, social, and reward processing, influencing behaviors like risk-taking, sensation seeking, and alcohol/drug use. The article suggests that enhanced sensitivity to rewards and decreased inhibition under emotional circumstances contribute to adolescent risk behavior patterns. This understanding has implications for public policies aimed at promoting adolescent health and well-being.
