## Prevalence of Dementia in Urban and Regional Aboriginal Australians

This study investigates the prevalence of dementia among urban and regional Aboriginal Australians, aiming to provide comparative data for further studies on dementia in disadvantaged fourth-world populations. The research involved a cross-sectional survey across five communities in Sydney and New South Wales, identifying participants through collaboration with local health organizations and community members. A total of 546 potential participants were identified, with 336 (61.5%) participating. Participants underwent cognitive screening tests and detailed medical assessments, which were reviewed by a panel of clinicians to determine diagnoses according to standard criteria. The study found that the crude prevalence of dementia was 13.4%, while age-standardized prevalence reached 21.0%. Alzheimer's disease and mixed dementia were the most common types, with estimated rates of MCI at 17.7%. These findings highlight high dementia rates among urban and regional Aboriginal Australians, particularly at younger ages compared to non-Indigenous populations.
