## Theatre of Wandering: Rehearsal and Devising Notes [Part 1]

The document provides an insightful look into the creation process of 'Theatre of Wandering', a performance piece co-created by Entelechy Arts, OiBokkeShi from Japan, and local Coventry participants. The project aims to explore themes of care, community, creativity, and dementia through performances on the streets of Coventry city center. Inspired by Naoki Sugawara's work 'Night Never Gets Darker', Theatre of Wandering is a collaborative effort involving various stakeholders including older actors, caregivers, and individuals living with dementia.

The process involves three stages: establishing relationships with local arts and health partners to incorporate creative processes into existing care mechanisms; engaging diverse community members as storytellers, writers, puppeteers, and sound artists to contribute their experiences and perspectives; and conducting formal and informal devising sessions across different locations within Coventry. Despite challenges posed by the pandemic, the team maintained regular teleconferences with collaborators in Japan.

The document highlights the importance of involving underrepresented voices in artistic creation and emphasizes how these collaborative efforts can lead to meaningful narratives that resonate deeply with audiences.
