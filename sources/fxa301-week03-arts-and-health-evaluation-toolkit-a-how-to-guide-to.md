## Arts and Health Evaluation Toolkit: A how-to guide to evaluate the health outcomes of arts programmes in the community

The Arts and Health Evaluation Toolkit (AHET) is a preliminary guide designed to enhance the effectiveness of arts-based health interventions through rigorous evaluation practices. It addresses the growing need for robust evidence in the field, where many studies claim benefits from arts programs but lack methodological rigor. The toolkit aims to strengthen the evidence base by providing guidance on how to conduct effective evaluations, including planning and designing evaluations, selecting appropriate measurement tools (both qualitative and quantitative), and data collection methods. Key chapters cover various aspects such as mental health outcomes, quality of life measures, social connections, physiological measures, and practical considerations for implementing evaluation practices. The AHET is particularly relevant in enhancing the integration of arts-based programs into mainstream healthcare services, promoting better understanding and adoption among policymakers and funders.
