## Comparative efficacy of various art therapies for patients with dementia: A network meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

This study conducted a network meta-analysis to compare the efficacy of various art therapies in improving cognitive function, activity of daily living, depression, anxiety, agitation behavior, and quality of life for patients with dementia. The analysis included 39 randomized controlled trials involving 2801 participants. Calligraphy therapy showed significant improvements in cognitive function compared to usual care and reminiscence therapy. Horticultural therapy was found to be effective in reducing agitation behavior relative to other therapies and usual care. These findings suggest that calligraphy therapy might be the most effective for enhancing cognitive function and quality of life, while horticultural therapy could be optimal for decreasing agitation behavior. The research underscores the potential benefits of art therapies as non-pharmacological interventions to support dementia patients' well-being.
