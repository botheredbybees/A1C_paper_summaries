## The Dance of Wellbeing: Defining the Musical Therapeutic Effect

The article 'The Dance of Wellbeing: Defining the Musical Therapeutic Effect' by Colwyn Trevarthen and Stephen N. Malloch explores how music therapy can facilitate communication and emotional well-being in individuals, particularly those under stress or with disabilities. The authors argue that humans possess an innate musicality, a capacity for rhythmic and melodic expression that transcends cultural boundaries. This intrinsic ability to communicate through sound and movement enables infants and adults alike to engage meaningfully in music therapy sessions, creating a profound therapeutic impact. Music therapy is seen as a form of intersubjective communication where therapists and clients share feelings and narratives through the intuitive exchange of rhythmic and melodic patterns. The paper also highlights the importance of spontaneous musical interactions between parents and infants, which suggest that the fundamental aspects of music making are deeply rooted in human psychology and can be harnessed for therapeutic purposes.
