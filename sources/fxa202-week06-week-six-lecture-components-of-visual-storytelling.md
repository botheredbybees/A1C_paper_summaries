## Week Six Lecture: Components of Visual Storytelling

The Week Six lecture focuses on the craft of visual storytelling, specifically addressing narrative structures and techniques for sequencing images. The lecture aims to help students describe the key components of visual storytelling, including linear and non-linear story structures, establishing and ending shots, and the role of character and detail in enriching a story. Inspirational examples from past student work are provided to encourage creativity, such as Lisa Hathaway’s photo essay using dioramas created during lockdowns. The lecture emphasizes the importance of making a shot list before photography sessions to guide storytelling elements like lead photos, establishing shots, character focus, detailed views, and closing shots. Students are tasked with creating a ‘Day in your life’ three-picture collage as part of their discussion activity, practicing the narrative techniques discussed.
