## 'It makes me feel like myself': Person-centered versus traditional visual arts activities for people with dementia

The study, conducted over a period of 15 months between February 2010 and April 2011, compared the impact of person-centered visual arts activities called Opening Minds through Art (OMA) versus traditional visual arts activities on individuals with dementia. The research involved observing participants during OMA sessions and traditional activities such as coloring books and scrapbooking at three long-term care facilities in Ohio. Utilizing a modified version of the Greater Cincinnati Chapter Well-Being Observation Tool, researchers collected data on well-being indicators including social interest, engagement, and pleasure, alongside negative affect and confusion. Results indicated higher levels of positive well-being during OMA sessions with significantly less disengagement compared to traditional activities. The study highlights the potential benefits of person-centered arts interventions in enhancing quality of life for individuals with dementia, emphasizing their role in promoting personhood and reducing stigma associated with the disease.
