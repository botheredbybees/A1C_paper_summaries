## Eudaimonia continued: Psychological Needs and Well-being

This lesson explores the concept of eudaimonic well-being through self-determination theory (SDT) and Carol Ryff's six-factor model. SDT by Ryan and Deci emphasizes three basic human needs: competence, autonomy, and relatedness, leading to optimal well-being when met. The lesson includes a 21-item questionnaire for assessing these needs. It also discusses criticisms of SDT regarding cultural limitations. Ryff's model, in contrast, defines psychological well-being through six factors: self-acceptance, positive relations with others, purpose in life, environmental mastery, autonomy, and personal growth. A 18-item questionnaire is provided to measure these factors. Both models are critiqued for being culturally biased but remain influential.
