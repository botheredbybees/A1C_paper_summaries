## Inclusive communication with LGBTIQ+ clients

This practice guide from the Australian Institute of Family Studies highlights the importance of inclusive communication in providing services to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, intersex or queer (LGBTIQ+) individuals. The document underscores that a lack of inclusive language can lead to stigma and discrimination, which may prevent LGBTIQ+ people from seeking necessary help. Inclusive communication involves verbal acknowledgment through appropriate terminology, written forms accommodating diverse identities, and visual elements such as flags or imagery representing LGBTIQ+ communities. Evidence suggests that when service providers do not adopt an inclusive approach, clients experience stress, fear of poor treatment, and a reluctance to disclose their identity, which can negatively impact their health outcomes.

The guide identifies three key strategies for ensuring inclusive communication: adopting an affirmative approach by understanding the nuances of sexual orientation, gender identity, and variations in sex characteristics; going beyond assumptions about a client's identity through open-ended questions without defaulting to heterosexual or cisgender norms; and demonstrating competence with LGBTIQ+ language to foster an environment where clients feel comfortable disclosing their identities. The guide also provides practical examples for practitioners to implement these strategies effectively.
