## Clowns and the Nature of Clowning

This lesson explores the history and role of clowns and jesters from ancient times to modern therapeutic clowning. It discusses how court jesters in medieval times used humour to influence political dynamics, often wearing brightly colored attire including patterns associated with Harlequin. The evolution of the term 'clown' is traced through theatrical performances like pantomimes and the significant contributions by Joseph Grimaldi who revolutionized clown costumes and makeup. Modern clowns are influenced by both Grimaldi's style and the 'Auguste' character type. Clowning can enhance actors' improvisational skills and non-verbal communication, providing therapeutic benefits for performers and audiences alike through laughter-induced health improvements. Social clowning initiatives like Clowns without Borders bring joy to children in challenging environments such as conflict zones or refugee camps.
