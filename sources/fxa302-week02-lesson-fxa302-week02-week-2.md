## Perspectives of the Arts on Health and Wellbeing - Week 2

This week's lesson explores the historical roots of modern medicine and cultural beliefs about health in ancient Greece and Egypt. It discusses how elements of Egyptian medical practices influenced Greek ideas, highlighting figures like Imhotep as early physicians. The text covers the role of shamanism in healing across various cultures, emphasizing the integration of spiritual and medical practices. Case studies include Homer's impact on perceptions of blindness and disability in Ancient Greece. Students learn about ancient papyrus documents such as the Ebers Papyrus and how they contributed to our understanding of anatomy and herbal remedies. The lesson also introduces channel theory from Ancient Egypt and its parallels with traditional Chinese medicine, illustrating how these historical ideas continue to shape contemporary views on health.
