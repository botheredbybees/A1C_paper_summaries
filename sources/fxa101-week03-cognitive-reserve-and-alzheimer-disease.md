## Cognitive Reserve and Alzheimer Disease

This article discusses the concept of cognitive reserve (CR) in relation to Alzheimer's disease (AD). The author, Yaakov Stern, explores how CR can mitigate the clinical impact of AD pathology. Two forms of reserve are identified: neural reserve and neural compensation. Neural reserve refers to more efficient or larger capacity brain networks that are less vulnerable to disruption. Neural compensation involves using alternative cognitive strategies or networks when primary ones are compromised by disease. The paper also reviews epidemiological evidence suggesting higher educational attainment correlates with a reduced risk of developing AD and slower cognitive decline in normal aging. The concept of CR offers an explanation for why individuals with higher IQ, education, or occupational attainment might be better equipped to cope with brain pathology before clinical symptoms manifest.
