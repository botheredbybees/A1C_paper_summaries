## Using Creative Arts Therapy to Promote Academic Success in the Postsecondary Setting: A Secondary Narrative Review of Preliminary Findings from 2009 to 2019

This article presents a secondary narrative review of creative arts therapy (CAT) interventions in postsecondary education, focusing on their potential to promote academic success from 2009 to 2019. The study analyzes empirical evidence gathered during a systematic review of CATs applied in K-12 settings and extends this analysis to higher education contexts. Key findings reveal that all creative arts therapies were represented, with a majority involving undergraduate students, qualitative data, and experiential learning methods. These studies demonstrated academic utility through an empirical lens, suggesting that CATs may be uniquely suited to enhance affective awareness and group processes for institutional integration. The review highlights the role of CATs in developing socioemotional competencies and leveraging these skills to improve educational outcomes, emphasizing their potential beyond mental health support towards achieving broader academic success goals.
