## Decolonising Psychology: Validating Social and Emotional Wellbeing

This paper explores the concept of social and emotional wellbeing (SEWB) for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples in Australia, emphasizing its multifaceted nature encompassing seven inter-related domains: body, mind and emotions, family and kinship, community, culture, Country, and spirituality. The authors present findings from the National Empowerment Project (NEP), a participatory action research initiative that highlights the importance of these SEWB domains for individual, familial, and communal health. They argue that acknowledging and addressing social determinants of Indigenous disadvantage is crucial for improving wellbeing. This decolonizing approach emphasizes the need to validate and incorporate traditional understandings of health and wellness within psychological practice.
