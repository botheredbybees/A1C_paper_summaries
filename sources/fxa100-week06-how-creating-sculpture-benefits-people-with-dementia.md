## How creating sculpture benefits people with dementia

Sumita Chauhan, a PhD candidate at the School of Engineering and Digital Arts (EDA) at the University of Kent, conducted research involving people with early-stage dementia in creating physical and digital sculptures. The project aimed to explore the creative potential of individuals living with dementia through meaningful artistic engagement and co-creation. Participants experimented with various forms of sculpture-making from clay and papier-mâché to virtual and digital formats over a period from November 2015 to April 2016 at The Beaney House of Art & Knowledge in Canterbury, UK. An exhibition titled 'Sculptural Revelations' showcased the work produced by these participants, highlighting their passion, personality, and beliefs through artistic expression. Chauhan’s research emphasizes the profound benefits that creative activities such as sculpture-making can offer to people with dementia, enhancing their cognitive functions and providing a platform for self-expression and social interaction.
