## Imaging structural and functional brain development in early childhood

This review discusses recent imaging studies that have started to elucidate the rapid structural and functional brain development during early childhood, a period crucial for cognitive abilities and neuropsychiatric health. The authors highlight that from birth to around two years of age, there is an extremely fast pace of brain growth in both grey and white matter structures. They also note significant developments in myelination and synaptic density within this timeframe. Despite challenges associated with imaging very young brains, these studies provide insights into the interrelation between structural and functional development, laying the groundwork for future research on genetic and environmental influences on early-childhood brain development.
