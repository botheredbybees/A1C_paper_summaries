## Puppets in an acute paediatric unit: Nurse’s experiences

The study examines the experiences of nurses using puppets within an educational framework called Pup-Ed (KRS Simulation) in a regional hospital's pediatric acute care setting. The framework aims to guide nurses on how to use puppets effectively, ensuring they have comprehensive knowledge about the child’s health situation and maintain a silent voice during interactions with children. Key findings indicate that puppets help reduce fear, anxiety, and barriers for children undergoing procedures while also serving as educational tools. Nurses reported challenges such as concerns over infection transfer and lack of confidence or time to use the puppets consistently. The study highlights the importance of a structured approach in using puppets for therapeutic purposes within healthcare settings.
