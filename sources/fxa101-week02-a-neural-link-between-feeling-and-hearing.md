## A Neural Link Between Feeling and Hearing

This study explores the anatomical connectivity between auditory and somatosensory cortices in humans, suggesting a neural basis for multisensory interactions. The authors used diffusion tensor imaging to demonstrate extensive ipsilateral connections between primary auditory cortex and primary/secondary somatosensory regions in normal participants. In a patient with acquired auditory-tactile synesthesia due to a thalamic lesion, these cross-modal connections were exaggerated, particularly between the auditory and secondary somatosensory cortices. This anatomical evidence supports the idea of multisensory integration mechanisms that link hearing and touch, explaining why certain sounds can induce tactile sensations.
