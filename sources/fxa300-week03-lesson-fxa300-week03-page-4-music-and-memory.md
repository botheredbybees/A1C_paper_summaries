## Music and Memory: Intersecting the two

This lesson explores the intersection between music and memory. It discusses how musical structures such as rhythm, pitch, contour, and tonality are processed in milliseconds by our cognitive systems, which share resources with auditory memory functions. The activation of limbic systems during musical experiences facilitates better encoding and storage of memories. Research indicates that musicians show stronger activations in areas like Wernicke's and Broca's areas compared to non-musicians, suggesting enhanced linguistic and verbal memories due to musical training. Implicit memory plays a crucial role in music learning through repeated exposure, while explicit (declarative) memory is involved in recalling specific musical features. The concept of the 'reminiscence bump' highlights the strong autobiographical memories associated with music from late adolescence and early adulthood, which can also cascade across generations.
