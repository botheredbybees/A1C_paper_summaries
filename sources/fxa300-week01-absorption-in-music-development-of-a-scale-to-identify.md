## Absorption in Music: Development of a Scale to Identify Individuals with Strong Emotional Responses to Music

The study by Sandstrom and Russo (2013) introduces the Absorption in Music Scale (AIMS), a 34-item measure designed to assess individuals' ability to immerse themselves emotionally within musical experiences. The scale is grounded in the concept of absorption, which refers to an individual's capacity for intense focus and self-altering attention when engaged with music. Through psychometric evaluations involving over 160 participants, the AIMS demonstrated strong reliability and validity. Key findings revealed that individuals who scored higher on the AIMS showed greater emotional responses to music but did not correlate as strongly with measures of empathy or formal musical training. This scale aims to provide a tool for researchers to identify listeners most susceptible to powerful emotional experiences through music, thereby contributing to the understanding of individual differences in the psychological impact of music.
