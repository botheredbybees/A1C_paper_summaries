## Painting for a Purpose: A Visual Arts Program as a Method to Promote Engagement, Communication, Cognition, and Quality of Life for Individuals With Dementia

This research article evaluates the efficacy of a visual arts program, specifically painting, as a therapeutic intervention for individuals living with dementia. The study aimed to determine if such programs could enhance engagement, communication, cognitive function, and quality of life in participants at various stages of dementia severity. Using an ABA reversal design over eight weeks, researchers compared outcomes between a free-form painting session and an active control condition (cooking activity) for three participants with mild-to-moderate, moderate, and moderate-to-severe dementia. Results indicated significant improvements in engagement and communication during the painting sessions relative to the control activities. There was also a non-significant positive trend towards enhanced self-reported quality of life from baseline to follow-up assessment. Cognitive-linguistic functions showed nominal changes pre- and post-intervention. The authors conclude that incorporating creative, meaningful activities like visual arts into daily routines for dementia patients can significantly boost engagement and communication, contributing positively to their overall quality of life.
