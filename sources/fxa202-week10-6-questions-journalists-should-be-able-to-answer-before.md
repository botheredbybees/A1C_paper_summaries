## 6 questions journalists should be able to answer before pitching a story

The article outlines six essential questions that journalists should be able to answer before pitching a story idea to an editor. The author emphasizes the importance of having a well-researched and specific story concept, rather than broad topics like 'the homeless' or 'drug gangs.' These questions help ensure reporters can effectively communicate their ideas and demonstrate why they are newsworthy and relevant to readers.

The first question asks about what initially intrigued the reporter and how it will drive them through the reporting process. The second seeks clarity on the timeliness and novelty of the story, including its relevance in a broader context or timeline. Thirdly, it prompts reporters to consider why their audience would be interested.

Fourth is an inquiry into digital storytelling opportunities across various platforms, such as videos or interactive graphics. Fifthly, journalists are encouraged to articulate key questions they plan to ask and sources they will consult during the reporting phase. Lastly, there's a discussion on the scope of the story—how much time it might take and how much space it deserves—in setting expectations between the reporter and editor.

This framework aids in refining pitches and ensuring that both reporters and editors understand the potential impact and execution of each proposed story.
