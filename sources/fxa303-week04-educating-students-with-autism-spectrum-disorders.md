## Educating Students with Autism Spectrum Disorders

The text 'Educating Students with Autism Spectrum Disorders' edited by Dianne Zager, Michael Wehmeyer, and Richard Simpson is a comprehensive guide to the current state of research and practice in educating students on the autism spectrum. The book covers historical perspectives, definitions and clinical characteristics, identification and diagnosis, various educational approaches, evidence-based practices, education through developmental stages, and interrelated disciplines such as language development and assistive technology. It emphasizes the need for interdisciplinary collaboration among professionals from multiple fields to serve individuals with autism effectively. The editors advocate for a research-driven approach to determine which interventions should be selected based on scientific evidence of their effectiveness rather than adhering strictly to specific educational orientations.
