## The Quest for Universals in Temporal Processing in Music

The paper by Drake and Bertrand explores the concept of universals in temporal processing within music, questioning whether certain cognitive processes are innate or learned through cultural exposure. The authors review studies on rhythm perception and production across different populations—adult musicians, non-musicians, children, and infants—to identify temporal processes that function similarly irrespective of age, acculturation, and musical training. They propose five candidate 'temporal universals' and suggest these could be tested in intercultural research to determine their applicability beyond specific cultural contexts. This investigation is crucial for understanding the cognitive foundations of music perception and its potential implications for creative arts and health.
