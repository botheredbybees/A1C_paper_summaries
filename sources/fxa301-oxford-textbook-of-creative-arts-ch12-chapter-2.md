## The Arts and Healing: The Power of an Idea

This chapter explores the burgeoning field of 'arts in health' in post-war Britain, emphasizing its growth from community initiatives to a funded component of public health strategies. The author highlights how arts engagement is perceived as beneficial for individual wellbeing and social inclusion. Key milestones include the establishment of the Review of Arts and Health Working Group by the Department of Health and Arts Council England's publication 'A Prospectus for Arts and Health.' Despite increased support, concerns remain about the evidence base for these interventions' effectiveness. Public belief in the therapeutic power of arts often precedes empirical validation, exemplified by phenomena like the Mozart effect.
