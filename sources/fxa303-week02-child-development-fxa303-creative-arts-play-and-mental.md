## Child Development - FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing

The document discusses the critical period of child development within the first thousand days, highlighting its significance for laying foundational health and wellbeing frameworks. It emphasizes that during this period, brain development is highly susceptible to environmental factors such as nutrition and cognitive stimulation, which can either foster or hinder optimal developmental trajectories. Research indicates an increasing focus on these early years both in Australia and internationally, aiming to address various health and social challenges through preventive measures.

The text also delves into the neural architecture of the brain, detailing how specific areas develop over time, particularly during the first two years after birth when there is significant growth in grey matter and cortical thickness. It further explains the concept of neuroplasticity and its importance for lifelong learning and adaptation. Additionally, it reviews theories of child development by prominent theorists like Erikson and Piaget, emphasizing their contributions to understanding developmental stages and cognitive growth.
