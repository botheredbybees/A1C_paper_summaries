## Role of art centres for Aboriginal Australians living with dementia in remote communities

The study explores the role of art centers in remote communities for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australians living with dementia. The research highlights that art programs are beneficial for both individuals with dementia and their caregivers, especially when these programs operate within culturally revered environments. Findings indicate that art centers play a crucial role in preserving traditions, culture, and practices unique to Indigenous populations but there is limited understanding of how they cater to the needs of people with dementia. With higher prevalence rates of dementia among Aboriginal Australians compared to non-Aboriginal individuals, particularly in remote areas where access to health services is scarce, addressing this gap through innovative policies and practices could enhance social participation and timely access to healthcare for those affected by dementia.
