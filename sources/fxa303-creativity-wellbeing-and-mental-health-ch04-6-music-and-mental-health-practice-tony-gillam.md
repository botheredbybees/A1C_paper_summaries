## Music and Mental Health Practice

This chapter explores the author's personal experiences using music in mental health settings, tracing the evolution of music therapy and its role in evidence-based practice. It highlights the Music Workshop Project (MWP), a community-focused initiative that shifted from a traditional therapy model to one emphasizing community engagement and user empowerment. The project emphasized improvisational musical activities aimed at enhancing wellbeing and reducing stigma around mental illness. Challenges related to maintaining an 'evidence base' alongside creative practices are discussed, along with the impact of community music movements on mental health care.
