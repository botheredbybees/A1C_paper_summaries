## Maximizing Personhood in Early Dementia

This chapter discusses the unique challenges faced by young-onset families dealing with early-stage dementia and offers strategies to address these issues. It emphasizes the importance of education about fronto-temporal dementia symptoms, safety concerns such as operating machinery and managing diabetes, and targeted interventions for both the person with dementia and their caregiver. The text highlights the value of support groups designed specifically for individuals in early stages of dementia and their caregivers, suggesting that these interventions can maximize personhood and improve decision-making capacity. It also discusses care values and preferences for assistance, advocating for structured dialogues to clarify these values and preferences between the affected individual and their caregiver. The chapter concludes by introducing the SHARE programme, which aims to facilitate discussions about care values and tasks in a way that reduces carer burden and enhances mutual understanding.
