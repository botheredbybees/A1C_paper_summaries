## Missing Out: Creative Ageing and Men

This report by David Cutler for the Baring Foundation explores the underrepresentation of older men in creative aging initiatives. It outlines the foundation's interest in ensuring equal access to arts and creativity for all, focusing on why men aged 60+ are less likely to participate compared to women. The document provides definitions for key terms such as 'creative ageing' and 'participatory arts.' It highlights a lack of guidance, research, and concerted action concerning older men's engagement in the arts. Case studies illustrate good practices from various organizations working with older male participants. The report concludes by calling for more research, greater awareness among relevant networks, leadership initiatives, dissemination of best practices, partnerships between Men’s Sheds and arts organizations, and targeted funding to address this underrepresentation.
