## FXA202 The Photo Essay: Storytelling with Image and Text

This lesson on photo essays explains the role of captions in enhancing visual storytelling. Captions are short texts placed near images to provide context and answer basic questions about who, what, where, when, and why. They help clarify and contextualize photographs, especially for readers unfamiliar with the scene depicted. The lesson emphasizes the importance of writing complete sentences, choosing between past or present tense based on the image's content, using active voice, avoiding obvious statements, and employing powerful verbs over adjectives. Tips include bringing a notebook to document details while shooting photos and considering creative approaches beyond informative text.
