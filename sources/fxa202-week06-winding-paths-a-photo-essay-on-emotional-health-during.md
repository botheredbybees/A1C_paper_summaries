## Winding paths: A Photo Essay on Emotional Health During Lockdown

Corinne Doran's photo essay 'Winding Paths' explores the personal impact of Greater Sydney’s second COVID-19 lockdown from July to September 2021. The essay reflects on how daily life adjustments due to stay-at-home orders affected emotional health over a three-month period. It highlights the concept of emotional wellbeing as an individual's capacity to manage feelings through challenge and change, emphasizing the negative impacts of prolonged pandemic stress. Doran captures moments of tension such as monitoring news updates and engaging in impulsive behaviors like phone scrolling. To mitigate these stresses, she adopts new routines and creative activities that promote mindfulness and flow states, which are essential for reducing worry and enhancing emotional awareness. Her photo essay includes images of her weaving practice, documenting how the repetitive motions involved contribute to a meditative state and help manage anxiety. The use of smartphone photography is described as facilitating an intimate relationship with the moment being captured. Doran also explores mindful photo walks in her neighborhood, connecting natural elements observed during these walks to her creative process at home. The essay concludes by emphasizing the benefits of visual storytelling for emotional health during a time of societal change and isolation, affirming that self-care through creativity can enhance readiness to support others.
