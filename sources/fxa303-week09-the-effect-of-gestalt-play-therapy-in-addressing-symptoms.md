## The effect of Gestalt play therapy in addressing symptoms associated with trauma in children in middle childhood

This mini-dissertation explores the impact of Gestalt play therapy on reducing trauma-related symptoms in middle-childhood-aged children. The study highlights that childhood trauma significantly hampers social, biological, psychological, and emotional functioning, impairing healthy self-regulation. Traditional therapies have shown limited success due to a lack of understanding regarding neurobiological principles. Therefore, the research aimed to investigate the efficacy of Gestalt play therapy in addressing these symptoms.

A mixed-methods approach was utilized, incorporating single-system design and phenomenological design simultaneously. Data were collected from five participants aged 9-11 years through structured and semi-structured interviews at three points during the study period. The findings indicated that trauma-related symptoms such as affect and behavior dysregulation, alterations in attention and consciousness, distortions in attribution and worldview, and interpersonal difficulties were prevalent among participants.

After undergoing Gestalt play therapy, there was a statistically significant improvement noted in the overall prevalence of these symptoms. This suggests that the therapeutic intervention is effective for addressing trauma-related issues in this age group, likely due to its sensory-based approach and use of non-verbal and creative techniques. The study concludes by recommending trauma-informed practices in therapeutic settings involving middle-childhood-aged children, emphasizing the importance of safe and accepting relationships.
