## Hearing What the Body Feels: Auditory Encoding of Rhythmic Movement

This study investigates the relationship between body movement and auditory perception of rhythm in adults, building on previous research with infants that showed how physical movements can influence musical rhythm perception. The authors trained participants to bounce their knees to music with no accented beats while interpreting it either as a march (bouncing every second beat) or a waltz (every third beat). Subsequent tests revealed that this movement influenced their auditory interpretation of the rhythm's metrical structure, aligning with the bouncing experience. The study also confirmed that visual cues were not necessary for this effect, but body movement was crucial. These findings suggest an early development and lifelong significance of cross-modal interaction between bodily movement and auditory perception in musical contexts.
