## The Cochrane Collaboration’s tool for assessing risk of bias in randomised trials

The Cochrane Collaboration’s tool for assessing risk of bias in randomized trials aims to enhance the clarity and accuracy of evaluating trial quality, addressing issues such as underestimation or overestimation of intervention effects due to various biases. The paper outlines a systematic approach developed by a multi-disciplinary team, focusing on seven key areas: allocation sequence generation, concealment, blinding, attrition, other generic sources, trial design-specific biases, and clinical specialty-specific biases. This framework assesses potential for bias in six domains (selection, performance, detection, attrition, reporting, and other) using detailed criteria provided in the Cochrane Handbook. The assessment includes both a descriptive summary of relevant trial characteristics and a judgment on high, low, or unclear risk of material bias for each item. This tool has been evaluated through focus groups and surveys to refine its application across various contexts within the Cochrane Collaboration.
