## Stream of Consciousness

Stream of consciousness is a literary technique that captures the natural flow of a character's thoughts and experiences. This style, associated with early 20th-century Modernist literature, often employs unconventional syntax, grammar, and punctuation to reflect the chaotic nature of human thought processes. It allows readers to experience a character’s internal world through associative leaps, sensory impressions, and repetitive motifs. Notable examples include Virginia Woolf's 'Mrs. Dalloway' and Toni Morrison's 'Beloved.' This narrative technique is significant in creative arts and health studies as it provides insight into the complex inner workings of individuals, mirroring mental states and emotional landscapes.
