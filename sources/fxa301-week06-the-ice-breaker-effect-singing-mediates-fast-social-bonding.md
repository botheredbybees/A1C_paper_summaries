## The ice-breaker effect: singing mediates fast social bonding

The study explores the role of group singing in facilitating social cohesion among strangers, comparing it with other activities like crafts or creative writing over a period of seven months. The research indicates that singing significantly accelerates the formation of close relationships within groups compared to non-singing activities. Participants reported feeling more connected immediately after singing sessions and experienced an increase in positive affect. Additionally, a proxy measure of endorphin release showed higher levels among singers. This suggests that singing may have evolved as a mechanism for rapid social bonding, enhancing group cohesion through synchronous activity and emotional synchrony.
