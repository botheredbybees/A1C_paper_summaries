## Adolescence as a Sensitive Period of Brain Development

This article explores the hypothesis that adolescence may represent a sensitive period of brain development, characterized by heightened neuroplasticity and vulnerability. The authors discuss three candidate areas for this hypothesis: memory, social stress effects, and drug use. They review evidence from rodent studies, neuroimaging, and large-scale behavioral studies in humans, which suggest increased neural plasticity during adolescence compared to childhood or adulthood. However, the article notes a lack of definitive empirical evidence demonstrating sensitive periods specifically in adolescent development. The authors emphasize that experimental studies directly manipulating environmental inputs are needed to establish conclusive evidence for heightened neuroplasticity in this period. This research is relevant to understanding mental well-being and potential interventions during adolescence.
