## Neurophysiological Effects of Trait Empathy in Music Listening

This paper explores the relationship between trait empathy and neural responses to musical sounds. The authors conducted two functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) experiments where participants listened to brief isolated musical timbres and excerpts of music in different conditions (familiar liked, disliked; unfamiliar liked, disliked). They found that emotional and cognitive forms of trait empathy modulated activity in sensorimotor and cognitive areas of the brain. In Experiment 1, increased activity was observed in the supplementary motor area (SMA), inferior frontal gyrus (IFG) and insula regions associated with empathy during isolated timbre listening. In Experiment 2, more activation occurred in prefrontal, temporo-parietal, and reward areas when participants listened to music excerpts. These findings suggest a complex interplay between bottom-up and top-down mechanisms of empathy when responding to musical sounds.
