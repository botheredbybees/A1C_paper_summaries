## Journalling Your Creative Process

This lesson explains the importance of a creative journal as a tool for documenting one's creative process. It highlights that a creative journal should be purposeful, disciplined, documentary, speculative, experimental, forward-thinking, self-reflective, and evaluative. The journal can take various forms including traditional notebooks or digital formats and must include elements like drawings, photographs, research notes, and reflections on progress. Examples of student journals illustrate how they document idea development, experimentation, technical information, and personal reflections to support creativity.
