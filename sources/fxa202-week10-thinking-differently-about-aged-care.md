## Thinking differently about aged care

Evonne Miller's chapter 'Thinking differently about aged care' delves into the transformative potential of creative arts-based research methods within the context of residential aged care. The author challenges conventional views by emphasizing the importance of resident voices and experiences, particularly through photovoice and documentary photography techniques. By focusing on these methodologies, Miller aims to provide a nuanced portrayal of life in aged care facilities, highlighting both positive and challenging aspects. She argues for a reimagining of aged care spaces as vibrant environments that foster community engagement and personal fulfillment rather than mere places of decline or isolation.

Miller underscores the significance of valuing frontline staff who often engage deeply with residents, offering compassionate support despite the challenges inherent in their work. The chapter also discusses the need to rethink physical designs and incorporate biophilic elements to create more inviting living spaces for older adults. Additionally, it advocates for greater public discourse on end-of-life care within aged care settings, encouraging readers to consider how they would like to live out their final years.

Overall, Miller's work is a call for innovative approaches that prioritize the holistic well-being of elderly residents and the recognition of aged care as an integral part of society’s healthcare infrastructure.
