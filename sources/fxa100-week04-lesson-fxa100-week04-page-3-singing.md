## Singing and the Brain in Dementia Care

This course material discusses the benefits of singing for individuals with dementia. It highlights how singing can enhance mood, foster group identity, and improve cognitive function in those with moderate to severe dementia. The content also delves into research on the neurological effects of singing, noting that it activates various brain regions such as the visual cortex, auditory cortex, hippocampus, and cerebellum. Additionally, the module covers studies demonstrating improved breathing control and respiratory health among patients with emphysema and those living in aged care facilities through singing programs. It also touches on the role of singing in speech therapy for conditions like Parkinson's disease and as a therapeutic intervention for people with advanced dementia.
