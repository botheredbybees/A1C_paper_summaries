## The Story of Ghetto Classics: An Orchestra Born from a Kenya Garbage Dump

Ghetto Classics is a youth orchestra based in Nairobi’s Korogocho neighborhood, which borders Kenya's largest rubbish dump, Dandora. The group was founded by Elizabeth Njoroge and Father Chris Mueni of St. John's Parish to provide music education to underprivileged children. Over the years, Ghetto Classics has gained international recognition, performing for notable figures such as former President Barack Obama, Pope Francis, and First Lady Jill Biden. Despite their challenging environment, members find solace and empowerment through music, which also helps them avoid negative influences like drug dealing and alcoholism. The orchestra’s story is documented in Ginanne Brownell's book 'Ghetto Classics: How a Youth Orchestra Changed a Nairobi Slum.' This initiative not only offers musical education but also fosters community engagement and resilience, highlighting the transformative power of arts in addressing socio-economic challenges.
