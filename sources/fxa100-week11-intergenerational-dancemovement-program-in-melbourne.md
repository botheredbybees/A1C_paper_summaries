## Intergenerational Dance/Movement Program in Melbourne, Australia

Heather Hill, a dance therapist in Victoria, Australia, developed an intergenerational dance/movement program that brought together older adults from a day center and children aged six to seven years old for nine weeks. The program aimed to foster relationships between generations through creative movement activities such as expressive exploration, singing, dramatic play, and folk dancing. Activities were structured to encourage participants to engage with each other on an individual level rather than stereotypical age-based roles. Over time, the group's dynamics evolved, allowing spontaneous creativity without much facilitation. The program culminated in a celebratory event where certificates of participation were awarded to the children. This intergenerational engagement provided developmental benefits for both children and adults by allowing them to experience generative roles and build trust through collaborative tasks. The study highlights that meaningful intergenerational experiences rely on fostering individual relationships, mutual interaction, and shared satisfaction from cooperative activities.
