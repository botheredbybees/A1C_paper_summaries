## Introduction: Art Therapy and Creative Aging

The introduction chapter of 'Art Therapy and Creative Aging' by Raquel Chapin Stephenson explores the author's personal journey into working with older adults. She reflects on her grandmother's skepticism about choosing this career path, emphasizing the inherent value in engaging with elders despite societal biases against aging. The text underscores the joy derived from witnessing clients experience comfort and happiness through creative arts therapy, which motivates Stephenson to inspire new therapists to work with older people. Stephenson discusses the concept of 'elderhood' as a distinct developmental stage following childhood and adulthood, highlighting growth rather than just biological decline in later life. She aims to reclaim this perspective on aging by reframing it as a time for continued development and well-being. Additionally, the chapter acknowledges the limitations of her approach due to cultural and geographical influences, encouraging readers to apply the insights with awareness towards diverse populations.
