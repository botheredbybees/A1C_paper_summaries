## Art Is the Evidence: Convincing Public Communication of Art-Based Research and Its Outcomes

This chapter discusses the presentation of art as evidence for improving well-being, focusing on personal experiences from 1972 when McNiff presented artwork by patients from an art therapy program at Danvers State Hospital. The exhibition convinced both a museum and institutional decision-makers about the therapeutic value of art-based research, emphasizing that artworks can speak directly to audiences without needing translation into scientific terms. It highlights the direct impact of such presentations in promoting arts in health and education.
