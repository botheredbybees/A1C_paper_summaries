## The neural basis of humour processing

This article explores the neural basis of humour in humans and its role in socio-affective functioning and cognitive processes. Humour is shown to engage a network of cortical and subcortical brain structures, including areas involved in detecting and resolving incongruity and reward processing. The authors discuss how individual differences influence humour perception and propose that neuroscientific studies can provide insights into typical human behavior and the mechanisms underlying neuropsychiatric disorders affecting social reward experiences.
