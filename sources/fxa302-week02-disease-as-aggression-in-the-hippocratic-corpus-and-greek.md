## Disease as Aggression in the Hippocratic Corpus and Greek Tragedy: Wild and Devouring Disease

Jacques Jouanna explores the contrasting yet overlapping representations of disease in ancient Greek literature, specifically within the Hippocratic Corpus and Greek tragedy. The chapter highlights how traditional medical texts contrast with tragic depictions by rationalizing disease's origins while tragedies often personify it as a forceful and destructive entity. Despite this divergence, Jouanna uncovers common linguistic elements where both traditions use metaphors depicting diseases as aggressive forces that invade and consume individuals, reflecting an older conceptual framework of disease representation. This shared metaphorical language suggests a persistent influence of pre-Hippocratic views in medical discourse, even after the establishment of more rational explanatory models.
