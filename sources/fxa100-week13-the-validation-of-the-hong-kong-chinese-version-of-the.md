## The Validation of the Hong Kong Chinese Version of the Lawton Instrumental Activities of Daily Living Scale for Institutionalized Elderly Persons

This study validates the Lawton Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADL) Scale in its Hong Kong Chinese version for use with elderly individuals living in institutions. The research aimed to assess the content validity, construct validity, and reliability of this scale among a sample of 155 institutionalized older adults from different facilities. Key findings indicate that all but one item ('handyman work') were culturally relevant, and refinements were made to improve scoring criteria and rating scales based on local cultural considerations. The study found high inter-rater reliability (ICC = 0.99), test-retest reliability (ICC = 0.90), and internal consistency (Cronbach's alpha = 0.86). Factor analysis confirmed the scale’s construct validity, showing that one factor was extracted from nine items of the IADL-CV scale. The known-groups method also demonstrated its effectiveness in discriminating between residents of different living institutions with a prediction accuracy of 78%. Overall, the study concludes that the Lawton IADL-Chinese version is a reliable and valid instrument for assessing the IADL abilities of elderly individuals to live independently in their communities.
