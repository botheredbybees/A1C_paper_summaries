## Anterograde Memory in Frontotemporal Dementia

The document explores anterograde memory in frontotemporal dementia (FTD), focusing on the behavioral variant FTD (bvFTD) and semantic dementia (SD). It discusses how patients with bvFTD may exhibit episodic memory deficits that are comparable to those found in Alzheimer's disease, despite amnesia being an exclusion criterion for bvFTD. The study highlights inconsistencies across research due to varying inclusion criteria of nonprogressive cases, which can mask the true extent of memory impairments seen in progressive FTD patients. Key findings reveal that progressive bvFTD and AD show similar levels of verbal and visual episodic memory impairment, while non-progressor cases exhibit only mild deficits. The role of frontal cortex atrophy, particularly in the orbitofrontal region, is emphasized as a critical factor influencing episodic memory performance. This research underscores the importance of distinguishing between progressive and non-progressive bvFTD for accurate diagnosis and understanding the underlying neuropathology associated with varying degrees of anterograde memory impairment.
