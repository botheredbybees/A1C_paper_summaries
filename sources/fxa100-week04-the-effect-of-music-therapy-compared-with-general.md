## The effect of music therapy compared with general recreational activities in reducing agitation in people with dementia: a randomised controlled trial

This study compares the effects of music therapy with general recreational activities in reducing agitation for individuals with dementia living in nursing homes. The research aimed to assess whether music therapy provides additional benefits over standard recreational activities. A total of 77 participants were randomly assigned to either a music therapy or general activity group and observed over four months. Both interventions resulted in short-term decreases in agitated behavior, but no statistically significant difference was found between the two groups. The study concluded that while both methods offer temporary relief from agitation, there is no evidence to suggest that music therapy has an extra benefit over standard recreational activities for reducing agitation among people with dementia.
