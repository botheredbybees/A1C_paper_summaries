## A 1-Year Randomized Controlled Trial Comparing Mind Body Exercise (Tai Chi) With Stretching and Toning Exercise on Cognitive Function in Older Chinese Adults at Risk of Cognitive Decline

The study examines the effectiveness of Tai Chi versus stretching and toning exercises in preserving cognitive function among older Chinese adults at risk of cognitive decline. It involves a one-year randomized controlled trial with participants divided into two groups: those practicing Tai Chi and those engaging in stretching and toning exercise. The results indicate that the Tai Chi group showed trends towards reduced risk of developing dementia, better preservation of CDR sum of boxes scores, and improvements in delay recall and depression measures compared to the control group. These findings suggest potential benefits of mind-body exercises like Tai Chi for cognitive health maintenance.
