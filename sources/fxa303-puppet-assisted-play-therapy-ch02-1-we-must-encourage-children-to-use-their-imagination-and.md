## Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy for Enhancing Creativity and Conflict Resolution in Children

This chapter discusses the use of puppetry in enhancing creativity and conflict resolution among children through play therapy. It highlights empirical evidence supporting the benefits of creative activities for child development, such as improved problem-solving skills and emotional well-being. The author presents a case study where she administered the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking to assess changes in students' creative thinking before and after implementing a creativity enhancement curriculum. Puppetry is also explored as an effective tool within Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for teaching various strategies, including conflict resolution, problem-solving techniques, breathing exercises, and imagery. The chapter emphasizes how puppet-assisted play therapy can help children adopt new social roles and improve their self-concept.
