## Can Traumatic Stress Alter the Brain? Understanding the Implications of Early Trauma on Brain Development and Learning

This review article discusses the potential impact of traumatic stress on brain development, particularly focusing on its implications for learning and mental well-being in youth. The authors summarize findings from studies presented at a National Summit on Stress and the Brain, emphasizing structural and functional imaging research involving the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex (PFC) in children with post-traumatic stress symptoms (PTSS). Research indicates that high cortisol levels are associated with reduced volume in these brain regions and impaired cognitive functions such as memory processing and executive function. The article highlights the neurobiological mechanisms underlying PTSS, suggesting a link between chronic traumatic stress and detrimental changes in neural circuitry. These findings have significant public health implications, especially concerning educational outcomes for children exposed to trauma. Effective interventions should aim to restore normal functioning of frontolimbic networks, enhancing learning capabilities among vulnerable youth.
