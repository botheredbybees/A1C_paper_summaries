## Creative Arts-Based Research in Aged Care: Photovoice, Photography and Poetry

In the preface of 'Creative Arts-Based Research in Aged Care: Photovoice, Photography and Poetry', author Evonne Miller introduces her research project titled Inside Aged Care. The study, conducted from 2014 to 2017, explores the daily lives of residents living in an aged care facility in Brisbane, Australia, using creative arts-based methods such as photovoice, documentary photography, and poetry. Over four years, Miller engaged with more than 100 participants to document their experiences of entering, living in, and dying in aged care. The book aims to share rare and intimate insights into these often-private lives through the use of poems and photographs taken by residents, staff, and researchers. It targets a broad audience including practitioners, policymakers, designers, and students interested in understanding and improving aged care experiences. By minimizing jargon, Miller ensures that her work is accessible across various disciplines. The book highlights the communicative and emotive power of creative arts-based research methods in addressing issues related to aging and health services.
