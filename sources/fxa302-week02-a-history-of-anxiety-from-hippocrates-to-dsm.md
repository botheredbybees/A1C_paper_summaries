## A history of anxiety: from Hippocrates to DSM

The article traces the historical development of anxiety as a distinct medical disorder from ancient Greece and Rome to modern psychiatric classifications, emphasizing that anxiety was recognized and treated by philosophers like Cicero and Seneca in antiquity. It details how Hippocratic texts identified phobias as medical conditions, while Stoic and Epicurean philosophies offered cognitive strategies to manage anxiety, anticipating contemporary approaches such as mindfulness meditation. The author highlights the continuity of understanding and treatment over centuries, challenging the notion that anxiety disorders are a recent construct. This historical perspective is crucial for understanding current classifications like DSM-5 and their evolution in diagnosing and treating mental health conditions.
