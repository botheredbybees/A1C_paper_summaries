## Arts, Health, and Well-Being in America

The white paper 'Arts, Health, and Well-Being in America,' commissioned by the National Organization for Arts in Health through support from Houston Methodist Center for Performing Arts Medicine, explores how the arts can enhance healthcare, public health, and community well-being. It outlines diverse artistic approaches including design to create uplifting environments, arts use in training health professionals, performing and visual arts for patient care, and interventions for aging and trauma response. The paper emphasizes that engaging creative experiences are vital to human health and well-being, bridging societal divides and enhancing the understanding of the human condition. By detailing various initiatives from hospital art exhibits to dance workshops for Parkinson's patients, it highlights how professionals across different fields integrate arts into healthcare services. Recommendations are provided in six distinct sections addressing different areas such as clinical environments, patient experience, and caregiver wellness. The document aims to unite the community around the belief in 'the power of the arts to foster health and wellness,' promoting collaboration and professionalism.
