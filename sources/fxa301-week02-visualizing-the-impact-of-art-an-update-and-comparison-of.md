## Visualizing the Impact of Art: An Update and Comparison of Current Psychological Models of Art Experience

This paper presents a comprehensive review and comparison of six influential cognitive models that aim to explain the psychological processes underlying art experience. The authors discuss how these models have evolved over recent years, driven by an increasing emphasis on empirical methods and interdisciplinary approaches in the study of aesthetics. Each model is redesigned using a unified visual framework to facilitate easier comparisons and highlight commonalities or differences. This standardized format allows researchers to better understand various aspects such as emotional responses, physiological reactions, and cognitive appraisals associated with art viewing. The review also highlights key theoretical implications for future research in the field of psychological aesthetics, emphasizing the importance of a clear visual representation for hypothesis formulation and empirical investigation.
