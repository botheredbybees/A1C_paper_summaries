## Arts Therapies Interventions and Their Outcomes in the Treatment of Eating Disorders: Scoping Review Protocol

This paper presents the protocol for a scoping review aimed at exploring arts therapies (AsTs) interventions and their outcomes in treating individuals with eating disorders (EDs). The study aims to evaluate various types of AsTs, including art therapy, drama therapy, music therapy, dance/movement therapy, and expressive therapies. It will analyze these interventions across different research designs, settings, intervention methods/materials, adverse effects reported, and therapeutic outcomes. The review methodology follows the Joanna Briggs Institute framework, incorporating multiple databases for a comprehensive literature search. By assessing the effectiveness and comparative benefits of AsTs in treating EDs, this study seeks to provide valuable insights into healthcare policies and treatment options for individuals suffering from these disorders.
