## Evaluation of Differences in Temporal Synchrony Between Brain Regions in Individuals With Autism and Typical Development

This study investigates the temporal dynamics of functional brain connectivity in individuals with autism compared to those with typical development, focusing on sustained and lagged temporal synchrony between brain regions. The researchers used multiband, multiecho functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data from 90 adolescent and adult males, including a replication sample of 1402 participants from the Autism Brain Imaging Data Exchange (ABIDE). Key findings indicate that individuals with autism exhibit prolonged temporal synchrony in their brain connectivity compared to controls. This increased duration was associated with symptom severity metrics but negatively correlated with cognitive processing speed in typically developing individuals. The study suggests that persistent brain connectivity may limit rapid shifts between neural states, contributing to the pathophysiology of autism.
