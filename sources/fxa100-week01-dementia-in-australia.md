## Dementia in Australia

This online report, published by the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, offers a comprehensive overview of dementia in Australia. It highlights that approximately 425,000 Australians were living with dementia in 2024 and projects over one million people will have dementia by 2065. Dementia is also identified as the leading cause of death in Australia in 2023, accounting for nearly 17,400 deaths or 9.5% of all deaths that year.

The report discusses key findings such as risk factors, including modifiable and non-modifiable ones, which influence dementia prevalence. It emphasizes the importance of lifestyle choices like physical activity, social engagement, and cognitive stimulation in reducing the likelihood of developing dementia. Additionally, it outlines health behaviors such as smoking, obesity, alcohol consumption, high blood pressure, untreated hearing loss, and depression that increase risk.

The report also covers awareness about dementia among Australians, revealing a general lack of knowledge but widespread adoption of protective behaviors to mitigate risks. Furthermore, it mentions the National Dementia Action Plan 2024-2034 aimed at improving care and services for people with dementia and their families. The content underscores the growing health and aged care challenge posed by dementia in Australia.
