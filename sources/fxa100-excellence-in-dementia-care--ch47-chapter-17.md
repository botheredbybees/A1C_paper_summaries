## Embodied Selfhood and Person-Centred Dementia Care

This chapter discusses the concept of embodied selfhood as it relates to person-centred care for individuals with Alzheimer's disease and other dementias. It examines how social history, acquired through socialization, is expressed through bodily movements and gestures even when cognitive faculties are impaired. The authors highlight instances where residents in dementia care units continue to demonstrate adherence to social conventions and etiquette despite their condition, emphasizing the importance of recognizing these expressions as meaningful self-expression rather than symptoms of illness. They argue that understanding embodied selfhood can improve quality of care by reducing reliance on pharmacological interventions or other forms of restraint.
