## Creative Health Review Report

The 'Creative Health Review' report by the All-Party Parliamentary Group on Arts, Health and Wellbeing and the National Centre for Creative Health aims to highlight the potential of creative activities in improving public health outcomes. The document presents a comprehensive review that includes contributions from individuals who have experienced the benefits of creative health interventions firsthand. It discusses how creativity can contribute significantly to mental and physical well-being across various stages of life, particularly within the context of chronic illness management and social determinants of health.

The report emphasizes that while modern medicine is crucial for transforming health outcomes, there are substantial opportunities for arts, culture, sports, and nature to complement these services in delivering holistic improvements. It argues that creative interventions not only enhance individual well-being but also reduce financial burdens on the healthcare system by addressing social determinants of health.

The review outlines several policy recommendations aimed at integrating creative health into national strategies through local government and arts organizations. These recommendations focus on developing a universal service provision for creative health across integrated care systems, recognizing creativity as an essential component in preventive healthcare approaches.

Overall, the report underscores the necessity for policymakers to consider creative health seriously within mainstream health and care policies, advocating for its inclusion alongside traditional medical interventions to improve overall health outcomes and reduce societal disparities.
