## How are Health Behaviours Associated with Mental Wellbeing Using the Short Warwick Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (SWEMWBS)? An Evaluation of SWEMWBS Using the Health Survey for England

This study evaluates the Short Warwick Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale (SWEMWBS) using data from the Health Survey for England to determine its reliability and validity as a measure of mental wellbeing in adults aged 16 years and older. The research aimed to compute age- and sex-specific norms for SWEMWBS scores and examine the scale's criterion and relative validity.

The study found that mean (median) SWEMWBS scores were statistically significant between men and women, with correlations moderately sized for happiness index, GHQ-12, EQ-5D. Spearman correlations showed weaker associations with self-rated health and longstanding illness. Key demographic variables such as general health, marital status, ethnicity, education, income, fruit and vegetable intake, alcohol consumption on the heaviest drinking day, and smoking status were associated with low versus medium wellbeing.

The research concluded that SWEMWBS distinguishes mental wellbeing between groups similarly to WEMWBS, making it a useful tool for measuring mental health where there are time and resource constraints.
