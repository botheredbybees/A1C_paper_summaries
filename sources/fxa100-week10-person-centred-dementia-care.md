## Person-Centred Dementia Care

The document discusses the VIPS Framework, a tool designed to improve person-centred care for people living with dementia. The framework consists of four core elements: Values (V), Individualization (I), Personal Perspective (P), and Social Support Environment (S). Each element is broken down into specific indicators that assess various aspects of care provision. For instance, the V element includes indicators such as Vision, Human Resources, Management Ethos, Training and Practice Development, Service Environments, and Quality Assurance. The I element focuses on Individual Lives by addressing individual support, regular reviews, personal possessions, preferences, life stories, and daily activities. P examines communication skills, empathy, physical environment awareness, health needs response, challenging behavior understanding, and advocacy. Finally, S assesses inclusion, respect, warmth, validation, enabling independence, community connections, and relationship management. The framework aims to help care providers evaluate their services and implement quality improvement plans through a reflective process.
