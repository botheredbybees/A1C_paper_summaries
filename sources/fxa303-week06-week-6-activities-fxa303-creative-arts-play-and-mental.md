## Week 6 Activities - FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing

This document outlines activities for week six of FXA303, focusing on the use of puppetry in therapeutic contexts to help children externalize emotions and enhance emotional regulation. The required reading is Carter's chapter from 'Puppet Play Therapy: A Practical Guidebook', which discusses how puppets can symbolically represent clients during therapy sessions. The document also includes a discussion prompt encouraging students to explore basic techniques for making expressive puppets and to analyze the gaze and eye design of existing puppets, highlighting their emotional implications. References provided include studies on puppetry's efficacy in addressing behavioral issues, aiding children from immigrant backgrounds, reducing hallucinations in schizophrenia patients, improving attention to social cues in autistic children, and facilitating therapeutic interactions in pediatric settings.
