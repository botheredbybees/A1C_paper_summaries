## Development of a Novel Benchmark Method to Identify and Characterize Best Practices in Home Care Across Six European Countries

The 'Identifying best practices for care-dependent elderly by Benchmarking Costs and outcomes of Community Care' (IBenC) project aims to develop a comprehensive benchmark method to identify high-quality home care practices across Europe. This research involved six European countries—Belgium, Finland, Germany, Iceland, Italy, and the Netherlands—and included data collection from 38 home care organizations, 2884 clients aged over 65 years, and 1067 professionals. The study design utilized a longitudinal approach for client assessments and cross-sectional surveys for organizational and professional characteristics. Key findings revealed substantial differences across countries in terms of demographics, care needs, and workforce profiles. By developing this benchmark method based on quality and cost indicators derived from the interRAI Home Care instrument, the project aims to inform health care policy decisions towards sustainable long-term care solutions.
