## Earliest Music Instruments Found

Researchers have discovered the oldest known musical instruments in a cave in southern Germany. The flutes, made from bird bone and mammoth ivory, date back between 42,000 and 43,000 years ago according to carbon dating. These findings were published in the Journal of Human Evolution. Prof Nick Conard, who excavated the site at Geissenkloesterle Cave, suggests that these instruments signify early human migration along the Danube River into Europe during a period when Neanderthals still existed but later went extinct around 30,000 years ago. Some experts theorize that music played a role in social cohesion and potentially contributed to modern humans’ competitive advantage over Neanderthals.
