## From helplessness to a renewed sense of agency: The integration of puppets in the Art & Storytelling school-based creative expression program with immigrant and refugee children

This article explores the use of puppetry in a school-based creative expression program designed to support immigrant and refugee children, particularly focusing on a case study involving Mounia, a seven-year-old Syrian refugee girl. The intervention aims to help children regain a sense of agency and control amid the challenges posed by migration and the COVID-19 pandemic. Through her engagement with puppetry during workshops, Mounia demonstrates how creative processes can contribute to emotional well-being and resilience. The study highlights that creativity acts as a protective buffer for children dealing with stress and adversity, supporting their adjustment and fostering coping mechanisms. By integrating puppets into her creative process, Mounia shows an enhanced ability to express herself, navigate difficult emotions, and regain control over her life narrative. This case underscores the importance of school-based interventions in providing psychological support and promoting mental health among vulnerable populations.
