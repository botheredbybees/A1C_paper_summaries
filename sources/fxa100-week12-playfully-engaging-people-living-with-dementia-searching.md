## Playfully engaging people living with dementia: searching for Yum Cha moments

The article explores the use of playful engagement through relational clowning to improve quality of life and reduce social isolation among individuals with mid-to-late-stage dementia. Researchers from Griffith University, in partnership with Wesley Mission Brisbane, conducted a pilot phase focusing on interactions between trained theatre artists and residents with dementia at aged care facilities. The study highlights how specific play vocabularies and tailored approaches can generate moments of meaningful connection and mutual recognition. For instance, during visits, the artists used linguistic and cultural references relevant to individual interests, such as Cantonese language for Tom, a resident from Hong Kong. This method fostered engagement by tapping into personal histories and preferences, significantly impacting participants' emotional states and interaction levels. The research emphasizes the importance of reflexive application of playful strategies based on real-time observations and participant responses.
