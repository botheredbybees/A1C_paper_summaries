## Physical activity and exercise as countermeasures to physical frailty and sarcopenia

This review article discusses the role of physical activity and exercise in combating sarcopenia and physical frailty among older adults. The study highlights that a sedentary lifestyle is a major contributor to muscle weakness, loss of muscle mass, and strength decline with age. In contrast, regular engagement in physical activities can effectively mitigate these conditions by improving skeletal muscle health through various mechanisms such as increased satellite cell activity, enhanced mitochondrial function, improved insulin sensitivity, and reduced chronic inflammation.

The article presents evidence from several randomized controlled trials that demonstrate the benefits of different types of exercise interventions—such as resistance training and combined aerobic/resistance programs—in enhancing muscle mass, strength, and physical performance among older adults. These findings underscore the importance of regular physical activity in maintaining robustness and preventing disability associated with aging.
