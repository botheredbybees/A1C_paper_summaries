## Music and Social Bonding: “Self-Other” Merging and Neurohormonal Mechanisms

This paper explores the mechanisms by which music fosters social bonding among individuals in group settings. The authors examine two primary pathways: self-other merging through interpersonal synchrony and the release of endorphins during exertive, rhythmic activities like dancing or singing. Synchrony is argued to blur boundaries between individuals due to shared neural pathways for action and perception, leading to increased social closeness. Endorphin release, triggered by synchronized movements and physical activity, enhances prosocial behaviors and trust. The paper suggests that both mechanisms are crucial in understanding how music contributes to social cohesion across cultures.
