## CALE Research Aged Care Deloraine Partnership - University of Tasmania

This transcript captures the discussion of a research project aimed at improving aged care through music and singing programs. The study sought to evaluate the impact of a singing program on residents' well-being and respiratory function in an aged care facility. Innovative use of kazoos was introduced as a non-invasive method for data collection, engaging participants without invasive medical equipment. The partnership emphasized the importance of making residents feel valued and purposeful through active participation in music activities. Future plans include expanding the project to benefit more people across Tasmania, integrating arts engagement into care practices to enhance quality of life.
