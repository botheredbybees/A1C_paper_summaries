## The role of music in adolescent development: much more than the same old song

This article explores the significant role of music in adolescent development, emphasizing its importance beyond mere entertainment. The author argues that music influences various aspects of adolescent psychosocial and biological growth. Citing recent empirical research, Miranda discusses three key points: the impact of music on developmental factors, its potential as a protective or risk factor, and its utility in prevention and intervention strategies. He highlights how music serves as an emotional outlet and social lubricant, impacting interpersonal relationships and identity formation. Additionally, Miranda notes that despite extensive literature on the topic, this information is not widely recognized within developmental psychology due to limited cross-disciplinary communication and publication trends.
