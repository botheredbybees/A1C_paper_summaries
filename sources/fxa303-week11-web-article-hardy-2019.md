## Queering Queer Spaces: Journey of a Creative Arts Program for Trans, Non-Binary, and Gender Creative Youth

This article explores the development and implementation of a creative arts program for transgender, non-binary, gender-creative youth aged 10 to 14 at the Utah Pride Center in Salt Lake City, USA. The authors, Spencer Hardy (music therapist) and Juniper Monypenny (art therapist), discuss their approach of 'queering' traditional mental health services by incorporating community-based strategies that prioritize safety, inclusivity, and empowerment. They highlight the importance of anti-oppressive practices to minimize power dynamics and embrace intersectionality within queer spaces. The article presents an abstract summarizing youth experiences, outlines a therapeutic framework based on co-tinkering with ideas and stories, and emphasizes the significance of leveraging music and art as tools for self-discovery and emotional safety.
