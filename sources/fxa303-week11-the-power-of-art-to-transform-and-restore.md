## The Power of Art to Transform and Restore

Karen Miner-Romanoff's paper 'Voices from Inside: The Power of Art to Transform and Restore' discusses an ongoing study on a promising art program for incarcerated adolescents in Ohio. The program, called Voices from Inside, involves community exhibits and charitable sales of the youths’ artwork. Survey results indicate significant benefits for the youth participants, including increased self-esteem, cooperation with others, task completion skills, decreased stress, and recognition of their abilities. Community attendees also reported positive attitude changes towards juvenile offenders, recognizing their humanity and value to society. The author argues that art programs can contribute to rehabilitation and restorative justice by fostering personal growth, social bonds, and motivation among incarcerated youth. Additionally, the study highlights the need for further research to determine the extent of these programs' impact on reducing behavioral incidents within correctional facilities.
