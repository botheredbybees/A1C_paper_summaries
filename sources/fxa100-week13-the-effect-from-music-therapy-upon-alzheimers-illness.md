## The Effect from Music Therapy Upon Alzheimer's Illness

The study explores the effectiveness of music therapy in managing symptoms and enhancing quality of life for individuals with Alzheimer's disease. Music therapy is recognized as a holistic approach that addresses physical, mental, and emotional needs through various activities such as singing, playing instruments, composing, dancing, and listening to music. The research reviewed 30 articles published between 2012 and 2020 and found consistent evidence supporting the use of music therapy in reducing anxiety, depression, pain, fatigue, and cognitive decline among Alzheimer's patients. Music interventions were also observed to improve social interaction, enhance memory recall, and provide a sense of empowerment and emotional well-being for individuals suffering from this progressive neurological disorder. The findings suggest that music therapy can serve as an effective complementary treatment option in healthcare settings.
