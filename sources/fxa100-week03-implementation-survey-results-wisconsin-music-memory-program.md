## Implementation Survey Results - Wisconsin Music & Memory Program

The document presents the implementation survey results for the Wisconsin Music & Memory Program conducted in two phases. Phase I was initiated in July 2014, with 100 out of 100 respondents completing the survey, while Phase II involved 144 out of 148 participants in August 2015. The surveys assess various aspects including program progress stages, number of songs curated for residents' personalized playlists, and improvements observed among residents such as reduced anxiety and better engagement due to music therapy.

Key findings reveal significant positive impacts on resident well-being; many respondents reported that their residents had shown marked improvement in calmness and happiness after implementing the Music & Memory program. Additionally, there were reductions in anti-psychotic medication usage and reliance on bed alarms among participating residents. The surveys also highlight the expansion of the program across multiple facilities and the positive feedback received from families, friends, and staff.

The results underscore the relevance of music therapy as a non-pharmacological intervention for improving quality of life in dementia care settings, aligning well with creative arts and health practices by emphasizing personalization and emotional connection through familiar musical experiences.
