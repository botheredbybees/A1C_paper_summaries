## Age-Related Effects and Sex Differences in Gray Matter Density, Volume, Mass, and Cortical Thickness from Childhood to Young Adulthood

This study explores age-related effects and sex differences in gray matter density (GMD), volume (GMV), mass (GMM), and cortical thickness (CT) from childhood to young adulthood using MRI data from the Philadelphia Neurodevelopmental Cohort. The research reveals that while GMV and CT generally decrease with age, GMD increases significantly, contrary to previous assumptions. Females exhibit lower GMV but higher GMD compared to males throughout development. These findings highlight the importance of considering multiple structural measures in understanding brain development and suggest that periadolescent gray matter loss may be less pronounced than previously believed.
