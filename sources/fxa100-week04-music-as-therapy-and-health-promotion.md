## Music as Therapy and Health Promotion

Chapter 12 of 'Music as Therapy and Health Promotion' explores the integration of music therapy with neuroscience, highlighting how scientific studies have transformed our understanding of music's therapeutic potential. Historically, music therapy focused on social science models to facilitate emotional wellbeing and social interaction. However, advancements in neuroimaging techniques since the early 1990s have led to a new clinical model based on neurological research. This chapter discusses cortical engagement during musical activities, motor synchronization with rhythm, and rehabilitative gains from engaging with music for conditions like Parkinson's disease, stroke, and multiple sclerosis. It emphasizes that engaging in music can promote changes in the brain’s cortex, including motor control areas and sensory networks, suggesting a multidisciplinary approach where neuroscientific findings inform therapeutic practices.
