## The Laughter Prescription: A Tool for Lifestyle Medicine

The article discusses the physiological and psychological benefits of laughter, emphasizing its potential role in lifestyle medicine. It reviews existing research indicating that laughter has quantifiable positive effects on health, particularly through its impact on stress hormones, pain tolerance, and immune function. The authors explore different theories behind why people laugh and categorize laughter into spontaneous and self-induced types. They suggest that even simulated laughter can provide similar physiological benefits to genuine laughter due to the 'motion creates emotion' theory. The paper argues for integrating humor and laughter as part of a holistic health approach, given its potential cost-effectiveness and ease of implementation in medical practices.
