## What is Music, Health, and Wellbeing?

The chapter 'What is Music, Health, and Wellbeing?' by Raymond MacDonald, Gunter Kreutz, and Laura Mitchell explores the multifaceted relationship between music and health. The authors emphasize that music's therapeutic potential extends beyond its acoustic properties to influence psychological, physiological, cultural, and behavioral aspects of human life. They discuss historical perspectives on music as a curative agent and highlight the importance of interdisciplinary approaches in contemporary research. Central to their argument is the idea that music can enhance well-being through emotional engagement, neurological effects, and distraction from distressing stimuli. The chapter also addresses methodological challenges in researching this field but underscores the growing body of evidence supporting music's positive impact on health and well-being.
