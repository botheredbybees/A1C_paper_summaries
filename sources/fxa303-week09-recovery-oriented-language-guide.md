## Recovery Oriented Language Guide

The Recovery Oriented Language Guide, published by the Mental Health Coordinating Council in 2022, emphasizes the importance of language choices in mental health discourse. The guide asserts that words can either promote hope and recovery or contribute to stigma and low expectations. Key principles include using respectful, non-judgmental, clear, trauma-informed, and strengths-based language. It advises against labeling individuals by their conditions and encourages asking how people prefer to be addressed. The document also covers specific guidelines for communicating with young people, older adults, and LGBTIQ+ individuals, as well as discussing suicide and grief in a recovery-oriented manner. This guide is crucial for mental health professionals aiming to foster an environment that supports recovery and wellbeing.
