## An investigation of the pharmacological applications used for the Ancient Egyptian systemic model ‘ra-ib’ compared with modern Traditional Chinese Medicine

This study investigates the ancient Egyptian medical concept 'ra-ib' and compares it with Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) to provide insights into non-Western healing traditions. The paper argues that 'ra-ib', traditionally translated as ‘stomach’, instead refers to a systemic pathway from mouth to anus, akin to TCM’s holistic approach to the body's interconnected systems. Through philological analysis of 34 ancient Egyptian prescriptions and comparison with the modern Chinese Pharmacopoeia, researchers found that understanding ra-ib as an 'inner thoroughfare' shifts perceptions on how ingredients were chosen for treatments. This new interpretation enhances our comprehension of historical healing practices by revealing potential universal themes in system-based medical traditions.
