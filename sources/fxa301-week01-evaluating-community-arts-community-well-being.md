## Evaluating Community Arts & Community Well-Being

The document 'Evaluating Community Arts & Community Well-Being' is an evaluation guide tailored for community arts practitioners, aimed at assessing the social outcomes of their projects systematically. Authored by Clare Keating and commissioned by several organizations including Arts Victoria, VicHealth, Darebin City Council, and City of Whittlesea, this guide seeks to provide a user-friendly tool that addresses the challenge of documenting and proving the positive impact of community arts on society beyond anecdotal evidence.

The guide is organized into stages covering preparation, planning, determining evaluation indicators, collecting data, analyzing data, and preparing an evaluation report. It includes both an Express Guide for quick reference and a comprehensive version offering in-depth tools that can be adapted to specific needs. The purpose of the document extends beyond mere documentation; it aims to foster continuous improvement by encouraging reflection on projects and identifying areas for enhancement.

Keating’s guide acknowledges the often overlooked aspect of evaluation within community arts practice, recognizing its importance not just as a method of proving effectiveness but also as a means of improving future projects and partnerships. The resource is intended to help practitioners overcome common barriers such as time constraints and lack of systematic approaches, making it easier for them to evaluate their work comprehensively while building a robust body of evidence about the impact of community arts on health and well-being.
