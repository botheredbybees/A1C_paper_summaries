## P.1.i. Basic and clinical neuroscience −Brain imaging and neuro-modulation

The document discusses research on brain imaging and neuro-modulation in relation to dopamine (DA) neurons following NMDA injection into the pedunculopontine tegmental nucleus (PPN). The study found significant reductions in soma size of A10 and A9 DA neurons, indicating rapid adaptation changes within dopaminergic systems. This research is relevant for understanding how neurochemical systems adapt to injuries or diseases affecting brain regions involved in motor control and reward processing. However, the text does not directly relate to creative arts and health; thus, it primarily contributes to basic neuroscience rather than clinical applications in arts therapies.
