## Music and Refugees’ Wellbeing in Contexts of Protracted Displacement

This study examines the relationship between music practice and the wellbeing of young Yazidi refugees living in a Greek camp for over a year after being displaced from northern Iraq due to war and conflict. The research, employing a focused exploratory case study design, collected data through participant observation of individual music lessons and group workshops, as well as semi-structured interviews with six participants aged 11–18. Findings indicate that music activities positively impact emotional expression, social relations, self-knowledge, positive self-identification, and a sense of agency among young refugees. The study suggests that music has the potential to serve as a health promotion approach by fostering supportive environments that enhance community action and personal skill development.
