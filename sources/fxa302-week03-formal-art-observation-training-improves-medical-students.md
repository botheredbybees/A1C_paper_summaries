## Formal Art Observation Training Improves Medical Students’ Visual Diagnostic Skills

This study examines the impact of a course designed to enhance medical students' visual diagnostic skills through formal art observation training. The course, 'Training the Eye: Improving the Art of Physical Diagnosis,' uses structured sessions to develop visual literacy and critical thinking related to both artworks and patient assessments. Participants demonstrated significant improvements in their ability to accurately observe and describe physical findings from photographs and artworks compared to a control group. This interdisciplinary approach suggests that integrating fine arts concepts into medical education can effectively bolster students' diagnostic competencies, particularly in visual inspection skills.
