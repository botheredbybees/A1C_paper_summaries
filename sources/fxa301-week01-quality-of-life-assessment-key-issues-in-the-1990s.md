## Quality of Life Assessment: Key Issues in the 1990s

This edited volume, published by Springer Science+Business Media in 1993, addresses key issues and methodologies related to the assessment of quality of life (QOL) in various disease areas. The book is divided into three main sections: philosophies, concepts, and key instruments; assessing QOL in major disease areas; and viewpoints and perspectives on ethical, industrial, regulatory, and healthcare purchaser aspects. Edited by Stuart R. Walker and Rachel M. Rosser, the text compiles contributions from numerous experts who explore various tools and techniques for measuring quality of life, such as the Quality of Well-being Scale, Sickness Impact Profile, Nottingham Health Profile, McMaster Health Index Questionnaire, EuroQol, and WHOQOL among others. The volume emphasizes the importance of patient-centered approaches in evaluating treatment efficacy and policy decisions within healthcare contexts.
