## We are still doing some magic: Exploring the effectiveness of online therapeutic clowning

This study investigates the effectiveness of online therapeutic clowning as a response to healthcare restrictions imposed by the global pandemic in 2020. Therapeutic clowns, also known as Clown Doctors or Hospital Clowns, engage patients and their families using physical comedy, imaginative play, and improvisation to support medical goals and generate positive social environments. Despite challenges posed by virtual platforms, therapeutic clowns successfully adapted their practices to maintain core competencies such as clown play, quality of presence, creativity, organizational finesse, flexibility, and relational skills. The research found that online therapeutic clowning was effective in empowering clients and forming therapeutic relationships, with some advantages over traditional in-person methods for certain patients.
