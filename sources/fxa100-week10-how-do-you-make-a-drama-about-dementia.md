## How do you make a drama about dementia?

This article explores the challenges and approaches dramatists take when creating works that depict dementia, focusing on recent productions like 'Still Alice,' 'Visitors,' 'An Evening with Dementia,' and 'How Did I Get Here?' These works aim to convey the personal and familial impact of dementia through various dramatic devices. The central argument is that while there are multiple ways to portray dementia on stage or screen, each approach seeks to accurately represent the experiences and emotions associated with the condition. Key findings include the importance of balancing medical accuracy with emotional truthfulness in storytelling, as well as the potential for these dramas to increase public understanding and empathy towards those affected by dementia. The relevance to creative arts and health lies in how such works contribute to societal awareness and dialogue about a condition that is increasingly prevalent but often misunderstood.
