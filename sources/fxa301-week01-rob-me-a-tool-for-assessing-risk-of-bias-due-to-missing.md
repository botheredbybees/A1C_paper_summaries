## ROB-ME: a tool for assessing risk of bias due to missing evidence in systematic reviews with meta-analysis

The article introduces the ROB-ME (risk of bias due to missing evidence) tool, which aims to address gaps in assessing biases arising from missing studies or results within meta-analyses. The paper emphasizes how selective non-publication and selective non-reporting can distort the systematic review process by omitting data that contradicts initial hypotheses. Existing methods focus on detecting these issues but do not assess their impact on the final analysis. ROB-ME provides a structured approach to evaluate the risk of bias when studies or particular results are missing due to factors like p-value, magnitude, and direction. The tool includes preliminary steps for selecting meta-analyses, identifying missing evidence, and assessing overall risk. This structured method is anticipated to help authors and users of systematic reviews better identify biases in their analyses and interpret results more accurately.
