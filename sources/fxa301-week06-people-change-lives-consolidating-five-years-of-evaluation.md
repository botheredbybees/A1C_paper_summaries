## People change lives: Consolidating five years of evaluation learning from Sistema Scotland’s Big Noise centres in Stirling, Glasgow and Aberdeen

This report consolidates the findings from five years of evaluation conducted by the Glasgow Centre for Population Health (GCPH) on Sistema Scotland's Big Noise centers in Stirling, Glasgow, and Aberdeen. The study highlights that the program has a significant positive impact on children and young people involved, enhancing their confidence, discipline, pride, aspiration, teamwork, communication skills, leadership abilities, academic performance, resilience, happiness, sense of belonging, musical skills, physical activity engagement, healthy eating habits, avoidance of harmful behaviors, social relationships, cultural involvement, and protection from adverse circumstances. The report emphasizes that the transformative effects of Big Noise are largely due to the long-term supportive relationships between musicians and participants. Musicians act as educators, mentors, and role models who foster positive life choices and behaviors, with recruitment focusing on individuals who are caring, compassionate, energetic, patient, and socially conscious. Additionally, the study notes that addressing health inequalities is a critical aspect of Big Noise's mission, as it targets disadvantaged communities and works to improve key determinants of health in adulthood.
