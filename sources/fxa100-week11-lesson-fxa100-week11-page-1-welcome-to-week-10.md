## Benefits of Dance and Movement for People with Dementia

This lesson explores the benefits of dance and movement therapies for people with dementia. It discusses how different types of dances engage various parts of the brain including the motor cortex, somatosensory cortex, cerebellum, and frontal lobe. A study by Brown et al., (2006) using fMRI on amateur tango dancers highlights that dance activates auditory and visual cortices in addition to regions involved in movement planning and spatial awareness. The lesson outlines learning outcomes such as describing the benefits of dance therapy for older adults and dementia patients, explaining which brain areas are engaged during dance activities, and understanding social, physical, and mental advantages from participating in dance or movement groups.
