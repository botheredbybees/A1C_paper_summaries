## Revised Cochrane risk of bias tool for randomized trials (RoB 2): Additional considerations for cluster-randomized trials

This document supplements the main guidance on the Cochrane Risk of Bias (RoB) tool version 2, specifically addressing adaptations necessary for cluster-randomized trials. The primary focus is on identifying and mitigating a unique type of bias called identification/recruitment bias, which occurs when individuals within clusters are recruited after knowing their intervention assignment. This work elaborates on the definition of participants in such trials and provides examples to clarify its application. It also discusses methodological aspects like stepped-wedge designs, unit-of-analysis errors, and randomization processes unique to cluster-randomized trials. The relevance to creative arts and health lies in enhancing research rigor through robust methodologies that can be applied across various fields, including community-based interventions.
