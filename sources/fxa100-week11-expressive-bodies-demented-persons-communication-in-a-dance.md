## Expressive bodies: demented persons’ communication in a dance therapy context

This study explores the communication abilities of individuals with dementia through dance therapy sessions, highlighting their capacity to express themselves beyond traditional limitations imposed by the disease. Focusing on group sessions conducted in Swedish nursing homes, the research examines how demented patients utilize body movements, free dance, speech, and singing to communicate thoughts, memories, and emotions. The analysis reveals that under specific conditions supportive of varied modes of expression, individuals with dementia can exhibit rich and diverse communication patterns. This study underscores the potential of expressive therapies like dance therapy in enhancing the quality of life for those suffering from dementia by facilitating non-verbal and multi-modal forms of communication.
