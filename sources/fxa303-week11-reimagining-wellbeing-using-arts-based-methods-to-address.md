## Reimagining wellbeing: Using arts-based methods to address sexual, gender and health inequalities

The article explores the Creative Change Laboratory (CCoLAB), an immersive learning space aimed at fostering unconventional solutions to community problems through diverse arts-based methods among young people in Cape Town, South Africa. The study highlights how CCoLAB utilized various forms of expression such as visual art, interactive drama, narrative writing, and photography/videography to address social justice challenges related to sexual, gender, and health inequalities. It discusses the project's potential to facilitate critical thinking while also identifying tensions around knowledge production in facilitated spaces. Key findings include the importance of connection and collaboration among marginalized groups for effective social change. The article argues that arts-based methods can both encourage and stifle critical thinking depending on how they are utilized.
