## Narrative-Based Medicine

The article by George Zaharias explores narrative-based medicine (NBM) as a valuable approach in medical consultations, emphasizing its potential benefits for both patients and physicians. NBM is defined as the application of narrative ideas to medical practice, centered around understanding the patient's unique story rather than just problem-solving. The author highlights key proponents Rita Charon and John Launer, discussing how NBM can strengthen the patient-doctor relationship by fostering better communication and empathy. Key challenges include overcoming resistance due to a lack of clear definition for NBM skills and addressing the 'four divides' between doctors and patients as outlined by Charon. While the healing power of narrative is acknowledged, further research is needed to define its role in clinical practice more precisely.
