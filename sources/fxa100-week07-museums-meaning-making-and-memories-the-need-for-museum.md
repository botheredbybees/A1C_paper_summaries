## Museums, Meaning Making, and Memories: The Need for Museum Programs for People with Dementia and Their Caregivers

Libby Rhoads's article discusses the importance of museums as venues for people with dementia and their caregivers, emphasizing that these individuals often find positive and meaningful experiences in museum settings despite being considered an unreachable audience. The paper highlights existing museum programs tailored to this demographic and underscores the need for further initiatives. It defines dementia and explains its impact on daily life while outlining non-pharmacological approaches like reminiscence, art therapy, music therapy, and activity therapy as viable support strategies. Rhoads argues that museums can foster social interaction and cognitive exploration through their resources and collections, thereby enhancing the quality of life for those living with dementia.
