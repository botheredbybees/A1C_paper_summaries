## Museums and Wellbeing: An Overview on Emerging Architectural and Museographic Trends

The paper explores the growing connection between museums and health, highlighting how museums are adapting their spaces to enhance visitors' wellbeing through innovative design and museography. Over recent years, there has been an increasing interest in incorporating elements that provide therapeutic benefits, especially for vulnerable groups like those with disabilities or cognitive difficulties. Museums have started integrating new rooms and devices designed specifically to improve the health of both visitors and staff by creating tranquil spaces conducive to relaxation and mental balance. These initiatives reflect a broader trend in architecture and design where considerations of physical and psychological wellbeing are integrated into various settings beyond healthcare institutions. The article emphasizes that while significant progress has been made, much work remains to fully realize the potential for museum spaces as therapeutic environments.
