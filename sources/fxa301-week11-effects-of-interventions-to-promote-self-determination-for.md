## Effects of Interventions to Promote Self-Determination for Individuals With Disabilities

The article reviews research on the effects of interventions designed to enhance self-determination among people with disabilities. Self-determination is defined as a combination of skills, knowledge, and beliefs enabling goal-directed autonomous behavior. The study identifies 51 relevant studies out of which 22 were analyzed quantitatively using meta-analysis techniques. Results showed significant positive outcomes from self-determination interventions across various disability groups, particularly in teaching choice making to individuals with moderate/severe mental retardation and self-advocacy skills to those with learning disabilities or mild mental retardation. The research emphasizes the importance of promoting self-determination as an outcome in special education and rehabilitation contexts. The article highlights legislative support for such initiatives and discusses practical strategies for implementing self-determination training.
