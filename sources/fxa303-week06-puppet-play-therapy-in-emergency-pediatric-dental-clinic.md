## Puppet play therapy in emergency pediatric dental clinic

This study explores the efficacy of puppet play therapy (PPT) compared to tell-show-do (TSD) behavior guidance in emergency pediatric dental clinics, focusing on children aged 3–6 years without prior dental experience. The research aimed to reduce anxiety and improve treatment outcomes during local anesthesia administration. A randomized clinical trial was conducted with 50 participants divided into two groups: PPT and TSD. Results indicated that the mean heart rates were lower in the PPT group, suggesting reduced anxiety levels as measured by the Facial Index Scale (FIS) and Venham Anxiety Rating Scale. This study highlights the potential of PPT as an effective method for managing behavior and reducing anxiety among young children undergoing urgent dental procedures.
