## Moving from Problem to Potential: Dance and Wellness in US Hospitals

Filipa Pereira-Stubbs explores the role of dance as a therapeutic practice within hospital settings across New York, Gainesville, and San Francisco during her four-week research visit in 2014. The study examines how wellness programs are integrated into hospitals' overall mission and delivered to patients, staff, caregivers, and community members. It highlights the principles behind arts-in-medicine initiatives, their impact on patient care, staff well-being, and the broader hospital environment. Pereira-Stubbs also meets with various dance practitioners who work in communities with elderly populations, including those living with Parkinson's disease, dementia, cancer, and other conditions. The research underscores the transcendent nature of dance, which bridges cultural, economic, and health divides to enhance participants' quality of life and sense of connection. By engaging patients, staff, and community members through dance, hospitals aim to foster better healing environments that support holistic well-being.
