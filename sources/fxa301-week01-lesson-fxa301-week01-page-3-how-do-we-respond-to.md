## How does the brain help us to respond to creative art?

The lesson discusses how the brain processes and responds to creative arts such as visual art and music. It begins by explaining that initial sensory inputs activate specific regions of the brain (e.g., areas for sight or hearing). As we attempt to interpret these stimuli, various brain networks become engaged, including those related to movement control and reward pathways involving neurotransmitters like dopamine and serotonin. Dopamine is linked to the anticipation and enjoyment of rewards, such as pleasure derived from art activities. Serotonin affects mood and emotion regulation but is harder to measure accurately than dopamine or cortisol. The concept of mirror neurons, which activate when we observe actions performed by others, also plays a role in learning through imitation. Additionally, hormones like cortisol (linked to stress) and oxytocin (involved in social bonding) are discussed as they can provide insights into the emotional impact of arts on health and wellbeing.
