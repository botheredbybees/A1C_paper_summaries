## The Cultural Value of Older People’s Experiences of Theater-making: A Review

The article by Bernard and Rickett (2017) explores the cultural value of older people's involvement in theater-making, focusing on the health benefits, social relationships, learning opportunities, and aesthetic appreciation derived from such activities. The review synthesizes literature from a variety of sources including academic databases and practitioner accounts to provide a nuanced understanding of how participation in drama and theater contributes to individual well-being and societal value. It emphasizes that while there is growing interest in this field, older people's contributions are often overlooked due to ageist stereotypes. The study calls for more research to understand the intrinsic cultural value beyond just health benefits, advocating for qualitative approaches alongside quantitative measures.
