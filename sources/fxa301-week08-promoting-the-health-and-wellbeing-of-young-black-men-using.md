## Promoting the health and wellbeing of young Black men using community-based drama

Martin Kemp's paper explores the use of drama and theatre in promoting emotional and social wellbeing among young Black men living in south London through a project called Brother to Brother (B2B). The initiative aimed to address issues such as self-esteem, identity, relationships, and sexual health. Using qualitative methods involving process and outcome evaluation, Kemp found that B2B successfully fostered participants' sense of agency, self-expression, reflection, and trust-building among peers. Key findings indicate the importance of creating a safe space for personal development and critical awareness of societal stereotypes affecting Black masculinity. The project’s success underscores the efficacy of arts-based approaches in community health initiatives and highlights the value of process-oriented qualitative evaluation.
