## SPARK Curriculum: Promoting Resilience Through Art Therapy

The chapter discusses the SPARK curriculum and program aimed at fostering resilience in children through art therapy activities following natural disasters, specifically Hurricane Harvey recovery efforts. The PR-6 model of resilience is outlined, emphasizing goal setting, composure, self-control, reasoning, decision making, perseverance, social connection, and health. Each week focuses on one of these domains with corresponding arts-based activities designed to develop personal strengths and coping mechanisms. Community engagement, including art projects and events like painting Stars of HOPE symbols, integrates participants into supportive networks. The shift to virtual programs during the COVID-19 pandemic adapted activities for remote learning using minimal supplies while maintaining engagement. SPARK's impact is measured through parent evaluations highlighting improvements in child confidence, friendship, respect, academics, self-control, perseverance, and problem-solving skills.
