## Theatre and Play in Dementia Care

This week's lesson explores the benefits of theatrical and playful engagement in dementia care. It discusses how play reinforces person-centered care by requiring knowledge about the individual's background. The content delves into Roger Caillois’s four forms of play: competitive, chance-based, simulation/mimicry, and awareness-altering (ilinx), and explains their application in various activities like role-playing games and VR experiences. It also outlines learning objectives related to describing playful encounters, evaluating theatre's role in raising dementia awareness, and discussing key concepts such as Recognition, Touchedness, Knowledge, and Aesthetic Expression.
