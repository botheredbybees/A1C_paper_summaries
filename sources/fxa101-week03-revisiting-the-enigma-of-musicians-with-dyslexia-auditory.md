## Revisiting the “Enigma” of Musicians With Dyslexia: Auditory Sequencing and Speech Abilities

The study explores auditory processing skills in musicians with dyslexia, comparing them to nonmusicians with dyslexia and typical musicians. It evaluates auditory sequencing and speech discrimination abilities across these groups through tasks measuring tone sequences and synthesized syllable continua varying in acoustic components necessary for intraphonemic discrimination. Results indicate that musicians with dyslexia perform better than nonmusicians with dyslexia on auditory sequencing and some aspects of speech discrimination, but show specific weaknesses in discerning amplitude cues within speech compared to typical musicians. This suggests a distinct profile of speech processing abilities in musicians with dyslexia, partially supporting the potential for musical training to enhance certain auditory skills crucial for literacy.
