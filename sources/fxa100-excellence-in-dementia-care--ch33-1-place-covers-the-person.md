## Guidance for Building Dementia-Friendly Communities

This chapter provides guidance on building dementia-friendly communities based on research by Innovations in Dementia and other organizations, emphasizing the need for broad criteria adaptable to various community sizes. It outlines key aspects of community life such as physical environment, local facilities, support services, social networks, and local groups, all crucial for people with dementia. The chapter also discusses four cornerstones: place, people, resources, and networks, essential in reducing negative effects of dementia and enhancing quality of life. National policies aim to ensure rights and entitlements for community participation while emphasizing the role of communities, businesses, and social networks. Internationally, examples from Germany, Japan, and Belgium highlight the importance of awareness, inclusion, and community engagement.
