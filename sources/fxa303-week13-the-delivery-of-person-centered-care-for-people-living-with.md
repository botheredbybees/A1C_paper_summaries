## The Delivery of Person-Centered Care for People Living With Dementia in Residential Aged Care: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

The article presents a systematic review and meta-analysis examining the delivery of person-centered care (PCC) for individuals with dementia in residential aged care facilities. The study aims to understand how PCC is implemented, its effectiveness, and the barriers and enablers from staff perspectives. Analyzing 41 studies, it identifies various frameworks, models, and programs used in PCC and assesses their impact on outcomes such as agitation, quality of life, and neuropsychiatric symptoms. The meta-analysis indicates no significant reduction in these measures. However, qualitative synthesis highlights that time constraints are a barrier to effective implementation, while staff collaboration is an enabler. This research underscores the need for further high-quality studies over extended periods to better understand how PCC can be optimally delivered in residential care settings.
