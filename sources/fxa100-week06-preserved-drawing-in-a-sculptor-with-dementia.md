## Preserved Drawing in a Sculptor with Dementia

This clinical report documents the case of Mary Hecht, a renowned sculptor diagnosed with Vascular Dementia (VD), who continued to draw spontaneous sketches despite significant cognitive decline. The study explores the persistence of creativity in advanced dementia, suggesting that certain artistic abilities may be subserved by distinct neural networks that remain intact even when other cognitive functions are severely impaired. Despite marked visuospatial and visual memory deficits, as evidenced by poor performance on standard neuropsychological tests like the Clock Drawing Test (CDT) and Rey-Osterrieth Complex Figure Test, Mary was able to produce accurate sketches from memory and create new artworks spontaneously. This observation supports the hypothesis that artistic creativity may be preserved in dementia due to relatively intact neural pathways involved in artistic expression.
