## Synaesthesia, creativity and art: What is the link?

The study explores the relationship between synaesthesia and creativity, suggesting that individuals with synaesthesia may engage more in creative arts such as visual art and music due to their unique perceptual experiences. The research involved a sample of 82 participants with various types of synaesthesia who completed psychometric tests for creativity (Remote Associates Test and Alternate Uses Test) and reported on time spent engaged in creative activities. Results indicated that synaesthetes were more likely to be involved in creative arts, particularly those experiencing visual sensations from auditory stimuli. However, there was no direct correlation between these artistic tendencies and the scores obtained in the psychometric tests of creativity. The authors conclude that while synaesthesia might provide enhanced access to novel associations, it does not necessarily translate into superior performance on measures of creative thinking such as divergent thinking tasks.
