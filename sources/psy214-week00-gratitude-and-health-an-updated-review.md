## Gratitude and health: An updated review

This review updates the understanding of gratitude's impact on human health by focusing on experimental and longitudinal studies published since 2010, aiming to clarify the causal relationship between gratitude and various dimensions of health. The authors explore both state (a short-term feeling) and trait (long-term perspective) forms of gratitude, examining their influence on physical and mental well-being, as well as emotional, psychological, and social aspects of health. Key findings include the positive associations between practicing gratitude and improvements in subjective well-being, resilience, and overall life satisfaction. The review highlights that while cross-sectional studies consistently show a link between gratitude and better health outcomes, experimental studies provide crucial evidence for causality. It also discusses different gratitude interventions such as journaling, writing letters of appreciation, and reflecting on positive experiences daily or weekly. These interventions have been found to reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety, enhance feelings of contentment, and improve social relationships, thereby supporting the broader concept of health as an adaptive capacity rather than merely the absence of illness.
