## Empathy, an integrative model

The paper presents an integrative model of empathy that situates empathic processes within a framework of communication and interaction, highlighting the interplay between psychological components, control systems, personality factors, and relational dynamics. The authors argue that empathy is a response to specific demands in context and propose four determining factors: psychological empathic components, twofold control system, personality traits, and relational factors. Observational data from children's empathic behaviors illustrate how these elements contribute to prosocial actions like comforting a distressed mother. This model offers a comprehensive account of the multifaceted nature of empathy relevant for understanding social interactions and emotional development.
