## Non-pharmacological Interventions to Reduce Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia

This article explores the prevalence and impact of behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) and reviews non-pharmacological interventions to manage these symptoms. The authors emphasize that BPSD significantly impacts the quality of life for individuals with dementia and their caregivers, contributing substantially to healthcare costs. They highlight the need for a person-centered approach in understanding and addressing BPSD, considering factors such as neurodegeneration, unmet needs, caregiver stress, and environmental influences. Non-pharmacological interventions recommended include music therapy, care staff education and training, cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), reminiscence therapy, and sensory stimulation. The article underscores the importance of tailoring these interventions to individual needs and preferences, suggesting that a holistic approach is essential for effective management of BPSD.
