## Space, People, Interventions and Time: A Model for Understanding Children’s Outdoor Play in Post-Disaster Contexts

This article explores the impact of post-disaster contexts on children's outdoor play opportunities in Tohoku, Japan, following the triple disaster of earthquake, tsunami, and nuclear power plant failure. The research utilizes a model called SPIT (Space, People, Interventions, Time) to analyze how these elements influence children’s play environments 13 months after the disasters. Key findings reveal constraints such as lack of safe spaces and loss of social connections which affect outdoor play opportunities for children. The study highlights that play is crucial in post-disaster situations for promoting resilience and supporting emotional well-being among affected children.
