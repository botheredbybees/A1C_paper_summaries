## Play and Arts-based therapies

The document discusses the effectiveness of play therapy and arts-based therapies for neurodiverse children, particularly those with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Research highlights that child-centered play therapy is beneficial in fostering social and emotional behaviors, aiding in communication skills and emotional regulation. Play therapy is adaptable to individual needs, using a child's interests as engagement points. Pivotal Response Therapy (PRT) is mentioned as an example of behavioral intervention that uses play-based strategies to improve social skills and positive behavior. Additionally, creative arts therapies such as dramatherapy and music therapy are shown to enhance expressive skills and emotional self-regulation. Music therapy, in particular, leverages a child's musical strengths for non-verbal expression and emotion regulation. Visual arts also serve as a medium for expressing oneself and exploring social interactions, contributing to motor skills and visual perception development. The document emphasizes the dose-related benefits of these therapies and their positive impact on mental wellbeing.
