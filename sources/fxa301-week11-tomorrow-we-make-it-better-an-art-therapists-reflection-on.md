## 'Tomorrow we make it better': an art therapist’s reflection on a community mural in a refugee camp in Greece

Emily Hollingsbee's article reflects on the creation of a community mural in a refugee camp in Greece, exploring the potential therapeutic benefits of such initiatives for displaced populations. The paper advocates for a psychosocial approach to art therapy, emphasizing its relevance when working with refugees and highlighting the importance of cross-cultural practices outside traditional therapy settings. Hollingsbee discusses her own experiences facilitating this project within a refugee camp, touching on the challenges encountered and the symbolic significance of the mural's creation process. She argues that community murals can serve as therapeutic tools to address psychological and social needs but emphasizes the need for further research involving participant perspectives to validate these claims. The paper underscores the potential of art therapy beyond conventional methods and its applicability in addressing complex humanitarian crises.
