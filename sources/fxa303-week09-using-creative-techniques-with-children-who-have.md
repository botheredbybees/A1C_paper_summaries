## Using Creative Techniques With Children Who Have Experienced Trauma

This article explores the use of creative techniques in counseling children who have experienced trauma, such as puppets, sandtray, letter writing, and art. Trauma is defined as a painful or terrifying experience that disrupts an individual's coping mechanisms and affects their psychological and social development. The authors argue that traditional talk therapy can be challenging for young traumatized children due to underdeveloped abstract thinking skills. Creative interventions are recommended because they provide age-appropriate mediums for expression, facilitate healing through storytelling, and empower the child by giving them a voice in therapeutic sessions. These techniques help therapists connect with children experiencing emotional and behavioral difficulties, enabling the exploration of traumatic experiences and fostering coping mechanisms.
