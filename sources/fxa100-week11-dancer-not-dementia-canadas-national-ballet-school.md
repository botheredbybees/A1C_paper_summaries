## Dancer Not Dementia | Canada's National Ballet School

The webpage 'Dancer Not Dementia' by Canada’s National Ballet School addresses the stigma associated with dementia and highlights how dance can counteract negative perceptions. It emphasizes that societal stereotypes often overshadow the individuality of people living with dementia, leading to social exclusion and compromised well-being. The initiative presents a short film and various resources aimed at challenging this stigma through dance, which is shown to offer physical, mental, and social benefits for older adults. By engaging in creative movement, individuals can demonstrate resilience and creativity, thereby shifting perceptions from focusing on limitations to celebrating abilities and unique expressions. The project involves research collaborations and community engagement initiatives such as Sharing Dance Older Adults, aiming to foster a culture of dance within dementia care communities.
