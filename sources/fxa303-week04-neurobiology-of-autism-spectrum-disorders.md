## Neurobiology of Autism Spectrum Disorders

This book, edited by Abdeslem El Idrissi and Dan McCloskey, delves into the complex interplay between genetics, brain structure and function, and environmental factors that contribute to autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The editors emphasize that ASD is a neurodevelopmental condition with significant variability in symptoms and challenges, affecting millions globally. This comprehensive resource synthesizes findings from multiple disciplines such as epidemiology, neurobiology, psychology, genetics, and clinical studies, presenting diverse perspectives on the mechanisms underlying ASD. Key chapters explore dysfunctional circuit mechanisms of sensory processing, prenatal environmental stressors, animal models of ASD, mitochondrial dysfunction, lipid-related pathophysiology, and more. The book aims to bridge the gap between cutting-edge research and practical knowledge, making it valuable for researchers, clinicians, educators, and individuals seeking a deeper understanding of the neurobiological foundations of ASD.
