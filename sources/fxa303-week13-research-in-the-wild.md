## Research in the wild

In their correspondence piece published in Nature, Van Hoyweghen and Penders discuss the growing trend of public involvement in human genetics research. They emphasize that laypeople and advocacy groups are increasingly demanding a say in biomedical research by contributing towards its funding, coordination, and even development of therapies. This shift reflects a broader movement where traditional scientific practices are being supplemented or challenged by community-led initiatives, advocating for more inclusive research methodologies that consider societal impact and ethical implications.
