## In the moment with music: an exploration of the embodied and sensory experiences of people living with dementia during improvised music-making

This article explores the concept of 'in the moment' musical experiences in dementia care through a qualitative study involving people living with dementia and their family carers during improvisatory music-making sessions. The research, utilizing video-observation and interviews, reveals four thematic observations: Sharing a life story through music, Musical agency ‘in the moment’, Feeling connected ‘in the moment’, and Musical ripples into everyday life. These findings highlight how musical experiences can foster creativity, enhance personal identity, and strengthen social connections for individuals with dementia. The study critiques the biomedical approach to measuring the success of music therapy programs, emphasizing instead the importance of subjective musical experiences in the present moment.
