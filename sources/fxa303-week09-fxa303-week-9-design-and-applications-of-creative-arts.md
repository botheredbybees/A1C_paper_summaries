## FXA303 Week 9: Design and Applications of Creative Arts Projects for Mental Wellbeing

Week 9 of FXA303, a course on Creative Arts, Play, and Mental Wellbeing, introduces Module 3, which focuses on the design and application of creative arts projects to support mental health in various contexts. The module highlights the importance of trauma-informed approaches that are empathetic and culturally sensitive when developing such projects for children, young people, and communities affected by traumatic events. It also emphasizes the need for flexibility and continuous monitoring of participants' well-being during project implementation. Key elements discussed include indicators of expected processing of trauma impacts and signs that specialized care may be necessary if mental wellbeing declines. The week includes case studies like Leigh Tesch's art and storytelling project in response to the 2018 Hobart floods, aimed at helping children process emotions and strengthen community bonds. Overall, this module underscores how well-designed creative arts projects can not only aid trauma recovery but also enhance self-confidence and resilience through knowledge-building and disaster preparedness.
