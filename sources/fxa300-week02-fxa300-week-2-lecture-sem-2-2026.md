## FXA300 Week 2 Lecture – Sem 2 2026

The lecture covers key concepts in music psychology, focusing on the neurological basis of musical experiences and perception. It begins with a discussion on earworms (involuntary repetition of melodies), absorption (immersion in music), and BRECVEMA (Brain, Rhythm, Emotion, Cognition, Vision, Memory, Expectation, Aesthetics) theory proposed by Juslin & Västfjäll. The lecture delves into the anatomy of the brain, detailing the functions of various regions such as the limbic system, auditory cortex, and Wernicke's area in processing music. It also discusses conditions like amusia and their neurological implications. The importance of engagement with musical activities is emphasized through recommended readings and discussion forums on how music impacts infant development.
