## Understanding Comics: The Invisible Art

Scott McCloud's book 'Understanding Comics' explores the essence of the comic medium as a unique form of visual communication. It begins by setting out to define what constitutes comics, distinguishing them from other forms like animation and graphic novels through their use of static juxtaposed images in sequence. The book also delves into historical aspects, examining early examples such as pre-Columbian manuscripts and tapestries that fit the definition of comics due to their sequential storytelling format. McCloud discusses various techniques unique to comic artistry, including panel layout, gutters (the space between panels), closure (reader’s imagination filling gaps in the narrative), and how words interact with pictures. The book argues for a serious consideration of comics as an art form capable of profound expression.
