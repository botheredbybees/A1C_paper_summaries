## Development and Validation of a Mobile Phone Photography Training Program (MobileGraphy) to Enhance Visual Perception Skills in Children with Specific Learning Disabilities

The study by Bahreini Motlagh et al., published in the Journal of Qualitative Research in Health Sciences, explores the development and validation of a mobile phone photography training program (MobileGraphy) to enhance visual perception skills in children with specific learning disabilities (SLDs). The research highlights that these children often struggle with visual-spatial relationships and perceptual difficulties despite having average or above-average intelligence. This study utilized a mixed-methods approach combining qualitative interviews with experts and quantitative pre-test/post-test assessments to evaluate the effectiveness of MobileGraphy. Qualitative analysis identified foundational instruction in mobile phone photography, motivation enhancement strategies, development of visual perception skills, and attention skill improvement as critical components. Quantitatively, significant positive effects were observed on visual perception in children with SLDs. The program leverages technology’s simplicity and accessibility to foster reflective thinking and heightened awareness among participants. It suggests that integrating MobileGraphy into educational settings can effectively support cognitive development for these students.
