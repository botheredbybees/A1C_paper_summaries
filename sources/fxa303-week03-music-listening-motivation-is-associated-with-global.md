## Music Listening Motivation Is Associated With Global Happiness in Canadian Late Adolescents

This study explores the relationship between self-determined motivation for listening to music and subjective well-being (SWB) in late adolescents from Canada. The researchers hypothesized that engaging with music for autonomous reasons would correlate positively with global happiness, given the increasing autonomy during this developmental phase. A sample of 229 participants aged 17-21 completed surveys assessing their self-determined motivation to listen to music and their levels of SWB, which included positive and negative affect as well as life satisfaction. Structural equation modeling confirmed that higher autonomous motives for listening to music predicted greater SWB among the adolescents. The findings suggest that adolescents who listen to music for intrinsic reasons experience more enduring happiness, contributing insights into the role of self-determined motivation in youth development.
