## Dementia discourse – A rethink?

Fran McInerney's editorial discusses the language used to describe dementia and its impact on individuals living with the condition, drawing parallels with how society has historically depicted other deadly illnesses like tuberculosis, cancer, and AIDS. McInerney critiques the metaphor of a 'dementia tsunami' for being dehumanizing and instead advocates for more compassionate and accurate terminologies that acknowledge suffering while respecting dignity. The piece highlights initiatives by various organizations to promote person-centered language guidelines aimed at empowering those with dementia and reducing stigma. However, it also raises concerns about excluding terms such as 'suffering,' which are important in the context of palliative care and recognizing the terminal nature of advanced dementia. McInerney argues for a balanced approach that acknowledges suffering while still advocating for dignity, worth, and citizenship of people with dementia.
