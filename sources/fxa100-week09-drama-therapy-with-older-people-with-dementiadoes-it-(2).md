## Drama therapy with older people with dementia—Does it improve quality of life?

This study explores the impact of drama therapy on the quality of life (QoL) for elderly individuals with mild to moderate dementia. Utilizing a mixed-method approach, researchers compared a group participating in drama therapy sessions with another group watching movies over four months. Assessments were conducted before and after the intervention using the QoL-AD scale, revealing no statistically significant difference but an increase in QoL scores for the drama therapy participants while it decreased slightly for the movie-watching group. Qualitative data analysis confirmed that drama therapy enabled participants to express ideas and feelings more effectively and fostered a greater awareness of their own wellbeing. The findings underscore the potential benefits of further research into drama therapy as a therapeutic intervention for dementia.
