## The Persisting Conflict of Interpretations of Shamanism

This review discusses two books on shamanism: James McClenon's *Wondrous Healing* and Michael Winkelman's *Shamanism*. The reviewer, Justus M. Ogembo, examines how both authors contribute to the understanding of shamanism from different perspectives. McClenon offers a speculative theory about the origins of religion grounded in biology and human evolution, while Winkelman focuses on the neurological basis of shamanic experiences and their impact on consciousness and healing. Both books aim to shed light on the enigmatic nature of shamanism and its role in human evolution and society. The review highlights Winkelman's theory that shamanism is a technique for altering consciousness, which can provide insights into various forms of human experience beyond traditional Western rational models.
