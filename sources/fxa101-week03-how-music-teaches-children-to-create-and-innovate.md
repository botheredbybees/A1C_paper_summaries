## How Music Teaches Children to Create and Innovate

The chapter 'Seeking Out the New' by Anita Collins explores how music education fosters creativity and innovation in children. Drawing parallels with activities like finding Wally in a Where’s Wally? book, Collins highlights that both seeking patterns and identifying novelties are key aspects of cognitive development. Music, with its inherent rules and expectations, provides an environment where these skills can be honed. Children learning to play instruments must initially adhere strictly to rules but later develop the openness required for creative exploration. The chapter notes a positive correlation between musical training and traits such as conscientiousness and openness, which are linked to innovation and creativity. It concludes that while strict adherence is necessary at first, the long-term goal is to balance discipline with the flexibility needed for creative thinking.
