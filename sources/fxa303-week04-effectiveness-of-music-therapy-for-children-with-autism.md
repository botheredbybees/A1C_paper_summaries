## Effectiveness of music therapy for children with autism spectrum disorder: meta-analysis and potential biological mechanisms

This study examines the efficacy of music therapy (MT) as an intervention for children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The authors conducted a meta-analysis of 18 studies, finding that MT significantly reduced core symptoms of ASD according to standardized measures like the Autism Behavior Checklist and Childhood Autism Rating Scale. Specific improvements were noted in social skills, behavioral abilities, sensory processing, emotional functioning, and verbal communication. However, the study acknowledges limitations due to the heterogeneity of methodologies across studies and calls for further research to validate these findings. The paper discusses potential biological mechanisms underlying MT's effectiveness, such as enhanced neural connectivity and brain plasticity observed in individuals who regularly engage in musical activities. These insights are crucial for understanding how non-pharmacological interventions like music therapy can complement existing treatment approaches and improve the quality of life for children with ASD.
