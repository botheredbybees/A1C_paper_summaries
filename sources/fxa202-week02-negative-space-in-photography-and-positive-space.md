## Negative space in Photography (and positive space)

The document discusses the concept of negative and positive spaces in photography, emphasizing their importance for effective visual storytelling. Positive space refers to the main subject or focal point of an image that captures the viewer's attention, whereas negative space complements this by providing context through surrounding areas, allowing the subject to 'breathe' and enhancing its prominence. The text argues that negative space is crucial as it enables photographers to isolate subjects and guide viewers’ focus, thereby creating a clearer visual hierarchy within compositions. It also draws parallels between photography and minimalist design principles, suggesting that thoughtful inclusion of elements rather than their removal leads to more compelling images. Examples are provided for using positive and negative spaces to convey vastness, isolate subjects, and create orderly compositions, highlighting how these techniques contribute to the overall clarity and impact of photographic works.
