## Person-centred Language Guidelines

The document 'Person-centred Language Guidelines' by the Alzheimer Society outlines recommendations for respectful and inclusive language when discussing dementia. It emphasizes that historically, language used to describe dementia has been predominantly negative, focusing on the deficits experienced rather than the person’s remaining strengths and capabilities. The guidelines aim to promote person-centered approaches in communication, ensuring dignity and respect are maintained throughout discussions involving individuals with dementia and their caregivers. By shifting focus from medical terminology and symptoms to the individual's identity and experiences, these guidelines seek to reduce stigma, enhance understanding, and foster supportive care environments. The principles of personhood, dignity and respect, acceptance and understanding, relationships, recognition and individuality, and relationships of trust are highlighted as foundational elements in promoting human rights for people with dementia.
