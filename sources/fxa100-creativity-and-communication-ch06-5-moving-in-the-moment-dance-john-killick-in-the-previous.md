## Moving in the Moment

This chapter discusses the therapeutic benefits of dance for individuals with dementia. It outlines how spontaneous movement can be a fundamental human activity that transcends intellectual constraints, emphasizing its ability to evoke buried memories and emotions stored in bodily tissues rather than just the brain. The text highlights the importance of creating welcoming spaces for dance activities where participants can move freely without inhibition. Specific case studies illustrate positive outcomes such as improved communication and emotional release among dementia patients through dance therapy sessions.
