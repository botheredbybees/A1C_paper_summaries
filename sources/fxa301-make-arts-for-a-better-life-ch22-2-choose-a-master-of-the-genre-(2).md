## Community Arts Promotion Through Cultural Publications and Clubs

This chapter details the steps for promoting local culture through publications and artists' associations in communities facing rapid urbanization and loss of traditional knowledge contexts. It outlines a process involving selecting mentors, recruiting apprentices, designing educational settings that balance tradition with modern commitments, implementing training programs, and fostering ongoing skill development. The publication scheme involves determining audience, editorial team, content sourcing, distribution plans, scheduling, execution, and feedback mechanisms. A case study examines PROMOCUL's Mûɔ láʼ journal in West Cameroon, which bridges urban-rural cultural gaps by documenting and disseminating Ngiemboon traditions to younger generations. Recommendations for organizing community events like festivals are provided to sustain local arts.
