## Street Photography Composition Lesson #12: Color Theory

In this lesson on color theory, Eric Kim discusses the importance of understanding complementary colors and warm vs. cool colors in street photography. Kim highlights that while black-and-white photography simplifies scenes, shooting in color can enhance storytelling if done effectively. He explains that complementary colors are those opposite each other on a color wheel, which create balance and harmony when used together. Examples include sports teams' logos like the Los Angeles Lakers (purple and yellow) and New York Knicks (blue and orange). Kim also illustrates how Steve McCurry uses red-green dynamics in his work to evoke emotional responses, such as in 'The Afghan Girl.' Additionally, he notes that warm colors, often seen at sunset, can convey a sense of hope or danger depending on the context. Understanding these principles helps photographers better utilize color to enhance visual storytelling and create impactful images.
