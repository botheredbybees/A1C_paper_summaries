## The role of similarity, sound and awareness in the appreciation of visual artwork via motor simulation

This study examines the influence of motor simulation on art appreciation, specifically testing whether viewers' actions during the observation of artwork affect their liking for that artwork. Previous research suggested that performing motions similar to those used in creating a piece of art could enhance appreciation; however, this study aimed to replicate and extend these findings with higher statistical power. The authors conducted four experiments: Experiment 1 attempted to directly replicate an earlier study but did not find the same results. Experiment 2 showed effects only when participants were more aware of the match between their actions and the artwork's style. Experiments 3 and 4 further explored conditions under which this effect occurs, indicating that awareness plays a significant role in the process. The research highlights the importance of embodiment theory and its implications for understanding art perception.
