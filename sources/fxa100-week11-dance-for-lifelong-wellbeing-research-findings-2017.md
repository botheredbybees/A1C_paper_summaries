## Dance for Lifelong Wellbeing Research Findings 2017

The 'Dance for Lifelong Wellbeing' project, led by the Royal Academy of Dance (RAD), focused on enhancing physical and mental well-being among older adults through dance. The study built upon a 2013 pilot with positive qualitative feedback but aimed to measure tangible improvements in balance, daily activity levels, and quality of life quantitatively. Key findings indicated significant physiological benefits such as improved balance for individuals with low mobility, which could reduce fall risks. Additionally, the project observed psychosocial impacts, including enhanced self-direction and confidence among participants. The research also underscored the importance of tailored teacher training to ensure safe and enjoyable dance sessions, addressing diverse needs within the older adult population. These findings contribute valuable insights into developing accessible and engaging programs for aging communities.
