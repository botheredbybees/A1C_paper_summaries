## Developing Creative Approaches in Youth Justice: A Guide for Youth Justice Professionals

This document is a comprehensive guide developed by Professor Laura Caulfield, Mike Botham, and Chad Smith, aimed at youth justice professionals to implement creative approaches in their work with young people involved in the justice system. The guide stems from an initiative funded by the Youth Justice Board (YJB) for developing innovative methods to address serious youth violence. It highlights the importance of involving young people's feedback and preferences in service design and delivery, emphasizing that traditional worksheet-based activities are often ineffective.

The authors share their journey at Sandwell Youth Offending Service where they implemented various creative arts programs, such as drama and art sessions, to engage with a diverse group of young people. Despite initial resource constraints, the approach proved successful in increasing attendance and engagement among participants who previously showed reluctance or disinterest in conventional interventions.

The guide offers practical tips for professionals looking to integrate creativity into their youth justice practices, stressing the importance of building capacity within teams, developing external relationships with arts organizations, and understanding the impact of creative engagements. It also underscores the necessity of evaluating these initiatives to ensure they are evidence-based and contribute positively to young people's well-being.
