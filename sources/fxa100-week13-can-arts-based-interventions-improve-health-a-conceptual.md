## Can arts-based interventions improve health? A conceptual and methodological critique

The article critiques the claim that engaging with art improves mental and physical health, arguing against the notion of a strong evidence base supporting arts-based interventions (ABIs) as effective treatments for various disorders. The authors examine the methodological quality of studies cited in support of ABIs, finding significant weaknesses such as lack of randomization and control over confounding factors. They also evaluate broader experimental designs to determine if they can definitively attribute health benefits to art itself rather than other aspects of interventions. Key findings include poor definitions of what constitutes 'art' and inadequate demonstration that art induces distinct physiological processes relevant to disorder etiology. The critique highlights ethical implications for patients treated with ABIs without robust evidence support, suggesting a need for rigorous methodological standards in future research.
