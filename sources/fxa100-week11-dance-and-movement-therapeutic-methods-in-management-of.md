## DANCE AND MOVEMENT THERAPEUTIC METHODS IN MANAGEMENT OF DEMENTIA: A RANDOMIZED, CONTROLLED STUDY

This letter to the editor presents a randomized controlled trial evaluating dance and movement therapy (DMT) as an intervention for patients with dementia. The study aimed to determine whether DMT could improve cognitive functioning or behavioral symptoms in individuals with dementia. Participants were randomly assigned to either a DMT group or a control group engaging in regular nursing home activities.

The primary outcome measures included the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE), Clock Drawing Test, Word List savings score, and various subscales of the Nurses’ Observation Scale for Geriatric Patients (NOSGER). Although there were no significant overall differences between groups due to a lack of power, some trends suggested potential benefits from DMT. Specifically, improvements in visuospatial abilities and planning were observed in the DMT group as measured by the Clock Drawing Test, while self-care ability and instrumental activities of daily living showed slight improvements.

These findings suggest that DMT may offer a viable nonpharmacological intervention for managing certain aspects of dementia-related symptoms. The study underscores the importance of considering diverse therapeutic approaches to address behavioral and cognitive challenges in dementia care.
