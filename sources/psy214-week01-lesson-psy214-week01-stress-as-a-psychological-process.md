## Stress as a Stimulus: Stressors

This lesson discusses stress from the perspective of external stimuli known as 'stressors', focusing on two categories: major life changes/events and daily hassles. Major life events are assessed using the Holmes Rahe Scale (SRRS), which measures personal readjustment required for significant life changes, whether positive or negative. Daily hassles refer to everyday irritations that can accumulate and cause stress comparable to major life events. Criticisms of the SRRS include lack of specificity in event descriptions and the assumption that all life changes are equally stressful without considering individual appraisals. The lesson highlights that daily minor hassles often predict physical and psychological health better than major life changes.
