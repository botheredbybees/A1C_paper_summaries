## Dementia and Imagination: a mixed-methods protocol for arts and science research

The article 'Dementia and Imagination' is a mixed-methods study protocol designed to explore the benefits of visual art activities in dementia care. The research aims to address current evidence limitations surrounding these activities by investigating their impact on quality of life (QoL) and well-being for individuals living with dementia, as well as potential social and community benefits. It recruits participants from various settings including residential care homes, National Health Service wards, and communities across England and Wales. The study employs a visual art intervention delivered over three months in the form of weekly 2-hour group sessions to a total of 100 people with dementia. Both quantitative and qualitative data are collected at multiple time points to assess changes in QoL and well-being for participants, alongside perceptions from carers and staff. The protocol also includes an economic evaluation component to measure social return on investment. Through this comprehensive approach, the study seeks to provide robust evidence about the efficacy of visual art interventions in dementia care, offering practical insights for policy and practice improvements.
