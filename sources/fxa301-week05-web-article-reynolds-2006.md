## The role of art-making in identity maintenance: case studies of people living with cancer

This qualitative research investigates the role of art-making as a leisure activity for people with cancer, focusing on how visual art can support identity maintenance and reconstruction during chronic illness. The study examines three case studies: two women with breast cancer aged 47 and 59 years, and a man with stomach and lung cancer aged 51 years. These individuals engaged in art after reflecting on their experiences but did not reject their pre-illness identities. Art-making provided opportunities to retain familiar personal and social identities and resist being dominated by illness-related labels. The research highlights the importance of meaningful activities for maintaining positive identity and coping with cancer.
