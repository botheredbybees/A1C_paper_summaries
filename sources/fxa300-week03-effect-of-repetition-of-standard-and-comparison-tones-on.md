## Effect of Repetition of Standard and Comparison Tones on Recognition Memory for Pitch

This study by Diana Deutsch explores the impact of repeating standard or comparison tones during an interpolated sequence on the accuracy of recognizing tone pitch after a delay. Participants were presented with a sequence including a standard (S) and comparison (C) tone, separated by four other tones. When S and C tones were identical in pitch, inserting a similar pitch tone reduced errors. However, when S and C tones differed in pitch, inserting a tone at the S pitch decreased errors while a tone at the C pitch increased them. The study suggests that memory for tonal pitch is susceptible to interference from other sounds, indicating complex interactive effects within the pitch memory store.
