## The Laughter Prescription: A Tool for Lifestyle Medicine

This article explores the physiological and psychological benefits of laughter, emphasizing its potential as a therapeutic tool in lifestyle medicine. The authors review existing research on laughter's impact on stress hormones, immune function, pain tolerance, and overall mood. They differentiate between spontaneous (genuine) and self-induced laughter, noting that both can elicit positive physiological responses but spontaneous laughter is more closely linked to emotional well-being. The study suggests that physicians could prescribe structured laughter sessions as a non-invasive, cost-effective intervention for patients suffering from various health issues. This prescription of laughter aligns with the principles of lifestyle medicine by focusing on natural and easily distributable benefits without adverse side effects.
