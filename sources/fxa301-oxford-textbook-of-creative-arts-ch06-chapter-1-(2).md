## Introduction to the Field of Creative Arts, Wellbeing, and Health

This chapter introduces the global perspective on the role of creative arts in relation to health and wellbeing, tracing its historical context from ancient Greece to contemporary debates. It highlights the growing interest in arts for health practice over the last century, emphasizing the need for robust evidence-based research and credible evaluations to support future developments. The authors discuss the establishment of dedicated journals and organizations such as Arts & Health: An International Journal for Research, Policy and Practice Applied Arts and Health, advocating for broader recognition of the arts' role in healthcare. They also outline recent international conferences focused on arts and health, and explore various definitions of 'health,' particularly focusing on the World Health Organization's original 1946 definition, its evolution through academic discourse, and continued relevance despite criticisms.
