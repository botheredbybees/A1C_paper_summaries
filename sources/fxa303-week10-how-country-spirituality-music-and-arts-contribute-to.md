## How Country, Spirituality, Music and Arts Contribute to Indigenous Mental Health and Wellbeing

The article explores how attachment to country, spirituality, music, and arts contribute to the mental health and social and emotional wellbeing (SEWB) of Indigenous Australians. The authors argue that these factors are linked through a complex interplay of contextual elements rather than a single cause-effect relationship. They present specific examples from their research, such as a woman’s singing in Central Australia that connects her to past memories and identity. Music, for example, not only serves as an expression of internal feelings but also fosters social connections and access to economic resources, enhancing mental health indirectly through these pathways. The authors emphasize the importance of considering the rich contextual details surrounding engagement in such activities rather than abstracting them into general theories.
