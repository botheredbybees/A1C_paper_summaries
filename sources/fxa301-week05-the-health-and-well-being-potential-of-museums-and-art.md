## The health and well-being potential of museums and art galleries

This editorial discusses the growing recognition of museums and art galleries as key players in health and well-being initiatives, emphasizing their role beyond traditional cultural offerings to include programs that address specific societal challenges such as dementia and mental health issues. The authors highlight a range of benefits associated with engagement in museum activities, including reduced social isolation, improved communication among family members and healthcare providers, enhanced learning opportunities, and increased positive emotional experiences. Despite the acknowledged benefits, the article underscores the need for more robust empirical evidence to demonstrate the efficacy of these interventions. It also proposes frameworks for museums to collaborate with health authorities to develop comprehensive health initiatives, potentially including prescription referral services. The special issue aims to contribute to the existing knowledge base by presenting studies that explore the interplay between cultural heritage engagement and various health outcomes.
