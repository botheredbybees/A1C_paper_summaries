## Cultural Differences in Defining and Experiencing Happiness and Well-Being

This lesson explores the cultural differences in defining and experiencing happiness and well-being. It highlights that Western philosophical traditions have dominated current research on happiness and well-being, often overlooking non-Western perspectives. Eastern cultures emphasize collectivist values such as harmony with others, compassion, balance, acceptance of suffering, moral development, and transcendence compared to individualistic and hedonic aspects in Western models. The lesson also mentions the importance of understanding Indigenous conceptions of social and emotional well-being in Australia, which involve connections to body, mind and emotion, family and kinship, community, culture, country, and spirituality. There is a growing recognition of the need for cross-cultural validation of well-being theories.
