## Arts and Cultural Engagement, Reportedly Antisocial or Criminalized Behaviors, and Potential Mediators in Two Longitudinal Cohorts of Adolescents

The study by Bone et al. (2022) explores the impact of arts and cultural engagement on reportedly antisocial or criminalized behaviors in adolescents, focusing on two large longitudinal cohorts. The research highlights that increased participation in arts and cultural activities is associated with a reduction in such behaviors, better self-control scores, and fewer positive perceptions towards these behaviors both concurrently and prospectively over one to two years. This association suggests that regular engagement with the arts can provide opportunities for positive developmental outcomes, potentially mitigating risks of antisocial or criminalized behaviors. The authors argue that understanding these mechanisms may contribute to the development of broader prevention strategies beyond targeted interventions aimed at adolescents identified as 'delinquents'.
