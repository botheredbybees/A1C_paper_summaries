## Web Lecture 3 - Arts in the Community for Health and Wellbeing

This web lecture by Dr Brendan Lamb examines the multifaceted role of arts in enhancing community health and wellbeing. It outlines various social and physical benefits associated with arts engagement, including improvements in cognitive skills, mental health, and even physical conditions like Parkinson's disease. The lecture references specific studies such as Daykin (2017) and Zarobe & Bungay (2017), which highlight the emotional resilience developed through artistic activities. It also discusses the economic benefits of arts participation, noting that active engagement correlates with higher mental wellbeing scores, as demonstrated by Davies et al., (2016). The lecture further explores international initiatives in creative health and public policy frameworks, emphasizing the potential for arts to reduce healthcare burdens and promote community cohesion.
