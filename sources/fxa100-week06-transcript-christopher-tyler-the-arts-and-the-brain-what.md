## The Arts and the Brain: What Does Your Brain See?

This transcript captures a discussion on the intersection between neuroscience, art, and perception. Christopher Tyler, director of the Smith-Kettlewell Brain Imaging Center, explores how visual art interacts with the human brain through embodied cognition. Key examples include ancient cave paintings, Botticelli’s 'Birth of Venus', Artemisia Gentileschi's works, Jackson Pollock’s abstract expressionism, and Leonardo da Vinci’s anatomical drawings. Tyler discusses the concept of mirror neurons in the context of appreciating art, emphasizing how artworks can evoke physical sensations in viewers. He also touches on the neurological aspects of drawing, such as a study by Laura Lickover that uses fMRI to examine blindfolded drawing processes and their impact on brain plasticity and memory.
