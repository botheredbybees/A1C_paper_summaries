## Free Play and Children’s Mental Health

The article discusses the relationship between children's free play opportunities and their mental health, emphasizing the importance of secure emotional attachments and positive stress for healthy development. It highlights that a lack of play can lead to underdeveloped brain regions and impaired social functioning. The study suggests that structured play regimens in therapeutic settings can significantly improve motor, cognitive, and social functions. Additionally, it critiques the current policy response to children's mental health issues in the UK, noting an absence of consideration for positive changes in children’s life experiences that could prevent such problems.
