## Time for Tony the Turtle: Experiences with the use of a hand puppet in a program for young children in domestic violence shelters

The article discusses a support program, 'Time for Tony the Turtle,' designed to aid young children in domestic violence shelters by using a hand puppet named Tony the Turtle as an interactive tool. The study aims to explore how this intervention helps develop self-regulation skills and emotional safety among children while also improving communication between them and their mothers or caregivers. Through qualitative interviews with 11 children, 15 mothers, and 18 professionals involved in the program, three main themes emerged: Tony is seen as a friendly and engaging figure; he serves as an empathetic companion who shares similar experiences to those of shelter residents; and he encourages open dialogue about sensitive issues. Overall, the use of a hand puppet seems beneficial for children facing domestic violence, offering them a trustworthy confidant through which they can project their emotions and seek solutions.
