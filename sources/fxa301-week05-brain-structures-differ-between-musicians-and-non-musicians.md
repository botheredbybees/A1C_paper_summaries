## Brain Structures Differ between Musicians and Non-Musicians

The study by Gaser and Schlaug investigates structural differences in the brains of professional musicians compared to amateur musicians and non-musicians using voxel-based morphometry (VBM). The researchers found significant gray matter volume variations in motor, auditory, and visual-spatial brain regions. These differences suggest that extensive musical training may lead to structural adaptations rather than being solely due to innate predisposition. Key findings include an association between the intensity of practice and structural changes in specific brain areas. While these results contribute valuable insights into neural plasticity associated with skill acquisition, further research is needed to determine the precise contributions of genetic factors versus prolonged practice.
