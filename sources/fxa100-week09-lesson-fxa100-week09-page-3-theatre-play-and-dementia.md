## Theatre, Play and Dementia Care

This lesson explores the application of theatre and play in dementia care through various methods such as simulation games, dramatherapy, and the Veder Contact Method. It discusses how applied theatre simulations are used for training professionals like teachers and nurses to manage real-life situations, while also raising awareness about bias and conflict management. The importance of person-centered approaches is emphasized with reference to Tom Kitwood’s theory on malignant social psychology and the work by Dassa and Harel combining musical theatre and dramatic play elements in dementia care. The lesson highlights the Veder Contact Method from the Netherlands, which uses theatrical stimuli like costumes, props, and poetry to enhance communication and improve quality of life for individuals with dementia. Scholarly research supports its implementation in nursing homes, showing positive impacts on both caregivers and residents, although it notes challenges such as training needs and staff pressure.
