## Psychological First Aid: Supporting People Affected by Disaster in Australia

The document 'Psychological First Aid: Supporting People Affected by Disaster in Australia' provides a comprehensive guide for those working in disaster preparedness, response, and recovery to address the psychological needs of individuals affected by emergencies. It emphasizes the importance of providing timely psychosocial support rather than clinical mental health interventions to most people who experience distress after disasters. The guide highlights common and complex reactions such as feelings of guilt, sadness, anger, fear, confusion, helplessness, and physical symptoms like sweating or trembling. Key elements of psychological first aid include establishing a supportive presence, ensuring safety and security, meeting basic needs, connecting with social support networks, and fostering coping strategies. Additionally, the guide offers specific advice for supporting children and individuals with pre-existing mental health conditions, as well as self-care tips for those providing assistance in emergency settings. This resource is crucial for enhancing resilience and recovery among communities affected by disasters.
