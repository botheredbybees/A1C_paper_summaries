## The Benefits of Music Instruction on Processing Speed, Verbal Fluency, and Cognitive Control in Aging

This study investigates the effects of active versus passive music instruction on executive function in older adults aged 60-85. Participants were randomly assigned to either group piano instruction (GPI) or music listening instruction (MLI). Cognitive assessments, including processing speed and verbal fluency, were conducted pre- and post-instruction. Results indicated that GPI participants showed significant improvements in processing speed, verbal fluency, and cognitive control compared to the MLI group. These findings suggest that active engagement in musical activities can enhance cognitive functions crucial for successful aging.
