## FXA301 Sem 1 2026 - Week 4 Lecture

The lecture focuses on the principles and practicalities of designing arts-based projects for community health initiatives, emphasizing critical questions to address before project initiation. It highlights the importance of engaging with the community during planning, delivery, and evaluation phases, ensuring that all activities are respectful and inclusive. The document also underscores the need for comprehensive risk management strategies and detailed evaluation methods to assess both processes and outcomes. Creative evaluation techniques, such as using music or photography to document participants' experiences, are discussed alongside traditional quantitative and qualitative approaches. The AESOP framework is introduced as a guide for developing robust research projects in arts and health, aiming to bridge the gap between artistic practice and scientific inquiry.
