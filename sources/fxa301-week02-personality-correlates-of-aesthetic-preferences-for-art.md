## Personality Correlates of Aesthetic Preferences for Art, Architecture, and Music

The study by Cleridou and Furnham (2014) investigates the relationship between aesthetic preferences in art, architecture, and music with personality traits as measured by the Big-Five model. The research aims to classify artistic styles into a consistent taxonomy across different domains and assess how personality factors influence these preferences.

The authors developed five stylistic categories for their study: Sophisticated, Contemporary, Intense, Mellow, and Unpretentious. Participants rated aesthetic stimuli representing these styles and completed the International Personality Item Pool inventory to measure Big-Five traits. The findings showed that Openness to Experience is a strong predictor of aesthetic preferences across art and architecture but less consistent for music.

The study also highlights gender differences in preferences, with women generally preferring representational artworks over abstract ones. Additionally, familiarity with architectural styles influenced preference scores, mediating the impact of personality traits on these preferences. The research suggests that personality traits influence individuals' emotional and intellectual engagement with different art forms, impacting their aesthetic appreciation.
