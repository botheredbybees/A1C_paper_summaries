## Creative arts in psychotherapy for traumatized children in South Africa: An evaluation study

The study evaluates the impact of a creative arts psychotherapy program on traumatized children in South Africa, focusing on posttraumatic stress symptoms, behavioral problems, and posttraumatic growth (PTG). The research design was a non-randomized controlled trial conducted across four sites with 125 participants aged 7 to 13 years. Children were divided into treatment and control groups, receiving either the creative arts in psychotherapy program or a low-level supportive intervention without therapeutic activities. Although attrition rates were high (63.4%), significant reductions in hyperarousal and avoidance symptoms were found for the treatment group compared to the control group, with no significant effects on behavioral problems or PTG. The study underscores the feasibility of creative arts therapies in addressing trauma-related issues among children in underprivileged settings.
