## The MoMA Alzheimer’s Project: Making Art Accessible to People with Dementia

The MoMA Alzheimer's Project is a guide developed by the Museum of Modern Art for museums and organizations interested in creating programs that make modern and contemporary art accessible to individuals living with Alzheimer’s disease and their caregivers. This initiative builds upon MoMA's Meet Me at MoMA program, which offers monthly gallery tours and discussions during non-public hours. The guide provides detailed instructions on designing a museum program, staffing requirements, logistical considerations, and planning specific activities within the galleries. It emphasizes the importance of creating inclusive environments that cater to the unique needs of people with dementia, focusing on art as an expressive outlet for dialogue and engagement. Through practical training sessions and web-based resources, the project aims to broaden its reach across various communities, advocating for greater accessibility in arts programming for those affected by Alzheimer’s disease.
