## Drama Therapy with Older People with Dementia—Does it Improve Quality of Life?

This study evaluates the impact of drama therapy on the quality of life (QoL) of older adults with mild to moderate dementia using a mixed-method approach. Participants were divided into a drama therapy group and a movie-watching control group, both assessed for QoL using the QoL-AD scale before and after 16 group meetings. Although no statistically significant difference was found between groups, qualitative data indicated that participants in the drama therapy group expressed ideas and feelings more effectively, showing increased self-awareness of their wellbeing. The study highlights the potential benefits of further research into the use of drama therapy for dementia patients to improve QoL.
