## Where Science Meets Stories: A Systematic Review on Narrative Medicine

The article presents a systematic review of narrative medicine, an interdisciplinary field that integrates storytelling with medical practice to prioritize patient-centered care over disease-focused approaches. The study aimed to analyze the impact of narrative medicine on patients and their caregivers' experiences using PRISMA guidelines across various databases until February 2024. Through screening and data extraction from 724 initial articles, 42 studies were identified as meeting inclusion criteria. Despite methodological heterogeneity, the review highlighted positive impacts of narrative medicine on patient well-being and caregiver perspectives. Patient narratives not only improved personal health outcomes but also emphasized their needs and concerns. The study addresses limitations such as lack of standardized protocols and suggests avenues for future research to further integrate narrative approaches into clinical practice.
