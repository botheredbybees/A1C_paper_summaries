## Research ethics in arts in health interventions

Chapter 12 of 'Arts in Health: Designing and researching interventions' by Daisy Fancourt discusses the importance of ethical considerations in arts in health practices and research, despite their seemingly positive intentions. The chapter outlines how ethics are crucial throughout all stages of a project's lifecycle and provides examples of well-intentioned interventions that had negative outcomes to emphasize this point. It delves into specific ethical issues such as obtaining informed consent from participants, especially those who may be vulnerable, like children or individuals with diminished capacity to give consent. The chapter also covers general ethics of conduct, including the need for respect, competence, responsibility, and integrity in professional practice, advocating for ongoing awareness of ethical principles and continual professional development.
