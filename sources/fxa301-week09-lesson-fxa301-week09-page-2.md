## Planning for Inclusiveness in Arts Projects

This lesson discusses the concept of Inclusive Arts, which focuses on creative arts involvement by and for people with mental illness or physical/cognitive limitations. It emphasizes high-quality art that values diverse contributions and reduces societal marginalization. Key aspects include using inclusive language, adhering to the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), understanding the Social Model of Disability, and planning projects that prioritize collaboration and communication. The lesson also explores alternative models and various types of inclusive arts projects aimed at enhancing self-esteem, community engagement, health outcomes, and creative expression.
