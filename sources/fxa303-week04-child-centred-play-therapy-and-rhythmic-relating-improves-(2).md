## Child-Centred Play Therapy and Rhythmic Relating improves emotion regulation in autism

This study examines the efficacy of Child-Centred Play Therapy (CCPT) combined with Rhythmic Relating to improve emotion regulation in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The authors conducted a single-case pilot intervention involving a 5-year-old autistic boy over ten months, consisting of 25 weekly sessions. The study aimed to assess whether this therapeutic approach could enhance emotional well-being by facilitating co-regulation through play and rhythmic experiences. Parents rated the child's emotion regulation using the Emotion Regulation Checklist (ERC), which was completed weekly throughout the intervention period. Statistical analysis compared the participant's improvement in emotion regulation with a comparison group of autistic boys not receiving psychological therapies. Results indicated statistically significant improvements in emotion regulation, suggesting that this therapeutic combination could be beneficial for children with ASD. The findings support the notion that improving emotion regulation through playful and rhythmic interactions can positively impact mental health outcomes in autistic children.
