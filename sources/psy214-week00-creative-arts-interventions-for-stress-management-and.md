## Creative Arts Interventions for Stress Management and Prevention—A Systematic Review

This systematic review evaluates the efficacy of Creative Arts Therapies (CATs) and arts interventions for stress reduction and management. The authors, including Lily Martin and colleagues from the Research Institute for Creative Arts Therapies at Alanus University in Germany, conducted a thorough search across multiple databases to identify studies that utilized CAT modalities such as art therapy, music therapy, dance/movement therapy, and drama therapy. They included 37 studies, predominantly randomized controlled trials (RCTs), which reported significant stress reduction among participants who engaged in various arts interventions. The review underscores the growing importance of embodied approaches in managing stress and highlights the potential for creative arts therapies to offer innovative solutions for coping with the increasing demands of modern life. By integrating non-verbal communication and creative expression, CATs provide a comprehensive framework for addressing both physiological and psychological aspects of stress, thereby contributing significantly to health promotion and prevention strategies.
