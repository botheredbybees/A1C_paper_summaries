## The Remediation of the Personal Photograph and the Politics of Self-Representation in Digital Storytelling

This article explores the transformation of personal photography in the era of social media and digital storytelling, focusing on how online image-sharing has reshaped practices around selection, curation, manipulation, and editing. The authors discuss how these changes impact self-representation and privacy, particularly among activist-oriented queer digital storytellers who use their personal stories to advocate for social change. They examine the historical context of personal photography from its origins with the Kodak camera in 1888 through contemporary platforms like Flickr and Facebook. The study highlights that while digital technologies have expanded opportunities for self-expression and public engagement, they also raise significant ethical and political concerns about privacy and representation.
