## Features of University Environments That Support Well-Being as Perceived by Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Questioning Undergraduate Music and Art Students

Nicholas E. Roseth's study explores the well-being of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and questioning (LGBQ) undergraduate music and art students in university environments. Using data from the College Student Report by the National Survey of Student Engagement between 2013-2015, Roseth examines how these students perceive their institutions’ support for well-being based on Seligman’s theory dimensions: positive emotions, engagement, relationships, meaning, and achievement. The study finds similar ratings for heterosexual and LGBQ music/art students in terms of positive emotion, engagement, and relationships but highlights significant differences in the perception of meaning and achievement among non-music/art LGBQ students compared to their peers. These findings underscore the importance of university environments that cater specifically to the well-being needs of diverse student populations.
