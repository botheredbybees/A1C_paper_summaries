## Researching Phototherapy and Therapeutic Photography

This chapter discusses research methodologies in phototherapy and therapeutic photography, emphasizing their iterative nature and the interplay between methodological issues of knowledge (epistemology) and existence (ontology). It highlights the cultural context of research definitions and explores qualitative and quantitative approaches to psychotherapeutic research. The text also delves into visual research methods such as photo elicitation and participatory productions, suggesting that these methodologies are essential for understanding therapeutic practices. It poses critical questions for researchers engaging in phototherapy or therapeutic photography studies, advocating for clarity on the researcher's position and approach.
