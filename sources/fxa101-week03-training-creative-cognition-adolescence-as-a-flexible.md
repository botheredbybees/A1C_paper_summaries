## Training creative cognition: adolescence as a flexible period for improving creativity

The article investigates the developmental progression of creative ideation from adolescence to adulthood and examines whether it can be enhanced through training in divergent thinking. Creative cognition is defined as the ability to generate ideas that are novel yet feasible, critical for problem-solving across various domains. The study employed an alternative uses task (AUT) to measure creativity's core components: fluency, flexibility, and originality. Participants included adolescents aged 13-16 and adults aged 23-30 who underwent training in creative ideation or control tasks over two weeks. Results showed improvements in creative ideation and cognitive flexibility across all groups but not in general ideation. Adolescents exhibited greater benefits from training regarding originality compared to adults, suggesting increased brain plasticity during adolescence optimizes learning and exploratory behavior. The findings underscore the potential for enhancing creativity through targeted interventions even in adulthood.
