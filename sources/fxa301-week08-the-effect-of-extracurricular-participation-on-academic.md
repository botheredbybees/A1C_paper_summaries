## The Effect of Extracurricular Participation on Academic Outcomes and Attendance

This dissertation explores the impact of extracurricular activities on academic performance and attendance among eighth-grade students in a southwestern New Jersey school district. The study investigates whether participating in non-academic activities, such as athletics or music programs, correlates with better academic outcomes like PARCC test scores and final grade averages, especially for specific demographics including female students and those eligible for free and reduced lunch. Data was collected over the 2014-2015 school year and analyzed to identify statistically significant effects of extracurricular participation on these metrics. The research suggests that certain groups benefit more from participating in such activities, highlighting the importance of continued funding and encouragement for after-school programs.
