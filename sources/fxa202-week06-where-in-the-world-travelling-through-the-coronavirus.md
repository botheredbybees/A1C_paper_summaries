## WHERE IN THE WORLD: Travelling through the Coronavirus lockdown

Lisa Hathaway's photo essay 'WHERE IN THE WORLD' captures the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on global tourism and wildlife. The essay highlights how travel restrictions have affected iconic destinations such as Hawaii, New York City, Angkor Wat in Cambodia, Japan's cherry blossom season, and various tourist spots in Australia and Germany. Hathaway uses creative imagery to depict deserted airports, grounded airplanes, silent ski resorts, and bustling wetlands now devoid of human presence due to the pandemic. The essay underscores the significant economic repercussions for countries heavily reliant on tourism, as well as the environmental impacts noted through reduced pollution and animal sightings where humans are absent. The work also draws attention to the plight of wildlife sanctuaries and zoos that struggle to sustain animals without public funding and volunteer support. Hathaway's piece is a poignant commentary on the interconnectedness of human activities and natural environments, particularly in an era marked by global health crises.
