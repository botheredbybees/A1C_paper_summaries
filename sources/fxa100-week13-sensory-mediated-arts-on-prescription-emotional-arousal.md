## Sensory-mediated “arts on prescription”: emotional arousal mechanisms through Portuguese Azulejo craft workshop

This study investigates the therapeutic potential of Portuguese Azulejo craft workshops within an Arts on Prescription framework, focusing on sensory engagement and emotional arousal mechanisms. The research involves a cross-cultural workshop with twenty-five participants who engaged in a series of activities that combined tactile and visual experiences to create traditional tiles. Emotional states were measured before and after the intervention using scales such as Cultural Cognition and Culture Preservation intent, Subjective Happiness Scale, and Positive and Negative Affect Schedule (PANAS). The study found significant improvements in cultural cognition, subjective well-being, and culture preservation intent post-intervention. Participants also showed a positive affect increase, with emotional responses varying across the creative stages of initial anticipation, mid-stage stabilization, and late-stage fluctuation linked to outcome expectations. Additionally, the research explored relationships between personality types (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator) and color-emotion associations in the context of the craft experience. The findings underscore the therapeutic value of multisensory craft experiences for emotional regulation and cultural engagement, highlighting their potential as group-based interventions.
