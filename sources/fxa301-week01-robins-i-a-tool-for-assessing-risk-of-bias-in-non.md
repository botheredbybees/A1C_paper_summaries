## ROBINS-I: A Tool for Assessing Risk of Bias in Non-Randomized Studies of Interventions

This paper introduces ROBINS-I (Risk Of Bias In Non-Randomized Studies - of Interventions), a tool designed to evaluate the risk of bias in non-randomized studies of interventions, which are crucial for healthcare evaluation but often subject to confounding and other biases. The authors developed this tool over three years through expert consensus, focusing on seven distinct domains where bias might occur. ROBINS-I uses 'signalling questions' to help assessors judge the risk of bias within each domain, with these judgments contributing to an overall assessment. The paper emphasizes that ROBINS-I considers each non-randomized study as attempting to emulate a hypothetical pragmatic randomized trial, ensuring the evaluation addresses internal validity while distinguishing it from issues of generalizability. This tool is particularly relevant for systematic reviews incorporating non-randomized studies due to its comprehensive approach and clarity in assessing methodological rigor.
