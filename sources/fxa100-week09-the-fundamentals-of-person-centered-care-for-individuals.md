## The Fundamentals of Person-Centered Care for Individuals With Dementia

This article discusses the philosophy of person-centered care in dementia management, emphasizing its importance over traditional medical models that focus on treating symptoms rather than meeting individual needs. The authors review the historical development of this approach, pioneered by Carl Rogers and further developed by Tom Kitwood, who argued that dementia should be understood through an interplay between neurological impairment and psychosocial factors. Key principles include recognizing and maintaining individuals' selfhood and identity, fostering high-quality interpersonal relationships, and tailoring care to preserve personal well-being. The article highlights the importance of commitment from all organizational members and outlines tools for measuring person-centered care effectiveness. It underscores that more research is needed to fully understand and improve the implementation of this approach in dementia care settings.
