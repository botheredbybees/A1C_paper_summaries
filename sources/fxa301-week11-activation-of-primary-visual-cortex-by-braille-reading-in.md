## Activation of Primary Visual Cortex by Braille Reading in Blind Subjects

This study investigates the neural activity in the visual cortex of blind individuals during tactile tasks, specifically Braille reading and non-discrimination tasks involving rough surfaces. Positron emission tomography (PET) scans were used to measure cerebral blood flow (CBF) changes in eight proficient Braille readers compared with normal sighted subjects performing similar tactile discrimination tasks. The results reveal that Braille reading activates the primary visual cortex (V1) and secondary visual areas bilaterally in blind individuals, while these same tasks result in deactivation of V1 in sighted participants. Additionally, non-Braille tactile discrimination tasks also activated V1 in blind subjects but with less intensity compared to Braille reading. In contrast, sighted participants exhibited a reduction in CBF within the visual cortex during such tasks. These findings suggest that sensory deprivation can lead to functional reorganization of the brain where normally vision-dedicated areas adapt to process tactile information, highlighting plasticity and cross-modal recruitment in cortical function.
