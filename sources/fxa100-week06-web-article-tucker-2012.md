## How Does the Brain Process Art?

This article discusses the emerging field of neuroaesthetics, which uses brain-imaging techniques to study how the brain processes art. Research has shown that viewing artworks can activate specific areas in the primary motor cortex and mirror neurons, reflecting an empathetic response to depicted actions or movements. Studies like David Freedberg's use transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) to monitor neural activity when subjects view detailed images from paintings such as Michelangelo’s _Expulsion from Paradise_. Neuroscientists like Ulrich Kirk explore the contextual impact of art presentation on brain responses, suggesting that the environment influences aesthetic appreciation.
