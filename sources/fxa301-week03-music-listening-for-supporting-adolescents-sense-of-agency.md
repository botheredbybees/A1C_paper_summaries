## Music Listening for Supporting Adolescents’ Sense of Agency in Daily Life

This study investigates the impact of music listening on adolescents' sense of agency in daily life. Sense of agency refers to one's ability to influence their environment and functioning, closely linked to self-efficacy and well-being. The research explores whether music can enhance this sense of agency during everyday activities and identifies contextual factors that might influence such effects. Using a mobile app called MuPsych, the study collected data from 44 adolescents (average age 14) over several days, recording their emotional states and reasons for listening to music in various situations. Analysis revealed that while there was no general increase in agency across all instances of music listening, specific situational factors significantly influenced the changes in both internal and external sense of agency. For instance, changing environments negatively impacted external agency, whereas initial mood and reasons such as enjoyment or coping positively affected internal agency. The study underscores the dynamic nature of agency and highlights that music can support adolescents' sense of agency under particular conditions, offering new insights into how this resource might be effectively employed for health and development.
