## Me and us: Cultivating presence and mental health through choir singing

This article explores the impact of choir singing on mental health and social-emotional well-being through qualitative research with participants engaged in a project called 'A Song for the Mind.' The study highlights how joining and engaging in choirs can enhance presence, awareness, and emotional connection, fostering both personal growth and relational solidarity. Key findings include the ability to connect with complex feelings and visualize challenges through singing, leading to self-awareness and an openness towards others and nature. This process is deemed meaningful and potentially transformative for individuals involved, offering therapeutic benefits beyond traditional clinical interventions. The research underscores the significance of music as a medium for social-emotional competence and suggests that choir activities hold learning and empowering potentials for mental health care.
