## Making Music

The chapter 'Making Music' explores the multifaceted impacts of music on mental and physical health. It highlights that music is not only a fundamental human pursuit but also an integral part of healing rituals and therapeutic interventions throughout history. The authors present evidence supporting the psychological benefits of music, such as reducing depression and enhancing well-being across various demographics. For instance, regular music engagement among older adults can prevent social isolation and depression, while group singing may improve quality of life for individuals with chronic health conditions.

The chapter also discusses physical health benefits like reduced post-operative pain and improved pulmonary function through rhythmic activities such as drumming. Two case studies are presented: one involving group drumming workshops for mental health service users in London, which significantly reduced anxiety and depression levels; the second involves singing programs for women with symptoms of postnatal depression (PND), showing faster symptom improvement compared to usual care.

Key mechanisms highlighted include music's emotional engagement, social inclusion, and physiological changes that contribute to improved well-being. The chapter concludes by emphasizing the potential of music in holistic health care and providing practical tips for engaging in musical activities for better health.
