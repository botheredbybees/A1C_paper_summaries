## Design matters in dementia care: the role of the physical environment in dementia care settings

This chapter discusses the significance of physical environmental design in enhancing quality of life for individuals with dementia, focusing on long-term care facilities. It explores how well-designed spaces can mitigate challenging behaviors and foster positive outcomes such as reduced agitation, increased social interaction, and improved daily functioning. Key theories discussed include individual competence and environmental press model, model of place, and progressively lowered threshold model, which emphasize the interplay between personal capability and environmental stressors on behavior and affective responses in dementia care settings.
