## Overview of Clowns and Clowning in Society

This lesson explores the historical and cultural significance of clowns and clowning. Ancient civilizations like Egypt, Greece, and Rome featured clown characters that used humor to critique societal norms. Medieval jesters played roles in royal courts and were key figures in theatrical productions such as Shakespeare's plays. In the modern era, clowns have been represented through various artistic mediums including paintings by Picasso and music by Schoenberg. The lesson also discusses psychological aspects of coulrophobia, the fear of clowns, and its impact on therapeutic clowning practices.
