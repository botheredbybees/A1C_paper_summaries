## Dementia discourse – A rethink?

In this editorial, Fran McInerney discusses the discourse surrounding dementia and argues for a reevaluation of certain terminologies used to describe individuals living with the condition. Drawing on Sontag’s critique of illness metaphors, McInerney critiques the use of terms like ‘dementia tsunami’ that depict people with dementia as natural disasters. She supports the movement towards person-centered language guidelines but highlights potential issues with excluding words such as 'suffering' and 'sufferer'. McInerney draws upon her background in palliative care to argue that acknowledging suffering is important for recognizing the needs of individuals in advanced stages of dementia, where unrelieved pain and other symptoms are common. The article emphasizes the need for a balanced approach that recognizes both dignity and the reality of human frailty associated with terminal conditions like advanced dementia.
