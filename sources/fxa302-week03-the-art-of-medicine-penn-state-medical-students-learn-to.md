## The Art of Medicine: Penn State Medical Students Learn to Communicate from the Masters

The Penn State College of Medicine offers a course, 'Impressionism and the Art of Communication,' designed to enhance medical students' communication skills through the study of impressionist art. Dr. Michael Flanagan teaches this four-week class using painting exercises and studying famous artists like Vincent van Gogh to illustrate effective communication techniques in medicine. The course emphasizes the importance of open-ended questioning, active listening, and empathy in patient interactions, enabling medical students to gather more comprehensive information from patients. This approach contrasts with traditional medical education that often lacks formal training in communication skills. Dr. Flanagan’s innovative curriculum aims to foster a more humanistic approach among future physicians, encouraging personalized engagement with patients while maintaining thoroughness.
