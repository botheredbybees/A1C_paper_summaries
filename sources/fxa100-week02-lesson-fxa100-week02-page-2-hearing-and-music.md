## Hearing and Music in Dementia Care

This lesson covers aspects of hearing and music processing. It describes the physiological process of sound perception through the ear, emphasizing the role of nerve fibers in converting vibrations into electrical signals for brain interpretation. The lesson also addresses misconceptions about left-brain/right-brain functionality using a video by SciShow. Additionally, it explains musical elements such as pitch, rhythm, volume, tempo, and timbre, providing definitions and examples. It highlights how music activates various parts of the brain during processing, recall, emotional response, active participation, and linguistic engagement.
