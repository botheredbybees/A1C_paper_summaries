## Shifting perspectives: exploring the intersection of traditional and digital photography in contemporary artistic practices

Hanan Muneer Al Sheikh's article explores the interplay between traditional and digital photography within contemporary artistic practices. The study centers on Palestinian artist Ahmed Al-Saffarini’s innovative experiment, which involves burying vintage photographic negatives underground for an extended period to explore their transformative potential when integrated with digital techniques. Through a philosophical approach that encompasses phenomenology, art-based research methodologies are advocated as essential in understanding the multifaceted nature of photography in the digital age. This study highlights how contemporary photography transcends mere documentation and becomes a medium of artistic expression, enriched by technological innovation. It also examines the role of voids and absences within photographic representation and their impact on our perception of reality. Al Sheikh’s research underscores the cultural and societal significance of this evolution, arguing for the continued relevance of interdisciplinary collaboration in exploring photography's transformative potential.
