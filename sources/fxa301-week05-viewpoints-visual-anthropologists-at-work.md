## Viewpoints: Visual Anthropologists at Work

The review discusses the book 'Viewpoints: Visual Anthropologists at Work' edited by Mary Strong and Laena Wilder, published in 2009 by University of Texas Press. The book provides a comprehensive overview of various approaches to visual anthropology through contributions from different practitioners. It includes chapters on photography, historical images, moving pictures like film and video, and less mainstream methods. Key strengths highlighted are the accessible layout, extensive use of imagery with detailed captions, and practical exercises for students. Particular emphasis is placed on sections dealing with ethnographic films and unusual subject areas such as primate studies and dance forms. The reviewer notes a notable absence in discussing web-based approaches but praises the volume overall for its broad coverage of theoretical and methodological applications in visual anthropology.
