## The Role of Memory in Creative Ideation

This paper introduces the Memory in Creative Ideation (MemiC) framework, which elucidates the role of memory across distinct stages of creative ideation: memory search, candidate idea construction, novelty evaluation, and effectiveness evaluation. The authors argue that creativity involves controlled retrieval from semantic and episodic memory rather than spontaneous activation alone, challenging traditional associative theories of creativity. By integrating insights from cognitive science and neuroscience, the MemiC framework offers a comprehensive account of how creative ideas emerge through dynamic engagement with stored information. This perspective is crucial for understanding the neurocognitive processes underlying human creativity in the context of contemporary psychological research.
