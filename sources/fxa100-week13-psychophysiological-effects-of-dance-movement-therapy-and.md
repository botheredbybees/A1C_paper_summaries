## Psychophysiological Effects of Dance Movement Therapy and Physical Exercise on Older Adults With Mild Dementia: A Randomized Controlled Trial

This study evaluates the psychophysiological effects of dance movement therapy (DMT) and physical exercise on older adults with mild dementia through a randomized controlled trial. Participants were assigned to DMT, exercise, or waitlist control groups. The results indicated significant improvements in depression, loneliness, negative mood, daily functioning, and diurnal cortisol slope for the DMT group but no similar benefits for the exercise group. These findings suggest that DMT is more effective than physical exercise in addressing multifaceted aspects of dementia, including psychological well-being and neuroendocrine functions. The research highlights the potential utility of DMT as a nonpharmacological intervention to enhance quality of life for individuals with mild cognitive decline.
