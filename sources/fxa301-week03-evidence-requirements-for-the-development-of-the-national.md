## Evidence Requirements for the Development of the National Australian Arts and Health Framework

The article by Helen Zigmond discusses the process of developing evidence to support the National Arts and Health Framework in Australia, endorsed by health ministers in November 2013. The Institute for Creative Health was tasked with providing necessary evidence throughout the framework's development and moderating sector responses to draft proposals. A two-phase approach was identified: first, to convince government of the value of arts in health, and second, to meet policy development requirements relevant to health portfolio needs. Zigmond highlights that arts and health practice had been predominantly funded through arts and community cultural development practices until a more coherent governmental approach emerged. The initiative also aligned with efforts to strengthen the contribution of arts to individual and community health within the National Cultural Policy. This framework exemplifies Australia's leadership in integrating arts into healthcare systems, alongside Finland, emphasizing the importance of appropriate evidence at all stages.
