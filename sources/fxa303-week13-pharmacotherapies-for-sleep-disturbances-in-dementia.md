## Pharmacotherapies for sleep disturbances in dementia

The review assesses the efficacy and adverse effects of various drug treatments for sleep disorders in people with dementia, focusing on melatonin, trazodone, ramelteon, and orexin antagonists. Despite some beneficial evidence from small trials regarding these drugs, particularly orexin antagonists showing potential improvements in nocturnal sleep time and decreased nighttime awakenings, the overall lack of robust evidence for commonly prescribed medications remains a significant issue. The review highlights the need for larger, more diverse clinical trials to better understand drug efficacy and safety profiles in this vulnerable population.
