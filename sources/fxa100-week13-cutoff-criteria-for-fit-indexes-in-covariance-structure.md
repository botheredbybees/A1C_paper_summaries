## Cutoff Criteria for Fit Indexes in Covariance Structure Analysis: Conventional Criteria Versus New Alternatives

This article evaluates the adequacy of conventional cutoff criteria and new alternatives for assessing model fit in structural equation modeling (SEM) using various fit indexes. The authors introduce a two-index presentation strategy involving standardized root mean squared residual (SRMR) supplemented with other fit indices such as Tucker-Lewis Index (TLI), Comparative Fit Index (CFI), etc. They find that cutoff values close to 0.95 for TLI, BL89, CFI, RNI, and Gamma Hat; around 0.90 for McDonald's Centrality Index (Mc); around 0.08 for SRMR; and around 0.06 for RMSEA indicate a relatively good fit between the hypothesized model and observed data. The study highlights the necessity of using these cutoffs in combination to reject models with misspecifications appropriately. Furthermore, it suggests that some indexes like TLI, Mc, and RMSEA tend to overreject true-population models at small sample sizes, making them less reliable for such samples.
