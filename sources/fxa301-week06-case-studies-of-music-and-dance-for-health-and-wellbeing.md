## Case Studies of Music and Dance for Health and Wellbeing

The document presents case studies focusing on the use of music and dance in healthcare environments to enhance well-being. The first case study, 'Music on the Move,' discusses initiatives at Launceston General Hospital where musicians perform live music in public hospital areas. This initiative aims to provide therapeutic benefits and improve patient, visitor, and staff morale through serendipitous encounters with live music. Registered music therapists consult on these programs, ensuring ethical conduct and offering specific training for musicians working within healthcare settings.

The second case study examines the use of dance as a therapeutic intervention for individuals living with Parkinson's disease. The neurological condition is characterized by motor symptoms such as tremor, slowed movement, freezing gait, and balance issues, all contributing to reduced quality of life. Rhythmic auditory stimulation, including music and dance, can help mitigate these symptoms by providing structured rhythmic cues that assist in maintaining regular movement patterns.

Overall, the document highlights the multifaceted role of arts in healthcare settings, emphasizing both the therapeutic benefits for patients and the rewarding experiences for musicians performing in such environments.
