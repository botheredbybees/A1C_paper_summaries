## I'm Still Here: A Breakthrough Approach To Understanding Someone Living With Alzheimer's

John Zeisel's book, 'I'm Still Here,' offers a comprehensive and compassionate approach to understanding the lived experience of individuals diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease. Dr. Zeisel draws from his extensive background in sociology and architecture to explore innovative methods for enhancing quality of life for those living with dementia. Central to the text is the ARTZ program he developed, which uses guided museum tours and cultural experiences to engage individuals with Alzheimer’s more deeply through the arts. The book emphasizes nonpharmacological treatments and highlights how architectural design can significantly improve daily living conditions for people with Alzheimer's by addressing specific needs such as exit control, walking paths, privacy, shared spaces, gardens, homelike qualities, sensory understanding, and supports for independence and empowerment. Zeisel argues that while certain cognitive functions decline, many aspects of emotional perception and observational skills persist, providing hope for continued engagement and interaction in the later stages of the disease. By sharing personal narratives and insights from both medical professionals and individuals with lived experience, Zeisel challenges common misconceptions about Alzheimer's and presents it as a condition amenable to intervention and improvement rather than one marked solely by decline.
