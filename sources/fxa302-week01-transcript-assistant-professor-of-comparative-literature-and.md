## Assistant Professor of Comparative Literature and Medicine on Medical Humanities

This transcript captures a discussion by Assistant Professor Alvan Ikoku about the intersection of medical humanities. The professor emphasizes critical thinking, challenging assumptions, and incorporating diverse arguments in humanistic inquiry relevant to various healthcare professionals. He defines medical humanities as interdisciplinary work involving medicine, literature, and social sciences, highlighting differences between policy-making and literary approaches while noting their commonalities. His research focuses on how novels produce knowledge about medicine and the rhetorical devices used by physicians to argue for specific medical interventions. The conversation also delves into ethical debates in both literature and policy declarations, such as those from the World Health Organization, illustrating a need to integrate scientific and humanistic perspectives for comprehensive care.
