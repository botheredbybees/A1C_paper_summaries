## Brain Development During Adolescence

This review article discusses the neuroscientific insights into brain development during adolescence, highlighting significant reorganization of neural structures and functions that occur beyond early childhood. The authors emphasize that adolescent brains undergo substantial changes in gray matter density and white matter volume, which are crucial for cognitive and emotional maturation. They note an imbalance between mature subcortical areas responsible for reward processing and less developed prefrontal cortex regions involved in executive function and behavioral control. This developmental disparity may contribute to typical teenage behaviors like risk-taking and impulsivity. The review also highlights the implications of this high brain plasticity on adolescent health, suggesting that while it enables learning and adaptation, it can also make adolescents more susceptible to harmful environmental influences.
