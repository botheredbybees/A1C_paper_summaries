## Ethical Considerations and Collaborative Practices in International Art Therapy for Disaster Recovery

This chapter discusses the ethical considerations and collaborative practices involved in an international art therapy project for children affected by natural disasters in Japan. The arts retreat was a multinational initiative aimed at providing psychosocial support to Japanese children following the Fukushima disaster. Art therapists from LASALLE College of the Arts, Singapore, facilitated open art studio sessions that were informed by theories related to trauma and culture. Key themes include ethical guidelines, cultural sensitivity, confidentiality issues, and the role of art therapy in promoting resilience among traumatized youth. The chapter also highlights challenges such as photo documentation, cross-cultural communication barriers, and the need for continuous ethical reflection.
