## Art and the brain: a view from dementia

This paper explores the impact of dementia on artistic production, focusing on visual art created by artists diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease (AD), fronto-temporal dementia (FTD), and dementia with Lewy bodies (DLB). The study analyzed artwork from 14 patients and found distinct changes in spatial aspects, color use, and thematic representation depending on the type of dementia. For instance, AD was associated with distortions in spatial relations and a reduction in detail, while FTD showed perseverative themes and realistic representations. DLB artworks were characterized by simplicity and bizarre content. The authors argue that these changes reflect specific neurobiological alterations within different brain regions affected by each type of dementia. They propose a novel classificatory scheme to better understand the neural mechanisms underlying higher artistic functions, emphasizing the need for an integrative approach considering cognitive, emotional, creative, and personality aspects.
