## Clown therapy for procedural pain in children: a systematic review and meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluates the effectiveness of clown therapy in reducing procedural pain and anxiety in children undergoing venipuncture or peripheral vein cannulation procedures. The study analyzed six randomized controlled trials involving a total of 517 pediatric subjects. Results indicate that, although clown therapy appears effective in alleviating procedural pain for school-aged children and adolescents, overall reductions were not statistically significant due to high variability and methodological limitations across studies. Moreover, the intervention led to significantly lower anxiety levels compared to standard care methods. The authors conclude that while clown therapy shows promise as a non-pharmacological approach, its efficacy remains uncertain given the limited quality of existing research.
