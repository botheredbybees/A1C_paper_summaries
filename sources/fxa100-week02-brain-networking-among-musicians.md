## Brain Networking among Musicians

The video transcript discusses the phenomenon of brain synchronization among musicians during performances, focusing on the work conducted by Johanna Zena and her research team. The study explores how the brains of musicians playing together synchronize their neural activity to achieve a harmonious sound. This neurosynchronicity is described as a form of communal brain function, where brain waves oscillate in tandem but not necessarily at identical intervals. Researchers observed significant synchronization during specific moments when two musicians start a new section together, suggesting that these coordination points are crucial for the overall network formation. The study also extends to examining audience members' brain wave activity, indicating potential broader neurological networks during musical performances. This research could have therapeutic implications for individuals with communication challenges due to cognitive disorders, such as autism or schizophrenia, by potentially improving their ability to communicate effectively.
