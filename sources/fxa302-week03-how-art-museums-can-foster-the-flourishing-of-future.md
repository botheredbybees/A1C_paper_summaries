## How Art Museums Can Foster the Flourishing of Future Physicians

This article explores the potential of art museums in fostering the flourishing of medical students, focusing on enhancing their holistic well-being and professional development through Visual Thinking Strategies (VTS) and other museum-based pedagogies. The authors argue that traditional medical education often prioritizes technical skills over humanistic understanding, leaving students vulnerable to emotional stress and disconnection from personal values. Art museums provide a supportive environment for emotional regulation, mindfulness, and reflection, promoting mental and physical health, happiness, life satisfaction, meaning and purpose, character virtues, and close social relationships. These activities help medical learners navigate the challenges of their training by fostering resilience, empathy, and connection to patients.
