## Health and well-being of male international migrants and non-migrants in Bangladesh: A cross-sectional follow-up study

This research article examines the health and well-being of male international migrants compared to non-migrants in Bangladesh through a longitudinal study using data from the Matlab subdistrict. The study found that current international migrants had better self-rated health and physical strength than non-migrants, possibly due to positive self-selection into migration. However, they also exhibited higher risks for overweight/obesity, hypertension, and depressive symptoms compared to both internal migrants and non-migrants. These negative health impacts may result from harsh migration conditions and assimilation into host population conditions. The research highlights the need for bilateral cooperation to address the health challenges faced by guest workers in international labor markets.
