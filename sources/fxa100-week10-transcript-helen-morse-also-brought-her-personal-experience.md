## Helen Morse's Interpretation of Dementia Through Drama

The transcript details Helen Morse's personal and professional journey involving dementia. She discusses the initial inspiration behind her work derived from her family situation with young children and elderly parents as well as a previous project about detention. Her involvement in meetings with carers of people with dementia further motivated her to create a narrative that respects the lived experiences of those affected by dementia. The main message emphasized is that dementia results in a change in a person rather than a loss, advocating for an understanding beyond 'doom and gloom' and embracing humor as part of the experience. Helen stresses the importance of physicality in representing emotional states through dance, highlighting the collaborative effort from various artists contributing to the project's multifaceted approach.
