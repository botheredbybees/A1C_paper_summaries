## The influence of low and moderate carotid stenosis on neurophysiologic status of patients undergoing on-pump coronary artery bypass grafting

This study examines the impact of low and moderate internal carotid artery (ICA) stenosis (<50%) on neurophysiologic status in patients undergoing coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). The research involved 45 patients aged between 45 to 70 years, divided into two groups: those with ICA stenosis and a control group without stenosis. Pre-operative assessments included neuropsychological tests and electroencephalography (EEG) for monitoring cognitive functions such as visual-motor reactions, short-term memory, and attention levels. The study revealed that patients with ICA stenosis exhibited negative neurophysiologic changes 6 months post-surgery compared to those without stenosis, indicating a higher risk of cerebral complications after CABG. These findings suggest that low and moderate ICA stenoses are significant factors affecting the brain health of coronary heart disease (CHD) patients undergoing surgical intervention.
