## Effects of Choir Singing on Mental Health: Results of an Online Cross-sectional Study

The study investigates the impact of choir singing on mental health and well-being among German adult choristers. The research utilized an online questionnaire to gather data from 847 participants, assessing various socio-demographic factors and choral-related characteristics. Key findings reveal that individuals generally perceive positive effects of singing on their mental health, with women rating these benefits higher than men and those with pre-existing vocal pathologies or lower well-being scores experiencing slightly reduced benefits. Factors such as longer choir membership duration, increased weekly singing hours, and high engagement in chorals were linked to greater mental health improvements. The study underscores the positive influence of collective singing on psychological wellness across different demographics, emphasizing the role of optimal vocal behavior and regular choral activity.
