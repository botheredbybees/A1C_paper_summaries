## Exploring the Use of Hip Hop-Based Music Therapy to Address Trauma in Asylum Seeker and Unaccompanied Minor Migrant Youth

This article discusses the prevalence of trauma among asylum seeker and unaccompanied minor migrant youth, particularly post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and complex PTSD (C-PTSD). It highlights a seven-month-long music therapy intervention in Spain using Hip Hop-based methods with young asylum seekers. The study identifies that interventions must be engaging and considerate to the needs and preferences of the target population. A new model, the Integral Hip Hop Methodology, was developed as an effective tool for trauma-informed care among this demographic.
