## Dementia Words Matter: Guidelines on Language About Dementia

The document 'Dementia Words Matter' provides guidelines for journalists, organizations, and communications departments regarding the use of language when discussing dementia. The central argument is that the language used influences how people with dementia are perceived by others and impacts their self-perception. Key findings indicate that individuals with dementia prefer accurate, balanced, and respectful descriptions over derogatory terms such as 'dementia sufferer,' 'demented,' or 'burden.' The document offers alternative phrases like 'person with dementia' to promote a more positive image of those living with the condition. It emphasizes the importance of avoiding sensationalist language that may be used for dramatic effect but can have negative implications for individuals with dementia. Additionally, it advises on using appropriate images and terminology when discussing personal stories or specific contexts involving people with dementia.
