## The experience of dementia

Tom Kitwood’s chapter on the experience of dementia addresses how people with cognitive impairments are often disregarded in discussions about subjective experiences. Historically, there has been a lack of recognition for the subjectivity and needs of individuals living with dementia, partly due to assumptions that their thoughts and feelings were unworthy of consideration or rational discussion. Kitwood emphasizes the importance of understanding each person’s unique experience and the role of intersubjectivity in care practice. He notes that while some progress has been made in recognizing the subjective experiences of those with mild cognitive impairments, deeper exploration into severe impairment remains challenging due to the fragmented nature of their lived reality. The chapter also explores how personality types can influence coping mechanisms and emotional responses, suggesting a wide variation in the experience of dementia from person to person.
