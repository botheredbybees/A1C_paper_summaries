## Active Energy Project and Wellbeing in Older Adults

The Active Energy project is described as a collaborative initiative between artists and older adults in Pittsburgh that aimed to address contemporary issues related to aging, health, and community engagement. The project featured large-scale video installations of interviews with Northside Seniors and Geezers, emphasizing the value of their life experiences. It also included an education program for young people and facilitated dialogue through a video booth for visitors to contribute their own stories. The project highlights how creative arts can enhance the wellbeing of older adults by providing them with opportunities to express their concerns and share valuable personal narratives. Key themes include the significance of community engagement, participatory methods in art projects, and the role of such initiatives in improving quality of life.
