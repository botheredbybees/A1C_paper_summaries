## Mental Health Among LGBT Youth

The chapter examines the mental health disparities among lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) youth compared to their heterosexual and cisgender peers. LGBT youth report higher rates of suicidality, depression, and substance use, which are largely attributed to experiences of minority stress due to discrimination, bullying, and internalized negativity. The chapter discusses the theory of minority stress, distinguishing between distal and proximal stressors that affect LGB individuals. Distal stressors involve external factors like verbal harassment and employment discrimination, while proximal stressors include internalized homonegativity and fear of rejection. Adolescents are particularly vulnerable due to peer feedback and parental reactions during disclosure of their sexual orientation. The chapter also explores resilience and protective factors within the LGBT youth population.
