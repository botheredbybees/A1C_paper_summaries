## Conclusions, Reflections and Where to Next?

In this concluding lesson of the FXA100 course on the arts and dementia care, Dr. Brendan Lamb reflects on the 12-week curriculum that covered various creative arts therapies for dementia patients, including music, visual arts, dance, and drama. The focus has been on the person-centered approach these therapies offer and their accessibility in supporting cognitive, emotional, social, and physical health of individuals with dementia. Key questions are posed to consider common benefits of arts-based therapies in dementia care, potential contributions to carers' support, and strategies for integrating creative arts in healthcare post-2026. The next section will summarize key findings from the course modules and explore contemporary case studies that highlight current trends and future research directions in this field.
