## A Qualitative Investigation of Older Adults’ Conceptualization of Quality of Life and a Think-Aloud Content Validation of the EQ-5D-5L, SF-12v2, Warwick Edinburgh Mental Well-Being Scale, and Office of National Statistics-4

This study explores older adults’ understanding of quality of life (QoL) and the content validity of health-related quality-of-life measures (EQ-5D-5L, SF-12v2) and well-being measures (Warwick Edinburgh Mental Wellbeing Scale [WEMWBS] and Office of National Statistics-4 [ONS-4]). Qualitative think-aloud interviews with 20 older adults aged 75+ revealed that QoL is centered around health, ability to perform usual activities, social contact, and emotional functioning. Participants noted issues with negatively phrased mental items and preferred the more function-focused EQ-5D-5L over subjective WEMWBS and ONS-4 items. The study suggests adding domains such as social contact, coping, security, dignity, and control to improve comprehensiveness in measuring QoL for older adults.
