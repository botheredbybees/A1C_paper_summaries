## Puppets Facilitate Attention to Social Cues in Children with ASD

This research article explores the effectiveness of using puppets as social agents to enhance visual attention in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The study compares how children with ASD and typically developing peers respond to a video featuring interactions between a puppet and a human speaker. Results indicate that, unlike typical-developing controls who exhibit significant interest in human faces, children with ASD showed limited engagement towards the human but exhibited comparable attention levels towards the puppet as their neurotypical counterparts. This suggests puppets could be effective therapeutic tools due to their ability to deliver simplified, predictable social cues and engage in reciprocal interactions, facilitating better social communication skills in young children diagnosed with ASD.
