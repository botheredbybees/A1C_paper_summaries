## Shared Music Listening

This chapter discusses the therapeutic benefits of shared music listening in dementia care, focusing on the concept of receptive music listening where individuals are open and present to the experience. The author, Claire Garabedian, explores how this form of musical activity can create a safe space for people with dementia and their caregivers or family members, allowing them to connect emotionally despite physical or cognitive barriers. Through case studies involving residents in care homes and their relatives or staff, the chapter highlights significant improvements in engagement and emotional bonding over weekly music sessions. For instance, Maggie, a resident, and her daughter Jean experienced a transformation from initial disconnection to joyful interaction during live cello performances. Similarly, Aileen, another resident, and her nephew Tommy developed a closer rapport through shared musical experiences, indicating that receptive music listening can foster meaningful relationships and alleviate feelings of isolation for those affected by dementia.
