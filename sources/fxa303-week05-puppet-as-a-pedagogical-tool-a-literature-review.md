## Puppet as a Pedagogical Tool: A Literature Review

The article presents a literature review of the use of puppets as pedagogical tools in educational contexts, focusing on their benefits and potential applications from 2000 to 2017. The review identifies five primary uses for puppets: generating communication, supporting positive classroom climate, enhancing creativity, fostering cooperation within groups, and changing attitudes. It highlights the role of puppets in various formal education settings, including kindergarten through secondary school, and discusses their effectiveness in different subject areas like art and science. Despite the potential benefits highlighted by numerous case studies and qualitative research, there is a noted lack of comprehensive empirical evidence supporting the use of puppets as pedagogical tools. The review also mentions that while puppetry can be used to address special needs children and enhance therapeutic practices, this article focuses exclusively on formal education contexts.
