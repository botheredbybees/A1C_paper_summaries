## Mental Health in Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender (LGBT) Youth

This review article discusses the mental health issues faced by lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) youth in contemporary society. Despite increasing public acceptance of LGBT individuals, these youths still face high risks for compromised mental health, including depression, anxiety, and suicidality. The authors provide an overview of current research on risk factors such as discrimination, family rejection, and lack of social support, alongside protective factors like strong peer networks and supportive school environments. They highlight the importance of legal and policy advancements in fostering better mental health outcomes for LGBT youth. The article also underscores the need for tailored interventions within clinical settings to address specific needs and challenges faced by these youths.
