## Reliability, validity, and clinical application of the visual analogue mood scale

This paper evaluates the reliability and validity of the Visual Analogue Mood Scale (VAMS) in measuring mood among psychiatric patients, comparing it with established scales like the Hamilton Scale and Self-Rating Depression Scale. The VAMS is demonstrated to be a reliable and valid tool for assessing mood even when administered by untrained personnel. It effectively distinguishes between affective disorders (manic or depressed type) and other conditions through its correlation with psychomotor performance as measured by the Digit-Symbol test. This study underscores the importance of quantitative psychological tests in clinical settings, contributing to the understanding and treatment of psychiatric disorders.
