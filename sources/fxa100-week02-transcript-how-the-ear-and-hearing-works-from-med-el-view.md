## How the Ear and Hearing Works

This transcript explains how sound is perceived by the human ear. It describes the process of converting sound waves into mechanical vibrations and then into electrical signals for interpretation by the brain. The ear drum, ossicular chain, and cochlea play key roles in this transformation. Hair cells within the cochlea are sensitive to different frequencies, allowing for perception across the entire spectrum of sound. Hearing loss can occur due to damage or malfunction at various points along this auditory pathway.
