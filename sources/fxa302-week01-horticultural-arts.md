## Horticultural Arts

Jonathan Coope's chapter on horticultural arts in 'The Routledge Companion to Health Humanities' explores the therapeutic and health benefits of gardens and gardening, particularly within the field of social and therapeutic horticulture (STH). STH encompasses both horticulture therapy (HT), where a trained therapist uses horticultural activities with specific clinical outcomes, and therapeutic horticulture (TH), which aims to improve general well-being across various populations. Historical and contemporary evidence supports the health benefits of green spaces, such as reduced stress and improved physical activity. The chapter discusses case studies like 'Minding the Garden' project in North Hampshire that demonstrate how gardening can help individuals with mental health issues gain confidence and skills. It also highlights policy initiatives to integrate STH into healthcare systems and emphasizes the importance of accessibility and safety considerations for all participants.
