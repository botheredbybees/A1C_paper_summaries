## Empathy Manipulation Impacts Music-Induced Emotions: A Psychophysiological Study on Opera

This study explores the influence of cognitive empathy on music-induced emotions using operatic performances as stimuli. Participants were instructed to either empathize or remain neutral while listening to music, leading to different emotional and physiological responses depending on the empathy level and the emotional content of the music. The results indicate that high-empathy conditions enhanced specific emotions and altered physiological measures such as skin conductance levels and respiration rates compared to low-empathy conditions. This research supports the idea that cognitive empathy is a critical mechanism in experiencing emotions through music, contributing valuable insights for understanding the role of empathy in emotional responses within the creative arts.
