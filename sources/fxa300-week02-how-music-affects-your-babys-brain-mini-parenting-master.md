## How music affects your baby’s brain: Mini Parenting Master Class

The document discusses the importance of music in child development, particularly for babies and young children. Dr. Ibrahim Baltagi, a music expert, explains that exposing a fetus to music can have positive effects on brain development during pregnancy. Around the 16-18th week of gestation, fetuses begin hearing sounds, with rapid ear development occurring by 24 weeks. Music not only soothes and uplifts pregnant women but also positively influences the unborn baby's cognitive growth.

Dr. Baltagi recommends playing classical music or gentle lullabies during the third trimester as these can be soothing for both mother and child. After birth, music continues to play a crucial role in early childhood development by enhancing language acquisition, reading skills, mathematical learning, and overall school readiness.

Using music to soothe infants is also beneficial; soft melodies help calm babies and establish sleep routines. Familiar tunes or parental singing can serve as effective forms of music therapy, slowing heartbeats and promoting relaxation. Additionally, lullabies are universal in cultures around the world and have been passed down through generations.

The document emphasizes that musical activities encourage socialization among children, helping them express emotions and connect with others. Parents are advised to engage their children in musical experiences early on to reap these developmental benefits.
