## A systematic review of mental health and wellbeing outcomes of group singing for adults with a mental health condition

The paper presents a systematic review of the literature on group singing as an intervention for adults with mental health conditions, focusing on its effects on mental health and wellbeing. The authors identified 13 studies involving 667 participants who had participated in community-based singing groups. Seven longitudinal quantitative studies demonstrated that engaging in choir singing led to significant improvements in mental health and wellbeing outcomes, including reduced anxiety and depression, with moderate to large effect sizes. Additionally, six qualitative studies highlighted the positive social impacts of group singing, such as increased enjoyment, emotional support, sense of belonging, and self-confidence among participants. However, the review also noted methodological limitations and biases in the studies reviewed, indicating that while group singing appears promising as a mental health intervention, further rigorous research is necessary to establish its efficacy conclusively.
