## National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research, 2007 (Updated 2018)

This document is the rescinded 'National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research' from 2007, updated to 2018. It provides guidelines for conducting ethical human research and outlines values and principles essential for researchers and review bodies such as HRECs (Human Research Ethics Committees). The publication covers sections detailing themes of risk and benefit, consent processes, specific considerations for different participant groups, and governance and ethical review procedures. However, due to its rescinded status, users are advised that this document no longer reflects current standards or positions from the National Health and Medical Research Council.
