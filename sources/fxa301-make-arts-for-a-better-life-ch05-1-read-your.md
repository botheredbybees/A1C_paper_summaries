## The Principles and Practices of Ethnographic Fieldwork

This chapter outlines principles for ethnographic fieldwork including initial jotting techniques and expanded notes. It emphasizes the verbatim principle, recording everything as said by subjects without interpretation, and the concrete principle, using specific language to describe events vividly. The text also discusses coding themes in fieldnotes and provides guidance on audio-visual documentation, emphasizing the importance of capturing details comprehensively for analysis and preservation. Additionally, it touches upon photography techniques and considerations for obtaining permissions and metadata for recorded materials. Legal and ethical aspects related to cultural research are briefly addressed.
