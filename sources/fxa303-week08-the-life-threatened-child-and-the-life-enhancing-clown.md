## The Life Threatened Child and the Life Enhancing Clown: Towards a Model of Therapeutic Clowning

This article explores the growing presence of clowns in pediatric hospital settings and their role as therapeutic clowns, which involves using gentle play and laughter to provide emotional support to ill children. The authors discuss how therapeutic clowning can serve as a complementary form of healthcare by reducing stress and enhancing coping mechanisms for both patients and families during hospitalization. They review the historical context of clowns in hospitals and present a model of therapeutic clowning that can be adapted across various pediatric care settings. Emphasizing the need for professional standards, the article highlights the importance of trained therapeutic clowns who understand their role within the healthcare team and contribute to patients' emotional well-being.
