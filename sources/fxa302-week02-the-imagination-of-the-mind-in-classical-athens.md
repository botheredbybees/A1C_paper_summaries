## The Imagination of the Mind in Classical Athens

The book 'The Imagination of the Mind in Classical Athens' explores the imaginative processes involved in the artefacts and literature of ancient Greece, focusing on how ancient Athenians conceptualized abstract ideas such as justice or death. The contributors approach this topic from various disciplines including philosophy, history, philology, literary studies, and art history, highlighting the absence of technical terminology used to articulate imagination in antiquity. By examining a range of visual and verbal artefacts from Classical Athens, the book aims to uncover the cultural roots of later theoretical inquiries into imagination and provides essential insights for those interested in ancient Greek thought, culture, and philosophy.
