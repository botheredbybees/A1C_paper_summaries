## Supporting youth mental health with arts-based strategies: a global perspective

The article by Golden et al. (2024) discusses the increasing global crisis of youth mental health and proposes arts-based strategies as a solution to address this issue effectively. The authors highlight that mental health disorders are among the leading causes of disability in children and adolescents, with escalating concerns due to factors such as poverty, violence, and socio-cultural stigma. They argue for an urgent need for innovative interventions that are accessible, scalable, and cost-effective across diverse communities globally.

The paper reviews the potential of arts- and culture-based strategies to support mental health among youth by leveraging existing local resources and cultural practices. It emphasizes the importance of elevating youth voice, developing core outcome measures, analyzing successful global models, and establishing clear funding pathways for research and implementation. The authors also note that engaging in the arts can improve community cohesion, promote self-expression, enhance cultural identity, and provide protective factors against mental health symptoms caused by discrimination.

Furthermore, the article explores mechanisms behind the effectiveness of arts-based programs in supporting mental health through psychological assessments, neuroscience studies, and clinical applications across various art forms. It concludes with a call for action to undertake critical research and translate evidence into accessible community practices.
