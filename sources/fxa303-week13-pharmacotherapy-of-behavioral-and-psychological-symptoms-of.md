## Pharmacotherapy of Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia: State of the Art and Future Progress

The article reviews the current knowledge and management strategies for behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD), emphasizing both pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions. The authors discuss how BPSD, including agitation, psychotic features, apathy, depression, and anxiety, significantly impact patients with Alzheimer's disease, frontotemporal dementia, and dementia with Lewy bodies. Non-pharmacological approaches are highlighted as a first-line treatment due to their effectiveness and fewer adverse effects compared to pharmacotherapy. However, practical challenges such as lack of trained personnel and immediate symptom resolution expectations hinder the widespread use of non-pharmacological strategies. The review also explores ongoing clinical trials and potential future therapeutic options in light of current limitations. This discussion is relevant to creative arts and health by highlighting the importance of person-centered care that addresses individual needs through both direct interventions with patients and indirect environmental adjustments, suggesting a holistic approach that can integrate creative therapies.
