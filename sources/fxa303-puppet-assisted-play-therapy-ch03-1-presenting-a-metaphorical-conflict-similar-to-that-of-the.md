## Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy

This chapter discusses the use of puppetry in cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) to enhance conflict resolution and emotional coping skills in children through storytelling and metaphorical techniques. It highlights how puppets can aid therapists in addressing various issues like bullying or family conflicts by creating storylines that resonate with a child's experiences without direct confrontation. The text outlines key steps for constructing therapeutic puppet stories, such as presenting metaphorical conflicts, personifying unconscious processes, integrating parallel learning situations, and developing new identities through resolution narratives. Additionally, an original research study is presented which evaluates the impact of puppetry on creativity among third and fourth-grade students using the Torrance Test for Creative Thinking (TTC). The results indicate positive outcomes with respect to enhancing divergent thinking skills, problem-solving abilities, and overall mental health.
