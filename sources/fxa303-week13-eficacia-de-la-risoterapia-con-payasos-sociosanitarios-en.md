## Eficacia de la risoterapia con payasos sociosanitarios en el estado de ánimo de adultos hospitalizados

This study evaluates the impact of laughter therapy conducted by healthcare clowns on the emotional state of hospitalized adults. The research, conducted as a quasi-experimental design, involved assessing 40 adult patients in internal medicine using the Adapted Transcultural Scale for Mood Evaluation (EVEA-H). Key findings revealed significant improvements in mood, with reductions observed in sadness/depression, anxiety, and hostility levels while an increase was noted in happiness. The study underscores the effectiveness of laughter therapy as a means to foster positive emotions and reduce psychological symptoms among hospitalized patients. Emphasizing collaborative efforts between healthcare professionals and clowns, it highlights the potential for such interventions to enhance emotional well-being in various clinical settings.
