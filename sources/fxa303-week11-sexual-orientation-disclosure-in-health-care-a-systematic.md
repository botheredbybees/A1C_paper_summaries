## Sexual orientation disclosure in health care: a systematic review

The article presents a systematic review investigating barriers and facilitators to sexual orientation (SO) disclosure by lesbian, gay, bisexual, or transgender (LGBT) adults in healthcare settings. The research aims to understand how SO disclosure impacts the provision of appropriate care. The study identifies four overarching themes: moment of disclosure, expected outcome, healthcare professional’s role, and environment. Key findings include perceptions about the relevance of SO to care, communication skills and language used by healthcare professionals, and fears regarding negative treatment or reactions to disclosure. The review concludes that modifiable factors related to these barriers can be targeted to improve awareness among healthcare providers and enhance inclusive environments for patients from diverse sexual orientations.
