## Transforming Dementia Care Through Theatre

The article discusses a groundbreaking initiative to use theatre as a medium for disseminating research findings about the challenges faced by healthcare assistants (HCAs) in dementia care settings. Researchers, who themselves worked as HCAs to gain insights into their daily struggles and coping mechanisms, found that traditional training methods were ineffective due to the fast-paced nature of work and lack of opportunities for skill development. To address this, they commissioned a play titled 'Inside Out of Mind' by Tanya Myers, which was performed alongside workshops designed to facilitate dialogue among HCAs, nurses, and other direct care workers. These events allowed participants to reflect on their roles and experiences in dementia care, fostering empathy and understanding. The initiative aimed to empower HCAs by recognizing the tacit knowledge they possess and encouraging them to use it effectively in their practice. This creative approach not only engaged a hard-to-reach audience but also highlighted the importance of person-centered care in improving the quality of life for individuals with dementia.
