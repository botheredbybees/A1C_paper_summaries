## Critical Reflection on '28 Solomon Street': A Photo Essay

Isabelle Osborne's critical reflection details her personal photo essay titled '28 Solomon Street', which documents the experience of caring for a family member with dementia. Using a mix of analogue photography and written captions, she conveys the emotional and physical impact of this journey within her own home setting. The narrative is fragmented to reflect the chaotic and disorienting nature of living with someone who has dementia. Osborne employs visual metaphors such as empty chairs and overgrown gardens to symbolize loss and absence. She discusses how the therapeutic process of creating the photo essay helped her make sense of grief, akin to Roland Barthes' experience in 'Camera Lucida'. This work highlights the power of photography and narrative in communicating complex emotions and experiences, offering a unique perspective on an often-inaccessible reality.
