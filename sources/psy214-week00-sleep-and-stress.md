## Sleep and stress

The article 'Sleep and Stress' by Sally Shuttleworth and Russell G. Foster explores the interrelationship between sleep and stress from both historical and contemporary perspectives. The authors highlight that while the physiological understanding of sleep has been developing for over a century, it is only recently that its significance to overall health and well-being is being fully realized at a mechanistic level. They discuss how lack of sleep can lead to various physical and mental health issues, including increased stress, anxiety, and susceptibility to diseases such as heart disease, cancer, and metabolic disorders. The piece also examines historical views on sleep and their relevance to today’s understanding, emphasizing the role of cultural practices in shaping perceptions of sleep deprivation and its consequences. This work underscores the importance of addressing sleep disruption as a public health issue.
