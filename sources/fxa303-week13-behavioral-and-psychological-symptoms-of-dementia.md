## Behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia

This article reviews the behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD), which are non-cognitive symptoms and behaviors present in individuals with various types of dementia. BPSD include agitation, aberrant motor behavior, anxiety, elation, irritability, depression, apathy, disinhibition, delusions, hallucinations, and changes in sleep or appetite patterns. These symptoms are highly prevalent, affecting up to 90% of people with dementia over the course of their illness. BPSD significantly impact patient well-being and caregiver distress, often necessitating long-term hospitalization and increasing healthcare costs.

The review highlights the complexity of BPSD in terms of symptom presentation and their multifactorial etiology involving psychological, social, and biological factors. It also emphasizes the importance of accurate diagnosis and treatment strategies that may involve both non-pharmacological and pharmacological interventions. Despite current therapeutic approaches, there is a need for novel targets to improve patient outcomes.
