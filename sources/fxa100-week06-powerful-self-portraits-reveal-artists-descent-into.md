## Powerful Self-Portraits Reveal Artist’s Descent Into Alzheimer’s Disease

The article discusses the profound impact of Alzheimer’s disease on William Utermohlen, an American artist diagnosed in 1995 at age 61. In response to his diagnosis, Utermohlen embarked on a series of self-portraits that documented his mental and physical deterioration over five years until he was admitted to a nursing home in 2000. These portraits illustrate the loss of detail and spatial awareness as his cognitive functions declined. The article emphasizes how these artworks not only serve as a personal record but also offer insights into the subjective experience of living with Alzheimer’s disease, highlighting the emotional and artistic challenges faced by individuals battling this condition. Utermohlen's widow Patricia reflects on her husband's relentless pursuit of art even when it became increasingly difficult for him to create due to his illness, suggesting that the act of drawing was a critical part of his identity until the very end.
