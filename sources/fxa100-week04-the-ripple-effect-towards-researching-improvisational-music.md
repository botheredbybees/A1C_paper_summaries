## The ‘ripple effect’: Towards researching improvisational music therapy in dementia care homes

This article explores the role of improvisational music therapy in dementia care settings, focusing on how it can create musical communities and support wellbeing for all stakeholders involved. The study examines the strategies employed by experienced therapists to foster a supportive environment through shared musicking experiences, which extend beyond formal therapy sessions to influence broader social dynamics within care homes. Key findings include the concept of the 'ripple effect', where the benefits of music therapy spread from individual interactions (micro level) to communal activities (meso level), and ultimately to organizational culture (macro level). The authors suggest that music therapy can enhance mutual flourishing, shared participation, and expanded self-identities for both residents and staff, moving beyond traditional roles and distress narratives. These insights imply a need for managers and funders to consider broader applications of music therapy in dementia care settings.
