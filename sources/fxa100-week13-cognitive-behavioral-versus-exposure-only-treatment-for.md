## Cognitive Behavioral Versus Exposure Only Treatment for Social Phobia: A Meta-Analysis

This study provides a comprehensive analysis comparing cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) and exposure-only treatments for social anxiety disorder. The research involves reviewing empirical data from various studies to determine if CBT is more effective than simple exposure therapy. Key findings indicate that both methods are equally effective in treating social phobia, with no significant advantage of one over the other. However, the study also highlights that longer durations of exposure therapy sessions can lead to better outcomes on measures related to social anxiety. The authors suggest that previous literature proposing superior efficacy of CBT may be due to methodological limitations and small sample sizes in individual studies. This meta-analysis underscores the importance of evidence-based practice in psychology, indicating that both techniques should continue to be utilized based on their comparable effectiveness.
