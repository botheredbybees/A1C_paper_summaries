## A systematic review of mental health and wellbeing outcomes of group singing for adults with a mental health condition

This systematic review evaluates the mental health and wellbeing benefits of group singing for adults with mental health conditions, including substance use disorders. The study analyzed 13 articles representing data from 667 participants who engaged in community choir singing. Findings indicate that longitudinal studies show significant improvements in mental health and wellbeing outcomes during the period of participation, with moderate to large effect sizes. Qualitative studies reported themes such as enjoyment, improved emotional states, a sense of belonging, and enhanced self-confidence among participants. Despite these promising results, methodological issues such as high risk of bias were noted, suggesting that more rigorous research is needed to confirm the therapeutic benefits of group singing in clinical populations.
