## The Arts of Desistance: Assessing the Role of Arts-based Programmes in Reducing Reoffending

The article examines the role of arts-based programs in reducing reoffending within criminal justice systems, focusing on their indirect contributions to desistance from crime through 'secondary desistance' mechanisms. Secondary desistance involves changes in self-perception and attitudes that indirectly promote primary desistance (abstinence from crime). The authors review empirical research highlighting how participation in arts-based activities can lead to psychological and attitudinal changes such as increased self-esteem, sense of achievement, empowerment, and reduced levels of depression and anger among prisoners. Additionally, the article suggests that prolonged engagement with art programs after release could enhance desistance outcomes by fostering continued personal growth and skill development.
