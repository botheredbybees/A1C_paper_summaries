## The brain on art: intense aesthetic experience activates the default mode network

This research article investigates the neural basis of aesthetic experiences in response to visual art through functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and behavioral analysis of individual differences in aesthetic responses. The study combines fMRI data with observer ratings on a 4-level scale, revealing two distinct patterns of brain activity: one associated with sensory processing and another linked to emotional and self-referential mentation involving the default mode network (DMN). Sensory regions such as occipito-temporal areas showed linear increases in activation proportional to aesthetic ratings. The striatum also exhibited a similar pattern but included below-baseline activations for low-rated artworks. In contrast, frontal brain regions including parts of the DMN demonstrated step-like increases only for highly moving artworks rated '4' and were relatively stable for other ratings. This study highlights how intense aesthetic experiences integrate sensory information with emotional and personal significance, underscoring the importance of individual subjective experience in aesthetic evaluations.
