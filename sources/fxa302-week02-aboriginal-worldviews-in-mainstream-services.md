## Aboriginal Worldviews in Mainstream Services

The chapter explores the incorporation of Aboriginal worldviews and traditional knowledges into mainstream health services in Australia. Fiona Stanley, a non-Aboriginal public health expert with over four decades of experience working closely with Aboriginal researchers, emphasizes the resurgence and revitalization of Aboriginal knowledge despite centuries of attempts to erase it. The text highlights several examples of how Elders are actively sharing their deep traditional ecological knowledge about food, medicine, and cultural practices that have been passed down through generations. These efforts not only preserve indigenous wisdom but also contribute significantly to health initiatives by integrating traditional knowledges with Western medical approaches.

Key findings include the successful use of participatory action research led by Aboriginal researchers like Dr Michael Wright, which engages community members from start to finish and incorporates local Indigenous knowledge into service delivery. This approach fosters meaningful relationships between Elders and mainstream service providers, enhancing understanding of historical impacts on health and promoting culturally appropriate interventions.

The chapter underscores the importance of reciprocal learning and mutual respect in bridging traditional Aboriginal worldviews with contemporary healthcare systems, ultimately improving health outcomes for First Nations populations.
