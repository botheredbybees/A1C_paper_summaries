## Traditional ancient Egyptian medicine: A review

The review article by Metwaly et al. (2021) explores the traditional medical practices of ancient Egypt, highlighting their advanced anatomical knowledge and sophisticated treatment methods for various ailments. The authors emphasize that ancient Egyptian medicine was deeply intertwined with magic and religious beliefs, lacking a clear distinction between therapeutic and spiritual practices. They describe key medical papyri such as Ebers, Edwin Smith, Kahun Gynecological Papyrus, and others which contain detailed records of medicinal treatments using plants, animal products, minerals, and metals. The review also discusses the identification and treatment of specific diseases like diabetes and cancer, reflecting a level of diagnostic capability that was ahead of its time. This historical perspective provides insights into early medical practices and highlights potential avenues for contemporary research in traditional medicine. Understanding these ancient techniques can offer new perspectives on holistic health approaches relevant to creative arts and health fields.
