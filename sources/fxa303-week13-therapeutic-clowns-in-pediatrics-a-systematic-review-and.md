## Therapeutic clowns in pediatrics: a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluates the clinical utility of hospital clowns in pediatric populations by synthesizing data from randomized controlled trials (RCTs). The authors conducted a comprehensive search to identify studies assessing the impact of clown therapy on reducing anxiety and pain in children undergoing medical procedures or hospitalization, as well as parental anxiety. They applied random effect models for heterogeneity analysis and utilized RevMan 5.3 software for risk of bias assessment and data synthesis.

A total of 19 eligible RCTs were included, with 16 contributing to the meta-analysis. The review concludes that hospital clowns significantly reduce both child anxiety (SMD = -0.83 [95% CI: -1.16, -0.51]) and state parental anxiety (SMD = -0.46 [95% CI: -0.70, -0.21]). This study is the first systematic review on this topic, confirming that clown therapy can alleviate stress and anxiety in children and their parents during medical interventions.

The findings are relevant to creative arts and health by demonstrating the therapeutic benefits of humor and play-based interventions in pediatric settings, which can enhance emotional well-being and support recovery processes.
