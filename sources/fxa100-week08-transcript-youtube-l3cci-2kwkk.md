## Art Therapy for Alzheimer's Patients: The Memories in the Making Program

The transcript discusses Sully Jenny's pioneering work on using art therapy to communicate with and empower individuals suffering from Alzheimer's disease. In 1988, she introduced the 'Memories in the Making' program which uses painting as a means of self-expression for patients who struggle with verbal communication due to the progression of their condition. The program reveals that even when words are lost, the spark of creativity remains within these individuals. Art provides an inexpensive and accessible tool that can bring out non-verbal expressions from residents in care facilities, often leading to powerful emotional connections between patient and caregiver. This therapy has been adopted widely and is used not only for therapeutic benefits but also as a way to raise awareness about Alzheimer's disease through public exhibitions of the artwork created.
