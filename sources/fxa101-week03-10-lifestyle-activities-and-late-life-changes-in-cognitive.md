## 10 Lifestyle activities and late-life changes in cognitive performance

This chapter explores the relationship between lifestyle activities and cognitive performance in older adults, focusing on two theoretical perspectives: cognitive reserve theory and substantive complexity theory. The authors discuss the passive and active models of cognitive reserve, highlighting how these models explain individual differences in cognitive functioning despite age-related brain changes. Evidence from studies examining both theories suggests that engaging in intellectually demanding activities can mitigate cognitive decline and protect against dementing diseases like Alzheimer's. However, the chapter also addresses methodological challenges in studying this relationship, such as accurately defining lifestyle activities and analyzing longitudinal data. The relevance to creative arts and health lies in understanding how continuous engagement in creative and mentally stimulating activities can enhance cognitive resilience and maintain neural plasticity in later life.
