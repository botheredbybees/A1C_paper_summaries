## Dramatherapy for Children and Adolescents with Autism Spectrum Disorder: A Systematic Integrative Review

This systematic integrative review explores the impact of dramatherapy on children and adolescents with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). The study identifies nine research studies, including six qualitative, two quantitative, and one mixed-methods approach to understand the efficacy of dramatherapy. Dramatherapy involves using drama and theatre arts in therapeutic settings to enhance emotional well-being, social skills, and behavioral adaptations for individuals with ASD. Despite some positive reports from clinical practice, empirical evidence supporting its effectiveness remains limited. The review highlights that dramatherapy can foster expressive, behavioral, and social skills among children and adolescents with ASD but suggests a need for more robust research methodologies to evaluate its impact comprehensively.
