## Arts for Health and Wellbeing: An Evaluation Framework

The document 'Arts for Health and Wellbeing: An Evaluation Framework' by Public Health England aims to provide a structured approach for evaluating arts-based interventions that support health and wellbeing. It acknowledges the growing recognition of the arts as contributors to public health but highlights the need for robust evidence to substantiate their effectiveness, impacts, and costs in healthcare services.

The framework consists of three main parts: background discussion on evaluation principles, descriptions of different types of evaluations suitable for arts-based programs (including theories of change and logic models), and a reporting tool designed to capture key components of project delivery and evaluation details. It emphasizes the importance of clear identification of rationale, evaluation questions, design methodologies, data collection methods, ethical considerations, and dissemination strategies.

This document is intended for various stakeholders including health commissioners, third sector organizations, funders, practitioners, managers, arts organizations, researchers, and others involved in developing or evaluating arts for health and wellbeing initiatives. It aims to bridge the gap between arts practitioners and evaluators by providing a standardized yet adaptable evaluation framework that can support more robust evidence gathering and comparative analysis across different projects.
