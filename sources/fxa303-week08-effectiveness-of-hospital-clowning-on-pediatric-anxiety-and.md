## Effectiveness of Hospital Clowning on Pediatric Anxiety and Pain: Network Meta-Analysis

This study, a network meta-analysis of randomized trials examining the effectiveness of hospital clowning on pediatric anxiety and pain during medical procedures, compared clown interventions with medication, presence of parents, standard care, and other non-pharmacological distraction methods. The analysis included 28 studies and revealed that clowning was significantly more effective in reducing anxiety compared to the presence of parents but showed no significant differences between clowning, medication, or other forms of non-medical distraction. For pain management, clowning interventions were found to be superior to parental presence and standard care but not different from other comparators. The study highlighted substantial heterogeneity among studies and noted a moderate-to-low certainty of evidence due to methodological limitations such as high risk of bias. The findings suggest that hospital clowns can play an important role in managing anxiety and pain in pediatric patients undergoing medical procedures, particularly when parents are present. However, the authors emphasize the need for more detailed descriptions of clowning interventions and comparators in future studies.
