## A Deeper Dive into the Measurement Invariance of the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking: Confirmatory and Exploratory Factor Analysis Across Gender and Ethnicity

Yoojoong Kim's study examines the measurement invariance of the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking-Figural (TTCT-F) across gender and ethnicity, a widely used creativity assessment in U.S. schools for identifying gifted students. The research aims to validate the psychometric properties of the TTCT-F, which measures creative thinking dimensions such as Fluency, Originality, Elaboration, Abstractness of Titles (AT), and Resistance to Premature Closure (RPC). Kim's analysis includes confirmatory factor analysis (CFA) and exploratory factor analysis (EFA) using data from 379 elementary school students. The study concludes that the Innovative-Adaptive model, with an additional cross-loading for RPC, best fits the TTCT-F structure. This two-factor model demonstrates measurement invariance across under- and over-represented ethnicities at configural, metric, and scalar levels but not across genders. These findings highlight the need for further research to understand gender differences in creative thinking.
