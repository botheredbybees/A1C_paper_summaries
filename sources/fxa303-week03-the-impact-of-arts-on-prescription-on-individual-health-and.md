## The Impact of Arts on Prescription on Individual Health and Wellbeing: A Systematic Review with Meta-Analysis

The study by Jensen et al. (2024) provides a systematic review and meta-analysis of the impact of Arts on Prescription (AoP) programs on individual health and wellbeing. AoP is a method under social prescribing that involves referrals to community-based arts activities aimed at enhancing psychosocial wellbeing. The authors systematically searched major electronic databases and conducted a narrative review for qualitative studies, alongside a meta-analysis for quantitative outcomes. They included 25 studies from various countries, revealing statistically significant improvements in wellbeing, particularly reductions in anxiety and depression. These programs are held in diverse settings such as community centers, GP surgeries, and schools, offering activities like visual arts, creative writing, dance, and music over periods ranging from eight to ten weeks. The review highlights social and psychological benefits alongside opportunities for progression. However, the study also identifies barriers to participation including physical, psychological, and social constraints. Overall, this work underscores the potential of AoP in improving mental health outcomes and fostering community connections.
