## The Brain That Changes Itself: Stories of Personal Triumph from the Frontiers of Brain Science (Preface)

The preface of 'The Brain That Changes Itself' by Norman Doidge introduces the groundbreaking concept that the human brain possesses neuroplasticity, the ability to change its structure and function in response to mental activities. This discovery challenges long-held beliefs about the unchanging nature of the brain after childhood, where it was thought to deteriorate over time or remain fixed with limitations. The preface outlines how Doidge's journey into this field led him to encounter scientists who demonstrated that damaged brains can reorganize and recover, and that abilities previously considered hardwired could be altered through neuroplastic interventions. The book explores stories of individuals whose lives were transformed by these discoveries, highlighting the potential for personal growth and recovery through understanding and utilizing neuroplasticity. This work has profound implications across various disciplines including psychology, education, technology, and culture, emphasizing the importance of recognizing brain plasticity in shaping human possibilities.
