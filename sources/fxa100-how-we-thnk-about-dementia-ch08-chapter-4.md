## I Am Still the Same Person

In this chapter from Hughes' book 'How We Think About Dementia', the concept of personhood in individuals with dementia is explored. The author begins by discussing the situated embodied agent view, emphasizing that people are not just passive but active agents embedded in a social context. Hughes highlights the Nuffield Council's report on ethical issues surrounding dementia and its emphasis on maintaining personhood despite cognitive decline. Challenges to this concept from philosophical arguments and everyday experiences of caregivers are addressed, along with the author's rebuttal focusing on emotional connections and relational aspects that define personhood beyond memory and cognition. The chapter concludes by stressing the legal protections and responsibilities towards individuals with severe dementia based on their continued personhood.
