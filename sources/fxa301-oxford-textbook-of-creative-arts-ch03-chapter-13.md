## Arts for Health in Community Settings: Promising Practices

This chapter explores the use of arts in enhancing wellness, access to healthcare, and health literacy within community settings around the world. It discusses models for arts and health programs in urban, rural, and developing areas, emphasizing the importance of understanding communities beyond geographical definitions. The authors highlight interdisciplinary theoretical frameworks that support these practices. They also examine challenges and benefits associated with using arts as a tool for promoting social engagement, reducing social stress, improving health behaviors, and preventing disease. Key examples include the Dance for PD programme in Brooklyn, which uses dance to improve physical and psychosocial wellbeing among individuals with Parkinson's disease, and TimeSlips, an improvisational storytelling method that enhances quality of life for older adults with dementia.
