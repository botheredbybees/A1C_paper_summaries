## Chocolate smells pink and stripy: Exploring olfactory-visual synesthesia

The study explores olfactory-visual synesthesia, where odors induce visual experiences in certain individuals. Six participants with this form of synesthesia were presented with various odorants to assess the reliability and nature of their induced visuals. The research aimed to determine if reliable synesthetic experiences depend on identifying or naming the odor, which characteristics of an odor drive these experiences, and whether retronasal odors generate similar visual responses as orthonasal ones. Key findings indicate that a consistent name for an odor correlates with a more reliable synesthetic image, while hedonic information (how pleasant or unpleasant an odor is perceived) also plays a significant role in triggering synesthetic visuals. The study highlights the importance of conceptual and semantic factors over purely perceptual processes in generating synesthetic experiences.
