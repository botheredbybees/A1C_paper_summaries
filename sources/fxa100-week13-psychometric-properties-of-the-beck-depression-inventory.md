## Psychometric Properties of the Beck Depression Inventory: Twenty-Five Years of Evaluation

This paper provides a comprehensive review of the psychometric properties of the Beck Depression Inventory (BDI), including its internal consistency, concurrent validity with clinical ratings and other scales like the Hamilton Psychiatric Rating Scale for Depression (HRSD). It analyzes studies conducted over 25 years on various populations, revealing high reliability and validity in psychiatric patients but lower validity in nonpsychiatric subjects. The review also discusses how different forms of the BDI correlate highly and its utility in detecting depression subtypes beyond just clinical samples.
