## A Pilot Randomized Controlled Clinical Trial of Dance Practice for Functionality in Autistic Children and Adolescent with All Levels of Need Support

This study investigates the impact of dance practice on communication, functional independence, and social behavior in autistic children and adolescents with varying levels of support needs. Participants were randomly assigned to either a dance group or a control group, both receiving 24 sessions over six months. The results showed that the dance group demonstrated significant improvements in communication skills, social cognition, and reduced autistic behaviors compared to the control group. Mothers also reported positive changes in their children’s social interactions. This study suggests that dance practice can enhance non-verbal communication, emotional recognition, and social reciprocity for individuals with autism.
