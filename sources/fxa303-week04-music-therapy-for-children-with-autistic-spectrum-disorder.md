## Music Therapy for Children With Autistic Spectrum Disorder and/or Other Neurodevelopmental Disorders: A Systematic Review

This systematic review evaluates the effectiveness of music therapy for children with neurodevelopmental disorders, focusing on autism spectrum disorder (ASD), intellectual disability (ID), communication disorders (CD), developmental coordination disorder (DCD), and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD). The study synthesizes findings from 39 empirical studies involving 1774 participants. Two primary forms of music therapy were identified: educational music therapy, which emphasizes structured learning through musical activities, and improvisational music therapy, focusing on spontaneous creation and interaction during sessions. Educational music therapy showed significant benefits in enhancing speech production for children with ASD, while improvisational music therapy improved social functioning. Children diagnosed with both ASD and ID exhibited higher response rates to these interventions. Although more research is needed, the preliminary evidence suggests that educational music therapy could be particularly beneficial for children with dyslexia. The review underscores the importance of personalized therapeutic approaches in addressing the diverse needs associated with neurodevelopmental disorders, highlighting the potential role of music therapy as a promising intervention strategy.
