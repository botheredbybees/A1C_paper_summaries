## "Song of Life (SOL)" study protocol: a multicenter, randomized trial on the emotional, spiritual, and psychobiological effects of music therapy in palliative care

The 'Song of Life' (SOL) study protocol details a randomized controlled trial assessing the effectiveness of music therapy in palliative care, focusing on emotional, spiritual, and psychobiological impacts. The primary objective is to improve psychological quality of life through three sessions of either SOL or relaxation exercises for 104 patients across two palliative care units. Secondary outcomes include spiritual well-being, ego-integrity, overall quality of life, distress, caregiver feedback, biopsychological stress markers, and qualitative analysis of treatment strengths and weaknesses. This study builds on earlier pilot work to refine the intervention and select appropriate endpoints, aiming to contribute to the evidence base for psychosocial interventions in palliative care.
