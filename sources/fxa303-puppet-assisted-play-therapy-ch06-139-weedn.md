## Introduction to Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy: Theory, Research, and Practice

Cheryl Hulburd's book introduces the concept of puppet-assisted play therapy (PAPT) as an innovative approach to working with children in therapeutic settings. The text explains how puppets facilitate emotional expression, distance from painful emotions, and role-playing scenarios, thereby aiding healing from trauma and managing social situations. It outlines the theoretical foundations, research findings, practical applications, case studies, and specific interventions that can be adapted based on individual practitioner's creativity and style. Hulburd emphasizes using puppetry to foster a connection with children, establish trust, and create a safe space for therapeutic engagement. The book includes discussions of historical perspectives, cognitive behavioral therapy techniques like the SIFT process (Sensations, Images, Feelings, Thoughts), mindfulness practices, and ways to integrate these tools into treatment plans.
