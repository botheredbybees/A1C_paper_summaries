## The taste & affect music database: Subjective rating norms for a new set of musical stimuli

This article presents subjective norms for a new set of 100 instrumental music stimuli, including taste correspondences and emotional dimensions such as joy, anger, sadness, fear, surprise, familiarity, valence, and arousal. The study evaluated the soundtracks using self-report measures of mood and taste preferences by 329 participants online and in the lab. Significant correlations were observed between taste ratings and emotions like pleasantness, indicating that music can reliably evoke taste attributes. Music sophistication levels, basic taste preferences, sex, and age showed few associations with stimulus evaluations.
