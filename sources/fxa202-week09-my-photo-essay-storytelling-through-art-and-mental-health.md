## My Photo Essay: Storytelling Through Art and Mental Health

The document discusses a photo essay that explores the author's personal journey with mental health and trauma through visual storytelling. The author recounts their use of art, specifically photography, as a medium to express thoughts and feelings they were unable to communicate verbally during childhood and adolescence. A TEDx Talk by Bryce Evans (2013) is highlighted for its emphasis on using therapeutic photography to learn, heal, and grow. This talk resonates deeply with the author's own experience of finding comfort and connection in art as a means of self-expression and healing. The essay also references a study by Charles & Felton (2020), which explores young people’s perceptions of mental health and well-being through photography. This research supports the idea that creative methods such as photography can offer alternative ways for individuals to communicate their experiences, especially those struggling with mental health difficulties. The author emphasizes the fragmented storyline approach used in their photo essay, reflecting the non-linear nature of healing processes.
