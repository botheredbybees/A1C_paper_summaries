## Where do you get your ideas?

In his essay 'Where do you get your ideas?', Neil Gaiman explores the common question posed to writers about their creative process. Initially, he humorously dismisses the question with witty but unhelpful answers. He later reveals that inspiration comes from various sources such as daydreaming and asking simple questions like 'What if...?' or 'If only...'. Gaiman emphasizes that ideas can originate from personal curiosity, hypothetical scenarios, and unique combinations of concepts. He also discusses how the act of writing itself can generate new ideas and stories, acknowledging the challenges and fears associated with creativity but ultimately highlighting the joy and necessity of storytelling in his life.
