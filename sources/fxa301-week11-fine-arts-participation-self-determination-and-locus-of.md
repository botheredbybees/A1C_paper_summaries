## Fine arts participation, self-determination, and locus of control among persons with developmental disabilities

The study by Robey et al. (2018) explores the relationship between fine arts participation and self-determination, as well as locus of control among individuals with developmental disabilities. The research aimed to investigate whether involvement in artistic activities could enhance psychological constructs related to personal agency and internal attribution of outcomes. Thirty-four participants engaged in fine arts programs were compared with eight non-participants who had neuromuscular and cognitive impairments. Quantitative measures indicated no significant differences between users, low users, or non-users of arts programming regarding self-determination or locus of control. However, qualitative analysis revealed that fine arts participants tended to emphasize effort over luck when attributing their circumstances and accomplishments, suggesting a more internalized sense of control. Despite the lack of statistical significance in quantitative data, the study highlights the potential value of further research into how artistic engagement might influence psychological well-being among people with developmental disabilities.
