## The THRivE Toolkit: Tools for Health Research and Evaluation in Arts and Heritage

The THRivE Toolkit (Tools for Health Research and Evaluation in Arts and Heritage) is a comprehensive resource designed to support the evaluation of arts and heritage programs aimed at improving health outcomes. Led by the Centre for Music and Health at the National University of Singapore, the toolkit provides practical guidance for researchers and practitioners in designing, implementing, and reporting evaluations effectively. It includes validated scales and measures for assessing mental health, quality of life, social connection, and other relevant health outcomes. The document emphasizes the importance of rigorous scientific methods while accommodating the complexity inherent in arts-based interventions. This resource is crucial as it addresses the growing need to establish evidence-based practices in integrating arts into healthcare and public health initiatives.
