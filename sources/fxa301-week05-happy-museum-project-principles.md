## Happy Museum Project Principles

The Happy Museum Project outlines principles aimed at transforming museums into spaces that promote well-being, environmental sustainability, and active citizenship. The project emphasizes the importance of creating conditions for well-being by encouraging playfulness, creativity, interaction, and aesthetics within communities. It also stresses the value of protecting natural and cultural environments, advocating for a lean and green approach to resource management. Additionally, it encourages museums to foster mutual relationships with staff, volunteers, and visitors, promoting co-creation and shared ownership. The principles advocate for learning as a means to resilience and support for experimental work, emphasizing adaptability and community involvement. Finally, the project suggests measuring what truly matters by focusing on qualitative outcomes that contribute to well-being rather than just quantitative metrics like visitor numbers.
