## Mental Health and Wellbeing across the Life Course - Extract

The document highlights the increasing prevalence of mental health conditions in the UK, stressing the importance of non-medical approaches like creative health to improve outcomes and reduce inequalities. Creative activities such as visual arts, group singing, music, dance, gardening, and engagement with heritage have shown significant benefits in improving mental wellbeing across different stages of life. These interventions can prevent common mental health conditions, offer person-centred care, and provide a holistic approach to managing mental health issues. The text emphasizes the need for cross-departmental government strategies to embed creative health into community, school, workplace, and healthcare settings as part of a whole-system approach to tackle the mental health crisis.
