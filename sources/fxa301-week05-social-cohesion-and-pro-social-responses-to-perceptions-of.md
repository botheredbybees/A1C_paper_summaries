## Social Cohesion and Pro-Social Responses to Perceptions of Crime

The report examines community perceptions of crime in Victoria, Australia, focusing on the factors influencing worry about crime and the subsequent behaviors. Key findings include that intense worry about crime is relatively rare but varies by crime type (cybercrime being a notable concern). Demographic variables like age and gender significantly predict worry, with middle-aged individuals showing higher concerns for specific crimes. Previous victimization increases perceived risk and heightens worry. Perceived consequences of victimization are severe, affecting daily life significantly. Urban residents express more worry about certain types of crime compared to rural areas. The study highlights the importance of social cohesion in addressing perceptions of crime, suggesting targeted interventions to enhance community safety.
