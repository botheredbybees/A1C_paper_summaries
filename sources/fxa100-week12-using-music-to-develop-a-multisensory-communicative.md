## Using Music to Develop a Multisensory Communicative Environment for People with Late-Stage Dementia

This article explores the role of music in facilitating communication and social interaction among people with late-stage dementia. The study involved eight individuals living in residential care, four care staff members, and three musicians who participated in weekly live music sessions. Visual grounded theory was applied to analyze video data collected during these sessions. Findings indicated that music created a multisensory environment that enabled nonverbal communication and social interaction over time. Key factors influencing this were time, one-to-one interactions within the group setting, and characteristics of the music itself. The study suggests that integrating music into care practices can enhance quality of life for people with dementia by fostering connections and improving their ability to communicate nonverbally.
