## Therapeutic clowning in paediatric practice

This paper explores the use of therapeutic clowning in pediatric care, examining its effects on medical procedures and patient well-being. The authors review literature indicating that clowns can significantly reduce anxiety in children undergoing anesthesia for surgery by providing distraction and creating a positive atmosphere. Studies show clowns are more effective than midazolam premedication and parental presence alone in reducing child anxiety during the induction of anesthesia. Clowns also help alleviate parental anxiety and improve overall hospital experiences, integrating humor and fun into clinical settings to empower children and reduce fear and loneliness associated with medical procedures.
