## Week 9 Lecture: Dramatherapy, Dance Therapy, and Applied Theatre in Dementia Care

This week's lecture in the course 'The Arts and Dementia Care' covers various therapeutic approaches such as drama therapy, dance/movement therapy, and applied theatre. Drama therapy is described as a method where performance arts are employed to effect psychological, emotional, and social changes in clients with dementia. Key elements include creating scenarios that engage the client and fostering indirect exploration of difficult experiences. Dance therapy focuses on forging a healing relationship through movement and dance, emphasizing deep connections between body, mind, and emotions. It offers proven benefits beyond physical health, including cognitive function and sensory development. Applied theatre techniques like Theatre of the Oppressed are discussed for their potential to empower individuals and facilitate meaningful social interactions. The Veder contact method is highlighted as a framework that uses costumes, props, songs, and poetry to trigger long-term memories and support person-centred care principles. Practical applications include addressing common stresses in aging through tailored coping strategies informed by dramatherapy techniques.
