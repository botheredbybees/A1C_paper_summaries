## Probe Tone Paradigm Reveals Less Differentiated Tonal Hierarchy in Rock Music

The study investigates the tonal hierarchy in classical and rock music using Krumhansl and Kessler's probe tone paradigm. The research hypothesizes that while both styles share a common Western tonality, rock music exhibits less differentiated hierarchical structures compared to classical music due to its more permissive pitch organization. Participants rated tones following key-defining contexts played on solo piano (classical) or electric guitar (rock). Results indicated that the tonal hierarchy in rock was indeed less differentiated than in classical music, aligning with theoretical predictions and previous empirical findings. This suggests that assumptions of universality in tonal hierarchies across Western styles may be overly simplistic, necessitating style-specific models for musical cognition research.
