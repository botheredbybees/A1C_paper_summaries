## Seriously Foolish and Foolishly Serious: The Art and Practice of Clowning in Children’s Rehabilitation

This paper explores the role of clowning in children's rehabilitation, challenging the conventional view that clowns should be seen as secondary to medical professionals and only focused on humor and happiness. The authors argue that clowning is fundamentally about foolishness—a brave willingness to engage with others despite potential failure and vulnerability. This approach supports emotional resilience and meaningful relationships, which are often overlooked in traditional health care models prioritizing 'serious' outcomes like cure and fix. By re-centering the value of aesthetic and pleasurable ways of being-in-the-world, clowns help create a more holistic and relational model of healthcare that includes failure and discomfort as essential aspects of healing.
