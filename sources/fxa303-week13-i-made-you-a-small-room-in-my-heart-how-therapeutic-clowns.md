## I made you a small room in my heart: how therapeutic clowns meet the needs of older adults in nursing homes

The article explores how therapeutic clowns, often referred to as elder-clowns in nursing home settings, address the unique needs of older adults through their performances and interactions. The study, employing an interpretivist descriptive methodology, involved interviews with 23 experienced therapeutic clowns from eight countries. They identified five major needs among elderly residents: escaping routine, seeking reassurance of worth, engaging in meaningful social interaction irrespective of communication barriers, reminiscing about culturally relevant experiences, and finding spaces where they can be unapologetically themselves. The clowns use creative and emotional strategies such as singing, physical comedy, and improvisation to meet these needs. These methods enhance residents' sense of belonging and dignity while also positively influencing staff and enriching interpersonal dynamics within the nursing home environment.
