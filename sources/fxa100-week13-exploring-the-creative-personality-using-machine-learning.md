## Exploring the Creative Personality: Using Machine Learning to Predict Fluency and Originality in Divergent Thinking

This study explores the personality attributes that predict ideational fluency and originality in divergent thinking (DT) using machine learning techniques on a dataset of self-reported personality items from 334 undergraduate participants. The research reveals distinct personality profiles for highly fluent and highly original individuals, characterized by a tension between seemingly opposed traits such as playful openness and disorderliness, enthusiasm for social interaction and depressive withdrawal, and dominant assertiveness tempered with awareness of others' feelings and emotional stability. Machine learning models identified specific personality items that predict these DT outcomes, suggesting the creative personality is composed of various dimensions from typical personality models like the Big Five. The findings highlight the complexity in understanding creativity through personality traits and underscore the importance of considering both quantity (fluency) and quality (originality) measures of creativity.
