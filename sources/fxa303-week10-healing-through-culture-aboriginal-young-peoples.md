## 'Healing through culture': Aboriginal Young People’s Experiences of Social and Emotional Wellbeing Impacts of Cultural Strengthening Programs

The study by Black, Frederico, and Bamblett explores the social and emotional wellbeing (SEWB) impacts of cultural strengthening programs for Aboriginal young people in Australia. The research highlights that participation in these programs helps strengthen SEWB through fostering connections to self, relationships, community, and culture, particularly among those who have experienced intergenerational trauma or out-of-home care. Key findings indicate that such initiatives build resilience against historical and ongoing adversities and encourage help-seeking behaviors. The study underscores the importance of engaging Aboriginal young people in both participating in and designing cultural programs as a means to promote healing and mental wellbeing.
