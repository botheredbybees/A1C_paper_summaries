## FXA302 Week 2 Welcome

This document introduces the second week of FXA302, focusing on ancient Greek perspectives on health and wellbeing. The text highlights how ideas from ancient physicians, philosophers, and creatives in Greece were foundational to discussions about art’s influence on mood and behavior for over two millennia. It emphasizes the significance of a classical education prevalent until mid-20th century Europe and its impact on understanding Graeco-Roman literature. The document also includes a case study on Homer, exploring notions of blindness and their symbolic meanings in ancient Greek thought. Additionally, it touches upon concepts such as Humourism and celestial music (music of the spheres), illustrating how these ideas persist through Western art and culture. This foundational content aims to provide students with key historical context for further exploration into creative arts and health.
