## Aperture and Shutter Priority Modes

This article by Darren Rowse from Digital Photography School explores the concepts of aperture and shutter priority modes on digital cameras. It explains that these semi-manual shooting modes give photographers control over either aperture or shutter speed, while the camera adjusts the other setting to ensure proper exposure. The author details how choosing different apertures impacts depth of field (DOF), with larger apertures resulting in shallow DOF and smaller ones leading to greater focus range. Similarly, varying shutter speeds affects motion blur; faster speeds freeze movement whereas slower ones introduce blur. Practical tips are provided for mastering these modes through experimentation and practice.
