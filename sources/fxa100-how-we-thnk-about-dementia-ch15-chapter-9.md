## The Body as the Articulation of Meaning in Dementia Care

The chapter explores how individuals with dementia can still communicate through non-verbal means such as gestures, sounds, and body movements despite cognitive impairments. It emphasizes that emotional expression and subjective experiences remain important even when language is lost. The concept of the 'collective subjective' highlights the shared nature of human inner lives and underscores the importance of situating individuals within their spatio-temporal reality to understand them fully. Examples from artists like Tatsumi Orimoto and Cathy Greenblat illustrate how art can facilitate meaningful interactions and enhance well-being for people with dementia, emphasizing the role of concern and solicitude in care practices.
