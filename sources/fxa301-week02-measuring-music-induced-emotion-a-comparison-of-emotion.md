## Measuring Music-Induced Emotion: A Comparison of Emotion Models, Personality Biases, and Intensity of Experiences

This study evaluates different models for measuring emotions induced by music, comparing the Geneva Emotional Music Scale (GEMS), a discrete emotion model, and a dimensional emotion model using valence and arousal. One hundred forty-eight participants listened to film music excerpts and rated their emotional responses. The dimensional model showed highest consistency in ratings and best discrimination of musical pieces. Individual differences based on personality traits influenced emotions particularly for the discrete emotion model. This research highlights that while more detailed models can provide nuanced insights, they may not be most economical for self-reporting felt emotions.
