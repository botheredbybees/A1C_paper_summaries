## Resilience and Hope: Exploring Immigrant and Refugee Youth Experiences through Community-based Arts Practice

The article examines the use of community-based arts practice to foster resilience and hope among immigrant and refugee youth in St. John’s, Newfoundland, Canada. The authors describe an Art Hive project designed to enhance mental well-being through art-making activities that promote interpersonal connections and community attachment. This initiative is grounded in Adlerian theory, collaborative community development, feminist thought, and social justice principles. The study focuses on the experiences of newcomer youth who face challenges such as social isolation and cultural disconnection in a predominantly homogeneous society. Through art-based participatory processes, the project seeks to explore themes related to resilience and hope and their relationship with the well-being of immigrant and refugee youth. The findings suggest that creative arts practices can play a significant role in fostering mental health resilience and building community among newcomer youth.
