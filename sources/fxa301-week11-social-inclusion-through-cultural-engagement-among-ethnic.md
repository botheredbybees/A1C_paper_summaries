## Social Inclusion Through Cultural Engagement Among Ethnic Communities

This article investigates the factors influencing arts attendance among different ethnic communities in Australia, using social inclusion as a theoretical framework. The research focuses on six ethnic groups categorized by their length of residence: Italian and Greek (long-established), Chinese, Vietnamese, and Indian (moderately established), and African (newly settled). Qualitative data from interviews and focus groups reveal four key themes linked to social inclusion: social connectedness, home culture links, host culture integration, and inclusive initiatives. The study highlights the importance of cultural engagement in facilitating social connectedness among these communities and suggests that arts marketing strategies should cater to varying levels of acculturation and different cultural backgrounds to promote social inclusion effectively.
