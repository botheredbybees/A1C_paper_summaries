## Effects of Neuromuscular Exercise and Dance Therapy on Physical Performance and Kinesiophobia in Geriatrics: A Randomized Controlled Study

This study evaluates the comparative effects of neuromuscular exercise and dance therapy on physical performance and kinesiophobia in geriatric patients. Fifty-two participants were randomly assigned to either a neuromuscular exercise group or a dance therapy group, both undergoing interventions for twelve weeks. The primary outcomes measured included balance (Berg Balance Scale, Tinetti Balance and Gait Assessment), lower extremity function (30-Second Sit-to-Stand Test), muscle strength (Manual Muscle Testing), range of motion, and kinesiophobia (Tampa Scale for Kinesiophobia). Significant improvements were observed in functional balance specifically within the dance therapy group. Both groups exhibited significant improvements in balance, lower limb function, and proximal muscle strength. The study concludes that both neuromuscular exercises and dance therapy are effective interventions to improve physical performance and reduce kinesiophobia in geriatrics. This research emphasizes the importance of incorporating these therapeutic modalities into rehabilitation programs to support active aging.
