## Trauma-informed care in the creative arts therapies

This editorial discusses the importance of trauma-informed care in creative arts therapies, emphasizing its relevance for practitioners working with traumatized individuals. The text highlights that childhood traumas can have significant and lasting impacts on physical and mental health due to neurobiological changes triggered by early life stress. It underscores the need for therapists to be sensitive to the residual distress experienced by those who endured repeated or single-event traumatic experiences during their formative years. The article also addresses the challenge of diagnosing developmental trauma disorder, a condition not included in the DSM-5 but proposed as crucial for understanding and treating children with complex histories of abuse and neglect. It stresses that creative arts therapies offer unique sensory-based interventions to help these individuals process trauma effectively. Finally, the piece calls for further research and training to ensure that practices align with contemporary standards of trauma-informed care.
