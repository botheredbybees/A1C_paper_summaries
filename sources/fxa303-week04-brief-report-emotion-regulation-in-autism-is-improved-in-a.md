## Brief Report: Emotion Regulation in Autism Is Improved in a Single-N Pilot Intervention Study Combining Child-Centered Play Therapy and Rhythmic Relating

This study evaluates the therapeutic potential of combining Child-Centered Play Therapy (CCPT) and Rhythmic Relating to enhance emotion regulation outcomes in autistic children over a period of ten months, involving 32 weekly sessions with a five-year-old girl diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder. The intervention shows statistically significant improvements in emotion regulation compared to two comparison groups: one comprising autistic children without cognitive impairment or psychological therapy and another consisting only of girls from the same group. The research underscores the value of prioritizing well-being and self-regulation over social behavioral changes, aligning with current priorities in autism support and intervention. It also highlights the importance of incorporating gender-based considerations into therapeutic approaches for better inclusivity and efficacy.
