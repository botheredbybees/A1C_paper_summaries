## Applied Theater: Storytelling Beyond Traditional Stages

The transcript discusses the concept of applied theater as a storytelling process that engages marginalized communities, corporate areas, prisons, newly arrived refugees, and age care facilities. It emphasizes the importance of storytelling to reflect on oneself and understand one's place in the world and future possibilities. The speaker mentions recent work with dementia patients involving clowns who develop personalized play scenarios aimed at creating pleasure and social relationships. Researchers from theater arts and dementia study the impact of such interventions.
