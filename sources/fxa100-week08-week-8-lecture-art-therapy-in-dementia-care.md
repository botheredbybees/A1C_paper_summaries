## Week 8 Lecture: Art Therapy in Dementia Care

This lecture by Dr Brendan Lamb for the course FXA100 focuses on art therapy and its application in dementia care. The presentation delineates between arts therapists, who apply therapeutic models through artistic activities, and facilitators who provide guidance but remain adaptable to participants' creative visions. Two modes of communication are highlighted: internal dialogue, where art serves as a form of self-reflection, and outward communication, aimed at sharing with others. Challenges in letting go of preconceptions about art making are discussed, suggesting that the process should be valued over the end product.

Guided initiation is introduced as a method to help individuals overcome cognitive barriers associated with dementia, emphasizing the importance of initiating actions like picking up a brush for someone experiencing executive function impairment. The lecture also covers safety considerations and cultural sensitivity when organizing art activities, noting the need to choose themes that resonate universally across diverse cultural backgrounds.

Additionally, it addresses how different types of dementia impact artistic abilities and creativity. For instance, Alzheimer's patients may show declining skills parallel to their cognitive decline, whereas Fronto-Temporal Dementia (FTD) might bring about new artistic talents due to reduced inhibition. Vascular dementia is associated with motor difficulties but often retains visual capabilities. The lecture concludes by posing key questions on communication modes and safety measures in art therapy, emphasizing the importance of creating a respectful environment where process takes precedence over outcome.
