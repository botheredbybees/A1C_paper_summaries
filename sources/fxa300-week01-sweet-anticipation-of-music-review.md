## Sweet Anticipation of Music Review

The review discusses two significant studies on musical expectation by David Huron and David Temperley, focusing on the psychological impact of prediction, tension, and reaction to events in music. Huron’s work introduces the 'ITPRA' theory, categorizing expectations into imagination, tension, prediction, reflexive reaction, and appraisal. This framework highlights how anticipation triggers emotional responses based on whether expectations are met or denied. Temperley explores Bayesian probability's application in understanding musical structures, emphasizing probabilistic models to infer musical keys and rhythms from surface elements like pitch classes. Both authors emphasize the importance of temporal predictability over periodicity in rhythmic expectation, suggesting that regular patterns evoke pleasure through predictability. This relevance to creative arts and health lies in how these theories explain the emotional and cognitive processes triggered by music, offering insights into therapeutic applications for sound and rhythm.
