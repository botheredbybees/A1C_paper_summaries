## Experimental evidence of the roles of music choice, social context, and listener personality in emotional reactions to music

This study examines the influence of music choice, social context, and individual personality traits on emotional reactions to music through an experimental approach. The research involved fifty university students (ages 20-43) who rated their emotions while listening to self-chosen or randomly selected music in solitary or social settings. Results indicated that participants experienced more intense emotions when the music was chosen by them and when they listened with a close friend or partner compared to alone. Additionally, individuals high on openness to experience reported stronger emotional responses than those low on this trait. The study highlights the importance of personal selection and social interaction in enhancing emotional engagement with music.
