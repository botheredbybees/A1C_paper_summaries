## FXA300 Music, Mind and Body - The Auditory System

The lesson covers the auditory system's role in recognizing sounds and identifying their location. It includes a video by Douglas L. Oliver on 'The Science of Hearing' explaining how sound waves travel through the ear to the cochlea where they are converted into neural signals. Key components like the tympanic membrane, malleus, incus, stapes, oval window, and organ of Corti are detailed. The brain's processing from the auditory cortex to other regions such as the midbrain, thalamus, cerebellum, limbic system, motor cortex, visual cortex, hippocampus, amygdala, and nucleus accumbens is also explained. Additionally, the TED Ed video by Anita Collins on 'How Playing an Instrument Benefits Your Brain' emphasizes how various brain areas contribute to musical activities.
