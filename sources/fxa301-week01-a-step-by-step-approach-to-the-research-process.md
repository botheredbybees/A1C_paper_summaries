## A step-by-step approach to the research process

Daisy Fancourt's chapter in 'Arts in Health: Designing and researching interventions' provides a comprehensive guide to the four stages involved in conducting research on arts interventions within health contexts. The chapter outlines these stages as developing an initial idea for a research study, designing the study, running the study, and determining the outcome of the research. It emphasizes the importance of identifying a research problem, developing research questions, theories, and feasibility assessments before initiating a project. Furthermore, it discusses choosing appropriate methodologies such as quantitative, qualitative, mixed methods, and economic evaluations. The chapter highlights the need to balance rigorous scientific standards with creative arts practices in health research, advocating for collaboration between these fields to enhance understanding of arts interventions' impact on health outcomes.
