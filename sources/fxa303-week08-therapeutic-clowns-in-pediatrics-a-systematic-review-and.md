## Therapeutic clowns in pediatrics: a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

This article presents a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials examining the effects of hospital clowns on pediatric patients undergoing medical procedures or hospitalization. The study found that therapeutic clown interventions effectively reduce anxiety and pain levels in children as well as state anxiety in their parents. Key findings include statistically significant reductions in child anxiety scores (SMD [95% CI] = −0.83 [−1.16, −0.51]) and parental state anxiety (SMD [95% CI] = −0.46 [−0.7, −0.21]). This review highlights the clinical utility of clown therapy in pediatric healthcare settings to alleviate stress and improve overall well-being for both children and their parents.
