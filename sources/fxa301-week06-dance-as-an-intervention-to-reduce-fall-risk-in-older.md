## Dance as an Intervention to Reduce Fall Risk in Older Adults: A Systematic Review With a Meta-Analysis

This study conducted a systematic review with meta-analysis to summarize the evidence from randomized clinical trials on the effects of dance interventions in reducing fall risk among older adults. The research aimed to evaluate how different types of dance programs impact fall risk, assessed through tests such as timed up and go (TUG), Berg Balance Scale (BBS), and one-leg stand (OLS). Key findings indicated a significant reduction in fall risk associated with dance interventions compared to control groups, with mean differences favoring the intervention group across all tests. The study highlights the potential of dance as an effective strategy for improving balance and functional capacity in older adults, thereby reducing their likelihood of falls. This has important implications for public health strategies aimed at preventing fall-related injuries among elderly populations.
