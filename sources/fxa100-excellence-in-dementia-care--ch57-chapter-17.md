## Embodied Selfhood and Creativity in Dementia Care

This chapter discusses the concept of embodied selfhood and its implications for dementia care. It highlights how individuals with Alzheimer's disease maintain meaningful social interactions and creativity through non-verbal means, such as tone of voice and gestures. The text provides examples from case studies and ethnographic research to illustrate that these forms of expression continue even when cognitive functions are severely impaired. It emphasizes the importance of recognizing embodied selfhood in person-centered care approaches, which can lead to better understanding and treatment of behavioral expressions in dementia patients.
