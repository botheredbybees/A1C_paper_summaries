## The Healing Power of Greek Tragedy

The article discusses Bryan Doerries' innovative project, Theater of War Productions, which uses ancient Greek plays to address contemporary mental health issues faced by veterans and their families. Through performances and discussions, the project aims to foster empathy and understanding among communities affected by war-related trauma. The story highlights how Doerries discovered the therapeutic power of classical tragedies through his personal experience with loss, specifically after the death of his girlfriend Laura. By translating and performing Greek tragedies in various settings across America, he encourages audiences to confront their emotions collectively, initiating healing conversations about pain, grief, and resilience. This initiative underscores the relevance of ancient literature in modern contexts, particularly for individuals grappling with psychological challenges stemming from war.
