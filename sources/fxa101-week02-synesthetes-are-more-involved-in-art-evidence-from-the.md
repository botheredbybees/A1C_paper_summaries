## Synesthetes are More Involved in Art — Evidence From the Artistic Creativity Domains Compendium (ACDC)

This study examines the involvement of synesthetes in various artistic domains and whether their type of synesthesia influences their preferred domain. The researchers tested a sample of 709 participants, including those with monotypical grapheme-color (GC), sound-color (SC), sequence-space (SS) synesthesia, multiple types of these synesthesias, and non-synesthetes using the Artistic Creativity Domains Compendium (ACDC). The ACDC assesses involvement in art on three levels: interest, ability, and performance across four domains—visual arts, literature, music, and performing arts. Results showed that synesthetes generally exhibit higher engagement in all artistic domains compared to non-synesthetes, with those having multiple types of synesthesia showing a pronounced preference for visual arts. This research suggests an association between synesthesia and heightened creativity in the artistic realm.
