## Stroke Choirs | Stroke Recovery Association NSW

The Stroke Recovery Association NSW highlights the benefits of participating in stroke survivor choirs for individuals recovering from a stroke. Research indicates that singing is an effective method to improve speech and is more enjoyable compared to traditional one-on-one therapy sessions. Choirs such as KeyStrokes (Crows Nest) and Brainwaves (Newcastle) offer members a supportive environment where they can connect with others who share similar experiences, fostering both physical and emotional recovery. The association has also produced a short film titled “Striking A Chord: Stroke Survivor Choirs in NSW,” directed by Tin Pang from Threecoin Pictures, which documents the impact of these choirs on stroke survivors. Additionally, an original song 'Aphasia' performed by the Brainwaves Choir underscores the unique challenges and triumphs faced by participants.
