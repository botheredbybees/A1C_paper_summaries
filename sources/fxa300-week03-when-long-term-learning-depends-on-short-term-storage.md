## When long-term learning depends on short-term storage

The study by A.D. Baddeley, C. Papagno, and G. Vallar investigates the relationship between short-term memory (STM) and long-term learning in a patient with impaired STM capacity. The researchers examine P.V., a case of a stroke survivor who exhibits selective auditory memory span impairment but maintains normal performance on many other cognitive tasks. Through various experiments involving word pair learning and foreign language vocabulary acquisition, they explore the necessity of short-term phonological storage for verbal learning. Their findings suggest that while short-term phonological storage is crucial for processing unfamiliar verbal material, it is less essential when forming associations between known meaningful items. The study also discusses implications for understanding vocabulary acquisition in children with developmental dyslexia, indicating a possible role of phonological memory deficits in reading difficulties and poor rote learning abilities.
