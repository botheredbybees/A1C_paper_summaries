## Case Studies of Therapeutic Creative Arts for Mental Health

This lesson discusses two case studies in therapeutic creative arts for mental health: 'Sing Your Heart Out' and Project eARTh. The former focuses on group singing as a treatment for individuals with mental health conditions, emphasizing the benefits of social bonding and emotional regulation. It includes insights from community-based singing groups like Choir of Hard Knocks and Sing Your Heart Out in Norfolk and Suffolk. The latter explores art therapy projects that involve creating artworks under professional guidance to help participants develop artistic skills and achieve therapeutic outcomes. Both studies highlight how engaging in creative arts can improve mental health, reduce stress, enhance social connections, and foster personal growth.
