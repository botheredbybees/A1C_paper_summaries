## School-based drama interventions in health promotion for children and adolescents: systematic review

The paper presents a systematic review of the literature on school-based drama interventions for health promotion in children and adolescents, focusing on their effectiveness. The study highlights that interactive methods like role-play are more effective than traditional didactic approaches in health education. Educational drama is based on theories emphasizing self-reflection and interaction between environment and person. Nine studies were included in the review, covering various aspects of health behavior, mental health, and social health. Interventions ranged from actor-performed dramas to classroom activities. While most studies reported positive outcomes regarding knowledge and attitudes towards health behaviors, there was a lack of consistent methods for evaluation. The authors conclude that more well-designed and theory-based studies are needed to understand the impact of drama interventions on health promotion.
