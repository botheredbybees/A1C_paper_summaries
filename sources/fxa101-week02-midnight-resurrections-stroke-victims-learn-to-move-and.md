## Midnight Resurrections: Stroke Victims Learn to Move and Speak Again

This chapter describes Dr. Michael Bernstein's recovery following a stroke using Edward Taub’s constraint-induced (CI) movement therapy. Initially, despite standard rehabilitation, Bernstein retained significant deficits such as needing a cane and lacking use of his left hand. CI therapy involved intensive training to encourage the brain to rewire itself by focusing on affected limbs through repetitive exercises. Bernstein made rapid progress, regaining fine motor skills in his left hand within weeks and eventually returning to work and playing tennis regularly. The chapter highlights Taub’s pioneering research into neuroplasticity for stroke recovery, which has shown promise not only for chronic stroke patients but also those with cerebral palsy, spinal cord injuries, Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis, and arthritis. Despite facing substantial criticism early in his career, Taub's work has since gained recognition for its transformative potential in neurological rehabilitation.
