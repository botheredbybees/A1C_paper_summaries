## Photography tips: Point of View Composition

Bill Hatcher discusses the application of point of view (POV) composition in photography, drawing parallels with its use in cinema and sports. He explains how POV can enhance visual storytelling by immersing viewers into the scene, often through dynamic perspectives such as over-the-shoulder or helmet-mounted camera angles. The article explores practical considerations for capturing POV shots using high-quality SLR cameras, including securing stable mounts on bikes and other vehicles to avoid damaging equipment while achieving unique vantage points. Hatcher demonstrates these techniques during a National Geographic assignment documenting cyclists crossing the Alaska Range, where he successfully integrated POV photos despite challenging terrain like flat tundra landscapes. The article highlights the importance of adaptability and pre-emptive testing in setting up reliable camera mounts for dynamic shooting conditions.
