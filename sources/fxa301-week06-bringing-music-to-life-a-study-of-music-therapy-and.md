## Bringing Music to Life: a Study of Music Therapy and Palliative Care Experiences in a Cancer Hospital

This research paper presents the findings of a three-month study conducted at the Peter MacCallum Cancer Institute, focusing on patients', visitors', and staff members' experiences with a music therapy program in a cancer hospital. The author employed thematic analysis based on grounded theory to examine responses from 128 participants who provided open-ended feedback about their interactions with music therapy sessions. Additionally, the researcher's reflective clinical journal was analyzed alongside these responses. Key themes identified include how music therapy can support palliative care aims throughout the illness trajectory by enhancing patients' quality of life and providing emotional comfort during arduous treatments. This study underscores the relevance of music therapy as a complementary approach in cancer treatment settings, offering qualitative evidence that aligns with broader health and creative arts discourses.
