## Long term music therapy for people with intellectual disabilities and the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS)

The article discusses the role of long-term music therapy for individuals with severe and profound intellectual disabilities within Australia's National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS). It highlights that despite the advent of NDIS which aims to provide self-determined access to services, there remains inconsistency in funding music therapy. The author presents a historical context of music therapy as beneficial for people with intellectual disabilities before focusing on recent initiatives to integrate it into NDIS plans more systematically. Case studies are used to illustrate how long-term engagement in music therapy can address specific needs and contribute positively towards participants' quality of life under the scheme.
