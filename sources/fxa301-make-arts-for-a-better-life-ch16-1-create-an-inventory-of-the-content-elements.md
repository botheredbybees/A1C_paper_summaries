## Analyzing Visual Messages and Cultural Context

This chapter discusses visual literacy and communication skills within communities. It outlines methods to analyze visual messages by identifying content elements, composition, and visual cues like color and form. The text emphasizes the cultural significance of these symbols and introduces Lester's perspectives for critical analysis: personal, historical, technical, ethical, cultural, and critical. These perspectives explore subjective viewer responses, historical context, production techniques, moral implications, cultural meanings, and broader human statements respectively. The chapter also addresses artists' roles in artistic communication and their interactions with communities. It highlights the necessity of understanding community attitudes towards different art forms and how these influence artistic creation, performance, and transmission. Finally, it examines creativity within communities by investigating processes of composition and the definition of 'newness'.
