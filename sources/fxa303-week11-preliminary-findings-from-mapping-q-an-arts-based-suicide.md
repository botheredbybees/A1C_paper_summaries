## Preliminary Findings From Mapping Q: An Arts-Based Suicide Prevention Program for Sexual and Gender Minority Youth

This study evaluates the preliminary effectiveness of an arts-based suicide prevention program, Mapping Q, for sexual and gender minority (SGM) youth in a large city in the Southwestern United States. The research involved 20 SGM participants aged between 13–24 years who completed pretest surveys during the initial week and posttests by the final sessions. Key findings revealed significant decreases in depressive symptoms and thwarted belongingness from pre- to posttest, although perceived burdensomeness and anxiety showed no significant changes but suggested potential reductions with larger samples. The study underscores Mapping Q's promise as an intervention for mitigating mental health disparities among SGM youth through enhancing social support networks and emotional regulation skills, thereby reducing suicidality risk factors.
