## A Preliminary Study of the Effects of an Arts Education Program on Executive Function, Behavior, and Brain Structure in a Sample of Nonclinical School-Aged Children

This study explores the impact of arts education on cognitive, emotional, and behavioral functions in children aged between 7 to 10 years. The research involved a nonclinical sample participating in either creative movement or musical arts programs for 15 weeks. Participants underwent evaluations using standardized tests measuring executive functions and scales assessing internalizing and externalizing symptoms. Additionally, brain magnetic resonance imaging was conducted to assess structural changes. Results indicated significant improvements in cognitive performance as measured by the Wisconsin Card Sorting Test, alongside enhanced emotional well-being reflected through reduced scores on depression inventory and conduct disorder assessments. Structural MRI data also revealed increased cortical thickness in specific brain regions and decreased mean diffusivity values in white matter tracts. These findings suggest that arts education positively influences executive functions associated with cognitive control, emotional regulation, and behavior modification in children.
