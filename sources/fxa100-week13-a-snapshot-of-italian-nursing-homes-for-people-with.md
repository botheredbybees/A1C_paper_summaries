## A snapshot of Italian nursing homes for people with dementia: A national survey

The study conducted a national survey to assess the characteristics and care services provided by Italian nursing homes (NHs) for people with dementia (PWD). The research involved 1,671 NHs, representing 46.3% of facilities and 53.1% of beds nationwide. Key findings included significant disparities across macro-areas in Italy, such as the number of PWD per available NH, distribution of R2D classification, staff availability, training levels, digital system integration, and wait times for admission. Northern NHs generally had more rooms, better-trained staff, and were more integrated with day care centers and palliative care services compared to southern facilities. The study highlights the fragmented nature of Italy's long-term care system, with uneven distribution of services and staff across different regions, underscoring the need for improved accessibility and affordability in NHs housing PWD.
