## You’re the only thing he comes out [of his room] for”: A qualitative study of engagement between Laughter Care Specialists and families of people with dementia in long-term care

The study examines the engagement between Laughter Care Specialists (LCSs) and families of people with dementia in long-term care settings. LCSs, trained to use humor and creative play, aim to engage individuals with dementia by providing joyous interactions and novel communication methods. Interviews with eight LCSs revealed that family members initially had varied degrees of openness towards the program but often became more accepting upon witnessing positive engagement between their loved ones and LCSs. The study highlights the potential benefits for families in learning new ways to communicate and connect, experiencing positive end-of-life interactions, and finding comfort into bereavement through Laughter Care. This approach offers novel means for communication and connection during advanced stages of dementia, potentially improving quality of life for both individuals with dementia and their families.
