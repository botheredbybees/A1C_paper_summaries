## The power of music

Oliver Sacks explores the profound impact of music on human beings, addressing its mysterious power despite being devoid of conceptual meaning or representation. He references Arthur C. Clarke’s novel 'Childhood's End' to illustrate how even highly intelligent extraterrestrial beings cannot comprehend this peculiarly human preoccupation. Sacks delves into philosophical perspectives from Schopenhauer and Nietzsche, discussing the physical and emotional responses music evokes in listeners. The commentary highlights music's communal function and its ability to induce trance-like states, illustrating both therapeutic benefits for neurological disorders such as Parkinson’s disease and Tourette’s syndrome, and potential negative effects if not properly moderated. Additionally, Sacks discusses how music can evoke deep emotions and memories in patients with Alzheimer’s disease or other dementias, underscoring the importance of familiar and evocative melodies in eliciting cognitive responses.
