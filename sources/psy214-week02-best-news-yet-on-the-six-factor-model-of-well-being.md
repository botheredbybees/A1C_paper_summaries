## Best news yet on the six-factor model of well-being

This commentary addresses Springer and Hauser's critique of Ryff’s six-factor model of psychological well-being, arguing that their analysis supports the original theory despite their interpretation suggesting otherwise. The authors highlight evidence from factorial validity studies, psychological correlates, sociodemographic correlations, biological indicators, and intervention studies to demonstrate the distinctiveness of the six dimensions identified in the Ryff Scales of Psychological Well-Being (RPWB). They critique Springer and Hauser’s methodological corrections, finding them problematic on both scientific rationale and implementation grounds. The commentary underscores the importance of a construct-oriented approach in personality assessment, emphasizing the theoretical foundation guiding the RPWB's development. This model is crucial for understanding positive psychological functioning beyond traditional subjective well-being measures.
