## Rehabilitation effects of game therapy in people living with dementia: A systematic review and meta-analysis

The study evaluates the effects of game therapy on people living with dementia (PLWD) through a systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized clinical trials and quasi-experimental studies. The analysis includes cognitive function, depression, and quality of life as outcome measures. Findings indicate that PLWD who underwent game therapy showed significantly improved scores in cognitive functioning measured by the Mini Mental State Examination (MMSE) and reduced symptoms of depression according to the Cornell Scale for Depression in Dementia. However, there was no significant difference observed in terms of quality of life between the intervention group and control group. The study concludes that game therapy can positively influence cognitive function and alleviate depression among PLWD, suggesting potential benefits from integrating diverse types of games tailored to individual needs.
