## An interdisciplinary approach of Synaesthesia: a brain process unifying the senses

Synaesthesia, a neurological condition characterized by the intermingling of sensory experiences, is explored from an interdisciplinary perspective in this paper. The authors delve into its genetic underpinnings, noting that while initially thought to be linked primarily through X-linked inheritance, more recent studies suggest complex genetic mechanisms and loci are involved. Neurochemically, serotonin hyperactivity has been proposed as a trigger for synaesthesia, particularly in cases of brain injury or use of psychedelic substances. Cognitive research highlights the role of cross-activation between closely located brain regions during early development, which can lead to synesthetic experiences. Additionally, the paper discusses potential cognitive advantages associated with synaesthesia, such as enhanced memory retention and creativity. These insights underscore the multifaceted nature of synaesthesia and its relevance across various scientific disciplines.
