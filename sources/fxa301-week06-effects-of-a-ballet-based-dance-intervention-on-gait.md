## Effects of a ballet-based dance intervention on gait variability and balance confidence of people with Parkinson’s

This study investigates the impact of weekly ballet dance classes on gait variability and balance confidence in people with Parkinson’s disease (PD). Despite previous research suggesting that dancing can improve balance and mobility for individuals with PD, this study did not find significant improvements in these areas after participating in ballet-based interventions. The researchers recruited 19 participants who were already attending regular ballet classes alongside a control group of 13 non-dancers to compare outcomes over the duration of the study. Although there is evidence that dance can enhance balance and gait, this research challenges those findings by suggesting that more specific conditions or frequencies of ballet class engagement may be required to observe positive effects on individuals with PD. The authors emphasize the need for further investigation into how particular types of dance movements might influence stability and functional improvement in people living with Parkinson’s.
