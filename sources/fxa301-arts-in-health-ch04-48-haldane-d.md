## Theoretical Background to Arts in Health

This chapter discusses the evolution of health definitions over two centuries, focusing on shifts from the biomedical model to more holistic approaches like the biopsychosocial model and psychosomatic medicine. It covers seminal contributions by figures such as George Engel and Sigmund Freud who introduced models considering psychological factors in physical illness. The text also delves into historical developments of public health practices, emphasizing environmental sanitation and social conditions' role in disease control and prevention.
