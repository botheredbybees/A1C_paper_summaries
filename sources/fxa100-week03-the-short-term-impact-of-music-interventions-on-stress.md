## The short-term impact of music interventions on stress: Results of a multinational cluster-randomized trial using salivary cortisol and alpha-amylase assessments in care home residents with dementia

This study investigates the impact of music interventions on stress in care home residents with dementia, utilizing salivary cortisol and alpha-amylase assessments to measure physiological changes before and after group music therapy or recreational choir singing sessions. The research aims to establish the feasibility of collecting saliva samples from individuals with advanced dementia for biomarker analysis and to detect differences between intervention groups and a control condition involving book reading. Key findings indicate that significant pre/post session changes in stress levels were observed within all study arms, though no significant difference was noted between interventions. Results suggest that group music therapy and choir singing can positively impact stress levels in care home residents with dementia, although the intensity of participation may influence outcomes. The feasibility of non-invasive saliva collection is also confirmed for this population. Future studies should consider methods to enhance data validity.
