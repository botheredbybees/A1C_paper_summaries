## Therapeutic Use of the Arts for Patients with Multiple Sclerosis

This review article discusses the potential benefits of therapeutic arts, including art therapy, dance/movement therapy, and music therapy, for patients with multiple sclerosis (MS). The authors argue that while MS presents a wide range of physical, emotional, and social challenges, these non-traditional treatments can address some of the physiological, psychological, cognitive, social, and spiritual needs of individuals living with MS. Key findings include improvements in self-efficacy, emotional well-being, and motor control. The review also highlights the need for further research to better understand the mechanisms and outcomes of therapeutic arts in managing MS-related disabilities. The article underscores the importance of comprehensive care approaches that incorporate various modalities, including complementary therapies, to enhance quality of life and promote wellness among individuals with chronic conditions like MS.
