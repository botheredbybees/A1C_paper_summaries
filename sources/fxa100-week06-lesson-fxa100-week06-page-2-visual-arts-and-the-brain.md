## FXA100 The Arts and Dementia Care: Visual Arts and the Brain

This lesson delves into the intersection of visual arts and neuroscience in dementia care. It highlights neuroaesthetics research by Professor Semir Zeki from University College London, who found that beautiful artworks trigger dopamine release in the brain's reward centers. Functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) was used to study brain responses to art, revealing increased blood flow to these regions when viewing aesthetically pleasing works. The lesson also explores an interactive light sculpture named _Affinity_ designed for dementia awareness, engaging multiple senses and activating various brain areas. Additionally, it discusses studies on embodied cognition involving 'mirror neurons' that mirror observed actions in the viewer's own motor cortex and emotional responses to artworks through personal relevance.
