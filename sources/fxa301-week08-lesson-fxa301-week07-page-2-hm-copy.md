## Case Studies of Theatre and Drama Engagement

This lesson explores case studies of theatre and drama engagement focusing on their health benefits and societal impact. It discusses the physiological and psychological advantages of laughter, including stress reduction and improved immune function. Theoretical underpinnings from Augusto Boal's 'Theater of the Oppressed' are introduced to highlight its role in addressing social issues through participatory theater techniques. Case studies include the 'Sharing Stories' program for sexual health education among culturally diverse youth, demonstrating improved knowledge and communication skills around sexual health. Another case is the 'Creative Gymnasium,' a drama project aimed at enhancing wellbeing and resilience among older people in Coventry, UK.
