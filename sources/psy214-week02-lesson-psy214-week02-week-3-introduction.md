## PSY114 PSY214 Stress, Self-Care and Mindfulness - Well-being and Happiness

This lesson explores the concepts of well-being and happiness in the context of stress management. It emphasizes that while managing stress is crucial, people generally seek more than just a lack of stress; they aim for positive states such as happiness and well-being. The discussion includes various models of happiness and well-being, including hedonia (focus on pleasure) and eudaimonia (pursuit of meaning), highlighting the complexity of defining these terms across individuals, researchers, and cultures. Students are encouraged to reflect on their personal definitions of happiness and well-being, considering how different aspects of life contribute to each state. Additionally, they will engage with well-being scales designed to measure various dimensions of psychological health, though these activities are optional.
