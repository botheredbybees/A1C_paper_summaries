## The Neurochemistry of Autism

The article reviews the neurochemical alterations underlying autism spectrum disorder (ASD), focusing on neurotransmitters such as GABA, glutamate, serotonin, dopamine, N-acetyl aspartate, oxytocin and arginine-vasopressin, melatonin, vitamin D, orexin, endogenous opioids, and acetylcholine. It highlights that ASD involves complex neuroanatomical and neurochemical events early in central nervous system development. The review discusses altered levels of these neurotransmitters in individuals with ASD compared to controls, suggesting disrupted excitatory/inhibitory balance as a mechanism for autistic behaviors. For example, reduced GABA and glutamate concentrations have been found in the striatum of individuals with idiopathic ASD and in mouse models, which correlates with impaired social behavior and information processing. The article emphasizes the need for further research on neurochemical alterations to develop better treatment and prevention strategies for autism.
