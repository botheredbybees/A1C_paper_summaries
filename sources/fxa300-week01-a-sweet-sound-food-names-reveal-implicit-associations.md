## A Sweet Sound? Food Names Reveal Implicit Associations Between Taste and Pitch

This study explores implicit associations between pitch and taste using the Implicit Association Test (IAT) and Go/No-Go Association Task (GNAT). The researchers found that both bitter and sour tastes are associated with low-pitched and high-pitched sounds, respectively. Experiment 1 aimed to determine if sweet and salty tastes also exhibit such sound-taste associations. High-pitched sounds were implicitly linked to the names of sweet food items, while no significant association was observed between low-pitched sounds and salty taste. Experiment 2 further investigated these associations using GNAT, revealing that both sour and sweet flavors are associated with high-pitch sounds, highlighting robust cross-modal associations between certain sound pitches and basic tastes.
