## FXA100 Week 2 Lecture: Music and the Brain

The lecture, titled 'Music and the Brain,' covers various aspects of auditory function, music components, and neuroscientific insights related to music and health. The session begins by detailing how different parts of the ear and brain process sound, moving into an exploration of common musical elements such as pitch, rhythm, volume, timbre, tempo, and form. It emphasizes that music involves intricate neural processes, with Daniel Levitin highlighting unique aspects like auditory memory encoding and the brain's predictive functions when reacting to music.

The lecture also discusses how music can aid in rehabilitation and pain management within dementia care contexts, citing studies that show improvements in cognitive function, emotional well-being, and physical movement. A case study of Keith Jarrett’s recovery from strokes illustrates practical applications of these theories.

Key readings such as Renwick & Woolhouse (2023) are suggested for further exploration on the reminiscence bump phenomenon, linking music to specific memories and psychological impacts. The lecture concludes with guidance on conducting research and APA 7 referencing standards pertinent to the course.
