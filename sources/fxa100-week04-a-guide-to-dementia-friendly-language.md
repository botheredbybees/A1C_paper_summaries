## A Guide to Dementia-Friendly Language

This document provides guidelines for using inclusive and non-stigmatizing language when discussing or writing about individuals living with dementia. It emphasizes the importance of language in shaping perceptions and attitudes towards people affected by dementia, advocating for terms that prioritize the person over their condition. The guide includes suggestions to use preferred terminology such as 'person living with dementia' instead of derogatory phrases like 'demented person.' Additionally, it highlights the need for culturally sensitive communication practices, especially considering diverse communities where specific terms might not exist or be misunderstood. The guidelines also encourage the use of realistic and respectful images in materials related to dementia care.
