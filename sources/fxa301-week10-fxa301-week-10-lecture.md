## FXA301 Week 10 Lecture

The lecture discusses the concept of 'flow' as developed by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi and its relevance to mental health through creative activities. It explores how experiencing flow can provide relief from mental disquiet and aid recovery, particularly in arts-based contexts such as singing or art therapy workshops. Case studies like Sing Your Heart Out in Norfolk and Suffolk highlight the effectiveness of such programs for mental health recovery due to their non-therapeutic approach and social benefits. Other examples include Project eARTH, which utilizes arts-on-prescription to support those with chronic mental health conditions, and the Montreal Museum’s collaboration on eating disorders through art workshops. The lecture also touches upon the role of creative arts in addressing PTSD and trauma, noting that these approaches can offer less anxiety-inducing ways for individuals to process traumatic experiences compared to traditional therapies.
