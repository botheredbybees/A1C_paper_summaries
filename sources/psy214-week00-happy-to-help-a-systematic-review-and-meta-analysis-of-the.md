## Happy to help? A systematic review and meta-analysis of the effects of performing acts of kindness on the well-being of the actor

The article conducts a systematic review and meta-analysis to investigate whether performing acts of kindness improves the well-being of the actor. The study reviewed 27 experimental studies involving 4,045 participants and found that the overall effect of kind acts on subjective well-being is small-to-medium (δ = 0.28). No significant moderating effects were observed based on sex, age, type of participant, intervention, control condition, or outcome measure. The study discusses theoretical frameworks such as kin altruism, mutualism, reciprocal altruism, and competitive altruism to explain the reasons behind kindness behaviors. It highlights that while acts of kindness can boost well-being, future research should test more specific theories about kindness, considering individual differences and the effects on different types of people to enhance our understanding of its causes and consequences.
