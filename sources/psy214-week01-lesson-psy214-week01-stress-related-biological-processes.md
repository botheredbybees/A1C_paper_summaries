## Stress-related biological processes and indicators

This lesson covers the biological impacts of chronic stress, focusing on immune, inflammatory, and oxidative processes. It discusses specific biomarkers such as heart rate variability, cortisol levels, pro-inflammatory cytokines, C-reactive protein (CRP), malondialdehyde, isoprostanes, antioxidant enzymes, T-cells, and natural killer cells. The lesson explains how these markers are influenced by stress and how they can be measured. It also highlights the importance of managing chronic stress to maintain immune health.
