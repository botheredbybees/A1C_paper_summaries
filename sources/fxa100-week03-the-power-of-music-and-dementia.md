## The Power of Music and Dementia

The document highlights the significant impact of music on individuals with dementia, challenging common misconceptions about memory loss in such conditions. Music therapists like Alexandra Morse emphasize that even people with severe forms of dementia retain the ability to respond to and engage with musical stimuli. This is due to the unique ways in which musical information is encoded in our memories, allowing for intact retrieval of long-term musical memories despite general cognitive decline. The interview with Morse delves into her personal journey towards music therapy and its applications in dementia care settings, stressing the therapeutic potential of music in maintaining personhood and facilitating communication when traditional methods may fail. Morse outlines various programs she conducts, from group singing sessions to individualized music mapping exercises aimed at preserving identity and fostering social connections. She also discusses measurable benefits such as reduced agitation and pharmacological interventions, alongside qualitative impacts like enhanced quality of life and improved family dynamics during end-of-life care.
