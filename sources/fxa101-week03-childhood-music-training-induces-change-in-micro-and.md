## Childhood Music Training Induces Change in Micro and Macroscopic Brain Structure: Results from a Longitudinal Study

This study examines the impact of childhood music training on brain development over a two-year period, beginning at age six. The research focuses on children from similar socio-economic backgrounds who participated in either a music education program or sports training, with a control group not involved in any systematic after-school activities. Key findings reveal that children engaged in music training showed differences in cortical thickness maturation rates and increased fractional anisotropy in the corpus callosum compared to their counterparts. These results suggest that musical training can induce structural changes in both macroscopic and microscopic brain structures, which are not attributable to pre-existing biological traits but rather to the influence of regular practice over time. The study contributes valuable insights into how structured activities like music education may shape neural development, particularly during critical developmental periods.
