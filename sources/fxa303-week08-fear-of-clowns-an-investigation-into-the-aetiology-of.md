## Fear of clowns: An investigation into the aetiology of coulrophobia

This study explores the origins of coulrophobia, or fear of clowns, which is prevalent in various cultures and age groups despite being under-researched. The research indicates that around half of adults surveyed have some level of fear towards clowns, with media portrayals playing a significant role alongside clown appearance and behavior unpredictability. Through reviewing existing literature and piloting a questionnaire among 528 participants who reported fear of clowns, the authors found that uncertainty about potential harm, media influences, and unpredictable behavior are critical factors contributing to coulrophobia. The paper also suggests that personal experience does not significantly contribute to this phobia. Overall, the study highlights the multifaceted nature of clown fear stemming from cultural representations, physical appearance, and behavioral unpredictability.
