## Risk Of Bias In Non-randomized Studies – Of Interventions, Version 2 (ROBINS-I V2) Assessment Tool

The document outlines the ROBINS-I Version 2 (V2) risk-of-bias assessment tool for non-randomized studies focusing on follow-up or cohort studies. It aims to evaluate the risk of bias in specific results comparing intervention strategies with comparator strategies, which might be alternative interventions, standard care, or no intervention at all. The key innovation is the concept of a 'target trial'—a hypothetical scenario that defines what would be observed without any bias due to confounding factors. This approach helps assess six domains of potential bias: confounding, classification of interventions, participant selection, missing data, measurement of outcomes, and reporting of results. Unlike previous versions, ROBINS-I V2 does not include a domain for deviations from intended protocols but considers this in the context of per-protocol effect analyses within Domain 1 (Variant B). The tool is designed to be used by multiple assessors who must agree on preliminary considerations before individual assessments are conducted.
