## The Impact of TimeSlips, a Creative Expression Intervention Program, on Nursing Home Residents With Dementia and their Caregivers

This study evaluates the impact of TimeSlips, a group storytelling program designed for individuals with dementia and their caregivers, on quality of life and care in nursing home settings. The intervention was implemented through a ten-week training period followed by an observational study at twenty nursing homes across two states. Findings indicate that residents in TS facilities showed higher engagement and alertness compared to those without the program. Additionally, staff members who participated in TimeSlips reported more positive attitudes towards dementia patients and engaged in more interactions with them. While there were no significant differences in job satisfaction or burnout levels between staff in intervention and control groups, the study suggests that TimeSlips can create a more supportive care environment for residents and caregivers alike.
