## Promoting wellbeing and health through active participation in music and dance: a systematic review

This systematic review by Sheppard and Broughton explores the relationship between music and dance participation and key social determinants of health for individuals without pre-existing health issues in diverse communities. The study reveals that music and dance are linked to various aspects of physical, mental, and cultural wellbeing from a socio-determinant perspective. Identified gaps include underrepresentation of specific demographic groups such as middle-aged participants, men, Indigenous populations, LGBTQIAP+, and migrants. Additionally, there is inconsistency in theoretical frameworks and methodologies used across different studies. The review highlights the potential for cross-disciplinary collaboration to enhance preventive healthcare approaches and policy-making. This research underscores the importance of integrating performing arts into public health strategies to promote widespread wellbeing.
