## The 2024 Happy Planet Index

The 2024 Happy Planet Index (HPI) is a report by the Hot or Cool Institute that assesses sustainable wellbeing across nations. It measures how well countries achieve high levels of life expectancy and self-reported wellbeing within an environmentally fair carbon footprint limit, aiming to support equitable living standards while ensuring future sustainability. The latest results indicate no country has achieved fully sustainable wellbeing as defined by the HPI's criteria. Vanuatu leads with a balanced approach to wellbeing and environmental impact, followed closely by Sweden despite having a larger carbon footprint than other wealthy countries. Costa Rica, which had previously topped the index since 2009, dropped due to pandemic impacts but still maintains high levels of wellbeing within near-fair consumption limits.

The report highlights that focusing on GDP growth often leads societies away from achieving sustainable wellbeing; many top-GDP nations have subpar HPI scores. The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic revealed significant declines in life expectancy and self-reported wellbeing, even as carbon emissions decreased globally, illustrating a complex interplay between economic downturns and public health outcomes.

Analyzing trends over time shows that while some regions like East Asia saw improvements in self-reported wellbeing with limited increases in carbon footprint, overall progress has been insufficient to address climate change effectively. The HPI also reveals stark inequalities within countries where higher income groups often have much larger carbon footprints without proportionate gains in reported wellbeing, underscoring the role of inequality in wasteful inefficiency.

Key lessons from the report emphasize the importance of moving beyond GDP-centric measures and developing alternative indicators that prioritize environmental sustainability and equitable living standards. It calls for greater citizen involvement in defining national success metrics.
