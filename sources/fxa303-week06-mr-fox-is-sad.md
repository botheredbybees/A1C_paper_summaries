## Mr. Fox Is Sad

The article explores the use of puppets in play therapy as a tool for emotional expression and therapeutic engagement with children. It emphasizes how puppetry facilitates projection, enabling children to communicate their concerns and externalize emotions without verbalizing them directly. The text highlights practical considerations such as choosing appropriate puppets based on cultural sensitivity and developmental appropriateness, suggesting the use of a diverse collection including aggressive, nurturing, magical, beautiful/ugly, and family-themed puppets. Techniques discussed include 'The Hidden Puppet Technique,' which helps anxious or resistant children by addressing puppet anxiety symbolically. A case study involving Emily, a 5-year-old with emotional dysregulation, demonstrates how creating a symbolic client through storytelling can engage the child in therapy and facilitate emotional expression.
