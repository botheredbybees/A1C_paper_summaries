## Data Analysis, Dissemination, and Ethical Considerations

This chapter discusses the ethical considerations in data analysis and dissemination, emphasizing participant anonymity and proper handling of sensitive information. It highlights the importance of unbiased language and transparency in reporting findings. Ethical authorship is also addressed according to the Vancouver Protocol, which requires substantial contributions for authorship attribution. The publication process involves acknowledging non-results, providing demographic details, outlining limitations, and avoiding overgeneralization or misrepresentation. Additionally, ethical approvals are outlined with a focus on research protocols, participant information sheets, and consent forms.
