## Cortical correlates of creative thinking assessed by the figural Torrance Test of Creative Thinking

This study explores the cortical correlates of creative thinking as measured by the figural Torrance Test of Creative Thinking (TTCT). The researchers identified two factors within the TTCT: ‘FO’ (fluency and originality) and ‘RAS’ (resistance to premature closure, abstractness of titles, sophistication/elaboration). Using voxel-based morphometry, they found that the FO factor is positively associated with cerebral volumes in parieto-temporal regions, while the RAS factor correlates with fronto-temporal regions. The findings suggest specific neural substrates for different dimensions of creativity and contribute to understanding the neuroanatomy underlying creative thinking.
