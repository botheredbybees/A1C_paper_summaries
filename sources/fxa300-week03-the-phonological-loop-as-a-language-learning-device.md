## The Phonological Loop as a Language Learning Device

This article explores the role of the phonological loop, a component of working memory, in language learning and development. The authors propose that while its function is not immediately apparent through experimental studies with adults, it plays a crucial role in helping children learn new words by providing temporary storage for unfamiliar phonological forms until more permanent representations can be constructed. Evidence from studies on vocabulary acquisition indicates strong correlations between short-term memory capacity and vocabulary knowledge, suggesting that the phonological loop supports the learning of novel word sounds—a fundamental skill during language development. The article challenges previous views on the significance of the phonological loop by emphasizing its importance in language acquisition processes.
