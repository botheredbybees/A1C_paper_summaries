## Refugee Art â€“ Expressing Experience Through Creativity

This webpage highlights the profound impact of refugee artists who transform their traumatic experiences into powerful expressions of resilience and hope. Kamaleshwaran Selladurai, a Tamil refugee from Sri Lanka, taught himself to paint while in Australian detention centers and uses his art to depict the struggles faced by detainees. Michael Adonai, an Eritrean refugee, has gained international recognition for his coptic-style artwork that reflects his experiences as a freedom fighter during wartime. Alwy Fadhel, an asylum seeker detained at Villawood Detention Centre, employs instant coffee powder as a medium to illustrate the emotional and physical hardships endured by detainees. Murtaza Hussaini, a refugee from Afghanistan, leverages his personal war narratives to create compelling portraits that challenge societal perceptions of refugees. These artists not only provide insight into the plight of displaced individuals but also demonstrate the therapeutic value of creative expression in healing and self-empowerment within the context of community arts.
