## Conclusions and implications for future research

This chapter concludes that arts-based approaches can contribute to the transformation of health and care needed to meet future challenges, while also identifying political, organizational, and social processes that create barriers and opportunities for change. Research demonstrates various health and well-being benefits across different art forms, with participation linked to improvements in individual and community levels. Despite these advances, challenges remain, such as uneven development in affluent countries, research focusing primarily on positive aspects without addressing broader questions about social impact. Issues of funding and resources have resulted in a fragmented field with short-term projects dependent on small organizations and individuals. The chapter highlights the need for more robust theoretical frameworks from social sciences to understand future developments, emphasizing the importance of user voices in policy decisions and the role of boundary work in reducing divisions.
