## Visual Art in Physical Rehabilitation: Experiences of People with Neurological Conditions

This qualitative study explores the experiences of adults participating in a visual art program as part of their outpatient physical rehabilitation for neurological conditions. The research aimed to understand from clients' perspectives how visual arts contribute to rehabilitation and recovery. Interviews with nine participants revealed that the art programme helped them meet individual goals, use time effectively, increase enjoyment, regain confidence, and plan future activities. These outcomes align with broader rehabilitation aims such as enhancing perceived control, self-esteem, and social support. The study suggests that visual art has a valuable role in physical rehabilitation but acknowledges economic constraints and the need for quantifiable outcomes to validate its place within treatment programs.
