## LGBTQ+ Choirs, Community Music, Queer Artistic Citizenship in London

Thomas Hilder’s chapter examines the role of LGBTQ+ choirs in fostering community and artistic citizenship, focusing on London-based ensembles such as the London Gay Men's Chorus (LGMC) and the Pink Singers. The text recounts how these groups came together after the Orlando nightclub shooting to mourn and support each other, highlighting their significance in providing safe spaces for queer individuals to express themselves creatively and foster solidarity. Hilder discusses the origins of LGBTQ+ choirs from the 1970s onwards, noting their potential to cater to contemporary needs through communal singing, queer national repertoires, and outreach projects. He employs theoretical frameworks such as 'musical asylums' by Tia DeNora to argue that these choirs offer respite and flourishing for LGBTQ+ members, addressing mental health issues prevalent in the community. The chapter also delves into the political dimensions of choir activities, including risks of co-optation through pink-washing or homonationalism, while emphasizing their role in promoting LGBTQ+ rights and creating transformative social change.
