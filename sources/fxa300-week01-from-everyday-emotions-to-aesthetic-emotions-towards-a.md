## From everyday emotions to aesthetic emotions: Towards a unified theory of musical emotions

Patrik N. Juslin's article in Physics of Life Reviews discusses the paradox of how abstract musical sounds can evoke profound emotions that are biologically linked to human survival. The study presents a framework called BRECVEMA, which includes eight psychological mechanisms that explain emotional responses to music: Brain Stem Reflex, Rhythmic Entrainment, Evaluative Conditioning, Contagion, Visual Imagery, Episodic Memory, Musical Expectancy, and Aesthetic Judgment. This revised model aims to integrate both 'everyday emotions' and 'aesthetic emotions', thereby reconciling different theoretical approaches in the field of musical emotion studies. The article highlights the ecological perspective on sound perception as crucial for understanding how sounds acquire meaning and influence emotional responses, offering a comprehensive theory that bridges everyday experiences with more profound aesthetic appreciations in music.
