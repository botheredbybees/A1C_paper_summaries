## Art, Ingenuity and Refugees

Syed Sadfar Ahmed discusses the role of art in opening minds, fostering diversity, and serving as a refuge during difficult times. He shares personal experiences and how art helped him navigate his teenage years. The focus shifts to the Refugee Art Project he founded, which aims to provide detained asylum seekers an outlet for self-expression through art classes held at detention centers like Villawood. This project counters negative public perceptions of refugees by displaying their artwork in community exhibitions. Ahmed highlights various artworks created under such conditions, addressing themes like war trauma, family separation, and the desire for a new life in Australia. The initiative also leverages social media to connect artists with audiences when physical presence isn't possible.
