## Theories of Creativity

This chapter explores various theories of creativity, providing a comprehensive overview and analysis from different perspectives within the field of creativity research. It delves into foundational concepts, historical developments, key theoretical frameworks, and contemporary debates surrounding creativity. The authors Aaron Kozbelt, Ronald A. Beghetto, Mark A. Runco, James C. Kaufman, and Robert J. Sternberg examine a range of theories including person-centered approaches (e.g., personality traits associated with creative individuals), product-oriented perspectives (focusing on the characteristics of creative outcomes), process-based models emphasizing cognitive processes during creativity, and contextual theories that consider environmental influences on creativity.
