## Negative Emotion in Music: What is the Attraction? A Qualitative Study

The paper explores why individuals seek out and enjoy listening to music that evokes negative emotions, despite such emotions generally being associated with unpleasantness in everyday life. Utilizing a qualitative approach based on individual differences psychology and mood management theory, the study examines five comparative interviews. Participants engaged in live musical experiences during these interviews. The research identifies several mechanisms allowing for enjoyment of sad music, including absorption and dissociation which inhibit displeasure centers while enabling emotional arousal to be experienced without discomfort. Additionally, participants sought psychological benefits such as reflection on life events, emotional communion, or catharsis through the experience of negative emotions in musical contexts.
