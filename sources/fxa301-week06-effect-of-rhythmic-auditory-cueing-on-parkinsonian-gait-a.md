## Effect of rhythmic auditory cueing on parkinsonian gait: A systematic review and meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis examines the impact of rhythmic auditory cueing (RAC) on gait performance in Parkinson's disease patients. The study analyzed 50 studies involving 1892 participants, finding a positive effect of RAC on gait velocity and stride length while observing no significant change in cadence. The review discusses neurophysiological mechanisms underlying the benefits of auditory cueing, including modulation of β oscillations and enhanced motor imagery. Additionally, it highlights the role of RAC as an adjunct therapy to conventional treatments such as pharmacological interventions. The research suggests that incorporating RAC into rehabilitation approaches can enhance motor performance and quality of life for individuals with Parkinson's disease.
