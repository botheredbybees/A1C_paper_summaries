## Do you struggle with constructing a photo story? Try this 3-picture technique

The article by Kav Dadfar addresses the common challenge faced by photographers in constructing compelling photo stories. It highlights that merely presenting a series of aesthetically pleasing images does not necessarily constitute a cohesive narrative. The author emphasizes the importance of having a clear story and suggests a method to train oneself: the 3-picture technique. This involves capturing three distinct types of photographs—beginning with a 'wow' factor shot, followed by one that conveys the main message or theme of the story, and concluding with an intimate detail shot. Dadfar asserts that these images must be interconnected yet unique enough to maintain viewer interest. He further explains how this technique can be expanded upon through repetition and practice for more extensive photo stories.
