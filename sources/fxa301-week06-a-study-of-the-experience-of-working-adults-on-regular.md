## A Study of the Experience of Working Adults on Regular Choral Activities: Applying Focus Groups

This study explores the experiences of working adults participating in regular choral activities, focusing on self-improvement and sense of accomplishment, interaction and harmony, discovery of positive self-identity, and healing and happiness. The research utilized focus group interviews to understand how choral participation can enhance quality of life through social engagement and personal development. The findings suggest that choir activities foster a sense of community and contribute to the psychological well-being of working adults, making it relevant for creative arts and health initiatives.
