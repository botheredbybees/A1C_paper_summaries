## Medicine within Health Humanities

Gail Allsopp's chapter, 'Medicine within Health Humanities,' explores the integration of creative and humanities-based practices in medical settings to enhance patient care, team dynamics, and practitioner well-being. The author argues that while medical humanities traditionally focus on the relationship between medicine and the arts, a broader approach encompassing health humanities is more inclusive and beneficial. Allsopp provides practical examples of how creativity can be incorporated into healthcare interactions with patients, such as using visual aids like videos or puppets to calm anxious children during examinations, employing role-play to address medication adherence issues in pediatric patients, and utilizing visualization techniques for adults undergoing chemotherapy. These methods not only improve patient experiences but also foster stronger connections between medical practitioners and their patients. The chapter emphasizes the importance of including all team members and caregivers in these creative approaches, rather than limiting them to 'medics.' By integrating health humanities into clinical practice, healthcare professionals can create more empathetic, effective, and joyful care environments.
