## What We Know Currently about Mirror Neurons

This minireview article discusses the current understanding of mirror neurons, a class of neuron that modulates activity during both action execution and observation in macaque monkeys. Mirror neurons were first discovered in the ventral premotor region F5 and have been extensively studied for their potential roles in social cognition and motor system function. The authors review studies directly recording from these neurons, noting variability in reported percentages over time due to methodological differences. They highlight mirror neuron specificity, anatomical subdivisions within area F5, responses to video stimuli, tool-based actions, auditory cues, and modulations by additional factors such as occlusion or reward value. This research contributes to understanding the neural mechanisms underlying action observation and execution, which are critical for social interaction and motor learning in both humans and non-human primates.
