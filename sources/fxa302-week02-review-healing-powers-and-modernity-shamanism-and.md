## Review: Healing Powers and Modernity, Shamanism, and Neurological Perspectives on Consciousness

Beth A. Conklin reviews three books that explore various aspects of shamanism: 'Healing Powers and Modernity', 'Shamanism: The Neural Ecology of Consciousness and Healing', and 'Was ist ein Schamane?/What is a Shaman?'. These works represent different approaches to studying shamanism, ranging from identifying cross-cultural universals to exploring the contemporary dynamism of spiritual healing practices in Asian societies. Conklin discusses how each book contributes unique insights into the subject matter: one focuses on universal characteristics of shamanic activities across cultures; another uses a neurophenomenological approach to explain the psychobiology and cognition involved in shamanic rituals; and the third examines the politics and global contexts affecting indigenous healing practices. The review highlights the evolving understanding of shamanism within the field of medical anthropology, emphasizing its relevance for contemporary health issues and cultural identity.
