## Social isolation and connectedness as determinants of well-being: Global evidence mapping focused on LGBTQ youth

This article explores the impact of social isolation and connectedness on the well-being of LGBTQ youth, presenting a global evidence mapping based on peer-reviewed research published between 2000 and 2018. It highlights that social isolation is characterized by a lack of belonging, inability to engage with others, and deterioration of relationships. The review reveals high incidences of family rejection, bullying, violence, and mental health issues among LGBTQ youth compared to heterosexual and cisgender counterparts. The study emphasizes the role of minority stress theory in understanding these disparities, suggesting that social isolation stems from victimization, discrimination, and internalized stigma experienced by LGBTQ youth in various environments such as families, schools, peers, religious organizations, and online platforms. It also underscores the importance of fostering supportive environments through interventions that address social, cultural, and structural dimensions to enhance well-being among this marginalized group.
