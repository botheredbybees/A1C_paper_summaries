## Commedia dell'Arte: Characters, Scenarios, and Rhetoric

The article discusses the enduring influence of Commedia dell'Arte, a form of theatre that flourished in Italy from the mid-sixteenth to the late eighteenth century. The author highlights the importance of extant documentation beyond iconography, such as scenarios and textual evidence, which has been underutilized by scholars and performers. Despite its rich visual legacy, Commedia dell'Arte's extensive literary and rhetorical context is crucial for a comprehensive understanding of the form. The piece reviews recent scholarly works that emphasize the interplay between commedia dell'arte performances and written drama, challenging the notion that it was purely improvisational and separate from literature. This article also critiques contemporary performance guides that overlook this broader historical context, advocating for a more nuanced approach to studying and performing Commedia dell'Arte in relation to both oral tradition and literary influence.
