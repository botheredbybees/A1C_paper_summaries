## Fading Notes: Music Education for the Next Generation of Primary Teachers

The document 'Fading Notes' investigates the provision of music education within Australian initial teacher education (ITE) degrees. It highlights that compulsory music training time has decreased significantly since 2009, underscoring a critical underinvestment in this area. Key findings reveal inadequate incentives for music education, limited practical learning opportunities, and insufficient emphasis on building necessary skills. The research also notes that most ITE students do not expect to teach music and require further professional development post-graduation. This lack of preparedness poses challenges for delivering quality music education in primary classrooms, impacting the holistic development of children. The study suggests immediate support through short-term solutions such as targeted professional development programs while recommending long-term reforms for curriculum redesign.
