## The FIVE project—addressing the stigma of mental health through community arts engagement

The FIVE project is a community arts initiative in Western Australia aimed at reducing stigma around mental health among FIFO and residential mining communities, which face unique challenges due to geographical isolation and social disconnection. The project was implemented by DADAA (Disability Access and Inclusion Development Association of Australia) in collaboration with Rio Tinto, local governments, and other organizations. Over two years, FIVE engaged over 7,000 people through various artistic interventions like sculpture and film to foster dialogue about mental health and wellbeing. The initiative targeted FIFO wives and families as well as mining workers directly, aiming to reduce social isolation and increase awareness of mental health issues. By embedding the arts project within Rio Tinto's operations, FIVE also facilitated conversations among senior leaders, contributing to broader awareness and support for mental health initiatives within the company. This project is relevant to creative arts and health by demonstrating how artistic interventions can be effective in addressing complex social issues, particularly those related to mental health stigma in remote and resource-dependent communities.
