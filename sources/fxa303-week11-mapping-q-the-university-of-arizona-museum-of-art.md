## Mapping Q | The University of Arizona Museum of Art

Mapping Q is an initiative by the University of Arizona Museum of Art in Tucson, AZ, aimed at providing arts-based activities for youth aged 14-24 who identify as LGBTQIA+. The program seeks to address issues related to self-care, community building, and harm reduction through visual art workshops. Since its inception in 2014, Mapping Q has offered a safe space for young individuals to explore their identities and communities, culminating in an exhibition at the museum's Our Stories Community Gallery. The central argument of this program is that belonging involves not just acceptance but also support, understanding, and empowerment to be one’s authentic self. This initiative highlights the importance of creative arts in fostering mental well-being among LGBTQIA+ youth by promoting resilience and self-expression through art-making activities.
