## Week 1 Lecture Transcript

This week’s lecture explores the concept of a photo essay as a powerful form of visual storytelling that combines multiple images with text to convey complex narratives. The lecturer emphasizes that photos can capture personal and communal moments, evoke emotional responses, and provide context to broader issues. Photo essays are noted for their ability to tell investigative stories or personal journeys in detail. Historical roots trace back to the 1930s–50s magazine era with examples like W. Eugene Smith’s “Country Doctor.” In the digital age, photo essays offer a deeper engagement than single images, resisting loss and manipulation due to their narrative depth and slower pace compared to video content. Benjamin Ball’s quote highlights the calming effect of photo essays in an era of rapid image circulation.
