## The Use of Puppets in Psychodynamic Child Therapy

The chapter explores the integration of puppets in psychodynamic child therapy, highlighting their role in enhancing therapeutic engagement and facilitating symbolic expression. Psychodynamic play therapy is based on four fundamental assumptions: symptoms have meaning, problems arise from unconscious conflicts or developmental deficits revealed through play, play itself is symbolic, and transference-based thoughts characterize much behavior. The use of puppets creates psychological safety by allowing children to externalize and miniaturize their distressing feelings, which helps them confront complex issues in manageable doses. This method aids in developing self-soothing skills, reducing anxiety, enhancing resilience, and fostering healthier development. The chapter also outlines practical considerations for therapists, including sourcing high-quality puppets from specialized stores.
