## Arts and Refugees: Multidisciplinary Perspectives (Vol. 2)

The article explores the role of arts in the lives and integration processes of refugees and migrants, emphasizing multidisciplinary perspectives. It highlights how artistic practices contribute to social, cultural, economic, and political participation, as well as fostering solidarity movements and claim-making processes. The authors discuss the therapeutic aspects of art for managing psychosomatic disorders and enhancing mental wellbeing among refugees, beyond health-related benefits. They also analyze gender dynamics in refugee artistic experiences and racial inequalities in cultural sectors that can be combated through alternative aesthetic norms proposed by minoritised populations. This work is relevant to creative arts and health as it demonstrates the power of art in healing trauma and fostering social inclusion.
