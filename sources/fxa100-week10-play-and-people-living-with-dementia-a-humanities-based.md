## Play and People Living With Dementia: A Humanities-Based Inquiry of TimeSlips and the Alzheimer’s Poetry Project

This paper explores the concept of 'play' in dementia care through a humanities-based inquiry using Johan Huizinga's framework of homo ludens ('man the player'). The authors argue that play, rather than being infantilizing or trivializing for people with dementia, offers potential for expression, meaning-making, and relationship-building. They analyze two participatory arts programs—TimeSlips and Alzheimer’s Poetry Project—in residential care settings in the Netherlands over ten weeks. Key findings include participants learning to play again, the power of collective play, and expressions of joy. The paper highlights that while existing research on dementia care often focuses on measurable outcomes like health or cognition, a humanistic approach through play can offer unique insights into the lived experiences and capabilities of people with dementia.
