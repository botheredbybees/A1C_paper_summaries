## Clowns Bring Joy to Refugee Children in Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh

This transcript from a BBC News video on March 5, 2018, features Hannah and Richard, performers with Clowns Without Borders UK, discussing their experience entertaining Rohingya refugee children in Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh. The camp houses over one million refugees fleeing violence in Myanmar, including more than 400,000 children. Despite the dire circumstances, clowns provide essential mental and emotional support by reducing anxiety through laughter and distraction from trauma. Performers recount their initial reactions to seeing smiling faces amidst suffering and address the ethical challenges of connecting with victims of such atrocities while providing entertainment.
