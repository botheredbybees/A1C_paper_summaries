## National Arts and Health Framework

The National Arts and Health Framework, endorsed by Australia’s Health Ministers and Cultural Ministers in May 2014, aims to enhance the integration of arts initiatives into health promotion, services, settings, and facilities across all states and territories. The framework underscores the value of arts in improving health outcomes for Australians and acknowledges the importance of collaborative relationships between the arts and health sectors at both national and local levels.

The document defines arts and health as applying artistic practices to address health problems and promote well-being in various settings, including hospitals, communities, and educational institutions. It highlights the diverse benefits of such initiatives, ranging from improved communication and staff retention rates in healthcare facilities to enhanced community resilience and reduced social isolation among marginalized populations.

Furthermore, the framework recognizes the unique contributions of arts practice to Indigenous health and cultural maintenance, promoting holistic approaches that integrate traditional practices into modern healthcare delivery. The document also emphasizes the importance of ongoing research to build evidence about the benefits of arts and health activities and encourages partnerships across sectors to strengthen awareness and acceptance of these practices in health services.

Overall, this framework serves as a comprehensive guide for integrating arts initiatives into health care settings and community programs, aiming to improve health outcomes and enhance quality of life through creative engagement.
