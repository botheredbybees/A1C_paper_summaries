## Neurodiversity and the Autism Spectrum

The text discusses neurodiversity in childhood, particularly focusing on autism spectrum disorder (ASD) and attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD). It highlights that the medical model of disability often dominates discussions due to opportunities for intervention during critical developmental stages. The document outlines how ASD is diagnosed based on persistent social interaction difficulties and impaired emotional and non-verbal skills, ranging from mild to severe in terms of symptom intensity. Research indicates differences in brain anatomy and function among individuals with ASD, suggesting that the disorder involves issues with neural connectivity rather than isolated brain regions. Therapies for autism range from behavioral interventions to developmental approaches using a child's interests to enhance communication and social skills. The efficacy of these therapies is influenced by factors such as family dynamics and socio-economic capabilities. The document underscores the importance of early intervention in improving outcomes for children with ASD.
