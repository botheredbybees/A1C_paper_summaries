## Arts on Prescription in Sefton: Programme Report

The 'Arts on Prescription' program, initiated by Creative Alternatives in Sefton in November 2006, is a pioneering arts and health initiative aimed at individuals experiencing mild to moderate depression or anxiety. This report provides an extensive evaluation of the program's outcomes and challenges over its first three years. The project was funded by Invest To Save and operates within NHS Sefton’s Social Prescribing network. It offers free creative activities such as visual arts, writing, storytelling, and workshops requiring specialist equipment like photography and pottery. Clients also participate in cultural outings to galleries and festivals.

Key findings indicate significant improvements in mental health among participants, with 65% showing reduced symptoms of depression or anxiety according to the Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale (HADS). Other measures such as the Dartmouth COOP Chart reflect substantial enhancements in clients' overall quality of life, mental health, social activities, and daily functioning. Additionally, 27% of clients reported a decrease in medication use for mental health conditions.

Despite these positive outcomes, the program faced logistical challenges including managing high demand and optimizing workshop sizes to ensure effective facilitation by artists and programme officers. The report concludes with recommendations for sustainable growth and continued impact on the community’s arts and health landscape.
