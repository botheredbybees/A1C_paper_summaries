## A Complete Medical Education Includes the Arts and Humanities

The article discusses the importance of integrating arts and humanities into medical education to foster empathy, professionalism, and ethical conduct among healthcare providers. The author highlights that while medical training often emphasizes technical competencies, there is a growing concern about declining empathy in the medical profession. Jones argues that engaging with literature, music, visual art, and other disciplines can enhance doctors' ability to connect empathetically with patients, develop cultural sensitivity, and reflect on their own values. He critiques the empirical approach of measuring outcomes from such interventions and suggests that the value of arts and humanities extends beyond quantifiable metrics to fostering a deeper understanding of human experience and ethical dilemmas.
