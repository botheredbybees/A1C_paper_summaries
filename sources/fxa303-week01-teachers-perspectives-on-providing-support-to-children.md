## Teachers’ Perspectives on Providing Support to Children After Trauma: A Qualitative Study

This qualitative study explores elementary school teachers' perspectives on supporting children who have experienced trauma. The research involved semistructured interviews with a purposively varied sample of 21 teachers aged between 22 and 55 years, representing diverse teaching backgrounds and experiences. Despite some confidence in their role, the predominant themes highlighted uncertainties about optimal support strategies, struggles to balance conflicting needs of traumatized children and their classmates, desires for enhanced knowledge and skills, and challenges related to emotional burdens. These findings suggest a need for further research into how teachers can effectively assist traumatized students and call for tailored advice and training from school psychologists.
