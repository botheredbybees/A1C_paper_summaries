## Process Evaluations and Research Implementation

This chapter discusses the importance of evaluating processes within healthcare interventions beyond mere outcome research. It covers different levels of process evaluation ranging from basic descriptions to detailed ethnographic studies that provide comprehensive insights into how an intervention operates within its context. The text emphasizes methods such as interviews, focus groups, and surveys for conducting thorough evaluations. Additionally, it outlines various ways a research study can run smoothly or encounter issues impacting data collection and validity. Finally, it provides 5-point scales to assess the outcomes of health and social research studies based on their depth and breadth.
