## The Anatomy of the Brain

This lesson provides an overview of the brain's anatomy focusing on its three main regions: cerebrum, brainstem, and cerebellum. It details the structure of each region with particular emphasis on the cerebrum’s role in higher functions like thought and reasoning, divided into two hemispheres connected by the corpus callosum. The lesson explains directional terms for locating areas within the brain such as anterior/posterior, rostral/caudal, superior/inferior, and dorsal/ventral. It also outlines the four lobes of each hemisphere: frontal (thinking, movement), parietal (language understanding, sensory processing), occipital (visual cortex function), and temporal (sound processing, emotion, memory). Additionally, it introduces key regions within these areas like Broca's area for speech production and the limbic system’s role in emotions and long-term memory. The motor and sensory regions responsible for movement and sensation are also highlighted.
