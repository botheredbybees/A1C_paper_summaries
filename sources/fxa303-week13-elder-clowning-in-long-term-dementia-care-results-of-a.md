## Elder-clowning in long-term dementia care: Results of a pilot study

The study evaluates the impact of elder-clowning on nursing home residents with dementia, focusing on behavioral and psychological symptoms (BPSD) primarily associated with Alzheimer’s disease. The intervention involved pairs of trained clowns visiting residents twice weekly for approximately ten minutes over twelve weeks, utilizing humor, improvisation, empathy, and expressive arts to engage the participants. Key findings revealed significant reductions in BPSD scores as measured by the Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Nursing Home version (NPI-NH) and improvements in quality of life assessed through Dementia Care Mapping (DCM). The study suggests elder-clowning can be a promising intervention, reducing BPSD symptoms without relying on psychotropic medications. This approach aligns with person-centered care principles and has potential implications for enhancing the wellbeing of dementia patients within creative arts contexts.
