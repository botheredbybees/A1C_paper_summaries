## Welcome to Week 4 - FXA100 The Arts and Dementia Care

This document introduces the fourth week of FXA100 The Arts and Dementia Care, focusing on participatory music activities in dementia care settings. It discusses various forms of musical interventions that range from passive listening to active participation such as singing and playing instruments. Music therapists play a crucial role by employing high-level performance skills combined with psychological knowledge to deliver tailored treatments. While advanced stages of dementia may limit participation due to cognitive constraints, engaging in music activities still offers significant benefits like emotional activation, temporary speech recovery, and overall quality-of-life improvement. The document emphasizes the importance of professional registration for music therapists through accredited courses provided by the Australian Music Therapy Association. Additionally, it highlights ongoing research into carer interventions and their impact on reducing depression and enhancing well-being among caregivers.
