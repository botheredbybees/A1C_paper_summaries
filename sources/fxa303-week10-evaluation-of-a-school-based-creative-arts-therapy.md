## Evaluation of a school-based creative arts therapy programme for adolescents from refugee backgrounds

This study evaluates the effectiveness of a school-based creative arts therapy program for adolescents from refugee backgrounds, utilizing a controlled trial format with an intervention group and control group. The research found significant reductions in behavioral difficulties and emotional symptoms among participants who underwent the therapy compared to those who did not receive it. The evaluation utilized standardized measures such as the Hopkins Symptoms Checklist-25 (HSCL-25) and Strengths and Difficulties Questionnaire (SDQ). This study provides empirical support for creative arts therapies as an effective approach in addressing mental health issues among refugee youth within a school setting, highlighting its potential benefits over traditional talking therapies due to higher levels of cognitive development required by the latter. The findings suggest that such programs can be crucial components of comprehensive care strategies designed for refugee children who have experienced various forms of adversity.
