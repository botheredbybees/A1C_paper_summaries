## Art and Alzheimer-Type Dementia: A Longitudinal Study

The study explores the impact of art activities on individuals with Alzheimer-type dementia over a three-year period at a long-term care facility in Ohio. Participants were assessed for compositional complexity and asymmetry in their artwork created biweekly during this time. The findings revealed that, while asymmetry did not significantly change with practice, pictorial complexity decreased over the sessions, indicating cognitive decline. This research highlights the potential of art activities to provide meaningful engagement for dementia patients and suggests that changes in artistic output could serve as an indicator of cognitive fluctuations.
