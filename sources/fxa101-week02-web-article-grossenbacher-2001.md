## Mechanisms of Synesthesia: Cognitive and Physiological Constraints

This article discusses the nature of synesthesia, a condition where individuals experience sensory attributes that are not typically experienced by others in similar conditions. The authors present recent findings from cognitive psychology and neuroimaging studies to explain the neural mechanisms underlying synesthesia within a hierarchical cortical sensory pathway framework. They propose a neurobiological theory that integrates these cognitive and physiological insights into a broader understanding of synesthetic experiences.
