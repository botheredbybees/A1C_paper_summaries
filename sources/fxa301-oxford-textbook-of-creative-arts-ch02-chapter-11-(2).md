## Ethical Issues in Arts-Based Health Research

This chapter discusses the ethical challenges associated with arts-based health research (ABHR), a method increasingly used to understand and represent experiences of health and illness through various artistic forms such as theatre, photography, and visual art. The authors emphasize that while ABHR offers significant benefits like enhanced participant engagement and improved dissemination of findings, it also introduces unique ethical issues related to the roles of participants, artists, and researchers. Key challenges include managing confidentiality, ensuring meaningful involvement of participants in co-creation processes, and balancing artistic expression with ethical responsibilities. There is a call for developing specific ethical guidelines tailored to ABHR due to its interdisciplinary nature and innovative methodologies.
