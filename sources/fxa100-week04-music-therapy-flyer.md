## Music Therapy Flyer

The document provides an overview of music therapy, describing it as a research-based allied health profession that utilizes music to support individuals in improving their physical and mental health and enhancing their quality of life. The flyer highlights the benefits of music therapy across various age groups and conditions, including anxiety, ADHD, autism, dementia, cancer, and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). It emphasizes that participation does not require musical ability and stresses the importance of working with a Registered Music Therapist (RMT) who is trained in understanding how music can affect behavior, thoughts, feelings, and actions. The document also outlines specific areas where music therapy can help, such as reducing anxiety, improving communication skills, and aiding physical functions like motor control and balance. Additionally, it details the settings where music therapy is practiced, including hospitals, schools, aged care homes, and community health programs, and distinguishes music therapy from music lessons and entertainment by focusing on non-musical goals.
