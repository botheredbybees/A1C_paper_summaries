## Social Dancing to Reduce Falls in Older Adults — A Cluster Randomised Controlled Trial

This study investigates the impact of social dancing on fall rates among older adults in retirement villages around Sydney, Australia. The cluster randomized controlled trial involved 530 seniors aged an average of 78 years who participated in either a social dance program (folk or ballroom) twice weekly for one hour over 12 months, or were advised to continue with their usual activities as control participants. Despite the interventions aimed at improving balance and gait risk factors associated with falls, there was no significant difference in fall rates between the intervention and control groups. Sub-group analysis indicated higher fall rates among those with a history of multiple falls and folk dance participants compared to ballroom dancers or controls. The study concludes that social dancing alone may not prevent falls but suggests further investigation into modified programs tailored to low and high-risk individuals.
