## What are the Required Elements Needed to Create an Effective Visual Art Intervention for People with Dementia? A Qualitative Exploration

This paper explores the essential components necessary for an effective visual art intervention aimed at individuals with dementia, based on a qualitative study involving interviews with 22 participants including people with dementia, their family members, and visual arts professionals. The research identified five critical factors: benefits of group work, skilled facilitation, participant choice, artistic ability, and activity content. It highlights the importance of supportive facilitators who possess core values such as empathy and compassion and have knowledge in both artistic practice and dementia care. The study emphasizes the value of a positive group dynamic, enjoyable activities that prevent feelings of failure, and a focus on the creative process. These findings contribute to evidence-based practices for implementing visual art interventions and improving the quality of life for people with dementia.
