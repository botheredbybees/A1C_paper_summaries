## Limbic Systems for Emotion and for Memory, but No Single Limbic System

Edmund T. Rolls challenges the traditional concept of a single limbic system in the brain and proposes that there are distinct neural systems for emotion and memory, each involving different sets of structures but not an overarching 'limbic system'. The 'emotion limbic system' includes areas such as the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) and amygdala, which process reward valuation and decision-making. In contrast, the 'memory limbic system' comprises structures like the hippocampus and its connections to posterior cingulate cortex and thalamus, essential for episodic or event memory formation. Rolls argues that while these systems operate independently, they are interconnected when emotional content needs to be integrated into memory. The paper emphasizes that understanding these distinct neural networks is crucial not only in neuroscience but also in related fields like neurology and psychiatry.
