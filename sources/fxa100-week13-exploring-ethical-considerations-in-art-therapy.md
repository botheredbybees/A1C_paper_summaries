## Exploring Ethical Considerations in Art Therapy

This study examines the ethical considerations in art therapy, emphasizing the importance of confidentiality and informed consent to build trust between therapists and clients. It outlines fundamental ethical concepts like dual relationships and cultural competency and discusses theoretical foundations such as utilitarianism and deontology. The author highlights that ethical awareness improves therapeutic outcomes by fostering a supportive environment where clients feel safe to express themselves through art. The paper underscores the necessity for therapists to practice cultural competence and navigate complex ethical dilemmas to ensure client welfare.
