## De Botton – Art is Therapy at the Rijksmuseum

In this transcript of Alain de Botton's talk at the Rijksmuseum, he discusses the role of museums in contemporary society and their historical connection to religious institutions. De Botton posits that the rise of museums coincided with secularization and argues that art can fulfill many of the functions traditionally associated with religion, such as offering guidance, moral direction, and consolation. He critiques the modern perception of art as an enigmatic private experience devoid of didactic purpose, advocating instead for a return to the idea of art as a therapeutic tool for emotional maturity. De Botton highlights how the Rijksmuseum's exhibition 'Art is Therapy' exemplifies this vision by showcasing artworks that were originally intended to be instructive and morally uplifting. The discussion underscores the potential for museums to serve as spaces where visitors can engage with art in a more profound, emotionally resonant way.
