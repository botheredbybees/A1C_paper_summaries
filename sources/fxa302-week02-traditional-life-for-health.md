## Traditional Life for Health

Sandra Eades explores the health and lifestyle of Noongar people in Western Australia before and after colonization. She highlights how Noongar ancestors were lean, muscular, and fit due to their active lifestyles involving hunting, gathering, and living on their traditional lands according to seasonal cycles. This cyclical life was governed by six seasons that dictated food availability, activities, and social gatherings. Eades describes the traditional practices such as firestick farming in Birak (December-January), fishing along the coast during Bunuru (February-March), mushroom foraging in Djeren (April-May), and hunting kangaroos in Makaru (June-July). These activities provided a balanced diet rich in fresh foods, ensured regular physical activity, and fostered strong community bonds. Eades emphasizes the importance of reconnecting with traditional knowledge to address contemporary health issues like obesity among Aboriginal communities.
