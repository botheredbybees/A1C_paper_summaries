## Reflection on the Therapeutic Benefits of Photography: A Personal Photo Essay Project

Louise Raftery reflects on her experience using photography as a means to promote self-growth and healing. Initially inspired by the potential of photography for processing grief, particularly after losing her beloved dog Charlie, she found it too painful to focus on his absence through images. Instead, she decided to create a photo essay centered around the mindfulness practice of 'noticing.' This involves paying attention intentionally to the present moment, which research suggests can contribute positively to psychological well-being. Raftery's project aimed to explore how this mindful photography could be therapeutic during times of sadness or grief. She incorporated various photographic techniques such as composition rules (e.g., rule of thirds), depth of field manipulation, and point of view adjustments to enhance the narrative and thematic elements of her photo essay. Her reflection highlights that the process helped organize her thoughts, provided a sense of purpose, and encouraged critical self-reflection on her emotional state. The project facilitated a shift in perspective towards seeing the world more positively. Raftery is optimistic about using therapeutic photography activities with clients in professional settings while being mindful of the appropriateness and potential adverse responses during different stages of their healing journeys.
