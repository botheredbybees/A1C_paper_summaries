## Overview of Dance Therapy by Donna Newman Bluestein

This transcript from a video produced by the American Dance Therapy Association presents an overview of dance therapy provided by Donna Newman Bluestein from Lesley University. The discussion covers the psychotherapeutic application of movement to address cognitive, spiritual, emotional, and neurological issues based on Reena Cornboom's philosophy that it integrates the whole person. It highlights practical applications like training students in therapeutic techniques used in violence prevention curricula, focusing on developing anti-bullying skills and anger management through dance movements. The Hancock Center for Dance Movement Therapy employs various methods such as individual sessions and group activities with props to cater to diverse populations including women, children, families, sexual abuse victims, and individuals with developmental disabilities.
