## Research ethics

The chapter 'Research ethics' by Daisy Fancourt discusses the importance of ethical considerations in arts in health interventions and research. Contrary to the perception that such interventions are harmless, Fancourt argues that they can have unforeseen negative consequences, emphasizing the need for thorough ethical scrutiny. The text highlights several examples of well-intentioned public health initiatives that led to harmful outcomes, underscoring the necessity of robust ethical frameworks in arts-related health projects. Key areas covered include gaining informed consent from participants, including vulnerable populations such as children and individuals without capacity to give consent. Fancourt also outlines general ethics of conduct, stressing principles like respect, competence, responsibility, and integrity for those involved in arts in health interventions. These guidelines are crucial not only at the initial stages but throughout the entire lifecycle of an intervention or research project.
