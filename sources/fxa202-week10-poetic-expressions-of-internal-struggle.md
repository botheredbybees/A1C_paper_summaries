## Poetic Expressions of Internal Struggle

The document presents a series of poems that explore the internal struggles and emotional turmoil experienced by individuals grappling with various forms of distress and adversity. Each poem is dedicated to a deity or spirit, such as Oizys (Spirit of Misery), Melinoë (Goddess of Madness, Ghosts, and the Past), Eris (Goddess of Strife and Discord), Deimos (Spirit of Terror), Penthos (God of Grief), Epiales (Spirit of Nightmares), and Achilles (The Wounded Hero). The poems delve into themes of unhealed scars, body dysmorphia, overwhelming rage, fear, grief, and the haunting presence of past traumas. They also highlight the resilience and continued existence of the narrator amidst these struggles, as seen in the final poem where the speaker acknowledges their survival despite the violent history surrounding them. These poetic expressions provide a profound insight into the psychological and emotional landscape that individuals navigate when dealing with significant trauma or mental health issues.
