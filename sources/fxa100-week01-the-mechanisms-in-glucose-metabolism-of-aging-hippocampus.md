## The mechanisms in glucose metabolism of aging hippocampus

This review article investigates the mechanisms of glucose metabolism in the aging hippocampus, focusing on its significance for cognitive decline and neurodegeneration. The authors discuss how disruptions in glucose metabolism serve as a primary mechanism leading to neuronal damage during aging. They highlight the importance of understanding these pathophysiological changes for early intervention strategies aimed at mitigating memory and learning impairments associated with energy metabolism decline. Key molecules like ALG5 and STT3A, involved in N-glycosylation pathways, are shown to influence glucose transport and uptake in hippocampal neurons. The study underscores the potential of targeting these metabolic processes to prevent or slow down neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.
