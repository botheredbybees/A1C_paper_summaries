## The Effectiveness of Art Therapy in the Treatment of Traumatized Adults: A Systematic Review on Art Therapy and Trauma

The article presents a systematic review of the effectiveness of art therapy in treating traumatized adults, focusing on empirical evidence from controlled comparative studies. Six studies were identified that demonstrated significant decreases in psychological trauma symptoms and depression among treated groups. Despite limitations such as small sample sizes and methodological quality issues, these results contribute to understanding the efficacy of art therapy for PTSD and other trauma-related conditions. The review highlights the potential of art therapy as an alternative treatment for patients who do not benefit from conventional treatments like EMDR or TF-CBT due to poor verbal memory or prolonged traumatic exposure. Art therapy is noted for its ability to facilitate non-verbal expression, which aligns with the nature of traumatic memories often described as wordless and non-verbal.
