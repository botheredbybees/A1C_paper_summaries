## “Yesterday I Couldn’t See. Tomorrow’s Sun Shines Now”: Sharing Migrant Stories Through Creative Arts to Foster Community Connections and Wellbeing

This article explores the experiences of refugee and migrant women in non-metropolitan areas of Australia who engaged in creative arts as part of the Stories to Freedom public exhibitions. The research highlights how these activities positively impacted the participants' wellbeing, fostering a sense of happiness, confidence, and pride. Visitors to the exhibitions were inspired by the artworks, contributing to the women's feelings of welcome and belonging within their communities. By sharing stories through creative practices, the project reshapes negative narratives about newcomers and enhances social inclusion. The study includes case studies from three different geographic locations in Australia, showcasing the transformative power of community-engaged research initiatives that use arts-based methods to promote integration and resilience among migrant populations.
