## Engaging in Community Music: An Introduction

This introductory text explores the growing field of community music, emphasizing its inclusive and participatory nature as a form of non-formal education. The authors argue that community music, as an active intervention between facilitators and participants, aims to foster social transformation through musical engagement. Key characteristics include people-centered approaches, context-specific practices, active participation, inclusion, and diversity. The book also highlights the expansion of research, publications, and organizations dedicated to community music globally, reflecting its interdisciplinary applications in health, well-being, political activism, and peace-making contexts. This text serves as a resource for educators, students, practitioners, and current professionals working in community music programs.
