## Music in the brain

The article by Vuust et al. (2022) reviews the cognitive neuroscience literature on music perception, focusing on how melody, harmony, and rhythm are processed in the brain. The authors introduce the Predictive Coding of Music (PCM) model which posits that music perception is based on a predictive framework wherein listeners continuously generate expectations about upcoming musical events. This process involves engagement with auditory networks as well as motor and emotional circuits. The PCM model explains how errors or deviations from expected patterns, such as syncopated rhythms, can elicit an urge to engage in compensatory actions like tapping the foot. Over time, these predictions are refined through learning processes that update internal models. The review underscores music’s unique ability to reveal principles of brain prediction and its relevance for understanding emotional responses and creativity in musical contexts.
