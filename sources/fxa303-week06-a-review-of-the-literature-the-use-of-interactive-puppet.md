## A Review of the Literature: The Use of Interactive Puppet Simulation in Nursing Education and Children's Healthcare

This review explores the use of puppets in health care and nursing education, focusing on their role as a tool for play therapy and simulation. The authors conducted an electronic search between 1980 and January 2015 across databases like CINAHL, PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science, and PsycINFO, identifying twelve articles that highlight the use of puppet simulation in nursing education and pediatric healthcare environments. Puppetry is found to be effective for health education, particularly in diabetes management, where it helps children express their feelings and communicate with healthcare professionals more effectively. The review identifies gaps in research on using puppets as a three-way communication tool in nursing and nurse education, emphasizing the need for further investigation into its application and benefits.
