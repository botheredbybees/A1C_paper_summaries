## Firstdraft exhibition brings artists together to better understand intellectual disability

The article discusses the Paired exhibition at Firstdraft gallery in Sydney, which showcases collaborative artworks between artists with intellectual and/or developmental disabilities and neuro-normative artists. Curated by Harriet Body, the exhibition aims to foster understanding and equality through artistic collaboration. The central argument of the piece is that such collaborations provide a platform for mutual learning and creativity, transcending typical power dynamics often present in relationships involving individuals with disabilities. Key findings include how these partnerships can lead to new forms of artistic expression and deepen personal connections between collaborators. This exhibition highlights the importance of inclusive practices in the arts sector and its potential to enrich creative output while fostering social inclusion.
