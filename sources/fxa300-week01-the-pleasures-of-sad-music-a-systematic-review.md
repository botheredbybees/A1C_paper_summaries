## The pleasures of sad music: a systematic review

This systematic review explores the phenomenon of experiencing pleasure from listening to sad music, despite sadness being generally considered an unpleasant emotion in everyday life. The authors argue that sad music can evoke a range of positive emotions and psychological benefits such as mood regulation, empathy, and cathartic release. They propose that the aesthetic experience of sadness in music is non-threatening, aesthetically pleasing, and psychologically beneficial. This framework offers insights into how stimuli typically associated with negative affect can induce positive emotional states. The review also synthesizes philosophical perspectives on the 'tragedy paradox'—the idea that people seek out negative emotions through art despite trying to avoid them in real life—and discusses neuroscientific research supporting these observations. The study suggests potential applications for music therapy and mental health interventions, particularly in treating disorders where experiencing pleasure is diminished.
