## Worldwide Prevalence and Prevention Strategies for Dementia

This chapter discusses the prevalence of dementia and Alzheimer's disease globally and emphasizes the importance of preventive measures, especially in less-developed countries. It highlights key studies such as the Cognitive Function and Ageing Study (CFAS), which is a longitudinal population-based study focusing on cognitive decline and dementia among elderly populations in the UK. Additionally, it mentions the work of international organizations like Alzheimer’s Disease International and the World Health Organization to address the growing issue of dementia. The text also reviews several epidemiological studies that investigate risk factors for dementia, including genetic predispositions and lifestyle behaviors such as alcohol consumption and diet.
