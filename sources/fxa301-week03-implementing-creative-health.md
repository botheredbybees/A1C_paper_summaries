## Implementing Creative Health

The document discusses the growing recognition of creative health's potential to improve individual and community well-being, particularly in addressing health inequalities. It highlights international interest in leveraging creativity and culture to support health and social care initiatives. The World Health Organization (WHO) has recognized the arts' role in tackling complex health challenges, contributing to the Sustainable Development Goals. Furthermore, the document explores cost-effectiveness analyses demonstrating that creative health interventions can lead to significant economic benefits by reducing healthcare usage and improving quality of life. It emphasizes the need for leadership across all levels of government and health systems to ensure equitable access to these initiatives. Economic evaluations show that arts-based social prescribing programs often yield substantial returns on investment, suggesting they are cost-effective alternatives to traditional medical treatments. The document underscores the importance of articulating creative health's value not only in terms of direct health benefits but also in fostering economic growth through its impact on the cultural industries and local communities.
