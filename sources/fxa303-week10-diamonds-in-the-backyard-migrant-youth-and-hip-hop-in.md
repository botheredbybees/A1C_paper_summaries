## Diamonds in the Backyard: Migrant Youth and Hip Hop in Australian Regional Towns

This chapter explores the role of hip hop among migrant youth in Australia's regional towns, an area largely overlooked by academic research despite growing migrant populations since the early 1990s. The study focuses on New South Wales and Queensland, highlighting how young migrants from diverse backgrounds use hip hop as a form of cultural expression and identity negotiation within their new communities. Despite facing challenges such as social isolation and racial discrimination, these youth leverage hip hop to establish themselves in regional Australian landscapes, contributing to the localization of international hip-hop culture. The research underscores the importance of understanding migrant experiences through creative arts, particularly how they navigate belonging and self-expression in culturally fragmented environments.
