## Social Determinants of Health

This chapter discusses the social determinants influencing health outcomes across different socioeconomic statuses. It highlights reports from the Commission on Social Determinants of Health and The Marmot Review, which emphasize the unequal distribution of resources leading to disparities in mental and physical health outcomes. These studies propose interventions such as improving living conditions, redistributing power and resources, promoting equity through taxes and social protection programs, and implementing universal but proportionate actions based on one’s position on the social gradient. The chapter also outlines a life course approach, advocating for early intervention to prevent disadvantage from accumulating throughout life.
