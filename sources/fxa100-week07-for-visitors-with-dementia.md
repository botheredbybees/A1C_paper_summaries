## For visitors with dementia

The document outlines the MCA Australia's 'Artful' program designed for individuals living with dementia and their support partners. The primary aim of this initiative is to enhance life-enriching experiences through contemporary art, fostering a therapeutic environment that promotes relaxation and engagement. Participants are offered six-week programs that include gallery visits, facilitated by trained artist educators who guide them in interacting with artworks and creating their own art pieces. These sessions culminate in community days where participants' work is exhibited alongside music and refreshments for all to enjoy.

The 'Artful Seasonal Programs' provide a comprehensive experience including full museum access, morning tea, hands-on workshops, and resources for continued engagement at home. Additionally, the program offers online creative care sessions via Zoom, tailored specifically for carers and their support networks. These virtual sessions include mindful moments with contemporary art, simple craft activities using household items, and opportunities to connect socially.

The document also mentions 'Art Yarns,' a free interactive program designed exclusively for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander older and Elder people, alongside their families and caregivers. This initiative aims to foster cultural safety and inclusivity by engaging participants in hands-on creative activities led by Muruwari woman Dr Virginia Keft.

Overall, the document underscores the significant role that contemporary art can play in enhancing quality of life for individuals living with dementia, emphasizing the value of artistic engagement as a tool for mental and emotional well-being.
