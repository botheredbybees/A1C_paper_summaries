## An education in music makes you a better employee. Are recruiters in tune?

This article discusses the transferable skills developed through music education and their relevance in non-music professional settings. The author, Diana Tolmie, conducted a nationwide survey involving 165 participants who were musically educated but not necessarily pursuing music careers. Preliminary findings indicate that musicians possess numerous workplace skills such as professionalism, autonomy, resilience, creativity, teamwork, communication, critical thinking, and leadership. These traits are attributed to the rigorous training required in music education, fostering disciplined attention and intrinsic motivation. Additionally, ensemble work enhances team dynamics, while experiences in live performance improve adaptability under pressure. The article concludes by emphasizing the need for employers and policymakers to recognize the value of musicians' skills across various fields.
