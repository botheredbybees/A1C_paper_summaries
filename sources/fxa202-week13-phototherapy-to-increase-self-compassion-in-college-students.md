## Phototherapy to Increase Self-Compassion in College Students

This study examines the effect of phototherapy on increasing self-compassion among college students at a university in Indonesia. The researchers used a quasi-experimental design with non-probability sampling, selecting 64 participants divided into experimental and control groups. The findings indicate that phototherapy training positively influences self-compassion levels in the experimental group compared to the control group. Key outcomes include an increase in self-understanding and emotional well-being among students experiencing depression or stress. Phototherapy is described as a novel form of art therapy that can aid mental health by helping individuals process complex emotions through imagery, fostering self-appreciation and acceptance. This study highlights the potential for phototherapy to serve as an effective intervention for college students struggling with psychological challenges.
