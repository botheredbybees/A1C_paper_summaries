## Music Therapy Promotes Mental Health in Schools

This fact sheet discusses the role of music therapy as an evidence-based allied health profession that supports students and young people in improving their mental health. Music therapy employs recovery-oriented approaches to foster positive outcomes and care experiences efficiently, serving as a powerful tool for prevention and early intervention against mental ill-health. It enables emotional expression through music for those who struggle with traditional talking therapies. Registered Music Therapists (RMTs) assist students by building emotional literacy, fostering relationships, enhancing motivation, developing coping strategies, exploring identity, and regulating emotions. The document also highlights the importance of RMTs in addressing situations where verbal communication is insufficient or challenging, and provides guidance on how to refer to an RMT when necessary. Music therapy programs are designed based on students' interests and strengths, offering tailored mental health services that can be delivered face-to-face or via telehealth. This service aims to improve educational engagement and outcomes by making a significant difference in the lives of young people.
