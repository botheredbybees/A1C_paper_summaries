## Stuck song syndrome: musical obsessions — when to look for OCD

The article discusses earworms or stuck song syndrome (SSS), a common phenomenon where catchy tunes involuntarily pop up in one's mind, potentially causing distress and disrupting daily functioning. It highlights that while SSS is generally benign for most people, it can be more pronounced and debilitating for individuals with obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). The authors explore the psychological mechanisms behind SSS, noting that earworms are related to memory and function as strong mnemonics, creating a cognitive itch that is exacerbated by attempts at suppression. They also differentiate SSS from auditory hallucinations and other neurological conditions. For patients with OCD, intrusive thoughts about unwanted songs can lead to severe distress and exacerbate symptoms such as mysophobia or checking behaviors. The article provides guidance for general practitioners on how to assess the severity of SSS and determine whether it indicates a need for psychiatric referral. Various treatment options are discussed, including self-help strategies like engagement and distraction, cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), exposure and response prevention (ERP), mindfulness techniques, and medication for OCD. These approaches aim to improve coping mechanisms rather than eradicate earworms entirely.
