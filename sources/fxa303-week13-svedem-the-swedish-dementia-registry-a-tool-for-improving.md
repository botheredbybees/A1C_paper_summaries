## SveDem, the Swedish Dementia Registry – A Tool for Improving the Quality of Diagnostics, Treatment and Care of Dementia Patients in Clinical Practice

The Swedish Dementia Registry (SveDem) is an internet-based quality registry aimed at improving the diagnostic process, treatment, and care for patients with dementia disorders in Sweden. Initiated in May 2007, SveDem collects detailed information on patient diagnosis, medical treatments, community support, and follow-up data annually from specialist units, primary care centers, and nursing homes across Sweden. The registry includes demographic data, cognitive evaluations using the Mini Mental State Examination (MMSE) score, diagnostic workup details, pharmacological and non-pharmacological treatment types, as well as support received from various levels of government services. Key findings indicate that SveDem covers nearly all regions in Sweden and provides comprehensive longitudinal data on dementia care quality, facilitating comparisons between different healthcare units at regional and national scales. The registry serves not only to improve the current state of dementia care but also aids in generating new research hypotheses and developing guidelines for improved care strategies.
