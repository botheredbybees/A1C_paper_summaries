## Puppets and Self-Care for Task 2

This document provides an overview of various types of puppets including glove, rod, marionettes, and shadow puppets. It discusses the characteristics, construction methods, and cultural significance of each type. Glove puppets are described as simple yet versatile, often used in therapeutic settings due to their ease of use and effectiveness in puppetry training. Rod puppets, which are operated from underneath with rods controlling movements, are highlighted for their versatility in shadow theatre performances. Shadow puppets feature intricate carving techniques and are prevalent in Asian cultures for storytelling through complex narratives. Marionettes, controlled by strings attached above the puppet, showcase a sophisticated form of puppetry requiring extensive training. The document also emphasizes the importance of self-care for students engaging with potentially traumatic content during their research process.
