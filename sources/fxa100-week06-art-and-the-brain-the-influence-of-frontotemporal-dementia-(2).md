## Art and the brain: The influence of frontotemporal dementia on an accomplished artist

This study explores the impact of frontotemporal dementia (FTD) on an accomplished visual artist's creative output. The artist developed progressive aphasia and FTD but experienced a significant transformation in her painting style as her cognitive abilities declined. Initially trained in both Western watercolor techniques and traditional Chinese brush painting, she began to experiment with abstract styles by incorporating Eastern cultural motifs into her work. As language deficits worsened, her paintings became freer and more emotionally expressive, characterized by bold colors and less realism. The research suggests that while linguistic skills deteriorated, visual creativity may have been enhanced or liberated from the constraints of formal training due to FTD. This case highlights the potential for artistic development in individuals with FTD and underscores the complex interplay between cognitive decline and creative expression.
