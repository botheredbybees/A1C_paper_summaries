## Activities for Week 2 - FXA100 The Arts and Dementia Care

This document outlines the activities and required readings for Week 2 of the FXA100 The Arts and Dementia Care course at the University of Tasmania. Key among these is Oliver Sacks' article, 'The Power of Music,' published in Brain (2006), which explores the profound impact music can have on neurological conditions such as dementia. The article details how musical engagement can evoke vivid memories and emotional responses, even when other cognitive functions are impaired. This week's lecture will further elaborate on these themes and introduce students to assessment tasks and library resources for finding scholarly sources relevant to their studies.

The document also includes a discussion prompt that encourages reflection on personal musical experiences from teenage years and the memories they evoke. It highlights how music can serve as a powerful tool in reminiscence therapy, aiding individuals with dementia in accessing past life events through familiar melodies and rhythms. The relevance of such therapeutic approaches to creative arts and health is underscored by their potential to enhance cognitive function, emotional well-being, and quality of life for those affected by neurological disorders.
