## The Impact of Cultural Activities on Health and Survival

This chapter discusses the influence of cultural activities on health and longevity based on a variety of studies including Bygren et al.'s (1996) Swedish interview survey. The research shows that engaging in cultural events, reading, and musical activities positively correlates with survival rates. Additional studies such as Clift’s work (2012) delve into creative arts contributing to public health. Other contributions like Chan et al.’s (1998) study on music training improving verbal memory indicate the broader impact of arts engagement on cognitive abilities. The chapter also includes findings from international collaborations, highlighting cultural participation's effects on wellbeing and mental health.
