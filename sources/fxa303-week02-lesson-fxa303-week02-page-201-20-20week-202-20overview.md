## Week 3 Overview: Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing

This week's course material provides an overview of the developmental processes occurring in adolescents' brains during their second decade of life. It discusses how environmental and genetic factors interact to shape brain development, emphasizing that adolescence presents a unique window for intervention due to ongoing brain maturation. The lesson highlights potential risks associated with premature engagement in adult activities despite incomplete brain development, which can lead to less optimal decision-making. Additionally, it outlines the importance of multi-dimensional learning experiences, including the creative arts, in fostering mental well-being and emotional regulation skills necessary for navigating adulthood.
