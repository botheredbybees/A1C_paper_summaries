## Dance Movement Therapy and Dementia Care

This lesson explores the use of dance movement therapy (DMT) in dementia care. It highlights how non-verbal communication through gestures can be misunderstood as 'behaviors' by caregivers but actually represent attempts to communicate. The effectiveness of interactive and improvised DMT is emphasized, with a focus on building relationships between people with dementia and their carers/family members while also developing new neural pathways. Key tools like the Octaband and Laban Movement Analysis are discussed, alongside various approaches such as Chacian DMT and Authentic Movement. Research indicates that regular dance participation can reduce dementia risk by 76%, and ongoing studies suggest that DMT can improve cognitive function and quality of life for those with existing diagnoses. Programs around the world demonstrate increased engagement in dance sessions due to community interaction, emphasizing fall prevention through balance and muscle strength improvement.
