## Nature's Cycle of Life and Death: A Reflection on Beauty in Decomposition

The text explores the author's contemplation on nature's cycles of life and death, particularly focusing on the intrinsic beauty found in the process of decomposition. Through vivid imagery and personal anecdotes, Wendy Whiley reflects on how observing these natural phenomena fosters a deeper connection to the environment and serves as a reminder of mortality ('Memento Mori'). She highlights that while the inevitability of death may seem daunting, it is an integral part of the ecological system and can lead to new forms of life. For instance, a dead ringtail possum's body becomes shelter for spiders and food for insects, indicating that even in death, its existence continues to contribute positively. Similarly, the bleached kangaroo skull standing out against the earthy landscape is an example of nature's ability to create striking compositions through the natural cycles of life and death. The author emphasizes that these observations restore her sense of wonder and appreciation for the interconnectedness of all living things.
