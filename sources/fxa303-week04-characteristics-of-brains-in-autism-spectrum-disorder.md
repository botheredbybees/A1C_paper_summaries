## Characteristics of Brains in Autism Spectrum Disorder: Structure, Function and Connectivity across the Lifespan

This paper reviews recent neuroimaging studies to characterize the brains of individuals with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) across different stages of life. The authors highlight structural Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), functional MRI, and diffusion tensor imaging findings that reveal atypical brain development in ASD. Key insights include accelerated total brain volume growth in early childhood followed by arrested growth, regional gray matter abnormalities, and altered connectivity patterns compared to typically developing individuals. These neurobiological mechanisms are linked to impaired social communication and restricted repetitive behaviors characteristic of ASD.
