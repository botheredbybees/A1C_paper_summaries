## Ways of Seeing: Using the Visual Arts in Nursing Education

The article by Frei, Alvarez, and Alexander (2010) discusses the integration of visual arts into nursing education to enhance observational skills, communication abilities, narrative sequencing, and empathy among nursing students. The authors describe an innovative clinical experience where nursing students participated in a guided art observation program at the Art Institute of Chicago. This program aimed to improve their understanding of patient encounters by fostering keen visual acuity and reflective thinking. Drawing on existing literature that highlights the benefits of visual arts education for medical professionals, the study emphasizes the importance of direct engagement with original artworks rather than reproductions or slides. The curriculum integrates formal art analysis techniques to encourage precise language use and careful observation from multiple perspectives. Feedback from students indicated that this unique approach broadened their appreciation of patient interactions and reinforced clinical objectives such as collaborative communication and prioritization of nursing diagnoses.
