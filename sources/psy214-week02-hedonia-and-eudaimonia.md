## Hedonia and Eudaimonia

The document discusses the concepts of hedonic well-being and eudaimonic well-being, which are central to understanding stress management and self-care in the context of mindfulness. Hedonic well-being, derived from Greek 'hedone', emphasizes pleasure, gratification, positive emotions, and feeling good as indicators of happiness or subjective well-being. On the other hand, eudaimonic well-being is rooted in the realization of one's potential, functioning according to virtues, and striving to be the best self possible, characterized by flourishing and full functionality. The text also highlights that while there may be a positive relationship between hedonia and eudaimonia, there is substantial debate within research frameworks regarding their interplay and distinct contributions to well-being.
