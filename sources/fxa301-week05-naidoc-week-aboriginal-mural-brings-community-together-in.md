## NAIDOC Week: Aboriginal mural brings community together in Shortland

This article highlights the positive impact of a large mural painting project in Shortland, New South Wales, during NAIDOC Week. The mural, depicting local elements such as a fig tree and eagles, was organized by Indigenous elder Aunty Elsie Randall and involved students, staff, and volunteers from Margaret Jurd College. Aunty Elsie emphasized that the mural is not just her work but a community effort reflecting the cultural significance for Aboriginal people. Principal Melise Sutton noted an increase in respect and understanding among non-Indigenous students towards their Indigenous peers as a result of this initiative. The project demonstrates how art can be used to foster greater connection, belonging, and mutual appreciation between different cultural groups within schools.
