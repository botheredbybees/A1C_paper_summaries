## Playfully engaging people living with dementia: searching for Yum Cha moments

The article explores the use of relational clowning as a method to engage individuals living with mid- to late-stage dementia in residential care facilities. The research, conducted over three years and funded by an Australian Research Council grant in partnership with Wesley Mission Brisbane, employs an approach called 'relational clowning' that focuses on playful interactions designed to enhance social engagement and quality of life for people suffering from dementia. Through a pilot phase involving 17 residents, the study highlights how playful moments like teaching Cantonese numbers or recalling childhood memories can significantly improve mood and interaction levels among participants. The key findings indicate that reflexive application of play vocabularies tailored to individual interests and stages of dementia is effective in generating 'moments' of engagement and mutual recognition. This approach holds relevance for creative arts and health by demonstrating the potential of non-traditional interventions like applied theatre practices to positively influence the lives of those with cognitive impairments.
