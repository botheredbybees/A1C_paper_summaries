## Social Prescribing Roundtable, November 2019: Report

The document presents a comprehensive report on the findings and recommendations of a roundtable discussion organized by the Consumers Health Forum of Australia (CHF) and The Royal Australian College of General Practitioners (RACGP). Held in November 2019, this event focused on social prescribing as an innovative approach to integrate health and community services for better patient care outcomes. The report highlights that social prescribing is a means by which healthcare professionals can refer patients to non-clinical services addressing socioeconomic factors linked to poor health outcomes such as social isolation, unstable housing, multimorbidity, and mental health problems.

Participants from various sectors including consumer advocates, clinicians, academics, government experts, and policy makers discussed the potential benefits of social prescribing in Australia. The central argument is that a strategic and systematic approach to integrating social prescribing into primary care can address non-health issues effectively, thereby promoting wellness over illness.

Key findings include the recognition that 20% of GP consultations are primarily for social problems which are best addressed through community services rather than clinical interventions. Additionally, it was noted that while healthcare professionals in Germany and the UK frequently coordinate with social services, this is less common in Australia. The report recommends a nationwide approach to implementing social prescribing, emphasizing its capacity to reduce reliance on health services, improve consumer engagement and self-management, and foster stronger community connections.

This initiative is particularly relevant to creative arts and mental wellbeing as it underscores the importance of addressing socio-economic determinants that impact overall health outcomes, including mental health. Social prescribing could integrate creative art therapies and activities into care plans for individuals facing social isolation or mental health challenges.
