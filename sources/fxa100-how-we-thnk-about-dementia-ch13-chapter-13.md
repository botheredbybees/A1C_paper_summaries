## The Art and Practice of Memory and Forgetting

This chapter from 'How We Think About Dementia' by Julian C. Hughes and Ashley McCormick explores how art and science can collaborate to deepen understanding of dementia. The authors draw on philosophical insights, particularly from Wittgenstein, to discuss the role of context in shaping perceptions of memory and forgetting. They argue that art enables a broader view of individuals with dementia by highlighting the humanistic aspects often overlooked by scientific reductionism. The chapter also addresses the concept of Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI), questioning its medical labeling and potential ethical implications. It emphasizes the importance of social context in defining when forgetfulness becomes pathological.
