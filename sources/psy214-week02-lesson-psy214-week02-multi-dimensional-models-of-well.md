## PSY114 PSY214 Stress, Self-Care and Mindfulness: Early Humanistic Approaches

This lesson explores the foundational concepts of humanistic psychology as they relate to stress, self-care, and mindfulness. It begins with an overview of ancient Greek maxims emphasizing self-awareness and authenticity. Key figures in this field include Abraham Maslow, known for his hierarchy of needs and concept of self-actualization, and Carl Rogers, famous for person-centered psychotherapy and the idea of a fully-functioning person. The text provides details on characteristics associated with self-actualized individuals according to Maslow and fully-functioning persons as described by Rogers. It highlights themes such as openness to experience, existential living, trust in oneself, freedom, creativity, and a rich life experience. These ideas are linked to mindfulness practices and the positive psychology movement which seeks to build on humanistic psychology's focus but with more scientific rigor.
