## Using Puppetry in a Coordinated School Health Program

The article discusses the use of puppetry as an innovative methodology to promote positive health behaviors among children. Puppetry is highlighted for its ability to foster social, emotional, cognitive, and literacy development while engaging learners through play. Various types of puppets such as finger, hand, rod, string, or marionette are described along with their potential applications in different educational contexts within a coordinated school health program. The article emphasizes that puppetry can be utilized for counseling to help children express feelings and address issues like grief, behavioral disorders, suicide prevention, and sensitivity towards disabilities. It also suggests the use of puppets in teaching diverse health topics including multiculturalism, HIV/AIDS awareness, arthritis education, first aid concepts, dog bite prevention, and hygiene practices.
