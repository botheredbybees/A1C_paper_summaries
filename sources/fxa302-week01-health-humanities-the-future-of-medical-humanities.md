## Health humanities: the future of medical humanities?

The paper reviews the evolution of medical humanities as an academic discipline and its contribution to healthcare provision, arguing for a more inclusive and outward-facing approach termed 'health humanities.' The authors critique the limitations of traditional medical humanities and advocate for integrating insights from allied health professionals, nurses, patients, and carers. They propose that health humanities should embrace interdisciplinary research and engage with diverse perspectives to enhance clinical empathy, critical reflection on healthcare policies, and therapeutic interventions in mental health care. This expansion aims to foster a more holistic understanding of human suffering and the complexities of healthcare practice, moving beyond evidence-based medicine to include arts and humanities approaches.
