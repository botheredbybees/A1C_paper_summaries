## The value of music for public health

This chapter explores the role of music in public health by examining its potential to enhance individual physical, psychological, social well-being and overall quality of life. It highlights epidemiological evidence linking cultural participation with reduced mortality risk, suggesting that engaging in cultural activities can have positive health impacts. The author argues for a reevaluation of the traditional dichotomy between health and illness, advocating instead for an integrative approach that acknowledges music's therapeutic value regardless of one’s medical condition. Music is described as universally accessible and beneficial throughout life stages, with potential applications in preventive care and therapy. Challenges to measuring music's health effects include contextual factors and methodological limitations, but the chapter underscores the importance of developing theoretical models to understand these mechanisms better.
