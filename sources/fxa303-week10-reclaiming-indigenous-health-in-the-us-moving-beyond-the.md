## Reclaiming Indigenous Health in the US: Moving beyond the Social Determinants of Health

This paper examines the limitations of applying the World Health Organization’s (WHO) social determinants of health (SDH) framework in Indigenous communities within the United States, arguing that it does not fully capture or reflect Indigenous conceptions of health and well-being. The study critiques the SDH framework for its non-Indigenous biases and suggests a need to Indigenize this framework to better address the unique social determinants faced by US Indigenous nations. Participants from tribal communities and mainstream educational institutions convened to develop a conceptual schema that aligns with Indigenous epistemologies and knowledge systems, emphasizing sovereignty and comprehensive understanding of health within these contexts. The authors advocate for community-driven definitions of health and well-being in policy-making processes and highlight the importance of recognizing and respecting Indigenous determinants of health beyond conventional SDH parameters. This research is crucial for enhancing the relevance and effectiveness of public health policies targeting US Indigenous communities.
