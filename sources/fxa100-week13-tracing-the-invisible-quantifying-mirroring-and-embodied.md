## Tracing the invisible: Quantifying mirroring and embodied attunement in dyadic and triadic Dance Movement Therapy

This paper presents the first quantitative exploration of mirroring in Dance Movement Therapy (DMT), focusing on the dynamics between clients and therapists in both dyadic and triadic formats. Using optical motion capture and time-series modeling, researchers quantified movement coordination during a structured mirroring exercise where participants alternated roles as leaders and followers. Results revealed that dyadic interactions showed tight temporal alignment and significant synchrony, particularly for female client-therapist pairs, while male client-therapist dyads exhibited weaker coupling. In triadic settings, temporal entrainment was attenuated but maintained stable postural scaffolding under divided attention. These findings suggest that mirroring is a flexible family of coordination modes tailored to relational contexts and client expressivity, highlighting the need for training in adaptive strategies for therapists working with groups.
