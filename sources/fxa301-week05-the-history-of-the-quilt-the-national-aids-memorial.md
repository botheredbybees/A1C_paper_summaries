## The History of the Quilt: The National AIDS Memorial

This document outlines the history and significance of the AIDS Memorial Quilt, initiated by Cleve Jones in November 1985 as a tribute to those lost to AIDS. Initially conceived during an annual march honoring San Francisco Supervisor Harvey Milk and Mayor George Moscone, the Quilt memorialized names of friends and loved ones who had died from AIDS on placards. The first panel was created for Marvin Feldman by Cleve Jones in 1986. Formal organization began in 1987 with Mike Smith and Gert McMullin joining Jones to establish the NAMES Project Foundation. By October 1987, the Quilt made its debut on Washington D.C.'s National Mall with 1,920 panels. The project garnered substantial public support, leading to a national tour in 1988 that increased panel numbers significantly and raised funds for AIDS service organizations. Over the years, it expanded globally, drawing international attention including nominations for the Nobel Peace Prize in recognition of its impact on HIV/AIDS awareness and activism. By 2019, the Quilt's legacy was secured with its relocation to San Francisco and archival collection transferred to the American Folklife Center at the Library of Congress.
