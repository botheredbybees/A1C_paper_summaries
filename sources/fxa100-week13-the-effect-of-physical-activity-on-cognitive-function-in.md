## The effect of physical activity on cognitive function in patients with dementia: A meta-analysis of randomized control trials

This study, a meta-analysis of randomized control trials, investigates the impact of physical activity on cognitive function in dementia patients. The research synthesizes data from 802 participants across 18 studies and finds that physical activity interventions positively influence cognitive function (SMD = 0.42). This beneficial effect was consistent regardless of clinical diagnosis or intervention frequency but was particularly pronounced for aerobic exercise. The study emphasizes the potential of non-pharmacological, physical activity-based approaches as an appealing alternative to current treatments in managing cognitive symptoms associated with dementia.
