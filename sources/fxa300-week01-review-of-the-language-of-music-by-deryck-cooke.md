## Review of 'The Language of Music' by Deryck Cooke

Victor Zuckerkandl's review of 'The Language of Music' by Deryck Cooke critiques the author’s attempt to assign emotional significance to musical intervals and patterns. Cooke argues that music is a language capable of expressing specific emotions, contrary to the prevailing view that it is an abstract construct devoid of extramusical references. Zuckerkandl admires Cooke's boldness but finds his theory lacking coherence. The review highlights Cooke’s classification of intervals and melodic patterns with assigned emotional meanings, such as major thirds being 'joyful' and minor seconds evoking 'anguish.' This approach aims to reclaim music from intellectual abstraction and reconnect it with human emotion, though Zuckerkandl questions its validity.
