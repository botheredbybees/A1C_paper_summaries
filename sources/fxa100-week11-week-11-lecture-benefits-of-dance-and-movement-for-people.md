## Week 11 Lecture: Benefits of Dance and Movement for People with Dementia

In this week's lecture, Dr Brendan Lamb discusses the benefits of dance movement therapy (DMT) for individuals with dementia. Key points include the efficacy of DMT in developing new neural pathways and providing protective effects against cognitive decline due to its combined physical and mental exercise components. The lecture explores how multiple brain areas are engaged during dance, such as the motor cortex, cerebellum, auditory cortex, sensory cortex, and hippocampus. Additionally, Laban Movement Analysis is introduced as a system for objectively analyzing individuals' movements and non-verbal communication. Chacian DMT, pioneered by Marian Chace, emphasizes neurocognitive benefits through music and physical interaction, fostering empathy and stress reduction in participants. The lecture concludes with discussions on the social, physical, and emotional advantages of DMT, emphasizing the need for well-designed studies to substantiate its efficacy for dementia care.
