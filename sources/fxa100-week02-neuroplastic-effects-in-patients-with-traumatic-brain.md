## Neuroplastic Effects in Patients With Traumatic Brain Injury After Music-Supported Therapy

This study investigates the impact of music-supported therapy on neuroplastic changes in patients with mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI). The research focuses on the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC), which is frequently damaged post-TBI and leads to behavioral changes such as difficulties with attention and concentration. A total of three groups participated: a clinical group of mTBI patients (n=7) who underwent music therapy, and two control groups consisting of healthy participants with or without music training. The intervention involved piano playing twice weekly over eight weeks for 30 minutes each session, supplemented by daily practice at home.

The study employed neuropsychological tests alongside functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), both task-based and resting-state, to evaluate changes in cognitive performance and neural connectivity. Significant improvements were observed in the mTBI group’s cognitive abilities and social interaction post-intervention, with six out of seven participants returning to work. Additionally, fMRI results indicated enhanced activity within OFC networks, suggesting that music therapy can facilitate neuroplasticity and may offer therapeutic benefits for behavioral problems associated with TBI.
