## The Value of Arts and Culture to People and Society: An Evidence Review

The document titled 'The Value of Arts and Culture to People and Society: An Evidence Review' by the Arts Council England provides an extensive overview of the intrinsic and extrinsic benefits that arts and culture confer upon individuals and society. The foreword emphasizes the inherent value of arts and culture in enriching lives, although quantifying these benefits has been a challenge for securing funding from both public and private sectors.

The executive summary outlines several key findings, including economic contributions through tourism and employment, health benefits linked to higher levels of engagement with arts and cultural activities, societal impacts such as increased civic participation among youth who engage in the arts at school, and educational advantages like improved academic performance associated with drama and library activities. The review also highlights gaps in existing research regarding longitudinal studies on health benefits and economic effects of museums and libraries.

Overall, this report underscores the multifaceted ways in which arts and culture enrich individuals’ lives and contribute to societal well-being, education, and economy.
