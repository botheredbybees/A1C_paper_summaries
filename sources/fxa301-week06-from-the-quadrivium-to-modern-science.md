## From the Quadrivium to Modern Science

Matteo Valleriani's article, 'From the Quadrivium to Modern Science,' explores the transformation of scientific knowledge organization from medieval times to the early modern period in Europe. The author argues that the emergence of modern science can be understood as a process of disintegration and desegregation of the quadrivium, an educational framework consisting of arithmetic, music, geometry, and astronomy, which was central to university curricula during the late Middle Ages. Valleriani examines how each discipline evolved and underwent a practical turn, reflecting changes in societal needs and scientific inquiry. He introduces a fifth autonomous discipline, calendric, emphasizing its significance within the quadrivium's context. By focusing on the period from the thirteenth to the seventeenth century, Valleriani highlights the gradual shift away from traditional knowledge structures towards more specialized fields of study, illustrating how modern science emerged through this process.
