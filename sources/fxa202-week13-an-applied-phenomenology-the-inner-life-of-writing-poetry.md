## An applied phenomenology: the inner life of writing poetry with street photography

Eddie Tay's paper 'An applied phenomenology: the inner life of writing poetry with street photography' explores how phenomenological principles contribute to understanding creative practices, specifically the intersection of poetry and street photography in urban settings like Hong Kong and Singapore. The author differentiates between pure phenomenology as a theoretical discipline and applied phenomenology as an experiential practice that enhances critical reflection on everyday creativity. Through this lens, Tay argues that applied phenomenology provides valuable insights into the lived experiences of creative practitioners, highlighting the interplay between bodily sensations, emotions, and cognitive processes involved in creating art. The paper emphasizes the importance of suspending preconceived notions to truly experience phenomena directly, which is crucial for deepening understanding of artistic practices within hypercapitalist urban environments.
