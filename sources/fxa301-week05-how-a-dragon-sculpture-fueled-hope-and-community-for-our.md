## How a dragon sculpture fueled hope and community for our patients

This article discusses a monumental dragon sculpture project called 'Okoa the Wave Rider' created at MD Anderson Cancer Center. Over five months, more than 1,300 patients, family members, and staff contributed to building this artwork as part of the Arts in Medicine Program. The project aimed to demonstrate the therapeutic benefits of community art in bringing joy and relief to those undergoing cancer treatment. Patients could engage in various art activities such as coloring dragon scales or painting watercolor paintings, which were integrated into the sculpture. This initiative not only provided a creative outlet but also fostered a sense of community among participants. The project highlights how artistic endeavors can significantly reduce pain, stress, and anxiety while promoting positive connections to the healthcare environment.
