## Ages and Stages: the place of theatre in the lives of older people

This paper examines the role of theatre in the lives of older people through a case study of the Victoria/New Victoria Theatre in North Staffordshire, England. The interdisciplinary Ages and Stages project involved social gerontologists, humanities scholars, psychologists, anthropologists, and theatre practitioners to explore the theatre's history and impact on its community. Key findings show that participation in creative activities can challenge negative stereotypes about aging and highlight positive messages about growing older. Theatre provides a medium for inclusion of both younger and older individuals, shaping meanings associated with life transitions such as bereavement and retirement.
