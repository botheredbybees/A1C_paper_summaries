## The Effect of a Creative Art Program on Self-Esteem, Hope, Perceived Social Support, and Self-Efficacy in Individuals With Multiple Sclerosis: A Pilot Study

This pilot study explores the impact of a creative art program on self-esteem, hope, perceived social support, and self-efficacy among individuals living with multiple sclerosis (MS). Using a one-group pretest/posttest design, the researchers recruited a convenience sample of 14 women aged between 29 and 70 years from MS Centers and the National MS Society. The creative art program consisted of weekly sessions over four weeks focusing on different activities like watercolor painting, collage making, beading, and knitting. Each session was facilitated by healthcare professionals with expertise in MS. Post-intervention analysis using various scales such as Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale and Herth Hope Index showed significant improvements across all measures (self-esteem: t = -3.05, p = 0.009; hope: t = -3.96, p = 0.002; social support: t = -2.21, p = 0.046; self-efficacy to function and control MS: significant effects). The study concludes that creative arts can positively influence these psychosocial measures in individuals with MS and suggests further investigation with larger samples.
