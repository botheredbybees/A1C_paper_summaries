## Clown Therapy: A Drama Therapy Approach to Addiction and Beyond

This paper discusses a drama therapy approach to addiction using clown therapy, where clients are guided to discover their inner clown through various techniques and embodiment exercises. The theoretical underpinning of this method draws on the concept of the transcendent function from Jungian psychology and Winnicott's transitional phenomena, both highlighting how paradoxical elements can be reconciled within a therapeutic setting. Through this approach, individuals struggling with addiction are encouraged to embrace their clown persona as an archetypal embodiment of paradox, thereby fostering resilience in managing conflicting aspects of self. The study suggests that the clown role serves as a bridge between opposing sides of one's personality, facilitating emotional healing and recovery.
