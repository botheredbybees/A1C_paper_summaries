## The role of arts activities in developing resilience and mental wellbeing in children and young people: A rapid review of the literature

This rapid review explores the role of arts activities in promoting mental wellbeing and resilience among children and young people aged between 11 and 18 years. Conducted by Zarobe and Bungay (2017), the study uses a systematic literature search across 18 databases to identify relevant research on creative activities, including music, dance, singing, drama, and visual arts. The review focuses specifically on community-based interventions aimed at enhancing mental health through arts participation.

The authors highlight that while the evidence is limited due to the heterogeneity of the studies included, participating in structured group arts activities can positively influence self-confidence, self-esteem, social skills, relationship building, and a sense of belonging. These factors are closely linked to resilience and overall mental wellbeing. The review underscores the potential of community-based arts programs as cost-effective alternatives to traditional mental health services, particularly given the increasing incidence of mental ill-health among young people.

Additionally, the study emphasizes the shift in policy towards social prescribing schemes that incorporate arts activities, suggesting a promising avenue for supporting children and young people's mental health. The findings contribute significantly to understanding how creative engagement can foster resilience and enhance wellbeing, making it relevant for policymakers, educators, and practitioners involved in youth development initiatives.
