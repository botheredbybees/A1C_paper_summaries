## Research In Aged Care Deloraine partnership - transcript

The document details a research project focused on using music and singing as therapeutic interventions for elderly care, particularly in the context of improving quality of life and respiratory health. The study aims to evaluate the effectiveness of a singing program by measuring both well-being outcomes and physiological parameters such as respiratory function. One innovative aspect involves the use of kazoos, which are non-invasive and engaging tools that help participants maintain breath control while providing data on their vocal capabilities. Participants express feelings of significance and purpose from being involved in this research, highlighting the importance of engagement with arts for elderly individuals who may otherwise feel marginalized due to age or physical limitations. The ultimate goal is to expand this initiative across Tasmania to enhance the lives of many more residents through accessible music and singing programs.
