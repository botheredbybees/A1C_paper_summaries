## Immigrants’ Artistic Practices in Amsterdam, 1970–2007: A Political Issue of Inclusion and Exclusion

Christine Delhaye's article explores the political dynamics surrounding immigrant artists' participation in Amsterdam's art scene from 1970 to 2007. The study highlights how migrant artists' demands for inclusion have challenged the exclusivity of the Western art world, leading to critical debates about cultural policy and artistic gatekeeping mechanisms. Delhaye focuses on the visual arts and theatre performances within high culture contexts in Amsterdam, emphasizing that these fields are governed by implicit rules that regulate access and visibility for diverse groups of artists. The analysis is situated within the context of significant demographic changes in Amsterdam since the 1970s due to immigration trends from various regions such as Indonesia and Suriname. These transformations have led to a reevaluation of cultural policies and practices, making Amsterdam a pivotal case study for understanding broader issues of inclusion and exclusion in multicultural societies.
