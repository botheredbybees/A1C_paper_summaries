## Galen’s Reading of Hippocratic Ethics

Jacques Jouanna's chapter explores Galen's interpretation and adaptation of Hippocratic ethics within the context of medical practice in ancient Greece. Despite Galen’s admiration for Hippocrates, he does not comment on deontological treatises such as The Oath or Law. Instead, his ethical discussions are found within other works where he addresses principles like the purpose of medical art and the doctor-patient relationship. Notably, Galen emphasizes the importance of ‘being useful’ or ‘doing no harm’ in disease treatment, reflecting a key Hippocratic principle. This chapter highlights how Galen's rigorous ethical stance contrasts with the more flexible approach found in later Hippocratic treatises, contributing to our understanding of medical ethics' evolution from Hippocrates to Galen.
