## Dancing and the Brain

The article 'Dancing and the Brain' by Scott Edwards, published on the Harvard Medical School website in Winter 2015, explores how dance can have significant neurological benefits. The author highlights studies that demonstrate the positive impact of dance on cognitive functions such as memory improvement and executive function enhancement, particularly among older adults and those with Parkinson's disease. Dance is noted for its ability to stimulate brain activity through physical movement and rhythmic engagement, which helps in alleviating motor symptoms associated with Parkinson’s like bradykinesia and balance issues. Additionally, the article discusses research indicating that structured forms of dance such as tai chi can improve overall cognitive performance, strength, flexibility, and reduce the risk of falls among individuals with neurodegenerative conditions. The central argument emphasizes the therapeutic potential of dance in enhancing brain health and managing neurological disorders, suggesting its relevance for creative arts therapies in healthcare.
