## Subjective Well-being and Stress, Self-Care and Mindfulness

This lesson explores the concept of subjective well-being (SWB) in psychology research. SWB is closely aligned with hedonia and can be understood through its three components: positive emotions, negative emotions, and life satisfaction. The measurement of these components includes tools such as the PANAS and SLS scales. Debates around the definition of happiness and its measurement are discussed, along with critical thinking exercises regarding Barbara Fredrickson's work on positivity ratios.
