## An Experience Sampling Study of Emotional Reactions to Music: Listener, Music, and Situation

This study investigates the emotional reactions to music experienced by individuals in their daily lives using the Experience Sampling Method (ESM). Thirty-two college students aged between 20 and 31 years were asked to carry a palmtop device that prompted them to complete questionnaires at random intervals over two weeks. The results indicated that music was present in 37% of the recorded instances, with participants reporting emotional responses to music in 64% of these cases. Emotions such as happiness and nostalgia were more commonly associated with musical episodes compared to non-musical ones, which often included feelings like anger or boredom. Emotional reactions to music also correlated with individual personality traits and situational factors. The study highlights the importance of considering listener characteristics, music attributes, and contextual elements when studying emotional responses to music.
