## Creativity is good for the brain and might even slow down its ageing – new study

This article discusses a study by neuroscientists Carlos Coronel and Agustín Ibáñez that explores the impact of creative activities on brain health, particularly in relation to slowing down brain aging. The researchers used artificial intelligence (AI) models known as 'brain clocks' to measure the age-related changes in participants’ brains after engaging in various forms of creativity such as dance classes, art classes, music lessons, or gaming. They found that regular participation in creative activities correlated with a younger appearance of the AI brain clock compared to chronological age, indicating enhanced brain efficiency and resilience.

The study included nearly 1,400 participants from different countries who were either experts in specific creative fields (e.g., tango dancers, musicians, visual artists) or non-experts. The results showed that even short-term engagement in creative activities led to measurable benefits for brain health, suggesting that creativity can be a powerful tool in maintaining cognitive and emotional wellbeing throughout life.
