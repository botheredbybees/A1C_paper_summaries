## Evaluation and Research in Healthcare

The chapter discusses the distinctions between evaluation and research within healthcare interventions. It highlights that evaluation focuses on assessing an intervention's performance to guide decision-making, whereas research aims to understand deeper mechanisms and generalize findings. Evaluation can occur at various stages of intervention development, such as formative, process, or outcome assessments. Research often follows a well-designed evaluation phase but requires ethical approval. The chapter also outlines the UK Medical Research Council’s tool for differentiating between research and evaluation based on criteria like randomization, patient care changes, and generalizability. Additionally, it distinguishes audits from both evaluation and research, emphasizing their role in ensuring adherence to best practices and quality improvement cycles. Furthermore, the text explores debates around rigorous methodologies in arts-in-health interventions, questioning whether traditional experimental designs suit such complex initiatives.
