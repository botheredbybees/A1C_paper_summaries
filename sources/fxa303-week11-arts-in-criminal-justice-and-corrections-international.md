## Arts in Criminal Justice and Corrections: International Perspectives on Methods

Arts in Criminal Justice and Corrections: International Perspectives on Methods is a comprehensive volume that explores the role of arts programs within criminal justice systems across various international contexts, including the USA, UK, Australia, New Zealand, and Belgium. Edited by Amanda Gardner and Laura Caulfield, this book brings together contributions from leading researchers who have been instrumental in developing methodologies to evidence the impact of arts-based interventions in correctional settings. The volume emphasizes the interdisciplinary nature of the field, incorporating perspectives from criminology, psychology, and the arts.

Key themes include the development of theoretical frameworks for designing and evaluating arts programs within prisons, methodologies for assessing their impact on rehabilitation and reintegration, and the importance of participatory approaches that involve those directly affected by criminal justice systems. The book also highlights the challenges and opportunities associated with implementing such programs in diverse cultural contexts.

The relevance to creative arts and health is profound, as the volume underscores how engagement with artistic practices can contribute positively to mental well-being among incarcerated individuals. By fostering creativity, self-expression, and social interaction, these programs not only enhance individual rehabilitation but also promote a more just and inclusive society.
