## Group singing fosters mental health and wellbeing: findings from the East Kent ‘singing for health’ network project

This paper describes an innovative community singing initiative in East Kent, UK, aimed at improving mental health and wellbeing among individuals with serious and enduring mental health issues. The project established a network of seven small choirs over nine months, involving approximately 137 participants who completed the CORE questionnaire assessing mental distress. Clinically significant improvements were observed in mental health outcomes, suggesting that group singing can offer substantial benefits for recovery and social inclusion. Key findings indicate reduced levels of mental distress as measured by the CORE-OM, supporting the therapeutic value of choral singing for individuals with enduring mental health conditions. The research also highlights qualitative evidence of emotional and social benefits derived from engaging in communal musical activities.
