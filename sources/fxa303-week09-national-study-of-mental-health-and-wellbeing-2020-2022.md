## National Study of Mental Health and Wellbeing, 2020-2022

The National Study of Mental Health and Wellbeing, conducted by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) from December 2020 to October 2022, provides comprehensive data on mental health in Australia. The study highlights that 42.9% of Australians aged 16–85 had experienced a lifetime mental disorder, with anxiety disorders being the most common type at 28.8%. Regarding current (12-month) prevalence, 21.5% of people suffered from mental disorders, again primarily characterized by anxiety disorders (17.2%). Notably, younger individuals aged 16–24 years had a higher rate of 12-month mental disorders at 38.8%. The study also found significant disparities based on demographic factors such as gender and sexual orientation. For instance, females were twice as likely to experience anxiety or affective disorders compared to males, while transgender individuals experienced significantly higher rates than cisgender individuals. These findings underscore the importance of targeted interventions and support services tailored to different populations to address mental health concerns effectively.
