## 10 Tips for Better Composition

The document '10 Tips for Better Composition' provides practical advice on enhancing photographic compositions. It emphasizes the importance of aiming the camera at visually appealing subjects to avoid distractions and create a cohesive visual narrative through foreground, mid-ground, and background elements. The text advises against rigid adherence to the rule of thirds, suggesting that off-grid placements can be equally effective if used thoughtfully. Lighting is highlighted as crucial; photographers are encouraged to take advantage of early morning and late afternoon light, along with inclement weather conditions for dramatic effect. The author also underscores the value of exploring various compositions through different lens angles and orientations, noting that extensive experimentation in the field often leads to better results. Visual lines are identified as powerful tools for guiding viewers' eyes within a photograph, while proper spacing between key elements is recommended to maintain neatness and clarity. Tips on using recognizable subjects like people to convey scale and practicing diligently round out the advice offered by Grant Ordelheide.
