## Are we ‘‘experienced listeners’’? A review of the musical capacities that do not depend on formal musical training

This paper reviews studies investigating the influence of formal musical training on various aspects of Western music perception, including theme variation comprehension, tension and relaxation recognition, expectancy generation, structural integration, emotional response, and learning new compositional systems. The authors argue that some musical competencies develop naturally through exposure to music without explicit instruction, allowing untrained individuals to understand and appreciate music as sophisticatedly as trained listeners do. This suggests a natural human aptitude for music processing, which is refined with training but present without it.
