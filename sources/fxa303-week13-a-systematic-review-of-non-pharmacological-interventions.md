## A Systematic Review of Non-pharmacological Interventions for BPSD in Nursing Home Residents with Dementia

This systematic review evaluates non-pharmacological interventions for Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD) in nursing home residents from an ergonomics perspective, emphasizing the importance of considering participants' capabilities. The study identified 64 clinical trials addressing BPSD symptoms with various types of interventions such as sensory-, cognition-, and movement-oriented approaches. Key findings indicate that while most trials reported significant reductions in BPSD, a substantial number (28) did not adequately consider the capabilities of persons with dementia (PwD), particularly regarding sensory functions. This review highlights the need for future research to integrate capability considerations into intervention designs to enhance evidence quality and ensure better outcomes.
