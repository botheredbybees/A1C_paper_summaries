## In Praise of ‘Negative Capability’

This chapter explores the concept of 'negative capability' as introduced by poet John Keats to discuss an aesthetic approach to dementia care. The author argues that understanding and interacting with people who have dementia should be seen through the lens of beauty, joy, and intuition rather than strictly factual or rational methods. Emphasizing the idea of 'negative capability,' which involves embracing uncertainties and doubts without seeking definitive answers, the chapter advocates for deep, spiritual connections in caregiving practices. The author draws on examples from John Killick's work to illustrate how artistic creativity and authentic human encounters can enrich interactions with individuals suffering from dementia.
