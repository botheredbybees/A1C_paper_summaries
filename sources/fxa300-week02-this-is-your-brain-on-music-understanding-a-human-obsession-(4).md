## This Is Your Brain On Music: Understanding a Human Obsession - Chapter 9

In Chapter 9 of 'This Is Your Brain On Music', Daniel Levitin explores the evolutionary origins of music and challenges Steven Pinker's argument that music is an evolutionary spandrel—a by-product with no clear adaptive purpose. Pinker posits that language, rather than music, is an adaptation that music piggybacks on due to its pleasurable nature but lacks functional utility for survival. Levitin counters this perspective by revisiting Darwin’s theory of natural selection and emphasizing the importance of sexual selection in understanding the evolutionary basis of traits like music. He argues against Pinker's dismissal by highlighting that many behaviors, including those related to music, contribute to reproductive success through social bonding and mate attraction, which aligns with an adaptive function rather than being merely a pleasurable accident.
