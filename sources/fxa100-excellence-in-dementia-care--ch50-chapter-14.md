## Chapter 14: Managing the Patient with Dementia

The chapter discusses the challenges faced by individuals with dementia and their caregivers, particularly focusing on behavioral issues stemming from dementia's neurological impact versus social interactions. It critiques the overreliance on pharmaceutical solutions like Seroquel for managing symptoms such as agitation or aggression. The text emphasizes Kitwood’s bio-psycho-social model of dementia care, which considers neuropathological damage alongside psychological and societal factors influencing behavior. Key aspects include the distinction between explicit and implicit memory in AD patients and case studies illustrating these concepts. The chapter also addresses issues like apraxia and visual agnosia resulting from brain damage and how such behaviors might be misinterpreted or mishandled.
