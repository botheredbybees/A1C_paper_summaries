## Life-writing and its implications for health and well-being

Frances Cadd's chapter in 'The Routledge Companion to Health Humanities' explores how life-writing, as a form of communication and sense-making, offers insights into the interplay between physical and social environments and individual health and well-being. The chapter highlights three key areas: everyday practices, mentalities and attitudes, and subjective perspectives. Through analyzing life-writings such as diaries, letters, oral testimonies, and autobiographies, Cadd shows how these sources reveal routine daily activities and the use of traditional remedies that were commonplace before modern healthcare systems. She also discusses shared beliefs and cultural scripts within communities, illustrating through Avis Hutt's experiences the prevailing attitudes towards nursing in the 1930s, which emphasized patient-centric care even at personal risk to the nurse’s health. This historical perspective underscores the relevance of life-writing as a method for contemporary health humanities research.
