## Music Engagement and Disability

This lesson explores how music can enhance the lives of individuals with disabilities through various forms of engagement. It highlights the formation of ensembles for musicians with physical and intellectual disabilities and provides a case study on the Spirit of Goodwill band. The text discusses the role of the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) in funding music therapy, emphasizing its benefits for communication and social interaction. Research using fMRI has shown that music therapy improves neural connections in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Additionally, it covers technological advancements in creating adaptive musical instruments and using virtual reality to enhance inclusivity in music-making. The lesson also touches on visual arts engagement, accessibility improvements in art galleries for people with disabilities, and tactile art experiences designed specifically for visually impaired individuals.
