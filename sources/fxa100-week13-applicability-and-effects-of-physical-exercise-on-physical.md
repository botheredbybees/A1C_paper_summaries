## Applicability and Effects of Physical Exercise on Physical and Cognitive Functions and Activities of Daily Living Among People With Dementia

The study conducted a systematic review to evaluate the applicability and effects of physical exercise on physical functions, cognitive functions, and activities of daily living (ADLs) among individuals with dementia. The review identified ten randomized controlled trials from various databases. Most participants were Alzheimer's patients in residential care facilities. Four studies reached moderate methodological quality, while six achieved low quality. Moderate-quality studies suggested that combined functional weight-bearing exercise is applicable regarding attendance and adverse events, showing improvement in walking performance and reducing the decline in ADLs. For individuals with unspecified dementia disorders in residential care facilities, self-selected speed walking had no significant effect on cognitive functions but may reduce declines in walking performance. The review concluded that physical exercise interventions lasting several months and task-specific exercises are beneficial for improving physical function but evidence regarding cognitive benefits remains unclear due to the limited number of high-quality studies evaluating this aspect.
