## Early childhood: Learning through visual art

Kim Jenson's article in Teacher explores the significance of arts education, particularly visual arts, for early childhood development. The author emphasizes that engaging with various artistic mediums like dance, music, drama, and visual art fosters holistic growth in infants, toddlers, and young children by enhancing cognitive, social, problem-solving, and personal competencies. Jenson asserts that educators should create an environment where children can freely explore their identities through arts activities without adult-imposed goals or agendas. This unrestricted creativity is crucial for developing fine motor skills and cognitive connections in young learners. Additionally, the article highlights how arts education supports language development by encouraging vocabulary exploration and meaningful use of language in discussions about art. Jenson stresses that guided participation and scaffolding are essential to maintain children's interest and engagement without stifling their creative processes.
