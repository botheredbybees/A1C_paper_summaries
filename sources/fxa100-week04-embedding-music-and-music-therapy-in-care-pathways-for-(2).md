## Embedding Music and Music Therapy in Care Pathways for People with Dementia in the 21st Century

This position paper discusses the role of music therapy in managing dementia, focusing on both direct and indirect interventions. The author explores how music therapy can enhance quality of life for individuals with dementia and their caregivers by addressing personalized needs through improvisation, singing, songwriting, and listening to live or recorded music. The paper highlights an international trial called Homeside that investigates the efficacy of musical and reading interventions in home settings for people living with dementia and their family carers. Key findings include improved relationships between couples attending therapy groups and reduced agitation among individuals living in care homes. The author emphasizes the need for increased training in music therapy to ensure accessible, high-quality interventions are embedded into daily care routines.
