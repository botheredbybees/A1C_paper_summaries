## Routines of Resistance: An Ethnography of the Care of People Living with Dementia in Acute Hospital Wards and Its Consequences

The paper explores the routine resistance to care by people living with dementia in acute hospital wards through an ethnographic study conducted over 20 months across five NHS hospitals in England and Wales. The study reveals that institutional routines, such as timetabled meals and medication rounds, often trigger patient resistance due to the lack of flexibility for individual needs. Ward staff felt constrained into delivering these routines, leading to short-term containment practices that frequently escalated into entrenched patterns of resistance from patients. This results in poor care experiences for patients and emotional and physical burnout for staff. The paper highlights the need for research on aligning institutional goals with patient-centered approaches, especially considering the growing population of people living with dementia in hospitals.
