## Physiological Responses to Music and Sound Stimuli

This chapter explores the physiological responses to music and sound stimuli, emphasizing their impact on various aspects of human health and well-being. It includes studies from multiple disciplines such as psychology, medicine, and neuroscience that investigate how music affects emotional states, stress levels, immune function, and other biological systems. Key research highlights the positive effects of choral singing, dance activities, and instrumental music programs on mental and physical health parameters like salivary immunoglobulin A (IgA) and cortisol levels. Additionally, it discusses the role of cultural engagement in reducing mortality rates among adults and enhancing cognitive functions in individuals with Parkinson's disease.
