## Web Lecture 5 - Engaging with Visual Arts

This web lecture focuses on engaging with visual arts through case studies and the importance of community involvement in such projects. The first case study discusses 'The Paper Apothecary' project at Beaney House, Canterbury UK, which involved local school children and community members creating happiness prescriptions using recycled paper and cardboard. This initiative led to increased museum visits and reported higher levels of happiness among participants. Another example is the Margaret Jurd College Mural Project in Shortland, near Newcastle, celebrating NAIDOC week by enhancing cultural understanding and cohesion through a mural depicting eagles significant to the Awabakal land. The NAMES Project AIDS Memorial Quilt, initiated in 1985, serves as a powerful memorial for those affected by HIV/AIDS, fostering remembrance and community healing. Lastly, Okoa the Wave Rider is highlighted as a community sculpture project where patients, families, and healthcare professionals collaborated on creating an enormous river dragon, promoting a sense of ownership and wellbeing among participants.
