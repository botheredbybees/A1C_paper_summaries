## Intervention of companion clowns in a special care unit: a 1-year pilot study

This study examines the impact of clown intervention in a special care unit (SCU) for residents with severe Behavioral and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSDs). Over a year, clowns visited the SCU twice weekly for three months each. Despite no significant change in overall BPSD scores, residents exhibited fewer delusions, hallucinations, euphoria, and aberrant motor behavior but more depression, apathy, disinhibition, and appetite disorders during clown interventions. The study highlights potential benefits of clown therapy in specific behavioral issues without altering the overall BPSD levels.
