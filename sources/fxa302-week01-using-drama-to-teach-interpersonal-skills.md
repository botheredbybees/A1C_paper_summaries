## Using drama to teach interpersonal skills

This article discusses an educational intervention that utilized a drama and theatre workshop to teach communication and interpersonal skills to mental health nursing students. The study highlights the effectiveness of using theatrical methods in fostering empathy, providing a safe learning environment, and enhancing knowledge and skills among nursing students. Key findings indicate that most participants found the workshop an excellent way to learn these essential skills for clinical practice. The authors argue that incorporating drama into nurse education can address gaps in training related to communication and interpersonal skills, which are crucial for effective patient care. This approach is relevant to creative arts and health as it integrates artistic methods with healthcare education to improve emotional intelligence and practical competencies among nursing students.
