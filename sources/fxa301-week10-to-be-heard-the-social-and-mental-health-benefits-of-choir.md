## 'To be heard': The social and mental health benefits of choir singing for disadvantaged adults

The study explores the wellbeing and social benefits of choir singing for individuals with chronic mental health issues or disabilities. The research, employing interpretative phenomenological analysis (IPA), involved semi-structured interviews with 21 members at three stages during their inaugural year. Findings reveal positive impacts on emotional regulation, spiritual experience, self-perception, connectedness within the choir and community, social functioning, and overall life quality. Key themes include personal growth through emotional expression, enhanced social bonds, and improved mental health outcomes. The study underscores the importance of group identity in fostering resilience and wellbeing among socially marginalized individuals.
