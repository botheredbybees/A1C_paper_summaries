## The Perceived Benefits of Singing: Findings from Preliminary Surveys

This study explores the perceived health and well-being benefits associated with active participation in choral singing through two exploratory surveys conducted among members of a university college choir. The first survey revealed that participants overwhelmingly reported social, emotional, physical, and spiritual benefits from their involvement in the choir, such as improved mood, increased control over breathing, and feelings of upliftment. A content analysis of written comments highlighted themes like meeting new people, feeling more positive, and experiencing spiritual upliftment. In a follow-up structured questionnaire with 91 participants, strong agreement was found regarding singing's positive impact on mood, emotional well-being, social connections, spirituality, breath control, and heart/immune system function. The study identifies six dimensions of benefit: well-being and relaxation, breathing and posture, social benefits, spiritual benefits, emotional benefits, and health benefits for the heart and immune system. Women were more likely to experience well-being benefits, younger participants had more social benefits, and those with religious beliefs reported greater spiritual benefits.
