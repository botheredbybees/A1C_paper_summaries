## The Assignment I'll Never Forget

In 'The Assignment I'll Never Forget,' Dorothea Lange recounts her experience capturing the iconic photograph 'Migrant Mother' for the Farm Security Administration in March 1936. The story describes how Lange was driving home after completing a photography assignment when she saw a sign indicating a pea-pickers camp. Despite initial reluctance, an inner compulsion drove her to return and take the famous picture of Florence Owens Thompson, who had sold her car's tires for food amidst the Great Depression's hardships. Lange emphasizes the importance of being deeply involved in one’s work to capture meaningful moments, highlighting the photograph's enduring impact as a symbol of resilience during tough times.
