## Creativity and Communication in Persons with Dementia : A Practical Guide

The document discusses a practical guide for integrating creative media into dementia care settings to enhance communication and identity among residents, families, and staff members. The focus is on a case study at Cedars, a long-term care unit for people with dementia, where the author introduces various creative activities such as life-story work, art, writing, drama, and drawing. Despite initial resistance from the management due to resource constraints, these activities fostered community and mutual understanding among residents and staff. Key elements include a story-wall timeline, object-based reminiscence sessions, and sensory-based writing projects that encourage personal connections. The project's success underscores the importance of creative engagement in dementia care for improving quality of life and fostering relationships.
