## More Than Just an Entertainment Show: Identification of Medical Clowns' Communication Skills and Therapeutic Goals

The study explores the communication skills and therapeutic goals of medical clowns (MCs) in pediatric settings, addressing challenges such as dealing with frustrated adolescents, anxious parents, and complex triadic interactions. Through qualitative analysis involving video-recorded simulations and interviews, the researchers identified 40 distinct skills used by MCs beyond traditional entertainment techniques. These skills encompass building relationships, managing emotions, enhancing a sense of control, providing care and encouragement, and motivating adherence to medical treatments. The study highlights the unique therapeutic role of MCs in improving patient well-being and suggests that other healthcare professionals can benefit from understanding these practices.
