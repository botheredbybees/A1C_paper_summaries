## Self-Actualization Myths: What Did Maslow Really Say?

William C. Compton's article addresses common misconceptions surrounding Abraham Maslow’s theory of self-actualization, a concept widely recognized but frequently misunderstood both within and outside the field of psychology. The paper debunks several myths about the theory and Maslow’s thoughts on science by referencing his original writings. Key topics include empirical support for the theory, movement through the hierarchy of needs, the nature of self in self-actualization, hidden elitism within the theory, and happiness among self-actualizing individuals. Compton emphasizes that while there is a substantial body of research supporting certain aspects of Maslow’s theory, much remains misunderstood or misinterpreted. He highlights studies showing correlations between self-actualization characteristics and higher levels of mental health and well-being, contributing to contemporary psychology's understanding of optimal human functioning.
