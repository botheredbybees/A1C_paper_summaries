## Transcript: Assistant Professor of Comparative Literature and Medicine Alvan Ikoku on Medical Humanities

The transcript captures Assistant Professor Alvan Ikoku's perspective on the medical humanities and its interdisciplinary nature. He emphasizes critical thinking and challenging assumptions as essential to both literary analysis and medical practice. Ikoku defines medical humanities as work at the intersection of medicine, humanistic inquiry, and expression, highlighting the importance of translation between these fields. The discussion includes how novels can produce knowledge about medicine through rhetorical devices and metaphors used by physicians of the time. He also explores the constitution of international fields like Global Health and World Literature through a cross-textual comparison involving literary works, scientific articles, and policy declarations. Ikoku underscores the failure of medical practice to fully address the complexities of human experience, advocating for an inclusive approach that values various forms of knowledge.
