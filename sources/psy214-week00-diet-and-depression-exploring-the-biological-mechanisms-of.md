## Diet and Depression: Exploring the Biological Mechanisms of Action

This review article explores the biological mechanisms by which diet influences mental health, specifically depression. The authors summarize observational and interventional data suggesting that dietary patterns such as the Mediterranean diet are associated with reduced risk of depression and improved depressive symptoms. Key pathways identified include inflammation modulation, oxidative stress reduction, epigenetic modifications, gut microbiota regulation, tryptophan-kynurenine metabolism adjustment, hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis stabilization, neurogenesis enhancement, brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) increase, and obesity prevention. The review highlights the need for further clinical trials to elucidate these mechanisms in humans fully. Understanding these pathways is crucial for developing dietary interventions that could serve as adjunctive therapies or preventive measures in mental health care.
