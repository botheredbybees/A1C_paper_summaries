## Post-traumatic stress disorder: the neurobiological impact of psychological trauma

This article reviews the neurobiological impact of psychological trauma, focusing on post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). It highlights that PTSD is not merely a response to traumatic events but rather involves individual predispositions and neurobiological vulnerabilities. The authors discuss endocrine dysregulation, particularly in the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis, and neurotransmitter pathways implicated in PTSD. They also explore how genetic factors, early developmental stages, prior trauma, gender, and physical injuries like traumatic brain injury can influence susceptibility to PTSD. The article suggests that some biological markers for PTSD may predate the traumatic event and are unmasked by stress exposure, rather than being acquired as a consequence of trauma alone. This insight into neurobiological vulnerabilities has significant implications for understanding the complex nature of PTSD in creative arts and health contexts.
