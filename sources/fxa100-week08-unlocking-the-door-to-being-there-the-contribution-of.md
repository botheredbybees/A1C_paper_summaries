## 'Unlocking the door to being there': The contribution of creative facilitators in supporting people living with dementia to engage with heritage settings

The article discusses the evaluation of 'Sensory Palaces', a project by Historic Royal Palaces that employs creative facilitators to support individuals living with dementia and their care partners in engaging with heritage sites like Hampton Court Palace and Kew Palace. The study focuses on the role of these facilitators, who are trained artists using sensory and creative methods to help participants connect with historical settings. Key findings highlight the importance of creating a safe environment for learning and engagement through multisensory experiences, fostering social inclusion and well-being among dementia patients. The project's approach aligns with relational perspectives in dementia care rather than individualized therapies, emphasizing the value of shared activities in heritage spaces. This research underscores the positive impact of such initiatives on improving quality of life for people with dementia.
