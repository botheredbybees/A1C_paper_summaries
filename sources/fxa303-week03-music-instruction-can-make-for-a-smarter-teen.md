## Music Instruction Can Make for a Smarter Teen

New research by Nina Kraus and colleagues at Northwestern University shows that music training can positively impact the developing adolescent brain, even when initiated in high school. The study followed two groups of Chicago-area students from low-income neighborhoods over four years, with one group engaged in formal music instruction while the other participated in JROTC activities. Key findings indicate enhanced auditory neural function and phonological awareness among those receiving musical training. Specifically, participants showed accelerated brain development in response to sound details and sustained heightened sensitivity to these sounds. These results underscore the importance of engaging adolescents in enrichment programs like music education to bolster their cognitive and linguistic skills, potentially enhancing overall learning abilities.
