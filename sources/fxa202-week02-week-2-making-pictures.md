## Week 2 – Making Pictures

The tutorial focuses on the importance of having a camera with you at all times for spontaneous and candid photography, emphasizing that the best camera is often the one available to use. The text discusses three types of cameras: mobile phone cameras, point-and-shoot cameras, and DSLRs. Mobile phones are praised for their portability and ease of use but criticized for lower image quality compared to dedicated digital cameras due to improper handling by users. Tips include keeping the lens clean and utilizing photo apps like Snapseed or Open Camera. Point-and-shoot cameras are highlighted for their automatic functions and wide-angle lenses, though they lack modifiability and struggle with focusing on specific subjects. The tutorial suggests holding the camera steady when shooting outdoors and avoiding direct sunlight to avoid overexposure issues. DSLRs offer high-quality photos due to megapixel count and image sensors but come at a higher cost and are bulkier. Tips for using DSLRs include understanding manual settings such as aperture control (Av mode) and exploring post-production options. The overall message encourages experimentation, creativity, and the development of photographic skills regardless of equipment choice.
