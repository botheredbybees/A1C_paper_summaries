## Creative Arts Adult Community Learning

This chapter discusses the role of creative arts in adult community learning (ACL) and its impact on mental health and well-being. It highlights that while ACL, particularly in creative arts such as visual arts, crafting, and creative writing, has been shown to be beneficial for participants' mental health and quality of life, there are debates about whether these activities should be justified solely based on their mental health benefits or recognized for their intrinsic educational value. The chapter reviews studies comparing the effectiveness of ACL in creative arts versus other subjects like gym/exercise classes or vocational courses, finding that creative arts often yield better well-being outcomes, especially among older adults and women. It also explores how participation in such activities can foster mutuality and social connection, contributing to mental health recovery through the development of personal resources and enhanced self-determination.
