## What Sort of Creative Are You?

The text explores the concept of creativity through a series of questions and reflections on one's creative identity. The author, Philippa Stanton, emphasizes that creativity is innate but can be nurtured by reconnecting with childhood curiosity and instinctive behaviors. She argues that understanding your type of creativity aids in focusing personal growth and artistic endeavors. Questions such as whether creativity is important to you and what sort of creative person one considers themselves help readers reflect on their relationship with creativity. Stanton encourages readers to observe the world like a child, emphasizing the importance of rekindling early-life curiosity and instinctive exploration. The text also addresses how various disciplines and personal aesthetics influence creative expression.
