## Art for better health and wellbeing

This article explores the intersection between medicine and art, suggesting that these two fields are more intertwined than they appear at first glance. The authors argue that dopamine, a neurotransmitter vital in Parkinson’s disease treatment, also plays a role in creativity and artistic expression, with dopaminergic medications stimulating creativity in up to 10% of patients. Artistic activities such as dance and music can help manage symptoms of Parkinson's disease by improving motor skills and emotional well-being.

The piece also discusses the therapeutic benefits of art for mental health and self-management, encouraging healthcare providers to consider art as a tool for personalized care tailored to individual patient needs. The article highlights how poets and artists can enhance communication between patients and doctors regarding complex symptoms like pain through metaphorical language. Additionally, it emphasizes that artistic training can aid medical professionals in dealing with clinical ambiguity and fostering empathy, essential qualities in humanistic healthcare.

The authors propose that integrating arts into medical education could help students develop a more holistic understanding of patient care, emphasizing the importance of intuition and personalized treatment over strict adherence to evidence-based protocols. Overall, the article advocates for a closer collaboration between medicine and art to improve patient care and health outcomes.
