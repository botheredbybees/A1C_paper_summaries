## Generative artificial intelligence, human creativity, and art

This paper explores the impact of generative artificial intelligence (AI) on human creativity and artistic productivity, focusing specifically on text-to-image systems like Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, and DALL-E. Using a dataset comprising over four million artworks from more than fifty thousand unique users, the study reveals that AI adoption significantly boosts artists' creative output by 25%, while also increasing the likelihood of artworks receiving positive evaluations (favorites) by approximately 50%. Although peak Content Novelty (the originality of subject matter and relations depicted in artworks) increases over time, average Content Novelty declines, indicating an inefficient idea space. Similarly, both peak and average Visual Novelty (pixel-level stylistic elements) decrease. The research underscores the importance of human ideation and artistic filtering skills in leveraging AI tools effectively to produce more novel and favorably evaluated artworks. This study highlights a harmonious blending of human exploration with AI exploitation, termed 'generative synesthesia,' which is crucial for discovering new creative workflows and processes.
