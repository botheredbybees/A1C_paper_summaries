## How Has the Internet Reshaped Human Cognition?

The article by Loh and Kanai explores how the advent of the Internet has profoundly reshaped human cognition, similar to earlier technological inventions such as tool-making, language, writing, and arithmetic. The authors argue that just over three decades since its inception, the Internet's multifaceted affordances have significantly altered cognitive behaviors, particularly among 'Digital Natives' who grew up with these technologies compared to older generations or ‘Digital Immigrants’. Digital natives exhibit a more rapid and non-linear mode of information processing characterized by decreased sustained attention and elaborative thinking. This shift is linked to higher levels of multitasking, increased distractibility, poorer executive control abilities, and heightened susceptibility to internet-related addictive behaviors. Neuroimaging studies suggest that these changes may correlate with structural modifications in the brain. Despite concerns over potential negative impacts on cognitive systems, the authors advocate for an objective assessment based on empirical evidence.
