## Creative and credible evaluation for arts, health and well-being: opportunities and challenges of co-production

This article discusses findings from a UK knowledge exchange project aimed at improving evaluation practices in the arts and health sector, which ran from September 2014 to August 2015. The study explored stakeholders' experiences of evaluation methods through surveys, interviews, and focus groups. Key challenges identified include a lack of agreed-upon evaluation frameworks and difficulties in practice due to structural and cultural barriers. Opportunities for arts initiatives are also highlighted within the context of current health and social care policies. The project concludes that effective co-production between diverse stakeholders is essential but can be hindered by unequal relationships and structural constraints. The findings support the development of online resources (www.creativeandcredible.co.uk) to strengthen evaluation practices in the sector, thus aiding advocacy efforts for arts in health and well-being.
