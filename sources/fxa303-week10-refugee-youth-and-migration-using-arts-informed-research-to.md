## Refugee Youth and Migration: Using Arts-Informed Research to Understand Changes in Their Roles and Responsibilities

This article explores the use of arts-informed research methods to understand changes in refugee youth's roles and responsibilities after migration to Canada. The study, conducted among Afghan, Karen, and Sudanese communities in Toronto, involved 57 newcomer youths who had arrived as refugees. Data was collected through focus group discussions that included a drawing activity, providing insights into the nuanced experiences of these youths beyond traditional textual methods. Key findings indicate an increase in youths' roles such as interpretation, translation, financial support, emotional care, and household chores, alongside educational pursuits. The use of drawings enriched qualitative data analysis by capturing tacit knowledge, emotions, and symbolic expressions that might be overlooked with text alone. This study underscores the importance of arts-informed methods for more culturally accurate research involving marginalized communities.
