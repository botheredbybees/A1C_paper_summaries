## The Storm Shifting South: Reflections on Public Baths in Winter

The text is a reflective poem about visiting public baths during winter amidst a global pandemic. The author describes the scene as changed yet personal, emphasizing the openness to natural elements. The poem captures the emotional and sensory experiences of the place, including the impact of weather on the physical structure and its surroundings. It highlights the weekly maintenance routine of refilling the pools with seawater, creating temporary aquatic life within them. The imagery is rich in color and texture, drawing parallels between personal memories and the historical layers evident in the baths' architecture. This poetic reflection underscores the therapeutic value of natural environments for mental health during challenging times.
