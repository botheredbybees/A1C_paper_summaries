## Good for the Heart, Good for the Soul: The Creative Arts and Brain Health in Later Life

This article explores the relationship between participation in creative arts programs and cognitive health in older adults, highlighting both empirical evidence and speculative theories on brain development and creativity. The authors review research by Gene Cohen which found that engagement in artistic activities could enhance mental health, physical well-being, and social interaction among seniors. They also discuss theoretical frameworks suggesting that different types of intelligence and the synthesis of arts and sciences can foster cognitive growth and resilience. Through case studies and longitudinal analysis, the paper argues that creative endeavors not only provide enjoyment but may also contribute to neurobiological benefits through mechanisms like mild stress-induced hormetic responses. The article concludes by emphasizing the need for further research in this emerging field.
