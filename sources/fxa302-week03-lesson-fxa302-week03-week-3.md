## Visual Arts as Medical Illustration

The lesson discusses how visual arts have played a crucial role in medical illustration since pre-printing press times. Manuscripts were copied by hand, spreading knowledge and ideas along with medical illustrations clarifying textual descriptions. Leonardo da Vinci conducted dissections and created accurate anatomical drawings but his work was largely unknown until the 1960s. Andreas Vesalius is credited with overturning Galen’s dominance through detailed anatomical studies published in 'De Humani Corporis Fabrica'. Gerard Blasius contributed significantly to understanding the brain and nervous system, including the first detailed study of the spinal cord. The 19th century saw advancements in lithography enabling more accurate and artistic medical illustrations. Henry Gray's Anatomy, with contributions from Henry Vandyke Carter, remains influential today.
