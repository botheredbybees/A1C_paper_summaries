## Diagnostic Categories or Dimensions? A Question for the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders—Fifth Edition

This article examines the debate between categorical and dimensional approaches to diagnosing mental disorders, focusing on the limitations of the current categorical model used in DSM-IV. The authors argue that excessive diagnostic comorbidity and unresolved boundary disputes challenge the validity of distinct clinical entities identified in DSM-IV. They propose that many co-occurring diagnoses may reflect shared pathologies rather than separate conditions, suggesting a shift towards dimensional models for future editions of the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-V). The study highlights the importance of considering dimensions such as negative affectivity and externalization to better understand and treat mental disorders. This perspective is crucial in creative arts and health fields where understanding underlying psychological mechanisms can inform therapeutic interventions.
