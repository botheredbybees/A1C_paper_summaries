## Autism Spectrum Disorder

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is characterized by impairments in social interaction, communication, and repetitive or restricted behaviors. This document explains that while ASD can be diagnosed at any age, it typically presents during early childhood with varying degrees of severity. Key signs include difficulties initiating conversations, maintaining eye contact, understanding others' perspectives, adhering rigidly to routines, displaying sensory sensitivities, and engaging in stereotyped movements or interests.

The diagnostic process involves initial developmental screenings followed by comprehensive assessments conducted by specialists who evaluate cognitive abilities, language skills, behavioral patterns, and family history. Early detection enables access to effective interventions aimed at mitigating challenges and building competencies through individualized education plans, social skills training, occupational therapy, and speech-language pathology.

Furthermore, the text highlights that ASD can co-occur with other conditions such as ADHD or anxiety disorders, necessitating thorough evaluations for accurate diagnoses. As individuals mature into adolescence and adulthood, symptoms may evolve; therefore, ongoing assessments help tailor supportive measures according to changing needs.

Overall, recognizing early signs of ASD is crucial for initiating appropriate interventions that enhance quality of life by addressing personal strengths alongside challenges associated with this neurodevelopmental condition.
