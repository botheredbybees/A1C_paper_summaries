## Research, Ageing and Dementia

This chapter discusses the importance of broadening the scope of research into ageing to include dementia. It argues that ageing research should adopt a comprehensive approach encompassing biological, psychological, social, and ethical dimensions. The text contrasts ageing research with cancer research, emphasizing that while both fields share common interests like genetics and cell death, ageing researchers aim for a broader understanding relevant to various age-related conditions beyond specific diseases. Key aims of ageing research include longevity, quality of life, and pure scientific enquiry. The chapter also addresses the misconception that dementia is part of normal aging by highlighting its prevalence increases with age but does not necessarily affect all elderly individuals. It critiques the use of the term 'normal' in defining ageing processes and suggests rethinking diagnostic criteria to better integrate ethical considerations and value judgments.
