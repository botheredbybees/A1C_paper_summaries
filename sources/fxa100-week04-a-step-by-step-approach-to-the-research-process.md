## A step-by-step approach to the research process

This chapter outlines a systematic approach to conducting research in arts and health interventions, emphasizing the integration of scientific rigor with creative processes. The author presents a framework that delineates four stages: developing an initial idea for a research study, designing the study, executing the research, and concluding with outcomes. Each stage is further broken down into sub-stages that guide researchers through decision-making processes concerning feasibility, methodology, implementation, reporting, and dissemination of findings. Key aspects include identifying research problems, formulating hypotheses, conducting literature reviews, choosing appropriate methodologies (quantitative, qualitative, mixed methods), and evaluating economic and process dimensions. The framework aims to enhance collaboration between arts and health sectors by providing a comprehensive tool for designing rigorous yet creative research projects. It highlights the importance of involving stakeholders such as patients and public in shaping these studies, thereby ensuring relevance and practical applicability.
