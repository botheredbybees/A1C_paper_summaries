## What Is Alzheimer's Disease

This transcript provides an overview of Alzheimer's disease as described by Dr. Alois Alzheimer in 1901 and its current status. It explains the formation of plaques from beta-amyloid proteins and neurofibrillary tangles from tau protein, both contributing to brain cell destruction. The progression of these proteins through various brain regions leads to symptoms such as memory loss, mood changes, hallucinations, and eventually death. Current research focuses on slowing disease progression with treatments like acetylcholine enhancers and beta-amyloid vaccines.
