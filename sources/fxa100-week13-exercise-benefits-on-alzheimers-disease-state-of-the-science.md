## Exercise Benefits on Alzheimer’s Disease: State-of-the-Science

This review article examines the potential benefits of regular physical exercise on Alzheimer's disease (AD) and cognitive decline. Despite extensive research, AD prevalence continues to rise globally. The authors highlight growing evidence suggesting that consistent physical activity can reduce the risk of AD and slow cognitive decline by enhancing cardiovascular health and neurogenesis through mechanisms such as increased BDNF production. Regular aerobic exercise has been shown to positively impact hippocampal volume in patients with AD. However, while some studies indicate potential benefits for biomarkers like amyloid beta and tau levels, others suggest inconsistent results regarding the effect of physical activity on these markers. The review proposes areas for future research focusing on biological mechanisms that might underlie protective effects of PA in AD.
