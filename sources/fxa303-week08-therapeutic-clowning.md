## Therapeutic Clowning

This document discusses the history and contemporary application of therapeutic clowning in healthcare settings. It begins by examining the historical role of fools and jesters in entertainment and social commentary before delving into the character of Shakespeare's fools, who often reveal truths to misguided characters like King Lear. The lecture then outlines two types of clowns: Whiteface and Auguste, describing their contrasting roles and visual characteristics. Healthcare clowning is explored through studies showing its effectiveness in reducing fear and anxiety for children undergoing medical procedures and hospitalization. Additionally, the document highlights a study on adolescent psychiatric care that suggests clown doctor sessions can enhance mood among young people with mental health conditions. The text also touches upon the use of therapeutic clowning for older adults, noting improvements in communication and reduction of agitation and aggression among dementia patients.
