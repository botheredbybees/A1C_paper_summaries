## Erikson's Stages of Psychosocial Development

Erikson's Stages of Psychosocial Development is a theory by psychologist and psychoanalyst Erik Erikson, expanding upon Freud's stages with an emphasis on social dynamics. The theory posits eight sequential stages influenced by biological, psychological, and social factors throughout the lifespan, impacting fields such as gerontology, personality development, identity formation, and life cycle development. Each stage is characterized by opposing tendencies—positive (syntonic) and negative (dystonic)—with successful navigation leading to ego strength or virtue crucial for later stages. Adolescence marks a critical period of identity formation essential for healthy adult functioning. Resolving developmental challenges early can positively impact lifelong psychosocial milestones, though those who struggle initially may catch up through life experiences like relationships and parenthood.
