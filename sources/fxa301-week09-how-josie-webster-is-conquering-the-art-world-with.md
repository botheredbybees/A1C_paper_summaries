## How Josie Webster is conquering the art world with exhibitions

The article highlights the story of Josie Webster, a young Australian artist with Down syndrome who has overcome discrimination and is now making waves in the international art world. After being excluded from her high school dance due to her disability, Josie turned to painting as an outlet for self-expression. Her work gained significant attention when it was selected for an online platform called Heart & Sold, which features artists with Down syndrome. Notably, she became the first Australian artist featured on this platform. Despite having only been painting for a year, Josie's oil paintings are now being considered for exhibitions in London and New York. Her mother credits art as a transformative medium that has helped Josie find her voice and gain recognition beyond the local community. This story underscores how creative arts can empower individuals with disabilities to challenge societal norms and achieve personal success.
