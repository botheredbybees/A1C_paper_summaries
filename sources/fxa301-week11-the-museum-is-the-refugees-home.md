## The Museum Is the Refugee's Home

This article discusses a significant exhibition at the Phillips Collection in Washington titled 'The Warmth of Other Suns,' curated by Massimiliano Gioni and Natalie Bell, which explores art created by refugees and their impact on modern culture over the past century. The exhibition includes works by artists such as Arshile Gorky, Mark Rothko, Vija Celmins, Mona Hatoum, and Danh Vo, who have themselves been refugees or experienced displacement. It highlights how refugee experiences have shaped Western art and literature significantly since ancient times, including through the works of philosophers like Hannah Arendt and Edward Said. The exhibition aims to provide a multifaceted view of migration, from direct depictions of contemporary crises to more abstract expressions by artists who were once refugees themselves.
