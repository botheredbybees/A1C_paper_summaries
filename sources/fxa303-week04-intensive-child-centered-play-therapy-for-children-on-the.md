## Intensive Child-Centered Play Therapy for Children on the Autism Spectrum: A Pilot Study

This study explores the effectiveness of intensive child-centered play therapy (CCPT) for children diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The research team randomly assigned 23 children, divided into an intervention group receiving CCPT and a control group without intervention. Participants who underwent 24 sessions of CCPT showed significant reductions in core ASD symptoms as well as behavioral issues like externalizing problems, attention difficulties, and aggression compared to the control group. The study emphasizes that early comprehensive interventions are crucial for improving quality of life and adaptive functioning in children with ASD. It highlights the need for approaches that foster self-regulation, choice, and natural play experiences, which can enhance overall well-being and developmental growth.
