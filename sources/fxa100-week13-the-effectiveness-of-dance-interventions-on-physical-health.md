## The Effectiveness of Dance Interventions on Physical Health Outcomes Compared to Other Forms of Physical Activity

This systematic review and meta-analysis examines the effectiveness of structured dance interventions on physical health outcomes compared to other forms of structured exercise programs. The study includes a comprehensive search across multiple databases, resulting in 28 studies that met the inclusion criteria with a total sample size of 1276 participants. Key findings indicate significant improvements in body composition, blood biomarkers, and musculoskeletal function due to dance interventions. Additionally, the review reveals equivalent effects for both interventions concerning cardiovascular function and self-perceived mobility. The research concludes that structured dance can be recommended as an effective alternative form of physical activity for improving various health measures, emphasizing its potential role in enhancing physical health across different life stages.
