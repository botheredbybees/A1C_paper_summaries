## The effect of medical/therapeutic clowns on the playfulness of children with intellectual disabilities

This study explores the impact of medical clowns on the playfulness of children with intellectual disabilities, examining whether a therapeutic clowning intervention can enhance their social and emotional development. The research involved two groups: one receiving weekly interventions for six months, the other for three months. Both groups showed significant improvements in playfulness, but those who received longer interventions demonstrated more substantial gains. Teachers reported additional benefits of clown interaction, including improved mood and engagement. The findings suggest that medical clowns can be a valuable tool in promoting play and social skills in children with intellectual disabilities.
