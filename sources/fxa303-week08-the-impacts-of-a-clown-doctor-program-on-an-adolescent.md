## The Impacts of a Clown Doctor Program on an Adolescent Psychiatric Unit: A Mixed Methods Investigation

This study investigates the impact of a clown doctor program on adolescent psychiatric inpatients, addressing concerns related to ward environment and patient experience. The research was conducted at the Stepping Stones Adolescent Inpatient Psychiatric Unit in Victoria, Australia, involving 77 adolescents aged 13–18 years, 22 staff members, and 11 clown doctors from The Humour Foundation. Custom surveys were used to collect quantitative self-report data and qualitative feedback from participants and observers. Results indicated that the clown doctor interventions significantly enhanced fun levels and positive mood among adolescent patients during sessions. Adolescents reported high levels of enjoyment and interaction with the clown doctors, while staff observed improvements in ward atmosphere and patient engagement. The study suggests promising outcomes for integrating clown doctor programs within adolescent psychiatric units but highlights areas for further development, such as tailoring sessions to developmental needs and refining interactions with mentally ill adolescents. These findings underscore the potential benefits of using humor-based interventions to improve mental health care experiences for young patients.
