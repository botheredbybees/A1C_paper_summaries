## Understanding Behavior: A Psychosocial Approach to Dementia Care

This chapter discusses the complex interplay between behavior and unmet needs in individuals with dementia, emphasizing a psychosocial approach over purely biomedical explanations. It argues that understanding behavior as an attempt to address physical, psychological, and social needs is crucial for effective care. Key theories such as Maslow's hierarchy of needs and Kitwood’s person-centered model are referenced. The chapter highlights the impact of cognitive impairment on communication and environmental factors affecting unmet needs. Physical discomfort, lack of social contact, boredom, depression, and sensory deprivation are identified as primary triggers of problematic behavior. Interventions focused on addressing these needs through psychosocial approaches are proposed to improve quality of life for people with dementia.
