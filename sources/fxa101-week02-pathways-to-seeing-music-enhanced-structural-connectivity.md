## Pathways to seeing music: Enhanced structural connectivity in colored-music synesthesia

This study explores the neural basis of colored-music synesthesia, a form of synesthesia where musical sounds elicit visual color perceptions. Utilizing diffusion tensor imaging (DTI), researchers compared white matter integrity in ten colored-music synesthetes and ten matched non-synesthete controls. Results indicated that synesthetes showed enhanced fractional anisotropy in the right inferior fronto-occipital fasciculus (IFOF), a major pathway connecting visual and auditory association areas with frontal regions. The study suggests that this enhanced white matter connectivity may underpin cross-modal associations, providing new insights into neural mechanisms of sensory integration.
