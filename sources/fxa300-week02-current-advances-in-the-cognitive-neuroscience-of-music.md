## Current Advances in the Cognitive Neuroscience of Music

This article reviews recent advances in the cognitive neuroscience of music, tracing its origins to ancient philosophical inquiries and examining modern neuroimaging techniques. The authors explore various aspects of musical cognition, including perception, structure, laterality effects, cultural influences, emotional processing, expertise, and amusias. Key findings highlight the role of music in informing broader theories of higher-order cognitive processes such as language evolution and emotional communication. The review also addresses debates on the evolutionary origins of music, contrasting views that consider it an incidental byproduct or a fundamental human capacity. This work is crucial for understanding how music interacts with other domains of cognition and its implications for neuroscientific theory.
