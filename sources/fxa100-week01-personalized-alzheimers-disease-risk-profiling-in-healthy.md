## Personalized Alzheimer’s disease risk profiling in healthy middle-aged individuals: The Cedars-Sinai Memory & Healthy Aging Program

This study presents the findings from a clinical and research program called the Cedars-Sinai Memory & Healthy Aging Program (MHAP), which aims to promote brain health through personalized risk profiling among asymptomatic adults at risk of Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias (ADRD). The study describes the demographics and risk profiles of 64 participants aged between 40 and 87 years who were part of this program during its first year. The majority of participants were female, non-Hispanic white or Asian, and highly educated. Key modifiable ADRD risk factors identified included sleep disorders, family history of ADRD in a first-degree relative, low physical activity levels, low socialization levels, obstructive sleep apnea, elevated LDL cholesterol, and poor MIND diet scores. Additionally, high blood pressure, a history of traumatic brain injury, and depressive mood were also prevalent among the participants. The Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA) results showed average scores indicating difficulties with delayed recall, language, visuospatial/executive function, orientation, and attention. These findings suggest that personalized risk profiling can effectively identify modifiable factors contributing to ADRD risk, providing a foundation for future research on mitigating these risks.
