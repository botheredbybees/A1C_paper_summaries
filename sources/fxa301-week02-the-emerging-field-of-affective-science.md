## The Emerging Field of Affective Science

This commentary discusses the emergence and growth of affective science, a new interdisciplinary field focused on studying emotions, moods, preferences, attitudes, values, and stress. The authors trace its origins back to the 1980s and highlight the surge in research and publications since then. They emphasize that affect is central to understanding various mental and physical phenomena, such as mental illness, health, resilience, immune function, memory, marketing, interpersonal relationships, decision-making, politics, aesthetics, and personality. The importance of affective science for translational research is also noted, particularly in the context of public health initiatives and mental health. The commentary concludes by noting that this field represents an opportunity to integrate knowledge across disciplines and potentially address some of psychology's most challenging questions.
