## Validation of the Chinese Translation of the 6-item De Jong Gierveld Loneliness Scale in Elderly Chinese

This study validates the Chinese translation of the 6-item De Jong Gierveld Loneliness Scale for use among elderly Chinese in Hong Kong. The scale, originally developed to measure emotional and social loneliness based on Weiss’s distinction, was translated into formal Chinese through a rigorous process including back-translation and expert review. The research involved 103 community-dwelling elders aged 60 years or older with no dementia at baseline. Results showed high content validity as confirmed by the Delphi panel, good internal consistency (Cronbach’s α = 0.76), and significant correlations between loneliness scores and several clinical variables such as depression in dementia, smoking status, and certain social characteristics. The study concludes that the Chinese version of the scale is a reliable and valid measure for assessing loneliness among elderly Chinese.
