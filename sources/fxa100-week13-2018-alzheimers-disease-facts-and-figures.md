## 2018 Alzheimer's disease facts and figures

The 2018 Alzheimer’s disease facts and figures report, published by the Alzheimer's Association, provides an extensive overview of the public health impact of Alzheimer’s disease (AD) in the United States. Key findings include that approximately 5.7 million Americans are living with Alzheimer’s dementia as of 2018, with projections suggesting this number could rise to nearly 14 million by mid-century due to demographic changes. The report highlights significant morbidity and mortality associated with AD, noting it is the sixth leading cause of death in the U.S., particularly for those aged 65 years or older. Additionally, the economic burden of caring for individuals with Alzheimer’s and other dementias includes a substantial cost borne by Medicare and Medicaid systems, as well as significant emotional and health impacts on family caregivers who provide unpaid care amounting to billions of hours annually. The report also addresses diagnostic advances, emphasizing early diagnosis via biomarkers can identify AD before the dementia stage begins, potentially offering personal and financial benefits through reduced healthcare costs.
