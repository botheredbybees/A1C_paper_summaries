## Galen’s Reading of the Hippocratic Treatise ‘The Nature of Man’

The chapter explores Galen’s commentary on the Hippocratic treatise ‘The Nature of Man’, emphasizing his role in disseminating and interpreting Hippocratic medicine, particularly its humoral theory involving blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile. Jouanna notes that Galen's extensive writings made him instrumental in spreading Hippocratic works, including detailed commentaries on these texts. The chapter highlights the importance of a critical edition for understanding this commentary, as it is essential for reconstructing the original Hippocratic text and appreciating Galen’s philosophical interpretations. Key aspects covered include the structure and technique of Galen's commentary, which involves alternating between excerpts from ‘The Nature of Man’ and his own explanatory comments. Jouanna also discusses the methodological challenges in reconstructing Hippocratic texts through Galenic commentaries, indicating that this approach is well-documented in both Arabic and Greek scholarly traditions.
