## The effects of reminiscence therapy on psychological well-being, depression, and loneliness among the institutionalized aged

This study investigates the effects of reminiscence therapy on psychological well-being, depression, and loneliness among institutionalized elderly individuals in Taiwan. The research utilized a randomized experimental design with 92 participants aged 65 years and over who were divided into an experimental group receiving eight sessions of reminiscence therapy over two months and a control group. Findings indicated significant positive short-term effects on depression, psychological well-being, and loneliness among the experimental group compared to the control group after three months follow-up. The study concludes that reminiscence therapy improves socialization, enhances feelings of accomplishment, and helps alleviate depression in elderly individuals.
