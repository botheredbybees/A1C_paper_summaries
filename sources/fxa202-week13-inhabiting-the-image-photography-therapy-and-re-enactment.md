## Inhabiting the Image: Photography, Therapy and Re-enactment Phototherapy

Rosy Martin's paper 'Inhabiting the Image: Photography, Therapy and Re-enactment Phototherapy' explores the therapeutic potential of photography within a psychotherapy context. The article discusses how photographs offer a slippery surface of meanings and narrative possibilities that can be exploited for personal exploration and transformation in therapy sessions. It contrasts the traditional family album as a repository of partially explored memories with its role in constructing an idealized mythology. Martin introduces re-enactment phototherapy, which involves creating new photographic representations through performative re-enactments within the therapeutic relationship. The paper also delves into theories of gaze and identity formation, highlighting the importance of embodiment and transformation within the therapy process. A case study is included to illustrate how this approach can be used in clinical practice. This work underscores the significance of visual culture in creative arts therapies, emphasizing the transformative potential of photographs as a therapeutic medium.
