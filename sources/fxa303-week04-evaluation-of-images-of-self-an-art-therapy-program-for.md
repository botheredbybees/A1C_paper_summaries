## Evaluation of 'Images of Self,' an Art Therapy Program for Children Diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD)

This study evaluates 'Images of Self,' an art therapy program designed for children diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD). The program, consisting of fifteen weekly sessions, aims to address difficulties in sense of self, emotion regulation, flexibility, and social behavior through the use of art-making activities. The evaluation involves a mixed-methods design using repeated single case studies over 12 participants. Various behavioral scales were utilized by parents, teachers, and therapists to assess changes pre- and post-intervention. Results indicate improvements in flexible and social behaviors among children, with overall high satisfaction ratings from the stakeholders involved. The study highlights the potential of art therapy in supporting emotional and behavioral development for children with ASD.
