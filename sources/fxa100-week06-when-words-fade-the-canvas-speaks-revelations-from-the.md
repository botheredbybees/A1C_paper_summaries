## When Words Fade, the Canvas Speaks: Revelations from the Science of Art and the Brain

The document 'When Words Fade, the Canvas Speaks: Revelations from the Science of Art and the Brain' explores how creative expression can serve as a vital communication tool for individuals experiencing dementia. It delves into several key insights derived from neurological research: creativity is supported by distributed neural networks rather than isolated brain centers; visual creativity often intensifies even when language abilities decline, as seen in the case of Anne Adams who developed transmodal creativity post-Primary Progressive Aphasia diagnosis; and vision problems associated with dementia are more likely to be due to brain issues rather than eye pathology. Additionally, it highlights that aesthetic experiences activate the brain's reward system, providing pleasure and emotional connection even when factual memories fade. The narrative concludes by emphasizing art’s role in facilitating an 'abstract poetic mode' of communication that bypasses traditional verbal barriers, allowing for profound engagement between patients and caregivers.
