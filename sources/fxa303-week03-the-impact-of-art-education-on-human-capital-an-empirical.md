## The Impact of Art-Education on Human Capital: An Empirical Assessment of a Youth Orchestra

This paper explores the impact of participating in a youth orchestra on cognitive and socio-emotional development among at-risk students in Curanilahue, Chile. The study evaluates an intervention by analyzing data from participants of the Curanilahue Youth Orchestra (CYO) over nine years. It finds positive effects on academic performance, particularly in language and mathematics, as measured by standardized tests similar to SATs. Moreover, it observes a progressive increase in test scores for students who participated multiple times, indicating improved socio-emotional skills such as orientation and persistence towards personal goals. The research addresses existing gaps in causal evidence linking extracurricular arts activities with educational outcomes, suggesting that these programs may have significant benefits beyond traditional schooling methods.
