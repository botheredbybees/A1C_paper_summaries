## Evaluating and Establishing National Norms for Mental Wellbeing Using the Short Warwick–Edinburgh Mental Well-being Scale (SWEMWBS): Findings from the Health Survey for England

The study evaluates the Short Warwick–Edinburgh Mental Well-being Scale (SWEMWBS) for measuring mental wellbeing in England, using data from the Health Survey for England conducted between 2010 and 2013. The research establishes national norms and assesses the scale's reliability by comparing it to the full version of the Warwick–Edinburgh Mental Well-being Scale (WEMWBS). Key findings include a mean score for SWEMWBS of 23.7 for men and 23.2 for women, indicating similar performance across genders. The study also examined correlations with other health indices such as GHQ-12, happiness index, EQ-VAS, and found moderate to strong associations. It further revealed that unhealthy behaviors like smoking and low fruit/vegetable intake were associated with lower mental wellbeing scores, while obesity was linked to higher scores at the low end of the scale but not at the high end. The study concludes that SWEMWBS effectively captures differences in mental wellbeing across demographic subgroups, though it shows less sensitivity to gender disparities compared to WEMWBS.
