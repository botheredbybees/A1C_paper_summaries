## John Iversen's Research on Music and Brain Function

The webpage features John Iversen, a cognitive neuroscientist exploring the intersection of music and brain function at UC San Diego's Institute for Neural Computation and Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience. His research delves into how rhythm perception influences our understanding of general brain functions, cultural impacts on perception, auditory senses' role in rhythm, and motor systems’ contribution to shaping perceptions. Iversen directs the SIMPHONY project studying music training effects on children's cognitive development and leads mobile brain-body imaging (MoBI) studies for real-world neuroscience applications, including human navigation and complex skill learning.
