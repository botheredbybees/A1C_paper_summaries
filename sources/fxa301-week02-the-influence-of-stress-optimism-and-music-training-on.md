## The Influence of Stress, Optimism, and Music Training on Music Uses and Preferences

The study explores the relationship between stress, optimism, and music training on individuals' motivations for listening to music and their musical preferences. Participants completed surveys measuring these variables alongside uses of music (emotional regulation and cognitive stimulation) and music preferences. The findings reveal that high stress levels correlate with using music for emotional regulation, whereas optimistic individuals also tend to use music emotionally despite being positively correlated with lower stress levels. Individuals with more extensive music training exhibit a preference for listening to music for cognitive reasons rather than emotional regulation, despite having higher stress ratings and lower optimism overall. These results shed light on the complex interplay between personal characteristics and musical behaviors, highlighting individual differences in how people engage with and perceive music's role in their lives.
