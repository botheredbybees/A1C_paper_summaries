## Summary of the evidence on modifiable risk factors for cognitive decline and dementia: A population-based perspective

This article provides a summary of the evidence on modifiable risk factors for cognitive decline and dementia from a population-based perspective. With an estimated 47 million people living with dementia in 2015, and projections indicating that this number will triple by 2050, there is a pressing need to identify ways to reduce the risk of developing dementia. The Alzheimer's Association evaluated existing literature on modifiable risk factors such as regular physical activity, cardiovascular risk factor management (including diabetes, obesity, smoking, and hypertension), healthy diet, and lifelong learning/cognitive training. From this evaluation, it was concluded that there is strong evidence linking these factors to reduced risks of cognitive decline and possibly dementia. The report emphasizes the importance of considering population health rather than clinical interventions when assessing modifiable risk factors.
