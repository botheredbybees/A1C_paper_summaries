## Sing Your Heart Out: community singing as part of mental health recovery

This paper evaluates a community singing initiative called 'Sing Your Heart Out' (SYHO) in Norfolk, UK, aimed at people with mental health conditions and the general public. The study found that SYHO significantly improves participants' mental well-being by providing an inclusive social environment where individuals can engage in group singing without pressure to discuss their mental health issues or receive explicit therapy. Participants reported significant improvements in mental health and stability due to regular attendance, highlighting the unique combination of singing with a supportive social component as crucial for recovery. The study underscores SYHO's potential as a low-commitment, low-cost method for promoting psychological well-being within communities.
