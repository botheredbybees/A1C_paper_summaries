## Body Talk: Examining a Collaborative Multiple-Visit Program for Visitors with Eating Disorders

The article 'Body Talk: Examining a Collaborative Multiple-Visit Program for Visitors with Eating Disorders' by Gwen Baddeley, Laura Evans, Marilyn Lajeunesse, and Stephen Legari explores the Sharing the Douglas/Sharing the Museum program at the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts (MMFA). This initiative is a partnership between the MMFA, the Douglas Mental Health Institute, and Concordia University's Department of Creative Arts Therapies. The program aims to offer a safe environment for individuals undergoing treatment for eating disorders by engaging them in museum tours followed by art-making sessions facilitated by certified art therapists. The study highlights the challenges and best practices involved in creating such programs, emphasizing the therapeutic benefits of combining museum education with art therapy. By providing an unthreatening space for self-discovery and fostering a positive body image through interactions with art, the program addresses a significant social issue affecting diverse populations.
