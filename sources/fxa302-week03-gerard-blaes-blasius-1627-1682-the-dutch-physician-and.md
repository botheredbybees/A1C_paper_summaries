## Gerard Blaes (Blasius) (1627-1682): The Dutch Physician and Chemist, His Work and Description of the Spinal Cord

This historical review examines the work of Gerard Blaes (Blasius), a Dutch physician, chemist, and anatomist who lived from 1627 to 1682. The document provides an extensive biography and career summary of Blasius, highlighting his significant contributions to anatomy, particularly in the field of neuroanatomy. Notable among these achievements is Blasius’s detailed description of the spinal cord, including its gray and white matter and nerve roots, which advanced understanding of this complex structure during the 17th century. Despite limited technological resources at the time, Blasius's meticulous observations and innovative descriptions marked a substantial advancement in anatomical knowledge. His work on comparative anatomy through both human and animal studies also underscores his pioneering role in this field.
