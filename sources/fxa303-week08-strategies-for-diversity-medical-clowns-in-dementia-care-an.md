## Strategies for diversity: medical clowns in dementia care - an ethnographic study

This study, conducted by Margareta Rämgård, Elisabeth Carlson, and Elisabeth Mangrio, explores the interaction strategies employed by medical clowns in culturally diverse dementia care settings. Through ethnographic observation over a ten-week period, the researchers identified that medical clowns interact with residents of nursing homes by being attentive to each individual’s unique life history and cultural background. The clowns used sensory triggers, encouragement, and confirmation to bond socially with residents while paying attention to significant places and habits from their past. Additionally, they contributed to joint interaction in common spaces using music and drama. The findings suggest that both verbal and non-verbal cultural content affected social interactions positively. This approach not only strengthened individuals' sense of self but also fostered a sense of community among the residents. The study highlights the potential for medical clowns’ strategies to improve communication and enhance social interaction in diverse nursing home settings, offering implications for improving care practices.
