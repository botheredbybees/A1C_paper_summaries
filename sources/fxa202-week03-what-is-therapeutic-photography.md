## What is Therapeutic Photography?

Chapter 1 of 'Therapeutic Photography: Enhancing Self-Esteem, Self-Efficacy and Resilience' by Neil Gibson introduces the concept of therapeutic photography. It explores how photography can be used to achieve intended outcomes that benefit users rather than just providing a recreational activity. The chapter traces the history of therapeutic photography back to Hugh Welch Diamond in the 1850s who employed photography at the Surrey County Lunatic Asylum, though the actual therapeutic benefits were questionable. Subsequently, the use of photography as therapy has evolved to include various applications such as recovery from injuries and trauma, physical and psychiatric illnesses, and personal development for individuals dealing with disabilities or terminal illness. The chapter emphasizes that therapeutic photography does not require formal qualifications in counseling or psychotherapy but can be used effectively by anyone due to its democratized nature facilitated by advancements in technology. Additionally, it discusses how photographs can communicate emotions and information non-verbally, enhancing self-reflection and cultural understanding.
