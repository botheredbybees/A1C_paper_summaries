## Pubertal hormones organize the adolescent brain and behavior

This review paper explores the relationship between pubertal hormones and their impact on adolescent brain development and behavior. The authors discuss how puberty, marked by elevated levels of gonadal steroid hormones, triggers significant changes in neural circuits during adolescence, influencing adult behaviors and risks for sex-biased psychopathologies. They argue that this period is crucial for the organizational effects of these hormones, which shape the nervous system permanently beyond initial hormone exposure. The paper synthesizes evidence showing how pubertal hormonal fluctuations sculpt brain structures, affecting decision-making strategies and social interactions in adulthood. It concludes with a discussion on whether adolescence represents a sensitive window for such hormonal influence, impacting individual behavioral trajectories.
