## Blindness in Ancient Greece and the case of Homer

This lesson explores ancient Greek attitudes towards disability and blindness through historical texts and literature. It highlights how Greek society evolved to view disabilities more humanely compared to other ancient civilizations, with legal codes supporting the care of disabled individuals. The influence of Hippocrates is noted in shifting perceptions from divine punishment to natural causes. However, literary works like Sophocles' Oedipus Rex use blindness as a metaphor for insight and truth beyond physical sight. Homer's depiction as a blind bard symbolizes visionary understanding, reinforcing the idea that loss of physical vision can bring spiritual or philosophical enlightenment.
