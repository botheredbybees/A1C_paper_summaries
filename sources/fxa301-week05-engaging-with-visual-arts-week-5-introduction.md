## Engaging with Visual Arts: Week 5 Introduction

The document outlines the introduction to Week 5 in the course 'FXA301 Arts in the Community' at the University of Tasmania. Dr Brendan Lamb discusses case studies of community-based visual arts projects that aim to foster social cohesion and engagement, with some also focusing on enhancing health and well-being. The text delves into historical contexts, highlighting the role of visual arts from prehistoric times through Renaissance individualism to modern therapeutic applications. It specifically mentions Kemp Prossor's pioneering work in using color therapy for World War I veterans suffering from 'shell-shock' (now recognized as PTSD) and Roy de Meistre’s subsequent efforts in Australia. These early initiatives paved the way for contemporary hospital arts programs, emphasizing the beneficial impact of visual aesthetics on patient recovery and well-being. The document sets out learning objectives for evaluating the effects of such projects on community health and social outcomes.
