## Washing Dishes to Wash the Dishes: Brief Instruction in an Informal Mindfulness Practice

This study examines whether washing dishes can serve as an informal mindfulness practice, promoting a mindful state and influencing emotional and attentional phenomena. Participants were divided into two groups: one received instructions for mindful dishwashing while the other engaged in a control condition without specific guidance. The results indicated that those who practiced mindful dishwashing showed greater levels of mindfulness, positive affect (specifically inspiration), reduced negative affect (particularly nervousness), and perceived an overestimation of time spent on the task compared to controls. These findings suggest potential benefits for well-being and emotional regulation through informal daily activities such as washing dishes.
