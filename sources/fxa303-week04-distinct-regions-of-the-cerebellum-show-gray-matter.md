## Distinct regions of the cerebellum show gray matter decreases in autism, ADHD, and developmental dyslexia

This research article conducts an anatomical likelihood estimate (ALE) meta-analysis on voxel-based morphometry (VBM) studies to identify cerebellar regions affected in autism spectrum disorder (ASD), attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), and developmental dyslexia. The study reveals that distinct cerebellar regions show gray matter decreases specific to each condition, indicating unique neuroanatomical signatures associated with ASD, ADHD, and dyslexia. For ASD, the findings include reduced gray matter in lobules IX, left VIIIB, and right Crus I; for ADHD, decreased gray matter is bilaterally present in lobule IX; while for developmental dyslexia, it involves a decrease in left lobule VI. The results suggest that cerebellar anomalies do not uniformly affect the same regions across these disorders but rather are disorder-specific, implicating different neural networks relevant to each condition's characteristic symptoms.
