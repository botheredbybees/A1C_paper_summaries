## DANCE MOVEMENT THERAPY IMPROVES EMOTIONAL RESPONSES AND MODULATES NEUROHORMONES IN ADOLESCENTS WITH MILD DEPRESSION

This study evaluated the impact of dance movement therapy (DMT) on emotional responses and neurohormones in adolescents with mild depression. Forty middle school seniors were randomly assigned to either a DMT group or a control group over twelve weeks. The DMT sessions included themes focused on body awareness, movement expressions, integration of feelings, among others. The study found significant reductions in psychological distress scores and improvements in emotional stability for the participants undergoing DMT. Additionally, the therapy resulted in increased plasma serotonin levels and decreased dopamine levels, suggesting a stabilization of the sympathetic nervous system. These findings indicate that DMT may be an effective non-pharmacological intervention to improve psychological health and neurohormonal balance in adolescents with mild depression.
