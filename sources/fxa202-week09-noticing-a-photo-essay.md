## Noticing: A Photo Essay

Louise Raftery's photo essay 'Noticing' explores the therapeutic value of paying attention to small details in everyday experiences and surroundings, particularly during periods of overwhelming sadness. By focusing on tangible sensations such as a husband's hug, the delicate blossoms of an apricot tree, or the tactile experience of mixing brownie batter, Raftery highlights how these observations can provide comfort and a sense of hope amidst difficult emotional states. The essay suggests that engaging with one’s environment through detailed observation serves not only to distract from negative feelings but also to find beauty in the ordinary aspects of life, contributing positively to mental well-being. This approach is relevant to creative arts and health practices, emphasizing the role of sensory engagement in healing and personal growth.
