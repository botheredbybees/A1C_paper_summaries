## Play therapy in children with autism: Its role, implications, and limitations

This article explores the role and implications of play therapy in children with autism, emphasizing its potential benefits despite limited evidence. Play is a crucial activity for children's development, promoting skills such as negotiation, problem-solving, and social interaction. Children with autism often exhibit limitations in adaptive behavior and social engagement. The review highlights how play therapy can help these children engage in activities of interest, express themselves more comfortably, and shift from unwanted behaviors to non-injurious forms of expression. Various types of play therapy are discussed, including sensory integration, art-play, equine-partnered, child-centered, and synergistic therapies. Proper evaluation is crucial for selecting the most appropriate type of intervention tailored to each child's unique needs.
