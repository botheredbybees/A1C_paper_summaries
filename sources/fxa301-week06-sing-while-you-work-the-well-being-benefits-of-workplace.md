## Sing while you work - the well-being benefits of workplace choirs

This report evaluates the health and wellbeing benefits of participating in a workplace choir for Health Service Executive (HSE) staff members. The study is the first of its kind in Ireland and one of few internationally that focuses on HSE workers specifically. It employs both quantitative and qualitative methods over a year-long period to assess various aspects such as work engagement, stress levels, resilience, and absenteeism.

Key findings indicate positive outcomes for participants including improved mental health perception and reduced depression levels, although the measurement challenges are acknowledged due to limited quantitative evidence in this field. Qualitative data also supports previous research by confirming that choir participation fosters social connectedness, enjoyment at work, increased engagement, stress reduction, and enhanced perceptions of overall health and wellbeing.

The report highlights the importance of professional choir directors for successful implementation and suggests that while workplace choirs offer significant benefits with low risk and cost, they might cater primarily to certain demographics (e.g., white Irish staff). Recommendations include further research to understand benefits for different cultural backgrounds and educational levels within HSE staff.
