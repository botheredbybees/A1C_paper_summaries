## Other Programs Inside and Outside of Galleries

This lesson explores various programs designed to engage individuals with dementia through art and cultural activities in galleries, museums, outdoor spaces, and photographic exhibitions. Programs highlighted include the Art Gallery of New South Wales' expanded 'Art and Dementia Program', Museum Victoria's mobile outreach service and specific collections like 'Memories of Childhood' and 'The Good Old Days'. Additionally, it covers UK initiatives such as the 'Heritage in Health' study by University College London, the Liverpool Museum's House of Memories project, and outdoor art installations at places like Yorkshire Sculpture Park. The lesson also mentions hybrid models from the Museum of Contemporary Art (MCA) in Sydney, focusing on combining gallery visits with home-based activities. It discusses opportunities for engagement through photographic images, highlighting programs like Australian Geographic Photographer of the Year exhibitions. Lastly, it touches upon Indigenous Australian communities' response to dementia through arts therapies, exemplified by the Milpara Art Mob's photographic project.
