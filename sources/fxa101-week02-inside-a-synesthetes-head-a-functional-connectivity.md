## Inside a Synesthete’s Head: A Functional Connectivity Analysis with Grapheme-Color Synesthetes

This study explores the brain regions and connectivity patterns involved in grapheme-color synesthesia, a condition where individuals perceive letters with associated colors. Using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) on 18 grapheme-color synesthetes and an equal number of control subjects, the research identifies specific activation differences between these groups, particularly in the inferior parietal lobule (IPL). The study highlights increased connectivity in synesthetes between the left IPL and primary/secondary visual areas, suggesting a more complex neural network involved in this form of synesthesia than previously understood. This finding challenges existing models that focus primarily on V4 and the fusiform cortex, indicating instead a broader involvement of parietal structures and a potential modulation at the level of the primary visual cortex.
