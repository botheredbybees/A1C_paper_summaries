## Brain research shows the arts promote mental health

This article discusses the positive impact of engaging in arts activities on mental health, supported by recent brain research. The author, an associate professor and arts educator with over two decades of experience, argues that participating in artistic pursuits can contribute to better emotional well-being and help manage stress. Research within neuroesthetics—a field studying the neurological basis of aesthetic experiences—indicates that engaging in arts activates different parts of the brain, inducing positive mental states and reducing cortisol levels, which are markers for stress. Additionally, arts-based activities promote mindfulness and flow states, enhancing creativity and attention while fostering a sense of well-being. The article emphasizes the importance of integrating arts into education systems to support students' mental health and cognitive development, suggesting that prioritizing the arts could be beneficial in addressing the current mental health crisis among young people.
