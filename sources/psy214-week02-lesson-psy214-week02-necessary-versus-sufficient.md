## Combined Models of Well-being: PERMA and Complete Mental Health

This lesson explores the PERMA model developed by Martin Seligman for well-being, integrating both hedonic and eudaimonic elements. It includes positive emotion, engagement, relationships, meaning, and accomplishment. The model is critiqued for potential cultural bias and its focus on positive emotions without acknowledging negative ones. Additionally, it introduces Corey Keyes' complete mental health model, which comprises emotional, psychological, and social well-being dimensions. This dual continua model emphasizes flourishing (high well-being with no illness) and languishing (low on both). The lesson also touches upon the cultural bias criticism of these models.
