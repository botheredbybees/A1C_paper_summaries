## PLAY THERAPY: Considerations and Applications for the Practitioner

The article 'Play Therapy: Considerations and Applications for the Practitioner' by Ritesh Kool, MD, and Timothy Lawver, DO, explores the theoretical foundations, practical considerations, and current research on play therapy. The authors review Piaget's theories about cognitive development stages in children, emphasizing that symbolic play is a crucial stage in emotional regulation and communication. They discuss Virginia Axline’s approach to play therapy, which balances symbolic play with language expression appropriate for the child's age. Key criteria for effective play therapy include establishing therapeutic relationships, diagnostic opportunities, breaking down defense mechanisms, facilitating articulation, providing therapeutic release, and anticipatory preparation. The article also outlines cognitive, affective, and interpersonal processes that contribute to lasting change in play therapy, such as schema transformation, emotional regulation, and corrective relationship building. Practical aspects of implementing play therapy are covered, including assessment strategies, the role of limit setting, and considerations for session frequency and duration. This review highlights the importance of play therapy for young children who may not be able to express themselves verbally but can communicate through play.
