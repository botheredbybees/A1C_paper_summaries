## The Use of Creative Activities in Dementia in Residential Aged Care Facilities in Australia: A Cross-Sectional Descriptive Study

This research examines the role and impact of creative activities for individuals with dementia residing in Australian residential aged care facilities. The study, employing a mix of quantitative and qualitative methods, aims to uncover the frequency and characteristics of these activities, as well as their efficacy in improving quality of life and managing Behavioural and Psychological Symptoms of Dementia (BPSD). Through an online survey conducted among 1675 facilities, it was found that over half reported offering music, visual arts, dance, or drama. Interviews with activity coordinators highlighted the importance of creativity for self-expression, social interaction, and cognitive engagement but also revealed inconsistent understanding regarding its implementation. The study concludes by suggesting that a more comprehensive investigation is needed to fully grasp the implications and benefits of creative activities in dementia care.
