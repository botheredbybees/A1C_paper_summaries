## Art Therapy for Individuals With Traumatic Brain Injury: A Comprehensive Neurorehabilitation-Informed Approach to Treatment

Tori Kline’s article discusses an art therapy approach tailored for individuals suffering from traumatic brain injury (TBI), emphasizing its integration into comprehensive neurorehabilitation practices. The author outlines the significance of TBI, noting that nearly 1.7 million people annually are diagnosed with this condition in the United States, leading to various physical, cognitive, behavioral, and emotional impairments. Kline advocates for a holistic approach to rehabilitation, which includes individualized treatment plans and supports clients' reintegration into their communities.

The article highlights how art therapy can be instrumental in enhancing patients’ quality of life by offering intrinsic motivation, encouraging self-exploration, and fostering a sense of control and identity reconstruction. It underscores the use of creative arts to help individuals redefine their identities post-injury, regain confidence, and engage meaningfully in future activities. Kline also details how art therapy can facilitate emotional expression, enhance perceived control, and build social support networks.

The article’s relevance lies in its contribution to the growing body of knowledge on therapeutic interventions for TBI patients, underscoring the need for personalized, interdisciplinary care that leverages neuroplasticity principles. It provides a framework for art therapists working within comprehensive rehabilitation settings, emphasizing the value of integrating artistic expression as part of an integrative treatment strategy.
