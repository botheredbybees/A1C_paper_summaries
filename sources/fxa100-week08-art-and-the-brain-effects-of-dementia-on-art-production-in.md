## Art and the Brain: Effects of Dementia on Art Production in Art Therapy

The article explores the impact of various types of dementia on art production in individuals undergoing art therapy, integrating perspectives from neurology and studio art therapy. The study discusses brain areas involved in art creation and how different dementias affect artistic expression based on localized brain damage. It examines cases where artists with specific neurological conditions, such as Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia (FTD), exhibit changes in their artwork over time. For example, individuals with FTD may develop new artistic skills due to preserved right posterior visual systems despite language decline, while those with Alzheimer’s disease tend to produce increasingly simplistic art as their visuospatial and organizational abilities deteriorate. The case of an artist diagnosed with corticobasal degeneration (CBD) is also detailed, highlighting the shift towards distorted images and uneven paint application as a result of brain lesions affecting motor control and visual processing. This interdisciplinary approach aims to inform therapeutic interventions tailored to neurological conditions, enhancing patient care in art therapy settings.
