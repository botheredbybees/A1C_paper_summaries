## Refugee Youth and Migration: Using Arts-Informed Research to Understand Changes in Their Roles and Responsibilities

The article explores the changes in roles and responsibilities of refugee youth from Afghan, Karen, and Sudanese communities who have resettled in Canada through an arts-informed research method that combines focus group discussions with drawing activities. The study reveals that these youths experience significant shifts in their familial duties, often taking on additional tasks such as translation, financial support, emotional care, and household management due to the challenges of migration. Utilizing visual methods alongside traditional qualitative techniques enriches understanding by capturing tacit knowledge and emotional dimensions not typically accessible through text alone. The research highlights the importance of employing creative methodologies in studying vulnerable populations to more accurately reflect their complex experiences.
