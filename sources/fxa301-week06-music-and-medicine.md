## Music and Medicine

This book, 'Music and Medicine,' edited by Gunter Kreutz and Günther Bernatzky, explores the intersection of music with medical practice in areas such as prevention, therapy, rehabilitation, and education. It emphasizes how cultural and neurological studies have shown that artistic disciplines like music significantly influence health and wellbeing through emotional engagement and cognitive activation. The book outlines historical perspectives on this relationship, from ancient philosophical understandings to modern scientific evidence linking neuroanatomy with creativity and psychophysical wellness. Key chapters discuss the socio-cultural, genetic, and neurobiological foundations of music's role in well-being, as well as specific applications like music therapy and physiological effects observed in medical settings. The editors also address epidemiological studies that highlight gene-environment interactions relevant to arts experiences and health outcomes across various population segments, including older adults and working populations.
