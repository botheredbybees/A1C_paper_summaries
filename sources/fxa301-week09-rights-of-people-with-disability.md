## Rights of People with Disability

The document 'Rights of People with Disability' by the Attorney-General's Department provides guidance on the rights and protections afforded to individuals with disabilities in Australia. It highlights that these rights stem from international treaties such as the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), which aims to ensure that people with disabilities can enjoy all human rights without discrimination. The document outlines specific rights covered by CRPD articles, including the right to live independently and participate fully in community life (Article 9) and the obligation to consult and involve people with disabilities in decision-making processes affecting them (Article 4). It also discusses the importance of reasonable accommodation and the role of legislative measures to promote disability inclusion. The text underscores the significance of ensuring accessibility in various settings such as education, healthcare, public facilities, and employment, emphasizing the need for comprehensive legal frameworks and policies that support these rights. This guidance is crucial for agencies and organizations involved in crafting legislation, policies, or programs related to persons with disabilities, underlining the importance of human rights scrutiny in fostering an inclusive society.
