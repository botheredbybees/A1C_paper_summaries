## Executive Summary: Creative Health Review

The document emphasizes the fundamental role of creativity in health and wellbeing, arguing that engaging with creative activities can significantly improve mental and physical health outcomes across various stages of life. The report discusses how creativity supports individuals in managing chronic conditions, reducing loneliness, and fostering community resilience. It highlights successful initiatives like Arts Council Wales' partnership with the NHS Confederation and Integrated Care Systems (ICSs) such as Creative Health Hubs in England that have demonstrated positive impacts on health outcomes and system efficiency. The review calls for a whole-system approach involving policymakers, funders, healthcare providers, and cultural sectors to integrate creative health into broader societal frameworks. It advocates for recognizing creativity as an essential determinant of health, promoting equitable access to creative opportunities, and embedding creative practices within health and social care systems to enhance quality of life and reduce health inequalities.
