## Der Demenz-Clown: Ein Konzept zur Kultivierung von Humor und Heiterkeit in der Demenzpflege

The document discusses the concept of a 'Demenz-Clown' (dementia clown) and its role in fostering humor and laughter among individuals with dementia, particularly within nursing home settings in Switzerland. The study describes an example session conducted by Clown Aurikla at an elderly care facility where she interacts with residents suffering from various stages of dementia through music, touch, and playful engagement. Positive reactions include increased attentiveness, vocal participation, and even verbal acknowledgment from previously unresponsive individuals. Research cited suggests that humor interventions may improve mood, reduce pain, enhance communication between caregivers and patients, and mitigate challenging behaviors associated with dementia. However, the author notes limited empirical studies on this topic due to methodological challenges in assessing outcomes accurately. The paper emphasizes the potential of integrating humor into everyday care practices as a non-invasive means to enhance quality of life for people living with dementia.
