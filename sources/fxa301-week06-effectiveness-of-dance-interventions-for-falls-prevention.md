## Effectiveness of Dance Interventions for Falls Prevention in Older Adults: Systematic Review and Meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluates the effectiveness of dance interventions for falls prevention in older adults. The study included forty-one studies, encompassing various types of dances such as ballroom, Latin, exercise-based, cultural, therapeutic, and low-impact forms. While these interventions are recognized as enjoyable and sociable exercises, the evidence supporting their efficacy in reducing fall incidence is limited. Meta-analysis of functional outcome measures showed no significant differences between dance intervention groups and control groups, with very low certainty of evidence regarding the impact of dance on falls prevention. The research concludes that more robust randomized controlled trials are necessary to establish the effectiveness and cost-effectiveness of dance interventions specifically aimed at preventing falls.
