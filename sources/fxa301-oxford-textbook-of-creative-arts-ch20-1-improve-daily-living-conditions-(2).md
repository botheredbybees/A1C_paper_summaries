## Social Determinants of Health: Policies for Action

This chapter focuses on the social determinants of health and reviews policies aimed at reducing health inequities through addressing socioeconomic disparities. It discusses the work of the Commission on Social Determinants of Health (CSDH) which highlighted that unequal distribution of power, money, and resources affects people's life chances and health outcomes. The Marmot Review applied these principles to an English context with six key recommendations. Subsequent reviews by WHO European Region further analyzed evidence from across Europe and proposed measures to ensure better daily living conditions, fair employment, social protection, equitable use of taxes, governance for health equity, and intersectoral responses. Key concepts include the social gradient and life course approach which illustrate how health outcomes are influenced throughout a person's lifespan due to socioeconomic factors.
