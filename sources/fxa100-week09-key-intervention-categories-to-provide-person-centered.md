## Key Intervention Categories to Provide Person-Centered Dementia Care: A Systematic Review of Person-Centered Interventions

The study presents a systematic review of person-centered care (PCC) interventions for individuals with dementia, aiming to categorize these interventions into key categories that guide the provision of PCC in dementia care. The research identifies nine key intervention categories: social contact, physical activities, cognitive training, sensory enhancement, daily living assistance, life history-oriented emotional support, training and support for professional caregivers, environmental adjustments, and organizational changes in care settings. These categories were derived from a review of 19 studies, which included diverse psychosocial interventions often involving multiple components. The study highlights the importance of personalized and relationship-focused approaches in PCC but notes the heterogeneity among existing interventions that complicates defining clear standards for PCC. This research underscores the need for further investigations to evaluate the effectiveness of these interventions under a more explicit focus on relationships, potentially yielding additional benefits.
