## Further evidence that singing fosters mental health and wellbeing: the West Kent and Medway project

This paper investigates the impact of group singing on mental health and wellbeing, replicating a previous study in a new geographical area (West Kent and Medway) to test the transferability of findings. The research involved four community singing groups for individuals with mental health issues over an eight-month period. Participants completed baseline and follow-up questionnaires using CORE-10 and WEMWBS scales. Results showed significant reductions in mental distress and improvements in wellbeing, aligning with outcomes from the earlier East Kent study. While the study underscores the benefits of group singing on mental health, limitations include a small sample size and lack of randomization. The findings contribute to the growing body of research highlighting the therapeutic value of music engagement for individuals facing mental health challenges.
