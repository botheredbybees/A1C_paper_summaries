## Critical reflection of the development of the Finding Joy photo essay

Simone Dalton's critical reflection on her 'Finding Joy' photo essay project examines the resilience and capacity for joy among eight regional Victorian individuals during prolonged pandemic lockdowns. The research aimed to document actions and strategies for maintaining positivity amidst significant health crisis constraints, focusing on social prescribing, mindful photography, and sharing lived experiences. Limitations in mobility led Dalton to use an online survey with questions about definitions of joy, its sources, challenges faced, and advice for others. Despite travel restrictions and a lack of face-to-face access, she successfully engaged diverse participants aged 20s-70s with various interests and philosophies. The project utilized indirect quotes for two sentences and direct quotes for the third sentence in each image description, aiming to give voice to the participants' experiences. Dalton's reflections highlight improvements in her own capacity for joy and the therapeutic benefits of engaging in photo storytelling. Key findings include the importance of capturing lived experience to foster resilience, hope, and coping strategies. The project also demonstrated how visual storytelling can inspire and offer preventive well-being benefits through daily photography activities. Future applications may involve community photo essay projects as a form of social prescribing to promote mental health recovery.
