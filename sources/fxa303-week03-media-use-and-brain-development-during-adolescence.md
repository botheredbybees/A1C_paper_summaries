## Media use and brain development during adolescence

This review article explores the relationship between media use and brain development during adolescence, focusing on how social media impacts emotional and cognitive processes. The authors argue that adolescents are particularly sensitive to acceptance and rejection through social media platforms due to their heightened emotional sensitivity and protracted development of reflective processing and cognitive control. They highlight that this period is marked by extensive changes in the structure and function of brain regions involved in social interactions, making them more susceptible to the influences of media use. The review emphasizes the importance of understanding these mutual influences between media and peers on adolescents' well-being and opinion formation.
