## Emotions During Live Music Performance: Links With Individual Differences in Empathy, Visual Imagery, and Mood

The study examines the emotional responses of participants attending a live performance of Puccini’s 'Madama Butterfly' and their relationship with individual differences in empathy, visual imagery, and mood. The opera elicited a blend of emotions characterized by high sublimity (awe or transcendence), low vitality (lack of excitement or joy), and unease. Higher levels of dispositional empathy were linked to increased sublimity, while positive mood was associated with heightened sublimity and vitality alongside reduced unease. The research supports the idea that empathy and visual imagery are critical mechanisms for emotional responses to music, consistent with findings from laboratory settings.
