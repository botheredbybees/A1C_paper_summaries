## A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials of laughter and humour interventions on depression, anxiety and sleep quality in adults

This meta-analysis quantifies the effect of laughter and humor interventions on depression, anxiety, and sleep quality in adults through a review of randomized controlled trials. The study includes ten studies with a total of 814 participants. Results indicate that these interventions significantly reduce depression and anxiety while improving sleep quality. Subgroup analysis suggests greater benefits for long-term laughter intervention in reducing depression. Conclusions emphasize the effectiveness of humor and laughter interventions, highlighting their potential as safe, convenient, and interesting methods to enhance mental well-being. The study underscores the need for high-quality future research with follow-up assessments.
