## The Largs Pier: A Visual Story of Grief and Healing

Alison Higgins explores her relationship with grief after losing her mother by creating a photographic essay centered around the Largs Pier in South Australia, where she scattered her mother’s ashes. The location holds significant memories for both Higgins and her mother, making it a poignant backdrop to reflect on the intense emotions associated with loss. Higgins discusses how grief can be deeply personal and influenced by factors such as the nature of the relationship with the deceased and individual coping mechanisms. She emphasizes that processing grief is not about moving past it entirely but rather finding ways to integrate the memories and emotions into one’s daily life, thereby creating space for new experiences and growth.

Through her project, Higgins employs photography as a therapeutic tool, engaging with natural surroundings to capture visual metaphors of her emotional journey. The process helps her externalize feelings and provides insights into her grief that verbal expression alone cannot convey. She finds comfort in the environment's sensory aspects and uses different camera techniques to create powerful images that tell a story beyond words. Higgins concludes by reflecting on how this project has been both challenging and therapeutic, enabling her to rebuild self-identity and adjust to life without her mother.
