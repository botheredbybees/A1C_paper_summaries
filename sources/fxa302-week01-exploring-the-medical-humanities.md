## Exploring the medical humanities

The article by Evans and Greaves explores the concept of integrating the humanities into medicine to enrich clinical practice beyond a mere additive perspective that softens traditional bio-medicine with empathetic understanding. The authors introduce an 'integrated' view where the nature, goals, and knowledge base of medical practice are fundamentally shaped by the human experience of suffering, illness, and disability. This perspective suggests that medicine should not only treat physical ailments but also address the experiential aspects of patients’ conditions, recognizing the interplay between material health issues and personal values.

The article underscores the potential benefits of engaging with humanities in medical education and practice, such as enhancing communication skills and fostering a deeper narrative understanding of patient experiences. It argues for the necessity of empirical research to validate these claims and highlights the need for journals like 'Medical Humanities' to facilitate this discourse, aiming to bridge the gap between theoretical discourse and practical application in medical settings. The piece is significant in highlighting how creative arts can play a crucial role in redefining and enhancing the therapeutic relationship and patient care within the broader context of health and wellbeing.
