## Missed Early Intervention Opportunities for Children With Autism Spectrum Disorder

This study investigates the factors associated with early intervention (EI) participation among children diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) before age two, using data from the National Survey of Children's Health (NSCH). The research found that only 15% of preschool-aged children with ASD received EI services by their second birthday. Key predictors for timely EI receipt include an earlier diagnosis of ASD, higher socioeconomic status (SES), and lower birth weight. Additionally, the study highlighted a critical gap in intervention opportunities for disadvantaged families, emphasizing the need to improve early detection mechanisms and reduce barriers to access for these groups. The findings suggest that expanding the criteria for eligibility under Part C of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) could significantly enhance EI participation rates among children at high risk of developmental delays due to ASD.
