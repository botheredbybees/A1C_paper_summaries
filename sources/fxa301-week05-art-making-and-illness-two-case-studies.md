## Art-making and Illness - Two Case Studies

This document presents two case studies examining the impact of art-making on individuals dealing with illness. The first case study, 'Joyce', a 59-year-old woman diagnosed with breast cancer, used her artistic pursuits to navigate emotional and physical challenges related to her diagnosis and treatment. Initially struggling with fears and worries, Joyce found solace in painting and textile work, which helped her manage anxiety and depression symptoms. Her engagement in art allowed for personal growth and a redefinition of self-identity beyond her illness. The second case study focuses on the 'Okoa the Wave Rider' sculpture project at MD Anderson Children's Cancer Hospital, involving around 1300 participants including patients, family members, healthcare professionals, and staff. This collaborative project aimed to create a joyful space for emotional expression and community building, promoting hope and well-being among participants.
