## Assessment of lifetime participation in cognitively stimulating activities

The chapter discusses the concept of cognitive reserve and its relation to neural reserve capacity in older adults, particularly focusing on how lifelong engagement in cognitively stimulating activities can mitigate the risk of dementia and cognitive decline. The authors propose that education is a proxy for lifetime exposure to such activities, which may enhance brain function resilience against neurodegenerative pathologies like Alzheimer's disease. They validate measures of cognitive activity through their correlation with educational attainment and cognitive performance. Longitudinal studies support the hypothesis that higher engagement in cognitively stimulating activities correlates with reduced risk of dementia, suggesting a protective effect on neural reserve capacity.
