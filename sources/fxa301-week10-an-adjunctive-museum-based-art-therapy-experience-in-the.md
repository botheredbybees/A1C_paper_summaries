## An adjunctive, museum-based art therapy experience in the treatment of women with severe eating disorders

This study examines the feasibility and short-term impact of a museum-based art therapy program for women with severe eating disorders. The research involved an educational visit to the Montreal Museum of Fine Arts followed by an art therapy workshop at the Douglas Institute Eating Disorders Program in Canada. Participants, who were adults undergoing specialized outpatient treatments, reported high satisfaction and found the experience enriching and conducive to self-expression. Although the program did not significantly alter body image concerns or eating preoccupations/urges immediately after completion, it positively influenced participants' moods. The study suggests that such museum-based interventions can be a suitable adjunctive therapy for patients with eating disorders.
