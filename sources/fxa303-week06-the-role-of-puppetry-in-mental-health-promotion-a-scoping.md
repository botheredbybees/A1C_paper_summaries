## The Role of Puppetry in Mental Health Promotion: A Scoping Review of Its Efficacy and Applications

This scoping review examines the efficacy and applications of puppetry-based interventions in promoting mental health across various populations, including children, adolescents, and vulnerable groups such as refugees and the elderly. The authors conducted a comprehensive search using the PICOS framework and PRISMA-ScR guidelines, evaluating 30 studies from multiple databases up to August 2024. Key findings indicate significant improvements in emotional regulation, anxiety management, and social interaction, particularly for pediatric populations and educational settings. However, the review highlights critical gaps in the literature, such as a lack of large-scale randomized controlled trials, longitudinal studies, and consistent theoretical frameworks. These limitations underscore the need for more rigorous research to optimize the benefits of puppetry interventions. The study emphasizes the potential of puppetry as a valuable therapeutic tool and suggests future directions for standardized protocols and long-term outcome evaluations.
