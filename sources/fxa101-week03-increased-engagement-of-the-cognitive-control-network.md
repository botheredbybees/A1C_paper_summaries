## Increased engagement of the cognitive control network associated with music training in children during an fMRI Stroop task

This study investigates the impact of music training on executive function in children using fMRI and behavioral tasks, specifically the Color-Word Stroop task. The research compares children with ongoing music training to those involved in sports or no extracurricular activities. During cognitive control trials, children who received musical instruction showed significantly greater bilateral activation in key brain regions such as the pre-Supplementary Motor Area (pre-SMA), Supplementary Motor Area (SMA), Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC), Inferior Frontal Gyrus (IFG), and insula compared to the control group. Despite similar performance on behavioral measures of executive function, these neural differences suggest that music training may influence cognitive control networks in ways not directly observable through standard task performance.
