## Blindness and Literacy in the Lives of Homer

Alexander Beecroft examines the portrayal of Homer as a blind poet in ancient literature and its implications on his perceived literacy. The article challenges prevailing scholarly views that question the possibility of Homer composing epic poetry while being blind, emphasizing instead the need to consider the context of blindness within the ancient world's understanding of literacy. Beecroft argues that, historically, a blind individual would be considered illiterate and unable to use writing in the composition and distribution of literature. This perspective sheds light on the biographical accounts' treatment of Homer’s blindness as an indicator of their views regarding the role of written text versus oral tradition in poetic creation. The article also explores how different ancient texts depict Homer's life, including his transition from a literate teacher to a blind poet who then focuses on oral composition methods.
