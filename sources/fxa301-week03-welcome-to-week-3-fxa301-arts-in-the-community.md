## Welcome to Week 3 - FXA301 Arts in the Community

The document provides an introduction to Week 3 of the FXA301 Arts in the Community course, focusing on how engagement with creative arts can contribute to health and wellbeing. Dr Brendan Lamb discusses the demographic changes across countries that are driving interest in improving health outcomes for aging populations. He highlights initiatives aimed at keeping people active, managing chronic illnesses, and fostering community connections as ways to enhance individual and societal benefits, often within tight economic constraints. The text emphasizes the role of arts engagement in mitigating risk factors associated with poor health outcomes, such as substance abuse and social disadvantage.

The document outlines key learning objectives for the week, including understanding research evidence supporting creative arts engagement, listing benefits for older adults, accessing relevant policy frameworks, and defining 'Arts on Prescription'. It also mentions that while financial considerations are significant drivers of these initiatives, improvements in health outcomes can result in lower healthcare utilization costs. The text underscores the potential of creative arts to provide emotional outlets and build resilience against life challenges, particularly as people age.

The introduction concludes with a preview for the next part of the lesson, which will delve into theoretical foundations supporting community-based arts programs aimed at enhancing various aspects of health and wellbeing.
