## Breaking Bars: Community-Based Art Therapy Mural Project

The article by Elizabeth DelliCarpini discusses a community-based art therapy mural project aimed at engaging young people involved in New York City's justice system to address racial and economic oppression through social bonding and power shifts within the collective community. The project, funded by Artistic Noise and supported by the School of Visual Arts, includes two cycles focused on murals titled 'Good Art: Bad City' and 'Welcome to Our World'. DelliCarpini emphasizes her awareness of social privilege and aims to dismantle white supremacy in practice. She engages in a visceral self-reflection process and positions her role based on recommendations for working with individuals confronting social oppression, fostering alliances and advocating on behalf of the young participants. The project follows best practices by acknowledging power differentials, building trust through community engagement, and applying guiding theories focused on social justice. Through collaborative artmaking, workshops, and public events, the initiative seeks to reduce stigma, increase leadership opportunities, and expand educational discourse.
