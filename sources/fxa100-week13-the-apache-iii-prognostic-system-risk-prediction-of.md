## The APACHE III Prognostic System: Risk Prediction of Hospital Mortality for Critically Ill Hospitalized Adults

The paper introduces the APACHE III system designed to predict hospital mortality risk for critically ill patients admitted to intensive care units (ICUs). Researchers collected data from over 17,440 unselected adult ICU admissions across 40 U.S. hospitals. The study aimed to refine the methodology of the APACHE scoring system by examining factors such as acute physiologic abnormalities, age, pre-existing functional limitations, and treatment location prior to ICU admission. Two main components of the APACHE III system are presented: a score for initial risk stratification within specific patient groups and an equation incorporating disease categories and treatment locations to estimate individual mortality risks. The paper emphasizes that objective risk assessment in ICUs is crucial given high costs and technological advancements, aiming to improve quality assurance and resource management. By monitoring changes in the APACHE III score daily, clinicians can better predict outcomes and make informed decisions regarding patient care and discharge.
