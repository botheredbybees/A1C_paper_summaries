## Ethics in Dementia Care: Storied Lives, Storied Ethics

This chapter explores ethical frameworks for dementia care and the unique challenges they face. It introduces story-based ethics as a way to understand and guide decisions about people with dementia, highlighting the importance of recognizing their life histories and personal narratives. The text discusses the limitations of common ethical theories like consequentialism, deontology, and principlism in addressing the complexities of dementia care. Through case studies such as Mr. and Mrs. Feldman, it illustrates how traditional ethical frameworks may fall short when applied to real-life situations involving individuals with dementia. The chapter emphasizes the need for a more nuanced approach that considers personal histories and community contexts in decision-making processes.
