## Daniel Levitin Explores Music as Medicine in “I Heard There Was a Secret Chord” – Video transcript

In the video transcript of an interview on AM Northwest, neuroscientist and author Daniel Levitin discusses his book 'I Heard There Was a Secret Chord.' He emphasizes music's therapeutic potential, noting that it can boost the immune system and alleviate pain by triggering natural opioids in the brain. Levitin highlights how different aspects of music engage various parts of the brain, aiding conditions like Alzheimer’s disease or post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). A case study involving singer-songwriter Joni Mitchell illustrates music's profound impact on recovery from a stroke. Levitin also discusses Glenn Campbell's resilience as a musician despite suffering from Alzheimer’s, underscoring the cognitive reserve built through lifelong musical engagement. The conversation underscores the relevance of personal favorite music and songs from one's youth in reconnecting individuals with their identities amid memory loss or brain damage.
