## Evidence Review of Indigenous Culture for Health and Wellbeing

This review article synthesizes evidence on the relationship between Indigenous culture and health outcomes, focusing specifically on Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples in Australia and other indigenous groups globally. The authors conducted a comprehensive search of literature published between 1997 and 2017 to assess how cultural elements influence physical and mental well-being. The study examined six cultural domains: country and caring for country, knowledge and beliefs, language, self-determination, family and kinship, and cultural expression. Seventy-two publications were included in the review, with findings indicating a significant positive correlation between cultural engagement and improved health outcomes. However, the quality of evidence was generally assessed as moderate or low due to methodological limitations such as lack of reliable measures and longitudinal studies. The review highlights the importance of mixed-methods approaches for future research on Indigenous cultures’ influence on health and well-being.
