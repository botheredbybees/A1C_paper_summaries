## Reminiscence bump invariance with respect to genre, age, and country

James Renwick and Matthew H. Woolhouse report a cross-cultural study investigating musical reminiscence bumps, which are memories of emotionally significant music from late adolescence and early adulthood. Using data from 4,824 participants across 102 countries, they found that musical reminiscence bumps are common across all age groups and both sexes, prevalent in different countries, and not restricted to specific genres. The study highlights the psychological phenomenon where individuals remain emotionally invested in music they listened to during their formative years, suggesting a biological and cultural ubiquity of this memory pattern. This research contributes valuable insights into how music can serve as a powerful tool for connecting past experiences with present identity, potentially aiding therapeutic interventions in dementia care.
