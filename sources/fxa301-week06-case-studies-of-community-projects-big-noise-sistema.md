## Case Studies of Community Projects - Big Noise Sistema Scotland

This document discusses the 'Big Noise' programme under Sistema Scotland, a social transformation charity that aims to provide orchestral music education in communities of social disadvantage. The programme operates in four Scottish cities: Raploch (Stirling), Govanhill (Glasgow), Torry (Aberdeen), and Douglas (Dundee). It offers an intensive environment for orchestral music making, starting from birth to adulthood, fostering educational performance, health and wellbeing, ambition, and resilience. The programme includes early childhood activities such as singing songs and learning concepts of pitch and rhythm with parents and toddlers. As children grow older, they receive weekly musical training sessions with professional musicians and educators. The focus is on inclusion, ensuring no child is ever turned away regardless of behavior or talent. A five-year study identified benefits in seven domains: educational, life skills, emotional, social, musical, physical, and protection.
