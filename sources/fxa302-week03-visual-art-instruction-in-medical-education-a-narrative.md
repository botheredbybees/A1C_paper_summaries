## Visual art instruction in medical education: a narrative review

This paper reviews the incorporation of visual arts training into medical education curricula to enhance clinical skills and professional development among medical students. The review highlights that visual art instruction focuses primarily on preclinical students and aims to develop observation, diagnostic skills, empathy, teamwork, communication abilities, resilience, and cultural sensitivity. Commonly used pedagogical approaches include Visual Thinking Strategies (VTS) and Artful Thinking. Partnerships with local art museums are prevalent, and sessions typically range from four to eighteen meetings of varying durations. The review notes that while existing programs show promise in improving observational skills and fostering empathy among medical students, rigorous evidence supporting the impact on other areas such as team building and resilience is limited. Recommendations are made for more robust research to support these educational initiatives.
