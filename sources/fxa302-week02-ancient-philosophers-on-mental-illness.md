## Ancient philosophers on mental illness

This article explores the ancient philosophers' understanding of mental illness from Plato to late antiquity, examining how the phenomenon was recognized and categorized in philosophical texts. It highlights that while ancient medicine did not have a distinct category for 'mental illness', philosophers such as Democritus and Socrates considered themselves 'doctors of the soul', addressing emotional disorders rather than medical conditions affecting the mind. The study identifies Plato's dialogue, Laws, as significant for recognizing mental illness in social contexts and distinguishing between those who are mentally ill and those with moral faults. Philosophical discussions about mental health reflect an understanding that influenced Western ideas on mental illness.
