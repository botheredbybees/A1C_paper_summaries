## Good Practice in Creative Communication-Based Reminiscence Work

This chapter discusses the principles of good practice in creative communication-based reminiscence work for individuals with dementia, emphasizing a person-centered approach over traditional recall and tell methods. It highlights the importance of supporting each individual's strengths, respecting their interests, and adapting to their needs through various sensory stimulations. The text includes a table summarizing key practices such as establishing rapport, being attentive to basic needs, showing interest, sensitivity, non-judgmental attitudes, cooperative conversation strategies, creating an easy atmosphere, and respecting the right to tell stories as they choose. It also touches on group activities, spontaneous reminiscence, family caregiver challenges, communication barriers in care settings, and debates surrounding psychosocial interventions versus drug treatments.
