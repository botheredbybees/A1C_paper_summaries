## Week 2 Lecture Sem 2 2026

The Week 2 lecture in the FXA101 'Creativity For Life' course explores various aspects of creativity and its neurological underpinnings. The lecture begins by defining creativity through multiple dictionaries, emphasizing that it encompasses a wide range of activities including artistic endeavors and innovative solutions to everyday problems. It then delves into the historical context of human creativity, linking it to survival and evolution, with examples such as bone flutes and cave paintings dating back thousands of years.

The lecture discusses recent research on the neural basis of creativity, particularly focusing on the Default Mode Network (DMN) which initiates creative ideas before other regions evaluate them. This leads into an exploration of synesthesia, a neurological phenomenon where different senses are interconnected, often leading to heightened artistic expression and enhanced memory recall for those with this condition. The lecture cites several studies that highlight how synesthetes can have greater involvement in arts and creativity.

The lecture concludes by encouraging students to reflect on their own creative experiences and the brain areas they think they use during these moments. It also reminds students of important course tasks, including an orientation quiz and the submission of LAPs (Learning Activity Packages) as well as the upcoming Creative Project 1 due date.
