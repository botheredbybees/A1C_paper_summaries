## Apples Never Fall: Exploring the Role of Food in Family Wellbeing and Memory through Therapeutic Photography

Jane Crosswell's essay 'Apples Never Fall' explores the therapeutic value of food and memory in family wellbeing through the lens of her mother's Alzheimer’s diagnosis. The work highlights how food memories are integral to personal identity, cognitive development, and emotional health. By documenting cherished moments through photography, Crosswell aims to preserve family history and enhance communication with those suffering from dementia. Her project involves creating a photo essay that captures significant culinary experiences across generations, illustrating the continuity of cultural traditions and familial bonds. Utilizing vibrant colors and meticulous composition, each image represents a distinct memory and evokes personal narratives. The therapeutic benefits of this visual storytelling are evident in its ability to promote reminiscence therapy and foster connections within aging communities. This project underscores the importance of arts in health contexts as a tool for emotional healing and intergenerational bonding.
