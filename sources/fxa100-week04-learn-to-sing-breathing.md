## Learn to Sing: Breathing

The document 'Learn to Sing: Breathing' on the BBC website provides guidance on proper breathing techniques essential for singing. It emphasizes the importance of deep diaphragmatic breathing, which involves expanding the lower lungs rather than lifting the shoulders during inhalation. The tutorial suggests lying flat with hands placed on the stomach to observe correct breath movement and encourages regular practice to enhance vocal technique and lung capacity.

The page includes exercises designed to improve control over breath volume and duration. For instance, Exercise 1 ('Hissing') involves inhaling at various counts (4, 6, 2) and exhaling with a consistent hissing sound for progressively longer durations, aiming to develop endurance in sustaining long vocal phrases efficiently. Exercise 2 focuses on 'Snatched Breaths,' where the diaphragm fills gradually in fractions of time as indicated by numbers ('1' - quarter full; '4' - fully filled), followed by a controlled exhalation.

These breathing techniques are relevant to creative arts and health, especially for individuals engaging in choral singing or vocal performances, ensuring they maintain optimal voice health while enhancing their artistic expression.
