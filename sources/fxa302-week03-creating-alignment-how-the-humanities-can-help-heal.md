## Creating Alignment: How the Humanities Can Help Heal Physicians and Patients

The article 'Creating Alignment: How the Humanities Can Help Heal Physicians and Patients' by Elizabeth Cerceo and Nagaswami Vasan discusses the increasing recognition of medical humanities in medical education. The authors argue that while science-based medicine is essential for standardization and patient safety, an exclusive focus on scientific rigor can devalue humanistic pursuits such as compassion, empathy, and resiliency. They contend that incorporating the medical humanities—encompassing ethics, social sciences, and fine arts—can provide a more holistic approach to healthcare education and practice. Courses in spirituality, racism in medicine, and art have been shown to improve observational skills and foster tolerance for ambiguity. The authors also highlight the importance of interdisciplinary approaches to address societal issues such as burnout among physicians and the challenges posed by pandemics like COVID-19. They emphasize that a humanities background can equip future healthcare professionals with essential skills and perspectives to navigate uncertainties in practice, thereby enhancing their ability to deliver compassionate care.
