## Prevalence of very mild and mild dementia in community-dwelling older Chinese people in Hong Kong

This study aimed to assess the prevalence of very mild and mild dementia among older Chinese individuals in Hong Kong through a two-phase household survey design. The first phase involved screening approximately 6,100 participants using the Cantonese version of the Mini-Mental State Examination (MMSE) and a short memory inventory. In the second phase, nearly 2,800 participants were further evaluated for dementia severity by psychiatrists employing the Clinical Dementia Rating (CDR). The study found that 8.5% of individuals aged 70 years or older had very mild dementia (VMD), while 8.9% had mild dementia. Logistic regression analyses indicated that advanced age, lower education levels, and significant cerebrovascular risk factors were associated with a higher likelihood of dementia, whereas regular physical exercise was found to be protective against the condition. The results highlight the importance of early intervention strategies in reducing potential morbidity among this vulnerable population.
