## The Birth of a New Profession

This chapter discusses the origins of medical clowning as a profession, initiated by Michael Christensen and his friends from the Big Apple Circus in 1986 at New York's Children’s Hospital. It details how regular visits to hospitals for paid performances led to the establishment of 'medical clowning' or 'therapeutic clowning.' The chapter tracks the spread of medical clowning across different countries, highlighting significant contributions by organizations like the Dream Doctors and academic institutions such as the University of Haifa in Israel and George Brown College in Canada. It emphasizes the importance of collaboration between clowns and healthcare professionals to enhance patient care through humor and music.
