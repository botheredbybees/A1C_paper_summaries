## Evaluating the Sharing Stories youth theatre program: an interactive theatre and drama-based strategy for sexual health promotion among multicultural youth

This study evaluates the effectiveness of the Sharing Stories youth theatre program, an interactive theatre and drama-based approach to sexual health promotion for multicultural youth in Western Australia. The research involved 18 participants aged between 14 and 21 years from diverse cultural backgrounds who engaged in weekly drama workshops addressing topics such as condom use, consent, bullying, and sexting. Using a mixed-methods design involving sexual health drama scenarios and questionnaires, the study found that participants showed increased confidence in discussing sexual health with peers, improved knowledge about accessing healthcare services for STIs, and more positive attitudes towards using condoms. The evaluation also highlighted the effectiveness of drama-based methods in engaging multicultural youth from various age groups and cultures. This program offers a promising strategy for up-skilling young people to become peer educators on sexual health issues.
