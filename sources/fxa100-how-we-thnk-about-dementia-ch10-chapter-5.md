## The Body in Dementia

This chapter explores the relevance of Merleau-Ponty's philosophy to understanding dementia through the concept of the 'body-subject'. It examines how physical embodiment influences mental life and meaning-making, particularly in severe cases where traditional communication is impossible. Drawing on Merleau-Ponty’s work, it discusses how bodily movements and habits can express emotional and affective states even without verbal communication. The chapter also emphasizes the importance of understanding dementia from a social perspective, recognizing the role of interaction with others in shaping experiences of selfhood.
