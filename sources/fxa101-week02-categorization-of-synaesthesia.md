## Categorization of Synaesthesia

Aleksandra Rogowska's article synthesizes current knowledge about synaesthesia across various fields including literature, arts, medicine, and psychology. The primary goal is to classify different types of synaesthesia, distinguishing between developmental (neonatal) and acquired forms, momentary (triggered temporarily), and associational synaesthesia that relates to universal sense relations in art or literature. Rogowska also proposes a hypothesis suggesting that each type of synaesthesia may serve either compensatory or integrative functions, depending on the individual's cognitive processes. The article outlines a spectrum from stronger forms like conceptual, synthetic, comprehensive, external and bidirectional synaesthesias to weaker ones such as sensory, perceptual, intramodal, analytic, partial, internal and unidirectional. This categorization aims to enhance understanding of synaesthesia’s mechanisms, its role in cognitive development, and the variability among individuals experiencing it.
