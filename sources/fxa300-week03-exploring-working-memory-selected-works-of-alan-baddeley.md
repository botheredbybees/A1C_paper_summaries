## Exploring Working Memory: Selected Works of Alan Baddeley

This document is an excerpt from 'Exploring Working Memory: Selected Works of Alan Baddeley' by Alan Baddeley, detailing the author's academic background and early career. The introduction highlights his initial interest in geography and philosophy before opting for psychology at University College London due to a fascination with experimental testing rather than philosophical debate. After graduation, he worked at Princeton and then joined the Medical Research Council Applied Psychology Unit in Cambridge, influenced by Donald Broadbent’s seminal work on perception and communication. At Cambridge, Baddeley conducted research on memorable postal codes, applying concepts from information theory to memory and language, which led to his interest in cognitive psychology. His four-year research period at the Unit provided extensive experience with experimental methods and exposure to new theories from both within and outside Europe, culminating in a PhD thesis that included 17 experiments. This period was also marked by debates in cognitive psychology regarding the nature of memory systems.
