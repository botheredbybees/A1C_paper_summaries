## Winding paths

In 'Winding paths,' Corinne Doran reflects on her experiences during a lockdown period in southwestern Sydney, Australia. The essay explores the interplay between nature's rhythms and personal creative endeavors as coping mechanisms for the ennui induced by prolonged stay-at-home orders. Doran describes observing local wildlife and seasonal changes while engaging with raffia weaving, which provides both tactile engagement and meditative focus. She contrasts the chaotic external environment with her own isolated creativity, noting how these activities offer a sense of order amidst disorder. The piece highlights the importance of cultivating new interests and finding solace in nature during times of uncertainty. Doran concludes by expressing an interest in remembering this period not as a test of productivity but rather as a journey towards deeper self-awareness through creative practices.
