## Cognitive Training and Rehabilitation in Dementia Care

The chapter discusses cognitive training and rehabilitation methods for individuals with mild to moderate dementia. It contrasts systematic reviews on cognitive training's lack of significant positive outcomes versus the benefits seen from cognitive stimulation, which includes reality orientation and reminiscence therapy. Cognitive rehabilitation focuses on optimizing remaining cognitive functions rather than recovery, employing internal and external strategies like spaced retrieval and memory aids. The chapter emphasizes culturally sensitive adaptations for different groups and highlights promising but under-researched areas in dementia care.
