## Arts Education and Society: The Role of the Arts in Promoting Emotional Wellbeing and Social Inclusion of Young People

Vassiliki Karkou and Judy Glasman's article discusses the role of arts in education, focusing on emotional wellbeing and social inclusion for young people. The authors highlight the debate within educational contexts about how to incorporate the arts effectively beyond traditional subjects' boundaries. They argue that while the National Curriculum confines arts education narrowly, there are broader benefits from using arts as therapeutic interventions to address students’ emotional needs and foster personal development. Karkou and Glasman explore the historical context of arts in education, noting shifts from child-centered approaches emphasizing self-expression to more cognitively focused methods aiming for comprehensive understanding of artistic experiences. They distinguish between arts education, which aims at knowledge acquisition about art forms, and arts therapies that use non-verbal creative processes to address emotional and social issues. The article underscores the potential for arts-based programs like Labyrinth Project to enhance students' overall wellbeing and integrate them socially, suggesting a more holistic approach to integrating arts in educational settings is beneficial.
