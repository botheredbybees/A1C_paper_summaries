## Art and Dementia | Art Gallery of New South Wales

The 'Art and Dementia' initiative by the Art Gallery of New South Wales is a comprehensive arts engagement model designed to enhance social inclusion and improve wellbeing for individuals living with dementia, their care partners, and communities. Danielle Gullotta from the gallery's access programs emphasizes that engagement with art can evoke emotional, intellectual, and sensory responses which positively impact an individual’s wellbeing. The program offers monthly discussion sessions and sensory art-making experiences to make the gallery’s collection accessible to people with dementia.

Research indicates that arts engagement significantly contributes to health and wellbeing by fostering self-identity, confidence, and opportunities for meaningful connections, thereby combating social isolation and loneliness. Key impacts observed in participants include enhanced imagination, social inclusion, self-expression, quality of life improvement, community awareness, and a shift from everyday concerns towards aesthetic experiences.

In 2016, Dr. Gail Kenning evaluated the Art Gallery’s arts access program for people with dementia. The study explored how such programs provide pleasure and enjoyment, contribute to wellbeing and quality of life, offer learnings for future evaluations, and support care and wellbeing needs including those related to disabilities.

Further initiatives like the Arts Engagement for Liveable Communities (AECL) project have expanded these practices beyond gallery walls into community settings such as Hazelhurst Arts Centre and Holdsworth Community Centre. This has led to an increase in demand from carers and individuals with dementia seeking arts engagement activities, not just at the gallery but also in their home environments.

The Art Gallery of NSW continues to develop resources and processes for integrating its unique art engagement model within residential care facilities and community centers, emphasizing inclusivity, sustainability, and ongoing rigorous evaluation.
