## 'A Motley to the View': The Clothing of Court Fools in Tudor England

This article examines the evolving attire of court fools during the Tudor era and challenges the conventional perception that they were always dressed in garish parti-colours or motley. Zoe Screti argues that while early Tudor fools wore coarse woolen fabric, later court fools adopted luxurious fabrics like silk and fur, despite remaining distinct from other courtiers through outdated fashion choices or contrasting colors to signify their status as jesters. Drawing on wardrobe accounts and historical context, the author reveals how fool clothing evolved alongside societal changes in religion, Renaissance ideals, and social hierarchy.
