## Children’s Play Environment after a Disaster: The Great East Japan Earthquake

This article explores the role of play and adventure playgrounds in supporting children's mental, physical, and social health after the Great East Japan Earthquake and subsequent disasters. The study highlights that post-disaster play activities help children express stress, improve their physical well-being through various body movements, and foster communication with peers to rebuild social connections. In Fukushima, where additional health issues like childhood obesity are prevalent, providing more play environments is crucial for a balanced recovery. The paper also discusses the importance of recognizing children's right to play in disaster recovery efforts, emphasizing the qualitative aspects of mental and social health through interviews and observations between 2011 and 2014.
