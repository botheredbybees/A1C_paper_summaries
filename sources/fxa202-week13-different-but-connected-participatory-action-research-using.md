## Different but Connected: Participatory Action Research Using Photovoice to Explore Well-being in Autistic Young Adults

The study explores the well-being of autistic young adults through a participatory action research (PAR) approach using photovoice, a method that allows participants to use photography to express their perspectives on life and well-being. The research involved 14 autistic individuals in a post-school transition program who were actively engaged throughout the process from designing the study, collecting data via photographs, analyzing results, and disseminating findings. Key themes identified include individuality and personal growth, significant relationships, and community connections. This participatory approach highlights the importance of including autistic voices to better understand their unique experiences and perspectives, leading to more effective service provision and policy development that supports quality of life for individuals on the autism spectrum.
