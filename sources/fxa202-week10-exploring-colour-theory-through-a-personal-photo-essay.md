## Exploring Colour Theory Through a Personal Photo Essay

Melanie Henderson's photo essay explores the theme of color theory within the context of mental health and emotional well-being. The essay is structured around a single day, using color as a metaphor for navigating life with someone who has a mental illness. Through her use of personal experiences and theoretical research, Henderson illustrates how colors can evoke different emotions and psychological responses, influenced by cultural cues and symbolic representation. Her work employs gendered personification of colors to reflect their psychodynamic nature, suggesting that color is not just an aesthetic element but also carries profound emotional weight. The essay emphasizes the therapeutic potential of creative storytelling through photography, as a way for individuals to reimagine and reframe life events, offering insights into personal identity and well-being.
