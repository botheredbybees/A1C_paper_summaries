## Being Moved by Unfamiliar Sad Music Is Associated with High Empathy

The study investigates the emotional responses elicited by unfamiliar sad music and their relationship to individual traits, focusing on empathy. The research involved 102 participants from a representative Finnish sample who listened to an excerpt of instrumental sad music and reported their emotional reactions. The results indicated that experiences of 'Moving sadness'—a profound emotional response characterized by feelings of being moved and intense sadness—are strongly associated with high trait empathy and emotional contagion. In contrast, the responses labeled as 'Relaxing sadness' and 'Nervous sadness' were not significantly linked to any individual traits. The findings contribute to understanding why some individuals derive enjoyment from sad music despite its negative valence, suggesting that the appeal of such music is related to empathetic engagement rather than external factors like mood regulation or autobiographical memories.
