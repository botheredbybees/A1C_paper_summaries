## Case Study: The Paper Apothecary and Community Engagement Through Art

This lesson explores the role of museums in building community engagement and enhancing well-being. It discusses Tony Butler's Happy Museum Project which reimagines museum roles beyond preservation to include social capital creation and active citizenship. The Paper Apothecary project at Beaney House, Canterbury, exemplifies how museums can foster happiness and connectedness through interactive activities like 'happiness prescriptions.' Community mural projects in Sheffield, Tasmania, and Shortland, NSW, highlight the aesthetic and social benefits of public art in revitalizing neighborhoods and promoting cultural understanding. The lesson also touches on quilting as a communal activity with historical and philanthropic significance.
