## Dance Movement Therapy for Neurodegenerative Diseases: A Systematic Review

This systematic review examines the impact of dance movement therapy (DMT) on neurodegenerative diseases, focusing on Parkinson’s disease (PD), mild cognitive impairment (MCI), and Alzheimer’s disease (AD). The review synthesizes findings from 33 studies on PD, 16 studies on MCI, and 4 studies on AD. Key findings indicate that DMT significantly enhances global cognitive function, memory, and executive function in individuals with MCI. For those with Parkinson’s disease, DMT notably improves overall health condition, balance, and gait compared to non-dance interventions. The review concludes that while DMT shows promising effects on motor function and cognitive deficits, its efficacy on mood and quality of life remains inconclusive. Future research should focus on larger sample sizes and long-term studies with clear reporting standards, particularly for Alzheimer’s disease.
