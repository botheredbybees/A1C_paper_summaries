## Wisdom poetry trialogue

The article 'Wisdom poetry trialogue' explores the concept of wisdom poetry through a cross-cultural dialogue between Robert Carroll, Kykosa Kajangu, and Jack Coulehan. Each author represents unique traditions in wisdom and healing: African wisdom keeper, poet physician with medical background, and psychiatrist-poet respectively. They discuss how proverbs convey cultural wisdom through poetry, transcending individual ethnic groups to express universal truths. The dialogue highlights the role of poetry in facilitating teaching and healing across different cultures, emphasizing its ability to capture enduring human experiences beyond specific events. This work underscores the significance of poetry as a means to transmit profound insights about life and existence, making it relevant to creative arts and health fields.
