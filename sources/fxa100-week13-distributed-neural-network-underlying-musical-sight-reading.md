## Distributed Neural Network Underlying Musical Sight-Reading and Keyboard Performance

This study investigates the functional neuroanatomy of musical sight-reading and keyboard performance in professional pianists using positron emission tomography (PET) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI). The research reveals distinct cortical activations for these activities, separate from those involved in verbal operations but located adjacent to them. These findings shed light on why musicians with brain damage might experience differential effects on their verbal and musical abilities based on the nature and location of the injury. Key aspects of music such as its rhythmic patterns, discrete pitches, expressive use, sequential motor activity, cultural rules, and linguistic parallels are discussed in relation to cognitive neuroscience.
