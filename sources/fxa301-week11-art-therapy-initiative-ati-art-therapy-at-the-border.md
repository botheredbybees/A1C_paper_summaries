## Art Therapy Initiative (ATI): Art therapy at the border: Holding the line of the kite

This article explores the use of kite making and flying in art therapy sessions with refugees at border camps in Europe. The authors, Kalmanowitz and Lloyd, discuss how these activities offer psychological relief and a sense of connection to life beyond refugee camps for individuals who have fled war or persecution. By engaging in such creative arts therapies, participants experience enhanced resilience, distraction from daily hardships, and a cultural reaffirmation through protective factors inherent in kite traditions. The article highlights the complexity of border experiences, blending aspirations for freedom with existential needs for belonging, which are central to refugees' psychological states.
