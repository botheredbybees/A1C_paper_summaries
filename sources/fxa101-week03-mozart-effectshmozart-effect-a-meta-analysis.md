## Mozart effect–Shmozart effect: A meta-analysis

The study by Pietschnig, Voracek, and Formann (2010) critically examines the controversial 'Mozart effect', a hypothesis suggesting that listening to Mozart's music can temporarily enhance spatial reasoning abilities. This paper conducts one of the largest meta-analyses on the subject, including nearly 40 studies with over 3000 participants. The research debunks claims about significant performance benefits from Mozart’s sonata KV 448 and concludes that any observed effects are negligible. Key findings indicate a small overall effect size (d = 0.37) when comparing the impact of listening to the Mozart sonata versus no music or non-musical stimuli, with similar results for other musical pieces. The authors emphasize publication bias influencing reported outcomes and highlight discrepancies between studies conducted by Rauscher et al. and others. This analysis provides substantial evidence against the existence of a specific performance-enhancing Mozart effect, contributing to debates around neuromyths in education and health.
