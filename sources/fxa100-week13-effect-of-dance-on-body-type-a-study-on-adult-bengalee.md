## Effect of Dance on Body Type: A Study on Adult Bengalee Female in North 24 Parganas, West Bengal

The study explores the impact of dance on body type and composition among adult Bengalee females in North 24 Parganas, West Bengal. The research compares a group of dancers who practice Kathak and Bharatanatyam with an age-matched control group that does not engage in formal dance training. Using Heath and Carter's somatotyping method, the study measures body composition through various anthropometric assessments such as skinfolds, girths, and body dimensions. Key findings reveal significant differences between dancers and non-dancers, with dancers exhibiting greater mesomorphy (muscularity) and lower endomorphy (fatness), indicating a leaner physique. Additionally, dancers were found to have a pear-shaped body type, characterized by a waist circumference smaller than the hip circumference. This research underscores the potential of dance as an effective physical activity for improving body composition and reducing health risks associated with obesity and sedentary lifestyles.
