## Music and Memory: An Introduction (review)

The review discusses Bob Snyder's 'Music and Memory: An Introduction', a text that explores cognitive concepts in relation to music without relying on traditional Western musical notation. The book aims to make complex theoretical ideas accessible to those with varying levels of musical training, including non-Western and electroacoustic musics. It is divided into two main sections: one covering cognitive principles such as memory systems (echoic, short-term, long-term), and the other applying these concepts to specific musical contexts like grouping, phrases, sets, and metaphors. The review highlights the book's ability to bridge interdisciplinary knowledge, offering valuable insights for both experienced musicians and those less familiar with traditional notation.
