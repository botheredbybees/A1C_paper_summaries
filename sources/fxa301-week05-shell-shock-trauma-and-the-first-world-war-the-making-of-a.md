## Shell Shock, Trauma, and the First World War: The Making of a Diagnosis and Its Histories

This article examines the concept of shell shock during World War I in Britain, challenging traditional views that it represents an early form of post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). It argues for understanding shell shock within its historical context as a wartime medical construction rather than as an unchanging manifestation of trauma. By doing so, it provides new insights into the development of psychology and psychiatry during this period. The author also explores methodological issues in studying shell shock and discusses how intellectual history relates to literary approaches that view trauma as timeless and universal.
