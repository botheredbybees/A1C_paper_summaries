## Developing a national arts and health policy framework

The paper 'Developing a national arts and health policy framework' by Deborah Mills discusses the need for a comprehensive approach to integrating arts into healthcare initiatives in Australia. The author highlights key moments that sparked interest in the potential of arts for enhancing public health, including an incident involving Minister John Hill witnessing art's therapeutic effects on a critically ill patient and another involving former MP Simon Crean’s experience with palliative care and music therapy. Mills emphasizes the importance of evidence-based practices to support the integration of arts into healthcare systems and outlines the role of organizations like the Institute for Creative Health (formerly Arts and Health Foundation) in promoting such initiatives. The paper also details ongoing efforts to develop a national policy framework, including the gathering of stakeholder feedback and research on the health benefits of art engagement. Mills argues that while substantial progress has been made toward adopting this framework, continued advocacy is necessary to ensure its successful implementation by government bodies.
