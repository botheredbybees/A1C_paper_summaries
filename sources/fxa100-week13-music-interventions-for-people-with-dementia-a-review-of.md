## Music Interventions for People with Dementia: A Review of the Literature

This paper presents a qualitative review of 21 empirical studies on the effects of various music activities on emotional and behavioral responses in people with dementia. The authors discuss setting, context, research findings, methodological issues such as observational methods, and theoretical frameworks like Kitwood’s theory of personhood. They conclude that while music has beneficial applications in dementia care, previous reviews have highlighted methodological weaknesses. Recommendations for future research include using continuous time sampling methodologies and recording the duration of observed behaviors to improve understanding of the mechanisms behind music's effects on people with dementia.
