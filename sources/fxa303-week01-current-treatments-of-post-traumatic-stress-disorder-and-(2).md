## Current Treatments of Post-traumatic Stress Disorder and Amygdala Ablation as a Potential Cutting-Edge Therapy in Its Refractory Cases

This article reviews current treatments for post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and explores the potential of amygdala ablation as a novel therapeutic approach. PTSD is characterized by intrusive memories, avoidance behaviors, cognitive changes, altered emotions, and heightened arousal following traumatic events. Common psychological interventions include trauma-focused therapies such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), prolonged exposure therapy, and eye movement desensitization and reprocessing (EMDR). Pharmacological treatments have been attempted with mixed success, including selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs) and mood stabilizers like lithium and valproate. The review highlights the role of amygdala hyperactivation in PTSD pathophysiology and discusses the potential for targeted laser ablation as a surgical intervention for refractory cases. This approach has shown efficacy in managing medically unresponsive medial temporal lobe epilepsy, suggesting its promise as an innovative therapy for severe, treatment-resistant PTSD.
