## Pearl, 85 and Living with Dementia, Adds Another String to Her Bow

The article highlights the story of Pearl Durrant, an 85-year-old care home resident living with dementia who has learned to play the violin as part of a community music group called BUDDY. Initiated by Bournemouth University and involving local professional musicians, the group includes individuals living with dementia along with their carers. Led by professional musicians, participants engage in weekly practices culminating in public performances. Pearl, despite her initial lack of musical experience, has shown significant progress since joining the group in January 2017. Her participation not only offers a new creative outlet but also enhances social connections and cognitive engagement. Family members have observed positive impacts on Pearl's reminiscence and mood improvement, indicating that such music programs can significantly benefit individuals living with dementia.
