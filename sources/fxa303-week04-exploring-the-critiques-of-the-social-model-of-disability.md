## Exploring the critiques of the social model of disability: the transformative possibility of Arendt’s notion of power

This article critically examines the social model of disability, a theoretical framework that has been instrumental in advocating for disabled people's rights and inclusion but faces critiques for being overly rigid and lacking nuance. The author traces the historical development of different forms of the social model across various regions (UK, Nordic countries, North America) and highlights their unique approaches and limitations. Criticisms are analyzed, focusing on the social model's narrow focus on oppression caused by capitalism and its failure to acknowledge differences in types of oppression experienced by disabled individuals. The article proposes incorporating Hannah Arendt’s concept of power as a means to enrich disability studies by offering a more pluralistic understanding of bodies and experiences.
