## Effect of background noise on food perception

This study investigates the impact of auditory background noise on gustatory and tactile perceptions of food, specifically focusing on sugar level, salt level, crunchiness, and overall liking. Participants consumed various foods while listening to different levels of white noise (no sound, quiet, loud) and rated these properties accordingly. The results showed that reported sweetness and saltiness were lower in the presence of loud noise compared to quiet conditions, whereas food crunchiness was perceived as more intense under noisy conditions. This suggests differential effects on gustatory and auditory-related sensory attributes: taste and sound cues are modulated differently by background sounds. Additionally, there is a correlation between participants' preference for background noise and their liking of the food consumed. The findings highlight the role of environmental sounds in influencing multisensory experiences and suggest that pleasant ambient music may enhance eating pleasure.
