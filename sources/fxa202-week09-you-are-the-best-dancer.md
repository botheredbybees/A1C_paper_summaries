## You Are the Best Dancer

Tanya Hughes’s personal narrative, 'You Are the Best Dancer,' recounts her journey through breast cancer diagnosis and treatment while navigating her identity as a dancer. The piece opens with a moment of confusion during a conversation that reveals her breast cancer diagnosis, setting a tone of disorientation and emotional turmoil. Hughes describes the physical and psychological toll of surgery and subsequent treatments, including hair loss and a sense of diminished dignity. Her frustration with the medical system is palpable but is balanced by moments of resilience, such as returning to work prematurely out of necessity. Hughes introduces Slo Mo, a toy sloth that serves as both a symbol of stoicism and a repository for her discarded mastectomy bras, further emphasizing the impact cancer has had on her sense of self. Throughout her ordeal, Hughes finds solace in creative outlets like playing saxophone and roller-disco dancing, which serve to maintain her connection with pre-cancer life and identity. The narrative highlights the importance of community support, particularly through a friend named Arlo who provides companionship during difficult times. Overall, 'You Are the Best Dancer' underscores the transformative power of creative expression in coping with illness and maintaining one's sense of self.
