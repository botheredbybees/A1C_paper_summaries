## Research Protocols: Structure, Purpose, and Application

This chapter discusses the structure and purpose of research protocols in comparison to 'show bibles' used in screen production. Protocols serve as critical documents for research projects, detailing their rationale, methods, feasibility, ethics considerations, funding sources, and stakeholder information. They are essential for internal use, ethical approval applications, and communication with stakeholders. The chapter outlines the typical sections of a protocol, including background rationale, objectives, design, participant identification, procedures, intervention details, analysis, data management, ethical and regulatory considerations, finance and insurance, publication policy, and references. It emphasizes the importance of clear explanations of the study's purpose and logistics for both internal and external audiences.
