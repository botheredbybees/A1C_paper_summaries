## Musical Training Enhances Information Processing Speed

Jennifer Bugos and Wendy Mostafa's study examines the impact of musical training on information processing speed in musicians compared to non-musicians. The research utilized standardized neuropsychological measures, including the Paced Auditory Serial Addition Task (PASAT) and the Trail Making Test (TMT), part A and B. Results indicated significantly enhanced performance by musicians compared to non-musicians on these tasks (p < .05). This suggests that musical training can enhance processing speed of auditory and visual content, which has implications for music educators in incorporating rhythmic sight-reading exercises and improvisational activities to reinforce cognitive skills such as processing speed. The study contributes to the understanding that musical training not only fosters creativity and aesthetic experiences but also enhances general cognitive abilities.
