## Environmental and Cognitive Enrichment in Childhood as Protective Factors in the Adult and Aging Brain

This review article examines the concept of cognitive reserve (CR) and its development through childhood environmental and cognitive enrichment as a protective factor against neurodegenerative diseases in later life. The authors emphasize that early childhood, particularly from birth to adolescence, is crucial for brain development and establishing CR. They argue that positive environmental factors contribute significantly to the proper development of cognitive, affective, and psychosocial capacities, thereby enhancing resilience against aging-related cognitive decline. Key findings include the beneficial impact of early social and sensory interactions on neural resilience and suggest that interventions aimed at enriching these environments can serve as a form of primary prevention for cognitive impairment later in life. The article underscores the significance of identifying neurodevelopmental disorders early to mitigate long-term impacts, making it relevant to the creative arts and health by highlighting the importance of holistic development and preventive measures.
