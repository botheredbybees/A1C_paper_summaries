## Professional Responsibilities and Ethical Considerations in Research Design

This chapter discusses the responsibilities of professionals towards participants, colleagues, and the public. It emphasizes general responsibility, including avoiding harm, misconduct, and ensuring awareness of colleagues' activities; termination and continuity of care, such as clear conditions for ending professional activities and referral to alternative sources if necessary. The text also covers honesty in interactions with stakeholders, preventing exploitation and conflicts of interest, maintaining personal boundaries, and addressing ethical misconduct. Additionally, it outlines guidelines for developing research studies ethically, including considering the potential benefits of research questions and balancing conflicting interests among researchers, funders, and sponsors. It highlights the importance of designing studies to prevent harm to participants, ensuring data confidentiality, involving stakeholders in data collection measures, and using appropriate recruitment strategies.
