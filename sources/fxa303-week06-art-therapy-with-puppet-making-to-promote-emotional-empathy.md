## Art Therapy With Puppet Making to Promote Emotional Empathy for an Adolescent With Autism

The article explores the use of art therapy, specifically puppet making and puppetry, to enhance emotional empathy in adolescents with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The study highlights the complex nature of empathy as a multidimensional concept consisting of cognitive and affective components. Although individuals with ASD often exhibit difficulties with cognitive empathy, recent research suggests that they may retain capabilities for emotional empathy. Art therapy is proposed as an effective method to promote this aspect of empathy by facilitating self-expression and enhancing social communication skills through creative engagement.

The article discusses the Expressive Therapies Continuum (ETC), a framework for visual expression based on three levels: kinesthetic/sensory, perceptual/affective, and cognitive/symbolic. It also mentions that puppetry in therapy has been used to express difficult feelings and resolve conflicts. The study focuses on a case of a 16-year-old African-American female with ASD who was engaged in art therapy sessions involving puppet making. This approach aimed at improving her emotional empathy and enhancing self-awareness, communication skills, and overall social interactions.
