## A Lot of Us Look at Life Differently: Homeless Youths and Art on the Outside

This article presents a qualitative analysis of the artistic expression of homeless youths, focusing on drawings and poetry collected from participants in New York City and Toronto. The study explores how art serves as a transformative medium for these youths, allowing them to explore their identities, communicate their experiences, and redirect emotional energy into creative processes. It also examines parallels between the marginalization faced by homeless youths and the concepts of L’Art Brut and Outsider Art movements. Key findings include the therapeutic benefits of artistic expression for marginalized individuals and the importance of understanding art as a form of communication and self-discovery.
