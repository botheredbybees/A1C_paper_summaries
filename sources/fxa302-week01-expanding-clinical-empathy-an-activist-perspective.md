## Expanding Clinical Empathy: An Activist Perspective

Rebecca Garden’s article, 'Expanding Clinical Empathy: An Activist Perspective,' explores the concept of clinical empathy in healthcare education and practice. The author argues that while discussions on empathy are valuable for enhancing communication between patients and physicians, they often lack an action-oriented component to alleviate patient suffering. Garden advocates for strategies that emphasize the patient's authority in describing their illness experiences and promote a social context understanding. She suggests expanding empathy beyond emotional engagement to include actions aimed at reducing distress and encourages health-care practitioners to engage more deeply with the social factors influencing patients' well-being.
