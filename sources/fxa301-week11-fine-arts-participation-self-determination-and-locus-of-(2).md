## Fine arts participation, self-determination, and locus of control among persons with developmental disabilities

This study explores the relationship between fine arts participation and self-determination, as well as locus of control among individuals with developmental disabilities. The research involved thirty-four participants in fine arts programs alongside eight non-participants, all with varying degrees of neuromuscular and cognitive impairments. Although no significant differences were found on quantitative measures, qualitative findings suggested that fine arts participants are more likely to attribute their circumstances and accomplishments to effort rather than luck compared to non-participants. The study highlights the potential for self-determination and locus of control as constructs worth further investigation in relation to arts participation among people with developmental disabilities.
