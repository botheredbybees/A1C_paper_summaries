## The Use of Visual Arts as a Window to Diagnosing Medical Pathologies

This article discusses the importance of visual literacy and careful observation in medical practice, which are critical skills rarely taught formally in medical education. It highlights how integrating visual arts such as paintings and films into medical curricula can enhance observational abilities, promote team collaboration, improve communication, and foster reflective thinking among medical students. The author describes a revamped curriculum at Bond University Medical School that includes workshops using visual thinking strategies, mixed media assignments, and community art exhibits to encourage visual literacy. Evaluations show positive feedback from students regarding the integration of arts education into medical training.
