## Emotions Evoked by the Sound of Music: Characterization, Classification, and Measurement

The article explores the emotional responses elicited by music, aiming to characterize and classify these emotions through empirical studies. The research includes four interconnected studies that compile emotion terms relevant to music, examine variations in emotional responses based on musical genre and type of response (felt vs perceived), and construct a model for measuring musically induced emotions using factor analysis. The study reveals significant differences in the emotional impacts across various types of music and proposes a domain-specific scale, the Geneva Emotional Music Scale, to better capture these nuanced experiences.
