## Evaluation, Research and Audits in Healthcare

The text outlines the distinctions between evaluation, research, and audits in healthcare contexts. It clarifies that while evaluations assess value and effectiveness of interventions, research aims to draw generalizable conclusions about how interventions work. Audits ensure adherence to standards without altering existing practices or introducing new interventions. The chapter also discusses debates around experimental methods like randomized controlled trials (RCTs) for assessing arts-in-health interventions, suggesting these may not be fully suitable due to the complexity and multifaceted nature of such programs.
