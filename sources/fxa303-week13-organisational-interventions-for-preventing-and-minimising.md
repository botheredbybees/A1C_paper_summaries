## Organisational interventions for preventing and minimising aggression directed toward healthcare workers by patients and patient advocates

This Cochrane Review protocol assesses the effectiveness of organizational interventions aimed at preventing and minimizing workplace aggression directed towards healthcare workers by patients and patient advocates. The prevalence of such aggressive behavior in healthcare settings is increasingly recognized as a significant occupational hazard, impacting both individuals and organizations adversely. Common causes of this aggression include mental health conditions, substance abuse, and communication issues between staff and service users. The protocol differentiates external aggression from patients or their advocates (the focus of the review) from internal workplace bullying, which is covered in a separate Cochrane review. Key findings indicate that up to 29% of healthcare workers experience physical aggression from patients, while up to 40% face aggressive behavior from co-workers or supervisors over various time periods. The review highlights the need for interventions that address organizational and environmental factors to mitigate these risks, recognizing that current literature lacks consistent definitions of workplace aggression terms such as harassment, violence, incivility, and bullying. Understanding these nuances is crucial for developing effective prevention strategies in healthcare environments.
