## Professional Responsibilities and Ethical Considerations in Research

This chapter discusses the professional responsibilities of researchers towards participants, colleagues, and the public. It outlines general responsibility to avoid deliberate harm and misconduct, as well as the obligation to ensure termination or continuity of care under certain conditions. Ethical considerations include honesty, avoiding conflicts of interest, maintaining personal boundaries, and addressing ethical misconduct promptly. Additionally, it emphasizes the importance of selecting research questions with potential benefits for participants and balancing individual agendas among researchers, funders, and sponsors to avoid biases. The chapter also covers safeguarding procedures during study design to mitigate risks of physical, psychological, social, economic, or legal harm to participants and discusses participant confidentiality and appropriate data collection measures.
