## Miss Lily's Fabulous Feather Boa

Miss Lily's Fabulous Feather Boa is a delightful puppet theatre production adapted from the book by Margaret Wild and Kerry Argent, directed by Michael Barlow. The show features charming characters such as Miss Lily, a flamboyant crocodile, and a lonely potoroo who finds her confidence at Miss Lily’s Tropical Holiday House. Through these characters, the play subtly addresses themes of conservation, non-judgment based on appearance, building others’ confidence, family, and belonging. The production's puppetry design by Iona McAuley brings these characters to life in various forms, with strong performances by Cezara Critti-Schnaars as Miss Lily and Tristan McInnes as the potoroo. The play is designed to entertain children during school holidays, combining humor with poignant messages that resonate with young audiences.
