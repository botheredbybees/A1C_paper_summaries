## Scenes from The Iliad in Ancient Art

This article explores various representations of scenes from Homer's 'The Iliad' within ancient Greek art forms such as drinking cups, water jars, mixing bowls, vases, plates, jugs, friezes, mosaics, and frescoes. Each piece visualizes an episode from the epic poem in distinct ways, often incorporating artistic conventions like 'heroic nudity.' The images depict key moments including Achilles' rage against Agamemnon, the taking of Briseis, Menelaus dueling Paris, Aeneas being wounded by Diomedes, Hector and Ajax's combat, and more. These artworks reflect both historical battle scenes and mythological interpretations, often blending Homer’s descriptions with contemporary Greek military attire and artistic styles.
