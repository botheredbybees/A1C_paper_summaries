## Creative Arts Interventions for Stress Management and Prevention—A Systematic Review

The article presents a systematic review of studies examining the efficacy of Creative Arts Therapies (CATs) and arts interventions in managing and preventing stress. The review includes art, music, dance/movement, and drama therapy modalities. Studies were selected based on their relevance to stress reduction using the PICOS principle and evaluated for evidence levels. Results indicate that over 80% of included studies reported significant reductions in stress among participants due to interventions from these four arts modalities. The review underscores the importance of CATs in addressing the growing problem of stress-related health issues, especially given its increasing prevalence in modern society. Additionally, it highlights how embodied approaches can be more effective than purely cognitive or behavioral methods for managing stress.
