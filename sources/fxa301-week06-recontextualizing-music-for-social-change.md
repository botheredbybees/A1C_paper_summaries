## Recontextualizing Music For Social Change

The document 'Recontextualizing Music For Social Change' by Mario Vázquez Morillas explores innovative methods to transform traditional vocal recitals into multidisciplinary performances that serve a specific social purpose. The paper focuses on using elements like innovation, ritualistic significance of music, and the potential for social change through artistic expression. Two doctoral recital performances are described: 'Defatalizing the Femme Fatale' and 'Un-Labelling Love'. The first performance aims to challenge negative stereotypes about women by reinterpreting Shostakovich's Michelangelo Suite alongside feminist poetry and prose. The second, 'Un-Labelling Love', uses a scientific lecture format to explore romantic love through art songs and narration, questioning traditional heteronormative assumptions. Both performances aim to promote social change and awareness regarding gender identity issues.
