## The nature of awe: Elicitors, appraisals, and effects on self-concept

This article explores the nature of awe, a distinct emotion characterized by elicitors that are vast and necessitate cognitive accommodation. The studies presented document the self-diminishing effects of awe experiences compared to other emotions like pride or joy, which tend to enhance self-concept. Key findings include the information-focused nature of awe elicitors, their asocial quality, and the impact on individuals' willingness to modify mental structures, particularly in relation to broad group identifications. The research underscores awe's role in expanding one's perception beyond oneself, contributing to a more inclusive self-concept that emphasizes universal membership rather than individual achievement.
