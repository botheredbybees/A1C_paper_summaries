## Week 4 Overview - FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing

This document provides an overview for Week 4 of the FXA303 Creative Arts, Play and Mental Wellbeing course. The week focuses on the contributions of creative arts and play to supporting mental wellbeing and social skill development in neurodiverse children and adolescents, particularly those with autism. Neurodiversity is defined as the diversity of brain development among individuals, which may lead to varied sensory perceptions, learning styles, and responses. The course contrasts medical and social models of disability, emphasizing that the social model values an individual’s agency and identity beyond their condition, whereas the medical model views disability primarily in terms of deficits requiring treatment or cure. For neurodiverse individuals, creative arts and play can support communication, emotional regulation, and learning, illustrating the importance of inclusive environments and tailored educational strategies.
