## The role, function and identity of music therapists in the 21st century

Helen Odell-Miller's article explores the evolving identity and function of music therapists in the 21st century, focusing on developments within the United Kingdom but also touching upon international trends. The piece highlights advancements in research indicating that music therapy should be widely available for populations such as those with dementia, autism, stroke, and mental health issues. Odell-Miller discusses how these advancements necessitate clear role definition and identity establishment among music therapists to effectively communicate their work. She emphasizes the importance of collaboration across multidisciplinary groups and celebrates achievements in research, thinking, and provision within the field of music therapy. The article also underscores the significance of recent research on music's impact on brain function, which contributes to a deeper understanding of how music therapy can benefit patients with cognitive decline.
