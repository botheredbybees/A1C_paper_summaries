## Therapeutic Photography: Enhancing Self-Esteem, Self-Efficacy, and Resilience

The content explores various photographic techniques in therapeutic contexts such as auto-driving, reflexive photography, and photo novella. These methods help participants interpret their own photographs or those presented by therapists to explore deeper personal meanings and social issues. John Collier is credited with pioneering photo elicitation for mental health research. The chapter discusses how photos can elicit perceptions, empower participants, reduce power imbalances, and be particularly effective when working with marginalized communities. It also highlights the benefits of using photography with children and adolescents to address bullying and trauma, emphasizing its potential in identity development and care planning.
