## Коррекция нейропсихических нарушений при болезни Альцгеймера

The article discusses the prevalence and impact of neuropsychiatric disorders (NPD) in Alzheimer's disease (AD), which affect up to 80% of patients and are a major cause of early hospitalization and increased healthcare costs. These symptoms, including agitation and aggression, can lead to significant distress for caregivers and worsen patient outcomes. Traditional pharmacological treatments often have limited efficacy and high toxicity, with antipsychotics being associated with severe side effects and higher mortality rates among AD patients. The study highlights the importance of non-pharmacological interventions that are more effective and safer than traditional medications. A clinical case is presented where memantine (Akatinol) was used to manage neuropsychiatric symptoms effectively without significant adverse reactions, suggesting its potential as a preferred treatment option for NPD in AD.
