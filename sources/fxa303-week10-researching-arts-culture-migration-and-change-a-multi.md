## Researching arts, culture, migration and change: a multi (trans)disciplinary challenge for international migration studies

The article explores the significance of researching the connections between migration, arts, and culture within international migration studies. It argues that this area has been relatively neglected in favor of disciplines such as demography, sociology, and political science. The author posits two reasons for this neglect: firstly, the perception that migrants primarily functioned as workers contributing to economic globalization; secondly, a broader disregard for arts and culture within social sciences and policy discussions. The paper outlines the growing interest since 2000 in exploring these intersections through various research networks like IMISCOE (International Migration Research Network), emphasizing multidisciplinary approaches that include sociology, anthropology, cultural studies, and political science. It also highlights recent efforts to expand this work into broader cultural forms including sports and fashion while engaging with methodologies such as visual studies and participatory arts-based research. The importance of examining these links is underscored by their potential to enrich our understanding of migration processes and to humanize the experiences of migrants.
