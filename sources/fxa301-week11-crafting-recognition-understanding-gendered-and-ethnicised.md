## Crafting Recognition: Understanding Gendered and Ethnicised Experiences in an Arts-Based Integration Project

This study explores the experiences of women participating in an arts-based integration project in Denmark, focusing on knitting, sewing, and crocheting activities designed to empower migrant and Danish women by professionalizing their handicrafts. The research reveals that gendered, ethnicized, and class-based categories are negotiated through these practices, influencing participants' perceptions of themselves and others within the group. National and cultural backgrounds significantly shape expectations and experiences, alongside factors such as family status and employment. The findings highlight the importance of social recognition and suggest that artistic practice enables members to engage with broader integration discourses while also positioning them within those frameworks.
