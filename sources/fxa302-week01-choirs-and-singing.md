## Choirs and Singing

The chapter 'Choirs and Singing' by Stephen Clift delves into the historical and contemporary perspectives on singing as a means of promoting well-being, particularly for individuals with long-term health conditions. The text begins by referencing William Byrd's insights from 1588 regarding the benefits of singing for health and well-being. It then discusses how modern research has substantiated these claims through systematic reviews and empirical studies conducted by the Sidney De Haan Research Centre at Canterbury Christ Church University. Clift outlines various research projects that have demonstrated positive mental, social, and physical health outcomes associated with regular group singing activities. These include benefits for older adults living independently, individuals managing chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and those experiencing long-term mental health challenges. The chapter also highlights the importance of competent facilitation and supportive environments in ensuring sustained engagement and improved well-being among participants. While significant progress has been made, Clift notes that there is still a need for broader policy implementation to increase access to singing programs across diverse populations.
