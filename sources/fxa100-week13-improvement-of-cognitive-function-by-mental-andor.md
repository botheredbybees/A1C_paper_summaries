## Improvement of Cognitive Function by Mental and/or Individualized Aerobic Training in Healthy Elderly Subjects

This study examines the effects of mental training, physical exercise, or a combination thereof on cognitive function in healthy elderly individuals aged between 60 and 76 years. The research involved assigning participants to one of four groups: aerobic training (AT), memory training (MT), combined aerobic and mental training (AMT), and a control group. All subjects underwent various cognitive tests before and after the two-month intervention period.

The findings indicate that both mental and physical training alone can enhance specific aspects of cognitive function such as logical memory, paired associates learning scores, and memory quotient. However, participants in the combined aerobic and mental training group showed significant improvements compared to those trained with either technique individually. These results suggest that personalized exercise regimens at the ventilatory threshold yield better physiological adaptations than standardized approaches.

The study underscores the potential for integrated cognitive exercises and physical activities to mitigate age-related declines in memory and learning capacity, offering valuable insights into non-pharmacological interventions aimed at maintaining mental acuity among older adults.
