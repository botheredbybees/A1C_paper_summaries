## Effects of Yoga-Like Exercises on Mild and Moderate Alzheimer Disease: A Randomized Controlled Trial Protocol

This research protocol outlines a randomized controlled trial designed to assess the efficacy of yoga-like exercises in patients with mild to moderate Alzheimer's disease (AD). The study aims to evaluate cognitive and psychological features, particularly focusing on attention, problem-solving abilities, mood, and neuropsychiatric symptoms. Patients will be randomly assigned to an intervention group involving 30 minutes of modified yoga-like activities three times a week for eight weeks or a control group with no intervention. Both groups undergo neurocognitive assessments at baseline and post-intervention periods. The anticipated results suggest improvements in attention, problem-solving skills, behavioral symptoms, visuospatial abilities, and balance due to the multi-component nature of the intervention. This study addresses gaps in current therapeutic approaches for AD by exploring gentle exercises that could be more accessible than traditional aerobic activities for patients with multiple disabilities.
