## Indigenous Methodology: An Ethical Systems Approach to Arts-Based Work with Native Communities in the U.S.

Michelle Napoli's article discusses the importance of Indigenous methodology in arts-based practices to address the mental health needs of Native communities, particularly within the context of historical colonialism and systemic racism. The author argues that considering an Indigenous approach is crucial for ethical and culturally respectful work with these populations. She outlines how Indigenous methodologies integrate sovereignty, critical analysis of harmful systems, and a focus on community resilience and survival. This framework respects and honors traditional knowledge, languages, and cultural practices while addressing contemporary health disparities through decolonizing approaches.
