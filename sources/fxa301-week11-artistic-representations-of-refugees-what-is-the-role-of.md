## Artistic Representations of Refugees: What Is the Role of the Artist?

The article by Isobel Blomfield and Caroline Lenette explores the ethical responsibilities of artists in collaborative projects involving refugees and asylum seekers. The authors argue that existing literature on this topic is sparse, highlighting a need for more robust discussions about the potential pitfalls and ethical considerations faced by artists in such collaborations. They discuss how artists can avoid perpetuating harmful stereotypes through genuine collaboration, informed consent, anonymity, focusing on mundane aspects of life, and being aware of the political nature of editing processes in filmmaking.

The paper emphasizes the importance of artists ensuring their own agendas are secondary to what refugees wish to convey, thereby valuing refugees' perspectives and voices. It provides examples of successful collaborative projects that promote nuanced tropes around diverse refugee experiences but also highlights ethical challenges such as perpetuating 'othering' narratives when artists lack personal experience with forced migration.

Through an autoethnographic reflection, Blomfield shares her insights from co-creating a short film featuring a young Somali asylum seeker. This case study underscores the need for reflexivity and ethical awareness among artists working in this area to ensure that their work is meaningful and contributes positively to public discourse on refugees.
