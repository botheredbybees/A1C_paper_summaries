## Laughter and Humor Therapy in Dialysis

This paper reviews the evidence for laughter and humor therapy as a medical intervention in dialysis patients, drawing on studies from related fields such as pediatrics, geriatrics, mental health, and oncology. The authors note that although there is anecdotal support for these therapies, limited empirical evidence exists to substantiate their physiological or psychological benefits specifically in chronic kidney disease (CKD) or dialysis contexts. They explore various forms of laughter therapy ranging from humorous videos and stories to more structured approaches like Laughter Yoga. Key findings suggest potential improvements in depression, anxiety, pain, immunity, fatigue, sleep quality, respiratory function, and blood glucose levels. However, the evidence is mixed and often lacking rigorous study designs, indicating a need for further research. The paper highlights that humor therapy has shown promise in improving quality of life and resilience in other chronic conditions, which may have implications for dialysis patients given similar health impacts. Overall, while laughter therapies offer potential therapeutic benefits, more controlled studies are necessary to establish their efficacy within the specific context of dialysis.
