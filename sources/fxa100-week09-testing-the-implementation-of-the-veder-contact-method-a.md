## Testing the Implementation of the Veder Contact Method: A Theatre-Based Communication Method in Dementia Care

The study assesses the implementation of the Veder Contact Method (VCM), a person-centered care approach using theatrical, poetic, and musical communication in dementia care settings. Conducted as a quasi-experimental study over one year with caregivers and residents across experimental and control wards, it evaluates changes in caregiver behavior, resident quality of life and mood, and care plan content. Key findings indicate significant improvements in communicative behavior among caregivers on high implementation score wards compared to low implementation score wards and controls, along with positive impacts on some aspects of resident well-being. However, no significant differences were observed in caregivers' attitudes or residents’ moods between the groups. The study highlights the importance of successful implementation factors for achieving positive outcomes in dementia care.
