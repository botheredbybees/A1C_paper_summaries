## Therapeutic Puppetry and Wellbeing

Therapeutic Puppetry and Wellbeing explores how puppets can be used as tools for mental health interventions, particularly in clinical therapy settings. Puppets are valuable diagnostic tools that allow children and adults to express deep emotions or unformed thoughts through non-verbal means or limited verbal expression with the puppet. They serve as a safe distance for processing memories and experiences, providing insights into family dynamics and trauma. However, the close identification with puppets can sometimes become more intrusive, leading to potential pitfalls such as overt aggression during play sessions. The text highlights that choosing animal-like puppets with specific attributes may be preferable over those resembling people, as they offer a safer emotional distance. Additionally, it mentions that making one's own puppets can help reduce hallucinations and reveal deeper psychological states in individuals diagnosed with schizophrenia. Overall, the document emphasizes numerous ways to use puppets for promoting mental wellbeing by facilitating self-expression, catharsis, awareness of feelings, problem-solving, and coping strategies. These characteristics enable puppets to captivate audiences and provide therapeutic benefits across various age groups.
