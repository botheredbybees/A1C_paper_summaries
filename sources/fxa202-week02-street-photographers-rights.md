## Street photographer’s rights

The document 'Street Photographer’s Rights' provides guidance on legal considerations for those taking photographs in public and private spaces. It addresses the general freedom to take photos of people, buildings, and landmarks in public places without needing permission, though it notes that this is subject to limitations such as privacy laws and anti-voyeurism statutes. The sheet advises photographers to be prepared for interactions with authorities or concerned members of the public by carrying information on their rights. For commercial photography involving individuals, obtaining a model release form is recommended. When dealing with private property, explicit permission from the landowner is necessary; failure to obtain it may result in legal actions such as trespassing charges and civil suits. The document also discusses exceptions for educational institutions, hospitals, shopping malls, and markets that are privately operated but accessible to the public. It highlights the importance of respecting privacy and community standards to avoid criminal offenses related to invasive photography.
