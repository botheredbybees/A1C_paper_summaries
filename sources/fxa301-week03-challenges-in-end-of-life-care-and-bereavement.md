## Challenges in End-of-Life Care and Bereavement

The document highlights significant challenges within end-of-life care, emphasizing longstanding inequities in access to palliative care services and rising demand due to demographic shifts and complex health conditions. It outlines barriers such as institutional cultures, lack of public awareness, and disparities affecting rural or socially deprived populations and ethnic minorities. The text underscores the importance of a personalized, integrated, and community-based approach to end-of-life care, with creative arts playing a crucial role in supporting both patients and caregivers. Creative health interventions can alleviate physical symptoms, foster emotional expression, and provide psychological support, enhancing quality of life for individuals nearing the end of life and their loved ones. Additionally, it mentions the use of advance care planning and innovative approaches like co-produced workshops to improve access to such services among marginalized communities.
