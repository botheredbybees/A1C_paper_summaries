## Psychometric Properties

The document 'Psychometric Properties' by Annie T. Ginty discusses the fundamental aspects of psychometrics, which involves constructing and validating measurement instruments to ensure they are reliable and valid measures in behavioral medicine contexts such as measuring individuals’ knowledge, personality, behaviors, and abilities. The text emphasizes that reliability pertains to a consistent measure across various conditions (e.g., repeated testing sessions), while validity is about accurately assessing the intended trait or behavior. A key point illustrated through an example involving darts demonstrates that being reliable does not necessarily equate to being valid; hitting the same spot consistently but missing the bull's eye represents reliability without validity. Conversely, a measure can be both reliable and valid if it hits the target (valid) and is consistent in doing so (reliable). The author also highlights the importance of ensuring that new scales used for measuring conditions such as depression are both reliable and valid to accurately assess depressive symptoms without conflating them with other moods or behaviors.
