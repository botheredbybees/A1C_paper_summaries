## Is There Compelling Evidence for Using the Arts in Health Care?

This document provides a comprehensive review of existing literature on the use of arts and culture to improve healthcare outcomes. The authors highlight that incorporating the arts can lead to more effective and efficient delivery of healthcare services, potentially resulting in cost savings through better symptom management and reduced health service utilization. Studies suggest that participating in arts-based practices can alleviate pain, reduce anxiety, enhance self-efficacy, and promote social interaction among patients. The review covers various settings, from acute care environments to community programs aimed at specific groups like the elderly or those with mental health issues. Evidence is drawn from multiple sources including case studies, systematic reviews, and large-scale epidemiological research, indicating that arts interventions are effective complements rather than alternatives to traditional medical treatments. This work underscores the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration between healthcare providers and artists in developing programs tailored to diverse populations.
