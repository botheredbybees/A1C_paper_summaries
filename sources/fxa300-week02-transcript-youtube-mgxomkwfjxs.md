## Anatomy and Functions of the Cerebral Cortex

The video discusses the structure and functions of the cerebral cortex, which is the outer layer of gray matter in the cerebrum. It covers the gyri (ridges) and sulci (grooves) that characterize its surface and explains how these folds increase the cortex's surface area, allowing for more complex information processing. The cerebral cortex is divided into four main lobes based on cranial bones: frontal, parietal, temporal, and occipital. Each lobe handles specific sensory or motor functions; notably, visual and somatosensory information tends to be processed in the opposite hemisphere of the brain, whereas other senses are typically processed bilaterally. Motor commands also cross hemispheres: for example, left cerebral cortex controls movements on the right side of the body. The video further distinguishes between primary cortices (handling basic sensory or motor functions) and association cortices (integrating information to perform more complex cognitive tasks). Language processing is predominantly localized in the left hemisphere for most individuals. Attention functions are highlighted as being processed by both hemispheres, with specific roles assigned to each side.
