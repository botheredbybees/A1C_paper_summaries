## What is Drama Therapy?

This webpage provides an overview of drama therapy, explaining it as the intentional use of drama and theater processes to achieve therapeutic goals. It discusses the roles of registered drama therapists (RDTs), the educational requirements for becoming a RDT, and how drama therapy works through various techniques such as improvisation, theater games, storytelling, and enactment. The page also highlights potential client populations that can benefit from this form of therapy, including those recovering from addiction or dealing with social stigmatization, isolation, and conflict. Additionally, it outlines the North American Drama Therapy Association's (NADTA) role in establishing professional standards and promoting drama therapy through advocacy and conferences.
