## The associations between behavioral-psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) and coping strategy, burden of care and personality style among low-income caregivers of patients with dementia

This study aimed to explore the associations between behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) and caregiver burden, with a focus on the mediating roles of coping strategies and personality traits. The research was conducted as a cross-sectional study involving 202 caregivers in Malaysia who were taking care of patients with dementia at home-based settings. Caregivers completed questionnaires assessing BPSD using Neuropsychiatric Inventory-Questionnaire (NPI-Q), caregiver burden via Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI), coping strategies through Brief COPE, and personality traits utilizing Big-Five Inventory.

The findings revealed that irritability, apathy, and agitation were the most frequently observed types of BPSD among patients with dementia. High levels of caregiver burden were associated with BPSD, except for anxiety, elation, and appetite disturbances. Among personality traits, only conscientiousness was found to mediate the relationship between BPSD and caregiver burden. Coping strategies such as self-distraction, active coping, planning, and acceptance also had a significant mediation effect on this relationship.

The study highlights the importance of understanding the interplay between BPSD, caregiver burden, personality traits, and coping mechanisms to provide tailored support for caregivers in managing their stress and improving their well-being. These insights are crucial for developing interventions aimed at enhancing the quality of life for both patients with dementia and their caregivers.
