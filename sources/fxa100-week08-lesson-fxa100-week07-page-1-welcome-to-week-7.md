## Participatory Visual Arts in Dementia Care

This week's dementia care module explores the use of visual arts as a therapeutic intervention. It highlights how participatory art programs provide cognitive stimulation and emotional support to individuals with dementia, aiming to alleviate social isolation, depression, and enhance self-actualization. The lesson discusses research findings indicating that up to 83% of Australian aged care facilities incorporate visual arts activities facilitated by artists or staff. Key factors for successful interventions include fostering a non-judgmental environment, providing participant choice, and having empathetic facilitators experienced in dementia care. Scholar et al.'s (2019) research underscores the importance of these elements in enhancing quality of life through cognitive engagement and person-centered approaches.
