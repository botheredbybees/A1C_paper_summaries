## Age-related differences in white matter diffusion measures in autism spectrum condition

This study examines age-related differences in white matter diffusion measures among individuals with autism spectrum condition (ASC) and typically developing controls aged between 7 to 17 years old, using a spatially unbiased tract-based spatial statistics (TBSS) approach. The research identifies significant age-related variations in white matter connectivity within widespread brain regions for ASC compared to neurotypical peers. Fractional anisotropy (FA) measures positively associate with age in both groups but show stronger associations in the ASC group, while radial diffusivity (RD) measures negatively correlate with age more strongly in individuals with ASC. The findings suggest that males on the autism spectrum exhibit a distinct trajectory of white matter maturation compared to neurotypical controls. However, limitations such as limited generalizability due to inclusion only right-handed males and IQ > 70 participants restrict the broader applicability of these results. Future longitudinal studies are recommended to better understand the developmental course of white matter changes in ASC.
