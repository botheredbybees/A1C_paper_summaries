## Absorption and spiritual experience: A review of evidence and potential mechanisms

This paper reviews evidence suggesting that the trait of absorption, a tendency to become deeply engrossed in sensory or imaginative experiences, is linked to spiritual and mystical experiences. The authors discuss how individuals with high scores on the Tellegen Absorption Scale report vivid experiences such as hearing God's voice during prayer, intense mystical experiences from psychedelics or placebo brain stimulation, and feelings of presence and transcendence when encountering natural beauty, virtual reality, or music. They propose that absorption intensifies inner and outer sensory experiences in a way that reflects both prior expectations and novel engagements, enabling individuals to blur the boundaries between imagination and perception, thereby making the supernatural seem more real.
