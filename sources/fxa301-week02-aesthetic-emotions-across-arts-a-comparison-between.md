## Aesthetic Emotions Across Arts: A Comparison Between Painting and Music

This study investigates the emotional responses to art in the form of painting and music, aiming to compare their frequency, contributing factors, and motivations. Conducted via an online survey with participants interested in both arts (N = 971), the research found that aesthetic emotions experienced while looking at paintings differ from those elicited by listening to music. For instance, wonder is more frequently reported for painting than joy or power for music. The study also revealed that emotional responses to music are often related to everyday life situations, such as tenderness and nostalgia. Stimulus features were seen as significant in influencing emotions towards paintings, while factors like prior mood, physical context, and social presence played a larger role in music-related emotions. Additionally, participants with visual arts education experienced more vitality-driven emotions when viewing art compared to those without formal training. This research highlights the diversity of emotional experiences in different artistic mediums and underscores the importance of considering both intrinsic characteristics of stimuli and individual/situational factors.
