## Introduction to Week 3: Listening to Music

This document introduces Week 3 of a course on arts and dementia care, focusing on the benefits of listening to music for individuals with dementia. The introduction highlights how music can improve quality of life by leveraging aural memory and triggering emotional responses. It mentions two internationally recognized programs: The Music and Memory project and the Power of Music and Dementia Study, which explore the efficacy of music-based interventions in dementia care. Additionally, it references cutting-edge research from the University of Melbourne on using wearable devices to adjust musical playlists based on agitation levels. The document also discusses cognitive benefits like rhythmic entrainment and synchronization, emotional responses, and dopamine release. It concludes with an activity that involves creating a culturally and generationally appropriate playlist for individuals living with dementia.
