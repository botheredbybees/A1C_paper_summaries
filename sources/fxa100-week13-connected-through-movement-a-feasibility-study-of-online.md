## Connected through movement: a feasibility study of online mindfulness-based dance/movement therapy for older adults with age-related cognitive decline during COVID-19

This study investigates the feasibility of an online mindfulness-based dance/movement therapy (M-DMT) program for older adults with age-related cognitive decline during the COVID-19 pandemic. The research explores whether M-DMT can alleviate loneliness, enhance psychological well-being, and promote physical activity among this demographic. Sixteen participants engaged in a 12-week online group intervention. Key findings indicate that the program met all feasibility criteria, resulting in significant reductions in loneliness and depression alongside enhanced positive affect and psychological well-being. Although there was an increase in physical activity, it did not reach statistical significance. Qualitative feedback highlighted improved social connectedness, overall quality of life, body awareness, and kinematic strategy among participants. The study concludes that online M-DMT holds promise in addressing the challenges of loneliness and mental health for older adults with cognitive decline, necessitating further research to validate these initial findings.
