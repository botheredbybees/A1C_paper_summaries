## Creative Arts in Health Professional Education and Practice: A Case Study Reflection

This chapter examines the role of creative arts in nursing and midwifery education at King’s College London's Florence Nightingale School of Nursing and Midwifery, focusing on the Culture and Care program. The authors explore how engagement with the creative arts can enhance students' ethical and values-based practices, aligning with Jane Cummings’ Compassion in Practice strategy that emphasizes care, compassion, competence, communication, courage, and commitment. The chapter details a complex intervention model that integrates creative arts into individual modules and throughout the curriculum, fostering an environment where staff and students explore emotional responses to care and practice creativity. Collaborations both within the college and with external partners have contributed significantly to the success of these initiatives. Specific activities like photography sessions, jewelry making workshops, historical film screenings, a choir, and composer residencies are highlighted as part of this multifaceted approach. The authors also discuss challenges in implementing such complex interventions and emphasize the importance of collaboration for sustained impact.
