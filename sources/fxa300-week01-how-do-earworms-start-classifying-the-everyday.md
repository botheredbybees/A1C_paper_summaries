## How do “Earworms” Start? Classifying the Everyday Circumstances of Involuntary Musical Imagery

This article explores the phenomenon of involuntary musical imagery (INMI) or “earworms,” where a tune enters and repeats in one’s mind without conscious control. The study uses qualitative analysis to classify reports of everyday INMI circumstances within two population samples—listeners of BBC Radio 6 Music and an online survey respondent group. Four abstract categories describe the characteristics of these circumstances: music exposure, memory triggers, affective states, and low attention states. The research highlights how different media can result in earworms and discusses their impact on voluntary musical engagement. It suggests that varying emotional and attentional conditions may influence the onset of INMI episodes.
