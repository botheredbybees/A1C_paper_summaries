## The Activities-specific Balance Confidence (ABC) Scale in Youth with Visual Impairments: Psychometrics and a Population-specific Short Form

This study examines the psychometric properties of the Activities-specific Balance Confidence (ABC) Scale in youth with visual impairments (VI). The ABC scale, a measure grounded in self-efficacy theory, assesses individuals' confidence in performing daily activities without falling. While widely used and validated across various populations, its appropriateness for youth with VI had not been extensively investigated prior to this study.

The research aimed to establish the reliability and validity of ABC scores in youth with VI, as well as to develop a short form version specific to this population. The findings suggest that total and item-level scores on the ABC scale demonstrated strong-to-acceptable levels of reliability and validity in youth with VI. However, the derived short form (ABC-6VI) showed certain limitations such as increased variability and decreased stability compared to the full-scale.

The study highlights the importance of psychometric validation for specific populations, emphasizing that assessments designed for one demographic may not translate directly to others without thorough testing and refinement. The research underscores the need for tailored assessment tools in clinical settings dealing with youth who have visual impairments.
