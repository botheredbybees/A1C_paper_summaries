## What is the evidence on the role of the arts in improving health and well-being? A scoping review

This report summarizes a scoping review that analyzed over 900 publications, including reviews, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and individual studies. The review found two broad themes regarding the role of arts in health and well-being: prevention and promotion; management and treatment. Prevention and promotion involve social determinants like social cohesion and inequalities, child development support, encouraging healthy behaviors, and preventing ill health through enhancing mental health and reducing trauma impact. Management and treatment include helping people experiencing mental illness, supporting acute condition care, aiding neurodevelopmental/neurological disorders, managing noncommunicable diseases, and end-of-life care. The arts are shown to foster social cohesion, reduce loneliness, enhance cooperation among diverse groups, improve cognitive skills, support conflict resolution, and build community capital.
