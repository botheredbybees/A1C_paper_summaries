## Singing with Pride: How Queer Choirs Strive for a Better World

This article discusses the role of queer choirs in providing safe spaces and fostering social justice through artistic expression. It traces the origins of gay choruses, such as San Francisco Gay Men's Chorus (SFGMC) formed in 1978, which was a response to the political climate at that time, including the assassination of Harvey Milk and the AIDS pandemic. Similarly, New York City Gay Men’s Chorus (NYCGMC) established less than a year later, serves as an emblematic voice for LGBTQ individuals. Queer choirs from Australia have evolved differently, typically embracing mixed-gender compositions. The article highlights how queer choirs contribute significantly to community visibility and acceptance, and how these choirs are inclusive of diverse gender identities and ethnic backgrounds. Additionally, it explores the impact of queer choirs in Asia and South America, where such groups face more challenges.
