## This Is Your Brain On Music: Understanding a Human Obsession - Chapter 4

In Chapter 4 of 'This Is Your Brain On Music', Daniel Levitin explores the concept of musical anticipation and how it shapes emotional responses to music. He discusses how composers use various techniques, such as deceptive cadences in classical music and unexpected chord progressions in rock, to create a sense of anticipation that is then skillfully manipulated to evoke strong emotions like joy or sadness. The chapter delves into specific examples from artists including Haydn, Steely Dan, Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and The Beatles, illustrating how these techniques work across different genres. Levitin also touches on the cognitive processes involved in perceiving music, noting that our brains construct a reality based partly on learned musical structures, which influence our expectations and emotional reactions to new sounds.
