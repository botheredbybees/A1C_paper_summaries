## A Meta-Analysis of Expressive Writing on Posttraumatic Stress, Posttraumatic Growth, and Quality of Life

This meta-analysis evaluates the efficacy of expressive writing in promoting positive psychological and physical health outcomes, particularly for individuals with posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD), posttraumatic growth, and quality of life. The study synthesizes findings from various studies employing expressive writing tasks across different populations and outcome variables. Results indicate a small overall effect size for PTSD reduction, while effects on posttraumatic growth and quality of life were negligible to small. However, the analysis found medium to large effects in studies requiring a diagnosis of PTSD. This research underscores the importance of considering symptom severity and other moderators when interpreting the efficacy of expressive writing interventions.
