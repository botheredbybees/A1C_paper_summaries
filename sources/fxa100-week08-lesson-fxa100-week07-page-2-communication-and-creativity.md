## Communication through Visual Arts

This lesson from the FXA100 course discusses how creativity through visual arts can serve as a potent form of non-verbal communication for individuals with dementia. It explores two modes of art-making described by Killick and Craig (2011): internal dialogue, where artwork is a personal expression not intended to be shared; and outward communication, meant for others to see. The lesson highlights the importance of creating an environment that encourages creativity without inhibition or self-critique. Examples include 'The Art of Alzheimer's' exhibition in Seattle, which showcases artworks by individuals with dementia, and the Memories in the Making program, designed to help those with dementia express memories and emotions through painting.
