## Week 9 Lecture - Dr Brendan Lamb

The Week 9 lecture of FXA301 focuses on inclusive arts and their role in health and wellbeing outcomes for individuals with disabilities or chronic illnesses. The term 'inclusive arts' is defined as programs that foster involvement through creative activities, recognizing diverse experiences while avoiding a sole therapeutic focus. Key points include the importance of inclusive language to reduce offense and exclusion, distinguishing between impairment and disability according to the social model, which views disability as arising from societal barriers rather than inherent personal limitations. Other models are discussed for their applicability in cases where medical intervention can provide benefits. The lecture emphasizes the expressive potential of creative arts, enabling self-agency and enhancing quality of life. Case studies highlight inclusive projects such as a choir for individuals with neurological communication disorders, which demonstrated improved communication skills and social interaction. Another example involves art programs for people with multiple sclerosis, showing improvements in coping skills, pain reduction, and enhanced self-esteem. Recommendations for planning inclusive arts projects include identifying the target group, ensuring benefits to participants, conducting relevant research, securing specialist input, involving participants in decision-making, delivering effective sessions, evaluating outcomes, and budgeting appropriately.
