## Child Development: The Critical First 1000 Days

This lesson explores the critical period of child development from the last trimester of pregnancy to a child's second birthday. It highlights how nutrition, care quality, cognitive stimulation, and environmental factors during this period significantly influence brain development, laying the groundwork for lifelong health and well-being. The material discusses the importance of neuroplasticity and the rapid growth of neural connections during early childhood, emphasizing prevention as a key strategy to optimize healthy brain development. Terminology such as cerebrum, neurons, white matter, grey matter, cortical thickness, myelination, and neuroplasticity is explained. Additionally, the lesson covers major theories of child development by Erikson, Piaget, and Vygotsky, detailing stages of cognitive and psychosocial growth and their relevance in understanding children's learning processes.
