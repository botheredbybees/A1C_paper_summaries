## Portraits of care: medical research through portraiture

The study 'Portraits of Care' used portraiture as a method to investigate the human dimensions of care at the intersection of art and medicine, involving both qualitative and quantitative research techniques. The study included 26 patient and 20 caregiver subjects across various ages and health conditions. Portraits were drawn by an artist to explore the identity and psychological status of patients and caregivers. Analysis revealed that patients appeared as whole individuals rather than being fragmented by their diagnoses, while caregivers' portraits highlighted their commitment and mutual relationships with those they care for. The public exhibition provided feedback emphasizing themes such as identity transformation, mortality awareness, hope amidst adversity, and empathy in healthcare. This study is unique in its use of visual arts to understand the human aspects of medical care.
