## "I made you a small room in my heart": how therapeutic clowns meet the needs of older adults in nursing homes

This study explores how therapeutic clowns, often referred to as elder-clowns in nursing home settings, meet the diverse needs of older adults through creative and emotional engagement. The research focuses on twenty-three experienced therapeutic clowns from eight countries who were interviewed to understand the unique contributions of their work within nursing homes. Participants identified five major needs among older residents: escaping routine, reassurance of worth, meaningful social interaction despite communication barriers, culturally relevant reminiscence opportunities, and a space for personal authenticity. The study highlights how elder-clowning can address these needs through artistic strategies that foster a sense of belonging and emotional support. This form of therapy not only improves the quality of life for residents but also positively impacts nursing staff and overall interpersonal interactions in the residence.
