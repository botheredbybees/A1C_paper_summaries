## Music Activates Regions of the Brain Spared by Alzheimer’s Disease

The study conducted at the University of Utah Health explores how music can activate specific regions of the brain that remain intact in individuals with Alzheimer’s disease, particularly the salience network. The research involved scanning participants' brains using functional MRI while they listened to familiar and unfamiliar musical clips or silence. Results indicated that listening to personally meaningful music enhanced connectivity between various neural networks including the visual, executive, and cerebellar systems. This activation suggests potential for personalized music interventions in managing symptoms such as anxiety and depression among dementia patients. Although promising, the study notes limitations due to its small sample size and lack of long-term outcomes data.
