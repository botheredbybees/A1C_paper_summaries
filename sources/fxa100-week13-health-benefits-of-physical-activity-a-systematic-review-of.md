## Health benefits of physical activity: a systematic review of current systematic reviews

The article presents a systematic review of recent systematic reviews to evaluate the dose-response relationship between physical activity and health outcomes, particularly premature mortality and chronic disease prevention. The authors challenge the conventional messaging that emphasizes meeting specific thresholds for physical activity (e.g., 150 minutes per week) as necessary to achieve significant health benefits. They argue that substantial evidence demonstrates clinically relevant health improvements can be achieved with much lower levels of physical activity. This finding is crucial for public health promotion and clinical practice, suggesting that simply increasing one's level of activity from an inactive state yields notable health benefits. The review highlights the importance of personalized approaches in promoting physical activity to avoid creating unnecessary barriers for individuals looking to improve their health through increased physical activity.
