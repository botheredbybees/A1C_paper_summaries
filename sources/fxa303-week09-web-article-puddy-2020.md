## This year's Australian bushfires prompt calls to heed lessons learnt after Ash Wednesday trauma

The article examines the long-term psychological impact of bushfires, focusing on the Ash Wednesday fires in 1983. It highlights how victims like Susan Laundy suffer from PTSD years after the event and discusses Professor Sandy McFarlane's research on the lingering trauma effects on children and adults. The article underscores the importance of long-term mental health support for those affected by bushfires, noting that many seek help only years later. It also provides advice for parents to protect their children's psychological well-being during such disasters.
