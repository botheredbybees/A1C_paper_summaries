## Leisure Activities and the Risk of Dementia in the Elderly

The study investigates the relationship between participation in leisure activities and the risk of developing dementia among individuals aged over 75 years who did not have dementia at baseline. The researchers found that engaging in cognitive activities such as reading, playing board games, participating in musical instruments, and dancing were associated with a reduced likelihood of developing various types of dementia, including Alzheimer’s disease and vascular dementia. This association remained significant after controlling for age, sex, educational level, pre-existing medical conditions, and initial cognitive status. The study also found that higher participation in cognitive activities was linked to slower rates of decline in memory over time. These findings suggest that engaging in mentally stimulating activities may contribute to maintaining cognitive reserve and reducing the risk of dementia.
