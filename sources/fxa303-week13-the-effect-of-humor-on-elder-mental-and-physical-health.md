## The effect of humor on elder mental and physical health

The study investigates the impact of humor therapy on mental and physical health among community-dwelling older adults. Through a quasi-experimental design, participants were assessed at baseline and six months later for measures including anxiety, depression, general well-being, quality of life, and physical health indicators. The findings indicate that engaging in a humor therapy workshop significantly reduced anxiety and depression levels while improving overall mental well-being compared to the control group. However, no significant differences were observed regarding physical health outcomes such as general health status or quality of life measures. These results suggest that humor interventions could be beneficial for enhancing mental health among older adults.
