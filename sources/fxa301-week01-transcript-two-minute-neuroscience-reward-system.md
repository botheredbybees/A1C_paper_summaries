## Two-Minute Neuroscience: Reward System

This transcript from Two-Minute Neuroscience introduces the concept of the brain's reward system, primarily focusing on the mesolimbic dopamine pathway. It explains that when exposed to rewarding stimuli, such as addictive drugs, the brain increases the release of dopamine through this pathway connecting the ventral tegmental area (VTA) and the nucleus accumbens in the ventral striatum. The role of dopamine is discussed beyond merely causing pleasure; it is involved in assigning importance to environmental cues associated with rewards and increasing reward-seeking behavior.
