## Reimagining Trauma Through Therapeutic Self Portraiture

This reflective essay explores the use of photography as a personal therapeutic modality to address post-traumatic experiences and emotional healing. The author, Abbie Miller, employs Greek mythological figures to symbolically represent internal states associated with trauma. Through technical processes such as depth of field manipulation, compositing, and color grading in Adobe Lightroom, Miller creates images that visually encode psychological conditions like dissociation and hyper-focus typical in PTSD sufferers. These techniques are used to articulate the fragmented nature of self-experience and emotional continuity following traumatic events.

Miller’s project also utilizes familiar domestic spaces as liminal zones for symbolic exploration and healing. The therapeutic process is framed within a theoretical context that includes Jungian archetypes, therapeutic photography practices, and color psychology. Ethical engagement with personal trauma through the photographic medium is emphasized to ensure safety and self-awareness throughout the creative process.

By engaging mythological figures and digital manipulation techniques, Miller demonstrates how traumatic experiences can be externalized and recontextualized in a way that facilitates emotional healing and recovery.
