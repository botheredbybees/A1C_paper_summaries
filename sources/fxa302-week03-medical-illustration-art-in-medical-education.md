## Medical Illustration: Art in Medical Education

In this article, Dr. Rachel Hajar discusses the historical significance and educational value of medical illustrations in medical education. The author traces the origins of anatomical representations back to prehistoric cave paintings and highlights the pivotal role of Alexandria in antiquity as a hub for scientific and intellectual development, particularly in medicine. Key figures such as Herophilus and Erasistratus contributed significantly to our understanding of anatomy through dissections, although their detailed illustrations are lost. The article also explores how historical misconceptions about the heart's function influenced language and literature, emphasizing the shift from heart-centric views to the recognition of brain dominance over emotions, thoughts, and intelligence. Hajar underscores the enduring importance of visual aids in medical education for clarity and comprehension.
