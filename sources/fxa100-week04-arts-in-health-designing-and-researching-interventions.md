## Arts in Health: Designing and researching interventions

Daisy Fancourt's chapter 'Fact File 10: Public Health' explores the intersection of arts and public health, highlighting key research findings that demonstrate the positive impacts of cultural engagement on mental and physical health outcomes. The author discusses how attending cultural events can improve perceived health, stimulate leisure activities may protect against dementia, and arts participation is linked with reduced obesity rates among teenagers and adult men. Furthermore, she points out that arts and cultural engagement are associated with a lower mortality risk, while mother-infant singing shows enhanced wellbeing and reduced symptoms of postnatal depression. The chapter also offers practical insights for using the arts to enhance public health initiatives, such as digital arts campaigns promoting healthy lifestyles or music programs targeting deprived communities. These ideas emphasize the potential of creative arts in fostering healthier behaviors and social development across diverse populations.
