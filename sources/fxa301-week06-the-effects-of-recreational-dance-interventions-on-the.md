## The Effects of Recreational Dance Interventions on the Health and Well-being of Children and Young People: A Systematic Review

This systematic review explores the impact of recreational dance on the health and well-being of children and young people aged between 5 to 21 years. The authors conducted a comprehensive literature search from various databases, including MEDLINE, AMED, PsycINFO, ERIC, EMBASE, and EBM reviews, focusing on studies published from 1947 to 2009 that reported health outcomes of dance interventions for young people. A total of fourteen studies were included in the review, covering a variety of dance styles, population groups, and settings.

The study findings indicate a positive correlation between recreational dance and improved cardiovascular fitness and bone health among children and adolescents. The research also suggests that participating in dance can contribute to preventing or reducing obesity. Additionally, there is evidence suggesting that dance may improve self-concept and body image as well as reduce anxiety levels in young participants.

The review highlights the importance of incorporating recreational dance programs into community and educational settings to address physical inactivity and promote overall health among children and adolescents. The authors recommend further high-quality research to substantiate these initial findings.
