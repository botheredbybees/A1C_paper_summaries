## Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy: Theory, Research, and Practice

Cheryl Hulburd's book 'Puppet-Assisted Play Therapy' offers an innovative approach to using puppets in play therapy for children. The text explores the history of puppetry, theoretical underpinnings, creative interventions, and practical applications including case studies and research on puppet therapy's benefits. Key arguments include how puppets facilitate emotional expression, enhance creativity, address psychological theories, and aid in specific therapeutic issues like medical conditions or trauma disclosure. Hulburd emphasizes that puppets provide a safe distance for children to explore feelings and experiences they might find challenging to articulate directly. This method is particularly valuable in fostering trust between child and therapist, enabling effective therapeutic engagement.
