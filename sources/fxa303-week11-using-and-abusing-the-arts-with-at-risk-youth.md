## Using and Abusing the Arts with ‘At-Risk’ Youth

Frances Howard's article explores the differential quality of arts programs offered to at-risk youth, suggesting that disadvantaged young people often receive lower-quality and mechanistic art experiences. The research, which includes multisited ethnography in five diverse UK settings, argues that arts programs for these youths are frequently deficit-oriented, focused on credentialing and behavior modification rather than holistic development. Howard questions the assumption that all arts activities benefit at-risk youth equally and highlights how such programs can unintentionally reinforce marginalization and poverty. This critical perspective is crucial for understanding the complex interplay between arts education and social inequality in youth services.
