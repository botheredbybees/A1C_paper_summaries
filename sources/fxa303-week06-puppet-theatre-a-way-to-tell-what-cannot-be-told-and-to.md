## Puppet theatre: A way to tell what cannot be told and to face pain

Andrea Markovits discusses the use of puppet therapy as a method to address trauma and memory in the context of Chile's military dictatorship (1973-1990). The article highlights how puppets serve as mediators for storytelling, helping victims and families of the disappeared express their painful memories. Puppet workshops facilitated by Puppets in Transit aim to restore social bonds and foster intergenerational dialogue about traumatic experiences. By engaging with symbolic objects like puppets, participants can externalize their pain and gain a sense of agency over their narratives. The process involves several stages, including activating collective memory, working with personal significant objects, mapping personal memories, and physicalizing grief through constructed puppets. Markovits emphasizes the importance of radical listening and empathy in these sessions to ensure that painful stories are heard and validated, thus contributing to healing and reparation.
