## A Systematic, Large-Scale Study of Synaesthesia: Implications for the Role of Early Experience in Lexical-Colour Associations

This study examines synaesthesia, a condition where stimuli in one sensory modality elicit anomalous experiences in another. Researchers surveyed 192 self-reported synaesthetes to determine the frequency and characteristics of different types of synaesthetic experiences, focusing on those involving letters, digits, and words eliciting colors ('lexical-color' synaesthesia). Key findings indicate that synaesthetes are more likely to engage in artistic pursuits. The study also highlights a striking consistency in color associations for certain letters and numbers across multiple individuals (e.g., 'R' often evokes red), suggesting these experiences may be influenced by early learning sequences such as the alphabet or number series. This implies that synaesthetic patterns could develop during critical periods of early development, where conventional sequences are learned.
