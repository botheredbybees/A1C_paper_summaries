## Critical Reflection – The Photo Essay

Wendy Whiley's critical reflection explores her personal journey in embracing decay and mortality through nature photography. She discusses how engaging with natural environments that include elements often deemed unpleasant or repulsive has become a profound practice for personal growth and acceptance. Drawing from Louw (2022)’s philosophical perspective on death as a natural process, Whiley navigates the emotional and intellectual complexities of finding beauty in decay. The reflection also touches upon Felisber3’s (2022) concept that ugliness can stimulate imagination by its disordered visual properties. Through this photo essay, Whiley aims to capture her evolving relationship with mortality and the natural world, using photography as a tool for introspection and artistic expression. Her work highlights how engaging with challenging aspects of nature fosters an appreciation for life's transient beauty.
