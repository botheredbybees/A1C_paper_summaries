## Indigenous and traditional arts in art therapy: Value, meaning, and clinical implications

This study explores the therapeutic value and cultural significance of Indigenous and traditional arts in art therapy practices, aiming to understand their role in promoting psychosocial well-being within diverse populations. Through grounded theory methodology, interviews were conducted with twenty-one art therapists who work closely with or belong to culturally diverse or Indigenous communities. The research highlights that these art forms are deeply rooted in the culture and land from which they originate and can foster cultural pride and identity reclamation when used by individuals familiar with their traditions. Specific therapeutic qualities of certain practices and materials were identified, offering potential benefits for wider populations beyond just those who traditionally engage in them. An emerging ecological model is proposed, emphasizing interconnectedness between the individual, family, community, and culture, advocating for ethical considerations to prevent cultural appropriation during art therapy sessions.
