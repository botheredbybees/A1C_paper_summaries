## People with dementia as 'spect-actors' in a musical theatre group with performing arts students from the community

This study explores a collaborative music and drama therapy initiative involving performing arts students and people with dementia residing in nursing homes. The research aims to develop an innovative framework that enables individuals with dementia to actively participate in creative activities, thereby enhancing their emotional expression and sense of identity despite cognitive decline. Through two consecutive groups (each comprising six residents and six students), the study reveals significant outcomes: integrating music and drama therapy expands modes of creative self-expression for people with dementia; student support helps participants engage more deeply in therapeutic processes; and a joint framework allows individuals to take on active roles as 'spect-actors' in final performances. This approach challenges conventional views by empowering people with dementia, promoting autonomy and enhancing their quality of life through creative engagement.
