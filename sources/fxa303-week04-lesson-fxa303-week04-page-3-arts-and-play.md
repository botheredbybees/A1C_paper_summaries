## Play and Arts-based Therapies in Neurodevelopmental Disorders

This lesson explores the use of play and arts-based therapies for neurodiverse children, particularly those with autism. It highlights how child-centered play therapy can foster social and emotional behaviors, enhance communication skills, and improve mental well-being. The video on Pivotal Response Therapy (PRT) demonstrates a behavioral approach to improving social interactions through play. Additionally, the lesson covers music therapy's role in developing expressive skills and emotional regulation by leveraging children's inherent musical strengths. Art therapy is discussed as an innovative method for visual expression and social learning, with studies showing positive impacts on various developmental aspects.
