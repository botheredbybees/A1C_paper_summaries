## Home hints for artwork and object documentation

This document provides guidance on photographing artworks and objects at home with various camera setups and lighting techniques. It emphasizes the importance of using consistent light sources to ensure accurate color representation, recommending manual settings or RAW format for DSLR cameras over auto mode. The guide offers tips such as setting up a DIY light box, utilizing reflectors, and bouncing flash off surfaces to soften shadows. For two-dimensional objects like paintings, it advises positioning lights equidistant from the work's middle, highlighting the need for even lighting distribution to prevent flaring at edges. Safety precautions are also emphasized to ensure safe handling of equipment and awareness of surroundings.
