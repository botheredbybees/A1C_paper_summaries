## Weapons of Mass Happiness: Social Justice and Health Equity in the Context of the Arts

This chapter explores the intersection of art, music, and health equity within a socio-political context, focusing on how these elements are utilized to address or manipulate societal issues. It examines cases like Music & Memory’s iPod program for dementia patients, highlighting potential ethical implications and commercialization. The author also delves into historical examples such as Billie Holiday's song 'Strange Fruit' and Louis Armstrong's cultural diplomacy during the Cold War era, illustrating how music can serve both as a tool for social change and an instrument of political strategy.
