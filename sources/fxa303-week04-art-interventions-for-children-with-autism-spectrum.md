## Art Interventions for Children With Autism Spectrum Disorder: A Scoping Review

This scoping review evaluates the efficacy of creative arts interventions for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) based on Level 1b or 2b evidence from peer-reviewed articles published between 2000 and 2020. The authors, including Allison Bernier et al., aimed to provide occupational therapists with evidence supporting the use of art therapies such as drawing, painting, music, and theater in enhancing occupation-based outcomes for children with ASD. After screening 18 full-text articles that met inclusion criteria from six databases, they found that creative arts interventions positively impacted two areas under the Occupational Therapy Practice Framework: Domain and Process (OTPF-4) related to performance skills and social interaction, as well as one area concerning body functions in client factors. Both group and individual intervention sessions yielded similar positive outcomes, but significant improvements required multiple sessions over time.
