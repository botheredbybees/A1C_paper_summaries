## Personalized Music for Persons with Dementia and Their Life Quality: Report on a Pilot Study of In-Home Use

This paper discusses the impact of personalized music on individuals with dementia living at home, based on a pilot study conducted by researchers at the University of Wisconsin Oshkosh. The study aimed to explore how customized playlists delivered via iPods could enhance quality of life and reduce agitation among people with various forms of dementia. Drawing from literature that highlights the therapeutic benefits of familiar music in reducing anxiety and enhancing cognitive functions, the research team recruited participants who were provided personalized musical content tailored to their preferences. Data was collected through surveys and interviews conducted approximately three months apart. The findings suggested significant improvements in mood and engagement, although challenges such as technical difficulties and participant compliance were noted. This study underscores the importance of music therapy and personalization in dementia care and highlights its potential to foster social interaction and emotional well-being for those living with cognitive impairments.
