## The Arts and Healing in Ancient Greece

This lesson explores how ancient Greek culture viewed the arts as integral to health and wellbeing. It discusses the role of music in society, including the mathematical principles behind musical intervals and architecture. Ancient Greek plays were seen not only for entertainment but also for their therapeutic and educational value through Aristotle's concept of catharsis. The lesson highlights the cultural significance of musical instruments like the lyre and aulos, and how these instruments were used to accompany poetry recitations and religious ceremonies. It further examines the philosophical idea of ethos, where music was believed to influence an individual’s character, as well as Hippocrates' theories on health and disease in contrast to supernatural beliefs. The lesson also introduces the theory of four humours which had a lasting impact on medical thought and personality descriptions.
