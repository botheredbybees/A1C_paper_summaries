## Arts and Creativity Interventions for Improving Health and Wellbeing in Older Adults: A Systematic Literature Review of Economic Evaluation Studies

This systematic literature review by Crealey et al. investigates the economic value and cost-effectiveness of arts and creativity interventions aimed at improving health and wellbeing in older adults. The study identifies a limited number of studies that provide evidence on the economic evaluation of such interventions, with encouraging results indicating positive returns on investment ranging from £1.20 to over £8 for every pound spent. However, significant heterogeneity was observed across these studies regarding participants, design, and outcomes assessed. Despite reported benefits in terms of physical, mental, and social wellbeing, the evidence on economic value remains sparse and challenging to generate due to methodological complexities. The review highlights the need for prioritizing data collection on cost-effectiveness and suggests developing evaluative methods that go beyond current health technology assessment frameworks.
