## What Happens After Nomination? Evaluating the Probability of Gifted Identification With the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking

The article investigates the impact of incorporating creativity assessments, specifically the Torrance Test of Creative Thinking (TTCT), in gifted identification processes within a diverse mid-sized urban school district. It examines whether including TTCT scores influences equitable identification outcomes for underrepresented student groups such as those from different racial/ethnic backgrounds and socio-economic statuses. The study found no significant difference in the probability of being identified as gifted when controlling for cognitive ability and academic achievement, even after incorporating TTCT-Figural scores. This indicates that while creativity assessments are important, they do not necessarily reduce disparities in gifted identification across diverse groups. The research highlights the need for more comprehensive approaches to enhance equity in gifted education beyond just including creativity measures.
