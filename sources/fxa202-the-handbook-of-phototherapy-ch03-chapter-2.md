## The Phototherapists’ Experience and Research on Talking Pictures Therapy

This chapter explores phototherapists' experiences in various settings, including a UK prison and school environments. It discusses the use of photographs as therapeutic tools to help clients express and explore aspects of their lives. The research presented includes a case study using Talking Pictures Therapy (TPT) as a brief therapy approach for children aged 12-14 years in a secondary school setting. TPT involves up to six sessions where photographs are used to facilitate communication about difficult life issues. The chapter also covers the theoretical background of phototherapy and therapeutic photography, including their development in the 1980s and recent resurgence due to digital technology. It highlights how photographs can serve as tools for accessing unconscious thoughts and facilitating personal growth. Methodologically, the study combines qualitative case studies with quantitative assessments like PHQ-9, GAD-7, and CORE-10 scales.
