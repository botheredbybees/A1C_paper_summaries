## The Global Refugee Crisis: Empirical Evidence and Policy Implications for Improving Public Attitudes and Facilitating Refugee Resettlement

The article explores the global refugee crisis, examining empirical evidence and policy implications for improving public attitudes toward refugees and facilitating their resettlement in host countries. It addresses the rising number of refugees worldwide due to conflicts such as Syria's civil war and the challenges this poses for durable solutions like repatriation or resettlement. The authors highlight the need for favorable public attitudes towards refugees, which is crucial for effective integration into new societies. They review literature on factors influencing public perceptions and mental health impacts on refugees, proposing strategies to improve these conditions. These include enhancing positive representations of refugees in media, fostering social contact between host communities and refugees, and implementing policies that promote economic stability and support services for refugees. The study underscores the importance of national responses, such as Canada's approach during the Syrian refugee crisis, in shaping public attitudes and supporting successful resettlement efforts.
