## Dementia facts and figures

The document 'Dementia facts and figures' by Dementia Australia provides an overview of dementia prevalence in Australia, highlighting that it is the leading cause of death among Australians. By 2026, approximately 446,500 people are estimated to be living with all forms of dementia, a number expected to rise to over one million by 2065 without significant intervention. Young onset dementia affects around 29,000 individuals aged 18-65 in Australia, projected to increase to 41,000 by 2054. Additionally, about 1,500 children live with childhood dementia in the country. The data also indicates that an estimated 1.7 million people in Australia are caregivers for individuals living with dementia. This document underscores the importance of understanding and addressing the rising prevalence of dementia across various demographics and regions within Australia.
