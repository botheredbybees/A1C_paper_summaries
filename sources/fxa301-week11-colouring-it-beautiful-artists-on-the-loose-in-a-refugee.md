## Colouring it beautiful: artists on the loose in a refugee camp - in pictures

The article 'Colouring it beautiful: artists on the loose in a refugee camp - in pictures' details an art project called Paint Outside the Lines, which provides Syrian children living in refugee camps with creative expression and a public platform for their voices. Due to almost four years of war in Syria, millions have been displaced into neighboring nations, including Iraq's Kurdistan region, where many different ethnic groups live side by side in camps, often leading to tense environments. The art project not only offers these children a way to express themselves but also fosters peace and collaboration among diverse communities through the creation of murals that reflect their identities and coexistences. Participants use everyday materials such as water bottles for paint cups and decorate community structures with vibrant colors and designs, including messages of hope like 'Hope gives wings to humanity.' These projects empower children in refugee camps by providing a creative outlet and contributing to building more peaceful communities.
