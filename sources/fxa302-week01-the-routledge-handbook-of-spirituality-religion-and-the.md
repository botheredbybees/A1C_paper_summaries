## The Routledge Handbook of Spirituality, Religion, and the Medical Humanities

The Routledge Handbook of Spirituality, Religion, and the Medical Humanities is an edited volume that explores the intersections between medical humanities, religious studies, theology, and health sciences. It addresses a significant gap in current academic discourse by integrating perspectives from various disciplines to understand how spirituality and religion influence mental and physical well-being. The book includes contributions from international scholars who examine themes such as religious studies and theology within the context of medical humanities, socio-spiritual identity, cultural expressions of health, space and place in healthcare contexts, beliefs and behaviors related to healing, and practical applications for integrating spirituality into healthcare settings. By fostering dialogue between these traditionally separate fields, this handbook provides a comprehensive resource that enriches our understanding of holistic approaches to health care and wellness.
