## Music, Memory, and Imagination

This article explores the relationship between music, memory, and imagination by examining how music can reliably cue both autobiographical memories and fictional imaginings. The authors argue that understanding this connection offers insights into broader questions about the interdependence of memory and imagination. Music is shown to be a reliable ecological cue for evoking vivid memories and fictional stories across diverse cultural contexts. Research demonstrates that music’s emotional, semantic, and contextual associations can systematically influence both types of mental processes, making it an ideal medium for investigating these phenomena. The article highlights the potential for manipulating musical features to study how they causally impact autobiographical memories and fictional imaginings.
