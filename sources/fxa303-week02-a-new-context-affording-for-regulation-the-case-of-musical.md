## A New Context Affording for Regulation: The Case of Musical Play

This article explores the relationship between regulation and musical play, proposing that these elements are directly linked in children's development. The study involved observing 36 children aged 6 to 8 during musical play sessions to identify regulatory behaviors such as cognitive monitoring and emotional/motivational monitoring. Key findings suggest a higher prevalence of socially-shared regulation compared to self- or co-regulation, with activities predominantly directed towards fundamental rather than superficial aspects of tasks. The research highlights the importance of playful contexts for fostering self-regulatory skills and provides insights into how musical play can support children's development in cognitive, emotional, and social domains.
