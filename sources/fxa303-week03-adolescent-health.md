## Adolescent Health

The document titled 'Adolescent Health' published by the World Health Organization in 2025 highlights the importance of adolescence as a unique phase of human development from ages 10 to 19. It emphasizes rapid physical, cognitive, and psychosocial growth during this period which impacts how adolescents perceive themselves and interact with their environment. Despite being generally perceived as a healthy life stage, there are significant health issues including preventable deaths, illnesses, and injuries among adolescents.

The article underscores the need for comprehensive sexuality education, opportunities to develop essential life skills, equitable and effective healthcare services, and supportive environments that foster good health behaviors. Adolescents also require platforms to actively participate in designing and implementing health interventions that cater to their specific needs and rights.

The text further elaborates on initiatives such as collaborations with Noora Health to support family caregivers and the release of guidelines aimed at preventing adolescent pregnancies and improving girls’ health. These efforts reflect a broader commitment towards addressing the unique health challenges faced by adolescents globally.
