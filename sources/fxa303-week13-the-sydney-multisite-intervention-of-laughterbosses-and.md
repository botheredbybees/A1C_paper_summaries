## The Sydney Multisite Intervention of LaughterBosses and ElderClowns (SMILE) study: cluster randomised trial of humour therapy in nursing homes

The study examines the impact of humour therapy on depression, agitation, behavioral disturbances, social engagement, and quality-of-life in nursing home residents through a cluster randomized controlled trial involving professional 'ElderClowns' and trained staff 'LaughterBosses'. Key findings indicate that while humour therapy did not significantly reduce depression or other behavioral disturbances except for agitation, it successfully reduced agitation levels at 13-week and 26-week follow-ups compared to control groups receiving usual care. This suggests that humor therapy may be a valuable psychosocial intervention before resorting to medication for managing agitation in nursing homes.
