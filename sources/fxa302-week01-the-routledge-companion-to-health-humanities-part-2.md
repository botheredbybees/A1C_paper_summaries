## The Routledge Companion to Health Humanities: Part 2 - Applications

Elaine Argyle's chapter on 'Clay Modeling' explores the therapeutic and social benefits of working with clay in various settings, including mental health practice and community arts programs. The text highlights how clay modeling combines physical engagement with emotional expression, fostering a direct connection between participants and their natural environment. It discusses its accessibility compared to other creative practices and emphasizes its potential for symbolic and expressive communication. Argyle also presents research findings from the Clay Transformations project, which involved workshops facilitated by local community arts providers, demonstrating significant improvements in social inclusion and mental well-being among diverse participant groups. The chapter concludes with reflections on how public exhibitions of participants' work can enhance their sense of achievement and pride while contributing to community engagement.
