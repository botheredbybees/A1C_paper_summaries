## Cultural determinants of health for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people – a narrative overview of reviews

This review examines the cultural determinants of health for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people in Australia, emphasizing a strengths-based approach that centers Indigenous social realities rather than deficit-oriented perspectives. The study synthesizes evidence from nine reviews to highlight key factors such as family/community connections, Country and place (cultural identity), self-determination, and connection to culture as positive determinants of health and wellbeing outcomes for Aboriginal peoples. It also underscores the interconnectedness of these cultural determinants and their roles in promoting resilience and resistance against adverse socio-economic conditions. The review advocates for further research to develop a comprehensive understanding of how cultural determinants influence health at multiple levels, ensuring that policies and practices are informed by Indigenous priorities.
