## Clowning in Health Care Settings: The Point of View of Adults

This paper explores the concept and effects of medical clowning on adults within healthcare settings, drawing from an extensive literature review across three databases: PubMed, PsycINFO, and Google Scholar. The authors highlight that while much research focuses on children's reactions to clown interventions, there is a growing interest in understanding the impact on adult patients, relatives, and healthcare staff. Key findings indicate that clowning can induce positive emotions among adults, reducing psychological symptoms such as anxiety and stress. Additionally, studies suggest that clown doctors are well-regarded by both family members and medical personnel, contributing to a more positive atmosphere within hospitals. However, the review notes a lack of comprehensive research on adult patients and clowns themselves, suggesting areas for future investigation.
