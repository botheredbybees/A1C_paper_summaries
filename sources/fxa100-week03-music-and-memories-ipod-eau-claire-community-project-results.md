## Music and Memories iPod Eau Claire Community Project Results

The study evaluated the impact of personalized music interventions using iPods on individuals with dementia and their caregivers in Eau Claire. The sample comprised 22 participants, ranging from mild to late-stage dementia, mostly cared for by spouses or daughters. Caregiver distress was measured using the Caregiver Distress Scale (CDS), which assesses emotional burden, relationship distress, care-receiver demands, social impact, and social cost. After three months of iPod use, there was a significant decrease in overall caregiver distress (22.10% reduction) and specific subscales like emotional burden (50.12%) and relationship distress (43.41%). Caregivers reported improved quality of life for both the individual with dementia and themselves, reduced stress, and enhanced engagement and relaxation through music. The study highlights personalized music as a valuable tool in alleviating caregiver stress and improving care recipient well-being.
