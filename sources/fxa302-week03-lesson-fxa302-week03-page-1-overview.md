## Week 3 Seeing more deeply - illustration and observation

This week's lesson explores the intersection between health sciences and visual arts, focusing on medical illustrations. It highlights how artists have contributed to detailed anatomical representations for centuries, aiding in medical education and surgical practices. The contemporary field of medical illustration continues to be vital, with a growing interest in using various mediums like watercolours, ink, computer graphics, and video animations to represent biology. These visual arts enhance observation skills and provide new insights to the public. Health Humanities programs collaborating with galleries and museums are shown to improve observational skills for health profession students. The lesson outlines historical developments in body representation, profiles notable medical illustrators, and discusses educational benefits of using visual arts.
