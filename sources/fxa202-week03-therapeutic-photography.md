## Therapeutic photography

This chapter explores the application of therapeutic photography in addressing personal challenges through two case studies. The first case involves a photographer documenting his depression and exhaustion by taking photos during a family holiday, which helped him come to terms with his emotional state. The second case is about capturing images of his childhood home after his mother's death to cope with loss and grief. The chapter underscores the significance of therapeutic photography as a tool for self-help but also highlights potential pitfalls such as prematurely moving past emotional issues without fully resolving them. It emphasizes the importance of trained professionals to guide therapeutic processes when necessary, ensuring that clients do not unintentionally venture into unexplored psychological territory.
