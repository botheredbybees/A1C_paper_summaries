## Week Three Lecture Transcript: Photography and Health

This lecture transcript from Week Three of the Photo Essay course explores the relationship between photography, particularly through social media, and mental health. The discussion begins by addressing how social media can both positively connect people and negatively impact mental well-being, introducing the term 'nomophobia' to describe anxiety related to being without one's mobile phone. It highlights the role of photography in documenting daily life but also notes the tendency to present idealized versions of oneself online. The lecture distinguishes between therapeutic photography (self-initiated activities) and phototherapy (guided by a professional). Key strategies for using photography to improve mental health and memory are discussed, along with case studies and artistic projects that resonate with personal experiences. Practical tips and mindfulness techniques for integrating photography into daily life to enhance well-being are provided, including the concept of a 'mindful photo walk.' The week's activity involves conducting a mindfulness photo walk in a natural setting, which students share and discuss on the course discussion board.
