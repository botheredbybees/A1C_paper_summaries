## The Importance of Disability Led Theatre

This article discusses the significance and impact of disability-led theatre in Britain, focusing on initiatives such as Lung Ha Theatre Company and Leeds Playhouse's dementia-friendly performances. It highlights how these theatrical efforts not only provide artistic opportunities for individuals with disabilities but also promote greater societal acceptance and understanding. Through detailed accounts from actors like Kay Ann Jacobs and Teri Robb, the piece emphasizes the confidence-building aspects of performing on stage for people with learning disabilities. Additionally, it showcases Leeds Playhouse’s dedication to inclusivity through their dementia-friendly performances, such as 'Still Alice,' which was adapted by Christine Mary Dunford and involved a consultant living with early-onset dementia. The article underscores the importance of inclusive theatre in fostering community engagement and enhancing quality of life for individuals with disabilities.
