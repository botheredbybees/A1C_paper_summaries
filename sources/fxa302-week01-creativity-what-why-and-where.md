## Creativity: What, Why, and Where?

In this chapter, Nellie Hermann explores the concept of creativity within narrative medicine and its importance in healthcare practice. She challenges the common perception that creativity is exclusive to artists by demonstrating how creative thinking can be integral to patient care and medical diagnosis. Hermann argues that creativity involves an openness to uncertainty, doubt, and ambiguity, which are fundamental aspects of clinical decision-making. Through personal anecdotes and examples from everyday life, she illustrates how engaging in creative acts can enhance a clinician's ability to understand patients' experiences and articulate their own complex feelings about healthcare encounters. The chapter also discusses the role of reflective and creative writing as tools for fostering empathy and improving patient care.
