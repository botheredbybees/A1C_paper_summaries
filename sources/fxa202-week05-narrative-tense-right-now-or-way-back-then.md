## Narrative Tense - Right Now or Way Back Then

This article by Bill Hill discusses the choice of narrative tense in storytelling, focusing on whether to use past or present tense. The author highlights that the decision between these tenses is significant as it affects how readers perceive and engage with the story. Past tense is described as more familiar and common, making it easier for readers to immerse themselves immediately. Present tense, though less conventional, can offer a sense of immediacy and novelty but may require readers to adjust their expectations. The article also notes that present tense is often associated with literary fiction and student writing, while past tense dominates genre novels. Hill advises writers to be consistent in their choice and consider the potential impact on reader engagement and publisher acceptance.
