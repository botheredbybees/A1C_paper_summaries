## Singing and Wellbeing: Ancient Wisdom, Modern Proof

The book 'Singing and Wellbeing: Ancient Wisdom, Modern Proof' by Kay Norton explores the multifaceted benefits of singing on human health and well-being. It synthesizes research from neuroscience, psychology, musicology, and other fields to demonstrate how singing impacts brain function, physiology, and social interactions. The text delves into historical and evolutionary contexts, highlighting the significance of singing in cultural practices across various societies and religions. Norton argues that singing is a fundamental human activity with profound therapeutic potential, particularly for conditions like dementia and mental health disorders. She emphasizes the role of singing in fostering community bonding, enhancing personal identity, and supporting healing processes. The interdisciplinary approach presented in this book underscores the importance of integrating vocal music therapy into healthcare systems to harness these benefits.
