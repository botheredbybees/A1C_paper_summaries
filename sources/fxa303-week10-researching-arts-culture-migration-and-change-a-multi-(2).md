## Researching arts, culture, migration and change: a multi (trans)disciplinary challenge for international migration studies

This article explores the significance of studying the interconnections between migration, arts, and culture within the broader field of international migration studies. The author highlights that despite a long-standing interest in cultural studies and the humanities, this area has been relatively neglected in migration research, which historically focused on demography, sociology, and political science. The paper argues for the importance of investigating these links to gain a more comprehensive understanding of migration processes and to 'rehumanize' migrants by focusing on their artistic and cultural contributions rather than viewing them primarily as economic actors or victims of restrictive policies. Martiniello discusses methodological approaches and outlines recent developments in research networks such as IMISCOE, emphasizing the need for multidisciplinary collaboration and broader definitions of culture to include various forms of artistic expression beyond traditional fields like music and literature. The article concludes by stressing the value of this research in moving away from a narrow focus on economic or policy-driven aspects of migration towards a more holistic approach that acknowledges the cultural and creative dimensions.
