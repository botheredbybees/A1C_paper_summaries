## Rating the Quality of a Body of Evidence on the Effectiveness of Health and Social Interventions: A Systematic Review and Mapping of Evidence Domains

This article presents a systematic review and mapping of evidence domains for rating the quality of a body of evidence on health and social interventions' effectiveness. It identifies 17 existing systems that vary greatly in their approaches, with most lacking rigorous development and dissemination processes. The study highlights the importance of considering complexity in these interventions when assessing evidence quality. Key evidence domains include study design, execution, consistency, measures of precision, directness, publication bias, magnitude of effect, dose-response relationship, plausible confounding, analogy, robustness, applicability, and coherence. The Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (GRADE) approach is noted for its comprehensive guidance and rigorous procedures. Understanding these systems can help researchers and policymakers better evaluate the credibility and trustworthiness of evidence in complex intervention studies.
