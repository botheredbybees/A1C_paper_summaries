## The Effect of Singing on Alert Responses in Persons with Late Stage Dementia

The study investigates the impact of singing on alert responses in individuals with late-stage dementia who are non-ambulatory and have minimal discernible language capabilities. Participants were exposed to three conditions during their sessions: reading, singing, and silence. The research found that singing resulted in the highest frequency of alert responses compared to both silent and reading states, although differences between reading and singing did not reach statistical significance. Over time, participants tended to show increased responsiveness across all conditions. The study highlights singing as a viable method for stimulating individuals with advanced dementia, indicating that it can be used effectively by caregivers without requiring musical accompaniment.
