## Memory: Inner or Outer?

This chapter discusses memory loss from clinical and philosophical perspectives, emphasizing that understanding memory informs interactions with people experiencing memory difficulties. It differentiates between causal explanations (where memory is linked to specific brain areas) and constitutive accounts (focusing on the subjective experience of remembering). Causal explanations, such as those involving the hippocampus for recall memory or procedural memory in other regions, are crucial but insufficient alone. The chapter also introduces the concept of 'externality of mind,' suggesting that mental events inherently involve worldly happenings and must adhere to normative criteria of correctness.
