## Music Therapy Sessions: Non-Verbal Communication

The transcript describes music therapy sessions involving children with autism and pervasive developmental disorder (PDD-NOS) who engage in non-verbal communication through music. Key figures include Aiden, a five-year-old diagnosed with childhood autism, Jack with PDD-NOS, and Matthew with childhood autism. The therapists use music to create structured interactions that help these children develop social skills and communicate effectively, often surpassing verbal limitations. The sessions also involve collaborative efforts between the therapist and parents or guardians, ensuring holistic development and transition from individual therapy to group settings as appropriate.
