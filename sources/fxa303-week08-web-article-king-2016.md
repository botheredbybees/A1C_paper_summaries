## The Creepy Clown Craze and Why We Mustn’t Forget the Value of Real Clowning

This article discusses the global phenomenon of 'creepy clowns' and its reflection on a misunderstanding of what professional clowning entails. The author, Barnaby King from Edge Hill University, highlights historical roots of clowning in social critique and satire, from medieval festivals to contemporary performances that promote healing and social engagement. While acknowledging the unsettling nature of clowns due to their ability to embody chaos and disorder, King emphasizes the positive aspects of professional clowning such as therapeutic benefits in hospitals and peace-building roles in conflict zones. The author criticizes recent prankster activities under the guise of clowning for lacking the transformative power of true clown performances.
