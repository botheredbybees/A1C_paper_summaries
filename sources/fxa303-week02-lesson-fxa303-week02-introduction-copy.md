## Adolescent Development and Brain Changes

This lesson focuses on the developmental changes during adolescence. It describes the period as a time of rapid cognitive, social, and emotional development marked by significant neurological transformations. Hormonal changes influence brain maturation, particularly in the prefrontal cortex, which is crucial for decision-making and self-control. The concept of synaptic pruning is introduced, highlighting how unused neural connections are eliminated to optimize brain function. Adolescents also experience increased neuroplasticity but may exhibit impulsive behavior due to immature frontal lobes compared to limbic systems. Socially, adolescents seek peer approval while developing personal identity and critical thinking skills.
