## Arts in Adolescence

The document 'Arts in Adolescence' highlights the significance of arts engagement for adolescent mental well-being based on findings from Mission Australia's youth survey. The survey reveals that around 30% of young people reported experiencing discrimination, with higher rates among Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander and gender diverse individuals. Mental health concerns are also a major issue, affecting 29% of respondents in 2024, up from 23% in the previous year. Despite these challenges, engagement in arts activities has shown potential to improve emotional well-being and social skills among adolescents. The document discusses how arts programs can be cost-effective and adaptable, enhancing community connections and providing opportunities for self-expression crucial for identity formation and emotional resilience. Additionally, it mentions the concept of Arts on Prescription, where medical practitioners prescribe art forms as therapeutic interventions to reduce anxiety and depression. Research also highlights the cognitive and socio-emotional benefits derived from participating in music, dance, theatre, and drama, which can translate into other areas such as language and mathematics skills.
