## Therapeutic Clowning: Benefits and Applications

This lesson explores the concept of therapeutic clowning in healthcare settings. It discusses the origins and development of the practice through figures like Patch Adams and Michael Christensen's Big Apple Circus Clown Care Unit. The content outlines how professional clowns use humor, play, and interaction to support mental wellbeing, particularly for children undergoing medical treatments. Research highlights benefits such as reduced stress and anxiety, improved social interactions, and shorter hospital stays. Applications include pediatric care, end-of-life care for older adults, and virtual clowning during the pandemic. The lesson also emphasizes the need for clowns to be culturally sensitive and adaptable in their approach.
