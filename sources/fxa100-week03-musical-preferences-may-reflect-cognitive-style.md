## Musical Preferences May Reflect Cognitive Style

Recent research indicates a correlation between an individual's cognitive style and their musical preferences. The study, conducted by David M. Greenberg at the University of Cambridge, explores how empathy and systemizing influence music choices. Empathizers tend to prefer mellow music characterized by lower energy levels and negative emotions, whereas systemizers favor high-arousal and complex music with positive emotions and structural intricacy. Interestingly, this preference pattern is a better predictor than the Big Five personality traits. The research also reveals that females score higher on empathy compared to males, while individuals on the autism spectrum typically exhibit elevated systemizing tendencies. This study contributes valuable insights into understanding human behavior through the lens of cognitive style and its impact on musical preferences.
