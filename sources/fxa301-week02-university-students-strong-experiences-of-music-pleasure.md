## University students’ strong experiences of music: Pleasure, engagement, and meaning

This study explores the nature of strong experiences of music listening among university students, focusing on how these experiences relate to components of happiness identified in positive psychology: pleasure, engagement, and meaning. The research involved free reports from 46 participants detailing their most intense musical experiences, which were analyzed thematically using an idiographic approach. Findings indicated that most strong experiences were positive and frequently occurred during live events with others. Music genres ranged widely, including classical, jazz, folk, and pop music, illustrating the versatility of music in evoking deep emotional responses. The study emphasizes that engaging deeply with music can lead to authentic happiness by simultaneously fulfilling hedonic, eudaimonic, and meaningful aspects of well-being.
