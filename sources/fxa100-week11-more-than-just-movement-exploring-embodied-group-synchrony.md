## More Than Just Movement: Exploring Embodied Group Synchrony During Seated Dance for Older Adults Living in Residential Aged Care Communities

This study explores the concept of embodied group synchrony during seated dance programs for older adults in residential aged care communities (RACCs). Using a mixed-methods approach, researchers observed and analyzed synchronicity between movement, music, language, and social interactions among participants. The findings suggest that older adults can engage in complex forms of embodied group synchrony despite physical and cognitive impairments. This engagement is associated with various benefits including improved emotional well-being, enhanced social connections, and potential therapeutic effects for specific health conditions. The research contributes to the understanding of how dance activities can be tailored to foster meaningful human connections and enhance quality of life among older adults living in RACCs.
