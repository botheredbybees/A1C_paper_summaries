## Arts Therapies Research and Practice with Persons on the Autism Spectrum

This volume presents cutting-edge research and practical applications of Creative Arts Therapies for individuals on the autism spectrum across all ages. The book is a collaborative effort with contributions from over 30 authors worldwide, covering various forms of art therapies including music, drama, dance, and expressive arts therapy. It outlines how these therapies have evolved to address diverse needs in health and social care settings. Key findings include innovative approaches to fostering creative social connections, enhancing empathy through mirroring techniques, and adapting interventions for digital contexts like tele-dance movement psychotherapy during the pandemic. The relevance of this work to creative arts and mental wellbeing is significant, as it not only supports individuals with autism but also offers insights beneficial for caregivers and therapists working in related fields.
