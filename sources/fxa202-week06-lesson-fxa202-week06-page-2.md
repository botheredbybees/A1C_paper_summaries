## Becoming a Visual Storyteller

This lesson focuses on the concept of visual storytelling through photography. It discusses how stories can be factual rather than fictional and highlights the power of a single image to convey drama, emotion, and context. The example of Dorothea Lange's 'Migrant Mother' photograph is used to illustrate how an image can tell the story of life during the Great Depression era in America. The lesson also covers implied narrative where viewers infer what has happened before or after looking at a photo, such as Alfred Eisenstaedt’s 'Kissing Sailor'. Furthermore, it explores visual metaphors and their use in photography to convey abstract ideas or emotions that are difficult to express in words.
