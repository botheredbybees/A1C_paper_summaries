## Creative Health and Care Experienced Children and Young People

The document discusses the role of creative health initiatives in improving mental well-being and overall quality of life for children and young people within the social care system. It highlights that around 400,000 children are linked to the English social care system, with many experiencing trauma or adverse childhood experiences leading to significant mental health challenges. Creative health interventions such as Plus One in Derby City Council and Skylark Café at Southbank Centre provide a supportive environment for self-expression, stress management, and improved educational and employment outcomes. These initiatives also foster a sense of belonging and community among care-experienced young people. The document underscores the importance of integrating creative health programs into social care pathways to address mental health disparities and enhance overall well-being.
