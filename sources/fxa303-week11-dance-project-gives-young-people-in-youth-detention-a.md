## Dance project gives young people in youth detention a chance to tell their stories

This article from ABC News discusses a dance project initiated by Phunktional Arts, a not-for-profit company, aimed at providing young people in youth detention centers an opportunity to express themselves creatively. The project involves choreographing dances and making videos that depict the experiences of these youths, including confrontations with police, court processes, and personal struggles such as homelessness and drug use. Through this initiative, participants gain a sense of control and empowerment, which is often scarce in their daily lives within the detention system. Kelly, one of the participants at Reiby Youth Justice Centre, uses dance to represent her journey from arrest through the legal process to eventual release. Meanwhile, at Frank Baxter Youth Justice Centre near Gosford, Indigenous dancers like Paul Skuthorpe-Spearim work with boys to incorporate cultural elements into their performances. The project underscores the importance of cultural nourishment and healing for Aboriginal youth in detention, who make up a disproportionate number of those held in custody. Ultimately, the program aims to help these young people heal from past traumas while expressing hope and ambition for the future.
