## Personhood Maintained

The chapter 'Personhood Maintained' by Tom Kitwood addresses the evolving approach to dementia care, emphasizing a person-centered paradigm rather than deterministic views of decline. It discusses the historical shift from negative perceptions of dementia to more optimistic and supportive practices. Key themes include the importance of societal and psychological factors in the progression of dementia, the role of therapeutic interventions like reality orientation (RO) and validation therapy (VT), and the significance of reminiscence work and biographical knowledge in care planning. The chapter highlights that although neurological impairment is a critical aspect, social and personal factors can significantly influence outcomes for individuals with dementia. It also explores innovative practices such as sensory stimulation methods and creative arts activities to enrich lives and maintain personhood. This approach aligns closely with contemporary understandings of health and well-being in creative arts therapies.
