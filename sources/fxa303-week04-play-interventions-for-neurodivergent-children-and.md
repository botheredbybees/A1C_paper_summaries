## Play Interventions for Neurodivergent Children and Adolescents: Promoting Growth, Empowerment, and Affirming Practices

Robert Jason Grant’s book ‘Play Interventions for Neurodivergent Children and Adolescents: Promoting Growth, Empowerment, and Affirming Practices’ offers a comprehensive guide to using play therapy in meaningful ways for neurodivergent children. The text is structured around the AutPlay® framework, providing practical interventions aimed at addressing common mental health needs such as social navigation, regulation, relationship development, anxiety issues, identity struggles, and self-advocacy. Grant emphasizes the importance of empathy and understanding when working with these clients, detailing specific techniques that help in various areas like emotional expression, sensory processing, and executive functioning. The book not only serves as a valuable resource for mental health professionals but also encourages therapists to enhance their own abilities through reflective practice. Overall, it represents an essential contribution to the field of play therapy within the context of neurodiversity.
