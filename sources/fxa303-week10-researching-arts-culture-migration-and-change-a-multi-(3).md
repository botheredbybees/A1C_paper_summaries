## Researching arts, culture, migration and change: a multi (trans)disciplinary challenge for international migration studies

Marco Martiniello's paper addresses the importance of researching the connections between migration, arts, and culture in international migration studies. Traditionally, this area has been neglected in favor of disciplines such as demography, sociology, and political science, partly due to a narrow focus on economic aspects of migration post-World War II and the perception that migrants were primarily workers with little cultural or artistic engagement. Martiniello argues for the significance of integrating arts and culture into migration research, suggesting it provides a more comprehensive understanding of migrant experiences beyond mere economic contributions. He outlines key methodological approaches to studying these intersections, emphasizing interdisciplinary collaboration across anthropology, sociology, literature, policy studies, and other fields. The paper also highlights the evolving landscape in migration research with networks like IMISCOE fostering dialogues on cultural production among migrants and their descendants. Martiniello underscores the potential of such research to rehumanize migrants by moving away from victimization narratives towards a more nuanced appreciation of migrant contributions to arts and culture.
