## Revive: a place for every story, a story for every place – Australia’s cultural policy for the next five years

The document 'Revive' is a five-year cultural policy for Australia that outlines strategies to support and enhance the arts sector. It builds on previous policies like Creative Nation (1994) and Creative Australia (2013), aiming to foster an inclusive, vibrant, and economically prosperous creative landscape. The policy emphasizes placing First Nations stories at its core, ensuring respect and recognition for their cultural heritage and contemporary contributions. Key pillars include empowering the artist as both creator and worker, strengthening cultural infrastructure, engaging diverse audiences, and promoting economic opportunities within the arts sector. Revive seeks to bridge social divides through shared cultural experiences and acknowledges the pivotal role of artists in defining Australia's national identity.
