## Therapeutic Dancing for Parkinson’s Disease

This systematic review evaluates the efficacy of different dance genres as a therapeutic intervention for Parkinson's disease (PD), focusing on outcomes such as mobility, gait, balance, and quality of life. The authors searched multiple databases to identify relevant studies published between 1982 and 2015, emphasizing randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and quasi-RCTs. They found emerging evidence suggesting that therapeutic dancing can be safe and feasible for individuals with mild to moderately severe PD, offering benefits such as improved walking ability, reduced freezing of gait, and enhanced quality of life. The review highlights the importance of understanding how specific elements like external cues, music, partners, and motivational teaching contribute to these outcomes. Despite positive findings, long-term effects have yet to be confirmed. This research underscores the potential for dance therapy to complement conventional treatments and enhance the overall well-being of people living with PD.
