## Functional Connectivity in the First Year of Life in Infants at Risk for Autism Spectrum Disorder: An EEG Study

The study investigates functional connectivity in infants at high risk for autism spectrum disorder (ASD) using electroencephalography (EEG). Infants with a family history of ASD and those without were tested at 6- and 12-months-old. The research focuses on intrahemispheric linear coherence between anterior and posterior sites while the infants listened to speech sounds. Results showed reduced functional connectivity in high-risk infants compared to low-risk ones by 12 months, with even lower connectivity observed in infants who were later diagnosed with ASD. This highlights a potential genetic vulnerability marker for ASD. The findings suggest that differences in neural integration may be an early endophenotype of the disorder, providing insights into the neurobiological underpinnings of autism development.
