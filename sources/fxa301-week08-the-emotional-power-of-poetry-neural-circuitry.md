## The emotional power of poetry: neural circuitry, psychophysiology and compositional principles

This study explores the emotional impact of poetry on human physiology and neural activity, comparing it to music's effects. The research team investigates the ability of poetic language to elicit strong emotions through psychophysiological measures (such as heart rate, skin conductance, and facial muscle activity), neuroimaging data, and behavioral responses. Key findings reveal that recited poetry can trigger peak emotional responses, including chills and goosebumps, engaging the brain's reward circuitry similar yet distinct from music's impact. The nucleus accumbens plays a crucial role in these reactions to poetry. Additionally, the study demonstrates that aesthetic pleasure from poetry can co-occur with negative affect indicators, providing insights into the compositional principles of poetry by analyzing chill distribution within poems.
