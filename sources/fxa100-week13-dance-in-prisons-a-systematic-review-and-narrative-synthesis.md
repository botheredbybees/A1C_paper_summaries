## Dance in Prisons: A Systematic Review and Narrative Synthesis

This systematic review assesses the application of dance and dance movement therapy (DMT) in prisons, aiming to evaluate therapeutic outcomes and research methodologies used within forensic settings. The review indicates that DMT can address a broad range of domains such as emotional, social, physical, cultural, cognitive, and integration using the Dunphy Outcomes Framework. However, the studies analyzed were generally at a low evidence level due to limited quantitative data. Despite this limitation, positive effects have been noted in terms of improved pro-social behavior, reduced aggression, and enhanced emotional wellbeing among incarcerated individuals. The review highlights the need for further empirical research to substantiate these promising findings.
