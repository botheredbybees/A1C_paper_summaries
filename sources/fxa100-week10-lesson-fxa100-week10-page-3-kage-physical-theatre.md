## KAGE Physical Theatre: Sundowner

KAGE Physical Theatre Company's production 'Sundowner' explores the impact of dementia on relationships and family dynamics. Conceived by Kate Denborough in partnership with David Denborough and directed by Kate Denborough, it features Helen Morse as Peggy, a writer in her early 50s living with younger-onset dementia. The play tours extensively throughout Australia from 2012 to raise public awareness about the multifaceted experience of dementia and its effects on families. It highlights the phenomenon of sundowning—a term used to describe increased agitation and confusion in individuals with dementia during late afternoon or early evening. Controversially, some researchers argue that using 'sundowning' can lead to over-prescription of antipsychotic medications and overlook other serious causes of agitation. The creation process involved collaboration with carers and people living with dementia through focus groups facilitated by Alzheimer's Australia (now Dementia Australia). This approach ensured the depiction of lived experiences, providing a nuanced understanding of dementia and its emotional impacts.
