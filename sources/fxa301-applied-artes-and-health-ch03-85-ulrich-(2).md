## Building Bridges Across Arts and Health

This chapter explores the integration of arts into healthcare settings to enhance patient experience and well-being, emphasizing a humanistic approach. It highlights how theatre and drama can be used in educational and therapeutic contexts for older adults living in care facilities. The authors describe a case study involving seniors participating in an arts-based intervention designed to engage sensory responses and evoke emotional connections through storytelling and interactive activities. They discuss the evolution of applied theatre practices, focusing on democratizing participation and blurring traditional boundaries between roles such as director, playwright, actors, and designers. Key themes include asset-based interventions, emergent design, servant leadership, and an ecological approach to process. The chapter underscores the importance of creating inclusive environments that foster intergenerational dialogue and mutual understanding.
