## Theories of Development: In Dialog with Jean Piaget

The article discusses Jean Piaget's theories of cognitive development, focusing on constructivism and stage theory. Constructivism emphasizes qualitative changes in representational systems over time, suggesting that new conceptual content emerges through learning processes rather than being innate or acquired solely through association. Stage theory addresses developmental changes in domain-general cognitive resources that facilitate or constrain the acquisition of specific concepts. The authors highlight Piaget's contributions to understanding how children construct theories about the physical world and argue for a continued relevance of his work within modern cognitive science, despite criticisms from other perspectives like nativism and empiricism. They also discuss the concept of conceptual change, particularly in relation to intuitive biology, illustrating how these theories have evolved yet remain foundational in contemporary discussions on child development and cognition.
