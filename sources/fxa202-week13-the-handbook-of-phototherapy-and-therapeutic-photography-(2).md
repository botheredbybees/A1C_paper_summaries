## The handbook of phototherapy and therapeutic photography: For the professional and activist client

Elisabetta Romani's review of 'The Handbook of Phototherapy and Therapeutic Photography: For the Professional and Activist Client' by Del Loewenthal highlights the book's profound exploration of therapeutic imagery through photography. The review notes that this publication adds a distinct voice to the existing literature on phototherapy, particularly from the United Kingdom. Romani emphasizes Loewenthal’s ability to distill complex concepts into an accessible format, making it suitable for both professionals and individuals seeking personal growth. The book features 12 insightful chapters covering theory, research, techniques, and ethical considerations in therapeutic photography. A standout feature is Chapter Five, which includes a deeply personal narrative from the author, demonstrating the healing potential of photography. Romani also discusses the ongoing debate about defining phototherapy versus therapeutic photography and Loewenthal's stance on the importance of self-help activism for therapeutic journeys. The review concludes by suggesting that broader categorizations could foster collaboration in the field, aligning with the book’s interdisciplinary approach to exploring the transformative power of photographs.
