## Practical recommendations for timely, accurate diagnosis of symptomatic Alzheimer’s disease (MCI and dementia) in primary care: a review and synthesis

This review synthesizes practical recommendations for the timely and accurate diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease (AD) in primary care settings, emphasizing early detection and management through biomarker confirmation. The authors argue that AD begins to develop silently decades before symptoms manifest as mild cognitive impairment (MCI) or dementia, necessitating a proactive approach in midlife with preventive interventions. They highlight the importance of collaboration between primary care clinicians (PCCs) and specialists to navigate emerging diagnostic tools and therapies. Challenges include time constraints for PCCs and insufficient specialist resources. Despite these challenges, recent developments in disease-modifying therapies offer hope for early intervention, underscoring the critical need for timely diagnosis to optimize patient care and leverage new treatments effectively.
