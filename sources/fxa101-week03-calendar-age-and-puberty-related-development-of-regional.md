## Calendar age and puberty-related development of regional gray matter volume and white matter tracts during adolescence

This study examines the impact of calendar age and pubertal status on regional gray matter volume (GMV) and white matter tract development during adolescence, using a longitudinal design with two cohorts. The research addresses inconsistencies in previous findings by distinguishing the influence of puberty from aging on brain maturation. Participants included healthy children aged 9-10 years and 12-13 years at baseline who were followed up annually for three years. MRI data measured GMV and white matter tracts. Results indicated that age best predicted longitudinal changes in most regional GMV and white matter tract measures, whereas precentral and paracentral cortical regions and the accumbens showed significant preference for pubertal status models. The study underscores the importance of considering both age and puberty when examining brain development, highlighting the need for further research with more diverse samples to confirm these findings.
