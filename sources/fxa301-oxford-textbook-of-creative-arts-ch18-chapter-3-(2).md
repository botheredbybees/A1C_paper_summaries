## The Fifth Wave of Public Health and Contributions of Culture and Arts

The chapter discusses the evolution of public health waves in response to societal changes and challenges since the Industrial Revolution. It identifies four historical waves addressing issues such as infectious diseases, improving living standards, welfare state development, and tackling chronic diseases and risky behaviors. The fifth wave is proposed for contemporary issues like obesity, inequality, and loss of wellbeing, which are emergent manifestations of modern culture rather than traditional health threats. Challenges include widespread obesity, exacerbated by obesogenic environments, and widening health inequalities despite economic growth. Addressing these requires new societal mindsets beyond technical interventions.
