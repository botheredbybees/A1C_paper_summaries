## National Dementia Action Plan indicators dashboard

The document provides an overview of the National Dementia Action Plan 2024–2034, which outlines Australia's national dementia policy framework aiming to improve the lives and care for people with dementia over ten years. The report introduces baseline indicators that will be tracked throughout the plan’s duration to evaluate its effectiveness. Key findings highlight a total of 53 reported indicators across eight action areas, including promoting equity, reducing stigma, minimizing risk, improving diagnosis and post-diagnostic care, enhancing treatment coordination, supporting carers, building workforce capability, and advancing data research. Data gaps are noted in several areas requiring further development to ensure comprehensive tracking. The report emphasizes the importance of continued government collaboration for developing new data sources and improving existing ones to achieve policy objectives effectively.
