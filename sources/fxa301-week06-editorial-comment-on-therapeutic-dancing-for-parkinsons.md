## Editorial Comment on “Therapeutic Dancing for Parkinson's Disease”

This editorial discusses the potential benefits of therapeutic dancing for individuals with Parkinson's Disease (PD). The author highlights that PD is a prevalent neurodegenerative condition in older adults, and while rehabilitation exercises can alleviate symptoms such as tremor and gait dysfunction, they may not be enjoyable over time. Therapeutic dancing aims to provide physical activity that enhances quality of life, social inclusion, and well-being for people with PD. The editorial reviews the findings from a study by Aguiar and Morris (2016) which examined 19 studies on therapeutic dancing for PD patients and found it to be beneficial for motor performance, balance function, disease severity, and quality of life. Additionally, the author emphasizes the need for further research to determine optimal frequency and intensity levels of therapeutic dance, as well as long-term effects compared to other rehabilitation exercises.
