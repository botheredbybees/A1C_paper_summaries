## Digital Storytelling

Carla Rice's chapter on digital storytelling challenges the normative and exclusionary nature of health discourse that often marginalizes individuals with differences such as disability, fatness, aging, and other non-conforming identities. The Re•Vision Centre for Art and Social Justice at the University of Guelph uses digital storytelling to create new representations of difference, shifting traditional understandings to advance social justice. By bringing together diverse stakeholders, including artists, researchers, and community members, Rice demonstrates how these workshops enable participants to explore issues related to race, class, indigeneity, age, disability, body size, gender, among others. The methodology emphasizes political, participatory, and process-oriented approaches, fostering dialogue and collaboration across power differentials while disrupting dominant narratives. Through the sharing of personal stories, individuals' experiences are brought into a communal space where they can be re-examined and reshaped, leading to transformative change in policy and practice.
