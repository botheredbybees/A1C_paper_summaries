## Developmental Psychology: From Infancy to Adulthood

This document is the front matter of 'Developmental Psychology: From Infancy to Adulthood' by David Livesey, Fiona White, and Brett Hayes. The book provides an overview of developmental psychology from infancy through adulthood. It covers foundational theories and research methods in the field, including historical perspectives, key theoretical approaches (such as Freud’s psychoanalytic theory, Erikson’s neo-Freudian theory, Piaget's cognitive theory, Bandura’s social cognitive theory, Bronfenbrenner’s ecological theory, Vygotsky’s sociocultural theory of cognitive development, and dynamic-systems theory), and research methodologies used in developmental psychology. The text discusses various genetic factors influencing development such as principles of genetic inheritance, twin studies, and adoption studies, alongside prenatal influences like teratogenic effects and foetal alcohol syndrome. It also examines physical growth patterns, motor skill development, and sensory-neurological processes underlying these developments. This book is relevant to the understanding of how creative arts can positively impact mental well-being during different stages of human development by providing a comprehensive overview of psychological changes across various developmental stages.
