## Birthing On Country And Dying On Country

The document discusses the significance of birthing on Country (aboriginal land) for Aboriginal people in Australia, emphasizing its spiritual and cultural importance. Fiona Stanley highlights the connection between birth and death to one's ancestral land, noting that both events are integral parts of a continuous cycle bound by identity and tradition. The Gathering the Seeds symposium presented initiatives aimed at integrating traditional birthing practices with modern medical care, such as the Koori Maternity Service and Birthing on Noongar Boodjar programs. These services strive to offer culturally safe environments where indigenous women can receive supportive healthcare while maintaining their cultural heritage during pregnancy and childbirth. The artwork 'Replanting the Birth Trees' symbolizes the strength of aboriginal communities in birthing and parenting, showcasing the resilience and continuity across generations. This initiative underscores the importance of collaboration between indigenous health professionals and non-indigenous allies to enhance maternal and child health outcomes.
