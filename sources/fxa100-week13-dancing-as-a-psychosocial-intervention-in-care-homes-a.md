## Dancing as a psychosocial intervention in care homes: a systematic review of the literature

This systematic review evaluates the role of dancing as a psychosocial intervention for individuals with dementia residing in care homes. The study identifies ten relevant studies, seven qualitative and three quantitative, that examine various forms of dance interventions such as therapeutic dance, dance movement therapy, social dancing, and psychomotor dance-based exercise. Key findings indicate positive impacts on mood enhancement, reduction of problematic behaviors, increased social interaction, and overall enjoyment for both residents and care staff. However, the evidence is limited due to methodological challenges, suggesting a need for further research. The review highlights the potential benefits of incorporating dancing into care home activities as an engaging and meaningful intervention that can improve quality of life for people with dementia.
