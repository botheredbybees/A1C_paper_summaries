## Health-related effects of creative and expressive writing

The paper by Geoff Lowe provides an overview of the health-related effects of creative and expressive writing, focusing on both physiological and psychological benefits. Studies show that engaging in expressive writing tasks can lead to improved health and well-being outcomes, including enhanced immune system functioning, reduced symptom severity for conditions like arthritis and asthma, and decreased distress levels. However, it is noted that these practices may not be universally beneficial, particularly for individuals with post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). The paper discusses the cognitive-behavioral mechanisms underlying writing therapy, such as self-regulation, re-framing negative feelings, and dealing effectively with trauma-related emotions. Additionally, Lowe highlights empirical evidence indicating a positive correlation between poetry and immune system function, suggesting potential therapeutic benefits for mental health conditions.
