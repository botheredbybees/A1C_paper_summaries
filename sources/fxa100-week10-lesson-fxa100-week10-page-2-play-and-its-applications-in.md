## Playful Engagement Project in Dementia Care

The Playful Engagement Program was a collaborative project between Wesley Mission Brisbane, Griffith University researchers, elderclowns Clark Crystal and Anna Yen, aimed at improving the lives of people with mid to late-stage dementia in aged care facilities. The program used playful engagement methods to combat isolation, loneliness, and disorientation, enhancing relationships between carers and residents. Additionally, the Elderflowers arts-in-health program in Scotland uses theatre and play for similar benefits. Participatory arts programs like TimeSlips and Alzheimer's Poetry Project have also shown positive impacts on dementia care. The use of theatre and film to raise public awareness about dementia is discussed, with examples including Inside Out of Mind by Meeting Ground Theatre Company and the TV show Mother and Son.
