## Changes in Communication for Persons with Dementia

This chapter discusses the critical role of communication in understanding and addressing the needs of people with dementia. It highlights three key reasons why communication is important: it reflects interdependence, provides essential insights for progress, and raises ethical considerations about valuing persons. The text emphasizes the psychological impact of communication challenges on individuals' personhood, noting that these changes are not solely due to brain damage but can be exacerbated by social and emotional factors. It also explores the importance of recognizing non-verbal forms of communication and how caregivers can use this understanding to enhance quality of life. Examples from persons with dementia illustrate both the difficulties in communication and potential creative solutions.
