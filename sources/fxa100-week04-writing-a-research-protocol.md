## Writing a Research Protocol

This chapter discusses the importance of research protocols in academic and artistic endeavors, likening them to 'show bibles' used in screen productions for consistency and guidance. It outlines three primary functions: rationale (justifying the study's necessity), methods (describing how it will be conducted), and feasibility (explaining practicalities). The text emphasizes that protocols are essential documents for internal coordination and external validation, especially regarding ethical considerations. For arts-based interventions, thorough protocols are recommended despite apparent low risk to participants, as they ensure transparency and integrity. The chapter provides a template protocol with key sections such as covering page information, ethics references, investigators' roles, and conflict of interest statements. It highlights the need for clear communication about study aims, methodologies, and logistics, applicable across various research scales.
