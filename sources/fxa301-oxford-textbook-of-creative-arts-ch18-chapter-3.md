## The Fifth Wave of Public Health and the Contributions of Culture and the Arts

This chapter explores the evolution of public health waves in the UK since the Industrial Revolution, focusing on the fifth wave addressing modern challenges like obesity, inequality, and loss of wellbeing. It discusses four previous waves: clean water and sanitation (first wave), scientific rationalism (second wave), welfare state development (third wave), and medical interventions for chronic diseases (fourth wave). The chapter highlights that technical interventions have been less effective against emergent social issues and advocates for cultural and artistic contributions to address the fifth wave's challenges.
