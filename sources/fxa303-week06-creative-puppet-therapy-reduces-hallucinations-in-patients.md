## Creative Puppet Therapy Reduces Hallucinations in Patients Diagnosed with Schizophrenia: Preliminary Findings

This study explores the efficacy of Creative Puppet Therapy (CPT) in reducing hallucinations among patients diagnosed with schizophrenia. The research, conducted as a double-blind controlled trial involving 24 participants, compared a group that created puppets using malleable materials to a pseudo-treatment group and control groups. Results indicated a significant reduction in hallucination frequency for those who engaged in CPT. Puppet facial features created by patients also exhibited distinct characteristics associated with negative emotions and specific physical attributes, suggesting a correlation between puppet creation and emotional expression. The findings support the potential of creative art therapies, particularly CPT, to enhance emotional expression and reduce symptoms like hallucinations among schizophrenia patients, contributing significantly to the field of creative arts and mental health.
