## Association of Music Interventions With Health-Related Quality of Life

This systematic review and meta-analysis explores the impact of music interventions on health-related quality of life (HRQOL) as measured by SF-36 and SF-12 surveys. The study includes data from 779 participants across 26 studies, focusing on various forms of music engagement such as listening to music, singing, and receiving active or receptive music therapy. Results indicate that these interventions are associated with statistically significant improvements in both mental (MCS) and physical (PCS) components of HRQOL. Notably, the effect sizes were consistent across different types and dosages of music interventions, suggesting broad applicability. However, individual variations underscore the need for further research to determine optimal intervention types and doses tailored to specific clinical and public health contexts. The findings support the potential role of music in enhancing well-being and HRQOL, emphasizing its relevance in creative arts and health.
