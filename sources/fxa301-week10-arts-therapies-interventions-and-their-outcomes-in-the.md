## Arts Therapies Interventions and Their Outcomes in the Treatment of Eating Disorders: Scoping Review Protocol

This document presents a scoping review protocol to explore the role of arts therapies in treating eating disorders (EDs). The study aims to examine the therapeutic outcomes reported by researchers on individuals with EDs and compare various types of art-based interventions. The authors will use the Joanna Briggs Institute methodology, searching databases like CINAHL Plus, EMBASE, MEDLINE, ProQuest Central, PsycINFO, PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science, along with grey literature sources such as Google Scholar and Mednar. They plan to assess titles/abstracts and full texts against inclusion criteria and extract data on different types of arts therapies according to research designs, country settings, intervention methods/materials, adverse effects, and therapeutic outcomes. The review highlights the increasing prevalence of EDs globally and underscores the need for effective interventions.
