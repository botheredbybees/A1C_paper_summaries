## Dance therapy as an intervention for stress and depression: a systematic review and meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis evaluates the efficacy of dance therapy as an intervention for stress and depression in adults with treatment-resistant depression (TRD). The study aimed to identify common practices used by dance therapists and determine if these interventions can reduce symptoms associated with TRD. Despite finding that dance therapy offers no statistically significant improvements in reducing depressive symptoms or stress, it was noted that participants experienced enhanced management strategies. Common therapeutic practices identified include improvisation, shared rhythm, and structured movement. These findings highlight the potential role of dance therapy in promoting psychological flexibility, emotional resilience, and self-regulation among individuals with TRD, even if direct symptom reduction is not observed.
