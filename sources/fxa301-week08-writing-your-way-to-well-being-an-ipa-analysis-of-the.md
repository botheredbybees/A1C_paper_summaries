## Writing your way to well-being: An IPA analysis of the therapeutic effects of creative writing on mental health and the processing of emotional difficulties

This study, conducted by Catherine Deveney and Patrick Lawson, explores the therapeutic benefits of creative writing compared to expressive writing on mental health and emotional processing. Through an Interpretative Phenomenological Analysis (IPA) involving nine participants from the creative industries, the research highlights that creative writing offers unique psychological benefits distinct from those of expressive writing. Participants engaged in a series of creative writing sessions focused on personal difficulties such as bereavement, divorce, and domestic violence. The study found that creative writing can lead to significant cognitive processing of emotional challenges, improved mood, and enhanced mental well-being. Unlike expressive writing, which often involves the direct expression of real-life events, creative writing encourages participants to explore emotions through fictional narratives, fostering objectivity and detachment from personal issues. This approach allows for a more nuanced understanding of one's experiences and can serve as an effective self-help tool and preparation for therapy sessions.
