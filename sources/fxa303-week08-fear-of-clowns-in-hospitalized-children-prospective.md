## Fear of clowns in hospitalized children: prospective experience

This study investigates the prevalence and characteristics of fear of clowns (coulrophobia) among hospitalized children, a phenomenon that has been increasingly observed in pediatric settings where medical clowns are present. The research, conducted at Carmel Medical Center, involved over 1100 children aged between one and fifteen years who were exposed to visits from medical clowns. Results showed that approximately 1.2% of the participants exhibited fear reactions ranging from crying and avoidance behaviors to more severe anxiety responses. Notably, girls constituted a significant majority (85.7%) of those affected by coulrophobia. The study underscores the importance of understanding this phenomenon given its potential impact on children's psychological well-being in hospital environments where medical clowns are utilized.
