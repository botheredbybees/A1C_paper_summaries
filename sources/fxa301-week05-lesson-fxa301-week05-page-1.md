## Engaging with Visual Arts: Community-Based Projects

This week's lesson explores community-based visual arts projects that aim to foster social cohesion and improve health and wellbeing. It discusses the historical shift in Western Europe where art was initially seen as a tool for cultural education but later gained therapeutic significance during World War I when Kemp Prossor introduced color therapy to help traumatised soldiers recover. The lesson also mentions Roy de Meistre's pioneering work in Australia, using artistic design for therapeutic purposes at a Red Cross convalescent home. Modern applications of these early ideas are highlighted, including the understanding that environmental light and color can influence mood, supported by neurobiological explanations.
