## Research Overview: Arts and Disability in Australia

This document, titled 'Research Overview: Arts and Disability in Australia,' provides a comprehensive review of the engagement of people with disabilities within the arts sector in Australia. It presents data and case studies that highlight the challenges and opportunities faced by individuals with disabilities as both creators and audience members. The report emphasizes the underrepresentation of artists with disabilities in creative careers, particularly noting disparities between Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander artists with and without disabilities. Additionally, it discusses the significant role of mentorship and networking for artists seeking to build professional networks and navigate career pathways. The overview also underscores the importance of recreational participation in the arts as a bridge to professional practice. Barriers such as negative attitudes and low expectations within the arts sector are highlighted as key impediments to employment opportunities for individuals with disabilities. Furthermore, the report examines the positive health and social inclusion benefits derived from creative expression by people with disabilities. While noting high attendance rates among people with disabilities at cultural events, it also identifies significant barriers related to cost, physical accessibility, transportation, and information access that hinder full participation in arts activities. The document underscores the need for further research to better understand specific challenges faced by Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people with disabilities and the impact of different art forms on audience engagement.
