## Can Creativity Be Taught?

Nellie Hermann's chapter explores the practical aspects of teaching creative and reflective writing in healthcare settings, a relatively unexplored area. The chapter emphasizes that creativity is not exclusive to professional writers but can be accessible to everyone, including medical students. It provides detailed guidance on how to structure writing seminars, choose appropriate texts for study, and create effective writing prompts. Hermann illustrates the benefits of creative assignments through examples from Columbia University’s Narrative Medicine courses where students engaged in various forms of creative work like photography and filmmaking. These exercises allowed them to reflect deeply on their medical experiences and personal growth. One powerful example is a student's film script about an elderly patient interaction, highlighting how creativity can serve as a therapeutic tool for self-expression and empathy enhancement.
