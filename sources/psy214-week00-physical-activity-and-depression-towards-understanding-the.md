## Physical activity and depression: Towards understanding the antidepressant mechanisms of physical activity

This review examines the biological and psychosocial mechanisms through which physical activity, particularly exercise, exerts antidepressant effects in adults with depression. The authors highlight several key areas including neuroplasticity, inflammation, oxidative stress, endocrine system changes, self-esteem enhancement, social support improvement, and increased self-efficacy. They argue that understanding these mechanisms can lead to better design of exercise interventions tailored for individuals suffering from depression. Additionally, the review underscores the role of physical activity in mitigating the risk of cardiovascular diseases associated with depression, thus offering a comprehensive approach to treating both mental and physical health issues. The authors also discuss potential moderators and confounders that may influence the relationship between physical activity and depressive symptoms.
