## 'MINI-MENTAL STATE': A PRACTICAL METHOD FOR GRADING THE COGNITIVE STATE OF PATIENTS FOR THE CLINICIAN

This paper introduces the Mini-Mental State (MMS), a simplified and practical method for assessing cognitive function in psychiatric patients, particularly those with delirium or dementia syndromes. The MMS comprises eleven questions that can be administered within 5-10 minutes and evaluates orientation, memory, attention, naming, verbal commands, writing abilities, and visual copying tasks. Validity and reliability were established through testing with various clinical groups including patients with dementia, affective disorders, and schizophrenia, alongside normal controls. The MMS effectively differentiates between cognitive impairment levels across these diagnostic categories, making it a valuable tool for clinicians in mental health settings.
