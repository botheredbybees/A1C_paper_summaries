## Promoting Mental Well-Being and Social Inclusion Through Art: Evaluation of an Arts and Mental Health Project

This study evaluates the impact of introductory art courses provided by an arts and mental health project in South Essex, UK. The evaluation was conducted using two methods: formative evaluation through focus groups with participants after four introductory courses and outcome evaluation using questionnaires measuring mental well-being and social inclusion at the beginning and end of 29 courses. Formative evaluations indicated gains in well-being and social inclusion among participants, alongside suggestions for clearer expectations management and individualized learning support. Outcome evaluations showed significant improvements in well-being and social inclusion measures with a high level of participant satisfaction attributed to arts participation. However, methodological issues such as low response rates limit the conclusions drawn, highlighting the need for controlled designs in future studies to more definitively attribute impacts to arts participation.
