## Exploring Child-Centered Play Therapy and Trauma: A Systematic Review of Literature

This article presents a systematic review of literature focusing on the efficacy and benefits of child-centered play therapy (CCPT) for children experiencing adverse childhood experiences (ACEs). Researchers highlight that ACEs, which include abuse, neglect, household dysfunction, poverty, systemic discrimination, and parental incarceration, significantly impact physical and mental health throughout life. The study identifies 32 research studies examining CCPT's effects on these issues and evaluates their methodological quality and potential biases. Findings suggest promising results for the use of CCPT with traumatized children despite some methodological limitations in current studies. This therapy employs play as a medium to help children process traumatic experiences, fostering emotional regulation and trust-building. The research underscores the importance of trauma-informed care and developmentally appropriate interventions to address the long-term negative impacts of ACEs on children's health and well-being.
