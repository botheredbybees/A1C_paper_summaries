## An Individualized Music-Based Intervention for Acute Neuropsychiatric Symptoms in Hospitalized Older Adults With Cognitive Impairment: A Prospective, Controlled, Nonrandomized Trial

This study evaluated the efficacy of an individualized music-based intervention in reducing neuropsychiatric symptoms among hospitalized older adults with cognitive impairment, specifically focusing on agitation and mood disturbances. The research aimed to address a gap in existing literature by investigating this intervention for patients recently admitted due to acute agitation or behavioral disturbance. Participants were divided into two groups: treatment as usual (20 participants) and an individualized music group (21 participants). Results indicated significant improvements in negative mood, positive mood, resistance to care, and reduction in the need for one-on-one nursing staff interventions and PRN medications for the music intervention group compared to the control group. The study highlights the potential of music-based interventions as a nonpharmacological approach that can be effectively implemented on hospital units to manage neuropsychiatric symptoms associated with dementia.
