## The impact of the Great East Japan earthquake on mandatory psychiatric emergency hospitalizations in Tokyo: a retrospective observational study

This study evaluates the impact of the Great East Japan earthquake and subsequent nuclear crisis on mandatory psychiatric emergency hospitalizations in Tokyo. The research, conducted retrospectively using clinical records from Tokyo Metropolitan Matsuzawa Hospital, compares hospitalization data from six months post-earthquake with eighteen months preceding it. Key findings reveal a significant increase in hospitalizations following the disaster, primarily driven by an uptick in patients diagnosed with schizophrenia. Additionally, individuals admitted after the earthquake had marginally shorter periods of education compared to those before. The study suggests that indirect exposure to the catastrophe through media coverage may have triggered exacerbations in psychiatric conditions, particularly among those with pre-existing vulnerabilities such as schizophrenia and lower educational attainment.
