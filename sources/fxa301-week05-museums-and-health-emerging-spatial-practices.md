## Museums and Health: Emerging Spatial Practices

The book 'Museums and Health: Emerging Spatial Practices' by Elena Montanari explores how museums are evolving to support public health and well-being, particularly in the context of post-pandemic times. The author argues that the increasing recognition of health as a societal issue has led to innovative practices within cultural institutions aimed at fostering visitors' health and wellness. This evolution is driven not only by recent crises like the COVID-19 pandemic but also by broader trends from the 20th century, which saw advancements in medical sciences leading to new interdisciplinary approaches between health science and architecture.

Montanari highlights several museum initiatives designed to promote health, such as sensory-friendly spaces, quiet rooms, therapeutic displays, and contemplation areas. These projects demonstrate how spatial design can actively contribute to both physical and mental well-being by addressing various aspects of public health, including chronic illnesses, psychological distress, and environmental challenges like climate change.

The book underscores the critical role of cultural actors in enhancing societal well-being, making it relevant for those interested in creative arts and health. It provides a comprehensive overview of how contemporary museum practices are integrating spatial design to improve visitor experiences and contribute positively to public health.
