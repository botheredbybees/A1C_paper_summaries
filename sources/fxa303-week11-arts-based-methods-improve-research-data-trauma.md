## Arts-Based Methods Improve Research Data, Trauma-Responsiveness, and Reciprocity

This article explores the use of arts-based methods in improving research data quality and addressing trauma-informed practices, particularly for youth violence studies focusing on girls and young women. The author highlights gender inequities within youth violence research, emphasizing the need for research methodologies that are not only sensitive to participants' experiences but also capable of generating comprehensive data sets. Through a mixed-methods approach incorporating surveys and arts-based strategies such as expressive writing and photovoice, the study uncovers valuable insights into the priorities, needs, and assets of the target population. The findings indicate that arts-based methods offer unique perspectives on violence experienced by young women, including aspects missed by traditional survey data. Moreover, these methods enhance reciprocity and community engagement, contributing to broader health equity initiatives in research practices.
