## Why Med Schools Are Requiring Art Classes

The article discusses the increasing trend of medical schools incorporating arts education into their curricula in the United States. Dr. Michael Flanagan at Penn State College of Medicine teaches a course where students engage with Impressionist art to improve communication skills and empathy towards patients. The broader argument is that engaging with the arts helps develop essential skills for doctors such as critical thinking, observational abilities, and bias awareness. This trend reflects a growing recognition of the importance of humanities in medical education, particularly in fostering empathy which can decline over time due to exposure to suffering and death. Programs like museum-based discussions of art are used across various institutions including Yale, Harvard, and Columbia University Medical Center. These programs not only improve diagnostic skills but also encourage curiosity and perspective-taking among students.
