## An introduction to research

This chapter explores the concept of research in arts in health and contrasts it with evaluation and audit practices, questioning whether arts interventions require unique research methodologies. It addresses common misconceptions about conducting randomized controlled trials (RCTs) for arts-based interventions and highlights the importance of interdisciplinary approaches to effectively capture both qualitative and quantitative data. The author argues that confirming seemingly obvious effects through rigorous research is crucial due to potential discrepancies between theoretical assumptions and practical outcomes, emphasizing the need to understand underlying mechanisms and account for individual differences among participants.
