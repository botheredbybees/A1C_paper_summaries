## Embedding Music and Music Therapy in Care Pathways for People with Dementia in the 21st Century—a Position Paper

This paper discusses the role of music therapy in dementia care from clinical, educational, and research perspectives. It addresses unique interventions by music therapists compared to indirect approaches facilitated by musicians or carers trained by therapists. The text highlights the growing demand for long-term care due to increasing numbers of people with dementia worldwide. Key findings include improved relationships and reduced agitation among individuals with dementia when engaging in music therapy sessions. The paper emphasizes the need for enhanced training and access to musical interventions at all stages of dementia, advocating for integration of such therapies into daily life and care pathways. It also introduces an international trial (Homeside) investigating reading and music interventions for dementia patients and their carers. Overall, it underscores the importance of high-quality, accessible music therapy in improving quality of life for people with dementia and their caregivers.
