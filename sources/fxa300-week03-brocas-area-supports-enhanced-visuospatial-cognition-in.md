## Broca’s Area Supports Enhanced Visuospatial Cognition in Orchestral Musicians

This study explores the enhanced visuospatial cognitive abilities of professional orchestral musicians and their correlation with brain function, specifically activation in Broca’s area during a three-dimensional mental rotation (3DMR) task. The research suggests that extensive musical training can alter brain circuits to enhance non-musical cognitive skills like 3DMR. Professional orchestral musicians demonstrated superior performance on the visuospatial test compared to matched controls and exhibited increased neural activation in Broca’s area, a region known for its role in sight reading and motor-sequence organization in music. This finding indicates that enhanced cognitive benefits derived from musical training extend beyond musical tasks to non-musical visuospatial cognition.
