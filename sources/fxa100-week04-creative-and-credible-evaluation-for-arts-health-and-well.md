## Creative and credible evaluation for arts, health and well-being: opportunities and challenges of co-production

This article discusses the findings of a UK knowledge exchange project that aimed to enhance evaluation practices within the arts and health sector through co-production methods. The study involved a mixed-methods approach, including surveys, interviews, and focus groups with stakeholders such as evaluators, researchers, artists, health professionals, commissioners, and funders. It highlighted both opportunities and challenges associated with current health and social care policies that impact arts initiatives in healthcare settings. Key findings include the need for agreed evaluation frameworks due to structural and cultural barriers within stakeholder relationships. The research emphasizes the importance of co-production between various stakeholders to improve evaluation practices and support sector development, reinforcing efforts towards effective evidence generation in arts and health interventions.
