## Conscious Creativity: Look, Connect, Create

Philippa Stanton's chapter 'What Sort of Creative Are You?' from the book 'Conscious Creativity' explores the importance of self-reflection and questioning one's creative identity. The text emphasizes that creativity is inherent in all humans, though it may manifest differently depending on individual inclinations and experiences. Stanton encourages readers to consider various aspects of their personality—such as whether they are artistic, practical, or logical—to better understand their unique approach to creativity. She also discusses the importance of curiosity and the willingness to explore new ideas without fear of making mistakes. Through a series of questions, she guides readers to identify their creative strengths and weaknesses, encouraging them to embrace their natural tendencies rather than conforming to conventional expectations. The chapter highlights the value of continuous practice and experimentation in nurturing one's creativity, even when it feels chaotic or unproductive initially.
