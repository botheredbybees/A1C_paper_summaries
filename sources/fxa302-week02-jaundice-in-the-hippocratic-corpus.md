## Jaundice in the Hippocratic Corpus

The article 'Jaundice in the Hippocratic Corpus' by Papavramidou, Fee, and Christopoulou-Aletra explores how ancient Greek physicians described five distinct types of jaundice. The authors highlight the remarkable insight of these early doctors who linked jaundice to liver dysfunction despite lacking anatomical knowledge due to their prohibition on dissection. Each type of jaundice was characterized by specific symptoms, prognoses, and treatments based on the Hippocratic humoral theory. Treatments included herbal remedies, dietary modifications, bloodletting, and baths, reflecting a holistic approach to health and illness that is still relevant in understanding modern hepatology and therapeutics.
