## Remembering Yesterday, Caring Today: Reminiscence in Dementia Care

The document discusses the essential skills and methods required for effective reminiscence work with individuals suffering from dementia. Key attitudes include good listening, receptiveness, curiosity, sensitivity, an accepting attitude, reasonable memory recall, a sense of humor, adaptability, cooperative conversational strategies, imagination, self-confidence, a democratic approach, group facilitation skills, caring, and practicality. The text emphasizes the importance of these skills in creating a supportive environment for reminiscence activities. Additionally, it highlights the use of sensory stimuli such as objects and photographs to trigger memories, with examples illustrating how specific items like work tools or enlarged photos can evoke detailed recollections from participants. This approach is relevant to creative arts and health by fostering engagement through various forms of expression, enhancing emotional connections, and improving quality of life for individuals with dementia.
