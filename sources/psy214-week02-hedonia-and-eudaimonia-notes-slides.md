## Hedonia and Eudaimonia Notes Slides

The document outlines the concepts of hedonic and eudaimonic well-being as central to understanding stress, self-care, and mindfulness. Hedonic well-being emphasizes pleasure, positive emotions, and feeling good, drawing on the Greek concept of hedone. In contrast, eudaimonic well-being focuses on personal fulfillment, functioning according to virtues, and living in accordance with one's true self or potential. This approach views well-being as 'doing good' or 'being your best self', reflecting the Greek term eudaimon. While these concepts are often discussed together due to their positive relationship, there is significant debate regarding which framework better captures overall well-being.
