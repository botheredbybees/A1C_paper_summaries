## Depth of Field: The Essential Guide (Tips)

The article 'Depth of Field: The Essential Guide' by Bruce Wunderlich provides a comprehensive explanation of depth of field (DoF) in photography. It explains that DoF refers to the zone within an image that appears sharp and focuses on factors affecting it, such as aperture settings, distance from the subject, and lens focal length. Deep DoF is desirable for landscape photos where detailed clarity across all elements is crucial, while shallow DoF creates a blurred background, emphasizing the main subject in portraits or macro photography. Understanding these principles empowers photographers to control artistic aspects of their images, enhancing creativity and visual impact.
