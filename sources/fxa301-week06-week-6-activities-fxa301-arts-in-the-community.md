## Week 6 Activities - FXA301 Arts in the Community

This week’s activities in the FXA301 Arts in the Community module focus on exploring recent research that examines how music and dance can promote health and wellbeing through active participation. The primary reference provided is a systematic review by Sheppard and Broughton (2020) published in the International Journal of Qualitative Studies on Health and Well-being, which critically reviews studies focusing on the benefits of engaging in these activities for personal wellness.

The article encourages students to focus particularly on the sections that summarize key findings regarding music and dance interventions. These summaries highlight how different populations benefit from active participation in these arts forms. Additionally, the discussion section asks students to identify underrepresented groups within health and wellbeing research involving music and dance, prompting critical thinking about equity and inclusivity in such studies.

The broader context of using music and dance for therapeutic purposes is further explored through supplementary readings that discuss workplace benefits of music and choir initiatives aimed at fostering community well-being. This interdisciplinary approach emphasizes the relevance of creative arts to health outcomes across various societal settings.
