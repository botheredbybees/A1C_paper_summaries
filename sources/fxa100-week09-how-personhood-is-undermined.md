## How Personhood is Undermined

In 'How Personhood is Undermined', Tom Kitwood critiques the conventional medical paradigm of dementia care, highlighting its technical and deterministic nature that overlooks the personal dimensions of individuals living with dementia. The chapter argues that standard approaches fail to address the social-psychological factors crucial for effective care and person-centered support. Through a detailed case study of Margaret B., Kitwood illustrates how societal context, family dynamics, and interpersonal relationships significantly impact the experience of dementia. He emphasizes the importance of understanding and nurturing personal identity amidst the disease's progression, advocating for a holistic approach that values individuality and relational integrity in care practices.
