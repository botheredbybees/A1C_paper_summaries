## Peer Handbook for Assessment Panels

The 'Peer Handbook for Assessment Panels' by Creative Australia is a comprehensive guide that outlines the processes and responsibilities involved in peer assessments for grant applications within the Australian arts sector. The document emphasizes the role of peers who are knowledgeable and independent, ensuring fair and transparent decision-making in funding allocations. It details the criteria for selecting panel members based on artistic practice, professional specialization, cultural diversity, disability representation, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander inclusion, regional coverage, gender balance, and age demographics. Additionally, it covers conflict of interest management, confidentiality, code of conduct, and remuneration for peer assessors. The handbook underscores the importance of maintaining integrity and impartiality in assessing grant applications to support diverse and equitable arts communities across Australia.
