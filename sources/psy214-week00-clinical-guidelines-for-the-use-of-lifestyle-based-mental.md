## Clinical guidelines for the use of lifestyle-based mental health care in major depressive disorder

This article presents international clinical guidelines for incorporating lifestyle-based mental health care in the treatment of Major Depressive Disorder (MDD). The guidelines, developed by a taskforce comprising experts from various disciplines and regions, aim to provide evidence-based recommendations for clinicians worldwide. The study reviews eight lifestyle domains: physical activity, smoking cessation, work-directed interventions, mindfulness-based therapies, diet, sleep, loneliness/social support, and green space interaction. Key findings indicate that physical activity, relaxation techniques, work-directed interventions, sleep, and mindfulness-based therapies are highly recommended (Grade 2) for improving MDD, while dietary changes and interactions with green spaces have a lower strength of evidence (Grade 3). Recommendations regarding smoking cessation and social support rely on expert opinion rather than empirical data. The guidelines emphasize the importance of integrating these lifestyle interventions within a biopsychosocial-cultural framework and highlight the need for further research to address gaps in efficacy and implementation strategies, especially concerning emerging approaches like green space interaction and loneliness/social support interventions.
