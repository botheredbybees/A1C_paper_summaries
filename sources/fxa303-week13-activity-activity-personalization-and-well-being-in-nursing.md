## Activity, Activity Personalization, and Well-Being in Nursing Home Residents With and Without Cognitive Impairment: An Integrative Review

This integrative review examines the impact of activities on well-being among nursing home residents, focusing particularly on the efficacy of personalized versus generic activities for those with and without cognitive impairment. The authors conducted a comprehensive search of literature from 2006 to 2018, identifying 45 studies that included both reviews and empirical articles related to activity interventions and their effects on well-being outcomes. Findings suggest consistent evidence supporting tailored activities in enhancing resident well-being, though specific or generic activities showed less efficacy except for music therapy. The review highlights the significance of engaging residents in personally relevant activities to improve their quality of life and underscores the importance of individualized approaches in long-term care settings.
