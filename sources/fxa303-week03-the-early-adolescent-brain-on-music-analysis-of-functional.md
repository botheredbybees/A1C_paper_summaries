## The early adolescent brain on music: Analysis of functional dynamics reveals engagement of orbitofrontal cortex reward system

This research article investigates how music listening modulates brain activity in preadolescents aged 10-11 years, focusing on the engagement of functional networks within the brain. Utilizing fMRI data and LEiDA (Leading Eigenvector Dynamics Analysis) to track changes in functional connectivity over time, the study reveals that music activates specific neural networks associated with reward processing, particularly involving the medial orbitofrontal cortex and ventromedial prefrontal cortices. The findings highlight a significant increase in network activity during music listening compared to periods without music, indicating an important role for these regions in emotional and hedonic responses to music. Moreover, higher musical reward sensitivity in participants correlated with increased connectivity patterns involving the insula before engagement of the reward system. These results provide new insights into the neural mechanisms underlying the pleasurable experience of music during early adolescence, emphasizing its potential benefits for developmental tasks and emotion regulation.
