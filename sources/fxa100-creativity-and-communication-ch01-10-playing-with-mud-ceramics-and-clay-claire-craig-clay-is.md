## Playing with Mud: Ceramics and Clay

This chapter explores the use of ceramics and clay as therapeutic materials for individuals with dementia, focusing on their tactile nature and potential for self-expression. It discusses initial reactions to handling clay, including resistance due to its texture or association with dirt, alongside positive engagement driven by its malleability. Techniques such as wedging are described for physical release, while creating small clay forms enables sensory exploration without pressure for specific outcomes. Alternatives like air-drying clay and Fimo are suggested for cost-effective activities. Mosaics are introduced as another creative medium offering tactile and visual satisfaction, emphasizing their permanence and storytelling potential.
