## The art of being mentally healthy: a study to quantify the relationship between recreational arts engagement and mental well-being in the general population

The study explores the relationship between recreational arts engagement and mental well-being in the general population, aiming to quantify this relationship based on theories of social epidemiology and the biopsychosocial model of health. The research involved a random sample of 702 Western Australian adults aged 18+ years who participated in a telephone survey assessing their involvement in the arts, mental well-being (using the Warwick-Edinburgh Mental Well-being Scale), demographics, potential confounders/effect modifiers such as sports engagement and general health. The study found that individuals engaging in at least 100 hours of recreational arts annually reported significantly better mental well-being compared to those with lower levels or no engagement. This research underscores the value of understanding the dose-response relationship between arts activities and mental health, potentially informing public health strategies aimed at improving population mental well-being through arts involvement.
