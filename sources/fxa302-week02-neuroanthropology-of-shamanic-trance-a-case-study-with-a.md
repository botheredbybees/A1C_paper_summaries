## Neuroanthropology of Shamanic Trance: A Case Study with a Ritual Specialist from Mexico

This study explores the neurobiological underpinnings of shamanic trance through a case study of an 81-year-old Mexican ritual specialist. The research employs electroencephalography (EEG) to document brain activity during various shamanic activities, including diagnosis and cleansing rituals. Key findings indicate increased alpha activity associated with focused attention and hypnagogic states, alongside predominant beta and gamma oscillations that suggest neural plasticity involved in integrating cultural beliefs and practices. The study contributes to the understanding of how cultural contexts shape non-ordinary states of consciousness, linking them to specific neurophysiological processes. This work underscores the interplay between traditional healing practices and modern neuroscience, offering insights into the biological mechanisms underlying spiritual experiences within the creative arts and health domain.
