## Research and Evidence in Arts, Health and Well-Being

This chapter offers an overview of the expanding research on arts, health, and well-being, highlighting a range of impacts associated with creative arts in various settings. It identifies seven domains within this field including targeted interventions, participatory arts, arts therapies, public health initiatives, cultural engagement, educational programs for healthcare professionals, and more. The chapter emphasizes that while research has shown positive effects such as reduced anxiety, improved pain management, enhanced community cohesion, and benefits for individuals across the life course, there are challenges in conducting robust studies due to small sample sizes and short project durations. Evidence is presented from focused reviews on specific topics like children's social skills improvement through drama and music, mental health recovery through arts-based interventions, physical benefits of dance for older adults, and improvements in healthcare professionals' diagnostic skills through arts education. The chapter also discusses methodological limitations and the growing body of research exploring general cultural engagement impacts.
