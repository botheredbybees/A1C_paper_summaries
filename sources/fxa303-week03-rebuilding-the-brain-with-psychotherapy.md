## Rebuilding the Brain with Psychotherapy

The article 'Rebuilding the Brain with Psychotherapy' by Savita Malhotra and Swapnajeet Sahoo discusses the evolving relationship between psychotherapy and neuroscience, focusing on how psychological interventions can influence neural architecture in ways that promote mental health. The authors delve into brain development processes, emphasizing its plasticity and adaptability to environmental experiences. They explore the concept of a 'Triune Brain' which links evolutionary brain development with emotional regulation and self-awareness. Additionally, they discuss Eric Kandel's five principles regarding the neurological basis of psychological functions, illustrating how experience can alter gene expression and neural connections. The paper also examines whether psychiatric disorders debuild brain structures and posits that psychotherapy has the potential to rebuild these neural mechanisms. Central to their argument is the idea that psychotherapy not only changes behavior but also produces long-term alterations in gene expression and synaptic strength, suggesting a significant role for therapeutic interventions in shaping mental health outcomes.
