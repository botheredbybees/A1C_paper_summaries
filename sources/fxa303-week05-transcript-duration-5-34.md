## Ancient Weiyang Storytelling of Indonesia

This transcript describes the ancient Indonesian art form of weiyang storytelling. Originating on the island of Jarrah in Indonesia for over ten centuries, it has been practiced at royal courts and in rural areas across various islands including Java, Lombok, Medora, Sumatra, and Borneo. Weiyang uses two principal types of puppets: three-dimensional wooden dolls (dolly) and flat leather shadow puppets, which are meticulously crafted with stylized features and movable parts controlled by master puppeteers. The performances often incorporate characters from indigenous myths and ancient Indian epics, alongside heroes from Persian tales, to convey philosophical, moral, and aesthetic values through storytelling. Additionally, the use of music, including choirs and orchestras, enhances the narrative atmosphere and engages audiences of all ages.
