## REMINDer randomized controlled study: Feasibility and impact of an online multimodal mind-body intervention in older adults

The article presents the REMINDer randomized controlled study, which evaluates a six-week online multimodal mind-body intervention designed for older adults to promote mental and physical well-being. The intervention integrates music, dance movement, and mindfulness, delivered via videoconferencing in two-hour sessions per week. Participants are randomly assigned either immediate or delayed intervention groups using a cross-over design (AB-BA). Primary outcomes include feasibility of the program and changes in mental and physical health measured by the SF-12 scale. Secondary outcomes focus on multi-domain health and lifestyle behaviors. The study aims to provide evidence for the feasibility and preliminary effectiveness of such an online intervention, which is scalable and accessible across diverse geographical regions, including rural areas. Findings suggest that multimodal interventions addressing multiple risk factors can effectively promote healthy aging and potentially prevent dementia.
