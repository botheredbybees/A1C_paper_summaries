## Validation of the Arts Observational Scale for the Evaluation of Performing Arts Activities in Health Care Settings

This article introduces the Arts Observational Scale (ArtsObS), a tool designed to evaluate the impact of performing arts activities in healthcare settings. The development involved consultation with over 1,500 patients, relatives, and staff over two years, followed by validation with 805 participants over six months. Construct validity was grounded in empirical and theoretical research, inter-rater reliability was found to be strong (r = .721), and concurrent validity also showed significant correlation (r = .641). The ArtsObS is a mixed-methods approach that includes demographic data collection, mood scoring on a seven-point scale before and after arts intervention, set criteria for evaluation applicable across various interventions, and flexible criteria specific to each project. This tool aims to provide evidence of the efficacy of arts programs in healthcare settings and support continuous quality improvement.
