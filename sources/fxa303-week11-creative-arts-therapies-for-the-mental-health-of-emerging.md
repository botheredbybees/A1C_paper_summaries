## Creative arts therapies for the mental health of emerging adults: A systematic review

This systematic review examines the efficacy of creative and expressive arts therapies (CATs) for emerging adults, who face significant mental health challenges yet often encounter barriers to traditional therapeutic approaches due to stigma and perceived ineffectiveness. The study analyzed 11 studies from a pool of over 7200 articles published between 1985 and 2020, focusing on CAT interventions for individuals aged 18–29. Art therapy and poetry therapy were found to be the most commonly studied forms of CATs in this population, with art therapy demonstrating greater statistical significance in efficacy studies compared to other modalities. The review underscores the potential of CATs as destigmatized and non-invasive alternatives that can engage emerging adults more effectively than traditional therapies. It highlights the need for further research employing rigorous methodologies to enhance understanding and application of these therapeutic approaches.
