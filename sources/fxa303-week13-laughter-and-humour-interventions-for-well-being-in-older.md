## Laughter and Humour Interventions for Well-being in Older Adults: A Systematic Review and Intervention Classification

This systematic review assesses the potential of laughter and humor interventions to enhance well-being in older adults aged 60 years or more. The study includes a classification system for different intervention types, aiming to compare their effectiveness and provide guidance for future intervention designs. After screening 178 research papers, five laughter interventions involving 369 participants were retained. These studies demonstrated statistically significant positive effects on well-being outcomes but also highlighted confounding factors inherent in the design of interventions. The review concludes that while such interventions appear to enhance well-being, there is insufficient evidence to isolate the benefits of laughter itself due to methodological limitations. Future research should focus on isolating, tracking, and measuring individual laughter to build a robust evidence base.
