## Arts-based psychosocial training after the Yolanda typhoon in the Philippines

This study examines an arts-based mental health and psychosocial support (MHPSS) intervention conducted in the Philippines after Typhoon Yolanda in 2013, facilitated by IsraAID. The research focuses on a Training of Trainers (TOT) program aimed at providing local education and healthcare professionals with the skills to offer psychosocial support within their communities. Interviews and visual artwork created by ten female participants were analyzed using Consensual Qualitative Research principles, revealing three primary domains: supportive and inhibiting factors for training course participants; community implementation challenges; and perceived benefits of the program for personal development and communal resilience. The study underscores the importance of creative processes in trauma recovery, group dynamics in multicultural settings, and the role of TOT models in disseminating psychosocial support techniques.
