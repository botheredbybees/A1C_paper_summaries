## Afloat: Children and Families Community Resilience Art Project

The Afloat project was initiated in response to the flood that affected Greater Hobart, Tasmania, in May 2018. It aimed to help communities prepare for future potential disasters by fostering resilience and recovery through creative means. The City of Hobart, supported financially by the Australian and Tasmanian governments, engaged professional artists Leigh Tesch and Rosie McKeand to work with children aged 3-6 and their families using various art forms such as drawing, model-making, music, storytelling, and movement. Eight workshops were held for children and two for parents and educators to promote awareness of change, connection to the environment, and community support. A celebratory anniversary event was organized on the Rivulet in South Hobart one year after the flood, offering creative activities, a communal feast, and expert advice from emergency services.
