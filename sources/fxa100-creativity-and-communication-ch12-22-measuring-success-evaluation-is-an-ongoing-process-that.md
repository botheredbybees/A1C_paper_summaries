## Measuring Success

This chapter discusses the evaluation process for arts-based projects involving individuals with dementia, emphasizing its importance for reflecting on experiences, giving voice to participants, and motivating ongoing work. The challenges of deciding what to measure and how are highlighted, along with the complexity in capturing qualitative data such as personal responses and interactions. It suggests making evaluation an integral part of activities rather than a separate process and encourages the use of creative methods like poetry or paintings for evaluations. Various tools and observational techniques like Dementia Care Mapping are recommended. The text also critiques narrow and inflexible evaluation criteria, advocating for flexible approaches that align with person-centered care principles. Finally, it stresses the importance of sharing findings through diverse media to celebrate achievements and inspire further projects.
