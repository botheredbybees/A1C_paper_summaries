## Ethical Considerations in Data Analysis and Dissemination

This chapter discusses ethical considerations in data analysis and dissemination for research projects. It emphasizes the importance of preserving participant anonymity and securely storing data to maintain confidentiality. The text highlights the need for transparent data reporting, avoiding biases like gender or racial stereotypes, and adhering to authorship guidelines established by the Vancouver Protocol. Ethical concerns regarding data falsification and selective reporting are also addressed. Additionally, it covers ethical approval processes, detailing necessary documents such as a research protocol, participant information sheet, and consent form.
