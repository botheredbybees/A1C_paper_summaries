## Putting Communities First: Inclusive Museum Practice through the CARES Framework

This article discusses how the National Gallery Singapore shifted its approach to inclusion and community engagement by adopting a framework called CARES (Capability Building, Advocacy, Research, Engagement & Representation, and Sustainability). The focus is on designing museum experiences with, rather than for, underserved communities such as neurodivergent individuals, disabled visitors, seniors, and migrant workers. Key initiatives include Gallery Wonders, which provides tailored visits based on specific access needs; Art With You and the Calm Room, designed to cater to dementia-friendly practices; and the HOLD Collection, which offers tactile and audio-described guides for blind and vision-impaired visitors. The article emphasizes the importance of asking community members what they need and incorporating their feedback into museum programming. It highlights how this inclusive approach enhances program relevance, visitor engagement, and institutional resilience.
