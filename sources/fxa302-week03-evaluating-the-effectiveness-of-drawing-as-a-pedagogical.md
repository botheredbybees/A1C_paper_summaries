## Evaluating the effectiveness of drawing as a pedagogical tool in teaching histology and human anatomy to dental and allied health sciences: a three-year observational study

This study evaluates the use of drawing as a pedagogical tool in teaching histology and human anatomy to dental and allied health sciences students over three years. Conducted at the University of Palermo, it involved 199 participants who engaged in drawing exercises during their Human Anatomy and Histology module studies. Pre- and post-activity surveys indicated that 80% of participants were inclined towards integrating drawing as a teaching method due to its perceived educational benefits. The research highlights the historical importance of visual arts in anatomical sciences, emphasizing how drawing enhances understanding, retention, and interest in structural sciences among students.
