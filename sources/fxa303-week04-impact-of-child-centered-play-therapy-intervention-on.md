## Impact of child-centered play therapy intervention on children with autism reflected by brain EEG activity: A randomized controlled trial

This study investigates the impact of child-centered play therapy (CCPT) on autistic children, focusing on changes in brain activity and behavioral outcomes. The research employed a randomized controlled trial design with 34 children assigned to an experimental group receiving CCPT sessions for eight weeks and 31 children as a waitlist control group. Pre- and post-intervention electroencephalography (EEG) recordings were conducted during social activities, alongside behavioral assessments using the Social Responsiveness Scale-2, Autism Spectrum Quotient-child, and Adaptive Behavior Assessment System-II. Results showed significant increases in EEG alpha power for the experimental group across various social contexts, indicating enhanced neural activity related to relaxation and social engagement. Behavioral measures also improved significantly in the experimental group, whereas there were no significant changes in the waitlist control group. The study concludes that CCPT is effective in enhancing both neural and behavioral aspects of social functioning in autistic children, providing valuable insights for future research on neurodevelopmental interventions.
