## Effectiveness of music therapy in children with autism spectrum disorder: A systematic review and meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis aimed to evaluate the efficacy of music therapy (MT) in children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). The study included eight randomized controlled trials involving 608 participants, which were systematically reviewed from databases such as PubMed, Embase, and Cochrane Library. Results indicated that MT significantly increased social reactions among children with ASD (SMD = 0.24), but had no significant impact on symptom severity, social adaptive behavior, or speech development. The meta-analysis suggests that while MT can improve social skills in children with ASD, there is no consensus regarding the persistence of these effects over time. These findings are crucial for informing clinical practice and promoting the use of MT as an effective intervention to enhance social interaction and communication in children with ASD.
