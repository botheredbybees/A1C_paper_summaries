## Key components of person-centered care for people with dementia: A systematic review of interventions to design a patient preference study

This systematic review aimed to identify key components of person-centered care (PCC) for people with dementia (PwD) by analyzing interventions from randomized controlled trials and non-randomized studies. The authors conducted a comprehensive search in PubMed, EMBASE, and Web of Science, resulting in the inclusion of 19 studies that covered a wide range of non-pharmacological, psychosocial offers. Nine intervention categories emerged: sensory enhancement/relaxation, social contact, cognitive training, validation and reminiscence, physical activities, environmental adjustments, caregiver training and support, care organization, and daily living assistance. The findings suggest that these components are crucial for designing a decision model to elicit PwD preferences. However, the review primarily focused on nursing home or hospital settings, highlighting the need for future research to explore community-dwelling individuals and primary care settings.
