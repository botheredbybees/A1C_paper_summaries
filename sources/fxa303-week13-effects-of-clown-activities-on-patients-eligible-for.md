## Effects of Clown Activities on Patients Eligible for Palliative Care in Primary Health Care

This study evaluates the impact of clown activities on quality of life, depression, stress, anxiety, and social support for patients eligible for palliative care in primary health settings. The quasi-experimental research involved 16 patients who received 24 home-based visits with trained clowns over several months. Key findings indicate improved quality of life and enhanced social support among participants, particularly in the dimension of social activities (p = 0.023). However, there was an observed increase in anxiety and depression scores (p = 0.007 and p = 0.023 respectively), highlighting mixed outcomes. The authors suggest that clown therapy can contribute positively to patient care by fostering humanized, integral, and relationship-centered approaches in primary health care settings.
