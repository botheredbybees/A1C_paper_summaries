## Revisioning self-identity: The role of portraits, neuroscience and the art therapist's ‘third hand’

The article explores an art therapy intervention designed to help patients with life-threatening and chronic illnesses who struggle with disrupted self-identity due to their conditions. The author, Susan M.D. Carr, introduces the concept of the 'third hand' used by art therapists to create portraits for these patients, enabling them to engage in a co-design process despite physical limitations that prevent them from making art themselves. Through this method, patients can regain a sense of self and identity through the creative interaction with their portrait. The study also incorporates insights from neuroscience, examining how visual imagery impacts emotional and cognitive processes within the brain and body. This intervention is aimed at enhancing quality of life by fostering a stronger, more coherent self-identity, thereby reducing stress and social isolation common among individuals facing serious illnesses.
