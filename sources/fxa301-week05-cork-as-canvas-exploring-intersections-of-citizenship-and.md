## Cork as canvas: Exploring intersections of citizenship and collective memory in the Shandon Big Wash Up murals

This paper examines the intersection of public space, art, and community memory through the lens of the Shandon Big Wash Up mural project in Cork, Ireland. The authors explore how these temporary washed-wall murals interact with the historic architecture of Shandon district, reflecting a symbiotic relationship between art and environment. The study highlights the murals' role as an expression of local community identity, past and present, emphasizing their contribution to urban citizenship by fostering re-enchantment and reconnecting citizens with their cultural heritage. Through this analysis, the paper underscores the murals' capacity to revitalize public spaces, enhance community engagement, and promote a sense of place, thereby enriching the collective memory of Cork's historic district.
