## Music Therapy comforts, soothes those with varying degrees of dementia

The transcript highlights the therapeutic effects of music on individuals suffering from Alzheimer's and other forms of dementia. Moren Bosch, a program coordinator at Clare Bridge of Beach who is also a board-certified music therapist, emphasizes how engaging in musical activities can activate multiple areas of the brain, enabling communication even when speech is impaired due to conditions like stroke or advanced stages of dementia. The story of Larry, an artist and musician who experienced significant cognitive decline after a seizure, illustrates the profound impact of music therapy on his well-being. Music not only provides comfort but also helps in rekindling lost skills and confidence, allowing residents like Larry to reconnect with their identity through musical engagement.
