## The Beaney Health and Wellbeing Toolkit

The document titled 'The Beaney Health and Wellbeing Toolkit' explores the integration of health and wellbeing initiatives within museums, specifically focusing on The Beaney House of Art and Knowledge in Canterbury, Kent. It emphasizes the growing body of evidence supporting the therapeutic value of museums and cultural institutions for individual and community mental and physical health. The toolkit provides guidance on planning, running, and evaluating health and wellbeing activities, including practical steps such as identifying target groups, recruiting participants, and collaborating with facilitators and support staff. It also highlights key benefits like reducing social isolation, improving self-esteem, and enhancing cognitive skills. Additionally, the document underscores the importance of cultural institutions in fostering an environment where health and wellbeing can flourish, alongside working with local healthcare organizations to meet community needs.
