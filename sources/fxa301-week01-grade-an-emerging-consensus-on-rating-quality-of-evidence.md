## GRADE: an emerging consensus on rating quality of evidence and strength of recommendations

This article introduces the GRADE system, which is designed to provide a consistent method for rating the quality of evidence and strength of recommendations in clinical guidelines. The authors argue that many organizations have previously used inconsistent methods for these ratings, leading to potential harm through inappropriate guideline adherence. The GRADE approach addresses this issue by offering clear criteria for evaluating both the quality of evidence and the balance between desirable and undesirable outcomes. It separates decisions about evidence quality from recommendation strength, providing a transparent framework that acknowledges patient values and preferences. While acknowledging limitations in formal grading systems, the authors contend that the benefits of structured guidance outweigh these drawbacks, particularly in informing clinicians and patients on healthcare management decisions.
