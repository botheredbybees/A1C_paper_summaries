## Storied Ethics in Dementia Care

This chapter discusses the application of a narrative or 'storied' ethical approach to dementia care. It highlights how Mrs. Feldman's life stories are being disrupted by her husband, who is increasingly controlling and managing her behavior due to perceived safety concerns related to her dementia. The chapter emphasizes the importance of understanding and mapping an individual’s narrative landscape, challenging negative meta-narratives about decline, promoting co-construction of narratives with carers, and drawing on communities of practice for ethical guidance in daily care routines. It underscores that ethical decisions are integral to ongoing story construction and character development.
