## Transcript: Dementia Australia - Vascular Cognitive Impairment

The transcript discusses vascular cognitive impairment (VCI), which occurs due to damage or blockage in blood vessels within the brain. It disrupts oxygen and nutrient supply to neurons, potentially leading to symptoms such as language difficulties, attention deficits, memory issues, reasoning problems, and more severe forms like vascular dementia. Vascular dementia can coexist with other types of dementia, such as Alzheimer's disease or Lewy body dementia, forming mixed dementia. The primary cause is small vessel disease affecting the brain's blood vessels, often exacerbated by strokes, which may be major or a series of minor ones. Symptoms include cognitive decline in planning and reasoning skills, confusion, memory issues, speech problems, changes in gait, behavioral alterations like agitation, bladder control issues, and depression.
