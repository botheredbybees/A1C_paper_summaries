## Epidemiological studies of the relationship between cultural experiences and public health

This chapter explores the epidemiological examination of cultural participation's influence on public health. It defines cultural participation broadly to include both active involvement in arts and passive consumption like reading or attending performances, without distinguishing between highbrow and lowbrow genres. The authors discuss the socio-economic stratification and gender differences in cultural consumption patterns across Sweden from 1976 to 2006, noting an increase in overall participation while acknowledging disparities. Technological advancements are highlighted as a factor contributing to increased cultural engagement through new mediums like portable music listening devices. However, active participation in arts such as playing musical instruments and singing has shown decreasing trends among younger age groups, contrasting with increasing engagement among the elderly.
