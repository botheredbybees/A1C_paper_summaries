## Introduction to Visual Storytelling

This week's lecture in the Photo Essay course focuses on visual storytelling, a fundamental aspect of human communication valued for its ability to convey complex narratives through images. The text emphasizes that while many people are natural storytellers, effective visual storytelling is an acquired skill. Key topics include caption writing and narrative tense, highlighting how choosing between past and present tenses situates the action within the story. Stream-of-consciousness techniques are introduced as a method for capturing the fluid nature of thought processes in language, though they may require editing to fit conventional formats like captions. Famous photographs such as 'Migrant Mother' by Dorothea Lange and 'Kissing Sailor' illustrate how images can evoke different narratives and ethical complexities. The lecture encourages students to use their personal image bank as a starting point for free-flowing writing exercises, exploring themes like memory and metaphor.
