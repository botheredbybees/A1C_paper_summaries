## Behavioral and Psychological Signs and Symptoms of Dementia: A Consensus Statement on Current Knowledge and Implications for Research and Treatment

This paper presents a consensus statement by experts from various institutions regarding the behavioral and psychological signs and symptoms of dementia (BPSD). The authors argue that BPSD are integral to the disease process, causing significant suffering for patients and caregivers, increasing societal costs, and necessitating humane and effective management strategies. Historically, these symptoms have received less attention than cognitive deficits, but recent demographic changes and social restructuring underscore their importance. The consensus emphasizes the need for research on BPSD's phenomenology, etiology, socioenvironmental influences, and treatment efficacy. They advocate for developing culturally appropriate assessment methods and longitudinal studies to understand symptom prevalence, underlying mechanisms, and impact on patients, families, and society. Additionally, there is a call for defining effective treatment profiles tailored to different types of BPSD through pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions.
