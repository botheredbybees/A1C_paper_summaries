## Dance movement therapy for dementia

This systematic review evaluates the effectiveness of dance movement therapy (DMT) as an intervention for people with dementia. The study aims to assess DMT's impact on behavioral, social, cognitive, and emotional symptoms compared to no treatment or standard care. Despite thorough searches across multiple databases from March 2016, no studies met the inclusion criteria due to a lack of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) involving qualified practitioners delivering DMT for dementia patients. The authors conclude that robust RCTs are necessary to establish whether DMT is an effective therapeutic approach for individuals with dementia. This review highlights the need for well-designed and methodologically rigorous research in this area, particularly given the growing prevalence of dementia worldwide.
