## Play That One Again: the Effect of Music Tempo on Consumer Behaviour in a Restaurant

This research investigates the effects of music tempo on consumer behavior within a restaurant setting. The study utilized a field experiment where consumers dined in a restaurant while slow and fast-paced music were alternately played. The findings reveal that when slow music was playing, customers spent significantly more time dining compared to when fast music was played. Although there was some indication that perceptions of time spent dining might be influenced by the music tempo, this effect did not reach statistical significance. Crucially, the study found a significant relationship between music tempo and money spent on both food and beverages at the restaurant. This work adds valuable insights into how environmental factors like music can influence consumer behavior in service industries.
