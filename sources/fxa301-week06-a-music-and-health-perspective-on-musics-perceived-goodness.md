## A Music and Health Perspective on Music's Perceived ‘Goodness’

Jane Edwards' article explores the concept of music's perceived 'goodness' from a music and health perspective, questioning the widespread belief that engaging with music inherently results in social benefits or is morally positive. The paper delves into various projects and practices where music is promoted as beneficial to health and well-being. Edwards critiques how these narratives can commodify music, reducing it to an agent for social cohesion rather than recognizing its multifaceted role within cultural contexts. She argues that while research often focuses on the measurable outcomes of music participation (such as cognitive benefits), this approach might undermine the intrinsic value of musical experiences in human culture and community expression.
