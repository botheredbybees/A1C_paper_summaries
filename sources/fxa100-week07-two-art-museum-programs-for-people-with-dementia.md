## Two Art Museum Programs for People with Dementia

The article discusses two pioneering art museum programs designed for individuals with dementia and their care partners: the Museum of Modern Art’s (MoMA) “Meet Me at MoMA” program and the Walker Art Center’s “Contemporary Journeys.” Both initiatives emphasize the use of visual arts as a means to promote self-expression, intellectual engagement, and social interaction. The programs provide structured gallery tours and creative workshops, fostering an inclusive environment where participants can engage deeply with art despite cognitive challenges. MoMA’s program includes both on-site visits and off-site sessions at care facilities, tailored to different stages of dementia progression. Evaluations indicate that these programs enhance quality of life for individuals affected by dementia and their caregivers by offering meaningful social experiences in a supportive setting.
