## An introduction to research

This chapter explores the role of research in arts and health interventions, questioning whether such research is exceptional or different from other types of health research. It addresses common misconceptions about arts in health research and discusses why even seemingly obvious questions necessitate rigorous scientific investigation to confirm their validity and influence policy. The author explains that research helps establish the mechanisms by which art impacts health outcomes, characterizes individual variations, and supports the development of the field through new discoveries. The chapter also differentiates between evaluation and research, emphasizing historical purposes, methodological approaches, and practical applications in determining the effectiveness of arts interventions.
