## Perspectives of the Arts on Health and Wellbeing

This unit explores the historical and contemporary intersections between creative arts and health, focusing on three modules. Module 1 covers ancient to seventeenth-century artistic figures and medical illustrations in healing contexts. Module 2 examines nineteenth and early twentieth-century art and its relationship with creativity and mental health through case studies of Beethoven, Monet, Van Gogh, and Schumann. Module 3 discusses twentieth and twenty-first century artists' responses to illness and disability. The unit includes assessments such as quizzes, poster presentations, and an essay. It introduces Medical Humanities by integrating humanities perspectives into medical education and emphasizes the holistic approach to patient care. Health Humanities broadens this scope further by incorporating nursing and creative arts therapies.
