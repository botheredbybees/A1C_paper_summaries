## Evaluation of a standardized humor group in a clinical setting: a feasibility study for older patients with depression

The study evaluates the efficacy of a standardized humor therapy group in a clinical setting for older patients with depression, comparing an experimental group (49 participants) to a control group (50 participants). The intervention consisted of eight sessions and was administered alongside other treatments such as drug therapy. Participants completed various questionnaires pre- and post-treatment to assess changes in depressive symptoms, cheerfulness, and satisfaction with life. Results indicated that the humor therapy group showed significant improvements in state seriousness and life satisfaction compared to the control group. Trends suggested higher increases in cheerfulness and resilience for participants in the humor therapy group. This study suggests that humor therapy can be an additional beneficial intervention for older depressed patients.
