## Becoming Our Story: Emergent Design through Affect

This chapter discusses the evolution of applied theatre and its implications for contemporary practice, focusing on a case study involving seniors in a residential care facility. The authors explore changes in theatrical methods from traditional narrative-driven plays to more experiential forms that involve audience participation. They describe their work with elderly residents using sensory experiences and Person-in-Role techniques to engage participants despite cognitive limitations. The chapter highlights the importance of democratizing theatre practices, emphasizing collaborative ensemble production and emergent design principles.
