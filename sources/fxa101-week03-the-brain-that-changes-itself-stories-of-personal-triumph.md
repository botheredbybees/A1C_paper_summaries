## The Brain That Changes Itself: Stories of Personal Triumph from the Frontiers of Brain Science

Norman Doidge's book 'The Brain That Changes Itself' introduces readers to the revolutionary concept of neuroplasticity, which challenges traditional beliefs about brain function and anatomy. The preface outlines how previously held views suggested that once a person reaches adulthood, their brain structure is fixed, and any damage or limitations are permanent. However, Doidge presents evidence from pioneering scientists who discovered that the human brain can indeed change itself through various activities, learning, and even thought processes. This breakthrough has significant implications for treating neurological conditions and enhancing cognitive abilities at all stages of life. The book explores how neuroplasticity enables individuals to recover from injuries or disorders previously deemed incurable, highlighting cases where patients with blindness or hearing loss have achieved unexpected improvements. Additionally, it discusses the potential of neuroplasticity in reshaping brain architecture through mental activities, suggesting that this discovery could fundamentally alter our understanding of human nature and behavior.
