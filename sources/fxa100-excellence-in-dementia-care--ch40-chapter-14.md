## Excellence in Dementia Care: Research into Practice

The chapter discusses the management and understanding of dementia symptoms from a bio-psycho-social perspective. It critiques the biomedical approach to dementia, highlighting that many behaviors labeled as 'symptoms' may stem from social factors rather than neurological damage alone. The text uses the case of Mr. U, an Army General with dementia, to illustrate how such behaviors can be misinterpreted and inappropriately treated pharmacologically. Furthermore, it delves into the impact of brain pathology on memory function, distinguishing between explicit and implicit memory processes affected by Alzheimer's disease (AD). Case examples are provided to demonstrate deficits in recall versus recognition abilities among individuals with AD. The chapter also explores motor coordination issues like apraxia and visual perception problems such as prosopagnosia after occipital lobe damage.
