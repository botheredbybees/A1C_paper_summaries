## Social ties and mental health

This article reviews the role of social ties in maintaining psychological well-being, focusing on four key insights from the literature. First, it presents two causal models explaining how social networks and supports influence mental health: the main effect model and the stress-buffering model. Second, it highlights that the benefits of social ties for mental health are not uniform across all groups; women with low resources may experience increased mental illness symptoms due to role strain associated with providing support to others. Third, it discusses how individual social ties are embedded within broader social structures, a concept known as social capital. Fourth, while interventions aimed at enhancing mental health through social support have shown success, further research is needed to better understand the design, timing, and effectiveness of such interventions.
