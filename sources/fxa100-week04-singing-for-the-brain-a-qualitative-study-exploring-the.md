## 'Singing for the Brain': A Qualitative Study Exploring the Health and Well-being Benefits of Singing for People with Dementia and Their Carers

The article presents a qualitative study that explores the impact of 'Singing for the Brain' (SftB), an intervention based on group singing activities developed by The Alzheimer’s Society, on people with dementia and their carers. Through semi-structured interviews with ten patient-carer pairs, the researchers found that social inclusiveness, improvements in relationships, memory, and mood were significant benefits reported by participants. Singing sessions also helped individuals accept and cope better with dementia. The study underscores the potential of music-based interventions to enhance well-being for both people diagnosed with dementia and their caregivers.
