## The Day the Music Died

This study investigates the impact of music therapy on depression in a nursing home setting. The research was conducted at Vålerengen bo- og servicesenter, an Oslo-based institution with 84 long-term residents. It observed that when regular music therapy sessions were halted for several months and then resumed, there was a significant reduction in depression levels among the residents as measured by the Montgomery Aasberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS). The study underscores the potential of music therapy to mitigate depressive symptoms in elderly individuals, suggesting its value as a supplementary treatment strategy. The findings highlight the importance of continued research into non-pharmacological interventions for managing depression in aging populations.
