## Interventions – Dementia Services Information and Development Centre

The document provides a comprehensive overview of both pharmacological and non-pharmacological interventions used in the care, treatment, and support of individuals with dementia. Pharmacological treatments include drugs such as Acetylcholine Inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) and Memantine, which aim to slow cognitive decline but have limited impact on overall quality of life. Non-pharmacological interventions encompass Cognitive Stimulation Therapy (CST), Reminiscence Therapy, Validation Therapy, Reality Orientation, Physical Exercise, Multisensory Stimulation (Snoezelen rooms), and Aromatherapy. Each intervention is described in detail, highlighting their benefits and application contexts. The document underscores the importance of a holistic approach to dementia care, emphasizing the integration of both drug-based and non-drug strategies to improve quality of life for individuals with dementia and support caregivers.
