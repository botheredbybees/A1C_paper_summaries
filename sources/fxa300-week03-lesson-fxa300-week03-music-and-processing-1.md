## Music cognition in more detail

This lesson covers fundamental aspects of music perception including pitch, contour, rhythm, and tonality. Definitions are provided for these terms from academic sources such as Grove Music Online and the Oxford English Dictionary. The concept of pitch is explored through the arrangement of frequencies in auditory cortex neurons and its psychological nature. Tonotopy, the spatial organization of sound frequency processing in the brain, is discussed alongside Diana Deutsch’s research on how exposure to music influences perception of intervals, scales, and consonance. Contour recognition involves understanding melody shapes separately from individual pitches, which develops early in childhood. Rhythm includes tempo, meter, accent, duration, and hierarchical perception through beat patterns and metrical structures.
