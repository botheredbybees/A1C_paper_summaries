## The Neural Basis of Human Dance

This study explores the neural basis of human dance by examining three core aspects: entrainment, meter, and patterned movement using positron emission tomography (PET). Amateur dancers performed tango steps to music while their brain activity was recorded. The research highlights the involvement of specific regions such as the anterior cerebellar vermis for rhythmic synchronization, right putamen for voluntary control over metric rhythm, and medial superior parietal lobule for spatial navigation during dance movements. Additional cortical, subcortical, and cerebellar areas were also active during these complex sensorimotor tasks. The findings contribute to understanding the integration of rhythmic, spatially patterned whole-body movements in human dance, providing insights into the neural mechanisms supporting such activities.
