## The effects of loving-kindness interventions on positive and negative mental health outcomes: A systematic review and meta-analysis

This systematic review and meta-analysis examines the effects of loving-kindness interventions (LKIs) on mental health outcomes, both positive (such as mindfulness, compassion, and positive affect) and negative (including negative affect and psychological symptoms), in adults across various sample types. The authors synthesized data from 23 randomized controlled studies to assess LKI efficacy relative to passive control groups, active controls, and alternative therapeutic treatments. They found that LKIs had significant positive effects on all mental health indices when compared to passive control conditions but no significant differences were observed between LKIs and other treatment alternatives or active control groups. The study also explored variables affecting the effectiveness of LKIs, such as intervention format, length, and presence of a live facilitator, finding that these factors did not significantly impact outcomes. This research underscores the potential utility of LKIs in mental health interventions but highlights the need for further investigation to establish their efficacy relative to other therapeutic approaches.
