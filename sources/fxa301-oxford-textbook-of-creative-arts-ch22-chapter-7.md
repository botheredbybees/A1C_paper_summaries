## Community Cultural Development for Health and Wellbeing

This chapter explores the concept of community cultural development (CCD) as a framework to enhance health and wellbeing through arts-based initiatives at various levels. CCD is described not just as an intervention but as part of ordinary communal life, aiming to improve public health outcomes by fostering social engagement and creativity. The text discusses both formally structured programs like the 'colourful condom' campaign and grassroots community events such as village art fairs. Key examples include local projects funded for cultural activities among immigrant communities in Seattle and international initiatives like UNESCO’s Creative Cities Network which supports sustainable development through culture. It highlights the importance of policy support for CCD to ensure its effectiveness, addressing social determinants of health and promoting equity.
