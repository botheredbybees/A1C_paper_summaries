## The Art of Observation: A Pedagogical Framework

Caroline Wellbery and Rebecca A. McAteer's paper, 'The Art of Observation: A Pedagogical Framework,' explores the role of observational skills in medical education, particularly through engagement with literary and visual arts. The authors argue that as technological advancements diminish face-to-face interactions between healthcare providers and patients, there is a growing interest in arts observation programs aimed at enhancing clinical skills such as empathy, communication, critical thinking, and attention to detail. They propose that training observational skills through the arts offers an integrated approach that can fortify these humanistic offerings more effectively than other medical humanities pedagogies. Observational skills not only align with scientific paradigms but also encourage a mindful and collective engagement in observing interactions between subject (observer) and object (patient), thereby fostering self-awareness and reflection on values within the prevailing medical culture. This approach aims to articulate problems that arise from standard practices, potentially leading to beneficial interventions for physicians, colleagues, and patients.
