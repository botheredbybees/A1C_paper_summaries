## Arts in Criminal Justice and Corrections

Arts in Criminal Justice and Corrections is an interdisciplinary volume that explores the role of arts programs in prison settings across various countries including the USA, UK, Australia, New Zealand, and Belgium. Edited by Amanda Gardner and Laura Caulfield, this book provides a comprehensive overview of research methods, challenges, and impacts of using arts as a tool for rehabilitation and re-entry into society for incarcerated individuals. It includes contributions from researchers with direct experience in prisons, such as those who were formerly incarcerated themselves or have transitioned from practitioner to academic roles. The volume highlights the importance of evidence-based approaches in documenting the impact of these programs on recidivism rates and societal well-being, advocating for greater investment and research into this promising field.
