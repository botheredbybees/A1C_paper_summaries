## Arts and Refugees: Multidisciplinary Perspectives (Vol. 2)

The article 'Arts and Refugees: Multidisciplinary Perspectives (Vol. 2)' by Marco Martiniello and Elsa Mescoli explores the multifaceted role of art in the lives of refugees within urban European contexts. The authors delve into how artistic practices contribute to the multidimensional integration process, foster solidarity movements, and serve as a means for refugees to claim their rights and status within new societies. The study adopts a broad definition of both 'refugees' and 'arts', encompassing individuals with varied migratory experiences and diverse cultural expressions. It highlights the therapeutic benefits of art in managing psychosomatic disorders and enhancing psychological well-being among refugees, while also emphasizing the political dimension through which art can be used as an alternative strategy for claiming rights and expressing demands. The article further discusses gender dynamics within refugee artistic practices and racial inequalities within cultural sectors, arguing that these practices challenge existing hegemonies and provide a transformative platform for representation and recognition.
