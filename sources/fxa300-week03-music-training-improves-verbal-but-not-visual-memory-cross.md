## Music Training Improves Verbal but Not Visual Memory: Cross-Sectional and Longitudinal Explorations in Children

This study examines the impact of music training on verbal and visual memory in children, building upon previous findings for adults. The research suggests that music training enhances verbal memory but does not affect visual memory, indicating a localized effect within the brain's left temporal lobe. Cross-sectional analysis reveals better verbal memory in musically trained children compared to their counterparts without such training. Longitudinal data collected over one year further supports this finding, showing improved verbal memory in those who continued or started music training but no improvement for those who discontinued it. These results align with neuroanatomical evidence suggesting that musical experience contributes specifically to the development of the left temporal lobe, thereby enhancing verbal memory functions.
