## A Life-Course Study of Cognitive Reserve in Dementia—From Childhood to Old Age

This study investigates the life-course model of cognitive reserve in dementia, examining how childhood school performance, formal education level, and occupational complexity influence the risk of developing dementia later in life. Utilizing data from a large Swedish population (N=7,574), the research spans a 21-year follow-up period. Results indicate that individuals with higher childhood academic achievement are less likely to develop dementia, as are those engaged in complex cognitive occupations. However, while professional or university education initially predicts lower risk of dementia, this effect diminishes when adjusted for occupational complexity. The study concludes that early-life cognitive performance provides significant protection against dementia risk and remains beneficial even without continued educational or occupational stimulation later in life.
