## Working with trauma: expanding, examining, and evidencing the value of art therapy

The editorial by Coles, Murphy, and Winter discusses the importance of expanding, examining, and evidencing the value of art therapy in addressing trauma. It highlights diverse approaches to working with people who have experienced trauma, including structured interventions and adaptive therapeutic practices that empower clients through creative participation. The guest editors reflect on the limited submissions for this special issue and suggest that art therapists may need to better communicate their approach's efficacy. They emphasize the importance of incorporating insights from other therapy modalities and engaging in co-production with former clients to innovate and strengthen the field of art therapy.
