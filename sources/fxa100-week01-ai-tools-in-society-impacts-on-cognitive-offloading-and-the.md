## AI Tools in Society: Impacts on Cognitive Offloading and the Future of Critical Thinking

This study investigates the relationship between artificial intelligence (AI) tool usage and critical thinking skills, focusing on cognitive offloading as a mediating factor. The research utilized a mixed-method approach involving surveys and in-depth interviews with participants from diverse age groups and educational backgrounds. Key findings revealed a significant negative correlation between frequent AI tool usage and critical thinking abilities, mediated by increased cognitive offloading. Younger participants showed higher dependence on AI tools and lower critical thinking scores compared to older participants. Higher educational attainment was associated with better critical thinking skills regardless of AI usage. The study highlights the potential cognitive costs of relying heavily on AI technologies and underscores the need for strategies that promote critical engagement with these tools in an increasingly digital world.
