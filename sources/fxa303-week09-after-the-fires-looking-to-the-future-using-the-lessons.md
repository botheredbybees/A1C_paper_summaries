## After the fires: looking to the future using the lessons from the past

The editorial 'After the fires: looking to the future using the lessons from the past' by Alexander C McFarlane and Beverley Raphael discusses the mental health implications of bushfires in Australia, focusing on the importance of informed care based on research findings. The authors emphasize the crucial role of general practitioners (GPs) in providing post-disaster services, suggesting that GPs should assess patients for physical and psychological trauma, provide guidance on health and wellbeing strategies, and ensure follow-up care. They also highlight the need to address ongoing mental health issues such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), depression, and grief among affected communities. The article draws lessons from previous disasters, particularly the 1983 Ash Wednesday bushfires, advocating for early intervention through screening and treatment protocols tailored to local clinic settings. It underscores the necessity of continued education programs for GPs in diagnosing and treating trauma-related disorders and highlights the importance of special attention towards emergency service personnel due to their prolonged exposure. The authors conclude by stressing that future research should build upon existing knowledge rather than replicating past studies, ensuring innovative approaches that do not burden those already suffering.
