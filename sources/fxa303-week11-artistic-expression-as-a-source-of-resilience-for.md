## Artistic expression as a source of resilience for transgender and gender diverse young people

The study examines the role of artistic expression in enhancing resilience among transgender and gender diverse (TGD) young people with multiple marginalized identities, focusing on those who are persons of color, immigrants, or live in rural areas. Using qualitative methods including photo elicitation and grounded theory, interviews were conducted with TGD youth aged 14-25 across the United States. The findings highlight that artistic expression serves as a form of authentic self-expression, coping mechanism, means to connect with others, and pathway toward personal agency for these young individuals. This research underscores the importance of engaging in creative arts within clinical settings to improve therapeutic engagement and promote healing among TGD youth.
