## Knowledge and Health Beliefs About Dementia Prevention Among Chinese Older Adults: A Qualitative Study

This qualitative study explores the knowledge and health beliefs of Chinese older adults regarding dementia prevention, using an informed grounded theory approach guided by the Health Belief Model (HBM). The research involved semi-structured interviews with 42 participants. Key themes identified include barriers and facilitators to dementia prevention influenced by cultural factors such as traditional superstitions, modern scientific thinking, aging paradigms, digital technology acceptance, and collective values. Positive transformations observed include reduced traditional superstitions and increased use of modern healthcare approaches. However, persistent elements like fear of illness and death contribute to stigma around dementia care. The study highlights the importance of culturally sensitive interventions in addressing dementia prevention among Chinese older adults.
