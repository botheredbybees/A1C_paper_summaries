## Musicians’ and nonmusicians’ short-term memory for verbal and musical sequences: Comparing phonological similarity and pitch proximity

The article by Williamson, Baddeley, and Hitch (2010) explores the comparison between verbal and musical short-term memory processes using a novel visual grid response method designed for both musicians and nonmusicians. The study aims to investigate whether the phonological loop of working memory can also process musical stimuli. Through three experiments, they found that short-term memory for tones shares similarities with verbal memory in terms of capacity limitations and sensitivity to pitch proximity in non-musical participants. However, musicians showed no effect of pitch proximity, suggesting potential strategy differences between these groups. The findings suggest a limited degree of correspondence between how verbal and musical sounds are processed within auditory short-term memory, supporting the notion that some resources may be shared but also highlighting domain-specific processing strategies.
