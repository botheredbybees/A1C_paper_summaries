## Music and Mood: Universal Responses and Cultural Contexts

This lesson explores the universal emotional responses to music across cultures, highlighting an experiment by Dr. Thomas Fritz involving a tribe in Cameroon with no exposure to Western music. It discusses how various musical scales and keys can evoke specific moods, emphasizing that these associations are culturally contingent rather than universally inherent. Additionally, it covers the historical context of music theory during the Baroque era, when emotions were linked to particular musical motifs and keys. The lesson also delves into the impact of tuning systems on emotional expression in music and examines how background music influences dining experiences and taste perceptions. Finally, it introduces Juslin's BRECVEMA model, which outlines eight mechanisms explaining emotional responses to music.
