## Hormones and the Adolescent Brain

The article explores the impact of hormones on adolescent brain development. During puberty, an influx of sex steroids such as estrogen and testosterone shapes significant changes in neural circuitry and cognitive functions. These hormonal surges contribute to the maturation of the cerebral cortex by stimulating axonal growth, enhancing myelination, and pruning weak synaptic connections. Key regions affected include the amygdala (for emotional processing), hippocampus (for memory formation), and prefrontal cortex (for executive function). The research underscores the role of hormones in fostering more sophisticated cognitive processes necessary for adult brain functionality.
