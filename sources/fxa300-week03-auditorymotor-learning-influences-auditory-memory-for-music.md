## Auditory–Motor Learning Influences Auditory Memory for Music

This study explores how auditory and motor learning influence performers' memory for music through two experiments involving skilled pianists. The research reveals that recognition of learned melodies is better following auditory-only or auditory-motor (both weakly and strongly coupled) training compared to motor-only training. Notably, strongly coupled auditory-motor learning leads to superior recognition compared to auditory-only learning. Individual differences in mental imagery capabilities also play a significant role: pianists with high auditory imagery skills showed enhanced memory when the learning involved motor tasks without sound feedback. The findings indicate that motor learning can improve auditory memory for music beyond what auditory training alone provides, and this benefit is influenced by variations in acoustic features of the melodies as well as individual differences in sensory-motor integration abilities.
