## Arts Engagement for People with Dementia: Independent Evaluation of the Art Access Program, Art Gallery Of New South Wales

This report evaluates the Art Access Program at the Art Gallery of New South Wales (AGNSW), which provides art engagement opportunities for people living with dementia, their carers, family members, and healthcare professionals. The program began as a six-month pilot in 2010 and has since expanded to accommodate over 900 participants annually. Key elements include guided tours by trained staff and volunteers who provide context and encourage discussions about artworks. Evaluations indicate that the program enhances social inclusion, combats stigma associated with dementia, and offers meaningful engagement for individuals at various stages of cognitive decline. The study uses a mixed-method approach to assess impacts on health and wellbeing, acknowledging the complexity in measuring social and cultural interventions.
