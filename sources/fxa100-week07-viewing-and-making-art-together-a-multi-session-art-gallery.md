## Viewing and making art together: a multi-session art-gallery-based intervention for people with dementia and their carers

This study investigates the impact of an eight-week art-gallery-based intervention for individuals with mild to moderate dementia and their caregivers, conducted at two distinct galleries—one featuring traditional European artwork and another showcasing contemporary international art. Utilizing a mixed-methods approach involving standardized questionnaires and interviews, the research sought to evaluate changes in social inclusion, caregiver burden, quality of life, and daily living activities. Despite no significant quantitative differences between groups, qualitative data indicated positive well-being benefits including enhanced cognitive capacities and improved social engagement for dementia patients and reduced caregiver stress. The study underscores the potential role of art galleries as non-clinical community resources for enhancing mental health and promoting social inclusion in dementia care.
