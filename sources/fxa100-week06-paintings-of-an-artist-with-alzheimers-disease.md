## Paintings of an artist with Alzheimer's disease: visuoconstructural deficits during dementia

This article investigates the visuoconstructural deficits experienced by Carolus Horn, a German artist diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease (AD), through an analysis of his artwork throughout the progression of his illness. The study provides valuable insights into how AD affects artistic expression and visual perception, revealing that Horn’s later works exhibit simplification in color schemes, loss of depth perception, and spatial errors indicative of visuospatial impairments associated with dementia. The research highlights the potential of studying an artist's work to understand cognitive decline related to AD, contributing to a deeper understanding of how the disease impacts not just neurological functions but also creative processes.
