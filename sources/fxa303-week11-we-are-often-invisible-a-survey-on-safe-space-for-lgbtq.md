## We are often invisible: A survey on safe space for LGBTQ students in secondary school choral programs

This article presents a comprehensive survey of LGBTQ college students reflecting on their experiences in middle and high school choral programs, revealing both quantitative data and qualitative insights. The study highlights that while high school choral classrooms were perceived as relatively safe environments for the majority of respondents, there were significant challenges faced by transgender students navigating their gender identities within these settings. Key findings include the importance of explicit non-discrimination policies and supportive language in fostering a sense of safety and inclusivity. Respondents emphasized the need for open acknowledgment of LGBTQ identities within choral classrooms to encourage an environment where all students can thrive without fear of bullying or harassment. The research underscores the critical role that music educators play in creating safe spaces for LGBTQ youth, advocating for professional development opportunities and training specifically tailored to support these students.
