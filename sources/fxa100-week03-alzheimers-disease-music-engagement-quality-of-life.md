## Alzheimer’s Disease & Music Engagement: Quality of Life Economic Impact Analysis

The report 'Alzheimer’s Disease & Music Engagement: Quality of Life Economic Impact Analysis' was prepared by Deloitte LLP for the NeuroArts Blueprint Initiative in collaboration with AARP and the A. Barry Rand Fund of the AARP Foundation, focusing on assessing the economic impact of music engagement on individuals with Alzheimer's disease and their caregivers. The study does not aim to provide a cost-benefit analysis but rather evaluates potential economic outcomes from various forms of music engagement.

Key findings highlight that active music engagement can improve quality of life metrics for patients by reducing agitation, enhancing activities of daily living (ADLs), and decreasing caregiver burden. Monetization pathways are detailed through a model framework to quantify the economic benefits arising from improved QOL measures such as reduced healthcare costs due to lessened need for medical interventions.

The report underscores the importance of adopting music engagement programs as a supplementary therapeutic approach alongside traditional treatments, given its positive influence on patient well-being and caregiver relief. Such initiatives are argued to have significant societal and economic relevance in managing dementia care effectively.
