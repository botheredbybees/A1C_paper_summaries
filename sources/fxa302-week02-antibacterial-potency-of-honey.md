## Antibacterial Potency of Honey

The review article by Najla A. Albaridi examines the antibacterial properties of honey, a natural product that has been used for centuries to combat infections and promote healing. Despite advancements in managing infectious diseases globally, antibiotic resistance remains a significant issue, prompting renewed interest in ancient remedies like honey. Honey’s complex composition includes sugars (fructose and glucose), proteins, enzymes, vitamins, minerals, polyphenols, and other minor compounds. Variations in these components are influenced by floral sources, seasons, and geographical regions. Darker honeys often exhibit higher concentrations of phenolic compounds, which correlate with enhanced antibacterial activity. The review highlights the mechanisms underlying honey’s antimicrobial properties, including hydrogen peroxide generation and enzymatic activities such as glucose oxidase and catalase. Additionally, it discusses methods for measuring honey’s antibacterial potency, emphasizing its potential in treating antibiotic-resistant bacterial infections.
