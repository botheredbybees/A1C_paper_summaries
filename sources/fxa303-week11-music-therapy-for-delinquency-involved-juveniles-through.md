## Music Therapy for Delinquency Involved Juveniles Through Tripartite Collaboration: A Mixed Method Study

This study explores the efficacy of a music therapy program for juveniles involved in delinquency through collaborative efforts between an educational institution, district prosecutor's office, and corporate sponsors. The project, named 'Young & Great Music,' targeted 178 adolescents who were either suspended from indictment or under supervised probation due to juvenile justice system involvement. Participants engaged in a 15-session program focusing on music creation and songwriting as means of fostering positive personal resources such as self-concept, resilience, and stress coping skills. Quantitative analysis via paired t-tests revealed significant improvements across all scales, indicating the program's effectiveness. Qualitative data from interviews underscored participants' experiences of gaining new perspectives, courage, perseverance, and self-awareness through music therapy sessions. The study highlights the potential for arts-based rehabilitation services to positively impact at-risk youth and suggests a need for sustained programming within juvenile justice frameworks.
