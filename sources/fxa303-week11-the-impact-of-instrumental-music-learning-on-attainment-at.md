## The impact of instrumental music learning on attainment at age 16: a pilot study

The study examines the impact of instrumental music learning on academic attainment at age 16 in the UK, addressing a gap in existing research that primarily focuses on international evidence. Utilizing data from 608 students, including 115 who played musical instruments, the findings indicate better academic outcomes and greater progress for those involved in instrument playing compared to their peers. The benefits were most pronounced among participants with longer engagement periods. Various factors influencing these results are discussed, such as the quality of music education programs and the role of supportive home environments. Additionally, the research draws upon international studies highlighting similar positive correlations between instrumental music learning and academic achievement, providing context for the UK findings.
