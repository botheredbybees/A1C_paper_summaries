## Brain Activities in a Skilled versus a Novice Artist: An fMRI Study

The study by Solso investigates the brain activity of a skilled portrait artist, Humphrey Ocean, and contrasts it with that of a novice during the task of drawing faces. Using functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI), the research reveals distinct differences in brain activation patterns between the expert and the non-expert. The skilled artist shows reduced activation in areas related to facial processing but increased activity in higher-order cognitive regions, suggesting more efficient processing of visual information. Additionally, Ocean's eye movements were found to be twice as long during drawing compared to when not engaged in the task. These findings highlight the importance of brain scanning technology and hand-eye movement measurement in understanding the neurological basis of artistic expertise.
