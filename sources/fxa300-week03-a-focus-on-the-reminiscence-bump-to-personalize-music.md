## A Focus on the Reminiscence Bump to Personalize Music Playlists for Dementia

This study explores the relationship between music and memory, particularly focusing on the reminiscence bump phenomenon where individuals recall memories vividly from their teenage years. The researchers surveyed participants to identify when they best remembered popular music from various decades between 1945 and 2015. They found that people could most accurately recall music from ages 13 to 19, regardless of the birth year. This evidence suggests that personalized music playlists focused on songs from this period can significantly enhance the quality of life for individuals living with dementia by triggering positive emotions and enhancing cognitive engagement.
