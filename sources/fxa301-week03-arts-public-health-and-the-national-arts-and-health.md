## Arts, public health and the National Arts and Health Framework: a lexicon for health professionals

This commentary introduces the National Arts and Health Framework endorsed by Australia's health ministers in 2014, emphasizing its role in integrating arts into public health initiatives for better mental, social, and physical well-being. The framework identifies three pathways to enhance health environments, reorient health services, contribute to policy-making, support community development, and personal growth. It highlights the benefits of arts engagement for mental health, such as increased confidence, self-esteem, and resilience among youth and elderly populations. Additionally, it discusses how participating in artistic activities can foster social connections, reduce isolation, and improve physical health through increased activity levels. The paper also addresses the distinction between art therapy and general arts engagement, underscoring that while both involve creative processes, their goals differ significantly.
