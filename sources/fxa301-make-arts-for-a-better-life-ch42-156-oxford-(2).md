## Collection of Academic Works in Ethnomusicology and Applied Anthropology

This document is a compendium of scholarly references and citations related to the fields of ethnomusicology, social anthropology, arts therapy, cultural heritage preservation, and interdisciplinary studies that integrate music with health and community development. The sources include books, journal articles, book chapters, conference papers, dissertations, and online resources. Authors like Brian Schrag contribute extensively through works such as 'Creating Local Arts Together' (2013a) and 'How Bamiléké Music-Makers Create Culture in Cameroon' (2005). Other significant contributors include Muriel Saville-Troike with her seminal work on communication ethnography, Richard Schechner discussing performance studies, and Huib Schippers exploring sustainability in music cultures. The collection also includes interdisciplinary works by scholars like Anthony Seeger on documentation of ethnomusicology practices, Ruth Stone examining African music contexts, and Robert Sternberg and Todd Lubart's theories on creativity and evaluation frameworks for arts-health initiatives.
