## Effect of Antidiabetic Drugs in Alzheimer's Disease: A Systematic Review

This review evaluates the impact of antidiabetic drugs on Alzheimer’s disease (AD) and AD-like dementia through preclinical models and clinical studies. The authors highlight that both AD and type 2 diabetes mellitus exhibit disruptions in brain insulin signaling, suggesting a potential therapeutic role for antidiabetic medications in mitigating cognitive decline. Various antidiabetic treatments were assessed, including GLP1 receptor agonists (e.g., exenatide), thiazolidinediones (e.g., pioglitazone), biguanides (e.g., metformin), sulphonylureas (SU), SGLT2 inhibitors, DPP4 inhibitors, insulin analogs, and others. The review found that these medications have promising effects on tau pathology, neuronal health, oxidative stress, neuroinflammation, vascular alterations, brain glucose metabolism, and cognitive function in preclinical models. However, further clinical research is needed to optimize their effectiveness in treating AD.
