## Environmental and mental conditions predicting the experience of involuntary musical imagery: An experience sampling method study

This study explores the environmental factors and psychological conditions related to involuntary musical imagery (INMI) through an experience sampling method (ESM). The research involved 40 volunteers who reported their INMI experiences, contextual information, and associated environmental conditions six times daily over one week. Bayesian network modeling was used to analyze the data, revealing that the activity engaged in by a person plays a crucial role in mind wandering, which facilitates INMI. The occurrence of INMI is independent of time of day but influenced by triggers affecting subjective evaluation. Key findings highlight the importance of contextual activities and mental states such as low cognitive load and mind wandering in predicting INMI experiences.
