## Connecting Refugees through Creativity, Sydney Theatre Company

This transcript captures a conversation at the Sydney Theatre Company on October 30, 2018, focusing on an innovative program called 'Connected' that uses drama to enhance English literacy among migrants, refugees, and asylum seekers. The program emphasizes non-verbal communication methods like body language and facial expressions to facilitate learning regardless of participants' initial English proficiency levels. Participants report increased confidence in speaking and listening skills, improved engagement in class activities, and a greater willingness to help peers and communicate in English. The Connected program has also helped students develop their creative potential, envision future possibilities, and transition into mainstream educational settings more successfully.
