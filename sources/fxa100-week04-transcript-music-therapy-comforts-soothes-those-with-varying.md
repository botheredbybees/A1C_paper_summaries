## Music Therapy for Individuals with Dementia

This transcript discusses the use of music therapy to help individuals with varying degrees of dementia. Moren Bosch, the program coordinator and board-certified music therapist, explains how engaging in musical activities activates multiple areas of the brain, enabling communication for those who have lost language abilities due to conditions like Alzheimer's or stroke-induced aphasia. The case study focuses on Larry, a former artist and musician whose wife noted his reluctance to engage in singing before moving to Clare Bridge ofal Beach. Under the influence of music therapy sessions led by Bosch, Larry has become more active musically, playing piano and engaging with musical activities, thereby regaining self-confidence and reconnecting with aspects of his identity.
