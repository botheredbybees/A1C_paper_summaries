## The Musical Ages of Modern Man: How Our Taste in Music Changes Over a Lifetime

This research article explores the transformation of personal music preferences throughout human life stages, emphasizing that while intrinsic to personality and experience, there are common trends in musical genre shifts associated with different life challenges. The study uses data from over a quarter million participants over ten years, categorizing genres into five categories: mellow, unpretentious, sophisticated, intense, and contemporary. Adolescence is marked by a preference for 'intense' music (e.g., punk, metal) as a means to assert independence, which evolves in early adulthood to 'contemporary' music (pop, rap) reflecting the search for acceptance and intimacy. Middle age sees a shift towards 'sophisticated' and 'unpretentious' genres (jazz, classical; country, folk), symbolizing social status attainment and emotional relaxation. The research highlights how music serves as an identity marker and a medium for social engagement at various life stages.
