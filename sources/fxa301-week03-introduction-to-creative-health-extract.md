## Introduction to Creative Health Extract

The document 'Introduction to Creative Health Extract' outlines the concept of creative health, emphasizing its multifaceted benefits and applications. Creative health is defined as activities and approaches that use creativity to enhance physical, mental, and social well-being. These can include visual arts, performing arts, crafts, film, literature, cooking, and nature-based activities like gardening. The text underscores the holistic view of health and wellbeing provided by creative health, noting its contributions to illness prevention, healthy behavior promotion, long-term condition management, and treatment recovery throughout life stages.

Creative health is particularly highlighted for its role in fostering social capital, cohesion, and overall well-being while addressing social determinants of health. The review discusses how engaging with creative activities can prevent or manage specific conditions such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, long-COVID, falls prevention, stroke recovery, cognitive decline, cancer risk factors, cardiovascular diseases, musculoskeletal conditions, and mental health issues like depression and anxiety.

The document argues that creative health must be available to everyone without reinforcing health inequalities. It encourages the integration of creative health into healthcare systems to ensure equitable access across all communities. Furthermore, it highlights the importance of creativity not being seen as an 'extra' but as essential for maintaining well-being throughout life stages—from early years through healthy aging and end-of-life care.
