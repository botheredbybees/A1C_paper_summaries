## Arts Education for Young Children of the 21st Century

In this document, Ann Veale explores the role of arts education in preparing young children for the future, focusing on Australia's context and cultural heritage. The paper reviews Howard Gardner's theory that early childhood is crucial for developing multiple forms of intelligence, including artistic intelligence. It highlights the significance of play as a means through which children can develop various cognitive abilities and social attributes necessary for collaborative work in an evolving technological world. Veale emphasizes the importance of introducing young children to cultural symbol systems through arts education to foster their understanding and engagement with societal norms and values. The document argues that by integrating art and play into educational programs, children are better equipped to make sense of their environment and develop a well-rounded set of intelligences needed for the 21st century. This perspective is particularly relevant in multicultural societies like Australia, where education must balance multiple cultural heritages, including Aboriginal traditions.
