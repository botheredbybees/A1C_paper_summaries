## Adolescent and Young Adult Health

The document provides key facts, an overview, and details on major health issues affecting adolescents and young adults aged 10–24 years. It highlights that over 1.5 million individuals in this age group died in 2021, with significant regional disparities in mortality rates. Leading causes of death include injuries (road traffic accidents, drowning), violence (interpersonal violence, self-harm), and maternal conditions for girls aged 15–19 years.

The text also emphasizes the importance of mental health issues such as depression and anxiety, noting that half of all mental health disorders in adulthood begin before age 18. Substance use, including alcohol and drugs, is another major concern, with high rates of heavy episodic drinking among adolescents. Additionally, the document discusses communicable diseases like HIV/AIDS and tuberculosis, which disproportionately affect young people.

Environmental factors also play a crucial role, increasing risks for unintentional injuries and infectious diseases due to immature physiological systems in adolescents. Early pregnancy remains a significant health issue globally, with varying birth rates across different regions. The importance of addressing these issues through prevention strategies, education, and policy is stressed.
