## Ancient Greek music: now we finally know what it sounded like

This article discusses the challenges and recent breakthroughs in understanding ancient Greek music, which was integral to classical Greece from around 750 BC to 350 BC. The author highlights that while literary texts provide detailed descriptions of notes, scales, effects, and instruments like the lyre and aulos, reconstructing actual music has been elusive due to unfamiliar terminology and sparse notated music. However, recent projects have reconstructed ancient Greek musical instruments accurately, providing insights into pitch ranges and tunings. The author's research on Euripides' 'Orestes,' dating back to 408 BC, demonstrates the use of quarter-tone intervals for word-painting and tonality in the composition. This reconstruction has been performed with aulos accompaniment, suggesting that ancient Greek music should be recognized as foundational to the European musical tradition.
