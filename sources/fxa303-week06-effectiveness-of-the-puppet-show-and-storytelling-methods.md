## Effectiveness of the Puppet Show and Storytelling Methods on Children’s Behavioral Problems

The study investigates the effectiveness of puppet shows and storytelling on behavioral problems (BPs) in preschool children aged 3 to 5 years. The research employed a quasi-experimental design with pretest-posttest evaluations across three groups: puppet show, storytelling, and control. Statistical analysis revealed significant improvements in BPs for both intervention groups compared to the control group immediately after and one month post-intervention. Notably, the puppet show method demonstrated superior effectiveness over storytelling in reducing behavioral issues. This study underscores the therapeutic value of creative arts like puppetry and storytelling as tools for addressing childhood behavioral challenges.
