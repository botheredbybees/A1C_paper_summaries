## Creative Arts in Health Professional Education and Practice: A Case Study

The chapter explores the integration of creative arts into nursing and midwifery education at King's College London through the Culture and Care program. Initiated in response to Jane Cummings' Compassion in Practice strategy, the program aims to enhance students’ non-normative ethics and values for person-centered care. The program includes various activities like photography projects, jewelry making workshops, film screenings, choirs, and interactive tours. It emphasizes complex interventions that involve multiple interacting elements difficult to define as a single ‘active ingredient’. Collaboration with external arts organizations and internal partners was key to the success of these initiatives. The chapter also discusses the role of creative arts in promoting communication skills, fostering professional intimacy, and developing students’ imaginative thinking.
