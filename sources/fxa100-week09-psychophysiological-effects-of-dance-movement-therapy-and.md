## Psychophysiological Effects of Dance Movement Therapy and Physical Exercise on Older Adults With Mild Dementia: A Randomized Controlled Trial

This study examines the psychophysiological impacts of dance movement therapy (DMT) and physical exercise on older adults diagnosed with mild dementia. A randomized controlled trial was conducted, recruiting 204 participants into DMT, exercise, or waitlist control groups over a period of 12 weeks. The findings indicate that the DMT group experienced significant improvements in depression levels, loneliness, negative mood, daily functioning, and diurnal cortisol slope, with these effects persisting up to one year post-intervention. In contrast, participants in the exercise intervention did not show such benefits. This study highlights the potential of DMT as a multifaceted approach for enhancing various aspects of well-being among older adults suffering from cognitive decline.
