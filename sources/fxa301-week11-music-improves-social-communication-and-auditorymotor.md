## Music improves social communication and auditory–motor connectivity in children with autism

This study, conducted as a randomized controlled trial (RCT), evaluates the impact of an individualized music intervention on social communication and brain connectivity in children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) aged between 6 to 12 years. Participants were randomly assigned either to a music-based intervention or a non-music behavioral control group. The music intervention utilized improvisational techniques through song and rhythm to enhance social communication skills, while the non-music control group received structurally matched behavioral interventions without musical elements. Post-intervention assessments indicated that children in the music group showed significant improvements in social communication scores compared to those in the non-music group. Furthermore, resting-state functional magnetic resonance imaging (rsfMRI) revealed enhanced connectivity between auditory and motor regions in the brain for the music intervention participants, suggesting a positive link between musical activities and neural network modulation pertinent to communication skills in ASD. This research contributes valuable neuroscientific evidence supporting music as a therapeutic tool that can positively influence both behavioral and neurological aspects of individuals with ASD, offering promising directions for future interventions tailored to individual strengths.
