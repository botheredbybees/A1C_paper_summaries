## Nurturing the Heart: Creativity, Art Therapy and Dementia

This monograph by Patricia Baines explores the importance of creativity in improving quality of life for individuals with dementia. Drawing on her background as an anthropologist, psychologist, and art therapist working at Alzheimer’s Australia Tasmania, Baines argues that creativity is a fundamental human need and can be especially therapeutic for those living with memory loss. The paper discusses empirical research supporting the benefits of art therapy, including its ability to enhance mood and cognitive function in individuals with dementia. Key findings highlight the value of creative expression as a means of self-expression and emotional release. Baines provides practical ideas for caregivers and therapists on how to encourage creative activities, such as painting or sculpting, which can remain meaningful even when memory fades. The monograph underscores the importance of fostering creativity not just in professional settings but also within families and communities caring for individuals with dementia.
