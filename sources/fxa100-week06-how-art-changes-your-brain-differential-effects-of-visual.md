## How Art Changes Your Brain: Differential Effects of Visual Art Production and Cognitive Art Evaluation on Functional Brain Connectivity

This study investigates the impact of visual art production and cognitive evaluation on functional brain connectivity, specifically focusing on the default mode network (DMN) in a group of post-retirement adults. The research used fMRI to examine changes before and after 10-week interventions: one involving active art creation and another focused on evaluating artwork cognitively. Participants were divided into two groups based on gender and age stratification, with one engaging in visual art production and the other in cognitive evaluation of art at museums. Results indicated that those who actively produced art showed greater improvements in functional connectivity within specific brain regions associated with resilience against stress. The study highlights the potential for visual art activities to enhance psychological well-being through neural mechanisms in non-clinical populations, contributing valuable insights into how artistic engagement can positively influence cognitive and emotional health.
