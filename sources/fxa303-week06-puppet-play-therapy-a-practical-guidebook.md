## Puppet Play Therapy: A Practical Guidebook

Puppet Play Therapy: A Practical Guidebook is a comprehensive resource written by experts in the field of puppet-based therapeutic interventions. The book aims to provide both novice and experienced clinicians with fundamental concepts, techniques, and applications for using puppets effectively in therapy sessions. It covers various theoretical approaches such as child-centered, psychodynamic, Adlerian, and solution-focused brief therapy, along with practical guidance on selecting appropriate puppets and conducting puppet assessments like the Berkeley Puppet Interview and Family Puppet Interview. The guide also addresses specific populations, including children with trauma and sexual abuse histories, adults in family therapy, and medical settings. Its relevance to creative arts and health lies in its innovative methods for emotional expression and engagement through puppetry, which can be particularly beneficial for individuals who find traditional talk therapies challenging.
