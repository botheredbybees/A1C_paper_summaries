## My daddy and me: Navigating the Japanese gardens at the Royal Tasmania Botanical Gardens

Melanie Hall's photo essay 'My daddy and me' captures a series of intimate moments between a young child and her father as they explore the Japanese gardens at the Royal Tasmanian Botanical Garden. The narrative is told through a lens that emphasizes the sensory experience, including sounds like distant trucks, laughter from a couple, birds chirping, and the sound of flowing water beneath an arched bridge. Hall's collection also includes images that represent physical barriers in daily life, such as lying on the floor trying to reach something out of reach or encountering obstacles while trying to go outside. The series is complemented by contributions from various individuals who encourage a deeper appreciation for nature and observation. This photo essay not only captures the essence of childhood exploration but also the bond between parent and child through unique visual storytelling, making it highly relevant in understanding how creative arts can be used to document personal experiences and promote an awareness of one's surroundings.
