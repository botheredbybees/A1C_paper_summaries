## Prevalence, correlates and course of behavioural and psychological symptoms of dementia in the population

This study explores the behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) among elderly individuals in England and Wales through the Medical Research Council Cognitive Function and Ageing Study (MRC CFAS). The research aimed to determine the prevalence, correlates, and course of BPSD. By analyzing data from 587 participants diagnosed with dementia and 2050 without dementia at baseline, along with subsequent follow-ups, the study found that nearly all individuals with dementia experience some form of BPSD. Key findings include a four-factor solution explaining symptom co-occurrence—psychosis/apathy, depression/anxiety, irritability/persecution, and wandering/sleep problems—and demographic factors influencing these symptoms such as age, self-reported health status, and cognitive decline. The study's insights contribute to understanding the complex interplay of socio-demographic and clinical characteristics with BPSD, highlighting their implications for developing effective treatment strategies and improving quality of life for both patients and caregivers.
