## Assessing Confidence in 'What Works' in Social Policy

This commentary by Yulia Shenderovich, Alex Sutherland, and Sean Grant discusses the challenges in assessing research evidence for policy decisions. The authors highlight that while there are various methods to evaluate evidence, these approaches can yield conflicting conclusions due to differences in methodologies and standards across disciplines. They review tools such as Risk of Bias (RoB) and Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation (GRADE), emphasizing the importance of transparency and systematic evaluation processes. The commentary underscores the need for clear communication from knowledge brokers about their confidence levels in research evidence to policymakers and practitioners. This is crucial for making informed decisions that can have significant impacts on public services and social policy.
