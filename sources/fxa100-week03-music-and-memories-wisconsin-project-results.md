## Music and Memories Wisconsin Project Results

The document presents the results of two projects examining the impact of personalized music programs on nursing home employees and community caregivers. The Nursing Home Employee Impact project surveyed 686 staff members across 83 facilities, finding that while job satisfaction was initially high, it decreased over six months post-program implementation. Employees reported significant positive impacts from personalized music, including enhanced pleasure in their work and improved care for residents with challenging behaviors. However, the program had no notable effect on overall agency work climate or staff turnover rates.

The Community Caregiver Impact project involved 25 participants with dementia and their caregivers. Despite some attrition, 22 participants completed the study. The Caregiving Distress Scale (CDS) measured significant reductions in caregiver distress, emotional burden, and relationship strain post-intervention. Specifically, overall CDS scores decreased by 6.45 points (p=.006), with substantial declines observed in emotional burden (3.36 points, p=.000) and relationship distress (2.20 points, p=.002). These results highlight the positive impact of personalized music on both employee job satisfaction and caregiver well-being in dementia care settings.
