## The Power of Recognition: A Qualitative Study of Social Connectedness and Wellbeing through LGBT Sporting, Creative and Social Groups in Ireland

The study examines the role of LGBT communities in promoting mental health and social wellbeing through various sporting, creative, and social groups in Ireland. Drawing from Honneth’s Theory of Recognition and the Ottawa Charter for Health Promotion, it highlights the importance of recognition and community connectedness. Through qualitative interviews with eleven self-identified LGBT individuals, the research underscores that social connectedness is crucial for mental health, especially within minority communities. The findings suggest that involvement in these groups not only provides a sense of security and resilience but also empowers members by fostering a positive collective identity and reinforcing mutual respect. This study emphasizes the need to acknowledge the protective factors and strengths inherent within LGBT communities rather than focusing solely on vulnerabilities.
