## The Oxford Handbook of Dance and Wellbeing

The Oxford Handbook of Dance and Wellbeing is a comprehensive resource that explores the multifaceted relationship between dance and various aspects of human wellbeing. Edited by Vicky Karkou, Sue Oliver, and Sophia Lycouris, this handbook consists of numerous chapters contributed by experts from diverse fields such as psychology, psychiatry, somatic disciplines, performing arts, education, health sciences, and social science. The book is structured into five parts, covering dance in the context of the body, within performative contexts, community engagement, healthcare settings, and research methodologies. Each part delves into specific themes like emotional benefits derived from authentic movement practices, therapeutic performance, wellbeing improvement through dance therapy for various conditions (including dementia, depression, schizophrenia, breast cancer, and coronary heart disease), as well as cultural adaptations of dance movement psychotherapy experiences.
