## In My Father's Words review – sophisticated dementia drama

Mark Fisher reviews 'In My Father's Words,' a play produced by Dundee Rep during the Commonwealth Games cultural program. Initially perceived as potentially sentimental, the drama turns out to be sophisticated and thought-provoking. The story centers around Dr Louis Bennett who returns to his home in Ontario to care for his estranged father, Don, suffering from dementia. Though Louis cares more out of common humanity than filial affection, the play delves into deeper themes such as memory, identity, sacrifice, and communication challenges between generations. A unique aspect is how Don’s condition leads him to speak Gaelic, shared by Flora Thompson (a care worker), leading to linguistic and cultural discussions about dead languages and minority tongues. The narrative mirrors Hollywood's fascination with ambitious sons and dysfunctional fathers but offers a richer exploration of the trauma of cultural separation. Despite some abruptness in the ending and scene changes, Philip Howard’s production effectively bridges domestic scenes with mythological ones, illustrating how Louis and Don represent two sides of the same rain.
