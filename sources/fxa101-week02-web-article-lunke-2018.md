## New insights into mechanisms of enhanced synaesthetic memory: Benefits are synaesthesia-type-specific

This study examines the memory benefits associated with different types of synaesthesia, focusing on grapheme-colour, sound-colour, grapheme-colour-and-sound-colour, and sequence-space synaesthetes compared to their matched controls. The research evaluates recognition memory for music, words, and colour stimuli across various synaesthetic groups, aiming to identify both general and specific benefits unique to each type of synaesthesia. Results indicate a general advantage in memory among synaesthetes but also reveal synaesthesia-type-specific effects: grapheme-colour synaesthetes exhibited better performance with colour-related stimuli, while those with both grapheme-colour and sound-colour synaesthesia showed an enhanced benefit for music-related tasks. These findings suggest that the mechanisms underlying memory advantages vary between different synaesthetic types.
