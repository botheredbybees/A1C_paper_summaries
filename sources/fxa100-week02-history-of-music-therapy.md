## History of Music Therapy

The history of music therapy traces back to ancient philosophers like Aristotle and Plato, who recognized the therapeutic potential of music. Early references in literature include an article titled 'Music Physically Considered' published in Columbian Magazine in 1789. The modern profession began after World War I when musicians played for veterans suffering from trauma, leading hospitals to hire trained musicians. This demand spurred the development of educational programs and professional associations. Key figures such as E. Thayer Gaston, Willem van de Wall, and Ira Altshuler were instrumental in establishing music therapy as a formal profession in universities during the 1940s. The National Association for Music Therapy (NAMT) was founded in 1950 to set educational standards and promote clinical training, while the American Association for Music Therapy (AAMT) emerged in 1971 with a focus on research and publication. In 1998, AMTA united NAMT and AAMT to form a single organization dedicated to advancing music therapy through advocacy, education, and research.
