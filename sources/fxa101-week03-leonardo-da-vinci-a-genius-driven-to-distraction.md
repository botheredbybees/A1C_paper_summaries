## Leonardo da Vinci: a genius driven to distraction

The article explores the paradox of Leonardo da Vinci, a man celebrated for his artistic and scientific genius but notorious for failing to complete many of his ambitious projects. Despite being recognized as an exceptional artist by both supporters and critics, Leonardo was often criticized for his lack of discipline, poor organizational skills, and difficulty in focusing on tasks to completion. His excessive planning and reluctance to start certain projects led to a sense of failure and frustration throughout his career. Even at the court of Ludovico il Moro, Leonardo struggled with gaining patron trust due to his perceived unreliability in finishing commissions. Additionally, his creative curiosity frequently distracted him from completing work on time, leading patrons to doubt his capabilities. However, this paradoxical nature also contributed to his innovative and experimental approach in art and science, making him an intriguing figure in the history of creativity.
