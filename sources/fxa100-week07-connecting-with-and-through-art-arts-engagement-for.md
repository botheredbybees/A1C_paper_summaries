## Connecting with and through art: Arts engagement for liveable communities

This document discusses a research project titled 'Connecting with and through art,' which explores the development and expansion of arts engagement programs for people living with dementia in Australia. The Arts Engagement for Liveable Communities (AECL) project, funded by the NSW Department of Family and Community Services, aimed to extend the reach and impact of an existing successful program at the Art Gallery of New South Wales (AGNSW). Over fourteen months, the project engaged over 300 attendees from various community spaces including residential care facilities, community centers, and schools. The study highlights the need for arts engagement programs in diverse settings such as galleries, community centers, aged care facilities, and homes to combat social isolation and provide meaningful connections through art. Key findings indicate that these programs foster a sense of connection, joy, and 'in-the-moment pleasure,' particularly beneficial for individuals living with dementia and their carers. The project emphasized the importance of ongoing, rather than one-off, engagement initiatives to reach broader audiences effectively.
