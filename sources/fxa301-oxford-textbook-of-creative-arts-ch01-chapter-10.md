## The Role of Qualitative Research in Arts and Health

This chapter explores the application of qualitative methods in arts and health contexts, emphasizing the importance of clear differentiation between qualitative and quantitative approaches for evaluation purposes. It discusses various qualitative methodologies such as ethnography, grounded theory, narrative research, discourse analysis, participatory action research, and arts-based methods, highlighting their roles in illuminating participant experiences and project processes. Challenges like ethical considerations and participant recruitment are addressed, along with the need to avoid pitfalls common among novice researchers. The chapter also underscores the limitations of the Medical Research Council’s framework for evaluating complex interventions when applied to qualitative studies that focus on deeper questions of meaning.
