## Breathing Prompts and Body Mapping in Trauma Therapy

This chapter discusses breathing techniques and body-mapping activities as therapeutic tools for managing trauma-related stress. The breathing exercise involves a pattern of inhaling through the nose for four counts, exhaling through the mouth for eight counts to calm the vagal system and autonomic nervous system (ANS). It is noted that this technique can be effective after five repetitions or approximately one minute of structured breathing. Additionally, body mapping is introduced as an expressive arts therapy method where individuals paint their bodies or create visual representations on paper to symbolize personal challenges, resilience, and support networks. The author emphasizes the integration of various art forms (e.g., drawing, movement, music) with traditional therapeutic approaches to enhance clients' ability to regulate emotions and transform traumatic narratives.
