## Two Traditions: Hedonia and Eudaimonia

This lesson explores the concepts of hedonia and eudaimonia as philosophical origins informing modern psychological theories on happiness and well-being. It discusses the historical context of these ideas and their relevance in contemporary models. Hedonia is associated with subjective pleasure, while eudaimonia pertains to living a fulfilling life through personal growth and authentic experiences. The lesson introduces thought experiments such as Robert Nozick's Experience Machine to challenge the notion that happiness solely equates to experiencing pleasure. It also outlines how these concepts influence psychological theories like humanistic psychology, PERMA model, and flourishing models.
