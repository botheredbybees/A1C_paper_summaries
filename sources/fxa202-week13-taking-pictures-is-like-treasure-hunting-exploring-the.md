## 'Taking Pictures is Like Treasure Hunting': Exploring the Therapeutic Value of Photography as a Qualitative Research Method

The article investigates the therapeutic benefits of photography as a qualitative research method for mental health service users, focusing on themes like social companionship, self-reflection, connection with nature, and occupation. Through analysis of visual and textual data from workshops and interviews, it highlights how photography can foster personal growth and well-being, reinforcing ethical principles in research. The study suggests that the use of photographs not only aids in qualitative data collection but also serves a therapeutic purpose, contributing to mental health recovery and empowerment.
