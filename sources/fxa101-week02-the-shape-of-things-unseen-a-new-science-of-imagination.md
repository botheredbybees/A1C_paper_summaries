## The Shape of Things Unseen: A New Science of Imagination

In 'The Shape of Things Unseen: A New Science of Imagination,' neurologist Adam Zeman argues that imagination is a fundamental aspect of human cognition, not just reserved for creative or escapist pursuits. According to Zeman, the ability to imagine permeates our waking and sleeping lives and contributes significantly to navigating daily experiences. The book explores various facets of imagination, including inner voices, hallucinations, learning processes, brain science, art, and music. For instance, imagining oneself on a beach or even inhaling demonstrates how imagination uses knowledge to construct mental models that guide us through life's complexities. Zeman’s work highlights the integral role of imagination in expanding personal worlds while also aiding practical navigation of everyday scenarios. This exploration underscores the importance of imagination not only in creative arts but also in understanding human cognition and health.
