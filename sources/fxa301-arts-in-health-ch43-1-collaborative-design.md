## Developing Partnerships and Briefs in Arts and Health Projects

This chapter discusses the development of partnerships between arts organizations and health institutions for collaborative projects. It highlights the importance of demonstrating a thorough understanding of healthcare contexts through case support documents, which can outline specific health challenges and proposed interventions. Partnerships may initiate from either side, with potential methods including direct approaches, limited competition (shortlisting candidates), or open submission. The chapter emphasizes the formation of committees to oversee tender processes, ensuring equitable participation by stakeholders such as clinicians, communications teams, financial advisors, and patient representatives. It also outlines key components for developing project briefs: aims, background, target participants, scope, resources, timeline, evaluation criteria, selection process details, and fees. The cost considerations include assessing professional rates, setting budgets, and evaluating competitive bids.
