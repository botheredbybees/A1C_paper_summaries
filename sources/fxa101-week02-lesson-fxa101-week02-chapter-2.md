## Introduction to the Human Brain

This lesson introduces the human brain as the central control unit for sensory information processing and bodily functions. It highlights the primary structures of the brain such as the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem, each playing crucial roles in various neurological processes. The frontal lobe oversees high-order functions like planning and emotion; the parietal lobe integrates sensory inputs; the occipital lobe handles visual information; and the temporal lobe is involved with hearing, smell, learning, and memory. Additionally, the lesson mentions the thalamus as a hub for signal transmission and consciousness, while the hippocampus forms new memories. The concept of neuroplasticity—where neural pathways can be adapted throughout life—is discussed in relation to learning and creativity.
