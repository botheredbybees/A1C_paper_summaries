## Dance-Specific Activity in People Living With Dementia: A Conceptual Framework and Systematic Review of Its Effects on Neuropsychiatric Symptoms

This study systematically reviews the effects of dance as a non-pharmacological therapy for neuropsychiatric symptoms (NPS) in people living with dementia, proposing a standardized terminology called 'Dance-Specific Activity' (DSA). The review includes four studies that were generally of low quality and varied significantly in intervention protocols. Two trials reported improvements in NPS while two showed no exacerbation. Key findings suggest DSA can be safe for individuals with dementia and should involve structured interventions differentiated by type, content, intention, and protocol. However, high-quality randomized controlled trials are needed to conclusively determine the efficacy of dance therapy. The study underscores the importance of a standardized terminology in enhancing comparability and transferability of research on dance interventions within dementia care.
