## Dance therapy in rehabilitation: a two-decade bibliometric analysis (2000–2024)

The study conducts a bibliometric analysis of dance therapy in rehabilitation, focusing on publications from 2000 to 2024. It identifies significant growth and diversification in research themes over two decades. Key findings include an increase in annual publications from three studies in 2000 to 188 in 2024, with major contributions from the United States, the United Kingdom, and China. The analysis highlights research hotspots such as dance therapy's efficacy for mental health (depression, anxiety), quality of life improvements, and neurodegenerative diseases like Parkinson’s disease. Emerging trends indicate a growing interest in complementary medicine approaches. The study underscores the need for interdisciplinary cooperation to further integrate dance therapy into comprehensive rehabilitation strategies, enhancing its role in promoting holistic well-being.
