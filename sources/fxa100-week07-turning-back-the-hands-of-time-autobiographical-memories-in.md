## Turning back the hands of time: Autobiographical memories in dementia cued by a museum setting

The study explores the impact of museum settings on autobiographical memory retrieval in individuals with dementia. Twelve older participants diagnosed with various forms of dementia were immersed into a historically authentic environment that recreated their youth's material and cultural context, compared to an everyday setting as control condition. Participants recalled more autobiographical memories in the museum setting, which were also more detailed and spontaneous, indicating the power of contextual cues in enhancing memory recall. This research highlights how specific environmental settings can facilitate episodic detail retrieval among dementia patients, potentially aiding therapeutic interventions aimed at preserving identity and enriching quality of life.
