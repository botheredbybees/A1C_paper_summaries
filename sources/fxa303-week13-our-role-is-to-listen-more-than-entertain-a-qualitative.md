## 'Our role is to listen more than entertain': A qualitative study of techniques used by Laughter Care Specialists with people who have dementia in long-term care

This study explores the techniques used by Laughter Care Specialists (LCSs) to engage with people who have dementia in long-term care facilities. The research highlights that LCSs prioritize valuing personhood and adopt individualized, empathetic approaches tailored to residents' needs. Techniques include creating opportunities for contribution, reminiscence, identity appreciation, and playful interaction. These methods aim to alleviate boredom, combat loneliness, and improve social engagement and quality of life for dementia patients. The study underscores the importance of non-pharmacological interventions in addressing unmet needs, thereby reducing responsive behaviors such as depression and agitation. Furthermore, it emphasizes the role of LCSs in fostering a person-centered approach that respects individual identity and dignity, aligning with Australia's Strengthened Aged Care Quality Standards.
