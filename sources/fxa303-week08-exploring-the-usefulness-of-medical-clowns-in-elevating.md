## Exploring the usefulness of medical clowns in elevating satisfaction and reducing aggressive tendencies in pediatric and adult hospital wards

This research article investigates the effectiveness of medical clowns in enhancing satisfaction and reducing aggressive tendencies among pediatric and adult health consumers. Through three studies involving different stakeholders, including medical staff, clowns, and patients, the authors aim to determine the optimal placement for medical clowns based on age groups. The findings reveal that while medical clowns are generally more beneficial in pediatric wards by improving child satisfaction and decreasing aggressive behaviors, they have an opposite effect on adults, potentially increasing dissatisfaction and aggression among older individuals. This suggests a nuanced approach is needed when integrating medical clown services into healthcare settings to maximize their therapeutic benefits.
