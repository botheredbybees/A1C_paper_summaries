## Music Therapy and Music-Assisted Caregiving for Dementia Patients

The MJHHS Dementia Care project details a three-year initiative funded by New York State Health Department to evaluate the impact of music therapy on dementia patients' agitation, depression, and anxiety at Shorefront. The program includes six protocols: group-based (sing-along, tonal music, movement) and individual-based (range of motion, music-assisted bathing/shower, wound care). Music is highlighted for its ability to engage individuals across all stages of dementia due to preserved brain areas responsible for processing it. Participants show significant improvements in mood and social interaction, enhancing the caregiving environment and reducing staff burnout. Financial benefits include low costs and easy implementation. The project also observes increased family visitation due to happier residents, fostering stronger relationships among patients, caregivers, and loved ones.
