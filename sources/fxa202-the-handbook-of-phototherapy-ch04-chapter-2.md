## Phenomenological-Hermeneutic Case Study Method in Talking Pictures Therapy

This chapter explores a phenomenological-hermeneutic case study method applied to Talking Pictures Therapy, which uses photographs as a therapeutic tool for brief counseling with children. The research involves two case examples of children from an area of relative deprivation in the South coast of England who underwent six weekly sessions of therapy. Each child chose photos that reflected their emotional states and progress through the intervention. Evaluative data were collected using standard assessment tools like PHQ-9, GAD-7, CORE-10, and a phobia scale to measure mental health improvement alongside qualitative insights from photo choices. The study illustrates how photographs serve as evaluative measures of therapeutic effectiveness.
