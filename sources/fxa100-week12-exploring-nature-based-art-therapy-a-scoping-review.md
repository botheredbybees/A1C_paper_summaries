## Exploring nature-based art therapy: a scoping review

The study presents a scoping review of nature-based art therapy, which utilizes natural elements and settings to promote mental health through creative expression facilitated by professional art therapists. The research highlights the growing interest in nature-based therapeutic approaches following the COVID-19 pandemic due to their accessibility and affordability. Four key themes emerged from the review: areas of focus, nature engagement, core elements, and challenges. Areas of focus include overall mental well-being, emotion regulation, cognitive development, social support, self-discovery, trauma management, creative expression, and environmental sustainability. Core elements encompass artwork, materials, and therapy settings. Despite promising benefits in nature-based art therapy, challenges such as client assistance complexities, limited access to nature, psychosocial barriers, and risk management were also identified. The review underscores the need for further theoretical and practical evidence-based development within this emerging field.
