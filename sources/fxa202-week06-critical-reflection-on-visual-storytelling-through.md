## Critical Reflection on Visual Storytelling Through Photography

Emma Barratt's critical reflection explores the process of creating a photo essay as a form of therapeutic and creative expression amidst personal struggles with memory loss, chronic fatigue, fibromyalgia, and dissociative identity disorder. Initially challenged by compressing her complex life story into visual images, Barratt found inspiration in non-linear storytelling techniques. Her narrative is structured around three key locations: home, Greens Beach, and Sheffield, each symbolizing different phases of her journey with trauma and recovery. The essay highlights the use of compositional elements such as rule of thirds, leading lines, repetition, and color to convey emotional depth and memory clarity. Barratt reflects on the therapeutic value of photography in documenting health experiences and fostering mindfulness, supported by scholarly references emphasizing the benefits of creative arts for mental well-being. Her work underscores the potential of photo essays as a powerful medium for self-expression and healing.
