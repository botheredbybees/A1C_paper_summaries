## Dementia and Ageing: A Broad Perspective

This chapter discusses the complexity of distinguishing between dementia and normal aging, emphasizing the importance of broad research perspectives in understanding both concepts. It argues that dementia should be viewed as part of broader aging research rather than a separate clinical syndrome, advocating for an interdisciplinary approach that encompasses biological, psychological, social, ethical, political, and economic dimensions. The text critiques the narrow focus often attributed to dementia research and suggests that focusing on ageing promotes comprehensive care approaches and community involvement. It also explores different types of research methodologies, such as basic, applied, and translational research, highlighting participatory action research as a more effective approach due to its real-world problem-solving orientation.
