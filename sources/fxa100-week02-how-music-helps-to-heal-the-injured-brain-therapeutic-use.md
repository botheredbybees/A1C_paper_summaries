## How Music Helps to Heal the Injured Brain: Therapeutic Use Crescendos Thanks to Advances in Brain Science

This article discusses how the role of music in therapy has evolved significantly due to advances in brain science, particularly in understanding the brain's plasticity and the mechanisms through which music interacts with various neurological functions. Initially perceived as a supplementary treatment, music therapy is now recognized for its potential to retrain and rehabilitate injured brains by engaging common neural networks associated with motor control, language processing, attention, memory, and executive function. Research has shown that rhythmic auditory cues can enhance movement control in patients recovering from stroke or suffering from Parkinson’s disease, leading to improvements in walking speed, coordination, and prevention of movement freezing. The article advocates for incorporating evidence-based neurologic music therapy into standard rehabilitation practices, emphasizing its efficacy supported by rigorous clinical research.
