## Physiological and Emotional Responses of Disabled Children to Therapeutic Clowns: A Pilot Study

This pilot study investigates the impact of therapeutic clowning on disabled children in a pediatric rehabilitation hospital. Ten children with various physical and verbal abilities participated, undergoing physiological monitoring, emotional assessment, and behavioral observation during interventions by therapeutic clowns and while watching television. Results showed significant positive changes in autonomic nervous system signals, mood reports, and facial expressions during the clown intervention compared to TV exposure. The study suggests that therapeutic clowning can positively influence children's physical and mental well-being, contributing to healthcare settings' use of complementary approaches for enhancing patient care.
