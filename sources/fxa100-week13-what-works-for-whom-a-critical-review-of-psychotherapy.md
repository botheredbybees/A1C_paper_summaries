## What Works for Whom? A Critical Review of Psychotherapy Research

The book 'What Works for Whom? A Critical Review of Psychotherapy Research' by Anthony Roth and Peter Fonagy critically examines the efficacy and applicability of various psychotherapeutic approaches. The authors argue that while evidence supports the effectiveness of certain therapeutic modalities, no single method is universally superior. They highlight the importance of considering individual patient characteristics and treatment settings when assessing the success of different psychotherapy methods. Key findings include the significance of defining 'effect size' in research studies and acknowledging the complexities involved in translating empirical research into practical clinical applications. The relevance to creative arts and health lies in its emphasis on personalized, context-specific interventions which align with holistic approaches advocated for in creative arts therapies.
