## Current Emotion Research in Music Psychology

This article reviews recent research in music psychology focusing on the connection between music and emotion, particularly how emotions are expressed and perceived through music. It discusses universal acoustic cues that contribute to emotional expressiveness across different cultures and the development of cultural-specific musical expectations over time. The authors also examine affective responses to music, highlighting that there is significant evidence suggesting people do experience genuine emotions in response to musical stimuli. Emotions perceived reliably in music change with age and development, influenced by repeated exposure to culturally familiar music. Overall, the article underscores the importance of both universal and culture-specific elements in understanding how music conveys emotion.
